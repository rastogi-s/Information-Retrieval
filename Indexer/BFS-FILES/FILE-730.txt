   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Sodium tail of the Moon
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       has been shown to have a "tail" of
       <a href="/wiki/Sodium" title="Sodium">
        sodium
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Atom" title="Atom">
        atoms
       </a>
       too faint to be detected by the
       <a href="/wiki/Human_eye" title="Human eye">
        human eye
       </a>
       . Hundreds of thousands of kilometers long, the feature was discovered in 1998 as a result of scientists from
       <a href="/wiki/Boston_University" title="Boston University">
        Boston University
       </a>
       observing the
       <a href="/wiki/Leonids" title="Leonids">
        Leonid
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Meteor_shower" title="Meteor shower">
        meteor shower
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Moon is constantly releasing atomic sodium as a fine dust from its surface due to
       <a href="/wiki/Photon" title="Photon">
        photon
       </a>
       -stimulated
       <a href="/wiki/Desorption" title="Desorption">
        desorption
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_wind" title="Solar wind">
        solar wind
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Sputtering" title="Sputtering">
        sputtering
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Meteorite" title="Meteorite">
        meteorite
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_event" title="Impact event">
        impacts
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_radiation" title="Solar radiation">
        Solar radiation
       </a>
       pressure accelerates the sodium atoms away from the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       , forming an elongated tail toward the
       <a href="/wiki/Antisolar_point" title="Antisolar point">
        antisolar direction
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The continual impacts of
       <a href="/wiki/Micrometeorite" title="Micrometeorite">
        small meteorites
       </a>
       produce a constant "tail" from the Moon, but the Leonids have intensified it,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       thus making it more observable from
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       than usual.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
