      <p>
       <b>
        IMDb
       </b>
       , also known as
       <b>
        Internet Movie Database
       </b>
       , is an
       <a href="/wiki/Online_database" title="Online database">
        online database
       </a>
       of information related to world films, television programs, home videos and video games, and internet streams, including cast, production crew, personnel and fictional character biographies, plot summaries, trivia, and fan reviews and ratings. An additional fan feature, message boards, was abandoned in February, 2017. The database is owned and operated by IMDb.com, Inc., a subsidiary of
       <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">
        Amazon
       </a>
       . As of December 2017
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       , IMDb has approximately 4.7 million titles (including episodes) and 8.3 million personalities in its database,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       as well as 83 million registered users. The movie and talent pages of IMDb are accessible to all internet users, but a registration process is necessary to contribute information to the site.
      </p>
      <p>
       Most data in the database is provided by volunteer contributors. The site enables registered users to submit new material and edits to existing entries. Users with a proven track record of submitting factual data are given instant approval for additions or corrections to cast, credits, and other demographics of media product and personalities. However, image, name, character name, plot summaries, and title changes are supposedly screened before publication, and usually take between 24–72 hours to appear.
      </p>
      <p>
       All registered users choose their own site name, and most operate anonymously. They have a
       <i>
        profile page
       </i>
       which shows how long a registered user has been a member, as well as personal movie ratings (should the user decide to display them) and, since 2015, "badges" are added representing how many contributions a particular registered user has submitted. These badges range from total contributions made to independent categories such as photos, trivia, bios, etc. If a registered user or visitor is in the entertainment industry and has an IMDb
       <i>
        page
       </i>
       , that user/visitor can add photos to that page by enrolling in IMDbPRO.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There is no single index of contributors, no index on each profile page of the items contributed, and, excepting for plot synopses and biographies, no identification of contributors to each product's or person's data pages.
      </p>
      <p>
       Users are also invited to rate any film on a scale of 1 to 10, and the totals are converted into a
       <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">
        weighted mean
       </a>
       -rating that is displayed beside each title, with online filters employed to deter
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballot-stuffing" title="Ballot-stuffing">
        ballot-stuffing
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History_before_website">
        History before website
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History before website">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       IMDb originated with a
       <a href="/wiki/Usenet" title="Usenet">
        Usenet
       </a>
       posting by British film fan and computer programmer
       <a href="/wiki/Col_Needham" title="Col Needham">
        Col Needham
       </a>
       entitled "Those Eyes", about actresses with beautiful eyes. Others with similar interests soon responded with additions or different lists of their own. Needham subsequently started an "Actors List", while Dave Knight began a "
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        Directors
       </a>
       List", and Andy Krieg took over "THE LIST" from Hank Driskill, which would later be renamed the "Actress List". Both lists had been restricted to people who were alive and working, but soon retired people were added, so Needham started what was then (but did not remain) a separate "Dead Actors/Actresses List". The goal of the participants now was to make the lists as inclusive as possible.
      </p>
      <p>
       By late 1990, the lists included almost 10,000 movies and television series correlated with actors and actresses appearing therein. On October 17, 1990, Needham developed and posted a collection of
       <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">
        Unix
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Shell_script" title="Shell script">
        shell scripts
       </a>
       which could be used to search the four lists, and thus the database that would become the IMDb was born.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       At the time, it was known as the "rec.arts.movies movie database".
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="On_the_web">
        On the web
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: On the web">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The database had been expanded to include additional categories of filmmakers and other demographic material as well as trivia, biographies, and plot summaries. The movie ratings had been properly integrated with the list data, and a centralized email interface for querying the database had been created by Alan Jay. Later, in 1993, it moved onto the
       <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">
        World Wide Web
       </a>
       , (a network in its infancy at that time) under the name of
       <i>
        Cardiff Internet Movie Database
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The database resided on the servers of the computer science department of
       <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_University" title="Cardiff University">
        Cardiff University
       </a>
       in Wales.
       <a href="/wiki/Rob_Hartill" title="Rob Hartill">
        Rob Hartill
       </a>
       was the original web interface author. In 1994, the email interface was revised to accept the submission of all information, which enabled people to email the specific list maintainer with their updates. However, the structure remained so that information received on a single film was divided among multiple section managers, the sections being defined and determined by categories of film personnel and the individual filmographies contained therein. Over the next few years, the database was run on a network of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Web_mirror" title="Web mirror">
        mirrors
       </a>
       across the world with donated bandwidth.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="As_an_independent_company">
        As an independent company
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: As an independent company">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In 1996 IMDb was incorporated in the United Kingdom, becoming the Internet Movie Database Ltd. Founder Col Needham became the primary owner as well as the figurehead. General revenue for site operations was generated through advertising, licensing and partnerships.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="As_Amazon.com_subsidiary_.281998.E2.80.93present.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="As_Amazon.com_subsidiary_(1998–present)">
        As Amazon.com subsidiary (1998–present)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: As Amazon.com subsidiary (1998–present)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In 1998,
       <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Bezos" title="Jeff Bezos">
        Jeff Bezos
       </a>
       , founder, owner and CEO of Amazon.com, struck a deal with Needham and other principal shareholders to buy IMDb outright for approximately $55 million
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and attach it to Amazon as a subsidiary, private company.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This gave IMDb the ability to pay the shareholders salaries for their work, while Amazon.com would be able to use IMDb as an advertising resource for selling DVDs and videotapes.
      </p>
      <p>
       IMDb continued to expand its functionality. On January 15, 2002, it added a subscription service known as IMDbPro, aimed at entertainment professionals. IMDbPro was announced and launched at the 2002
       <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">
        Sundance Film Festival
       </a>
       . It provides a variety of services including film production and box office details, as well as a company directory and the ability of subscribers to add personal information pages with details at variance with pages about them appearing in the database.
      </p>
      <p>
       As an additional incentive for users, as of 2003, users identified as one of "the top 100 contributors" of hard data received complimentary free access to IMDbPro for the following calendar year; for 2006 this was increased to the top 150 contributors, and for 2010 to the top 250.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2008 IMDb launched their first official foreign language version with the German IMDb.de. Also in 2008, IMDb acquired two other companies,
       <a href="/wiki/Withoutabox" title="Withoutabox">
        Withoutabox
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Box_Office_Mojo" title="Box Office Mojo">
        Box Office Mojo
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The website was originally
       <a href="/wiki/Perl" title="Perl">
        Perl
       </a>
       -based, but IMDb no longer discloses what software it uses for reasons of security.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As of May 2011, the site has been
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_censorship_in_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Internet censorship in the People's Republic of China">
        filtered in China
       </a>
       for more than one year, although many users address it through
       <a href="/wiki/Proxy_server" title="Proxy server">
        proxy server
       </a>
       or by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VPN" title="VPN">
        VPN
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On October 17, 2010, IMDb launched original video (www.imdb.com/20) in celebration of its 20th anniversary.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="IMDbPro">
        IMDbPro
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: IMDbPro">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Actors, crew, and industry executives can post their own resume and upload photos of themselves for a yearly fee. This fee gives them membership in IMDbPro. IMDbPro can be accessed by anyone willing to pay the fee, which is $19.99 USD per month, or if paid annually, $149.99. Membership enables a user to access the rank order of each industry personality, as well as agent contact information for any actor, producer, director etc. that has an IMDb page. Enrolling in IMDbPro for industry personnel, enables those members the ability to upload a head shot to open their page, as well as the ability to upload hundreds of photos to accompany their page. Anyone can register as an IMDb user and contribute to the site as well as view its content, however those users enrolled in IMDbPro have greater access and privileges.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span id="Copyright.2C_vandalism_and_error_issues">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Copyright,_vandalism_and_error_issues">
        Copyright, vandalism and error issues
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Copyright, vandalism and error issues">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       All volunteers who contribute content to the database technically retain copyright on their contributions but the compilation of the content becomes the exclusive property of IMDb with the full right to copy, modify, and sublicense it and they are verified before posting.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Credit is not given on specific title or filmography pages to the contributor(s) who have provided information. Conversely, a credited text entry, such as a plot summary, may be corrected for content, grammar, sentence structure, perceived omission or error, by other contributors without having to add their names as co-authors. Due to the time required for processing submitted data or text before it is displayed, IMDb is different from user-contributed projects like Wikipedia,
       <a href="/wiki/Discogs" title="Discogs">
        Discogs
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/OpenStreetMap" title="OpenStreetMap">
        OpenStreetMap
       </a>
       in that contributors cannot add, delete, or modify the data or text on impulse, and the manipulation of data is controlled by IMDb technology and salaried staff.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       IMDb has been subject to deliberate additions of false information; in 2012 a spokesperson said: "We make it easy for users and professionals to update much of our content, which is why we have an 'edit page.' The data that is submitted goes through a series of consistency checks before it goes live. Given the sheer volume of the information, occasional mistakes are inevitable, and, when reported, they are promptly fixed. We always welcome corrections."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Java Movie Database (JMDB)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jmdb_23-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jmdb-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is reportedly creating an IMDb_Error.log file that lists all the errors found while processing the IMDb plain text files. A Wiki alternative to IMDb is Open Media Database
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       whose content is also contributed by users but licensed under
       <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons_license" title="Creative Commons license">
        CC-by
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/GNU_Free_Documentation_License" title="GNU Free Documentation License">
        GFDL
       </a>
       . Since 2007, IMDb has been experimenting with wiki-programmed sections for complete film synopses, parental guides, and FAQs about titles as determined by (and answered by) individual contributors.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Data_format_and_access">
        Data format and access
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Data format and access">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       IMDb does not provide an
       <a href="/wiki/Web_API" title="Web API">
        API
       </a>
       for automated queries. However, most of the data can be downloaded as
       <a href="/wiki/Data_compression" title="Data compression">
        compressed
       </a>
       plain text files and the information can be extracted using the
       <a href="/wiki/Command-line_interface" title="Command-line interface">
        command-line interface
       </a>
       tools provided.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There is also a Java-based
       <a href="/wiki/Graphical_user_interface" title="Graphical user interface">
        graphical user interface
       </a>
       (GUI) application available that is able to process the compressed plain text files, which allows a search and a display of the information.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jmdb_23-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-jmdb-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This GUI application supports different languages, but the movie related data are in English, as made available by IMDb. A
       <a href="/wiki/Python_(programming_language)" title="Python (programming language)">
        Python
       </a>
       package called IMDbPY can also be used to process the compressed plain text files into a number of different
       <a href="/wiki/SQL" title="SQL">
        SQL
       </a>
       databases, enabling easier access to the entire dataset for searching or data mining.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ancillary_features">

       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="User_ratings_of_films">
        User ratings of films
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: User ratings of films">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       As one adjunct to data, the IMDb offers a
       <a href="/wiki/Rating_scale" title="Rating scale">
        rating scale
       </a>
       that allows users to rate films on a scale of one to ten. It has been alleged that the rating system is flawed, for several reasons.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       IMDb indicates that submitted ratings are filtered and weighted in various ways in order to produce a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean">
        weighted mean
       </a>
       that is displayed for each film, series, and so on. It states that filters are used to avoid
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballot_stuffing" title="Ballot stuffing">
        ballot stuffing
       </a>
       ; the method is not described in detail to avoid attempts to circumvent it. In fact, it sometimes produces an extreme difference between the weighted average and the arithmetic mean.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Rankings">
        Rankings
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Rankings">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       The IMDb Top 250 is a list of the top rated 250 films, based on ratings by the registered users of the website using the methods described. As of 30 September 2017
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Shawshank_Redemption" title="The Shawshank Redemption">
         The Shawshank Redemption
        </a>
       </i>
       is #1 on the list.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The 'top 250' rating is based on only the ratings of "regular voters". The number of votes a registered user would have to make to be considered as a user who votes regularly has been kept secret. IMDb has stated that to maintain the effectiveness of the top 250 list they
       <i>
        "deliberately do not disclose the criteria used for a person to be counted as a regular voter"
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In addition to other weightings, the top 250 films are also based on a weighted rating formula referred to in
       <a href="/wiki/Actuarial_science" title="Actuarial science">
        actuarial science
       </a>
       as a
       <i>
        credibility formula
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Message_boards">
        Message boards
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Message boards">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Beginning in 2001, the Internet Movie Database also maintained message boards for every title (excepting, as of 2013, TV episodes
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
        <a href="#cite_note-35">
         [35]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ) and name entry, along with over 140 main boards. This began in 2001. In order to post on the message boards a user needed to "authenticate" their account via cell phone, credit card, or by having been a recent customer of the parent company
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">
        Amazon.com
       </a>
       . Message boards expanded in recent years. The Soapbox started in 1999 as a general message board meant for debates on any subjects. The Politics board started in 2007 was a message board to discuss politics, news events, and current affairs, as well as history and economics.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       As of February 20, 2017, all the message boards and their content have been permanently removed. According to the website, the decision was made because the boards were "no longer providing a positive, useful experience for the vast majority of our more than 250 million monthly users worldwide".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
        <a href="#cite_note-36">
         [36]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Col Needham also mentioned in a post some months earlier that the boards received less income from ads, and that their members only made up a very small part of the website's visitors. The boards were costly to run due to the system's age and dated design, which did not make business sense.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
        <a href="#cite_note-37">
         [37]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The decision to remove the message boards was met with outspoken backlash across the web and on social media, and sparked an online petition garnering over 8,000 signatures.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
        <a href="#cite_note-38">
         [38]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the days leading up to February 20, 2017, both Archive.org
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
        <a href="#cite_note-39">
         [39]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and MovieChat.org
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
        <a href="#cite_note-40">
         [40]
        </a>
       </sup>
       preserved the entire contents of the IMDb message boards using web scraping technologies.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Litigation">
        Litigation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=IMDb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Litigation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In 2011, in the case of
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hoang_v._Amazon.com" title="Hoang v. Amazon.com">
         Hoang v. Amazon.com
        </a>
       </i>
       , IMDb was sued by an anonymous actress for more than
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">
        US$
       </a>
       1,000,000 due to IMDb's revealing her age (40, at the time).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
        <a href="#cite_note-41">
         [41]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The actress claimed that revealing her age could cause her to lose acting opportunities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
        <a href="#cite_note-42">
         [42]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Judge
       <a href="/wiki/Marsha_J._Pechman" title="Marsha J. Pechman">
        Marsha J. Pechman
       </a>
       , a U.S. district judge in Seattle, dismissed the lawsuit, saying the actress had no grounds to proceed with an anonymous complaint. The actress re-filed and so revealed that she was Huong Hoang of Texas, who uses the stage name Junie Hoang.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
        <a href="#cite_note-43">
         [43]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2013, Pechman dismissed all causes of action except for a
       <a href="/wiki/Breach_of_contract" title="Breach of contract">
        breach of contract
       </a>
       claim against IMDb; a jury then sided with IMDb on that claim.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
        <a href="#cite_note-44">
         [44]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Ninth_Circuit" title="Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit">
        Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit
       </a>
       affirmed the district court judgment in March 2015.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
        <a href="#cite_note-45">
         [45]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Also in 2011, in the case of United Video Properties Inc., et al. v. Amazon.Com Inc. et al.,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">
        <a href="#cite_note-46">
         [46]
        </a>
       </sup>
       IMDb and Amazon were sued by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rovi_Corporation" title="Rovi Corporation">
        Rovi Corporation
       </a>
       and others for patent infringement over their various program listing offerings.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">
        <a href="#cite_note-47">
         [47]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The patent claims were ultimately construed in a way favorable to IMDb and Rovi/United Video Properties lost the case, though as of November 2014 it is on appeal.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">
        <a href="#cite_note-48">
         [48]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On January 1, 2017, the State of California implemented state bill AB-1687, a
       <a href="/wiki/SAG-AFTRA" title="SAG-AFTRA">
        SAG-AFTRA
       </a>
       -backed anti-ageism statute which requires "commercial online entertainment employment services" to honor requests by their subscribers for their ages and birthdays to be hidden.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thr-imdblaw_49-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-thr-imdblaw-49">
         [49]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On February 23, 2017, Judge Vince Girdhari Chhabria issued a stay on the bill pending a further trial, claiming that it possibly violated the
       <a href="/wiki/First_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="First Amendment to the United States Constitution">
        First Amendment
       </a>
       because it inhibited the public consumption of factual information. He also questioned the intent of the bill, as it was ostensibly meant to target IMDb.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thr-lawstruck_50-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-thr-lawstruck-50">
         [50]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       As of the beginning of 2017, IMDb has received 2,300 requests from individuals to remove their date of birth from the site. Included in this group were 10
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
        Academy Award
       </a>
       winners and another 71 who’ve been nominated for Oscars,
       <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">
        Emmys
       </a>
       , or
       <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">
        Golden Globes
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51">
        <a href="#cite_note-51">
         [51]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
