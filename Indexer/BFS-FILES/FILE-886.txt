      <p>
       <b>
        The Lizard
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">
        Cornish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="kw" xml:lang="kw">
         An Lysardh
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">
        peninsula
       </a>
       in southern
       <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">
        Cornwall
       </a>
       , England, United Kingdom. The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extreme_points_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Extreme points of the United Kingdom">
        most southerly point
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">
        British mainland
       </a>
       is near
       <a href="/wiki/Lizard_Point,_Cornwall" title="Lizard Point, Cornwall">
        Lizard Point
       </a>
       at
       <a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">
        grid reference
       </a>
       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion" style="white-space: nowrap">
        <a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=The_Lizard&amp;params=SW+701115_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">
         SW 701115
        </a>
       </span>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Lizard_(village)" title="Lizard (village)">
        Lizard
       </a>
       village is the most southerly on the British mainland, and is in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">
        civil parish
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Landewednack" title="Landewednack">
        Landewednack
       </a>
       , the most southerly parish. The valleys of the
       <a href="/wiki/Helford_River" title="Helford River">
        River Helford
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/The_Loe" title="The Loe">
        Loe Pool
       </a>
       form the northern boundary, with the rest of the peninsula surrounded by sea.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The area measures about 14 by 14 miles (23 km × 23 km). The Lizard is one of England's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_regions" title="Natural regions">
        natural regions
       </a>
       and has been designated as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_character_area" title="National character area">
        national character area
       </a>
       157 by
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">
        Natural England
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The peninsula is known for its
       <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">
        geology
       </a>
       and for its
       <a href="/wiki/Botany" title="Botany">
        rare plants
       </a>
       and lies within the
       <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">
        Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty
       </a>
       (AONB).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lawman_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-lawman-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Almost a third of Cornwall has AONB designation, with the same status and protection as a National Park.
      </p>
      <p>
       The name "Lizard" is most probably a corruption of the Cornish name "Lys Ardh", meaning "high court";
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       it is purely coincidental that much of the peninsula is composed of
       <a href="/wiki/Serpentinite" title="Serpentinite">
        serpentinite
       </a>
       -bearing rock. The Lizard peninsula's original name may have been the
       <a href="/wiki/Celtic_languages" title="Celtic languages">
        Celtic
       </a>
       name "Predannack" ("British one");
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       during the
       <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">
        Iron Age
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Pytheas" title="Pytheas">
        Pytheas
       </a>
       c. 325 BC) and Roman period, Britain was known as Pretannike (in Greek) and as
       <a href="/wiki/Albion" title="Albion">
        Albion
       </a>
       (and Britons the "Pretani").
      </p>
      <p>
       The Lizard's coast is particularly hazardous to shipping and the seaways round the peninsula were historically known as the "Graveyard of Ships" (
       <i>
        see below
       </i>
       ). The
       <a href="/wiki/Lizard_Lighthouse" title="Lizard Lighthouse">
        Lizard Lighthouse
       </a>
       was built at Lizard Point in 1752 and the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Lifeboat_Institution" title="Royal National Lifeboat Institution">
        RNLI
       </a>
       operates
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Lizard_lifeboat_station" title="The Lizard lifeboat station">
        The Lizard lifeboat station
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Lizard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There is evidence of early habitation with several
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burial_mounds" title="Burial mounds">
        burial mounds
       </a>
       and stones. Part of the peninsula is known as the
       <a href="/wiki/Meneage" title="Meneage">
        Meneage
       </a>
       (land of the monks).
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Helston" title="Helston">
        Helston
       </a>
       , the nearest town to the Lizard peninsula, is said to have once headed the estuary of the
       <a href="/wiki/River_Cober" title="River Cober">
        River Cober
       </a>
       , before it was cut off from the sea by
       <a href="/wiki/The_Loe" title="The Loe">
        Loe Bar
       </a>
       in the 13th century. It is a matter of debate as to whether Helston was once a port, albeit no actual records still exist.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Geomorphology" title="Geomorphology">
        Geomorphologists
       </a>
       believe the bar was most likely formed by rising sea levels, after the
       <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">
        last ice age
       </a>
       , blocking the river and creating a
       <a href="/wiki/Shoal" title="Shoal">
        barrier beach
       </a>
       . The beach is formed mostly of flint and the nearest source is found offshore under the drowned terraces of the former river that flowed between England and France, and now under the
       <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">
        English Channel
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The medieval port of Helston was at
       <a href="/wiki/Gweek" title="Gweek">
        Gweek
       </a>
       , possibly from around 1260 onwards, on the
       <a href="/wiki/Helford_River" title="Helford River">
        Helford river
       </a>
       which exported
       <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">
        tin
       </a>
       and copper. Helston was believed to be in existence in the sixth century, around the
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dowr_Kohar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dowr Kohar (page does not exist)">
         Dowr Kohar
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The name comes from the Cornish "hen lis" or "old court" and "ton" added later to denote a Saxon manor; the
       <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">
        Domesday Book
       </a>
       refers to it as Henliston (which survives as the name of a road in the town).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was granted its charter by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_of_England" title="John of England">
        King John
       </a>
       in 1201.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was here that tin ingots were weighed to determine the duty due to the
       <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cornwall" title="Duke of Cornwall">
        Duke of Cornwall
       </a>
       when a number of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stannary_town" title="Stannary town">
        stannary towns
       </a>
       were authorised by royal decree.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The royal manor of
       <b>
        Winnianton
       </b>
       , which was held by King William I at the time of the
       <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">
        Domesday Book
       </a>
       (1086), was also the head manor of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kerrier_Hundred" title="Kerrier Hundred">
        hundred of Kerrier
       </a>
       and the largest estate in Cornwall.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was assessed as having fifteen hides before 1066. At the time of Domesday there was land for sixty ploughs, but in the lord's land there were two ploughs and in the lands held by villeins twenty-four ploughs. There were twenty-four villeins, forty-one freedmen, thirty-three smallholders and fourteen slaves. There was 6 acres (24,000 m
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
       ), eight square leagues of pasture and half a square league of woodland. The livestock was fourteen unbroken mares, three cattle and one hundred and twenty-eight sheep (in total 145 beasts); its value was £12 annually. 11 of the hides were held by the
       <a href="/wiki/Robert,_Count_of_Mortain" title="Robert, Count of Mortain">
        Count of Mortain
       </a>
       and there is more arable and pasture and 13 more persons are recorded:
       <a href="/wiki/Rinsey" title="Rinsey">
        Rinsey
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trelowarren" title="Trelowarren">
        Trelowarren
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mawgan-in-Meneage" title="Mawgan-in-Meneage">
        Mawgan-in-Meneage
       </a>
       and seventeen other lands are also recorded under Winnianton.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Mullion,_Cornwall" title="Mullion, Cornwall">
        Mullion
       </a>
       has the 15th century church of St Mellanus, and the Old Inn from the 16th century. The harbour was completed in 1895 and financed by Lord Robartes of
       <a href="/wiki/Lanhydrock" title="Lanhydrock">
        Lanhydrock
       </a>
       as a recompense to the fishermen for several disastrous pilchard seasons.
      </p>
      <p>
       The small church of St Peter in
       <a href="/wiki/Coverack" title="Coverack">
        Coverack
       </a>
       , built in 1885 for £500, has a serpentinite pulpit.
      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">

       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/GWR_road_motor_services" title="GWR road motor services">

        Helston railway station

       <a href="/wiki/Light_railway" title="Light railway">
        light railway
       </a>
       .
      </p>

       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_11_August_1999" title="Solar eclipse of 11 August 1999">
        Solar eclipse of 11 August 1999
       </a>
       departed the UK mainland from the Lizard.
      </p>

       The transatlantic record run of the unaccompanied one hand sailor
       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Coville" title="Thomas Coville">
        Thomas Coville
       </a>
       within less than 5 days in his sailboat
       <i>
        Sodebo Ultim
       </i>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Nautical">
        Nautical
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Lizard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nautical">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Lizard has been the site of many maritime disasters. It forms a natural obstacle to entry and exit of
       <a href="/wiki/Falmouth,_Cornwall" title="Falmouth, Cornwall">
        Falmouth
       </a>
       and its naturally deep estuary. At Lizard Point stands the
       <a href="/wiki/Lizard_Lighthouse" title="Lizard Lighthouse">
        Lizard Lighthouse
       </a>
       . In fact, the light was erected by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Killigrew_(d.1584)" title="John Killigrew (d.1584)">
        Sir John Killigrew
       </a>
       by his own expense: It was built at the cost of "20 nobles a year" for 30 years, but it caused an uproar over the following years, as King James I considered charging vessels to pass. This caused so many problems that the lighthouse was demolished, but was successfully rebuilt in 1751 by order of
       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Fonnereau" title="Thomas Fonnereau">
        Thomas Fonnereau
       </a>
       and remains almost unchanged today. Further east lie
       <a href="/wiki/The_Manacles" title="The Manacles">
        The Manacles
       </a>
       , near
       <a href="/wiki/Porthoustock" title="Porthoustock">
        Porthoustock
       </a>
       :
       <span class="frac nowrap">
        1
        <span class="visualhide">
        </span>
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        ⁄
        <sub>
         2
        </sub>
       </span>
       square miles (4 km
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
       ) of jagged rocks just beneath the waves.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In 1721 the Royal Anne Galley, an oared frigate, was wrecked at Lizard Point. Of a crew of 185 only three survived; lost was Lord Belhaven who was en route to take up the Governorship of
        <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">
         Barbados
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        A 44 gun frigate,
        <a href="/wiki/HMS_Anson_(1781)" title="HMS Anson (1781)">
         HMS
         <i>
          Anson
         </i>
        </a>
        , was wrecked at Loe Bar in 1807. Although it wrecked close to shore, many lost their lives in the storm. This inspired
        <a href="/wiki/Henry_Trengrouse" title="Henry Trengrouse">
         Henry Trengrouse
        </a>
        to invent the rocket fired line, later to become the
        <a href="/wiki/Breeches_buoy" title="Breeches buoy">
         Breeches buoy
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        The transport ship
        <i>
         Dispatch
        </i>
        ran aground on the Manacles in 1809 on its return from the
        <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">
         Peninsular War
        </a>
        , losing 104 men from the 7th Hussars. The following day, with local villagers still attempting a rescue, the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cruizer_class_brig-sloop" title="Cruizer class brig-sloop">
         Cruizer class brig-sloop
        </a>
        <a href="/wiki/HMS_Primrose_(1807)" title="HMS Primrose (1807)">
         HMS
         <i>
          Primrose
         </i>
        </a>
        hit the northern end of these rocks. The only survivor of its 126 officers, men and boys was a drummer boy.
       </li>
       <li>
        5 Sept 1856 the Cherubim and Ocean Home collided off Lizard Point
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/SS_Mohegan" title="SS Mohegan">
         SS
         <i>
          Mohegan
         </i>
        </a>
        , a 7,000 tonne passenger liner, also hit the Manacles in 1898 with the loss of 106 lives.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
         <a href="#cite_note-15">
          [15]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        The American passenger liner, the
        <i>
         Paris
        </i>
        , was stranded on the Manacles in 1899, with no loss of life.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The biggest rescue in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RNLI" title="RNLI">
        RNLI
       </a>
       's history was 17 March 1907 when the 12,000 tonne liner
       <a href="/wiki/SS_Suevic" title="SS Suevic">
        SS
        <i>
         Suevic
        </i>
       </a>
       hit the Maenheere Reef near
       <a href="/wiki/Lizard_Point,_Cornwall" title="Lizard Point, Cornwall">
        Lizard Point
       </a>
       in Cornwall. In a strong gale and dense fog RNLI lifeboat volunteers rescued 456 passengers, including 70 babies. Crews from the Lizard,
       <a href="/wiki/Cadgwith" title="Cadgwith">
        Cadgwith
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Coverack" title="Coverack">
        Coverack
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Porthleven" title="Porthleven">
        Porthleven
       </a>
       rowed out repeatedly for 16 hours to rescue all of the people on board. Six silver RNLI medals were later awarded, two to
       <i>
        Suevic
       </i>
       crew members.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_at_the_Lizard" title="Battle at the Lizard">
        Battle at the Lizard
       </a>
       , a naval battle, took place off The Lizard on 21 October 1707.
      </p>
      <p>
       Smuggling was a regular, and often necessary, way of life in these parts, despite the efforts of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coastguard" title="Coastguard">
        coastguards
       </a>
       or "Preventive men". In 1801, the king's
       <a href="/wiki/Pardon" title="Pardon">
        pardon
       </a>
       was offered to any smuggler giving information on the Mullion musket men involved in a gunfight with the crew of HM Gun Vessel
       <i>
        Hecate
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aviation">
        Aviation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <p>
       In the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">
        First World War
       </a>
       a Naval Air Station was established at Bonython, flying mainly
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blimps" title="Blimps">
        blimps
       </a>
       used for spotting
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U_boats" title="U boats">
        U boats
       </a>
       . One was sunk and several probably damaged by bombs dropped by the blimps. The airfield site is now occupied by the wind farm.
      </p>
      <p>
       RAF Predannack Down (see
       <a href="/wiki/Predannack_Airfield" title="Predannack Airfield">
        Predannack Airfield
       </a>
       ) was a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">
        Second World War
       </a>
       airbase, from which
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coastal_Command" title="Coastal Command">
        Coastal Command
       </a>
       squadrons flew anti-submarine sorties into the
       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Biscay" title="Bay of Biscay">
        Bay of Biscay
       </a>
       as well as convoy support in the western
       <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">
        English Channel
       </a>
       . The runways still exist and the site is used by a local Air Cadet Volunteer
       <a href="/wiki/Gliding" title="Gliding">
        gliding
       </a>
       Squadron 626VGS and as an emergency/relief base for
       <a href="/wiki/RNAS_Culdrose_(HMS_Seahawk)" title="RNAS Culdrose (HMS Seahawk)">
        RNAS
        <i>
         Culdrose
        </i>
        (HMS
        <i>
         Seahawk
        </i>
        )
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       RNAS Culdrose is Europe's largest
       <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">
        helicopter
       </a>
       base, and currently hosts the Training and Operational Conversion Unit operating the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EH101" title="EH101">
        EH101 "Merlin"
       </a>
       helicopter. It is also the home base for Merlin Squadrons embarked upon
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">
        Royal Navy
       </a>
       warships, the
       <a href="/wiki/Westland_Sea_King" title="Westland Sea King">
        Westland Sea King
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AEW" title="AEW">
        AEW
       </a>
       variant helicopter, a Search And Rescue (Sea King, again) helicopter flight, and some
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAe_Hawk" title="BAe Hawk">
        BAe Hawk
       </a>
       T.1 trainer jets used for training purposes by the Royal Navy. The base also operates some other types of fixed wing aircraft for calibration and other training purposes. As befits the base's name, a non-flying example of a
       <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Sea_Hawk" title="Hawker Sea Hawk">
        Hawker Sea Hawk
       </a>
       forms the main gate guardian static display. RNAS Culdrose is a major contributor to the economy of The Lizard area.
      </p>

       The Lizard's political history includes the 1497

       which began in
       <a href="/wiki/St_Keverne" title="St Keverne">

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Technology">
        Technology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Lizard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Technology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Titanium" title="Titanium">
        Titanium
       </a>
       was discovered here by the Reverend
       <a href="/wiki/William_Gregor" title="William Gregor">
        William Gregor
       </a>
       in 1791.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1869, John Pender formed the Falmouth Gibraltar and Malta Telegraph company, intending to connect
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        India
       </a>
       to England with an undersea cable. Although intended to land at Falmouth, the final landing point was
       <a href="/wiki/Porthcurno" title="Porthcurno">
        Porthcurno
       </a>
       near
       <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End" title="Land's End">
        Land's End
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1900
       <a href="/wiki/Guglielmo_Marconi" title="Guglielmo Marconi">
        Guglielmo Marconi
       </a>
       stayed the Housel Bay Hotel in his quest to locate a coastal radio station to receive signals from ships equipped with his apparatus. He leased a plot "in the wheat field adjoining the hotel" where the Lizard Wireless Telegraph Station still stands today. Recently restored by the National Trust, it looks as it did in January 1901, when Marconi received the distance record signals of 186 miles (299 km) from his transmitter station at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Niton,_Isle_of_Wight" title="Niton, Isle of Wight">
        Niton, Isle of Wight
       </a>
       . The Lizard Wireless Station is the oldest Marconi station to survive in its original state, and is located to the west of the Lloyds Signal Station in what appears to be a wooden hut. On 12 December 1901 Poldhu Point was the site of the first
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        trans Atlantic
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Wireless" title="Wireless">
        wireless signal radio communication
       </a>
       when Marconi sent a signal to
       <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        St John’s
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newfoundland" title="Newfoundland">
        Newfoundland
       </a>
       . The technology is one of the key advances to the development of
       <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">
        radio
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
        television
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satellites" title="Satellites">
        satellites
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">
        internet
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A radar station called
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">
        RAF
       </a>
       Drytree was built during
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        World War II
       </a>
       . The site was later chosen for the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telstar_1" title="Telstar 1">
        Telstar
       </a>
       project in 1962; its rocky foundations, clear atmosphere and extreme southerly location being uniquely suitable. This became the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goonhilly" title="Goonhilly">
        Goonhilly
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">
        satellite
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth_station_(communications)" title="Earth station (communications)">
        earth station
       </a>
       , now owned by
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.goonhilly.org" rel="nofollow">
        Goonhilly Earth Station Ltd
       </a>
       . Some important developments in television satellite transmission were made at Goonhilly station. A
       <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">
        wind farm
       </a>
       exists near to the Goonhilly station site.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">
        Geology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Lizard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Geology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geology_of_Lizard" title="Geology of Lizard">
        Geology of Lizard
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="800" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg/220px-LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg/330px-LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg/440px-LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LizardGeologyMapCornwall.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Sketchmap of The Lizard geology
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Known as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lizard_Complex" title="Lizard Complex">
        the Lizard complex
       </a>
       , the peninsula's geology is the best preserved example of an exposed
       <a href="/wiki/Ophiolite" title="Ophiolite">
        ophiolite
       </a>
       in the United Kingdom.
      </p>
      <p>
       An ophiolite is a suite of geological formations which represent a slice through a section of
       <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_crust" title="Oceanic crust">
        ocean crust
       </a>
       (including the upper level of the
       <a href="/wiki/Mantle_(geology)" title="Mantle (geology)">
        mantle
       </a>
       ) thrust onto the
       <a href="/wiki/Continental_crust" title="Continental crust">
        continental crust
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Lizard formations comprise three main units; the
       <a href="/wiki/Serpentinite" title="Serpentinite">
        serpentinites
       </a>
       , the "oceanic complex" and the
       <a href="/wiki/Metamorphic_rock" title="Metamorphic rock">
        metamorphic
       </a>
       basement.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">
        Ecology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Lizard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ecology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Several nature sites exist on the Lizard Peninsula; Predannack nature reserve, Mullion Island,
       <a href="/wiki/Goonhilly_Downs" title="Goonhilly Downs">
        Goonhilly Downs
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Seal_Sanctuary" title="Cornish Seal Sanctuary">
        Cornish Seal Sanctuary
       </a>
       at
       <a href="/wiki/Gweek" title="Gweek">
        Gweek
       </a>
       . An area of the Lizard covering 16.62 square kilometres (6.42 sq mi) is designated a
       <a href="/wiki/National_nature_reserve_(United_Kingdom)" title="National nature reserve (United Kingdom)">
        national nature reserve
       </a>
       because of its coastal grasslands and heaths and inland heaths.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The peninsula contains 3 main
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sites_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Sites of Special Scientific Interest">
        Sites of Special Scientific Interest
       </a>
       (SSSI), both noted for their endangered insects and plants, as well as their geology. The first is
       <b>
        East Lizard Heathlands SSSI
       </b>
       , the second is
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Caerthillian_to_Kennack" title="Caerthillian to Kennack">
         Caerthillian to Kennack
        </a>
        SSSI
       </i>
       and the third is
       <b>
        West Lizard SSSI
       </b>
       , of which the important wetland,
       <a href="/wiki/Hayle_Kimbro_Pool" title="Hayle Kimbro Pool">
        Hayle Kimbro Pool
       </a>
       , forms a part of.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The area is also home to one of England's rarest breeding birds — the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornish_chough" title="Cornish chough">
        Cornish chough
       </a>
       . This species of
       <a href="/wiki/Corvidae" title="Corvidae">
        corvid
       </a>
       is distinctive due to its red beak and legs and haunting "chee-aw" call. Chough began breeding on Lizard in 2002 following a concerted effort by the Cornish Chough Project in conjunction with
       <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" title="Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">
        DEFRA
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RSPB" title="RSPB">
        RSPB
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Lizard contains some of the most specialised flora of any area in Britain, including many
       <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">
        Red Data Book
       </a>
       plant species. Of particular note is the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornish_heath" title="Cornish heath">
        Cornish heath
       </a>
       ,
       <i>
        Erica vagans
       </i>
       , that occurs in abundance here, but which is found nowhere else in Britain. There are more than 600 species of flowering plants on the Lizard, nearly a quarter of all UK species.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The reason for this richness is partly because of the many different and unusual Lizard rocks on the Lizard Peninsula. But above all, it is a coming together of multiple factors: a very mild maritime climate, but one prone to gales and salt winds; waterlogged and boggy soils, but ones that often parch and dry out in the summer; soils of greatly contrasting fertility and pH; and lastly man's influence. Any single factor taken on its own would influence the flora; taken together, they combine, overlap and interact. Contrasting plant communities grow side-by-side in a mosaic that changes within a few metres but also changes markedly over time with the cycle of heath fires. It's not so much that conditions are ideal for growth, but that there is such a variety of different, difficult conditions. Each habitat, with its own combination of factors, attracts its own specialist plants.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is also one of the few places where the rare
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formicine" title="Formicine">
        formicine
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Ant" title="Ant">
        ant
       </a>
       ,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Formica_exsecta" title="Formica exsecta">
         Formica exsecta
        </a>
       </i>
       , (the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrow-headed_ant" title="Narrow-headed ant">
        narrow-headed ant
       </a>
       ), can be found.
      </p>
