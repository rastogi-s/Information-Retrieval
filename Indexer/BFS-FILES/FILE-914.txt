      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chita,_Zabaykalsky_Krai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Pyotr_Beketov" title="Pyotr Beketov">
        Pyotr Beketov
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Cossacks" title="Cossacks">
        Cossacks
       </a>
       founded Chita in 1653.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Founded_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Founded-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-gr-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chita_1885.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="381" data-file-width="532" height="158" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Chita_1885.png/220px-Chita_1885.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Chita_1885.png/330px-Chita_1885.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Chita_1885.png/440px-Chita_1885.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chita_1885.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Chita 1885
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       After 1825, several of the
       <a href="/wiki/Decembrist_revolt" title="Decembrist revolt">
        Decembrists
       </a>
       suffered
       <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">
        exile
       </a>
       to Chita.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-EB1911-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       According to
       <a href="/wiki/George_Kennan_(explorer)" title="George Kennan (explorer)">
        George Kennan
       </a>
       , "Among the exiles in Chita were some of the brightest, most cultivated, most sympathetic men and women that we had met in Eastern Siberia."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kennan_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Kennan-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       When
       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Maack" title="Richard Maack">
        Richard Maack
       </a>
       visited the city in 1855, he saw a wooden town, with one, also wooden, church. He estimated Chita's population at under 1,000, but predicted that the city would soon experience fast growth, due to the upcoming
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amur_Annexation" title="Amur Annexation">
        annexation
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Amur_River" title="Amur River">
        Amur
       </a>
       valley by Russia.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       By 1885, Chita's population had reached 5,728,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       and by 1897 it increased to 11,500.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_10-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-gr-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chita_Mosque.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="403" height="246" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Chita_Mosque.jpg/220px-Chita_Mosque.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Chita_Mosque.jpg/330px-Chita_Mosque.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c3/Chita_Mosque.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chita_Mosque.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Chita Mosque
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chita-1910.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="472" data-file-width="703" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chita-1910.jpg/220px-Chita-1910.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chita-1910.jpg/330px-Chita-1910.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chita-1910.jpg/440px-Chita-1910.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>

         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chita-1910.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Chita railway station in 1910
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Russia_Chita_railroad_station.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2592" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Russia_Chita_railroad_station.jpg/220px-Russia_Chita_railroad_station.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Russia_Chita_railroad_station.jpg/330px-Russia_Chita_railroad_station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Russia_Chita_railroad_station.jpg/440px-Russia_Chita_railroad_station.jpg 2x" width="220"/>

      <p>
       In 1945,
       <a href="/wiki/Puyi" title="Puyi">
        Puyi
       </a>
       , the last Emperor of China, and some of his associates were held prisoner in the city, in a former
       <a href="/wiki/Sanatorium" title="Sanatorium">
        sanatorium
       </a>
       for officers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">
        Administrative and municipal status
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chita,_Zabaykalsky_Krai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Chita is the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">
        administrative center
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Zabaykalsky_Krai" title="Zabaykalsky Krai">
        Zabaykalsky Krai
       </a>
       , and, within the
       <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">
        framework of administrative divisions
       </a>
       , it also serves as the administrative center of
       <a href="/wiki/Chitinsky_District" title="Chitinsky District">
        Chitinsky District
       </a>
       , to which it is also subordinated.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref468_1-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ref468-1">
         [1]

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="City_districts">
        City districts
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chita,_Zabaykalsky_Krai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: City districts">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The city is subdivided into four administrative districts:
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chernovsky_Administrative_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chernovsky Administrative District (page does not exist)">
        Chernovsky
       </a>
       (named after the Chernovskiye coal mines and colloquially known as "Chernovskiye"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ),
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ingodinsky_Administrative_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ingodinsky Administrative District (page does not exist)">
        Ingodinsky
       </a>
       (named after the
       <a href="/wiki/Ingoda_River" title="Ingoda River">
        Ingoda River
       </a>
       ),
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tsentralny_Administrative_District,_Chita&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tsentralny Administrative District, Chita (page does not exist)">
        Tsentralny
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zheleznodorozhny_Administrative_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zheleznodorozhny Administrative District (page does not exist)">
        Zheleznodorozhny
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Chernovsky Administrative District used to be a mining settlement, which was incorporated into Chita in 1941.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Chernovskiye mines themselves are a geological
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nature_monument" title="Nature monument">
        nature monument
       </a>
       of international status.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
