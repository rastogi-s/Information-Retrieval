      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Proceedings of the Royal Society
        </b>
       </i>
       is the parent title of two
       <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">
        scientific journals
       </a>
       published by the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">
        Royal Society
       </a>
       . Originally a single journal, it was split into two separate journals in 1905:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <b>
         Series A
        </b>
        : which publishes research related to
        <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">
         mathematical
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">
         physical
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">
         engineering sciences
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <b>
         Series B
        </b>
        : which publishes research related to
        <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">
         biology
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The two journals are the Royal Society's main research journals. Many celebrated names in science have published their research in the
       <i>
        Proceedings of the Royal Society
       </i>
       , including
       <a href="/wiki/Paul_Dirac" title="Paul Dirac">
        Paul Dirac
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Werner_Heisenberg" title="Werner Heisenberg">
        Werner Heisenberg
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Rutherford" title="Ernest Rutherford">
        Ernest Rutherford
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Erwin_Schr%C3%B6dinger" title="Erwin Schrödinger">
        Erwin Schrödinger
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Proceedings_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The journal started out in 1800 as the
       <i>
        Abstracts of the Papers Printed in the Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London
       </i>
       . The Royal Society published four volumes, from 1800 to 1843. Volumes 5 and 6, which appeared from 1843 to 1854, were called
       <i>
        Abstracts of the Papers Communicated to the Royal Society of London
       </i>
       . Starting with volume 7, in 1854, the Proceedings first appeared under the name
       <i>
        Proceedings of the Royal Society of London
       </i>
       . Publication of the proceedings in this form continued to volume 75 in 1905.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Starting with volume 76, the Proceedings were split into
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Proceedings of the Royal Society of London. Series A, Containing Papers of a Mathematical and Physical Character
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Proceedings of the Royal Society of London. Series B, Containing Papers of a Biological Character
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The Proceedings have since undergone further name changes. As of 2017
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Proceedings_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       , the two series are called
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Proceedings of the Royal Society A — Mathematical, Physical and Engineering Sciences
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Proceedings of the Royal Society B — Biological Sciences
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>

      <p>
       <i>
        Proceedings of the Royal Society B
       </i>
       publishes research related to biological sciences. As of 2017
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Proceedings_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the editor-in-chief is Professor
       <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Barrett_(ecologist)" title="Spencer Barrett (ecologist)">
        Spencer Barrett
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barrett2017_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Barrett2017-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Topics covered in particular include
       <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">
        ecology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Behavioral_ecology" title="Behavioral ecology">
        behavioural ecology
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Evolutionary_biology" title="Evolutionary biology">
        evolutionary biology
       </a>
       , as well as
       <a href="/wiki/Epidemiology" title="Epidemiology">
        epidemiology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Human_biology" title="Human biology">
        human biology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Neuroscience" title="Neuroscience">
        neuroscience
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Paleontology" title="Paleontology">
        palaeontology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">
        psychology
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Biomechanics" title="Biomechanics">
        biomechanics
       </a>
       . The journal publishes predominately research articles and reviews, as well as comments, replies, and commentaries. In 2005,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Biology_Letters" title="Biology Letters">
         Biology Letters
        </a>
       </i>
       (originally a supplement to
       <i>
        Proceedings B
       </i>
       ), was launched as an independent journal publishing short articles from across biology. According to
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Journal_Citation_Reports" title="Journal Citation Reports">
         Journal Citation Reports
        </a>
       </i>
       , As of 2015
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Proceedings_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the journal has an
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">
        impact factor
       </a>
       of 4.823.
