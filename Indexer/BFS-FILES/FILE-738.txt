    Total penumbral lunar eclipse
   </h1>

      <p>
       A
       <b>
        total penumbral eclipse
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_eclipse" title="Lunar eclipse">
        lunar eclipse
       </a>
       that occurs when the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        moon
       </a>
       becomes completely immersed in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penumbra" title="Penumbra">
        penumbral cone
       </a>
       of the Earth without touching the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umbra" title="Umbra">
        umbra
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       It is a narrow path for the moon to pass within the penumbra and outside the umbra. It can happen on the Earth's northern or southern penumbral edges. In addition, the size of the penumbra is sometimes too small where the moon enters it to contain the moon. The width of the Earth's penumbra is determined by the sun's
       <a href="/wiki/Angular_diameter" title="Angular diameter">
        angular diameter
       </a>
       at the time of the eclipse, and the moon's angular diameter is larger than the sun over part of its
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elliptical_orbit" title="Elliptical orbit">
        elliptical orbit
       </a>
       , depending on whether the eclipse occurs at its nearest (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perigee" title="Perigee">
        perigee
       </a>
       ) or farthest point (apogee) in its orbit around the earth. The majority of the time, the size of the moon and the size of the Earth's penumbra where the moon crosses it mean that most eclipses will not be total penumbral in nature.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Frequency">
        Frequency
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Total_penumbral_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Frequency">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Such eclipses constitute a relatively small fraction of lunar eclipses, and the distribution of these events is uneven, occurring between 0 and 9 times per century. The period of this variation is about 600 years and also correlates with the frequency of total umbral eclipses and tetrads. The maximum number in Fred's 5K canon
       <a class="external free" href="https://eclipse.gsfc.nasa.gov/LEcat5/LEcatalog.html" rel="nofollow">
        https://eclipse.gsfc.nasa.gov/LEcat5/LEcatalog.html
       </a>
       is 8, for saros 19 see
       <a class="external free" href="https://eclipse.gsfc.nasa.gov/LEsaros/LEsaros019.html" rel="nofollow">
        https://eclipse.gsfc.nasa.gov/LEsaros/LEsaros019.html
       </a>
       Saros 32 and 132 have 7. Saros 58, 95, and 114 have 6. 114 is the only saros in the canon to have a total of 7 total penumbral lunar eclipses that are NOT all in a row. And see also this table from
       <a class="external free" href="http://www.marco-peuschel.de/" rel="nofollow">
        http://www.marco-peuschel.de/
       </a>
       :
      </p>
      <p>
       Saros -339 8x Saros -266 13x Saros -258 9x Saros -247 10x Saros -237 9x Saros -218 12x Saros -157 7x Saros -118 7x Saros -116 7x Saros -105 11x Saros -39 9x Saros -26 8x Saros +19 8x Saros +32 7x Saros +58 6x Saros +114 6x Saros +132 7x
      </p>
      <p>
       And see also
       <a class="external free" href="http://www.jir1667.plus.com/shadow/consecutive-penumbral-total-lunar-eclipses.png" rel="nofollow">
        http://www.jir1667.plus.com/shadow/consecutive-penumbral-total-lunar-eclipses.png
       </a>
       Red dots means positieve gamma and blue dots means negative gamma, or this juist inverted. Source:
       <a class="external free" href="https://www.iau.org/administration/membership/individual/6886/" rel="nofollow">
        https://www.iau.org/administration/membership/individual/6886/
       </a>
      </p>
