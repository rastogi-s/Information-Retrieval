      <p>
       <b>
        Zhong Kang
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">
        Chinese
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">
        仲康
       </span>
       ) was the fourth king of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xia_Dynasty" title="Xia Dynasty">
        Xia Dynasty
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Family">
        Family
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zhong_Kang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Zhong Kang was a son of King
       <a href="/wiki/Qi_of_Xia" title="Qi of Xia">
        Qi of Xia
       </a>
       and thus the younger brother of
       <a href="/wiki/Tai_Kang" title="Tai Kang">
        Tai Kang
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reign">
        Reign
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zhong_Kang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reign">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       According to the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Bamboo_Annals" title="Bamboo Annals">
         Bamboo Annals
        </a>
       </i>
       , Zhong Kang took the throne in the year of
       <i>
        Jichou
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        己丑
       </i>
       ). His capital was at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhenxun" title="Zhenxun">
        Zhenxun
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       On the day of
       <i>
        Gengwu
       </i>
       , the 9th month in the 5th year of his reign, there was a
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipse
       </a>
       . Since the royal astronomers at that time had grown degenerate in licentiousness and drunkenness, they failed to predict this eclipse, resulting in confusion and panic among the common people. Zhong Kang sent the nobleman Yin to punish the Ministers of Astronomy for thus neglecting their duties. This is described more fully in the document known as the
       <i>
        Punitive Expedition of Yin
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In the 6th year of Zhong Kang's reign, he appointed the prince of
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kunwu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kunwu (page does not exist)">
        Kunwu
       </a>
       (
       <i>
        昆吾
       </i>
       ) as his
       <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">
        prime minister
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In his 7th year he died, and his son, named
       <a href="/wiki/Xiang_of_Xia" title="Xiang of Xia">
        Xiang
       </a>
       , declared a new capital at
       <a href="/wiki/Shangqiu" title="Shangqiu">
        Shangqiu
       </a>
       , supported by the prince of Pi.
      </p>
