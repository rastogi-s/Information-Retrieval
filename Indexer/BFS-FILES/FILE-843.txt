   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Betty Trask Award
   </h1>

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Betty Trask Prize and Awards
       </b>
       are for first novels written by authors under the age of 35, who reside in a current or former
       <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">
        Commonwealth
       </a>
       nation. Each year the awards total
       <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">
        £
       </a>
       20,000, with one author receiving a larger prize amount, called the "Prize", and the remainder given to one or more other writers, called the "Awards".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The award was established in 1984 by the
       <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Authors" title="Society of Authors">
        Society of Authors
       </a>
       , at the bequest of the late
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Trask&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Betty Trask (page does not exist)">
        Betty Trask
       </a>
       , a reclusive author of over thirty romance novels.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The awards are given to traditional or romantic novels, rather than those of an experimental style, and can be for published or unpublished works.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
