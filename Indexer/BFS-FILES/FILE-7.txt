      <p>
       An
       <b>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse#Types" title="Solar eclipse">
         annular solar eclipse
        </a>
       </b>
       took place on
       <b>
        May 20, 2012
       </b>
       (May 21, 2012 in local time in the
       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Hemisphere" title="Eastern Hemisphere">
        Eastern Hemisphere
       </a>
       ). One of the three main types of
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipses
       </a>
       , an annular solar eclipse occurs when the Moon's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_diameter" title="Apparent diameter">
        apparent diameter
       </a>
       is smaller than the Sun's, as compared to a
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse#Types" title="Solar eclipse">
        total solar eclipse
       </a>
       , when the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       completely blocks the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The eclipse was visible in large areas of the Eastern Hemisphere and the
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">
        Western Hemisphere
       </a>
       , including the land masses of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Asia" title="Eastern Asia">
        Eastern Asia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       . It was the first annular eclipse to pass over the
       <a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">
        contiguous United States
       </a>
       since the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_May_10,_1994" title="Solar eclipse of May 10, 1994">
        solar eclipse of May 10, 1994
       </a>
       , which occurred 18 years prior to this event.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Post_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Post-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was the first over Asia since the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_January_15,_2010" title="Solar eclipse of January 15, 2010">
        solar eclipse of January 15, 2010
       </a>
       .
      </p>

        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SE2012May20A.gif" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Animated path of the eclipse
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antumbra" title="Antumbra">
        antumbra
       </a>
       stretched 236 kilometres (147 mi) wide and travelled eastbound at an average rate of .62 miles (1.1 kilometers) per minute, remaining above the
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equator
       </a>
       throughout the event. The longest duration of annularity was 5 minutes and 43 seconds, occurring just south of the
       <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">
        Aleutian Islands
       </a>
       . The greatest extent (or width) of the eclipse occurred about 3 minutes after the greatest duration, with 89% of the sun being covered by the moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Annular_Solar_Eclipse_of_2012_May_20_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Annular_Solar_Eclipse_of_2012_May_20-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The eclipse is notable for beginning on a Monday and ending on the previous Sunday, as it crossed the
       <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">
        International Date Line
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Post_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Post-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">
        Asia
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_May_20,_2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Asia">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The eclipse began in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_China" title="Southern China">
        Southern China
       </a>
       at sunrise, shortly after 6:00 a.m. local time. Within an hour, the
       <a href="/wiki/Umbra,_penumbra_and_antumbra" title="Umbra, penumbra and antumbra">
        antumbra
       </a>
       of the eclipse had approached and passed over the cities of
       <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">
        Guangzhou
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Xiamen" title="Xiamen">
        Xiamen
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Quanzhou" title="Quanzhou">
        Quanzhou
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Fuzhou" title="Fuzhou">
        Fuzhou
       </a>
       , as well as
       <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">
        Hong Kong
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taipei,_Taiwan" title="Taipei, Taiwan">
        Taipei, Taiwan
       </a>
       . After crossing the
       <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">
        East China Sea
       </a>
       , the eclipse passed over much of eastern
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japan
       </a>
       , including the cities of
       <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">
        Nagoya
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">
        Tokyo
       </a>
       , before entering the Pacific Ocean. The
       <a href="/wiki/Umbra,_penumbra_and_antumbra" title="Umbra, penumbra and antumbra">
        penumbra
       </a>
       of the eclipse was visible throughout
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Asia" title="Eastern Asia">
        Eastern Asia
       </a>
       and various
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_in_the_Pacific_Ocean" title="List of islands in the Pacific Ocean">
        islands in the Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       , lasting until late morning.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Annular_Solar_Eclipse_of_2012_May_20_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Annular_Solar_Eclipse_of_2012_May_20-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Map_of_Eclipse_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Map_of_Eclipse-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The path of the antumbra over highly populated areas allowed at least an estimated 100 million people to view annularity.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_20th's_Annular_Eclipse_of_the_Sun_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-May_20th's_Annular_Eclipse_of_the_Sun-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Because it took place during the Summer
       <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">
        monsoon season
       </a>
       in Southeast Asia, viewing conditions were not ideal in some areas, including Hong Kong.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">
        North America
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_May_20,_2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: North America">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       After traveling approximately 4,000 miles (6,437 kilometers) across the Pacific Ocean, the antumbra entered North America between the coastlines of
       <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">
        Oregon
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">
        California
       </a>
       , at 5:28 p.m. local time. Within 45 minutes, it had passed over
       <a href="/wiki/Medford,_Oregon" title="Medford, Oregon">
        Medford, Oregon
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Redding,_California" title="Redding, California">
        Redding, California
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Reno,_Nevada" title="Reno, Nevada">
        Reno, Nevada
       </a>
       . It continued traveling southeast, passing 30 miles north of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">
        Las Vegas, Nevada
       </a>
       , and directly over
       <a href="/wiki/Cedar_City,_Utah" title="Cedar City, Utah">
        Cedar City, Utah
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque,_New_Mexico" title="Albuquerque, New Mexico">
        Albuquerque, New Mexico
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Lubbock,_Texas" title="Lubbock, Texas">
        Lubbock, Texas
       </a>
       before terminating above central Texas at sunset. The prenumbra was visible throughout most of
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       , including the islands of
       <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">
        Hawaii
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Annular_Solar_Eclipse_of_2012_May_20_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Annular_Solar_Eclipse_of_2012_May_20-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Map_of_Eclipse_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Map_of_Eclipse-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solar_Eclipse_Visible_From_California_to_Texas_Sunday_Afternoon_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solar_Eclipse_Visible_From_California_to_Texas_Sunday_Afternoon-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       An estimated 6 million people lived under the path of the antumbra. Numerous gatherings were held to view the eclipse, with some involving thousands of people.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solar_Eclipse_Visible_From_California_to_Texas_Sunday_Afternoon_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solar_Eclipse_Visible_From_California_to_Texas_Sunday_Afternoon-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id='cite_ref-Spectacular_"Ring_of_Fire"_Solar_Eclipse_Wows_Millions_8-0'>
        <a href='#cite_note-Spectacular_"Ring_of_Fire"_Solar_Eclipse_Wows_Millions-8'>
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The eclipse was broadcast on various media outlets for those who couldn't see the eclipse.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solar_Eclipse_2012:_How_To_See_The_May_20_Annular_Eclipse_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solar_Eclipse_2012:_How_To_See_The_May_20_Annular_Eclipse-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Thirty-three national parks were under the direct path of the eclipse.
       <sup class="reference" id='cite_ref-Solar_Eclipse_2012:_How_to_See_"Ring_of_Fire"_May_20_10-0'>
        <a href='#cite_note-Solar_Eclipse_2012:_How_to_See_"Ring_of_Fire"_May_20-10'>
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Saros_128">
        Saros 128
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_May_20,_2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Saros 128">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       This eclipse is a part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_Saros_128" title="Solar Saros 128">
        Solar Saros cycle 128
       </a>
       , which contains 73 events occurring every 18 years and 11 days. From May 16, 1417 through June 18, 1471 the series produced total solar eclipses, followed by hybrid eclipses from June 28, 1489 through July 31, 1543, and annular eclipses from August 11, 1561 through July 25, 2120.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
