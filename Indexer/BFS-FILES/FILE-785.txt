   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Lunar magma ocean
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       According to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giant_impact_hypothesis" title="Giant impact hypothesis">
        giant impact hypothesis
       </a>
       a large amount of energy was liberated in the formation of the Moon and it is inferred that as a result a large portion of the Moon was once completely molten, forming a
       <b>
        lunar magma ocean
       </b>
       . Evidence for the
       <a href="/wiki/Magma_ocean" title="Magma ocean">
        magma ocean
       </a>
       hypothesis comes from the highly
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anorthositic" title="Anorthositic">
        anorthositic
       </a>
       compositions of the
       <a href="/wiki/Crust_(geology)" title="Crust (geology)">
        crust
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_the_Moon" title="Geology of the Moon">
        lunar highlands
       </a>
       , as well as the existence of rocks with a high concentration of the geochemical component referred to as
       <a href="/wiki/KREEP" title="KREEP">
        KREEP
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Ages of formation and crystallization of the lunar magma ocean have been constrained by studies of isotopes of
       <a href="/wiki/Hafnium" title="Hafnium">
        hafnium
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tungsten" title="Tungsten">
        tungsten
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Samarium" title="Samarium">
        samarium
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Neodymium" title="Neodymium">
        neodymium
       </a>
       . The magma ocean formed about 70 million years after the history of the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
        Solar System
       </a>
       began, and most of the ocean had crystallized by about 215 million years after that beginning (Brandon, 2007).
      </p>
