      <p>
       <b>
        Vouziers
       </b>
       (
       <small>
        French pronunciation: ​
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">
         [vu.zje]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">
        commune
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Ardennes_(department)" title="Ardennes (department)">
        Ardennes
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">
        department
       </a>
       in northern
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Vouziers is the burial place of the pioneer
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">
        First World War
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Fighter_pilot" title="Fighter pilot">
        fighter pilot
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roland_Garros_(aviator)" title="Roland Garros (aviator)">
        Roland Garros
       </a>
       , after whom the
       <a href="/wiki/Stade_Roland_Garros" title="Stade Roland Garros">
        Stade Roland Garros
       </a>
       in Paris (the location of the
       <a href="/wiki/French_Open" title="French Open">
        French Open
       </a>
       tennis tournament) is named.
       <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1%C5%A1_Garrigue_Masaryk" title="Tomáš Garrigue Masaryk">
        Tomáš Garrigue Masaryk
       </a>
       (the first president of Czechoslovakia) fought at Vouziers with the
       <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_Legion_in_France" title="Czechoslovak Legion in France">
        Czechoslovak Legion in France
       </a>
       ; there is a monument to the legion, and the president's name given to the city
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lycee" title="Lycee">
        lycee
       </a>
       (high school).
      </p>
      <p>
       The town was on the path of totality for the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_August_11,_1999" title="Solar eclipse of August 11, 1999">
        Solar eclipse of August 11, 1999
       </a>
       . It hosted a major observation event. Because of its proximity to the Belgian border, it was gridlocked by visiting Belgian cars on the morning of the eclipse.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
        Geography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vouziers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The river
       <a href="/wiki/Aisne_(river)" title="Aisne (river)">
        Aisne
       </a>
       flows through Vouziers, doubled by a branch of the "
       <a href="/wiki/Canal_des_Ardennes" title="Canal des Ardennes">
        canal des Ardennes
       </a>
       ".
      </p>
      <p>
       The town lies between the
       <a href="/wiki/Forest_of_Argonne" title="Forest of Argonne">
        Forest of Argonne
       </a>
       , the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pre-Ardennes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pre-Ardennes (page does not exist)">
        pre-Ardennes
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Champagne_(province)" title="Champagne (province)">
        Champagne
       </a>
       , and near the
       <a href="/wiki/Ardennes" title="Ardennes">
        Ardennes Massif
       </a>
       (dense woodland). It is not far from
       <a href="/wiki/Charleville-M%C3%A9zi%C3%A8res" title="Charleville-Mézières">
        Charleville-Mézières
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sedan,_France" title="Sedan, France">
        Sedan
       </a>
       (the largest medieval castle in
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Rocroi" title="Rocroi">
        Rocroi
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Reims" title="Reims">
        Reims
       </a>
       (where the kings of France were crowned),
       <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2lons-en-Champagne" title="Châlons-en-Champagne">
        Châlons-en-Champagne
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Varennes-en-Argonne" title="Varennes-en-Argonne">
        Varennes-en-Argonne
       </a>
       (where Louis XVI was arrested during the French Revolution). On 1 June 2016, the former communes
       <a href="/wiki/Terron-sur-Aisne" title="Terron-sur-Aisne">
        Terron-sur-Aisne
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Vrizy" title="Vrizy">
        Vrizy
       </a>
       were merged into Vouziers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
