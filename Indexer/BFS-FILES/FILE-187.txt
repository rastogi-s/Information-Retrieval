      <p>
       <b>
        B&amp;H Photo Video
       </b>
       , founded in 1973 and located at 420
       <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Ninth Avenue (Manhattan)">
        Ninth Avenue
       </a>
       on the corner of
       <a href="/wiki/34th_Street_(Manhattan)" title="34th Street (Manhattan)">
        West 34th Street
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">
        Manhattan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
        New York City
       </a>
       , is the largest non-chain
       <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">
        photo
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Video" title="Video">
        video
       </a>
       equipment store in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-sales-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership_and_clientele">
        Ownership and clientele
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=B%26H_Photo_Video&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ownership and clientele">

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bhphotovideo.JPG">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1506" data-file-width="2489" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Bhphotovideo.JPG/300px-Bhphotovideo.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Bhphotovideo.JPG/450px-Bhphotovideo.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Bhphotovideo.JPG/600px-Bhphotovideo.JPG 2x" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bhphotovideo.JPG" title="Enlarge">
          </a>

         The exterior of the store on West 34th Street

      <p>
       B&amp;H Photo Video targets professional
       <a href="/wiki/Photographer" title="Photographer">
        photographers
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Videographer" title="Videographer">
        videographers
       </a>
       . Although it is visited by more than 5,000 customers every day from Sunday to Friday, most of the company's business comes from the Internet and corporate sales. It also runs a warehouse in the
       <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Navy_Yard" title="Brooklyn Navy Yard">
        Brooklyn Navy Yard
       </a>
       located in
       <a href="/wiki/Williamsburg,_Brooklyn" title="Williamsburg, Brooklyn">
        Williamsburg, Brooklyn
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-sales-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The store carries a wide range of electronics products, with emphasis on professional and specialty photographic equipment.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The business owner, Herman Schreiber, and many of the store's employees are observant
       <a href="/wiki/Satmar_(Hasidic_dynasty)" title="Satmar (Hasidic dynasty)">
        Satmar
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hasidic_Jews" title="Hasidic Jews">
        Hasidic Jews
       </a>
       . The store is closed on
       <a href="/wiki/Shabbat" title="Shabbat">
        Shabbat
       </a>
       , most
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish_holiday" title="Jewish holiday">
        Jewish holidays
       </a>
       (except for
       <a href="/wiki/Hanukkah" title="Hanukkah">
        Hanukkah
       </a>
       , when business dealings are permitted), and
       <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">
        Christmas
       </a>
       . Although the web site remains open,
       <a href="/wiki/E-commerce" title="E-commerce">
        e-commerce
       </a>
       orders are not taken or shipped between Friday evening and Saturday evening, or on Jewish holidays.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-B_H_homepage_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-B_H_homepage-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-B_H_schedule_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-B_H_schedule-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brick_and_mortar_store" title="Brick and mortar store">
        brick and mortar store
       </a>
       employs hundreds of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthodox_Jew" title="Orthodox Jew">
        Orthodox Jews
       </a>
       . An Orthodox Jewish bus company provides daily service to and from
       <a href="/wiki/Kiryas_Joel,_New_York" title="Kiryas Joel, New York">
        Kiryas Joel
       </a>
       , a Satmar village in
       <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_New_York" title="Orange County, New York">
        Orange County, New York
       </a>
       , as well as
       <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">
        Brooklyn
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queens,_New_York_City" title="Queens, New York City">
        Queens
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=B%26H_Photo_Video&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       B&amp;H opened in 1973 as a storefront film shop at 17 Warren Street in
       <a href="/wiki/Tribeca" title="Tribeca">
        Tribeca
       </a>
       , and obtained its name from the initials of owners Blimie Schreiber and her husband, Herman. Later in the 1970s, B&amp;H moved to a large loft at 119
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/17th_Street_(Manhattan)" title="17th Street (Manhattan)">
        West 17th Street
       </a>
       between
       <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Sixth Avenue (Manhattan)">
        Sixth
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Seventh_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Seventh Avenue (Manhattan)">
        Seventh
       </a>
       Avenues in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photo_District" title="Photo District">
        Photo District
       </a>
       and began to expand its stock to a wider range of film and photography products. In 1997, the store moved to its present location. It now has more than 2,000 employees.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sun_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-sun-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       B&amp;H is located just south of
       <a href="/wiki/Hell%27s_Kitchen,_Manhattan" title="Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan">
        Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan
       </a>
       , at 420
       <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Ninth Avenue (Manhattan)">
        Ninth Avenue
       </a>
       at the intersection with
       <a href="/wiki/34th_Street_(Manhattan)" title="34th Street (Manhattan)">
        West 34th Street
       </a>
       . In 2007, B&amp;H opened a second floor above its original sales floor making a total of 70,000 sq ft (6,500 m
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
       ) of sales space. The first floor sells professional lighting, binoculars, scopes, video, audio, darkroom, film and both home and portable entertainment; the second floor focuses on film and digital photography, computers, printers, tripods, scanners and related accessories.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-sales-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2007, B&amp;H began making sales through
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Checkout" title="Google Checkout">
        Google Checkout
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The store is noted for having an extensive conveyor belt system that runs along the ceiling.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The store sponsors reviews and events for many tech-savvy websites and tech enthusiast websites including AndroidGuys and Imaging Resource.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2015, their
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Play" title="Google Play">
        Google Play
       </a>
       app was named the "Best Mobile Shopping App" at the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mobile_Shop_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mobile Shop Conference (page does not exist)">
        Mobile Shop Conference
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2015, B&amp;H hired SiteSpect, a
       <a href="/wiki/Personalization" title="Personalization">
        personalization
       </a>
       platform, to help them with online shoppers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       B&amp;H often host noted photographers on their podcasts to discuss their books and photos.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They have a human operator (or employee) maintaining an active presence on Twitter, YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, and company blogs.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2015, B&amp;H added an
       <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">
        Apple
       </a>
       authorized shop to the computer department featuring two Apple iconic wooden tables with an assortment of Apple products from
       <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" title="Macintosh">
        Macs
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">
        iPads
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/wiki/Apple_Watch" title="Apple Watch">
        Apple Watch
       </a>
       . There is an Apple employee in shop to assist and consult.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In June 2015, B&amp;H Photo offered Apple Pay in its iOS app.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2016,
       <a href="/wiki/Vaio" title="Vaio">
        Vaio
       </a>
       announced that they signed a contract with B&amp;H photo video, to offer their products once again in New York.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In April 2016 B&amp;H Photo announced that they joined forces with CE Week and would be present on the tech show in that June.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In November 2016, B&amp;H Photo offered Apple Pay on mobile web, iOS, and in store.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">
        Controversies
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=B%26H_Photo_Video&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversies">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In October 2007, it was announced that B&amp;H Photo agreed to pay US$4.3 million to settle allegations that it discriminated against Hispanic workers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EEOC_22-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-EEOC-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In November 2009, a lawsuit against B&amp;H Photo alleged that the store
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gender_discrimination_in_the_United_States" title="Gender discrimination in the United States">
        refused to hire women
       </a>
       , in violation of New York City and New York State Human Rights Laws.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The lawsuit, brought by four women, sought class action status on behalf of all women discriminated against by B&amp;H over the course of many years.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Given B&amp;H's prior alleged discriminatory practices,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EEOC_22-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-EEOC-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the lawsuit sought US$19 million in compensatory and
       <a href="/wiki/Punitive_damages" title="Punitive damages">
        punitive damages
       </a>
       in order to deter future discriminatory practices.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2011, a lawsuit alleged
       <a href="/wiki/Racism_in_the_United_States" title="Racism in the United States">
        discrimination
       </a>
       against Hispanic workers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In February 2016 the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Labor" title="United States Department of Labor">
        United States Department of Labor
       </a>
       filed a lawsuit against B&amp;H alleging that the company had only hired Hispanic men into entry-level jobs in a Brooklyn warehouse and then subjecting them to harassment and unsanitary conditions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On August 16, 2017, the company announced that it had settled this second lawsuit for a reported $3.22 million.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
