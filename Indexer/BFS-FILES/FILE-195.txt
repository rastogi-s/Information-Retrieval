      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Collier's Encyclopedia
        </b>
       </i>
       (full title:
       <i>
        <b>
         Collier's Encyclopedia with Bibliography and Index
        </b>
       </i>
       ) was a United States-based general
       <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedia" title="Encyclopedia">
        encyclopedia
       </a>
       published by
       <a href="/wiki/Collier_Books" title="Collier Books">
        Crowell, Collier and Macmillan
       </a>
       . Self-described in its preface as "a scholarly, systematic, continuously revised summary of the knowledge that is most significant to mankind", it was long considered one of the three major contemporary English-language general encyclopedias, together with
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedia_Americana" title="Encyclopedia Americana">
         Encyclopedia Americana
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A6dia_Britannica" title="Encyclopædia Britannica">
         Encyclopædia Britannica
        </a>
       </i>
       : the three were sometimes collectively called "the ABCs".
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Collier%27s_Encyclopedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Fenelon_Collier" title="Peter Fenelon Collier">
        P.F. Collier &amp; Son Company
       </a>
       published
       <i>
        Collier's New Encyclopedia
       </i>
       from 1902–1929, initially in 16 volumes and later in 10 volumes.
      </p>
      <p>
       Collier's 11 volume
       <i>
        National Encyclopedia
       </i>
       (1932–1950) replaced
       <i>
        Collier's New Encyclopedia
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1949 the entirely new 20 volume
       <i>
        Collier's Encyclopedia
       </i>
       replaced the
       <i>
        National Encyclopedia
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       After
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Joseph_Collier" title="Robert Joseph Collier">
        Robert Collier's
       </a>
       death in 1918, P.F. Collier &amp; Son Company was bought by Crowell Publishing Company (later the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crowell_Collier_Publishing_Company" title="Crowell Collier Publishing Company">
        Crowell-Collier Publishing Company
       </a>
       ). In 1950
       <a href="/wiki/Collier_Books" title="Collier Books">
        Crowell, Collier and Macmillan
       </a>
       published the 20 volume
       <i>
        Collier's Encyclopedia
       </i>
       (full title
       <i>
        Collier's Encyclopedia with Bibliography and Index
       </i>
       ). It was expanded to 24 volumes in 1962. Until its print edition ceased in 1998,
       <i>
        Collier's Encyclopedia
       </i>
       was sold almost exclusively
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Door-to-door_salesman" title="Door-to-door salesman">
        door-to-door
       </a>
       , one of the last big-ticket
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2016)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       items of that nature in the United States.
      </p>
      <p>
       The 1997 edition has 23,000 entries with few short entries, as related subjects are usually consolidated into longer articles. A high percentage of the illustrations are in color, and more full-color illustrations had been added in recent years resulting in pictorial matter accounting for about two-fifths of the pages. Bibliographies are found in the last volume which also contains the 450,000 entry essential index. An annual
       <i>
        Collier's Year Book
       </i>
       was also published.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1998
       <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">
        Microsoft
       </a>
       bought the rights to Collier's electronic version and incorporated it into its
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Encarta" title="Encarta">
         Encarta
        </a>
       </i>
       electronic encyclopedia. Atlas Editions (formerly Collier Newfield) retained the rights to publish the encyclopedia in book form, though since then, Collier's has ceased to be in print.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="Kister.27s_comparison">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Kister's_comparison">
        Kister's comparison
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Collier%27s_Encyclopedia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kister's comparison">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A well-known comparison is that of
       <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Kister" title="Kenneth Kister">
        Kenneth Kister
       </a>
       , who gave a qualitative and quantitative comparison of
       <i>
        Collier's Encyclopedia
       </i>
       with the
       <i>
        Encyclopædia Britannica
       </i>
       and the
       <i>
        Encyclopedia Americana
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kister_1994_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-kister_1994-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       For the
       <i>
        quantitative
       </i>
       analysis, ten articles were selected at random (
       <a href="/wiki/Circumcision" title="Circumcision">
        circumcision
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Charles_R._Drew" title="Charles R. Drew">
        Charles Drew
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galileo" title="Galileo">
        Galileo
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Philip_Glass" title="Philip Glass">
        Philip Glass
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_disease" title="Heart disease">
        heart disease
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IQ" title="IQ">
        IQ
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panda_bear" title="Panda bear">
        panda bear
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sexual_harassment" title="Sexual harassment">
        sexual harassment
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Shroud_of_Turin" title="Shroud of Turin">
        Shroud of Turin
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">
        Uzbekistan
       </a>
       ) and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letter_grade" title="Letter grade">
        letter grades
       </a>
       (A–D, F) were awarded in four categories: coverage, accuracy, clarity, and recency. In all four categories and for all three encyclopaedias, the four average grades fell between
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grade_conversion" title="Grade conversion">
        B− and B+
       </a>
       , chiefly because not one encyclopaedia had an article on
       <a href="/wiki/Sexual_harassment" title="Sexual harassment">
        sexual harassment
       </a>
       in 1994. In the accuracy category,
       <i>
        Collier's
       </i>
       received one
       <i>
        D
       </i>
       and seven
       <i>
        A
       </i>
       s.
       <i>
        Encyclopedia Americana
       </i>
       received eight
       <i>
        A
       </i>
       s, and the
       <i>
        Britannica
       </i>
       received one
       <i>
        D
       </i>
       and eight
       <i>
        A
       </i>
       s; thus,
       <i>
        Collier's
       </i>
       received an average score of 92% for accuracy to
       <i>
        Americana
       </i>
       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">
        '
       </span>
       s 95% and
       <i>
        Britannica
       </i>
       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">
        '
       </span>
       s 92%. In the timeliness category,
       <i>
        Collier's
       </i>
       averaged an 85% to
       <i>
        Americana
       </i>
       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">
        '
       </span>
       s 90% and
       <i>
        Britannica
       </i>
       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">
        '
       </span>
       s 86%. After a more thorough
       <i>
        qualitative
       </i>
       comparison of all three encyclopedias, Kister recommended
       <i>
        Collier's Encyclopedia
       </i>
       primarily on the strength of its writing, presentation and navigation.
      </p>
