      <p>
       A total
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipse
       </a>
       occurred on July 22, 2009. It was the longest total solar eclipse during the 21st century. It lasted a maximum of 6 minutes and 39 seconds off the coast of Southeast Asia,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFP_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-AFP-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       causing tourist interest in
       <a href="/wiki/East_China" title="East China">
        eastern China
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">
        Pakistan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        India
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">
        Nepal
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">
        Bangladesh
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFP_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-AFP-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Xinhua14_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Xinhua14-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Visibility">
        Visibility
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_July_22,_2009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Visibility">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A partial eclipse was seen within the broad path of the Moon's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penumbra" title="Penumbra">
        penumbra
       </a>
       , including most of
       <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">
        Southeast Asia
       </a>
       (all of Pakistan, India and China) and north-eastern
       <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">
        Oceania
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The total eclipse was visible from a narrow corridor through northern
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        India
       </a>
       , eastern
       <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">
        Nepal
       </a>
       , northern
       <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">
        Bangladesh
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">
        Bhutan
       </a>
       , the northern tip of
       <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">
        Myanmar
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Central_China" title="Central China">
        central China
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       , including the northern part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_Islands" title="Ryukyu Islands">
        Ryukyu Islands
       </a>
       , the whole
       <a href="/wiki/Volcano_Islands" title="Volcano Islands">
        Volcano Islands
       </a>
       except
       <a href="/wiki/South_Iwo_Jima" title="South Iwo Jima">
        South Iwo Jima
       </a>
       ,

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Observations">
        Observations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:322px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:22Jul09_eclipse_Varanasi.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="240" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/22Jul09_eclipse_Varanasi.jpg/320px-22Jul09_eclipse_Varanasi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/22Jul09_eclipse_Varanasi.jpg/480px-22Jul09_eclipse_Varanasi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0d/22Jul09_eclipse_Varanasi.jpg 2x" width="320"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:22Jul09_eclipse_Varanasi.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Crowds gather on the ghats of
         <a href="/wiki/Ganges" title="Ganges">
          Ganges
         </a>
         for the eclipse in
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Varanasi,_India" title="Varanasi, India">
          Varanasi, India
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Thousands of pilgrims gathered on the banks of the
       <a href="/wiki/Ganges" title="Ganges">
        Ganges River
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Varanasi" title="Varanasi">
        Varanasi, India
       </a>
       to experience the eclipse as a religious or spiritual event. Some people expected that there would be a relationship, either positive or negative, between their health and the occurrence of the eclipse.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg/220px-Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg/330px-Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg/440px-Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Varanasi_during_Total_Solar_Eclipse_2009.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         View from a boat in
         <a href="/wiki/Ganges" title="Ganges">
          Ganges
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Indian scientists observed the solar eclipse from an
       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Air_Force" title="Indian Air Force">
        Indian Air Force
       </a>
       plane.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Government of the People's Republic of China">
        Chinese government
       </a>
       used the opportunity to provide
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_education" title="Scientific education">
        scientific education
       </a>
       and to dispel any superstition. A flight by
       <a href="/wiki/China_Eastern_Airlines" title="China Eastern Airlines">
        China Eastern Airlines
       </a>
       from Wuhan to Shanghai took a slight detour and followed the course of the eclipse to allow longer observation time for the scientists on board.
      </p>
      <p>
       Observers in Japan were excited by the prospect of experiencing the first eclipse in 46 years, but found the experience dampened by cloudy skies obscuring the view.
      </p>
      <p>
       In Bangladesh, where the eclipse lasted approximately 3 minutes and 44 seconds, thousands of people were able to witness the eclipse despite rain and overcast skies.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Duration">
        Duration
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_July_22,_2009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Duration">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Moonsize.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="474" data-file-width="773" height="172" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Moonsize.jpg/280px-Moonsize.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Moonsize.jpg/420px-Moonsize.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Moonsize.jpg/560px-Moonsize.jpg 2x" width="280"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Moonsize.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         These identically scaled photos compare the
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_diameter" title="Apparent diameter">
          apparent diameter
         </a>
         of the full moon (near apogee) to the nearly new moon (visible by
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earthshine" title="Earthshine">
          earthshine
         </a>
         ) on the day before the solar eclipse near lunar perigee.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       This solar eclipse was the longest total solar eclipse to occur in the 21st century, and will not be surpassed in duration until
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_June_13,_2132" title="Solar eclipse of June 13, 2132">
        13 June 2132
       </a>
       . Totality lasted for up to 6 minutes and 39 seconds, with the maximum eclipse occurring in the ocean at 02:35:21
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">
        UTC
       </a>
       about 100 km south of the
       <a href="/wiki/Bonin_Islands" title="Bonin Islands">
        Bonin Islands
       </a>
       , southeast of
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japan
       </a>
       . The uninhabited
       <a href="/wiki/North_Iwo_Jima" title="North Iwo Jima">
        North Iwo Jima
       </a>
       island was the landmass with totality time closest to maximum, while the closest inhabited point was
       <a href="/wiki/Akusekijima" title="Akusekijima">
        Akusekijima
       </a>
       , where the eclipse lasted 6 minutes and 26 seconds.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The cruise ship
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Costa_Classica" title="Costa Classica">
         Costa Classica
        </a>
       </i>
       was chartered specifically to view this eclipse and by viewing the eclipse at the point of maximum duration and cruising along the centerline during the event, duration was extended to 6 minutes, 42 seconds.
      </p>
      <p>
       The eclipse was part of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saros_cycle" title="Saros cycle">
        Saros series
       </a>
       136, as was the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_July_11,_1991" title="Solar eclipse of July 11, 1991">
        solar eclipse of July 11, 1991
       </a>
       , which was slightly longer, lasting up to 6 minutes 53 seconds (previous eclipses of the same saros series on June 30, 1973 and June 20, 1955, were longer, lasting 7 min 04 and 7 min 08, respectively). The next event from this series will be on August 2, 2027.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The exceptional duration was a result of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       being near
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perigee" title="Perigee">
        perigee
       </a>
       , with the apparent diameter of the Moon 8% larger than the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Magnitude_of_eclipse" title="Magnitude of eclipse">
        magnitude
       </a>
       1.080) and the Earth being near
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aphelion" title="Aphelion">
        aphelion
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       where the Sun appeared slightly smaller.
      </p>
      <p>
       In contrast the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annular_eclipse" title="Annular eclipse">
        annular
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_January_26,_2009" title="Solar eclipse of January 26, 2009">
        solar eclipse of January 26, 2009
       </a>
       occurred near lunar
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apogee" title="Apogee">
        apogee
       </a>
       and 7% smaller apparent diameter to the sun. And the next
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_January_15,_2010" title="Solar eclipse of January 15, 2010">
        solar eclipse of January 15, 2010
       </a>
       was also annular, with the Moon 8.1% smaller than the Sun.
      </p>

      <p>
       This total eclipse the second in the series of three eclipses in a one-month period, with two minor
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penumbral_lunar_eclipse" title="Penumbral lunar eclipse">
        penumbral lunar eclipses
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/July_2009_lunar_eclipse" title="July 2009 lunar eclipse">
        first on July 7
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/August_2009_lunar_eclipse" title="August 2009 lunar eclipse">
        last on August 6
       </a>
       .
      </p>

       <span id="Solar_eclipses_2008.E2.80.932011">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_eclipses_2008–2011">
        Solar eclipses 2008–2011
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">

       </a>
       of the Moon's orbit.

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Saros_series">
        Saros series
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_July_22,_2009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Saros series">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_Saros_136" title="Solar Saros 136">
        Solar Saros 136
       </a>
       , repeating every 18 years, 11 days, contains 71 events. The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saros_cycle" title="Saros cycle">
        series
       </a>
       started with partial solar eclipse on June 14, 1360, and reached a first annular eclipse on September 8, 1504. It was a hybrid event from November 22, 1612, through January 17, 1703, and total eclipses from January 27, 1721 through May 13, 2496. The series ends at member 71 as a partial eclipse on July 30, 2622, with the entire series lasting 1262 years. The longest eclipse occurred on June 20, 1955, with a maximum duration of totality at 7 minutes, 8 seconds.

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Metonic_cycle">
        Metonic cycle
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_July_22,_2009&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Metonic cycle">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Metonic_cycle" title="Metonic cycle">
        metonic series
       </a>
       repeats eclipses every 19 years (6939.69 days), lasting about 5 cycles. Eclipses occur in nearly the same calendar date. In addition, the octon subseries repeats 1/5 of that or every 3.8 years (1387.94 days).
