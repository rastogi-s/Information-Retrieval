   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Nodal period
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">

    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        nodal period
       </b>
       of a
       <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">
        satellite
       </a>
       is the time interval between successive passages of a satellite through successive
       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_node" title="Orbital node">
        orbital nodes
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This applies to artificial satellites like weather satellites, and
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">
        natural satellites
       </a>
       like the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . The nodal period of the Moon is 27.2122 days.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
