      <p>
       <b>
        Perranporth
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">
        Cornish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="kw" xml:lang="kw">
         Porthperan
        </span>
       </i>
       )
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is a medium-sized
       <a href="/wiki/Seaside_resort" title="Seaside resort">
        seaside resort
       </a>
       town on the north coast of
       <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">
        Cornwall
       </a>
       , England, United Kingdom. It is situated 1 mile east of the St Agnes Heritage Coastline, and is around 8 miles south-west of Newquay. Perranporth and its 3 miles (5 km) long beach face the Atlantic Ocean.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OS2_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-OS2-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It has a population of 3,066,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and is the largest settlement in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">
        civil parish
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Perranzabuloe" title="Perranzabuloe">
        Perranzabuloe
       </a>
       . It has an electoral ward in its own name, to which the population was 4,270 in the 2011 census.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The town's modern name comes from
       <i>
        Porth Peran
       </i>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">
        Cornish
       </a>
       for the cove of
       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Piran" title="Saint Piran">
        Saint Piran
       </a>
       , the patron saint of Cornwall. He founded
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Piran%27s_Oratory" title="St Piran's Oratory">
        St Piran's Oratory
       </a>
       on Penhale Sands near Perranporth in the 7th century. Buried under sand for many centuries, it was unearthed in the 19th century.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perranporth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <i>
        Voorspoed
       </i>
       ran ashore in a northerly gale in Perran Bay on 7 March 1901, while travelling from Cardiff to Bahia. The wreck was one of the last to be looted.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Perranporth_Airfield" title="Perranporth Airfield">
        Perranporth Airfield
       </a>
       , built during World War II as an RAF fighter station, is now a civil airfield. It is located at Cligga Head, on the
       <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">
        plateau
       </a>
       above the cliffs.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OS2_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-OS2-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Until the 1960s, Perranporth was served by a railway line. Built as the
       <a href="/wiki/Truro_and_Newquay_Railway" title="Truro and Newquay Railway">
        Truro and Newquay Railway
       </a>
       , the line ran from
       <a href="/wiki/Chacewater" title="Chacewater">
        Chacewater
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">
        Newquay
       </a>
       and the principal intermediate stop was
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perranporth_railway_station" title="Perranporth railway station">
        Perranporth station
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Perranporth also had a second station, known as Perranporth Beach Halt.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
        Geography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perranporth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Perranporth is centred on a main street, St Piran's Road, part of the B3285 Newquay to
       <a href="/wiki/St_Agnes,_Cornwall" title="St Agnes, Cornwall">
        St Agnes
       </a>
       road. The town centre has various shops, cafés and pubs. The long-distance
       <a href="/wiki/South_West_Coast_Path" title="South West Coast Path">
        South West Coast Path
       </a>
       runs past the town. There is a long-distance coach service provided by
       <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">
        National Express
       </a>
       (service 316) which runs between London and Perranporth.
      </p>
      <p>
       Perranporth is a popular family holiday destination. A wide sandy beach, Perran Beach, extends northeast of the town for about 3 km (nearly 2 miles) to
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ligger_Point&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ligger Point (page does not exist)">
        Ligger Point
       </a>
       . The beach faces west onto Perran Bay and the Atlantic Ocean and is a popular
       <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">
        surfing
       </a>
       location.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OS2_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-OS2-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There are lifeguard beach patrols from May to September and the beach is generally safe for bathing, although there are dangerous
       <a href="/wiki/Rip_current" title="Rip current">
        rip currents
       </a>
       around Chapel Rock at
       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Ebb_tide" title="wikt:Ebb tide">
        ebb tides
       </a>
       . Gear Sands is a sprawling holiday camp and caravan site to the north of the town centre.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       At the south end of the beach are cliffs with
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_arch" title="Natural arch">
        natural arches
       </a>
       , natural
       <a href="/wiki/Stack_(geology)" title="Stack (geology)">
        stacks
       </a>
       and tin-mining
       <a href="/wiki/Adit" title="Adit">
        adits
       </a>
       . There is a
       <a href="/wiki/Youth_Hostels_Association_(England_%26_Wales)" title="Youth Hostels Association (England &amp; Wales)">
        youth hostel
       </a>
       above the cliffs at
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Droskyn_Point&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Droskyn Point (page does not exist)">
        Droskyn Point
       </a>
       . Nearby is the 19th century Droskyn Castle, formerly a hotel and now divided into apartments.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Protected_areas">
        Protected areas
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perranporth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Protected areas">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Perran beach is backed by extensive
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sand_dunes" title="Sand dunes">
        sand dunes
       </a>
       which reach nearly a mile inland. Known as
       <a href="/wiki/Penhale_Sands" title="Penhale Sands">
        Penhale Sands
       </a>
       , the dunes are used for
       <a href="/wiki/Orienteering" title="Orienteering">
        orienteering
       </a>
       competitions, and there is an 18-hole links golf course.
      </p>
      <p>
       The far northern end of the beach is used as a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naturist" title="Naturist">
        naturist
       </a>
       beach,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       although the MoD discouraged naturism in the sand dunes that boarded their property.
      </p>
      <p>
       The dunes are also a valuable resource for wildlife, with many rare plants and insects including Cornwall's largest colony of the
       <a href="/wiki/Silver-studded_blue" title="Silver-studded blue">
        silver-studded blue
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">
        Red Data Book
       </a>
       species.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Southwest of Perranporth, the coast becomes more rocky, with cliffs rising to about 300 feet (90 metres) at Cligga Head. These cliffs form the Cligga Head SSSI (
       <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">
        Site of Special Scientific Interest
       </a>
       ), noted for its
       <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_Cornwall" title="Geology of Cornwall">
        geological
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_of_Cornwall" title="Flora and fauna of Cornwall">
        biological
       </a>
       characteristics.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_events">
        Annual events
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perranporth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Annual events">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The "Perranporth Shout" Sea-song and Shanty Festival is held over the third weekend in April, with performers from as far away as Scotland and Norway. It has expanded over the last nine years to be a three-day, five venue occasion. In 2016 the name was changed to 'The Loudest Shout', in recognition of the new event on the Friday night when up to 60 singers take part in a mass singing session.
       </li>
       <li>
        Perranporth used to host an inter-
        <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">
         Celtic
        </a>
        festival each October, Lowender Peran, drawing people in from Cornwall and the other five
        <a href="/wiki/Celtic_nations" title="Celtic nations">
         Celtic nations
        </a>
        . The festival moved to nearby Newquay when the hotel that hosted it closed in 2015.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span id="Places_of_worship.2C_associations_and_clubs">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_worship,_associations_and_clubs">
        Places of worship, associations and clubs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perranporth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Places of worship, associations and clubs">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The parish church, which is in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Catholic" title="Anglo-Catholic">
        Anglo-Catholic
       </a>
       tradition of the
       <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">
        Church of England
       </a>
       , is in
       <a href="/wiki/Perranzabuloe" title="Perranzabuloe">
        Perranzabuloe
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       An Anglican
       <a href="/wiki/Chapel_of_ease" title="Chapel of ease">
        chapel of ease
       </a>
       in Perranporth dedicated to St Michael opened in 1872 and seats 100 people.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The town also has its own
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">
        Roman Catholic
       </a>
       church, dedicated to Christ the King, on Wheal Leisure Road, which is part of the Diocese of Plymouth
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Masonic_and_fraternal_associations">
        Masonic and fraternal associations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perranporth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Masonic and fraternal associations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The town's
       <a href="/wiki/Freemasonry" title="Freemasonry">
        Masonic
       </a>
       centre in Liskey Hill is home to 16 Masonic bodies, which makes it one of the foremost centres of Masonic activity in Cornwall.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       There used to be a Lodge of the
       <a href="/wiki/Independent_Order_of_Odd_Fellows" title="Independent Order of Odd Fellows">
        Independent Order of Odd Fellows
       </a>
       (Manchester Unity), who at one time, owned the Odd Fellows Hall near the Ponsmere Hotel, but due to declining numbers this Lodge closed and the building is now rented by Cornwall Council as the town's library. The Oddfellows building is owned by Perranzabuloe Museum,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       which has a display gallery and research area above the library.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">
        Sport
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Perranporth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sport">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">
        Surfing
       </a>
       is popular in Perranporth with its long sands and beach break. The Perranporth Surf Life Saving Club is one of the oldest in the country and hosts an extreme triathlon event every autumn.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="one of the oldest (August 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The beach is a destination for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kite_surfing" title="Kite surfing">
        kite surfing
       </a>
       enthusiasts. There is a golf club, Perranporth Golf Club, just north of the town, while the football team Perranporth A.F.C. play in Division One West of the
       <a href="/wiki/South_West_Peninsula_League" title="South West Peninsula League">
        South West Peninsula League
       </a>
       . There is a rugby club, "The Brewers" , and a tennis club.
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">
        Notable people
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
