      <p>
       <b>
        Maurice Félix Charles Allais
       </b>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (31 May 1911 – 9 October 2010) was a French physicist and economist, the 1988 winner of the
       <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Memorial_Prize_in_Economic_Sciences" title="Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences">
        Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences
       </a>
       "for his pioneering contributions to the theory of markets and efficient utilization of resources". These pairs
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2017)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       considered that Maurice Allais could have been recognized, along with
       <a href="/wiki/John_Hicks" title="John Hicks">
        John Hicks
       </a>
       (Value and Capital, 1939) and
       <a href="/wiki/Paul_Samuelson" title="Paul Samuelson">
        Paul Samuelson
       </a>
       (The Foundations of Economic Analysis, 1947), as the principal architect of the neoclassical synthesis. They formalize the self-regulation of markets, that
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keynes" title="Keynes">
        Keynes
       </a>
       refuted, while reiterating some of his ideas.
      </p>
      <p>
       Born in
       <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">
        Paris
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       , Allais attended the
       <a href="/wiki/Lyc%C3%A9e_Lakanal" title="Lycée Lakanal">
        Lycée Lakanal
       </a>
       , graduated from the
       <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Polytechnique" title="École Polytechnique">
        École Polytechnique
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">
        Paris
       </a>
       and studied at the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_nationale_sup%C3%A9rieure_des_mines_de_Paris" title="École nationale supérieure des mines de Paris">
        École nationale supérieure des mines de Paris
       </a>
       . His academic and other posts have included being Professor of Economics at the École Nationale Supérieure des Mines de Paris (since 1944) and Director of its Economic Analysis Centre (since 1946). In 1949, he received the title of doctor-engineer from the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paris" title="University of Paris">
        University of Paris
       </a>
       , Faculty of Science. He also held teaching positions at various institutions, including at the University of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_X" title="Paris X">
        Paris X
       </a>
       -Nanterre. His first works oriented him towards the sciences of the concrete and the experiments of fundamental physics, on which he will also publish numerous works, notably on pendular oscillations and the laws of gravitation. It's after a trip in 1933 to the United States during the Great Depression, that he decides to make the economy.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Allais died at his home in
       <a href="/wiki/Saint-Cloud" title="Saint-Cloud">
        Saint-Cloud
       </a>
       , near Paris, at the age of 99.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bno20101010_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-bno20101010-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He considered, as his principal inspirers,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leon_Walras" title="Leon Walras">
        Leon Walras
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wilfredo_Pareto" title="Wilfredo Pareto">
        Wilfredo Pareto
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Irving_Fisher" title="Irving Fisher">
        Irving Fisher
       </a>
       . He was reluctant to write in or translate his work into English, and many of his major contributions became known to the dominant community only when they were independently rediscovered or popularized by English-speaking economists. At the same time, he claimed Keynes's liberalism and declared himself in favor of an important public sector. Allais attended the inaugural meeting of the
       <a href="/wiki/Mont_Pelerin_Society" title="Mont Pelerin Society">
        Mont Pelerin Society
       </a>
       , but he was alone among the attendees to refuse to sign the statement of aims because of a disagreement over the extent of property rights. He exerted an important influence, at the end of the war, on French economists such as
       <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Debreu" title="Gérard Debreu">
        Gérard Debreu
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Edmond_Malinvaud" title="Edmond Malinvaud">
        Edmond Malinvaud
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marcel_Boiteux&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marcel Boiteux (page does not exist)">
        Marcel Boiteux
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Paul_Samuelson" title="Paul Samuelson">
        Paul Samuelson
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       said "
       <i>
        Had Allais earliest writings been in English, a generation of economic theory would have taken a different course"
       </i>
       and the Nobel Prize should have been awarded to him much earlier. Assar Lindbeck, the chairman of the selection committee considered Allais as "a giant within the world of economic analysis".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Contribution_as_an_economist">
        Contribution as an economist
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurice_Allais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Contribution as an economist">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Author of several theoretical and applied economics studies,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       his work has focused on the development of mathematical economics, especially in the fields of general equilibrium theory, capital theory,
       <a href="/wiki/Decision_theory" title="Decision theory">
        decision theory
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Monetary_policy" title="Monetary policy">
        monetary policy
       </a>
       . A pioneer in macroeconomic monetary analyses, the economist has been authoritative for his theoretical studies of risk, illustrated by his famous paradox:
       <i>
        "the less the risk is, the more speculators flee."
       </i>
       He has also been a pioneer in various fields such as the role of central banks and the pricing of public services.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Inspired_at_first_by_Walras">
        Inspired at first by Walras
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurice_Allais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Inspired at first by Walras">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       His first book develops the microeconomic aspect. With
       <i>
        Traité d'économie pure
       </i>
       , which he wrote between January 1941 and July 1943, these pairs considered that Maurice Allais could have been recognized, along with Hicks and Samuelson, as the principal architect of the
       <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_synthesis" title="Neoclassical synthesis">
        neoclassical synthesis
       </a>
       . He anticipates several of the propositions and theorems put forward by Hicks, Samuelson and others, sometimes giving them a more general and rigorous formulation. In particular, he demonstrates the equivalence theorems that
       <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Arrow" title="Kenneth Arrow">
        Kenneth Arrow
       </a>
       and Gerard Debreu will find in 1954: "Every equilibrium situation in a market economy is a situation of maximum efficiency, and reciprocally, every situation of maximum efficiency Is a equilibrium situation in a market economy. " The market thus ensures economic efficiency and optimal distribution of income in the nation. At the same time, Samuelson exposed the process of trial and error which leads to the equilibrium of markets.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1947, in the second part of his work
       <i>
        Économie et Intérêt
       </i>
       , Allais introduced time and currency and thus tackled the dynamics and growth of capitalist economies. Again, he made several proposals, Whose discovery will be later attributed to other economists. He introduced the first
       <a href="/wiki/Overlapping_generations_model" title="Overlapping generations model">
        overlapping generations model
       </a>
       (OLG model) (later popularized and attributed to
       <a href="/wiki/Paul_Samuelson" title="Paul Samuelson">
        Paul Samuelson
       </a>
       in 1958), introduced the
       <a href="/wiki/Golden_Rule_savings_rate" title="Golden Rule savings rate">
        golden rule of optimal growth
       </a>
       before
       <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Swan" title="Trevor Swan">
        Trevor Swan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Phelps" title="Edmund Phelps">
        Edmund Phelps
       </a>
       , show that an interest rate equal to the growth rate maximizes consumption, he described the transaction demand for money rule before
       <a href="/wiki/William_Baumol" title="William Baumol">
        William Baumol
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/James_Tobin" title="James Tobin">
        James Tobin
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He was also responsible for early work in
       <a href="/wiki/Behavioral_economics" title="Behavioral economics">
        Behavioral economics
       </a>
       , which in the US is generally attributed to
       <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Kahneman" title="Daniel Kahneman">
        Daniel Kahneman
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Amos_Tversky" title="Amos Tversky">
        Amos Tversky
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the 1940s, Allais worked on "decision theory" (or "theory of choice") under uncertainty and developed a theory of
       <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_utility" title="Cardinal utility">
        cardinal utility
       </a>
       . Due to war conditions and his commitment to publish in French, his work was undertaken independently of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theory_of_games_and_economic_behavior" title="Theory of games and economic behavior">
        Theory of games and economic behavior
       </a>
       developed by
       <a href="/wiki/John_von_Neumann" title="John von Neumann">
        John von Neumann
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Oskar_Morgenstern" title="Oskar Morgenstern">
        Oskar Morgenstern
       </a>
       . He formulated the
       <a href="/wiki/Allais_paradox" title="Allais paradox">
        Allais paradox
       </a>
       in 1953, which questions the traditional model of rationality of choices and contradicts the
       <a href="/wiki/Expected_utility_hypothesis" title="Expected utility hypothesis">
        expected utility hypothesis
       </a>
       . It shows that when confronted with a lottery, an individual does not maximize his hoped-for gains, but rather aims at security.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Rejection_of_the_general_equilibrium_theory">
        Rejection of the general equilibrium theory
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurice_Allais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rejection of the general equilibrium theory">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Finally he criticizes the drifts of a discipline that privileges according to him, mathematical virtuosity at the expense of realism. With this "new scholastic totalitarianism" he moved away, in the 60s, from the analysis of the general equilibrium developed by Walras and replace it with a study focusing on real markets rather than a utopian market, favoring the study of imbalance and based on the idea of surplus. The economic dynamics are thus characterized by the research, the realization and the distribution of a surplus and there is a general equilibrium when there is no longer any realizable surplus.
      </p>
      <p>
       Allais’s
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hereditary" title="Hereditary">
        Hereditary
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Relativist" title="Relativist">
        Relativist
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Logistic_function" title="Logistic function">
        Logistic
       </a>
       (HRL) theory of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monetary" title="Monetary">
        monetary
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Monetary_economics" title="Monetary economics">
        dynamics
       </a>
       contains an original theory of expectations formation that is a genuine alternative to both
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adaptive" title="Adaptive">
        adaptive
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rational" title="Rational">
        rational
       </a>
       expectations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was praised by
       <a href="/wiki/Milton_Friedman" title="Milton Friedman">
        Milton Friedman
       </a>
       in 1968 with the following words: "
       <i>
        This work
       </i>
       [the HRL formulation]
       <i>
        introduces a very basic and important distinction between
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychological" title="Psychological">
         psychological
        </a>
        time and chronological
        <a href="/wiki/Time" title="Time">
         time
        </a>
        . It is one of the most important and original paper that has been written for a long time … for its consideration of the problem of the formation of expectations".
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
         <a href="#cite_note-11">
          [11]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </i>
       Allais's contribution has nevertheless been "lost": it has been absent from the debate about expectations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Position_against_globalization">
        Position against globalization
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurice_Allais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Position against globalization">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       On the first page, he dedicates his book
       <i>
        La mondialisation: destruction des emplois et de la croissance
       </i>
       (1990),
       <i>
        Globalization: destruction of jobs and growth
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        "To the countless victims worldwide of the free-trade ideology, ideology as fatal as it is erroneous, and to all those who are not blind to some partisan passion"
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Allais believes that Ricardo's theory is valid only in a steady state, but disappears when the specializations evolve and when the capital is mobile.
      </p>
      <p>
       According to him
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       , "Globalization can only bring everywhere instability, unemployment, injustices [...] and "widespread globalization is neither inevitable nor necessary nor desirable". He considers that
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       "unemployment arise from the offshoring, themselves due to the excessive differences in wages"; "reasoned protectionism between countries with very different incomes, is not only justified but absolutely necessary"; and the absence of protection will destroy all activities of each country with higher incomes.
      </p>
      <p>
       In his opinion, crisis and globalization are linked:
       <i>
        "The financial and banking crisis which, is only the spectacular symptom of a deeper economic crisis: the deregulation
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
         <a href="#cite_note-16">
          [16]
         </a>
        </sup>
        of competition in the global labor market
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
         <a href="#cite_note-17">
          [17]
         </a>
        </sup>
        "
       </i>
       . "Current unemployment is due to this total liberalization of trade[...] As such, it constitutes a major foolishness, starting from an unbelievable contradiction. Just as attributing the crisis of 1929 to protectionist causes is a historical contradiction. The true origin was already in the careless development of credit in the years preceding it."
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1992, Maurice Allais criticised the
       <a href="/wiki/Maastricht_Treaty" title="Maastricht Treaty">
        Maastricht Treaty
       </a>
       for its excessive emphasis on
       <a href="/wiki/Free_trade" title="Free trade">
        free trade
       </a>
       . He also expressed reservations on the
       <a href="/wiki/Economic_and_Monetary_Union_of_the_European_Union" title="Economic and Monetary Union of the European Union">
        single European currency
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2005, he expressed similar reservations concerning the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_constitution" title="European constitution">
        European constitution
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Contribution_in_physics">
        Contribution in physics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurice_Allais&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Contribution in physics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Allais_effect" title="Allais effect">
        Allais effect
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Besides his career in economics, he performed experiments between 1952 and 1960 in the fields of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gravitation" title="Gravitation">
        gravitation
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Special_relativity" title="Special relativity">
        special relativity
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetism" title="Electromagnetism">
        electromagnetism
       </a>
       , to investigate possible links between the fields. He reported three effects:
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        An unexpected anomalous effect in the angular velocity of the plane of oscillation of a
        <a href="/wiki/Paraconical_pendulum" title="Paraconical pendulum">
         paraconical pendulum
        </a>
        , detected during two partial
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_eclipses" title="Solar eclipses">
         solar eclipses
        </a>
        in 1954 and 1959. The claimed effect is now called the
        <a href="/wiki/Allais_effect" title="Allais effect">
         Allais effect
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Anomalous irregularities in the oscillation of the
        <a href="/wiki/Paraconical_pendulum" title="Paraconical pendulum">
         paraconical pendulum
        </a>
        with respect to a
        <a href="/wiki/Sidereal_time" title="Sidereal time">
         sidereal diurnal periodicity
        </a>
        of 23 hours 56 minutes and
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal" title="Tidal">
         tidal
        </a>
        periodicity of 24 hours 50 minutes.
       </li>
       <li>
        Anomalous irregularities in optical
        <a href="/wiki/Theodolite" title="Theodolite">
         theodolite
        </a>
        measurements, with the same
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunisolar" title="Lunisolar">
         lunisolar
        </a>
        periodicities.
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       Over the years, a number of pendulum experiments were performed by scientists around the world to test his findings. However, the results were mixed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1999_20-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NASA_1999-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1999_B_21-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NASA_1999_B-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
