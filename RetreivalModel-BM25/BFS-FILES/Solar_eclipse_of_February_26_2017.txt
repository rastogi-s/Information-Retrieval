      <p>
       An annular
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipse
       </a>
       took place on February 26, 2017. A
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipse
       </a>
       occurs when the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       passes between
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       , thereby totally or partly obscuring the image of the Sun for a viewer on Earth. An annular solar eclipse occurs when the Moon's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_diameter" title="Apparent diameter">
        apparent diameter
       </a>
       is smaller than the Sun's, blocking most of the Sun's light and causing the Sun to look like an
       <a href="/wiki/Annulus_(mathematics)" title="Annulus (mathematics)">
        annulus
       </a>
       (ring). An annular eclipse appears as a partial eclipse over a region of the Earth thousands of kilometres wide.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was visible across southern
       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">
        South America
       </a>
       in the morning and ended in south-western
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       at sunset. In
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       , the best places to see the eclipse were located in the south of the
       <a href="/wiki/Chubut_Province" title="Chubut Province">
        Chubut Province
       </a>
       , in the towns of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Facundo_(Chubut)" title="Facundo (Chubut)">
        Facundo
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sarmiento,_Chubut" title="Sarmiento, Chubut">
        Sarmiento
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Camarones,_Chubut" title="Camarones, Chubut">
        Camarones
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Metonic_cycle">
        Metonic cycle
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_February_26,_2017&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Metonic cycle">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Metonic_cycle" title="Metonic cycle">
        metonic series
       </a>
       repeats eclipses every 19 years (6939.69 days), lasting about 5 cycles. Eclipses occur in nearly the same calendar date. In addition, the octon subseries repeats 1/5 of that or every 3.8 years (1387.94 days).
