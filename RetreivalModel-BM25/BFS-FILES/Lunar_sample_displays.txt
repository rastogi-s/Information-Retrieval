   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Lunar sample displays
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar sample displays
       </b>
       are two
       <a href="/wiki/Commemorative_plaque" title="Commemorative plaque">
        commemorative plaques
       </a>
       consisting of small fragments of moon specimen brought back with the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">
        Apollo 11
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17" title="Apollo 17">
        Apollo 17
       </a>
       lunar missions and given in the 1970s to the people of 135 countries worldwide, the 50 states of the United States and its overseas territories by United States President
       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">
        Richard Nixon
       </a>
       as goodwill gifts.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moon_rocks_Apollo_11_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-moon_rocks_Apollo_11-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moon_rocks_Apollo_17_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-moon_rocks_Apollo_17-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      </p>

      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Apollo_11">
        Apollo 11
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_sample_displays&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Apollo 11">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4389" data-file-width="3873" height="113" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg/100px-Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg/150px-Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg/200px-Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg 2x" width="100"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Norway_Apollo_11_display.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Norway Apollo 11 'moon rocks' display
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_11_lunar_sample_display" title="Apollo 11 lunar sample display">
        Apollo 11 lunar sample display
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Apollo 11 lunar sample display is a commemorative podium style plaque display consisting of four rice-size dust particle specimens (dubbed "moon rocks"), the recipient's flag and two small metal plates attached with descriptive messages.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1970, US president
       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">
        Richard Nixon
       </a>
       gave presentation samples of moon rock brought back by Apollo 11 as gifts to 135 countries and 50 US states.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moon_rocks_Apollo_11_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-moon_rocks_Apollo_11-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Apollo_17">
        Apollo 17
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_sample_displays&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Apollo 17">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="651" data-file-width="431" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg/100px-NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg/150px-NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg/200px-NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg 2x" width="100"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NASA_photo_Honduras_Apollo_17_plaque.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Honduras plaque of Apollo 17
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17_lunar_sample_display" title="Apollo 17 lunar sample display">
        Apollo 17 lunar sample display
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Apollo 17 lunar sample display consists of a Moon rock fragment from a lava Moon stone identified as lunar basalt 70017, the recipient's flag and two small metal plates attached with descriptive messages, all inside a wooden commemorative plaque.
      </p>
      <p>
       Near the end of their third and final moonwalk, and what would be the last moonwalk of the Apollo program, Apollo 17 astronauts
       <a href="/wiki/Eugene_Cernan" title="Eugene Cernan">
        Eugene Cernan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Schmitt" title="Harrison Schmitt">
        Harrison Schmitt
       </a>
       "picked up a very significant rock, typical of what we have here in the valley of Taurus-Littrow... composed of many fragments, of many sizes, and many shapes, probably from all parts of the Moon, perhaps billions of years old" and made a special dedication to the young people of Earth. This rock was later labeled sample 70017.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       President Nixon ordered the distribution of fragments of the rock to 135 foreign heads of state and the 50 U.S. states. These gifts were distributed in 1973.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moon_rocks_Apollo_17_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-moon_rocks_Apollo_17-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Current_fate">
        Current fate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_sample_displays&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Current fate">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Stolen_and_missing_moon_rocks" title="Stolen and missing moon rocks">
        Stolen and missing moon rocks
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Of the 270 Apollo 11 Moon Rocks and Apollo 17 Goodwill Moon Rocks that were given to the nations of the world by the Nixon Administration, approximately 180 are currently unaccounted for. Many of the Moon rocks that are accounted for have been locked away in storage for decades. The location of the rocks has been tracked by researchers and hobbyists because of their rarity and the difficulty of obtaining more. Moon rocks have been subjects of theft and forgery as well.
      </p>
