      <p>
       <b>
        Sungazing
       </b>
       is the act of looking directly into the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        sun
       </a>
       . It is sometimes done as part of a spiritual or religious practice.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marinfilm_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-marinfilm-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The human eye is very sensitive, and prolonged exposure to direct
       <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">
        sunlight
       </a>
       can lead to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_retinopathy" title="Solar retinopathy">
        solar retinopathy
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Pterygium_(conjunctiva)" title="Pterygium (conjunctiva)">
        pterygium
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solomon2006_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solomon2006-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Cataract" title="Cataract">
        cataracts
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neale2003_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Neale2003-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and often blindness.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stokkermans_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Stokkermans-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norren_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Norren-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chen_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Chen-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Studies have shown that even when viewing a
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipse
       </a>
       the eye can still be exposed to harmful levels of
       <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet#Eye" title="Ultraviolet">
        ultraviolet
       </a>
       radiation.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Movements">
        Movements
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sungazing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Movements">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Referred to as
       <i>
        sunning
       </i>
       by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Horatio_Bates" title="William Horatio Bates">
        William Horatio Bates
       </a>
       as one of a series of exercises included in his
       <a href="/wiki/Bates_method#Sunning" title="Bates method">
        Bates method
       </a>
       , it became a popular form of
       <a href="/wiki/Alternative_medicine" title="Alternative medicine">
        alternative therapy
       </a>
       in the early 20th century.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chou2004_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Chou2004-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       His methods were widely debated at the time but ultimately discredited for lack of scientific rigor. The
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Medical_Journal" title="British Medical Journal">
         British Medical Journal
        </a>
       </i>
       reported in 1967 that "Bates (1920) advocated prolonged sun-gazing as the treatment of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Myopia" title="Myopia">
        myopia
       </a>
       , with disastrous results".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
