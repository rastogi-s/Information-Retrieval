      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Airport
        </b>
       </i>
       is a British documentary television series based at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Heathrow_Airport" title="London Heathrow Airport">
        London Heathrow Airport
       </a>
       , broadcast by the
       <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">
        BBC
       </a>
       and syndicated to
       <a href="/wiki/Dave_(TV_channel)" title="Dave (TV channel)">
        Dave
       </a>
       , part of the
       <a href="/wiki/UKTV" title="UKTV">
        UKTV
       </a>
       network.
      </p>
      <p>
       The show is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fly-on-the-wall" title="Fly-on-the-wall">
        fly-on-the-wall
       </a>
       series following passengers and staff who work for the airlines, the airport operator
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAA_plc" title="BAA plc">
        BAA
       </a>
       , the animal reception centre, emergency services, customs and immigration, and others. The airlines and staff featured change from series to series, although certain staff feature in multiple series.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The first series of
       <i>
        Airport
       </i>
       aired on
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">
        BBC Two
       </a>
       in 1996. Originally intended to be a low-key behind-the-scenes documentary, the emergence of several memorable recurring characters gave the show a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Docusoap" title="Docusoap">
        docusoap
       </a>
       feel. As a result, the series was transferred to a primetime
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">
        BBC One
       </a>
       slot the following year. Special fame was gained by
       <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Spake" title="Jeremy Spake">
        Jeremy Spake
       </a>
       , an
       <a href="/wiki/Aeroflot" title="Aeroflot">
        Aeroflot
       </a>
       employee, whose flamboyance earned him a series of short 'bumpers' on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UKTV_People" title="UKTV People">
        UKTV People
       </a>
       explaining how to best use airports. Other airlines featured throughout the series are
       <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">
        Aer Lingus
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Air_Jamaica" title="Air Jamaica">
        Air Jamaica
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Avianca" title="Avianca">
        Avianca
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMI_(airline)" title="BMI (airline)">
        BMI
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Airlines" title="Canadian Airlines">
        Canadian Airlines
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyprus_Airways" title="Cyprus Airways">
        Cyprus Airways
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Czech_Airlines" title="Czech Airlines">
        Czech Airlines
       </a>

        El Al

        Emirates
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Air" title="Gulf Air">
        Gulf Air
       </a>

      </p>
      <p>
       There have also been several special
       <i>
        Airport
       </i>
       programmes over the years featuring airports other than Heathrow, such as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_International_Airport" title="Frankfurt International Airport">
        Frankfurt
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Airport" title="Edinburgh Airport">
        Edinburgh
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The opening titles and music were given a make over for the start of the 7th series in 2002. From the 7th series onwards, incidental music (composed by Cherry Lodge) was added over the scenes shown on screen. The title music was once again revamped for the 10th series in 2005. In addition,
       <a href="/wiki/Liza_Tarbuck" title="Liza Tarbuck">
        Liza Tarbuck
       </a>
       replaced
       <a href="/wiki/John_Nettles" title="John Nettles">
        John Nettles
       </a>
       as the show's narrator for series 10.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Production_status">
        Production status
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production status">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Repeats of
       <i>
        Airport
       </i>
       have been shown on
       <a href="/wiki/Dave_(TV_channel)" title="Dave (TV channel)">
        Dave
       </a>
       and UKTV People, and they often ran an
       <i>
        Airport
       </i>
       day or weekend, showing back-to-back episodes for the duration of the day or weekend. Repeats have also be seen on
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">
        BBC One
       </a>
       , although less frequently. There was a new series broadcast in the first half of 2005, which finished in July 2005. Several episodes were broadcast, before a gap of about a month, then the rest of the series was shown.
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Airport
       </i>
       is also shown once a week on the Australian Lifestyle channel, a feature channel on Australia's Foxtel Digital network and also screens on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AUSTAR" title="AUSTAR">
        AUSTAR
       </a>
       network. It is also aired on the free-to-air
       <a href="/wiki/Nine_Network" title="Nine Network">
        Nine Network
       </a>
       .
      </p>

       <span class="mw-editsection">

       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>

       <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">
        BBC
       </a>
       broadcast a new 5-part series titled
       <i>
        Return to... Airport
       </i>
       . The show featured interviews with stars of the original series and behind the scenes recollections from cast members, as well as original series highlights.
