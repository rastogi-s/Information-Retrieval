      <p>
       <b>
        Monoranjan Bhattacharya
       </b>
       , nicknamed
       <i>
        Mona
       </i>
       , born at
       <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">
        Kolkata
       </a>
       , India is an Indian
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">
        Football
       </a>
       international player and a club level Coach and Manager.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       During his playing career he represented the "Big Two" of Kolkata football
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Bengal_Club" title="East Bengal Club">
        East Bengal Club
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohun_Bagan_AC" title="Mohun Bagan AC">
        Mohun Bagan AC
       </a>
       . He also represented India in various international tournaments from 1978 till 1989.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eastbengalfootballclub.com_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-eastbengalfootballclub.com-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He had an operation on his left leg in August 2016.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">
        Club career
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monoranjan_Bhattacharya_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Monoranjan joined
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Bengal_Club" title="East Bengal Club">
        East Bengal Club
       </a>
       in 1977 and created a club record for continuously representing the club in a period spanning 14 years.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eastbengalfootballclub.com_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-eastbengalfootballclub.com-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He was one of the most successful defenders of his time and was known for his strong and skillful game. He captained the club in 1981 season. At the end of 1990-91 season he left East Bengal Club to join their arch-rival
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohun_Bagan_AC" title="Mohun Bagan AC">
        Mohun Bagan AC
       </a>
       . After spending a couple of seasons with Mohun Bagan, Monoranjan returned to East Bengal in 1993 for his last season as a player.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">
        International career
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monoranjan_Bhattacharya_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Monoranjan was a regular for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_national_football_team" title="Indian national football team">
        Indian national football team
       </a>
       between 1978 and 1989 and represented
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        India
       </a>
       in various football tournaments within the period.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eastbengalfootballclub.com_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-eastbengalfootballclub.com-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">
        Coaching career
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monoranjan_Bhattacharya_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coaching career">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Monoranjan is a qualified AFC Coach. He was the coach of East Bengal Club from 1996 to 1998 and again for the period of 2000-2001.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eastbengalfootballclub.com_2-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-eastbengalfootballclub.com-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Under his coaching, the club won its first National Football League title in 2000-2001. Due to East Bengal's poor showing in the inaugural I-League 2007-08, their coach
       <a href="/wiki/Subrata_Bhattacharya_(footballer)" title="Subrata Bhattacharya (footballer)">
        Subrata Bhattacharya
       </a>
       resigned and Monoranjan became the coach of East Bengal for the third time.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He guided the club hanging in the relegation zone to a sixth-place finish in the I-League.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       After the league ended, he decided not continue in the post citing prior family engagements.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
