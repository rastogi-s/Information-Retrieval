      <p>
       <b>
        ENTSO-E
       </b>
       , the
       <b>
        European Network of Transmission System Operators
       </b>
       , represents 43 electricity
       <a href="/wiki/Transmission_system_operator" title="Transmission system operator">
        transmission system operators
       </a>
       (TSOs) from 36 countries across Europe, thus extending beyond EU borders. ENTSO-E was established and given legal mandates by the
       <a href="/wiki/Third_Energy_Package" title="Third Energy Package">
        EU’s Third Package
       </a>
       for the Internal Energy Market in 2009, which aims at further liberalising the gas and electricity markets in the EU.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       On 27 June 2008, 36 European electricity
       <a href="/wiki/Transmission_system_operator" title="Transmission system operator">
        transmission system operators
       </a>
       (TSOs) signed in
       <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">
        Prague
       </a>
       a declaration of intent to create the ENTSO-E.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entsoe010708_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-entsoe010708-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ENTSO-E was established on 19 December 2008 in
       <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">
        Brussels
       </a>
       by 42 TSOs as a successor of six regional associations of the electricity transmission system operators.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entsoe191208_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-entsoe191208-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statnett191208_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-statnett191208-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ENTSO-E became operational on 1 July 2009. The former associations ETSO, ATSOI, UKTSOA, NORDEL, UCTE and BALTSO became a part of the ENTSO-E, while still offering data by their predecessors for public interest.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entsoe010709_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-entsoe010709-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Creation of ENTSO-E was initiated by the adoption of the European Union
       <a href="/wiki/Third_Energy_Package" title="Third Energy Package">
        third legislative package on the gas and electricity markets
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entsoe010708_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-entsoe010708-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2003, the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">
        European Commission
       </a>
       conducted a sector inquiry concerning the competition of
       <a href="/wiki/Electricity_market" title="Electricity market">
        electricity market
       </a>
       in six European countries.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inquiry_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-inquiry-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Examining competition in these countries, the final report stated serious issues to be solved.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-final_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-final-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was noticed that the integration between member state's markets is still insufficient. Additionally, the absence of transparently available market information was assessed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inquiry_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-inquiry-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As a result, the third legislative package on the EU gas and electricity markets was adopted by the European Commission in September 2007.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-third_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-third-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">
        Objectives
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Objectives">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       According to its website, "ENTSO-E promotes closer cooperation across Europe’s
       <a href="/wiki/Transmission_system_operator" title="Transmission system operator">
        TSOs
       </a>
       to support the implementation of
       <a href="/wiki/Energy_policy_of_the_European_Union" title="Energy policy of the European Union">
        EU energy policy
       </a>
       and achieve Europe’s energy &amp; climate policy objectives, which are changing the very nature of the power system. The main objectives of ENTSO-E centre on the integration of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_sources" title="Renewable energy sources">
        renewable energy sources (RES)
       </a>
       such as wind and solar power into the power system, and the completion of the internal energy market (IEM), which is central to meeting the European Union’s energy policy objectives of affordability, sustainability and security of supply. [...] ENTSO-E aims to be the focal point for all technical, market and policy issues relating to TSOs and the European network, interfacing with power system users, EU institutions, regulators and national governments."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Members">
        Members
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Members">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Transmission_system_operator" title="Transmission system operator">
        TSOs
       </a>
       are responsible for the
       <a href="/wiki/Electric_power_transmission" title="Electric power transmission">
        bulk transmission of electric power
       </a>
       on the main
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-voltage_lines" title="High-voltage lines">
        high voltage
       </a>
       electric networks. TSOs provide
       <a href="/wiki/Electrical_grid" title="Electrical grid">
        grid
       </a>
       access to the
       <a href="/wiki/Electricity_market" title="Electricity market">
        electricity market
       </a>
       players (i.e., generating companies, traders, suppliers, distributors, and directly connected customers) according to non-discriminatory and transparent rules. In many countries, TSOs are in charge of the development of the grid infrastructure, too. TSOs in the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">

       </a>
       internal electricity market are entities operating independently from the other electricity market players (unbundling)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">

        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_area">
        Geographical area
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geographical area">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The geographical area covered by ENTSO-E’s member TSOs is divided into five
       <a href="/wiki/Wide_area_synchronous_grid" title="Wide area synchronous grid">
        synchronous areas
       </a>
       and two isolated systems (Cyprus and Iceland). Synchronous areas are groups of countries that are connected via their respective power systems. The system
       <a href="/wiki/Utility_frequency" title="Utility frequency">
        frequency
       </a>
       (50 Hz, with usually very minor deviations) is synchronous within each area, and a disturbance at one single point in the area will be registered across the entire zone. Individual synchronous areas are interconnected through
       <a href="/wiki/Direct_current" title="Direct current">
        direct current
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Interconnector" title="Interconnector">
        interconnectors
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The benefits of synchronous areas include pooling of generation capacities, common provisioning of reserves, both resulting in cost-savings, and mutual assistance in the event of disturbances, resulting in cheaper reserve power costs (for instance in case of a disturbance or outage).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_basis">
        Legal basis
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legal basis">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Third_Energy_Package" title="Third Energy Package">
        Third Energy Package
       </a>
       and Regulation (EC) No 714/2009
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       on conditions for access to the network for cross-border exchanges in electricity regulation stipulate ENTSO-E’s tasks and responsibilities. Regulation (EU) 838/2010
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       on guidelines relating to the inter-TSO compensation mechanism sets out the methodology by which TSOs receive compensation for the costs incurred in hosting cross-border flows of electricity. Regulation (EU) 347/2013
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       on guidelines for trans-European energy infrastructure defines European Projects of Common Interest (PCIs)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       identifies ENTSO-E's ten-year network development plan (TYNDP)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       as the basis for the selection of PCIs. ENTSO-E is also mandated to develop a corresponding cost–benefit methodology
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entsoe2_17-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-entsoe2-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       for the assessment of transmission infrastructure projects.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Transparency Regulation (EU) No. 543/2013
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       on submission and publication of data in electricity markets makes it mandatory for European Member State data providers and owners to submit fundamental information related to electricity generation, load, transmission, balancing, outages, and congestion management for publication through the ENTSO-E Transparency Platform.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Key_activities">
        Key activities
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Key activities">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span id="Pan-European_Transmission_Network_plans_and_cost.E2.80.93benefit_analysis">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Pan-European_Transmission_Network_plans_and_cost–benefit_analysis">
        Pan-European Transmission Network plans and cost–benefit analysis
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pan-European Transmission Network plans and cost–benefit analysis">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The ten-year network development plan 2016 (TYNDP)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is drafted by ENTSO-E, in close cooperation with stakeholders, under scrutiny of
       <a href="/wiki/Agency_for_the_Cooperation_of_Energy_Regulators" title="Agency for the Cooperation of Energy Regulators">
        ACER
       </a>
       and is finally adopted by the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">
        European Commission
       </a>
       . It is the only existing pan-European network development plan. It is the basis for the selection of EU projects of common interest (PCIs).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The list of PCIs is not fixed by ENTSO-E and is subject to a different process led by the European Commission and EU Member States.
      </p>
      <p>
       The TYNDP is updated every two years. For inclusion in the TYNDP, each project, whether transmission or storage, has to go through a cost–benefit analysis. The benefit analysis methodology
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entsoe2_17-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-entsoe2-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is developed by ENTSO-E in consultation with stakeholders and adopted by the European Commission. It assesses projects against socio-economic and environmental criteria.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Adequacy_forecasts">
        Adequacy forecasts
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Adequacy forecasts">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       ENTSO-E publishes summer and winter generation outlooks,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
       well as a long-term system adequacy forecast, the Scenario Outlook &amp; Adequacy Forecast (SO&amp;AF).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The seasonal outlooks assess if there is enough generation to cover supply and highlight possibilities for neighbouring countries to contribute to the generation/demand balance in critical situations in a specific country. The SO&amp;AF analyses system adequacy on the long-term and is connected to investment decisions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Network_codes">
        Network codes
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Network codes">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       ENTSO-E’s network codes are binding pan-European rules drafted by ENTSO-E in consultation with stakeholders, with guidance from
       <a href="/wiki/Agency_for_the_Cooperation_of_Energy_Regulators" title="Agency for the Cooperation of Energy Regulators">
        ACER
       </a>
       . Network codes are grouped in three areas:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        connection codes connecting electricity generators, demand, and direct current lines to the transmission grids;
       </li>
       <li>
        operational codes governing how the pan-European electricity systems are operated;
       </li>
       <li>
        and market codes facilitating and harmonising electricity trading across European borders.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
          [11]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
         <a href="#cite_note-23">
          [23]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The drafting and adoption process of network codes is defined by the
       <a href="/wiki/Third_Energy_Package" title="Third Energy Package">
        Third Package
       </a>
       . ACER develops a framework guideline setting the policy choices for each code. On this basis, the codes are drafted by ENTSO-E in consultation with stakeholders. After ACER’s opinion and recommendation for adoption, each code is submitted to the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">
        European Commission
       </a>
       for approval through the
       <a href="/wiki/Comitology" title="Comitology">
        Comitology process
       </a>
       , i.e., to be voted on by Member State representatives and thus to become EU law, directly binding and implemented across all Member States.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Transparency_Platform_.28TP.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Transparency_Platform_(TP)">
        Transparency Platform (TP)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transparency Platform (TP)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       ENTSO-E’s Central Information Transparency Platform
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       provides free access to fundamental data and information on pan-European wholesale energy generation, transmission, and consumption.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Research.2C_development.2C_and_innovation_.28RDI.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Research,_development,_and_innovation_(RDI)">
        Research, development, and innovation (RDI)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Research, development, and innovation (RDI)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       ENTSO-E’s R&amp;D Roadmap
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       provides the ENTSO-E vision on grid projects to be carried out by
       <a href="/wiki/Transmission_system_operator" title="Transmission system operator">
        TSOs
       </a>
       to meet EU objectives. The roadmap is supported by the annual R&amp;D Implementation Plan,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       which combines both top-down and bottom-up approaches in meeting the requirements of the roadmap. ENTSO-E publishes annually a R&amp;D Monitoring Report
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       that assesses the progress of TSO-related R&amp;D work.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">
        Governance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Governance">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       ENTSO-E is an international non-profit association (
       <a href="/wiki/Association_without_lucrative_purpose" title="Association without lucrative purpose">
        AISBL
       </a>
       ) established according to Belgian law. ENTSO-E is financed by its members. The TSOs contribute to the budget according to the number of countries and the population served.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The highest body of ENTSO-E is the Assembly, which is composed of representatives at CEO level of all the currently 43 members. The ENTSO-E Board is elected every two years from the overall membership and through the Assembly. It includes 12 representatives. The president, vice president, and committee chairs are invited to board meetings. The board coordinates the committees and LRG work and implements Assembly decisions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       ENTSO-E has established four specialized committees
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
       composed of managers from member TSOs. Each committee leads a number of regional groups and working groups.
      </p>
      <p>
       At the same level as the four committees, the transversal Legal &amp; Regulatory Group
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
       advises all ENTSO-E bodies on legal and regulatory issues. In addition, expert groups on data, network codes implementation, and EU affairs provide specific expertise and work products to the association.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_11-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       ENTSO-E’s Secretariat is based in Brussels. It is headed by the secretary-general and represents ENTSO-E to the European institutions, regulators, and stakeholders.
      </p>
