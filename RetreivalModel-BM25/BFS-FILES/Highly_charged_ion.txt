    Highly charged ion
   </h1>

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Highly charged ions
       </b>
       (HCI) are
       <a href="/wiki/Ion" title="Ion">
        ions
       </a>
       in very high charge states due to the loss of many or most of their bound
       <a href="/wiki/Electron" title="Electron">
        electrons
       </a>
       by energetic collisions or high-energy
       <a href="/wiki/Photon" title="Photon">
        photon
       </a>
       absorption. Examples are 13-fold ionized iron,
       <span class="chemf nowrap">
        Fe
        <span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left">
         <sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">
          13+
         </sup>
         <br/>
        </span>
       </span>
       or Fe XIV in spectroscopic notation, found in the Sun's
       <a href="/wiki/Corona" title="Corona">
        corona
       </a>
       , or
       <i>
        naked
       </i>
       uranium,
       <span class="chemf nowrap">
        U
        <span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left">
         <sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">
          92+
         </sup>
         <br/>
        </span>
       </span>
       or U XCIII, bare all bound electrons, which requires very high energy for its production. HCI are found in stellar
       <a href="/wiki/Corona" title="Corona">
        coronae
       </a>
       , in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Active_galactic_nuclei" title="Active galactic nuclei">
        active galactic nuclei
       </a>
       , in
       <a href="/wiki/Supernova_remnant" title="Supernova remnant">
        supernova remnants
       </a>
       , and in
       <a href="/wiki/Accretion_disk" title="Accretion disk">
        accretion disks
       </a>
       . Most of the visible matter found in the universe consists of highly charged ions
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       . High temperature
       <a href="/wiki/Plasma_(physics)" title="Plasma (physics)">
        plasmas
       </a>
       used for
       <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fusion" title="Nuclear fusion">
        nuclear fusion
       </a>
       energy research also contain HCI generated by the plasma-wall interaction (see
       <a href="/wiki/Tokamak" title="Tokamak">
        Tokamak
       </a>
       ). In the laboratory, HCI are investigated by means of heavy ion
       <a href="/wiki/Particle_accelerator" title="Particle accelerator">
        particle accelerators
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Electron_beam_ion_trap" title="Electron beam ion trap">
        electron beam ion traps
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       .
      </p>
