      <p>
       Solar
       <a href="/wiki/Saros_(astronomy)" title="Saros (astronomy)">
        Saros
       </a>
       139 repeats every 18 years, 11 days. Saros 139 contains 71 events of which 16 will be partial eclipses, 12 will be hybrid and 43 will be total. The first total eclipse occurred on December 21, 1843 over southern Asia and lasted 1 minute and 43 seconds. The last total eclipse will occur on March 26, 2601 over
       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">
        Antarctica
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_ocean" title="Southern ocean">
        southern ocean
       </a>
       lasting 35 seconds.
      </p>
      <p>
       This series is currently producing total eclipses over 4 minutes long, with each one gradually increasing in length. It will continue to do so until
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_July_16,_2186" title="Solar eclipse of July 16, 2186">
        July 16, 2186
       </a>
       when it will produce the longest total eclipse calculated for the ten millennia from 3999 BCE to 6000 CE.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The last eclipse to occur was a total eclipse on
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_March_29,_2006" title="Solar eclipse of March 29, 2006">
        March 29, 2006
       </a>
       , lasting 4 minutes 7 seconds and passing over portions of Africa and western Asia. The next eclipse will occur on
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_April_8,_2024" title="Solar eclipse of April 8, 2024">
        April 8, 2024
       </a>
       , lasting 4 minutes 28 seconds over central
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       , entering in
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
        Mexico
       </a>
       , crossing the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       , and leaving in eastern
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       .
      </p>
