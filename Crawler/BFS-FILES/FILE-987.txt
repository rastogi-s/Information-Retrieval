   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Coronal seismology
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">

    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Coronal seismology
       </b>
       is a technique of studying the
       <a href="/wiki/Plasma_(physics)" title="Plasma (physics)">
        plasma
       </a>
       of the Sun's
       <a href="/wiki/Corona" title="Corona">
        corona
       </a>
       with the use of
       <a href="/wiki/Magnetohydrodynamics" title="Magnetohydrodynamics">
        magnetohydrodynamic
       </a>
       (MHD)
       <a href="/wiki/Wave" title="Wave">
        waves
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Oscillation" title="Oscillation">
        oscillations
       </a>
       . Magnetohydrodynamics studies the
       <a href="/wiki/Dynamics_(mechanics)" title="Dynamics (mechanics)">
        dynamics
       </a>
       of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_conduction" title="Electrical conduction">
        electrically conducting
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Fluid" title="Fluid">
        fluids
       </a>
       - in this case the fluid is the coronal plasma. Observed properties of the waves (e.g.
       <a href="/wiki/Frequency" title="Frequency">
        period
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Wavelength" title="Wavelength">
        wavelength
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Amplitude" title="Amplitude">
        amplitude
       </a>
       , temporal and spatial signatures (what is the shape of the wave perturbation?), characteristic scenarios of the wave evolution (is the wave damped?), combined with a theoretical modelling of the wave phenomena (
       <a href="/wiki/Dispersion_relation" title="Dispersion relation">
        dispersion relations
       </a>
       , evolutionary equations, etc.), may reflect physical parameters of the corona which are not accessible
       <i>
        in situ,
       </i>
       such as the coronal
       <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_field" title="Magnetic field">
        magnetic field
       </a>
       strength and
       <a href="/wiki/Alfv%C3%A9n_wave#Alfvén_velocity" title="Alfvén wave">
        Alfvén velocity
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nakariakov2001_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Nakariakov2001-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and coronal
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dissipative" title="Dissipative">
        dissipative
       </a>
       coefficients.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Originally, the method of MHD coronal seismology was suggested by Y. Uchida in 1970
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       for propagating waves, and B. Roberts et al. in 1984
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       for standing waves, but was not practically applied until the late 90s due to a lack of necessary observational resolution. Philosophically, coronal seismology is similar to the Earth's
       <a href="/wiki/Seismology" title="Seismology">
        seismology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Helioseismology" title="Helioseismology">
        helioseismology
       </a>
       , and MHD spectroscopy of laboratory plasma devices. In all these approaches, waves of various kind are used to probe a medium.
      </p>
      <p>
       The theoretical foundation of coronal seismology is the
       <a href="/wiki/Dispersion_relation" title="Dispersion relation">
        dispersion relation
       </a>
       of MHD modes of a plasma cylinder: a plasma structure which is nonuniform in the transverse direction and extended along the magnetic field. This model works well for the description of a number of plasma structures observed in the solar corona: e.g.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coronal_loops" title="Coronal loops">
        coronal loops
       </a>
       , prominence fibrils, plumes, various filaments. Such a structure acts as a
       <a href="/wiki/Waveguide" title="Waveguide">
        waveguide
       </a>
       of MHD waves.
      </p>
      <p>
       This discussion is adapted from Nakariakov &amp; Verwichte (2009).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_magnetohydrodynamic_waves">
        Types of magnetohydrodynamic waves
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Coronal_seismology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types of magnetohydrodynamic waves">
         edit

        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are several distinct kinds of MHD modes which have quite different
       <a href="/wiki/Dispersive_mass_transfer" title="Dispersive mass transfer">
        dispersive
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Polarization_(waves)" title="Polarization (waves)">
        polarisation
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Wave_propagation" title="Wave propagation">
        propagation
       </a>
       properties:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Kink (or
        <a href="/wiki/Transverse_wave" title="Transverse wave">
         transverse
        </a>
        ) modes, which are
        <a href="/wiki/Oblique_shock" title="Oblique shock">
         oblique
        </a>
        fast magnetoacoustic (also known as
        <a href="/wiki/Magnetosonic_wave" title="Magnetosonic wave">
         magnetosonic waves
        </a>
        ) guided by the plasma structure; the mode causes the displacement of the axis of the plasma structure. These modes are weakly
        <a href="/wiki/Compressibility" title="Compressibility">
         compressible
        </a>
        , but could nevertheless be observed with imaging instruments as periodic standing or propagating displacements of coronal structures, e.g.
        <a href="/wiki/Coronal_loop" title="Coronal loop">
         coronal loops
        </a>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Observations">
        Observations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Coronal_seismology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Observations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Coronal_arcade.png">
         <img alt="coronal arcade after flare" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="463" data-file-width="635" height="160" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Coronal_arcade.png/220px-Coronal_arcade.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Coronal_arcade.png/330px-Coronal_arcade.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Coronal_arcade.png/440px-Coronal_arcade.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Coronal_arcade.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         TRACE image of a coronal arcade
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Wave and oscillatory phenomena are observed in the hot plasma of the corona mainly in EUV, optical and microwave bands with a number of spaceborne and ground-based instruments, e.g. the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_and_Heliospheric_Observatory" title="Solar and Heliospheric Observatory">
        Solar and Heliospheric Observatory
       </a>
       (SOHO), the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transition_Region_and_Coronal_Explorer" title="Transition Region and Coronal Explorer">
        Transition Region and Coronal Explorer
       </a>
       (TRACE), the Nobeyama Radioheliograph (NoRH, see the
       <a href="/wiki/Nobeyama_radio_observatory" title="Nobeyama radio observatory">
        Nobeyama radio observatory
       </a>
       ). Phenomenologically, researchers distinguish between compressible waves in polar plumes and in legs of large
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coronal_loops" title="Coronal loops">
        coronal loops
       </a>
       , flare-generated transverse oscillations of loops, acoustic oscillations of loops, propagating kink waves in loops and in structures above arcades (an
       <b>
        arcade
       </b>
       being a close collection of loops in a cylindrical structure, see image to right), sausage oscillations of flaring loops, and oscillations of prominences and fibrils (see
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_prominence" title="Solar prominence">
        solar prominence
       </a>
       ), and this list is continuously updated.
      </p>
      <p>
       Coronal seismology is one of the aims of the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_Dynamics_Observatory#Atmospheric_Imaging_Assembly_(AIA)" title="Solar Dynamics Observatory">
        Atmospheric Imaging Assembly
       </a>
       (AIA) instrument on the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_Dynamics_Observatory" title="Solar Dynamics Observatory">
        Solar Dynamics Observatory
       </a>
       (SDO) mission.
      </p>
      <p>
       A mission to send a spacecraft as close as 9 solar radii from the sun,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NASA_Solar_probe" title="NASA Solar probe">
        NASA Solar probe
       </a>
       , is planned for launch in 2015 and aims to provide in-situ measurements of the solar magnetic field, solar wind and corona. It should include a magnetometer and plasma wave sensor, allowing unprecedented observations for coronal seismology.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Conclusions">
        Conclusions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Coronal_seismology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conclusions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The potential of coronal seismology in the estimation of the coronal
       <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_field" title="Magnetic field">
        magnetic field
       </a>
       , density
       <a href="/wiki/Scale_height" title="Scale height">
        scale height
       </a>
       , "fine structure" (by which is meant the variation in structure of an inhomogeneous structure such as an inhomogeneous coronal loop) and heating has been demonstrated by different research groups. Work relating to the coronal magnetic field was mentioned earlier.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nakariakov2001_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Nakariakov2001-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It has been shown that sufficiently broadband slow magnetoacoustic waves, consistent with currently available observations in the low frequency part of the spectrum, could provide the rate of heat deposition sufficient to heat a
       <a href="/wiki/Coronal_loop" title="Coronal loop">
        coronal loop
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Regarding the density scale height, transverse oscillations of coronal loops that have both variable circular cross-sectional area and plasma density in the longitudinal direction have been studied theoretically. A second order ordinary differential equation has been derived describing the displacement of the loop axis. Together with boundary conditions, solving this equation determines the eigenfrequencies and eigenmodes. The coronal density scale height could then be estimated by using the observed ratio of the fundamental frequency and first overtone of loop kink oscillations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Little is known of coronal fine structure. Doppler shift oscillations in hot active region loops obtained with the Solar Ultraviolet Measurements of Emitted Radiation instrument (SUMER) aboard SOHO have been studied. The spectra were recorded along a 300 arcsec slit placed at a fixed position in the corona above the active regions. Some oscillations showed phase propagation along the slit in one or both directions with apparent speeds in the range of 8–102 km per second, together with distinctly different intensity and line width distributions along the slit. These features can be explained by the excitation of the oscillation at a footpoint of an inhomogeneous coronal loop, e.g. a loop with
       <b>
        fine structure
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
