      <p>
       <b>
        Phil Whitaker
       </b>
       (born 1966) is a British novelist and physician.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He qualified in medicine at the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nottingham" title="University of Nottingham">
        University of Nottingham
       </a>
       in 1990, and the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">
        University of Oxford
       </a>
       where he undertook postgraduate training in general practice. He also completed a MA in Creative Writing at the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_East_Anglia" title="University of East Anglia">
        University of East Anglia
       </a>
       in 1996.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UEA_V-Z_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-UEA_V-Z-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       His
       <a href="/wiki/Debut_novel" title="Debut novel">
        debut novel
       </a>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_of_the_Sun_(novel)" title="Eclipse of the Sun (novel)">
         Eclipse of the Sun
        </a>
       </i>
       received the 1997
       <a href="/wiki/John_Llewellyn_Rhys_Prize" title="John Llewellyn Rhys Prize">
        John Llewellyn Rhys Prize
       </a>
       and the 1998
       <a href="/wiki/Betty_Trask_Award" title="Betty Trask Award">
        Betty Trask Award
       </a>
       , and was shortlisted for the
       <a href="/wiki/1997_Whitbread_Awards" title="1997 Whitbread Awards">
        1997 Whitbread First Novel Award
       </a>
       . His second novel
       <i>
        Triangulation
       </i>
       won the 2000
       <a href="/wiki/Encore_Award" title="Encore Award">
        Encore Award
       </a>
       . He also writes for the
       <a href="/wiki/New_Statesman" title="New Statesman">
        New Statesman
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
