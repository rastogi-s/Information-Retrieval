      <p>
       <b>
        Srikakulam
       </b>
       is a city and the district headquarters of
       <a href="/wiki/Srikakulam_district" title="Srikakulam district">
        Srikakulam district
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        Indian
       </a>
       state of
       <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">
        Andhra Pradesh
       </a>
       . It is a
       <a href="/wiki/Municipal_corporation" title="Municipal corporation">
        municipal corporation
       </a>
       and also the mandal headquarters of Srikakulam
       <a href="/wiki/Tehsil" title="Tehsil">
        mandal
       </a>
       in Srikakulam revenue division.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-census-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As of 2011
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Srikakulam&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Census_of_India,_2011" title="Census of India, 2011">
        census
       </a>
       , it was most populous city of Srikaulam district and
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Andhra_Pradesh" title="List of cities in Andhra Pradesh">
        24th populous city
       </a>
       of the state with a population of 146,988. The city is believed to have been founded by
       <a href="/wiki/Balarama" title="Balarama">
        Balarama
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">
        Demographics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Srikakulam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       As of 2011
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Srikakulam&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Census_of_India" title="Census of India">
        Census of India
       </a>
       , the city had a population of 146,988. The total population constitute, 73,077 males and 73,911 females —a sex ratio of 1011 females per 1000 males. 11,607 children are in the age group of 0–6 years. The average literacy rate stands at 84.62% 96,744 literates, significantly higher than the national average of 73.00%.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Urban agglomeration had a population of 147,015, of which males constitute 73,077, females constitute 73,911 —a sex ratio of 931 females per 1000 males and 12,741 children are in the age group of 0–6 years. There are a total of 115,061 literates with an average literacy rate of 85.71%.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UA_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-UA-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Civic_administration">
        Civic administration
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Srikakulam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Civic administration">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Srikakulam_Municipal_Corporation" title="Srikakulam Municipal Corporation">
        Srikakulam Municipal Corporation
       </a>
       is the civic body of the city. It was constituted as a municipality in the year 1856 and was upgraded to corporation on 9 December 2015.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-civicbody_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-civicbody-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Its jurisdictional area has an extent of 20.89 km
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
       (8.07 sq mi) with 36 wards.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-civicbody_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-civicbody-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Srikakulam
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_agglomeration" title="Urban agglomeration">
        urban agglomeration
       </a>
       constituents of the city include, Srikakulam municipality,
       <a href="/wiki/Census_town" title="Census town">
        census town
       </a>
       of Balaga, fully
       <a href="/wiki/Out_growth" title="Out growth">
        out growths
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Arasavalli" title="Arasavalli">
        Arasavalli
       </a>
       , partly outgrowths of Kusulapuram, Thotapalem, Patrunivalasa and Patha Srikakulam (rural). While, Ponugutivalasa of
       <a href="/wiki/Santhakavati" title="Santhakavati">
        Santhakavati
       </a>
       mandal is now a part of Rajam
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nagar_Panchayat" title="Nagar Panchayat">
        Nagar Panchayat
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">
        Politics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh_Legislative_Assembly" title="Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly">
        Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly
       </a>
       . Gunda Lakshmi Devi is the present
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Legislative_Assembly_(India)" title="Member of Legislative Assembly (India)">
        MLA
       </a>
       of the constituency from
       <a href="/wiki/Telugu_Desam_Party" title="Telugu Desam Party">
        Telugu Desam Party
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is also a part of
       <a href="/wiki/Srikakulam_(Lok_Sabha_constituency)" title="Srikakulam (Lok Sabha constituency)">
        Srikakulam (Lok Sabha constituency)
       </a>
       which was won by
       <a href="/wiki/Rammohan_Naidu_Kinjarapu" title="Rammohan Naidu Kinjarapu">
        Rammohan Naidu Kinjarapu
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Telugu_Desam_Party" title="Telugu Desam Party">
        Telugu Desam Party
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mla_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-mla-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Members of Parliament
       </b>
       : 1952, 1957, 1962, 1967, 1972 and 1977 – Boddepalli Rajagopala Rao 1995, 1999 and 2004 – Yerrannaidu Kinjarapu 2009 Killi kruparani 2014 Rammohan Naidu Kinjarapu
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Members of Legislative Assembly

      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh_State_Road_Transport_Corporation" title="Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation">
        Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation
       </a>
       operates bus services from Srikakulam bus station.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/National_Highway_16_(India)" title="National Highway 16 (India)">
        National Highway 16 (India)
       </a>
       , a part of
       <a href="/wiki/Golden_Quadrilateral" title="Golden Quadrilateral">
        Golden Quadrilateral
       </a>
       highway network, bypasses the city.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Srikakulam_Road_railway_station" title="Srikakulam Road railway station">
        Srikakulam Road railway station
       </a>
       (station code:CHE)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is an
       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Railways" title="Indian Railways">
        Indian railway station
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Amudalavalasa" title="Amudalavalasa">
        Amudalavalasa
       </a>
       near to
       <a class="mw-selflink selflink">
        Srikakulam
       </a>
       town of
       <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">
        Andhra Pradesh
       </a>
       . It lies on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khurda_Road-Visakhapatnam_section" title="Khurda Road-Visakhapatnam section">
        Khurda Road-Visakhapatnam section
       </a>
       of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Howrah-Chennai_main_line" title="Howrah-Chennai main line">
        Howrah-Chennai main line
       </a>
       and is administered under
       <a href="/wiki/Waltair_railway_division" title="Waltair railway division">
        Waltair railway division
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Railway_zone" title="East Coast Railway zone">
        East Coast Railway zone
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">
        Sports
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Srikakulam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sports">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       International athletes like
       <a href="/wiki/Karnam_Malleswari" title="Karnam Malleswari">
        Karnam Malleswari
       </a>
       (weightlifting) and Korada Mrudula (running, 400 m) are from Srikakulam. Kodi Ram Murthy Stadium near Govt. Degree College is a multi-purpose sporting facility in the town. In 1991, an unofficial cricket match was played between Sunil Gavaskar XI and Kapil Dev XI with both the legends being a part of the game as well. The only swimming pool (maintained by SAAP) is located in Santhinagar.
      </p>
