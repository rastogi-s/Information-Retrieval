      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Russian Academy of Sciences
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        RAS
       </b>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
        Russian
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="ru">
        Росси́йская акаде́мия нау́к (РАН)
       </span>
       <i>
        Rossíiskaya akadémiya naúk
       </i>
       ) consists of the
       <a href="/wiki/National_academy" title="National academy">
        national academy
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
        Russia
       </a>
       ; a network of scientific research institutes from across the Russian Federation; and additional scientific and social units such as libraries, publishing units, and hospitals.
      </p>
      <p>
       Headquartered in
       <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">
        Moscow
       </a>
       , the Academy (RAS) is considered a civil, self-governed, non-commercial organization
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       chartered by the
       <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Russia" title="Government of Russia">
        Government of Russia
       </a>
       . It combines the members of RAS (see below) and scientists employed by institutions. Near the central academy building there is a monument to
       <a href="/wiki/Monument_to_Yuri_Gagarin" title="Monument to Yuri Gagarin">
        Yuri Gagarin
       </a>
       in the square bearing his name.
      </p>
      <p>
       As of November 2017, the Academy included 1008 institutions and other units;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       totally about 125,000 people were employed of which 47,000 are scientific researchers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">
        Membership
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Membership">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are three types of membership in the RAS: full members (
       <a href="/wiki/Academician" title="Academician">
        academicians
       </a>
       ), corresponding members, and foreign members. Academicians and corresponding members must be citizens of the Russian Federation when elected. However, some academicians and corresponding members were elected before the collapse of the USSR and are now citizens of other countries. Members of RAS are elected based on their scientific contributions – election to membership is considered very prestigious.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-members_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-members-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the years 2005–2012, the academy had approximately 500 full and 700 corresponding members. But in 2013, after the Russian Academy of Agricultural Sciences and the Russian Academy of Medical Sciences became incorporated into the RAS, a number of the RAS members accordingly increased.
      </p>
      <p>
       The last elections to the renewed Russian Academy of Sciences were organized in October 2016. Earlier, in winter 2015/2016, the Academy assigned the so-called
       <a href="/wiki/Professor_of_the_Russian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Professor of the Russian Academy of Sciences">
        RAS Professors
       </a>
       (now there are 492 professors), subordinated to the regular members and considered as possible candidates for membership;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-listsofprofs_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-listsofprofs-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       104 of them were elected already in autumn 2016 and are now titled "RAS professor, corresponding member of the RAS". Totally, as of January 2018, the Academy had 2024 Russian members (full: 901, corresponding: 1123) and 487 foreign members.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Present_Structure_of_the_Academy">
        Present Structure of the Academy
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Present Structure of the Academy">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The RAS consists of 13 specialized scientific divisions, three territorial branches and 15 regional scientific centers. The Academy has numerous councils, committees, and commissions, all organized for different purposes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Territorial_branches">
        Territorial branches
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Territorial branches">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Siberian Branch of the Russian Academy of Sciences (SB RAS)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        The Siberian Branch was established in 1957, with Mikhail Lavrentyev as founding chairman. Research centers are in
        <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk" title="Novosibirsk">
         Novosibirsk
        </a>
        (
        <a href="/wiki/Akademgorodok" title="Akademgorodok">
         Akademgorodok
        </a>
        ),
        <a href="/wiki/Tomsk" title="Tomsk">
         Tomsk
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Krasnoyarsk" title="Krasnoyarsk">
         Krasnoyarsk
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Irkutsk" title="Irkutsk">
         Irkutsk
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Yakutsk" title="Yakutsk">
         Yakutsk
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Ulan-Ude" title="Ulan-Ude">
         Ulan-Ude
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Kemerovo" title="Kemerovo">
         Kemerovo
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Tyumen" title="Tyumen">
         Tyumen
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Omsk" title="Omsk">
         Omsk
        </a>
        . As of 2005, the Branch employed over 33,000 employees, 58 of whom were members of the Academy.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbras_8-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-sbras-8">
          [8]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Ural Branch of the Russian Academy of Sciences (UB RAS)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        The Ural Branch was established in 1932, with Aleksandr Fersman as its founding chairman. Research centers are in
        <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">
         Yekaterinburg
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Perm" title="Perm">
         Perm
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheliabinsk" title="Cheliabinsk">
         Cheliabinsk
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Izhevsk" title="Izhevsk">
         Izhevsk
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Orenburg" title="Orenburg">
         Orenburg
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Ufa" title="Ufa">
         Ufa
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Syktyvkar" title="Syktyvkar">
         Syktyvkar
        </a>
        . As of 2007, the Branch employed 3,600 scientists, 590 full professors, 31 full members, and 58 corresponding members of the Academy.
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Far East Branch of the Russian Academy of Sciences (FEB RAS)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        The Far East Branch includes the Primorsky Scientific Center in
        <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">
         Vladivostok
        </a>
        , the Amur Scientific Center in
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blagoveschensk" title="Blagoveschensk">
         Blagoveschensk
        </a>
        , the Khabarovsk Scientific Center, the Sakhalin Scientific Center in
        <a href="/wiki/Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk" title="Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk">
         Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk

      <p>
       Member institutions are linked via a dedicated Russian Space Science Internet (RSSI). Started with just three members, The RSSI now has 3,100 members, including 57 from the largest research institutions.
      </p>
      <p>
       Russian universities and technical institutes are not under the supervision of the RAS (they are subordinated to the Ministry of Education of Russian Federation), but a number of leading universities, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Moscow_State_University" title="Moscow State University">
        Moscow State University
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg_State_University" title="St. Petersburg State University">
        St. Petersburg State University
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk_State_University" title="Novosibirsk State University">
        Novosibirsk State University
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Institute_of_Physics_and_Technology" title="Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology">
        Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology
       </a>
       , make use of the staff and facilities of many institutes of the RAS (as well as of other research institutions); the
       <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Institute_of_Physics_and_Technology" title="Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology">
        MIPT
       </a>
       faculty refers to this arrangement as the "Phystech System".
      </p>
      <p>
       From 1933 to 1992, the main scientific journal of the Soviet Academy of Sciences was the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Proceedings_of_the_USSR_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Proceedings of the USSR Academy of Sciences">
         Proceedings of the USSR Academy of Sciences
        </a>
       </i>
       (Doklady Akademii Nauk SSSR); after 1992, it became simply
       <i>
        Proceedings of the Academy of Sciences
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        Doklady Akademii Nauk
       </i>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       The Academy is also increasing its presence in the educational area. In 1990 the
       <a href="/wiki/Higher_Chemical_College_of_the_Russian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Higher Chemical College of the Russian Academy of Sciences">
        Higher Chemical College of the Russian Academy of Sciences
       </a>
       was founded, a specialized university intended to provide extensive opportunities for students to choose an academic path.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation._The_Russian_Empire_times">
        Foundation. The Russian Empire times
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Foundation. The Russian Empire times">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2384" data-file-width="3600" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg/250px-Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg/375px-Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg/500px-Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg 2x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kunstkamera_SPB_01.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Original headquarters of the Imperial Academy of Sciences - the
         <a href="/wiki/Kunstkamera" title="Kunstkamera">
          Kunstkamera
         </a>
         in
         <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">
          Saint Petersburg
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Emperor
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_I_of_Russia" title="Peter I of Russia">
        Peter the Great
       </a>
       , inspired and advised by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gottfried_Leibniz" title="Gottfried Leibniz">
        Gottfried Leibniz
       </a>
       , founded the Academy in
       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">
        Saint Petersburg
       </a>
       ; the Senate
       <a href="/wiki/Decree" title="Decree">
        decree
       </a>
       of February 8 (January 28 old style), 1724 implemented the establishment.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NTRS_13-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NTRS-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Originally called
       <i>
        The Saint Petersburg Academy of Sciences
       </i>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
        Russian
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="ru">
        <a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9F%D0%B5%D1%82%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B1%D1%83%D1%80%D0%B3%D1%81%D0%BA%D0%B0%D1%8F_%D0%90%D0%BA%D0%B0%D0%B4%D0%B5%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%8F_%D0%BD%D0%B0%D1%83%D0%BA" title="ru:Петербургская Академия наук">
         Петербургская Академия наук
        </a>
       </span>
       ), the organization went under various names over the years, becoming
       <i>
        The Imperial Academy of Sciences and Arts
       </i>
       (Императорская Академия наук и художеств; 1747–1803),
       <i>
        The Imperial Academy of Sciences
       </i>
       (Императорская Академия Наук; 1803—1836), and finally,
       <i>
        The Imperial Saint Petersburg Academy of Sciences
       </i>
       (Императорская Санкт-Петербургская Академия Наук, from 1836 and until the end of the
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">
        empire
       </a>
       in 1917).
      </p>
      <p>
       Foreign scholars invited to work at the academy included the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mathematicians" title="Mathematicians">
        mathematicians
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Leonhard_Euler" title="Leonhard Euler">
        Leonhard Euler
       </a>
       (1707-1783),
       <a href="/wiki/Anders_Johan_Lexell" title="Anders Johan Lexell">
        Anders Johan Lexell
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Christian_Goldbach" title="Christian Goldbach">
        Christian Goldbach
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Georg_Bernhard_Bilfinger" title="Georg Bernhard Bilfinger">
        Georg Bernhard Bilfinger
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicholas_II_Bernoulli" title="Nicholas II Bernoulli">
        Nicholas Bernoulli
       </a>
       (1695-1726) and
       <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bernoulli" title="Daniel Bernoulli">
        Daniel Bernoulli
       </a>
       (1700-1782), botanist
       <a href="/wiki/Johann_Georg_Gmelin" title="Johann Georg Gmelin">
        Johann Georg Gmelin
       </a>
       , embryologists
       <a href="/wiki/Caspar_Friedrich_Wolff" title="Caspar Friedrich Wolff">
        Caspar Friedrich Wolff
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">
        astronomer
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Geographer" title="Geographer">
        geographer
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Joseph-Nicolas_Delisle" title="Joseph-Nicolas Delisle">
        Joseph-Nicolas Delisle
       </a>
       , physicist
       <a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9A%D1%80%D0%B0%D1%84%D1%82,_%D0%93%D0%B5%D0%BE%D1%80%D0%B3_%D0%92%D0%BE%D0%BB%D1%8C%D1%84%D0%B3%D0%B0%D0%BD%D0%B3" title="ru:Крафт, Георг Вольфганг">
        Georg Wolfgang Kraft
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Historian" title="Historian">
        historian
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Friedrich_M%C3%BCller" title="Gerhard Friedrich Müller">
        Gerhard Friedrich Müller
       </a>
       and English
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomer_Royal" title="Astronomer Royal">
        Astronomer Royal
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Nevil_Maskelyne" title="Nevil Maskelyne">
        Nevil Maskelyne
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (1732-1811).
      </p>
      <p>
       Expeditions to explore remote parts of the country had Academy scientists as their leaders or most active participants. These included
       <a href="/wiki/Vitus_Bering" title="Vitus Bering">
        Vitus Bering
       </a>
       's Second
       <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Peninsula" title="Kamchatka Peninsula">
        Kamchatka
       </a>
       Expedition of 1733–1743, expeditions to observe the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1761_transit_of_Venus" title="1761 transit of Venus">
        1769 transit of Venus
       </a>
       from eight locations in
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">
        Russian Empire
       </a>
       , and the expeditions of
       <a href="/wiki/Peter_Simon_Pallas" title="Peter Simon Pallas">
        Peter Simon Pallas
       </a>
       (1741-1811) to
       <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">
        Siberia
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       A separate organization, called the
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Academy" title="Russian Academy">
        Russian Academy
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
        Russian
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="ru">
        Академия Российская
       </span>
       ), was created in 1783 to work on the study of the
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
        Russian language
       </a>
       . Presided over by Princess
       <a href="/wiki/Yekaterina_Vorontsova-Dashkova" title="Yekaterina Vorontsova-Dashkova">
        Yekaterina Dashkova
       </a>
       (who at the same time was the Director of the Imperial Academy of Arts and Sciences, i.e., the country's "main" academy), the Russian Academy was engaged in compiling the six-volume
       <i>
        Academic Dictionary of the Russian Language
       </i>
       (1789–1794). The Russian Academy was merged into the Imperial Saint Petersburg Academy of Sciences in 1841.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Academy_of_Sciences_of_the_USSR">
        The Academy of Sciences of the USSR
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The Academy of Sciences of the USSR">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Shortly after the
       <a href="/wiki/October_Revolution" title="October Revolution">
        October Revolution
       </a>
       , in December 1917,
       <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Oldenburg" title="Sergey Oldenburg">
        Sergey Fedorovich Oldenburg
       </a>
       , a leading
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnographer" title="Ethnographer">
        ethnographer
       </a>
       and political activist in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kadet" title="Kadet">
        Kadet
       </a>
       party, met with
       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">
        Vladimir Lenin
       </a>
       to discuss the future of the Academy. They agreed that the expertise of the Academy would be applied to addressing questions of state construction, while in return the Soviet regime would give the Academy financial and political support.
      </p>
      <p>
       The most important activities of the Academy in the 1920s included an investigation of the large
       <a href="/wiki/Kursk_Magnetic_Anomaly" title="Kursk Magnetic Anomaly">
        Kursk Magnetic Anomaly
       </a>
       , of the minerals in the
       <a href="/wiki/Kola_Peninsula" title="Kola Peninsula">
        Kola Peninsula
       </a>
       , and participation in the
       <a href="/wiki/GOELRO_plan" title="GOELRO plan">
        GOELRO plan
       </a>
       targeted electrification of the whole country. In these years, many research institutons were established, and the number of scientists became four times larger than in 1917. In 1925 the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_government" title="Soviet government">
        Soviet government
       </a>
       recognized the Russian Academy of Sciences as the "highest all-Union scientific institution" and renamed it the Academy of Sciences of the USSR.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1934 the Academy headquarters moved from
       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">
        Leningrad
       </a>
       to the capital,
       <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">
        Moscow
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Josef_Stalin" title="Josef Stalin">
        Stalin
       </a>
       years are marked with a rapid
       <a href="/wiki/Industrialisation" title="Industrialisation">
        industrialisation
       </a>
       of the Soviet Union for which a great deal of research, mainly in the technical fields, has been done. However, on the other hand, in these very times, many scientists underwent
       <a href="/wiki/Ideological_repression_in_the_Soviet_Union#Ideological_repression_in_science" title="Ideological repression in the Soviet Union">
        repressions
       </a>
       from ideological reasons.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the years of the
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        Second World War
       </a>
       , the Soviet Academy of Sciences made a big contribution to a development of modern weapons –
       <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">
        tanks
       </a>
       (new series of
       <a href="/wiki/T-34" title="T-34">
        T-34
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Airplane" title="Airplane">
        airplanes
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Degaussing" title="Degaussing">
        degaussing
       </a>
       the ships (for protection against the
       <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">
        naval mines
       </a>
       ) etc. – and therefore to victory of the USSR over the
       <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">
        Nazi Germany
       </a>
       . During and after the war, the Academy was involved in the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_atomic_bomb_project" title="Soviet atomic bomb project">
        Soviet atomic bomb project
       </a>
       ; due to its success and other achievements in military techniques, the USSR became one of the
       <a href="/wiki/Superpower" title="Superpower">
        superpowers
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">
        Cold War
       </a>
       era.
      </p>
      <p>
       At the end-1940th, the Academy consisted of eight divisions (Physico-Mathematical Science, Chemical Sciences, Geological-Geographical Sciences, Biological Science, Technical Science, History and Philosophy, Economics and Law, Literature and Languages); three committees (one for coordinating the scientific work of the Academies of the Republics, one for scientific and technical propaganda, and one for editorial and publications), two commissions (for publishing popular scientific literature, and for museums and archives), a laboratory for scientific photography and cinematography and Academy of Science Press departments external to the divisions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Academy of Sciences of the USSR helped to establish national Academies of Sciences in all Soviet republics (with the exception of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_SFSR" title="Russian SFSR">
        Russian SFSR
       </a>
       ), in many cases delegating prominent scientists to live and work in other republics. In the case of the Ukraine, its academy was formed by the local Ukrainian scientists and prior to occupation of the
       <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_People%27s_Republic" title="Ukrainian People's Republic">
        Ukrainian People's Republic
       </a>
       by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolshevik" title="Bolshevik">
        Bolsheviks
       </a>
       . These academies were:

      <p>
       Among the most important achievements of the Academy of the second half of the XX century, there is, first of all, the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_space_program" title="Soviet space program">
        Soviet space program
       </a>
       . In 1957 the
       <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">
        first satellite
       </a>
       was launched, in 1961
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yury_Gagarin" title="Yury Gagarin">
        Yury Gagarin
       </a>
       became the first person in space, and in 1971 the first
       <a href="/wiki/Salyut_1" title="Salyut 1">
        space station Salyut 1
       </a>
       began its operation. Substantial discoveries were also made in the nuclear branch and in other fields of physics. Furthermore, the Academy participated in opening new universities or new study programs in the already existed universities, whose best absolvents started their career at the research institutes of the Academy.
      </p>
      <p>
       Generally, the Soviet period was the most fruitful in the history of the Russian (Soviet, at these times) Academy of Sciences and is now recalled with nostalgy by many Russian scientists.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Soviet_period_of_the_Academy">
        Post-Soviet period of the Academy
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Post-Soviet period of the Academy">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       After the
       <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">
        collapse of the Soviet Union
       </a>
       , by
       <a href="/wiki/Decree_of_the_President_of_Russia" title="Decree of the President of Russia">
        decree
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/President_of_Russia" title="President of Russia">
        President of Russia
       </a>
       of December 2, 1991, the academy again became the
       <i>
        Russian Academy of Sciences
       </i>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       inheriting all facilities of the USSR Academy of Sciences in the territory of the Russian Federation.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Economic_history_of_the_Russian_Federation" title="Economic history of the Russian Federation">
        crisis of the 1990s
       </a>
       in the post-Soviet Russia and a consequent drastic reduction of the state support for science have forced many scientists to leave Russia for Europe, Israel or United States. Some excellent university graduates who could become promising researchers, preferred to switch to other activities, predominately in commerce. The Russian Academy has practically lost a generation of people born from mid-1960s to mid-1970s, this age category is now underrepresented in all research institutes.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the 2000s, the situation in the Russian science and technology has improved, the government announced a
       <a href="/wiki/Medvedev_modernisation_programme" title="Medvedev modernisation programme">
        modernization campaign
       </a>
       . Nevertheless, according to the Russian Academy of Sciences, total R&amp;D spending in 2013 still hovered about 40% below the pre-crisis 1990 levels.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-postsoviet_16-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-postsoviet-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Furthermore, a lack of competition, decayed infrastructure and continuing, though slightly reduced,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brain_drain" title="Brain drain">
        brain drain
       </a>
       play their part.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span id="Reforms_.28since_2013.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reforms_(since_2013)">
        Reforms (since 2013)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reforms (since 2013)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       On June 28, 2013, the Russian Government unexpectedly announced a draft law presuming a dissolution of the RAS and creation of a new "public-governmental" organization with the same name. The buildings and other property of the Academy were supposed to be taken under control of a government-established
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Federal_Agency_for_Scientific_Organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Federal Agency for Scientific Organizations (page does not exist)">
        Federal Agency for Scientific Organizations
       </a>
       (FASO Russia). The declared idea was to enable scientists to concentrate exclusively on research activities without worrying about housing-maintenance services or administrative things. The reform was allegedly authored by
       <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Kovalchuk" title="Mikhail Kovalchuk">
        Mikhail Kovalchuk
       </a>
       , brother of
       <a href="/wiki/Yury_Kovalchuk" title="Yury Kovalchuk">
        Yury Kovalchuk
       </a>
       , known as
       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">
        Vladimir Putin
       </a>
       's personal banker.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The draft law, which, in its initial form, would have fundamentally changed the system of science organization in Russia, provoked conflicts with the academic circles and strong refutation by many prominent individuals.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A large group of the RAS members signalized their intention not to join the new academy if the reform is run as planned in the draft.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The world's leading scientists (including
       <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Deligne" title="Pierre Deligne">
        Pierre Deligne
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_Atiyah" title="Michael Atiyah">
        Michael Atiyah
       </a>
       , Mumford, and others) have written open letters which referred to the planned reform of the "shocking" and even "criminal".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In this situation, the draft was softened in some details, e.g. there remained no words about “dissolution” in the text, — and approved on September 27, 2013.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now, all the academy institutions are already under jurisdiction of the FASO; this agency was empowered to “evaluate”, relying on its own criteria, an efficiency of the institutions and rearrange ineffective ones (this point is felt dangerous by many scientists
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ). Furthermore, according to the law, the two other Russian national academies —
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Russian_Academy_of_Agricultural_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Russian Academy of Agricultural Sciences (page does not exist)">
        for Agriculture
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Academy_of_Medical_Sciences" title="Russian Academy of Medical Sciences">
        for Medicine
       </a>
       — were fused to the RAS as its new specialized scientific divisions.
      </p>
      <p>
       During the years 2014—2017 there occurred no large-scale protest actions, but, in general, a scientific community has not supported the launched reforms. Sometimes these reforms are interpreted as nothing else than a redistribution of real estate. In 2017, when the new presidium of the Academy was being elected, the candidates for presidency negatively estimated the intermediate results of the reforms. The elected RAS president
       <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Sergeev_(physicist)" title="Alexander Sergeev (physicist)">
        Alexander Sergeev
       </a>
       claimed a change of the legal status of the Russian Academy of Sciences as one of the urgent problems. Nevertheless, he is ready to establish working relationships with the state authorities at various levels, including the FASO.
      </p>
