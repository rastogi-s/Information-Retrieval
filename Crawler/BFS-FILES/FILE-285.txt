      <p>
       Solar
       <a href="/wiki/Saros_(astronomy)" title="Saros (astronomy)">
        saros
       </a>
       145 is an
       <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_cycle" title="Eclipse cycle">
        eclipse cycle
       </a>
       with 77
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipses
       </a>
       repeating every 18 years, 11 days, 8 hours. It is currently a young cycle producing total eclipses less than 3 minutes in length. The longest duration eclipse in the cycle will be member 50 at 7 minutes and 12 seconds in length after which the durations of eclipses will decrease until the end of the cycle. In its
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse#Terminology_for_central_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        central phase
       </a>
       it will produce mainly total eclipses (41 of 43 central eclipses).
      </p>
