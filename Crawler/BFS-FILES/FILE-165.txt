      <p>
       The
       <b>
        synchronous grid of Continental Europe
       </b>
       (also known as
       <b>
        Continental Synchronous Area
       </b>
       ; formerly known as the
       <b>
        UCTE grid
       </b>
       ) is the largest
       <a href="/wiki/Wide_area_synchronous_grid" title="Wide area synchronous grid">
        synchronous electrical grid
       </a>
       (by connected power) in the world. It is interconnected as a single phase-locked
       <span class="nowrap">
        50 Hz
       </span>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mains_frequency" title="Mains frequency">
        mains frequency
       </a>
       electricity grid that supplies over
       <span class="nowrap">
        400 million
       </span>
       customers in
       <span class="nowrap">
        24 countries
       </span>
       , including most of the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
        European Union
       </a>
       . In 2009,
       <span class="nowrap">
        667
        <a href="/wiki/Watt#Multiples" title="Watt">
         GW
        </a>
       </span>
       of production capacity was connected to the grid, providing approximately
       <span class="nowrap">
        80 GW
       </span>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Operating_reserve" title="Operating reserve">
        operating reserve
       </a>
       margin.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Transmission_system_operator" title="Transmission system operator">
        transmission system operators
       </a>
       operating this grid formed the Union for the Coordination of Transmission of Electricity (UCTE), now part of the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Network_of_Transmission_System_Operators_for_Electricity" title="European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity">
        European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity
       </a>
       (ENTSO-E).
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Area">
        Area
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchronous_grid_of_Continental_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Area">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The synchronous grid of
       <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">
        Continental Europe
       </a>
       covers territory of the ENTSO-E Continental Europe regional group and some neighboring countries not involved in the ENTSO-E. The synchronous grid includes part or all of
       <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">
        Austria
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">
        Belgium
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">
        Bosnia and Herzegovina
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">
        Bulgaria
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">
        Croatia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">
        Czech Republic
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">
        Denmark
       </a>
       <small>
        (western part)
       </small>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
        Germany
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">
        Greece
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">
        Hungary
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">
        Luxembourg
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">
        Macedonia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">
        Montenegro
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">
        Netherlands
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
        Poland
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
        Portugal
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">
        Romania
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">
        Serbia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">
        Slovakia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">
        Slovenia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
        Switzerland
       </a>
       as a members of the ENTSO-E Continental Europe regional group. In addition to the ENTSO-E members, the small west electricity island of
       <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">
        Ukraine
       </a>
       is synchronized with the grid of Continental Europe.
       <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">
        Albania
       </a>
       is operating the national grid synchronously with the synchronous grid of Continental Europe. The grids of
       <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">
        Morocco
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">
        Algeria
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">
        Tunisia
       </a>
       are synchronized with the European grid through the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gibraltar_AC_link&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gibraltar AC link (page does not exist)">
        Gibraltar AC link
       </a>
       and form the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=South_West_Mediterranean_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="South West Mediterranean Block (page does not exist)">
        SWMB
       </a>
       . In April 2015, the grid of
       <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">
        Turkey
       </a>
       was synchronized with the European grid.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Although synchronous, some countries operate in a near island mode, with low connectivity to other countries. The
       <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">
        European Commission
       </a>
       considers high connectivity to be beneficial,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eil_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-eil-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and has listed several interconnection projects as "Projects of Common Interest".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, the national grids must also be upgraded to handle increased power flows if the values of a free energy market are to be realised in the EU.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Electricity_island_networks">
        Electricity island networks
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchronous_grid_of_Continental_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Electricity island networks">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Grid_(UK)" title="National Grid (UK)">
        British grid
       </a>
       is not synchronized with the Continental Europe frequency, but it is inter-connected via the
       <a href="/wiki/HVDC_Cross-Channel" title="HVDC Cross-Channel">
        HVDC Cross-Channel
       </a>
       link and
       <a href="/wiki/BritNed" title="BritNed">
        BritNed
       </a>
       to an electricity interconnection level of 6%.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eil_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-eil-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Similarly, the Nordic regional group of ENTSO-E (former NORDEL), composed of Norway, Sweden, Finland and the eastern part of Denmark (
       <a href="/wiki/Zealand" title="Zealand">
        Zealand
       </a>
       with islands and
       <a href="/wiki/Bornholm" title="Bornholm">
        Bornholm
       </a>
       ), is not synchronized with the Continental Europe, but has a number of non-synchronous DC connections with the Continental Europe grid.
       <a href="/wiki/Gotland" title="Gotland">
        Gotland
       </a>
       is not synchronized with the Swedish mainland, as it is connected by
       <a href="/wiki/HVDC_Gotland" title="HVDC Gotland">
        HVDC
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The networks of Ireland and
       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">
        Northern Ireland
       </a>
       form the ENTSO-E Irish regional group, which is not yet interconnected with the Continental Europe grid, but has interconnection with the British network through the
       <a href="/wiki/HVDC_Moyle" title="HVDC Moyle">
        Moyle Interconnector
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/East%E2%80%93West_Interconnector" title="East–West Interconnector">
        East–West Interconnector
       </a>
       . Similarly, the network of ENTSO-E Baltic regional group, composed of Lithuania, Latvia and Estonia, currently part of the
       <a href="/wiki/IPS/UPS" title="IPS/UPS">
        IPS/UPS
       </a>
       system, is interconnected with the Nordic grid through the
       <a href="/wiki/Estlink" title="Estlink">
        Estlink
       </a>
       cables and the
       <a href="/wiki/NordBalt" title="NordBalt">
        NordBalt
       </a>
       cable, at an electricity interconnection level of 10%.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eil_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-eil-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Baltics are connected with the Continental Europe grid through the
       <a href="/wiki/LitPol_Link" title="LitPol Link">
        Lithuania–Poland interconnection
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The networks of Iceland and Cyprus, are not yet interconnected with the other grids. Malta is connected up to 35% via the Malta-Sicily interconnector, commissioned in 2015
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Future_extension_plans">
        Future extension plans
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Synchronous_grid_of_Continental_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Future extension plans">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       UCTE/ENTSO-E and the corresponding partner TSO companies are planning the following extensions of the grid and synchronous frequency:
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        UCTE-Ukraine/Moldova, to disconnect only these networks from the IPS/UPS system and synchronously interconnecting them to the synchronized grid of Central Europe
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ExtensionsUCTE_6-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-ExtensionsUCTE-6">
          [6]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        Tunisia-Libya synchronous connection, that would extend the Continental Europe frequency to Libya, Egypt, Jordan, Syria and Lebanon (the latter five countries constitute the
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=South_East_Mediterranean_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="South East Mediterranean Block (page does not exist)">
         SEMB
        </a>
        of the
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MedRing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MedRing (page does not exist)">
         Mediterranean Electricity Ring
        </a>
        project.
       </li>
       <li>
        Syria-Turkey interconnection, completing the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">
         Mediterranean
        </a>
        ring.
        <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.auptde.org/NewSite/UploadFiles/Activityfile/155.ppt#293,8" rel="nofollow">
         [1]
        </a>
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       Studies are made on the synchronous connection between the Continental Europe and IPS/UPS networks
       <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ucte-ipsups.org/" rel="nofollow">
        [2]
       </a>
       . Separately, there is another option concerning Ukraine and Moldova, and the ENTSO-E Baltic regional group – to disconnect only these networks from the IPS/UPS system and synchronously interconnecting them to the synchronized grid of Central Europe. Further possibilities are extension to the Nordic regional group, the Turkey-Iraq interconnection, and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DESERTEC" title="DESERTEC">
        DESERTEC
       </a>
       concept for interconnection with other countries in the Middle East. In the past Armenia and Turkmenistan networks were part of the Soviet unified system, but currently they are connected to the Iran grid.
      </p>
