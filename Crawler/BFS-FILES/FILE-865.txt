      <p>
       <b>
        Lizard
       </b>
       (sometimes referred to as
       <i>
        The Lizard
       </i>
       ) is a village on
       <a href="/wiki/The_Lizard" title="The Lizard">
        the Lizard peninsula
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">
        Cornwall
       </a>
       , England, United Kingdom. It is situated about ten miles (16 km) south of
       <a href="/wiki/Helston" title="Helston">
        Helston
       </a>
       , and is mainland
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">
        Britain
       </a>
       's most southerly settlement.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Lizard is a tourist centre and its large
       <a href="/wiki/Village_green" title="Village green">
        village green
       </a>
       is surrounded by cafes and gift shops.
      </p>
      <p>
       The name derives from the
       <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">
        Cornish
       </a>
       <i>
        Lis
       </i>
       for 'place' and
       <i>
        Ard
       </i>
       for 'high'. The village is in the
       <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">
        civil parish
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Landewednack" title="Landewednack">
        Landewednack
       </a>
       , the most southerly parish on the British mainland.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genuki_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-genuki-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The parish church is dedicated to St
       <a href="/wiki/Winwaloe" title="Winwaloe">
        Winwallow
       </a>
       and is the most southerly in mainland
       <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
        Britain
       </a>
       . It is built of local
       <a href="/wiki/Serpentinite" title="Serpentinite">
        Serpentinite
       </a>
       stone (see
       <a href="/wiki/Lizard_complex" title="Lizard complex">
        Lizard complex
       </a>
       for more information on the peninsula's geology) and is situated in the hamlet of Landewednack, now a suburb of Lizard village.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-genuki_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-genuki-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Lizard_Lighthouse" title="Lizard Lighthouse">
        Lizard Lighthouse
       </a>
       , the oldest mainland light in Cornwall, is situated half-a-mile (800 m) south of the village. It has twin towers and was erected in 1752 although there had been a light here since 1619.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Lizard_Lifeboat_Station" title="The Lizard Lifeboat Station">
        The Lizard Lifeboat Station
       </a>
       , operated by the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Lifeboat_Institution" title="Royal National Lifeboat Institution">
        RNLI
       </a>
       , is situated at Kilcobben Cove half-a-mile east of the village.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armada" title="Spanish Armada">
        Spanish Armada
       </a>
       was first spotted from near Lizard village in 1588.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">

       </sup>
      </p>
