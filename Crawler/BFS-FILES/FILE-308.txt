      <p>
       This is a
       <b>
        list of solar eclipses visible from the United Kingdom
       </b>
       (i.e. the islands of
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">
        Great Britain
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">
        Ireland
       </a>
       ) between
       <b>
        AD 1000 – AD 2091
       </b>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       A
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipse
       </a>
       occurs when the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       passes between
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       , thereby totally or partially obscuring Earth's view of the Sun. Below is a complete list of total and annular eclipses visible anywhere within the modern extent of the United Kingdom between AD 1000 and AD 2090
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and a description of forthcoming partial solar eclipses visible in Britain in the next fifteen years or so.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       For a complete list of solar eclipses visible from the United Kingdom between AD 1501 and AD 2500, see the Journal of the British Astronomical Association, February 2001.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span id="12th_century_.28AD_1101_.E2.80.93_1200.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="12th_century_(AD_1101_–_1200)">
        12th century (AD 1101 – 1200)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_solar_eclipses_visible_from_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 12th century (AD 1101 – 1200)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        2 August 1133
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-4">
          [4]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <ul>
         <li>
          "
          <a href="/wiki/Henry_I_of_England" title="Henry I of England">
           King Henry
          </a>
          's Eclipse": A total eclipse, recorded in the
          <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_Chronicle" title="Peterborough Chronicle">
           Peterborough Chronicle
          </a>
          (under 1135 due to the vagaries of the dating system in use
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
           <a href="#cite_note-5">
            [5]
           </a>
          </sup>
          ):
          <i>
           and the next day, as he lay asleep on ship, the day darkened over all lands, and the Sun was all **
          </i>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        20 March 1140
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
         <a href="#cite_note-6">
          [6]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <ul>
         <li>
          A total eclipse, recorded by
          <a href="/wiki/William_of_Malmesbury" title="William of Malmesbury">
           William of Malmesbury
          </a>
          in his
          <i>
           <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Historia_Novella&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Historia Novella (page does not exist)">
            Historia Novella
           </a>
          </i>
          . In his opinion this was a sign which foretold the capture of
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Stephen_of_England" title="King Stephen of England">
           King Stephen
          </a>
          in the
          <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lincoln_(1141)" title="Battle of Lincoln (1141)">
           Battle of Lincoln
          </a>
          in 1141. This is the
          <a href="/wiki/Lent" title="Lent">
           Lenten
          </a>
          eclipse also reported in the
          <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_Chronicle" title="Peterborough Chronicle">
           Peterborough Chronicle
          </a>
          as being on the thirteenth day before the
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kalends" title="Kalends">
           kalends
          </a>
          of April: '
          <i>
           After this, during Lent, the sun and the day darkened about the noon-tide of the day, when men were eating; and they lighted candles to eat by. That was the thirteenth day before the kalends of April. Men were greatly wonderstricken
          </i>
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
           <a href="#cite_note-7">
            [7]
           </a>
          </sup>
          Totality was experienced at about 3.00 pm at the centre line of the eclipse (near Derby).
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>

       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_solar_eclipses_visible_from_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 20th century (AD 1901 – 2000)">

      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_January_24,_1925" title="Solar eclipse of January 24, 1925">
         24 January 1925
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total Solar Eclipse: A short duration total eclipse at
          <a href="/wiki/Sunset" title="Sunset">
           sunset
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
           British
          </a>
          waters to the north of the
          <a href="/wiki/Hebrides" title="Hebrides">
           Hebrides
          </a>
          . Although it nowhere touched land, the path of totality ran very close to several outlying Scottish islands, including
          <a href="/wiki/St_Kilda,_Scotland" title="St Kilda, Scotland">
           St Kilda
          </a>
          ; the islet of
          <a href="/wiki/Sula_Sgeir" title="Sula Sgeir">

          </a>

         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_June_29,_1927" title="Solar eclipse of June 29, 1927">
         29 June 1927
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total Solar Eclipse: A mere 24 seconds of totality in the early morning, along a narrow track from
          <a href="/wiki/North_Wales" title="North Wales">
           North Wales
          </a>
          , through
          <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">
           Lancashire
          </a>
          to the English north-east coast, but weather was very poor with cloud and high winds. However the
          <a href="/wiki/Astronomer_Royal" title="Astronomer Royal">
           Astronomer Royal
          </a>
          's expedition to
          <a href="/wiki/Giggleswick" title="Giggleswick">
           Giggleswick
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">
           North Yorkshire
          </a>
          was amongst the few to catch sight of totality.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_June_30,_1954" title="Solar eclipse of June 30, 1954">
         30 June 1954
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total Solar Eclipse at
          <a href="/wiki/Unst" title="Unst">
           Unst
          </a>
          in the
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shetland_Islands" title="Shetland Islands">
           Shetland Islands
          </a>
          , although the centre line was north of British territorial waters. A large partial eclipse was widely observed over the whole of the UK.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_October_2,_1959" title="Solar eclipse of October 2, 1959">
         2 October 1959
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          A partial eclipse visible over the whole of the United Kingdom ranging from approximately 20% in Northern Scotland to approximately 40% in South West Cornwall.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_February_15,_1961" title="Solar eclipse of February 15, 1961">
         15 February 1961
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The United Kingdom was greeted at dawn with a large portion of the Sun covered with maximum eclipse being approximately on the horizon ranging from 85% in Northern Scotland to between 92% and 95% in Southern England.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>

      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_October_12,_1996" title="Solar eclipse of October 12, 1996">
         12 October 1996
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          A partial solar eclipse which covered 60% of the Sun over the
          <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">
           British Isles
          </a>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_August_11,_1999" title="Solar eclipse of August 11, 1999">
         11 August 1999
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total Solar Eclipse over
          <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">
           Cornwall
          </a>
          and part of south
          <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">
           Devon
          </a>
          , partial over the rest of the United Kingdom. Totality was observable from
          <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">
           English Channel
          </a>
          and the island of
          <a href="/wiki/Alderney" title="Alderney">
           Alderney
          </a>
          in the
          <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">
           Channel Islands
          </a>
          , but was almost universally clouded out on the British mainland. The clouds did clear in the
          <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">
           Newquay
          </a>
          area, though, allowing observation of full totality. A large partial eclipse was visible in the south-east of England and south Wales. Observers in various places noted birds falling silent, daylight colours turning to grey, and temperatures falling, augmented by a passing wisp of cloud at the moment of peak eclipse.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>

      <h2>
       <span id="22nd_century_.28AD_2101_.E2.80.93_2200.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="22nd_century_(AD_2101_–_2200)">
        22nd century (AD 2101 – 2200)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_solar_eclipses_visible_from_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 22nd century (AD 2101 – 2200)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_June_3,_2133&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Solar eclipse of June 3, 2133 (page does not exist)">
         3 June 2133
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total eclipse over the far north-west of Scotland, including the Isles of Lewis, Harris, northern Skye and Shetland, partial eclipse elsewhere. Maximum duration will be 3 minutes and 36 seconds.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_October_7,_2135" title="Solar eclipse of October 7, 2135">
         7 October 2135
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total eclipse over central and southern Scotland and north-east England. The centre line runs from the islands of Tiree and Mull, north of Glasgow and south of Edinburgh, through Livingston to Seahouses in Northumberland. Maximum duration will be 4 minutes and 50 seconds.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_June_14,_2151&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Solar eclipse of June 14, 2151 (page does not exist)">
         14 June 2151
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total eclipse over south-west Scotland, Northern Ireland, the
          <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">
           Isle of Man
          </a>
          , north, central and eastern England and north-east Wales. The centre line runs from the southern tip of the Kintyre peninsula in Argyll and Bute, through Lancashire, West and South Yorkshire, Lincolnshire and the Wash to the Suffolk coast. Maximum duration will be 3 minutes and 48 seconds.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_June_4,_2160&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Solar eclipse of June 4, 2160 (page does not exist)">
         4 June 2160
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total eclipse over Land's End in Cornwall and the Scilly Isles, as well as the far southwestern tip of
          <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">
           Ireland
          </a>
          , partial eclipse elsewhere. Maximum duration will be 2 minutes and 58 seconds.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_November_8,_2189&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Solar eclipse of November 8, 2189 (page does not exist)">
         8 November 2189
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total eclipse over Cornwall and south Devon and the Channel Islands, as well as southwest Ireland, partial eclipse elsewhere. The centre line runs from Tralee, through Truro and passing just to the south of Jersey. Maximum duration will be 4 minutes and 10 seconds.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_eclipse_of_April_14,_2200&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Solar eclipse of April 14, 2200 (page does not exist)">
         14 April 2200
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Total eclipse over Northern Ireland and northern England. The centre line runs from Enniskillen, through Armagh, Downpatrick, the Isle of Man, Morecambe Bay and North Yorkshire to the East coast between Bridlington and Hornsea. Maximum duration will be 1 minute and 23 seconds.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
