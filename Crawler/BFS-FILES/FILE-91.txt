      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Kızılırmak
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">
        Turkish
       </a>
       for "Red River"), also known as the
       <b>
        Halys River
       </b>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language" title="Ancient Greek language">
        Ancient Greek
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">
        Ἅλυς
       </span>
       ), is the longest river entirely within
       <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">
        Turkey
       </a>
       . It is a source of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric_power" title="Hydroelectric power">
        hydroelectric power
       </a>
       and is not used for navigation.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
        Geography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C4%B1z%C4%B1l%C4%B1rmak_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Kızılırmak flows for a total of 1,355 kilometres (842 mi),
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       rising in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Anatolia" title="Eastern Anatolia">
        Eastern Anatolia
       </a>
       around

           <span class="longitude">
            38°18′E
           </span>
          </span>

          ﻿ / ﻿
         </span>
         <span class="geo-default">
          <span class="vcard">
           <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
            39.8°N 38.3°E
           </span>
           <span style="display:none">

            <span class="fn org">

            )
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       , flowing first to the west and southwest until
       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">
        <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=K%C4%B1z%C4%B1l%C4%B1rmak_River&amp;params=38.7_N_34.8_E_region:TK_type:river">
         <span class="geo-nondefault">

        delta

        Derbent

      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C4%B1z%C4%B1l%C4%B1rmak_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Hittites" title="Hittites">
        Hittites
       </a>
       called it the Maraššantiya. It formed the western boundary of Hatti, the core land of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hittite_empire" title="Hittite empire">
        Hittite empire
       </a>
       . In
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classical_Antiquity" title="Classical Antiquity">
        Classical Antiquity
       </a>
       , it was the boundary between
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">
        Asia Minor
       </a>
       and the rest of
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       , and also the boundary between
       <a href="/wiki/Pontus_(region)" title="Pontus (region)">
        Pontos
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Paphlagonia" title="Paphlagonia">
        Paphlagonia
       </a>
       . As the site of the Battle of Halys or
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Eclipse" title="Battle of the Eclipse">
        Battle of the Eclipse
       </a>
       on May 28, 585 BC,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       it was the border between
       <a href="/wiki/Lydia" title="Lydia">
        Lydia
       </a>
       to the west and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Median_Empire" title="Median Empire">
        Media
       </a>
       to the east until
       <a href="/wiki/Croesus" title="Croesus">
        Croesus
       </a>
       of Lydia crossed it to attack
       <a href="/wiki/Cyrus_the_Great" title="Cyrus the Great">
        Cyrus the Great
       </a>
       in 547 BC. He was defeated and Persia expanded to the
       <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">
        Aegean Sea
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">
        Agriculture
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C4%B1z%C4%B1l%C4%B1rmak_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Agriculture">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The river water is used to grow rice and in some areas
       <a href="/wiki/Water_buffalo" title="Water buffalo">
        water buffalo
       </a>
       are kept.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">
        In popular culture
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=K%C4%B1z%C4%B1l%C4%B1rmak_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In popular culture">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The historical fiction manga
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Red_River_(manga)" title="Red River (manga)">
         Red River
        </a>
       </i>
       is named after and largely set in the Kızılırmak basin, during the age of the Hittites.
      </p>
