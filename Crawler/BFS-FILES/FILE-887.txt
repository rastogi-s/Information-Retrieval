      <p>
       <b>
        Lake Balaton
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">
        German
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="de" xml:lang="de">
         Plattensee
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">
        freshwater
       </a>
       lake in the
       <a href="/wiki/Transdanubia" title="Transdanubia">
        Transdanubian
       </a>
       region of
       <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">
        Hungary
       </a>
       . It is the
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_lakes_of_Europe" title="List of largest lakes of Europe">
        largest lake
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">
        Central Europe
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and one of the region's foremost tourist destinations. The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zala_River" title="Zala River">
        Zala River
       </a>
       provides the largest inflow of water to the lake, and the canalised
       <a href="/wiki/Si%C3%B3" title="Sió">
        Sió
       </a>
       is the only outflow.
      </p>
      <p>
       The mountainous region of the northern shore is known both for its
       <a href="/wiki/Transdanubia#History" title="Transdanubia">
        historic
       </a>
       character and as a major
       <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_wine" title="Hungarian wine">
        wine region
       </a>
       , while the flat southern shore is known for its
       <a href="/wiki/Resort_town" title="Resort town">
        resort towns
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Balatonf%C3%BCred" title="Balatonfüred">
        Balatonfüred
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9v%C3%ADz" title="Hévíz">
        Hévíz
       </a>
       developed early as resorts for the wealthy, but it was not until the late 19th century when landowners, ruined by
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Phylloxera" title="Phylloxera">
         Phylloxera
        </a>
       </i>
       attacking their
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grape_vine" title="Grape vine">
        grape vines
       </a>
       , began building summer homes to rent out to the burgeoning middle classes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
        Name
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [

      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">
        Hungarian
       </a>
       , the lake is known simply as
       <i>
        Balaton
       </i>
       . It was called
       <i>
        lacus Pelsodis
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        Pelso
       </i>
       by the
       <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">
        Romans
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The name is
       <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_languages" title="Indo-European languages">
        Indo-European
       </a>
       in origin (cf. Czech
       <i>
        pleso
       </i>
       ‘sinkhole, deep end of a lake’), later replaced by the
       <a href="/wiki/Slavic_languages" title="Slavic languages">
        Slavic
       </a>
       *
       <i>
        bolto
       </i>
       (Czech and Slovak
       <i>
        bláto
       </i>
       , Polish "błoto") meaning 'mud, swamp' (from earlier
       <a href="/wiki/Proto-Slavic" title="Proto-Slavic">
        Proto-Slavic
       </a>
       <i>
        boltьno
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Slovene_language" title="Slovene language">
        Slovene
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="sl" xml:lang="sl">
         Blatno jezero
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">
        Slovak
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="sk" xml:lang="sk">
         Blatenské jazero
        </span>
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ).
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">
        Slavic
       </a>
       prince
       <a href="/wiki/Pribina" title="Pribina">
        Pribina
       </a>
       began to build in January 846 a fortress as his seat of power and several churches in the region of Lake Balaton, in a territory of modern
       <a href="/wiki/Zalav%C3%A1r" title="Zalavár">
        Zalavár
       </a>
       surrounded by forests and swamps along the river
       <a href="/wiki/Zala_(river)" title="Zala (river)">
        Zala
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartl_19_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Bartl_19-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Róna-Tas_243_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Róna-Tas_243-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldberg_85_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Goldberg_85-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       His well fortified castle and capital of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balaton_Principality" title="Balaton Principality">
        Balaton Principality
       </a>
       that became known as
       <i>
        Blatnohrad
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        Moosburg
       </i>
       ("Swamp Fortress") served as a bulwark both against the
       <a href="/wiki/Bulgarians" title="Bulgarians">
        Bulgarians
       </a>
       and the Moravians.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartl_19_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Bartl_19-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Róna-Tas_243_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Róna-Tas_243-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldberg_85_10-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Goldberg_85-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">
        German
       </a>
       name for the lake is
       <i>
        <span lang="de" xml:lang="de">
         Plattensee
        </span>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is unlikely that the Germans named the lake so for being shallow since the adjective
       <i>
        <span lang="de" xml:lang="de">
         platt
        </span>
       </i>
       is a Greek loanword that was borrowed via French and entered the general German vocabulary in the 17th century.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is also noteworthy that the average depth of Balaton (3.2 m [10 ft])
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neusiedler_See" title="Neusiedler See">

      <div class="thumb tright">

        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ancient_balaton.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="588" data-file-width="800" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Ancient_balaton.jpg/220px-Ancient_balaton.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Ancient_balaton.jpg/330px-Ancient_balaton.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Ancient_balaton.jpg/440px-Ancient_balaton.jpg 2x" width="220"/>

      <p>
       Lake Balaton affects the local area precipitation every year. The area receives approximately 5–7 cm (2–3 in) more precipitation than most of
       <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">
        Hungary
       </a>
       , resulting in more cloudy days and less extreme temperatures. The lake's surface freezes during winters. The microclimate around Lake Balaton has also made the region ideal for
       <a href="/wiki/Viticulture" title="Viticulture">
        viticulture
       </a>
       . The Mediterranean-like climate, combined with the soil (containing volcanic rock), has made the region notable for its production of wines since the
       <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">
        Roman period
       </a>
       two thousand years ago.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PELSO.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="630" data-file-width="1343" height="103" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/PELSO.png/220px-PELSO.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/PELSO.png/330px-PELSO.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/PELSO.png/440px-PELSO.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PELSO.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Spread of Seuso at Lake Balaton

      <p>
       While a few settlements on Lake Balaton, including
       <a href="/wiki/Balatonf%C3%BCred" title="Balatonfüred">
        Balatonfüred
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9v%C3%ADz" title="Hévíz">
        Hévíz
       </a>
       , have long been resort centres for the Hungarian aristocracy, it was only in the late 19th century that the Hungarian middle class began to visit the lake.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The construction of railways in 1861 and 1909 increased tourism substantially, but the post-war boom of the 1950s was much larger.
      </p>
      <p>
       The last major German offensive of
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        World War II
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operation_Fr%C3%BChlingserwachen" title="Operation Frühlingserwachen">
        Operation Frühlingserwachen
       </a>
       , was conducted in the region of Lake Balaton in March 1945, being referred to as "the Lake Balaton Offensive" in many British histories of the war. The battle was a German attack by
       <a href="/wiki/Sepp_Dietrich" title="Sepp Dietrich">
        Sepp Dietrich
       </a>
       's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixth_Panzer_Army" title="Sixth Panzer Army">
        Sixth Panzer Army
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_Third_Army" title="Hungarian Third Army">
        Hungarian Third Army
       </a>
       between 6 March and 16 March 1945, and in the end, resulted in a Red Army victory. Several
       <a href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Il-2" title="Ilyushin Il-2">
        Ilyushin Il-2
       </a>
       wrecks have been pulled out of the lake after having been shot down during the later months of the war.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       During the 1960s and 1970s, Balaton became a major tourist destination for ordinary working Hungarians and especially for subsidised holiday excursions for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_Unions" title="Trade Unions">
        union
       </a>
       members.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       It also attracted many
       <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">
        East Germans
       </a>
       and other residents of the
       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Bloc" title="Eastern Bloc">
        Eastern Bloc
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">
        West Germans
       </a>
       could also visit, making Balaton a common meeting place for families and friends separated by the
       <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">
        Berlin Wall
       </a>
       until 1989.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The collapse of the Soviet Union after 1991 and the dismantling of the unions saw the gradual but steady reduction in numbers of lower-paid Hungarian visitors.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       The major resorts around the lake are
       <a href="/wiki/Si%C3%B3fok" title="Siófok">
        Siófok
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Keszthely" title="Keszthely">
        Keszthely
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Balatonf%C3%BCred" title="Balatonfüred">
        Balatonfüred
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Zam%C3%A1rdi" title="Zamárdi">
        Zamárdi
       </a>
       , another resort town on the southern shore, has been the site of
       <a href="/wiki/Balaton_Sound" title="Balaton Sound">
        Balaton Sound
       </a>
       , a notable
       <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">
        electronic music
       </a>
       festival since 2007. Balatonkenese has hosted numerous traditional gastronomic events. Siófok is known for attracting young people to it because of its large clubs. Keszthely is the site of the
       <a href="/wiki/Festetics_Palace" title="Festetics Palace">
        Festetics Palace
       </a>
       and Balatonfüred is a historical bathing town which hosts the annual
       <a href="/wiki/Belle_of_the_Anna-ball" title="Belle of the Anna-ball">
        Anna Ball
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The peak tourist season extends from June until the end of August. The average water temperature during the summer is 25 °C (77 °F), which makes bathing and swimming popular on the lake. Most of the beaches consist of either grass, rocks, or the silty sand that also makes up most of the bottom of the lake. Many resorts have artificial sandy beaches and all beaches have step access to the water. Other tourist attractions include sailing, fishing, and other water sports, as well as visiting the countryside and hills, wineries on the north coast, and nightlife on the south shore. The
       <a href="/wiki/Tihany" title="Tihany">
        Tihany
       </a>
       Peninsula is a historical district.
       <a href="/wiki/Badacsony" title="Badacsony">
        Badacsony
       </a>
       is a volcanic mountain and wine-growing region as well as a lakeside resort. The lake is almost completely surrounded by separated bike lanes to facilitate bicycle tourism. Although the peak season at the lake is the summer, Balaton is also frequented during the winter, when visitors go
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice-fishing" title="Ice-fishing">
        ice-fishing
       </a>
       or even skate, sledge, or ice-sail on the lake if it freezes over.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C3%A1rmell%C3%A9k_International_Airport" title="Sármellék International Airport">
        Sármellék International Airport
       </a>
       provides air service to Balaton (although most service is only seasonal).
      </p>

       Other resort towns include:
       <a href="/wiki/Balatonalm%C3%A1di" title="Balatonalmádi">

      </p>
