      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         The Sky at Night
        </b>
       </i>
       is a monthly
       <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">
        documentary
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">
        television programme
       </a>
       on
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">
        astronomy
       </a>
       produced by the
       <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">
        BBC
       </a>
       . The show had the same permanent presenter,
       <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Moore" title="Patrick Moore">
        Patrick Moore
       </a>
       , from its first broadcast on 24 April 1957 until 7 January 2013, Moore having died
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       on 9 December 2012. This made it the longest-running programme with the same presenter in television history.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Many early episodes are missing, either because the tapes were wiped, thrown out, or because the episode was broadcast live and never recorded in the first place.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian1_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-guardian1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Beginning with the 3 February 2013 edition, the show has been co-presented by
       <a href="/wiki/Lucie_Green" title="Lucie Green">
        Lucie Green
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Chris_Lintott" title="Chris Lintott">
        Chris Lintott
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       with
       <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Aderin-Pocock" title="Maggie Aderin-Pocock">
        Maggie Aderin-Pocock
       </a>
       announced in December 2013 as a new presenter.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The programme's opening and closing theme music is "At the Castle Gate", from the incidental music to
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Pell%C3%A9as_et_M%C3%A9lisande_(Sibelius)" title="Pelléas et Mélisande (Sibelius)">
         Pelléas et Mélisande
        </a>
       </i>
       , written in 1905 by
       <a href="/wiki/Jean_Sibelius" title="Jean Sibelius">
        Jean Sibelius
       </a>
       , performed by the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Philharmonic Orchestra">
        Royal Philharmonic Orchestra
       </a>
       and conducted by
       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Beecham" title="Thomas Beecham">
        Sir Thomas Beecham
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Content">
        Content
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Sky_at_Night&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The programme covers a wide range of general astronomical and space-related topics. Topics include
       <a href="/wiki/Stellar_evolution" title="Stellar evolution">
        stellar life cycles
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Radio_astronomy" title="Radio astronomy">
        radio astronomy
       </a>
       , artificial
       <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">
        satellites
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Black_hole" title="Black hole">
        black holes
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Neutron_star" title="Neutron star">
        neutron stars
       </a>
       and many others. The programme also covers what is happening in the night sky at the time it is being broadcast, especially when something less common, such as a
       <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">
        comet
       </a>
       or a
       <a href="/wiki/Meteor_shower" title="Meteor shower">
        meteor shower
       </a>
       , is present.
      </p>
      <p>
       Explaining the show's enduring appeal, Moore said: "Astronomy's a fascinating subject. You look up... you can't help getting interested and it's there. We've tried to bring it to the people.. it's not me, it's the appeal of the subject."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_guests">

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        Milky Way galaxy
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Fred_Hoyle" title="Fred Hoyle">
        Fred Hoyle
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Carl_Sagan" title="Carl Sagan">
        Carl Sagan
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Jocelyn_Bell_Burnell" title="Jocelyn Bell Burnell">
        Jocelyn Bell Burnell
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Tolansky" title="Samuel Tolansky">
        Samuel Tolansky
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Harold_Spencer_Jones" title="Harold Spencer Jones">
        Harold Spencer Jones
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Martin_Ryle" title="Martin Ryle">
        Martin Ryle
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Frenk" title="Carlos Frenk">
        Carlos Frenk
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Bart_Bok" title="Bart Bok">
        Bart Bok
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Other guests have included
       <a href="/wiki/Arthur_C._Clarke" title="Arthur C. Clarke">
        Arthur C. Clarke
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian1_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-guardian1-3">

        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomer_Royal" title="Astronomer Royal">
        Astronomer Royal
       </a>
       Sir
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Rees,_Baron_Rees_of_Ludlow" title="Martin Rees, Baron Rees of Ludlow">
        Martin Rees
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Wolfendale" title="Arnold Wolfendale">
        Arnold Wolfendale
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Allan_Chapman_(historian)" title="Allan Chapman (historian)">
        Allan Chapman
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Lovell" title="Bernard Lovell">
        Sir Bernard Lovell
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bentine" title="Michael Bentine">
        Michael Bentine
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Wernher_von_Braun" title="Wernher von Braun">
        Wernher von Braun
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Open_University" title="Open University">
        Open University
       </a>
       professors
       <a href="/wiki/John_Zarnecki" title="John Zarnecki">
        John Zarnecki
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Monica_Grady" title="Monica Grady">
        Monica Grady
       </a>
       , Edwin Maher and
       <a href="/wiki/Colin_Pillinger" title="Colin Pillinger">
        Colin Pillinger
       </a>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Many well-known astronauts have also appeared on the programme, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Piers_Sellers" title="Piers Sellers">
        Piers Sellers
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Eugene_Cernan" title="Eugene Cernan">
        Eugene Cernan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Buzz_Aldrin" title="Buzz Aldrin">
        Buzz Aldrin
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armstrong" title="Neil Armstrong">
        Neil Armstrong
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In July 2004, Moore was unable to make the broadcast owing to a severe bout of
       <a href="/wiki/Salmonellosis" title="Salmonellosis">
        salmonellosis
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He was replaced for this one occasion by the cosmologist
       <a href="/wiki/Chris_Lintott" title="Chris Lintott">
        Chris Lintott
       </a>
       of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxford_University" title="Oxford University">
        Oxford University
       </a>
       , but returned for the August programme. This was the only occasion in the 55 years of Moore's tenure that he did not host the programme.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Brian_May" title="Brian May">
        Brian May
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">
        Queen
       </a>
       guitarist and
       <a href="/wiki/Astrophysics" title="Astrophysics">
        astrophysicist
       </a>
       , has been a guest on the show from time to time.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On 1 April 2007, Moore presented the 50th Anniversary edition of the show, a special "time travel" edition which included the appearance of
       <a href="/wiki/Jon_Culshaw" title="Jon Culshaw">
        Jon Culshaw
       </a>
       as Moore's younger self. The 50th anniversary programme was filmed at
       <a href="/wiki/Teddington_Studios" title="Teddington Studios">
        Teddington Studios
       </a>
       as the 1957 home of the programme,
       <a href="/wiki/Lime_Grove_Studios" title="Lime Grove Studios">
        Lime Grove Studios
       </a>
       , had been demolished in 1992.
      </p>
      <p>
       On 6 March 2011, Moore presented the 700th edition of the show, a special retrospective episode which included
       <a href="/wiki/Jon_Culshaw" title="Jon Culshaw">
        Jon Culshaw
       </a>
       once again appearing as Moore's younger self, as well as Brian May.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Rumoured_cancellation">
        Rumoured cancellation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Sky_at_Night&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rumoured cancellation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In September 2013 the BBC announced that the programme's future after December 2013 was under review, prompting speculation that the corporation would end it, and a petition asking for the show to be retained.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On 29 October it was announced that the programme would continue, but would only be shown on
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">
        BBC Four
       </a>
       , ending a 54-year run on the BBC's flagship channel.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Commemorative_honours">
        Commemorative honours
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Sky_at_Night&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commemorative honours">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">
        International Astronomical Union
       </a>
       celebrated the 50th anniversary of the show by naming an asteroid
       <a href="/wiki/57424_Caelumnoctu" title="57424 Caelumnoctu">
        57424 Caelumnoctu
       </a>
       , the number referring to the first broadcast date and the name being Latin for "The Sky at Night".
      </p>
      <p>
       In February 2007, the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Mail" title="Royal Mail">
        Royal Mail
       </a>
       issued a set of six astronomy stamps to celebrate the 50th anniversary of the programme.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_Book_of_World_Records" title="Guinness Book of World Records">
         Guinness Book of World Records
        </a>
       </i>
       , Patrick Moore is listed as the most prolific TV presenter in the world, having hosted all but one episode of the programme between 1957 and January 2013.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      </p>
