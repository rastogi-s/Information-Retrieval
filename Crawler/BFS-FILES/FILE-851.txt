      <p>
       <b>
        Râmnicu Vâlcea
       </b>
       (also spelled
       <i>
        Rîmnicu Vîlcea
       </i>
       ,
       <small>
        Romanian pronunciation:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Romanian" title="Help:IPA/Romanian">
         [ˈrɨmniku ˈvɨlt͡ʃe̯a]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) (population: 92,573) is the capital city of
       <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A2lcea_County" title="Vâlcea County">
        Vâlcea County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">
        Romania
       </a>
       (in the historical province of
       <a href="/wiki/Oltenia" title="Oltenia">
        Oltenia
       </a>
       ).
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">
        Geography and climate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A2mnicu_V%C3%A2lcea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Râmnicu Vâlcea is situated in the central-south area of Romania. Set at the foothills of the
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Carpathians" title="Southern Carpathians">
        Southern Carpathians
       </a>
       , the city is located at about 12 kilometres (7.5 mi) from the Cozia Mountains and about 40 kilometres (25 mi) from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C4%83g%C4%83ra%C5%9F_Mountains" title="Făgăraş Mountains">
        Făgăraş
       </a>
       and Lotrului Mountains. The southern limit of Râmnicu Vâlcea is formed by the Getic Plateau and the
       <a href="/wiki/Olt_River" title="Olt River">
        Oltului Valley
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Olt_River" title="Olt River">
        Olt River
       </a>
       crosses the town of Râmnicu Vâlcea as well as the E81 road of European interest and one of the main national railway routes.
      </p>
      <p>
       The climate is defined as
       <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">
        Dfa"
       </a>
       (humid continental with warm summers) bordering
       <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">
        Cfa"
       </a>
       (humid subtropical). Precipitation amount is higher in the summer, although not high enough in order to be a "Dwa" type climate.
      </p>

      <p>
       The area has been inhabited since
       <a href="/wiki/Dacia" title="Dacia">
        Dacian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">
        Roman
       </a>
       times, and was the site of a
       <a href="/wiki/Castra" title="Castra">
        castrum
       </a>
       . A new fortress was built on the location during the
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">
        Middle Ages
       </a>
       . Râmnicu Vâlcea was first attested during the rule of
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Wallachia" title="List of rulers of Wallachia">
        Prince
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mircea_cel_B%C4%83tr%C3%A2n_of_Wallachia" title="Mircea cel Bătrân of Wallachia">
        Mircea cel Bătrân
       </a>
       , as "
       <i>
        the princely town of Râmnic
       </i>
       " (4 September 1388), and confirmed as the seat of a Vâlcea County during the same period (8 January 1392).
      </p>
      <p>
       The town seal dates to 1505.
       <i>
        Cetăţuia
       </i>
       , the actual fortress, served as the residence of Oltenian
       <a href="/wiki/Ban_(title)" title="Ban (title)">
        Bans
       </a>
       and, from 1504, of the
       <a href="/wiki/Romanian_Orthodox_Church" title="Romanian Orthodox Church">
        Orthodox
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">
        bishops
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Archdiocese_of_R%C3%A2mnic" title="Archdiocese of Râmnic">
        Râmnic Diocese
       </a>
       ; in 1543, Prince
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radu_de_la_Afuma%C5%A3i" title="Radu de la Afumaţi">
        Radu de la Afumaţi
       </a>
       was killed in
       <i>
        Cetăţuia
       </i>
       by a
       <a href="/wiki/Boyar" title="Boyar">
        boyar
       </a>
       conspiracy.
      </p>
      <p>
       During the rules of
       <a href="/wiki/Matei_Basarab" title="Matei Basarab">
        Matei Basarab
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Constantin_Br%C3%A2ncoveanu" title="Constantin Brâncoveanu">
        Constantin Brâncoveanu
       </a>
       , it became an important cultural center. It was here where the first
       <a href="/wiki/Paper_mill" title="Paper mill">
        paper mill
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Printing_press" title="Printing press">
        printing press
       </a>
       in Romania were built (
       <i>
        see
        <a href="/wiki/Anthim_the_Iberian" title="Anthim the Iberian">
         Anthim the Iberian
        </a>
       </i>
       ). The city was heavily damaged during the
       <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">
        Habsburg
       </a>
       takeover of Oltenia between 1718 and 1739, and its purpose was again reduced to that of a fortress.
      </p>
      <p>
       During the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1848_Wallachian_Revolution" title="1848 Wallachian Revolution">
        Wallachian Revolution
       </a>
       , on 29 July 1848,
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/De%C5%9Fteapt%C4%83-te,_rom%C3%A2ne!" title="Deşteaptă-te, române!">
         Deşteaptă-te, române!
        </a>
       </i>
       (the
       <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">
        national anthem
       </a>
       of Romania), with lyrics written by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andrei_Mure%C5%9Fanu" title="Andrei Mureşanu">
        Andrei Mureşanu
       </a>
       and music composed by
       <a href="/wiki/Anton_Pann" title="Anton Pann">
        Anton Pann
       </a>
       (whose memorial house lies in the center of the town), was sung for the first time in Râmnicu Vâlcea.
       <a href="/wiki/Gheorghe_Magheru" title="Gheorghe Magheru">
        Gheorghe Magheru
       </a>
       gathered his military force in Râureni, now part of the city, in an attempt to face the anti-revolutionary forces of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_Russia" title="Imperial Russia">
        Imperial Russia
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">
        Ottoman Empire
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In the 1980s, the city was completely rebuilt in a style combining
       <a href="/wiki/Socialist_realism" title="Socialist realism">
        Socialist realism
       </a>
       with local vernacular
       <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">
        architecture
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The city was the center and peak point of the path of total darkness of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_11_August_1999" title="Solar eclipse of 11 August 1999">
        Solar eclipse of 11 August 1999
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Oltchim_S.A.">
        <a href="/wiki/Oltchim_S.A." title="Oltchim S.A.">
         Oltchim S.A.
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A2mnicu_V%C3%A2lcea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Oltchim S.A.">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Oltchim_S.A." title="Oltchim S.A.">
        Oltchim S.A.
       </a>
       Ramnicu Valcea was one of the largest chemical companies in Romania.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It provided chemical products since 1966, it was an exporter on Chlorosodics, Polyether Polyols and Propylene Oxide markets in Eastern and Central Europe, and it was the second largest PVC producer in the area.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2009 and 2010, the company lowered production and laid off employees because of the financial crisis and it became one of the first companies to receive aid from the government.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2012, the company was declared bankrupt.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cybercrime">
        Cybercrime
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A2mnicu_V%C3%A2lcea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cybercrime">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Râmnicu Vâlcea has gained notoriety as a global centre of
       <a href="/wiki/Cybercrime" title="Cybercrime">
        cybercrime
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       including but not limited to
       <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay">
        EBay
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Craigslist" title="Craigslist">
        Craigslist
       </a>
       scammers who steal thousands of dollars per transaction from foreign buyers, generating millions of dollars in revenue. Wealthy profiteers provide revenue for a strong
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxury_car" title="Luxury car">
        luxury car
       </a>
       industry.
      </p>
      <p>
       Due to the cybercrime, the city has been nicknamed
       <b>
        Hackerville
       </b>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">
        Sports
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A2mnicu_V%C3%A2lcea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sports">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Râmnicu Vâlcea is home to
       <a href="/wiki/CS_Oltchim_R%C3%A2mnicu_V%C3%A2lcea" title="CS Oltchim Râmnicu Vâlcea">
        CS Oltchim Râmnicu Vâlcea
       </a>
       which is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_handball" title="Team handball">
        women handball
       </a>
       club that competes in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liga_Na%C5%A3ional%C4%83_(Handbal_Feminin)" title="Liga Naţională (Handbal Feminin)">
        Liga Naţională
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EHF_Women%27s_Champions_League" title="EHF Women's Champions League">
        Women's Champions League
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Villages">
        Villages
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A2mnicu_V%C3%A2lcea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Villages">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The city administers thirteen villages: Aranghel, Căzănești, Copăcelu, Dealu Malului, Fețeni, Goranu, Lespezi, Poenari, Priba, Râureni, Săliștea, Stolniceni and Troian. Goranu, Fețeni, Lespezi and Săliștea were a separate commune,
       <i>
        Goranu
       </i>
       , until 1996, when they were merged into the city.
      </p>
