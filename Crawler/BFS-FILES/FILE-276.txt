      <p>
       <a class="mw-selflink selflink">
        Solar Saros 136
       </a>
       , repeating every 18 years, 11 days, contains 71 events. The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saros_cycle" title="Saros cycle">
        series
       </a>
       started with partial solar eclipse on June 14, 1360, and reached a first annular eclipse on September 8, 1504. It was a hybrid event from November 22, 1612, through January 17, 1703, and total eclipses from January 27, 1721 through May 13, 2496. The series ends at member 71 as a partial eclipse on July 30, 2622, with the entire series lasting 1262 years. The longest eclipse occurred on June 20, 1955, with a maximum duration of totality at 7 minutes, 8 seconds.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       <b>
        Solar Saros 136
       </b>
       is for now producing the longest total solar eclipses. It produced the six longest total solar eclipses of the 20th century, three of them over seven minutes long.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It also produced the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_July_22,_2009" title="Solar eclipse of July 22, 2009">
        longest total eclipse of the 21st century
       </a>
       at 6 min 39 sec, and overall will produce the century's three longest total eclipses. Each eclipse is getting slightly shorter and this series will be surpassed in total eclipse length by
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_Saros_139" title="Solar Saros 139">
        Solar Saros 139
       </a>
       (whose eclipses are getting slightly longer) on
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_May_11,_2078" title="Solar eclipse of May 11, 2078">
        May 11, 2078
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Saros 136 will ultimately produce a total of 44 total eclipses. It produced the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamma_(solar_eclipse)" title="Gamma (solar eclipse)">
        most central
       </a>
       total eclipse between the years 1209 and 2718 and the greatest
       <a href="/wiki/Magnitude_of_eclipse" title="Magnitude of eclipse">
        magnitude
       </a>
       of any eclipse since the year 540 on
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_July_11,_1991" title="Solar eclipse of July 11, 1991">
        July 11, 1991
       </a>
       .
      </p>
