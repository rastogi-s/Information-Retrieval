      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_semiconductors">
        In semiconductors
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Debye_length&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In semiconductors">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Debye length has become increasingly significant in the modeling of solid state devices as improvements in lithographic technologies have enabled smaller geometries.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Debye length of
       <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">
        semiconductors
       </a>
       is given:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <span class="mwe-math-element">
         <span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;">
          <math alttext="{\displaystyle {\mathit {L}}_{D}={\sqrt {\frac {\varepsilon k_{B}T}{q^{2}N_{d}}}}}" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
           <semantics>
            <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
             <mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0">
              <msub>
               <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
                <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
                 <mi class="MJX-tex-mathit" mathvariant="italic">
                  L
                 </mi>
                </mrow>
               </mrow>
               <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
                <mi>
                 D
                </mi>
               </mrow>
              </msub>
              <mo>
               =
              </mo>
              <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
               <msqrt>
                <mfrac>
                 <mrow>
                  <mi>
                   ε
                   <!-- ε -->
                  </mi>
                  <msub>
                   <mi>
                    k
                   </mi>
                   <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
                    <mi>
                     B
                    </mi>
                   </mrow>
                  </msub>
                  <mi>
                   T
                  </mi>
                 </mrow>
                 <mrow>
                  <msup>
                   <mi>
                    q
                   </mi>
                   <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
                    <mn>
                     2
                    </mn>
                   </mrow>
                  </msup>
                  <msub>
                   <mi>
                    N
                   </mi>
                   <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
                    <mi>
                     d
                    </mi>
                   </mrow>
                  </msub>
                 </mrow>
                </mfrac>
               </msqrt>
              </mrow>
             </mstyle>
            </mrow>
            <annotation encoding="application/x-tex">
             {\displaystyle {\mathit {L}}_{D}={\sqrt {\frac {\varepsilon k_{B}T}{q^{2}N_{d}}}}}
            </annotation>
           </semantics>
          </math>
         </span>
         <img alt="{\mathit {L}}_{D}={\sqrt {\frac {\varepsilon k_{B}T}{q^{2}N_{d}}}}" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/028e487e7a563cba23e85ea05ac3b6004247187f" style="vertical-align: -3.338ex; width:14.719ex; height:7.676ex;"/>
        </span>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       where
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         ε
        </i>
        is the dielectric constant,
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         k
        </i>
        <sub>
         B
        </sub>
        is the Boltzmann's constant,
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         T
        </i>
        is the absolute temperature in kelvins,
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         q
        </i>
        is the elementary charge, and
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         N
         <sub>
          d
         </sub>
        </i>
        is the density of dopants (either donors or acceptors).
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       When doping profiles exceed the Debye length, majority carriers no longer behave according to the distribution of the dopants. Instead, a measure of the profile of the doping gradients provides an "effective" profile that better matches the profile of the majority carrier density.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the context of solids, the Debye length is also called the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas%E2%80%93Fermi_screening_length" title="Thomas–Fermi screening length">
        Thomas–Fermi screening length
       </a>
       .
      </p>
