      <p>
       <b>
        Universe Today
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        UT
       </b>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Popular_science" title="Popular science">
        popular
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North American
       </a>
       -based non-commercial
       <a href="/wiki/Space" title="Space">
        space
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">
        astronomy
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Online_newspaper" title="Online newspaper">
        news website
       </a>
       . The domain was registered on December 31, 1998,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and the website went live in March 1999, founded by Fraser Cain.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <i>
        Universe Today
       </i>
       assumed its current form on 24 July 2003, featuring astronomy news and space-related issues.
      </p>
      <p>
       By early September 2005, the forum section merged with
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Bad_Astronomy" title="Bad Astronomy">
         Bad Astronomy
        </a>
       </i>
       as a combined site with the BAUT forum. During April 2011, the
       <a href="/wiki/Association_of_British_Science_Writers" title="Association of British Science Writers">
        Association of British Science Writers
       </a>
       noted that
       <i>
        Universe Today
       </i>
       decided not to make preparations for reporting on
       <a href="/wiki/News_embargo" title="News embargo">
        embargoed stories
       </a>
       until they are public knowledge.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-utembargo_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-utembargo-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Emily_Lakdawalla" title="Emily Lakdawalla">
        Emily Lakdawalla
       </a>
       said that she relies on
       <i>
        Universe Today
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BadAstronomy.com" title="BadAstronomy.com">
         Bad Astronomy
        </a>
       </i>
       to "give ... an independent look at big news stories".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
