      <p>
       The
       <b>
        BBC One Balloon idents
       </b>
       were a series of
       <a href="/wiki/Station_identification" title="Station identification">
        idents
       </a>
       used on
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">
        BBC One
       </a>
       from 4 October 1997 to 29 March 2002. The balloon theme replaced the computer generated spinning globe which had been used as the main
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_ident" title="Television ident">
        ident
       </a>
       on the channel since 1991.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It launched on the same day as a BBC-wide rebrand, and thus the new idents also carried the new BBC logo. The channel's name also changed, from BBC1 to BBC One. This was the last ident set used by the channel to be used at closedown; their last closedown took place on 8 November 1997; starting the following day,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_News_24" title="BBC News 24">
        BBC News 24
       </a>
       would broadcast on BBC One in the late night hours.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Hot_air_balloon" title="Hot air balloon">
        hot air balloon
       </a>
       featured in the idents was filmed on location and also added to scenes by computer generation. It was built by
       <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Balloons" title="Cameron Balloons">
        Cameron Balloons
       </a>
       in 1997, and made its first flight that year, flying from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bristol_Balloon_Fiesta" title="Bristol Balloon Fiesta">
        Bristol Balloon Fiesta
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It made its final flight in August 2002 before being placed into storage. The balloon's flight certificate from the
       <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">
        Civil Aviation Authority
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       expired on 17 July 2003.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Launch">
        Launch
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_One_%27Balloon%27_idents&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Launch">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       As part of a large relaunch of the BBC's corporate logo and the ident packages of BBC's One and Two, these new idents were used to replace the old
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_One_%27Virtual_Globe%27_ident" title="BBC One 'Virtual Globe' ident">
        virtual globe
       </a>
       . As a result of the rebrand, which saw the channel name lose all personality, it was proposed by the design agency
       <a href="/wiki/Lambie-Nairn" title="Lambie-Nairn">
        Lambie-Nairn
       </a>
       that the personality be added a different way, namely by making the globe interact in the country and the peoples lives.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The idea was designed and conceptualised by the Lambie-Nairn design agency in London, with the balloon itself made in
       <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">
        Bristol
       </a>
       by Cameron Balloons Ltd: its aircraft registration was "G-IBBC".
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Components_of_look">
        Components of look
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_One_%27Balloon%27_idents&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Components of look">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The look itself featured a predominantly red balloon, with the map of the world picked out in orange with white clouds on top, floating over various scenes of the British landscape. The colours were chosen because a blue balloon with a green map would have been more difficult to see against the land and sea. The size of the balloon was originally proposed to be 100 ft, but was reduced to 60 ft when it was made. The idents featured a soundtrack of ambient music, with more lively versions being used for more industrial or recreational settings. This made them the first regular BBC One idents to actually use music. The new
       <a href="/wiki/Logo_of_the_BBC#1997" title="Logo of the BBC">
        BBC logo
       </a>
       , along with the channel name 'ONE' immediately to the right of it, was overlaid at the bottom of the screen. The new logo design was an attempt to unify all of the BBC's services and brands under a single logo design, with personality expressed through the idents themselves. The idents were, from October 1998, shown in
       <a href="/wiki/Widescreen#Television" title="Widescreen">
        Widescreen
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_Online" title="BBC Online">
        bbc.co.uk
       </a>
       URL was added above the logo soon after.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_PressOffice_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-BBC_PressOffice-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVARK1997_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVARK1997-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The new look also featured a clock, which used the same software and layout as before, and used the balloon canvas as the background. The clock was also retained following the change to widescreen, however the software was changed so that the minute hand, instead of moving every second as it had previously, only moved once a minute.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVARK1997_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVARK1997-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Promotions and static captions both featured text and logos centred for Widescreen use, with the BBC One logo at the bottom of the screen and a colour palette of mainly oranges and reds, however colours varied according to theme and programme. The use of static captions was reduced slightly, but still remained a key part of continuity links.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVARK1997C_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVARK1997C-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Original_locations">
        Original locations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_One_%27Balloon%27_idents&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Original locations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The original sequences were filmed over six weeks in June and July 1997 at eleven locations around the United Kingdom. From these eleven locations, forty-seven different 35-second films were produced featuring the balloon floating serenely over British landscapes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVARK1997_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVARK1997-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Later_additions">
        Later additions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_One_%27Balloon%27_idents&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later additions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       A year after launch in 1998, several more idents were created and added to the collection. The main difference between these new additions and the originals was that people were now included in the sequences.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVARK1997_6-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVARK1997-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, the balloon itself was inserted digitally by computer on to pre-filmed locations
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and did not actually fly over the following locations:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Angel_of_the_North" title="Angel of the North">
         Angel of the North
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/St_Michael%27s_Mount" title="St Michael's Mount">
         St Michael's Mount
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Second_Severn_Crossing" title="Second Severn Crossing">
         Second Severn Crossing
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_Tower" title="Blackpool Tower">
         Blackpool Tower
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Needles" title="The Needles">
         The Needles
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">
         Isle of Wight
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Dunluce_Castle" title="Dunluce Castle">
         Dunluce Castle
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">
         Northern Ireland
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Exhibition_and_Conference_Centre" title="Scottish Exhibition and Conference Centre">
         Scottish Exhibition and Conference Centre
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">
         Glasgow
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       In 2000, the
       <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">
        BBC
       </a>
       wanted the balloon idents to become more inclusive, so they introduced the 'lifestyle' idents. These featured
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skateboarder" title="Skateboarder">
        skateboarders
       </a>
       , a busy
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Market_(place)" title="Market (place)">
        market
       </a>
       scene, a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bungee_jump" title="Bungee jump">
        bungee jumper
       </a>
       and a
       <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">
        carnival
       </a>
       , all of which featured the balloon flying past in the background.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVARK1997_6-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVARK1997-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Replacement">
        Replacement
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_One_%27Balloon%27_idents&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Replacement">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In 2001, Lorraine Heggessey became controller of BBC One and immediately ordered a review of the channel's branding. In her opinion the balloon was "slow and distant" and so, in 2002, after much speculation, the balloon idents were replaced as the icon of BBC One with a set of idents with the theme of
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_One_%27Rhythm_%26_Movement%27_idents" title="BBC One 'Rhythm &amp; Movement' idents">
        Rhythm &amp; Movement
       </a>
       , making it the final motif of the globe logo for the channel after 39 years.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVRoom1_4-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVRoom1-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVARK1997_6-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-TVARK1997-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The balloon itself last took to the skies in the summer of 2002 when it took part in the
       <a href="/wiki/Bristol_International_Balloon_Fiesta" title="Bristol International Balloon Fiesta">
        Bristol International Balloon Fiesta
       </a>
       . It is now wrapped up and held in a museum by a preservation society.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
        Reception
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_One_%27Balloon%27_idents&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The idents were well received by viewers and hold a loyal fan base even up to this day.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="BBC_America">
        BBC America
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_One_%27Balloon%27_idents&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: BBC America">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The balloon idents were also shown on
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_America" title="BBC America">
        BBC America
       </a>
       between 1999 and 2003, and lasted several months after it had been retired by
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">
        BBC One
       </a>
       . Unlike BBC One, BBC America employed shorter, snappier cuts of various balloon sequences with slight changes to the familiar musical score.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
