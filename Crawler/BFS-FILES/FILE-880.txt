      <p>
       <b>
        Doordarshan
       </b>
       (abbreviated in English as
       <b>
        DD
       </b>
       ) is an autonomous
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Public_broadcasting" title="Public broadcasting">
        public service broadcaster
       </a>
       founded by the
       <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">
        Government of India
       </a>
       , which is owned by the
       <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Information_and_Broadcasting_(India)" title="Ministry of Information and Broadcasting (India)">
        Broadcasting Ministry of India
       </a>
       and is one of two divisions of
       <a href="/wiki/Prasar_Bharati" title="Prasar Bharati">
        Prasar Bharati
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is one of India's largest broadcasting organisations in terms of
       <a href="/wiki/Studio" title="Studio">
        studio
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Transmitter" title="Transmitter">
        transmitter
       </a>
       infrastructure, having been established on 15 September 1959.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It also broadcasts on digital terrestrial transmitters. DD provides television, radio, online and mobile services throughout metropolitan and regional India, as well as overseas, through the Indian Network and Radio India.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doordarshan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Doordarshan had a modest beginning as an experimental telecast starting in
       <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">
        Delhi
       </a>
       on 15 September 1959, with a small transmitter and a makeshift studio. Regular daily transmission started in 1965 as a part of
       <a href="/wiki/All_India_Radio" title="All India Radio">
        All India Radio
       </a>
       . Doordarshan began a five-minute news bulletin in the same year. Pratima Puri was the first newsreader.
       <a href="/wiki/Salma_Sultan" title="Salma Sultan">
        Salma Sultan
       </a>
       joined Doordarshan in 1967, and later became a news anchor. The television service was extended to Bombay (now
       <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">
        Mumbai
       </a>
       ) and
       <a href="/wiki/Amritsar" title="Amritsar">
        Amritsar
       </a>
       in 1972.
      </p>
      <p>
       Up until 1975, only seven Indian cities had a television service and Doordarshan remained the sole provider of television in India. Television services were separated from radio on 1 April 1976.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Each office of All India Radio and Doordarshan was placed under the management of two separate Director Generals in New Delhi.
      </p>
      <p>
       Finally, in 1982, Doordarshan took shape as a National Broadcaster.
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Krishi_Darshan" title="Krishi Darshan">
         Krishi Darshan
        </a>
       </i>
       was the first program telecast on Doordarshan. It commenced on 26 January 1967 and is one of the longest running programs on Indian television.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deb2009lad_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-deb2009lad-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       National telecasts were introduced in 1982. In the same year,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colour_TV" title="Colour TV">
        colour TV
       </a>
       was introduced to India with the live telecast of the Independence Day speech by the
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_India" title="List of Prime Ministers of India">
        Prime Minister of India
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi" title="Indira Gandhi">
        Indira Gandhi
       </a>
       , on 15 August 1982. This was followed by the
       <a href="/wiki/1982_Asian_Games" title="1982 Asian Games">
        1982 Asian Games
       </a>
       held in Delhi.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Now more than 90 percent of the Indian population can receive Doordarshan (DD National) programmes through a network of nearly 1,400 terrestrial transmitters. There are about 46 Doordarshan studios producing TV programmes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       For the
       <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">
        2012 Summer Olympics
       </a>
       , live telecasts of the opening and closing ceremonies of the games were broadcast on its national channel. DD Sports provided round-the-clock coverage of sport events.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On 17 November 2014, Doordarshan relaunched with a new theme of pink and purple, accompanied by a new punchline,
       <i>
        Desh Ka Apna Channel
       </i>
       , meaning "the country's own channel". It was announced by Vijayalaxmi Chhabra, Director General of Doordarshan.

        <a href="#cite_note-10">

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Channels">
        Channels
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doordarshan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Channels">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Doordarshan operates 34 channels:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Two All India channels (available terrestrially),
        <a href="/wiki/DD_National" title="DD National">
         DD National
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/DD_News" title="DD News">
         DD News
        </a>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
         <a href="#cite_note-12">
          [12]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        16 regional language satellite channels (RLSC), 11 state networks (SN), an international channel, a sports channel,
        <a href="/wiki/DD_Sports" title="DD Sports">
         DD Sports
        </a>
        , DD Bharati, DD Urdu &amp; DD Kisan.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       On
       <a href="/wiki/DD_National" title="DD National">
        DD National
       </a>
       aka (DD-1), regional programs and local programs are carried on time-sharing basis.
       <a href="/wiki/DD_News" title="DD News">
        DD News
       </a>
       channel, launched on 3 November 2003, which replaced the
       <a href="/wiki/DD_Metro" title="DD Metro">
        DD Metro
       </a>
       formerly known as DD-2 entertainment channel, provides 24-hour news service.
      </p>
      <p>
       The regional languages satellite channels have two components – the regional service for the particular state relayed by all terrestrial transmitters in the state and additional programs in the regional language in prime time and non-prime time available only through cable operators. DD-Sports Channel is exclusively devoted to the broadcasting of sporting events of national and international importance. This is the only sports channel which telecasts rural sports like kho-kho and kabbadi, something which private broadcasters will not attempt to telecast as it will not attract any revenue.
      </p>

        Active Doordarshan
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doordarshan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Active Doordarshan">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>

      <p>
       This is an interactive service of
       <a href="/wiki/Tata_Sky" title="Tata Sky">
        Tata Sky
       </a>
       to show TV channels of Doordarshan which are not available on Tata sky as normal channels. Active Doordarshan channels are DD Kashir,
       <a href="/wiki/DD_Podhigai" title="DD Podhigai">
        DD Podhigai
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/DD_Malayalam" title="DD Malayalam">
        DD Malayalam
       </a>
       , DD Punjabi, DD Sahyadri, DD Chandana and
       <a href="/wiki/DD_Girnar" title="DD Girnar">
        DD Girnar
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       DD has its own DTH service called
       <a href="/wiki/DD_Free_Dish" title="DD Free Dish">
        DD Free Dish
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcasting">

       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <p>

       Satellite on the Sky system on Channel 833 (the logo is shown as Rayat TV). The timing and programming of DD-India international is different from that of India. Transmissions via

       </a>
       ceased in June 2008 and those via
       <a href="/wiki/DirecTV" title="DirecTV">

       </a>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Allegations_of_the_state_control">
        Allegations of the state control
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doordarshan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Allegations of the state control">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Prasar_Bharati" title="Prasar Bharati">
         Prasar Bharati
        </a>
        is the parent body of Doordarshan, and has all board members appointed by the
        <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">
         Government of India
        </a>
        acting through the
        <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Information_and_Broadcasting_(India)" title="Ministry of Information and Broadcasting (India)">
         Information and Broadcasting Ministry
        </a>
        .
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
         <a href="#cite_note-14">
          [14]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        It had been actively used especially during the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Emergency" title="Indian Emergency">
         Emergency
        </a>
        for government propaganda.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
         <a href="#cite_note-15">
          [15]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        During
        <a href="/wiki/Operation_Blue_Star" title="Operation Blue Star">
         Operation Blue Star
        </a>
        in 1984, only government sources were used for reporting the story. Here, Doordarshan was complicit in the production of a video that claimed acts of violence which when investigated by independent journalists were found to be false.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
         <a href="#cite_note-16">
          [16]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        In 2004, it censored the airing of a controversial documentary on
        <a href="/wiki/Jayaprakash_Narayan" title="Jayaprakash Narayan">
         Jayaprakash Narayan
        </a>
        , one of the opposition leaders during the Emergency.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
         <a href="#cite_note-17">
          [17]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        When Doordarshan broadcast the 70-minute-long
        <a href="/wiki/Vijayadashami" title="Vijayadashami">
         Vijayadashami
        </a>
        speech of
        <a href="/wiki/Mohan_Bhagwat" title="Mohan Bhagwat">
         Mohan Bhagwat
        </a>
        , the leader of the
        <a href="/wiki/Rashtriya_Swayamsevak_Sangh" title="Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh">
         Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh
        </a>
        (RSS), the
        <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">
         Narendra Modi
        </a>
        administration and the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BJP" title="BJP">
         BJP
        </a>
        were criticized for "misusing" the public broadcaster. While Director General of DD, Archana Datta issued a clarification on speech and said, "Speech was like any other news event therefore we covered it."
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
         <a href="#cite_note-18">
          [18]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
         <a href="#cite_note-19">
          [19]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
         <a href="#cite_note-20">
          [20]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_viability">
        Commercial viability
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Doordarshan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Commercial viability">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        After private television channels were allowed in 1991, Doordarshan has seen a steep decline in viewership in homes, due to general public acceptance of
        <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">
         cable
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">
         satellite television
        </a>
        , which in 2002 was just at 2.38% for DD National.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thehindubusinessline.com_21-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-thehindubusinessline.com-21">
          [21]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        While it earns significant advertising revenue due to the compulsory feed given to it by the highest bidder to national events, including cricket tournaments,
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thehindubusinessline.com_21-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-thehindubusinessline.com-21">
          [21]
         </a>
        </sup>
        there has been a proposal to give it funds by imposing a license fee to own a television in India.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
         <a href="#cite_note-22">
          [22]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
