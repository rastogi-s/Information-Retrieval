      <p>
       This is a list of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Lunar_mare" title="Lunar mare">
         maria
        </a>
       </i>
       (large, dark,
       <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">
        basaltic
       </a>
       plains) on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . It also includes the one
       <i>
        oceanus
       </i>
       and the features known by the names
       <i>
        lacus
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        palus
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        sinus
       </i>
       . The modern system of lunar nomenclature was introduced in 1651 by Giovanni Battista
       <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Battista_Riccioli" title="Giovanni Battista Riccioli">
        Riccioli
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Riccioli's map of the moon was drawn by
       <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Maria_Grimaldi" title="Francesco Maria Grimaldi">
        Francesco Maria Grimaldi
       </a>
       , who has a crater named after him.
      </p>

      <p>
       There was also a region on the Lunar farside that was briefly misidentified as a mare and named
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Mare_Desiderii" title="Mare Desiderii">
         Mare Desiderii
        </a>
       </i>
       (Sea of Desire). It is no longer recognized. Other former maria include:
      </p>
