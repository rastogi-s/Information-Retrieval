       <b>
        Einar A. Tandberg-Hanssen
       </b>
       (6 August 1921 – 5 November 2012) was a Norwegian-American astrophysicist with a specialty in
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_physics" title="Solar physics">
        solar physics
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">

        </a>
       </sup>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
        Background
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Einar_Tandberg-Hanssen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Tandberg-Hanssen was born in
       <a href="/wiki/Bergen" title="Bergen">
        Bergen
       </a>
       , Norway. He was the son of Birger Tandberg-Hanssen (1883-1951) and Mona Meier (1895-1967). He grew up in
       <a href="/wiki/Langesund" title="Langesund">
        Langesund
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Skien" title="Skien">
        Skien
       </a>
       where he
       <a href="/wiki/Examen_artium" title="Examen artium">
        finished
       </a>
       his secondary education in 1941. He took his undergraduate degree in astronomy at the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oslo" title="University of Oslo">
        University of Oslo
       </a>
       in 1950, and after fellowships at
       <a href="/wiki/Institut_d%27astrophysique_de_Paris" title="Institut d'astrophysique de Paris">
        Institut d'astrophysique de Paris
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caltech" title="Caltech">
        Caltech
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/High_Altitude_Observatory" title="High Altitude Observatory">
        High Altitude Observatory
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Cavendish_Laboratory" title="Cavendish Laboratory">
        Cavendish Laboratory
       </a>
       , he took his doctorate at the University of Oslo in 1960. His thesis was titled
       <i>
        An Investigation of the Temperature Conditions in Prominence with a Special Study of the Excitation of Helium
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit1_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-obit1-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
        Career
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Einar_Tandberg-Hanssen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Tandberg-Hanssen was briefly a professor at the University in Oslo, before working at the High Altitude Observatory in
       <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">
        Boulder, Colorado
       </a>
       from 1961 to 1974. He was then a senior research scientist, later deputy director at the Space Science Laboratory at NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in
       <a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Alabama" title="Huntsville, Alabama">
        Huntsville, Alabama
       </a>
       . From 1987 to 1993 he was the center's director. After retiring, he also served part-time as professor of physics at the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alabama" title="University of Alabama">
        University of Alabama
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit1_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-obit1-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Tandberg-Hanssen was vice president of Commission 10 of the
       <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">
        International Astronomical Union
       </a>
       from 1979 to 1982 and president from 1982 to 1985. He was president of the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Federation_of_Astronomical_and_Geophysical_Data_Analysis_Services&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Federation of Astronomical and Geophysical Data Analysis Services (page does not exist)">
        Federation of Astronomical and Geophysical Data Analysis Services
       </a>
       from 1990 to 1994. He was a fellow of the
       <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Academy_of_Science_and_Letters" title="Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters">
        Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters
       </a>
       from 1982 and received the
       <a href="/wiki/NASA_Exceptional_Service_Medal" title="NASA Exceptional Service Medal">
        NASA Exceptional Service Medal
       </a>
       . He died in July 2011 in Huntsville.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit1_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-obit1-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
