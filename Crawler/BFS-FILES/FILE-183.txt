      <p>
       <b>
        Fred Espenak
       </b>
       (born 1953
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       ) is a retired
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retirement_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-retirement-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Emeritus" title="Emeritus">
        emeritus
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emeritus_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-emeritus-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        American
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astrophysicist" title="Astrophysicist">
        astrophysicist
       </a>
       . He worked at the
       <a href="/wiki/Goddard_Space_Flight_Center" title="Goddard Space Flight Center">
        Goddard Space Flight Center
       </a>
       . He is best known for his work on
       <a href="/wiki/Eclipse" title="Eclipse">
        eclipse
       </a>
       predictions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twanight_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-twanight-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He became interested in astronomy when he was 7–8 years old, and had his first telescope when he was around 9–10 years old.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retirement_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-retirement-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Espenak earned a bachelor's degree in physics from
       <a href="/wiki/Wagner_College" title="Wagner College">
        Wagner College
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">
        Staten Island
       </a>
       , where he worked in the
       <a href="/wiki/Planetarium" title="Planetarium">
        planetarium
       </a>
       . His master's degree is from the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toledo" title="University of Toledo">
        University of Toledo
       </a>
       , based on studies he did at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitt_Peak_Observatory" title="Kitt Peak Observatory">
        Kitt Peak Observatory
       </a>
       of eruptive and flare stars among
       <a href="/wiki/Red_dwarf" title="Red dwarf">
        red dwarfs
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fred_Espenak.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="646" data-file-width="500" height="284" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Fred_Espenak.jpg/220px-Fred_Espenak.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Fred_Espenak.jpg/330px-Fred_Espenak.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Fred_Espenak.jpg/440px-Fred_Espenak.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fred_Espenak.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Espenak with his
         <a href="/wiki/Solar_telescope" title="Solar telescope">
          solar telescope
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       He was employed at
       <a href="/wiki/Goddard_Space_Flight_Center" title="Goddard Space Flight Center">
        Goddard Space Flight Center
       </a>
       , where he used
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infrared_spectrometer" title="Infrared spectrometer">
        infrared spectrometers
       </a>
       to measure the atmosphere of planets in the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
        Solar System
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twanight_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-twanight-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He provided
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       's eclipse bulletins since 1978. He is the author of several canonical works on eclipse predictions, such as the
       <i>
        Fifty Year Canon of Solar Eclipses: 1986–2035
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        Fifty Year Canon of Lunar Eclipses: 1986–2035
       </i>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retirement_1-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-retirement-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       both of which are standard references on eclipses.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twanight_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-twanight-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The first eclipse he saw was the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_March_7,_1970" title="Solar eclipse of March 7, 1970">
        solar eclipse of March 7, 1970
       </a>
       , which sparked his interest in eclipses,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twanight_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-twanight-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and he has since seen over 20 eclipses.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retirement_1-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-retirement-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He is co-author with
       <a href="/wiki/Jean_Meeus" title="Jean Meeus">
        Jean Meeus
       </a>
       of
       <i>
        Five Millennium Canon of Solar Eclipses
       </i>
       , which covers all types of solar eclipses (partial, total, annular, or hybrid) from 2000 BC to AD 3000.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FiveMillennium_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FiveMillennium-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He is also a co-author (with
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Littmann&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mark Littmann (page does not exist)">
        Mark Littmann
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ken_Willcoxof&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ken Willcoxof (page does not exist)">
        Ken Willcoxof
       </a>
       ) of
       <i>
        Totality: Eclipses of the Sun
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twanight_3-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-twanight-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He was the co-investigator of an atmospheric experiment flown on
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Discovery" title="Space Shuttle Discovery">
        Space Shuttle Discovery
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spearstravel_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-spearstravel-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He is also known as "Mr. Eclipse."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mreclipse_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-mreclipse-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He gives public lectures on eclipses and astrophotophy. Astronomical photographs taken by Espenak have been published in
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" title="National Geographic (magazine)">
         National Geographic
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">
         Newsweek
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nature_(magazine)" title="Nature (magazine)">
         Nature
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/New_Scientist" title="New Scientist">
         New Scientist
        </a>
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ciel_et_Espace&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ciel et Espace (page does not exist)">
         Ciel et Espace
        </a>
        <span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">
         (
         <a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ciel_et_Espace" title="fr:Ciel et Espace">
          fr
         </a>
         )
        </span>
       </i>
       magazines.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twanight_3-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-twanight-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He retired in 2009.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retirement_1-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-retirement-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">
        Asteroid
       </a>
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/14120_Espenak" title="14120 Espenak">
         14120 Espenak
        </a>
       </i>
       was named in his honor in 2003.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twanight_3-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-twanight-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
