      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">
        mathematics
       </a>
       , an
       <b>
        annulus
       </b>
       (the
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latin
       </a>
       word for "little ring" is
       <i>
        anulus
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        annulus
       </i>
       , with plural
       <i>
        anuli
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        annuli
       </i>
       ) is a ring-shaped object, a region bounded by two concentric circles. The adjectival form is
       <b>
        annular
       </b>
       (as in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annular_eclipse" title="Annular eclipse">
        annular eclipse
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       The open annulus is
       <a href="/wiki/Homeomorphism" title="Homeomorphism">
        topologically equivalent
       </a>
       to both the open
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cylinder_(geometry)" title="Cylinder (geometry)">
        cylinder
       </a>
       <span class="texhtml">
        <i>
         S
        </i>
        <sup>
         1
        </sup>
        × (0,1)
       </span>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punctured_plane" title="Punctured plane">
        punctured plane
       </a>
       . Informally, it has the shape of a
       <a href="/wiki/Washer_(hardware)" title="Washer (hardware)">
        hardware washer
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Complex_structure">
        Complex structure
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Annulus_(mathematics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Complex structure">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Complex_analysis" title="Complex analysis">
        complex analysis
       </a>
       an
       <b>
        annulus
       </b>
       <span class="texhtml">
        ann(
        <i>
         a
        </i>
        ;
        <i>
         r
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         R
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Complex_plane" title="Complex plane">
        complex plane
       </a>
       is an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_region" title="Open region">
        open region
       </a>
       defined as
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <span class="mwe-math-element">
         <span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;">
          <math alttext="{\displaystyle r&lt;|z-a|&lt;R.}" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
           <semantics>
            <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
             <mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0">
              <mi>
               r
              </mi>
              <mo>
               &lt;
              </mo>
              <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
               <mo stretchy="false">
                |
               </mo>
              </mrow>
              <mi>
               z
              </mi>
              <mo>
               −
               <!-- − -->
              </mo>
              <mi>
               a
              </mi>
              <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
               <mo stretchy="false">
                |
               </mo>
              </mrow>
              <mo>
               &lt;
              </mo>
              <mi>
               R
              </mi>
              <mo>
               .
              </mo>
             </mstyle>
            </mrow>
            <annotation encoding="application/x-tex">
             {\displaystyle r&lt;|z-a|&lt;R.}
            </annotation>
           </semantics>
          </math>
         </span>
         <img alt="{\displaystyle r&lt;|z-a|&lt;R.}" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/7067cf6785dc372d620df0d22be69e57d1cc21c0" style="vertical-align: -0.838ex; width:16.108ex; height:2.843ex;"/>
        </span>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       If
       <span class="texhtml">
        <i>
         r
        </i>
       </span>
       is
       <span class="texhtml">
        0
       </span>
       , the region is known as the
       <b>
        punctured disk
       </b>
       of radius
       <span class="texhtml">
        <i>
         R
        </i>
       </span>
       around the point
       <span class="texhtml">
        <i>
         a
        </i>
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       As a subset of the complex
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plane_(mathematics)" title="Plane (mathematics)">
        plane
       </a>
       , an annulus can be considered as a
       <a href="/wiki/Riemann_surface" title="Riemann surface">
        Riemann surface
       </a>
       . The complex structure of an annulus depends only on the ratio
       <span class="texhtml">
        <span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;">
         <span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">
          <i>
           r
          </i>
         </span>
         <span class="visualhide">
          /
         </span>
         <span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">
          <i>
           R
          </i>
         </span>
        </span>
       </span>
       . Each annulus
       <span class="texhtml">
        ann(
        <i>
         a
        </i>
        ;
        <i>
         r
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         R
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       can be
       <a href="/wiki/Holomorphic_function" title="Holomorphic function">
        holomorphically
       </a>
       mapped to a standard one centered at the origin and with outer radius 1 by the map
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <span class="mwe-math-element">
         <span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;">
          <math alttext="{\displaystyle z\mapsto {\frac {z-a}{R}}.}" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
           <semantics>
            <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
             <mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0">
              <mi>
               z
              </mi>
              <mo stretchy="false">
               ↦
               <!-- ↦ -->
              </mo>
              <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
               <mfrac>
                <mrow>
                 <mi>
                  z
                 </mi>
                 <mo>
                  −
                  <!-- − -->
                 </mo>
                 <mi>
                  a
                 </mi>
                </mrow>
                <mi>
                 R
                </mi>
               </mfrac>
              </mrow>
              <mo>
               .
              </mo>
             </mstyle>
            </mrow>
            <annotation encoding="application/x-tex">
             {\displaystyle z\mapsto {\frac {z-a}{R}}.}
            </annotation>
           </semantics>
          </math>
         </span>
         <img alt="{\displaystyle z\mapsto {\frac {z-a}{R}}.}" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ed4a0f2edf0bf8420fb9a4da1c25aab581750fad" style="vertical-align: -2.005ex; width:11.344ex; height:5.176ex;"/>
        </span>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       The inner radius is then
       <span class="texhtml">
        <span class="sfrac nowrap" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:-0.5em; font-size:85%; text-align:center;">
         <span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em;">
          <i>
           r
          </i>
         </span>
         <span class="visualhide">
          /
         </span>
         <span style="display:block; line-height:1em; margin:0 0.1em; border-top:1px solid;">
          <i>
           R
          </i>
         </span>
        </span>
        &lt; 1
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Hadamard_three-circle_theorem" title="Hadamard three-circle theorem">
        Hadamard three-circle theorem
       </a>
       is a statement about the maximum value a holomorphic function may take inside an annulus.
      </p>
