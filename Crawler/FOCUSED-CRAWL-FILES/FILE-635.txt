      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Tank on the Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        French
       </a>
       2007
       <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">
        documentary film
       </a>
       about the development, launch, and operation of the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_rover" title="Lunar rover">
        exploration rovers
       </a>
       ,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Lunokhod_1" title="Lunokhod 1">
         Lunokhod 1
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Lunokhod_2" title="Lunokhod 2">
         Lunokhod 2
        </a>
       </i>
       in the period from 1970 to 1973. The film uses historical footage from
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        American
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
        Russian
       </a>
       and French archives featuring
       <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Brezhnev" title="Leonid Brezhnev">
        Leonid Brezhnev
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">
        Yuri Gagarin
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyndon_Johnson" title="Lyndon Johnson">
        Lyndon Johnson
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">
        John F. Kennedy
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev" title="Nikita Khrushchev">
        Nikita Khrushchev
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Korolev" title="Sergei Korolev">
        Sergei Korolev
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Kosygin" title="Alexei Kosygin">
        Alexei Kosygin
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexei_Leonov" title="Alexei Leonov">
        Alexei Leonov
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sam_Rayburn" title="Sam Rayburn">
        Sam Rayburn
       </a>
       and many other contemporary figures. A special emphasis is placed on the Lunokhods' chief designer,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Kemurdjian" title="Alexander Kemurdjian">
        Alexander Kemurdjian
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">
        Summary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tank_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       During the 1960s, the United States and the Soviet Union were engaged in a feverish technological competition, popularly known as the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_race" title="Space race">
        space race
       </a>
       , to be the first to land a human on the Moon. The United States won the race, but less is known about a "secret chapter" from the
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">
        Cold War
       </a>
       era. Overshadowed by the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo Moon landings
       </a>
       and largely ignored in the
       <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">
        West
       </a>
       at the time, the Soviets were also exploring the Moon with a series of successful robotic Moon missions. The Soviets never sent humans to the Moon, but they successfully guided two freely-roving robots by remote control from the Earth. For 16 months between 1970 and 1973, these Lunokhods traveled more than fifty kilometers (31 miles) over the Moon's surface. Kept in utmost secrecy for two decades, the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_space_program#Program_Secrecy" title="Soviet space program">
        secret Soviet space archives
       </a>
       concerning this program were finally declassified.
      </p>
      <p>
       With these archives, along with the recollections by surviving participants in the Lunokhod program, and archived news films, the full story of the Soviet lunar-roving robots is revealed; the innovative development, the difficult deployment, the spectacular technological achievements, and the legacy passed down to the
       <a href="/wiki/Rover_(space_exploration)" title="Rover (space exploration)">
        new generation of planetary robotic rovers
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Production_and_video_release">
        Production and video release
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tank_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production and video release">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The following companies and television stations helped produce and broadcast this film; Zed (Paris), with Corona Films (Saint-Petersburg), France 5, Channel 5 (Russia), CBC/RDI (Canada), SVT (Sweden), RTBF (Belgium), NHK (Japan).
       <a href="/wiki/Louis_Friedman" title="Louis Friedman">
        Louis Friedman
       </a>
       , executive director of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planetary_Society" title="Planetary Society">
        Planetary Society
       </a>
       appears in the film as a commentator. The first
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_American" title="North American">
        North American
       </a>
       broadcast was on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Science_Channel" title="The Science Channel">
        The Science Channel
       </a>
       February 12, 2008.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       This video documentary was produced in French, Brazilian Portuguese and English languages. An English language DVD was released in 2008, however the video is currently available only by digital download.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
