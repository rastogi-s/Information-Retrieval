      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         The Boy Who Climbed Into the Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is a 2010 children's novel by
       <a href="/wiki/David_Almond" title="David Almond">
        David Almond
       </a>
       . It is about a boy, Paul, who wants to touch the sky; he then climbs a ladder to the Moon and goes inside.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
        Reception
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Boy_Who_Climbed_Into_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reception">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Booktrust" title="Booktrust">
         Booktrust
        </a>
       </i>
       review of
       <i>
        The Boy Who Climbed Into the Moon
       </i>
       wrote "Beautiful colour illustrations complement a surreal modern fairytale, which fluent young readers will enjoy on their own but which could equally well be read aloud."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and in a starred review
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Kirkus_Reviews" title="Kirkus Reviews">
         Kirkus Reviews
        </a>
       </i>
       wrote "Roald Dahl meets Antoine de Saint-Exupéry in this delightfully improbable tale .. Madmen are heroes and crackpots are geniuses in this charmingly over-the-top read-aloud that challenges readers to imagine the impossible. Dunbar’s abundant full-color illustrations perfectly capture the beautiful barminess of it all."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">
         Guardian
        </a>
       </i>
       review found it "charming without being twee; quirky without being whimsical; and genuinely thought-provoking without being clever-clever."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BookList" title="BookList">
         BookList
        </a>
       </i>
       called it a "quirky tale"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wccls_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-wccls-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       while the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/School_Library_Journal" title="School Library Journal">
         School Library Journal
        </a>
       </i>
       referred to "the thematically overstuffed, disjointed, and arbitrary plot".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wccls_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-wccls-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Horn_Book_Magazine" title="The Horn Book Magazine">
         The Horn Book
        </a>
       </i>
       found it "Part
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Little_Prince" title="The Little Prince">
         Little Prince
        </a>
       </i>
       and part
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Phantom_Tollbooth" title="The Phantom Tollbooth">
         Phantom Tollbooth
        </a>
       </i>
       "
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wccls_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-wccls-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        The Boy Who Climbed Into the Moon
       </i>
       has also been reviewed by
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Publishers_Weekly" title="Publishers Weekly">
         Publishers Weekly
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Common_Sense_Media" title="Common Sense Media">
         Common Sense Media

       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>

       and
       <i>
