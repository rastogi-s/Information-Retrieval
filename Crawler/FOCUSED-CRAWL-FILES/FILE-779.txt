      <p>
       The
       <b>
        JUpiter ICy moons Explorer
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        JUICE
       </b>
       ) is an interplanetary spacecraft in development by the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">
        European Space Agency
       </a>
       (ESA) with
       <a href="/wiki/Airbus_Defence_and_Space" title="Airbus Defence and Space">
        Airbus Defence and Space
       </a>
       as the main contractor. The mission is being developed to visit the
       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
        Jovian
       </a>
       system focused on studying three of Jupiter's
       <a href="/wiki/Galilean_moons" title="Galilean moons">
        Galilean moons
       </a>
       :
       <a href="/wiki/Ganymede_(moon)" title="Ganymede (moon)">
        Ganymede
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Callisto_(moon)" title="Callisto (moon)">
        Callisto
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Europa_(moon)" title="Europa (moon)">
        Europa
       </a>
       (excluding the more volcanically active
       <a href="/wiki/Io_(moon)" title="Io (moon)">
        Io
       </a>
       ) all of which are thought to have significant bodies of liquid water beneath their surfaces, making them
       <a href="/wiki/Planetary_habitability" title="Planetary habitability">
        potentially habitable
       </a>
       environments.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-l1_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-l1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The spacecraft is set for launch in 2022 and would reach Jupiter in 2030. By 2033 the spacecraft should enter orbit around Ganymede for its close up science mission and becoming the first spacecraft to orbit a moon other than the moon of Earth. The selection of this mission for the L1 launch slot of ESA's
       <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_Vision" title="Cosmic Vision">
        Cosmic Vision
       </a>
       science programme was announced on 2 May 2012.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-selection_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-selection-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Its period of operations will overlap with
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Europa_Clipper" title="Europa Clipper">
        Europa Clipper
       </a>
       mission, also launching in 2022.
      </p>

      <p>
       The mission started as a reformulation of the
       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter_Ganymede_Orbiter" title="Jupiter Ganymede Orbiter">
        Jupiter Ganymede Orbiter
       </a>
       proposal, which was to be
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ESA" title="ESA">
        ESA
       </a>
       's component of the cancelled
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EJSM/Laplace" title="EJSM/Laplace">
        Europa Jupiter System Mission—Laplace
       </a>
       (EJSM-Laplace).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It became a candidate for the first L-class mission (L1) of the ESA
       <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_Vision" title="Cosmic Vision">
        Cosmic Vision
       </a>
       Programme, and its selection was announced on 2 May 2012.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-selection_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-selection-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In April 2012 JUICE was recommended over the proposed
       <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Telescope_for_High_Energy_Astrophysics" title="Advanced Telescope for High Energy Astrophysics">
        ATHENA
       </a>
       X-ray telescope and a
       <a href="/wiki/Gravitational_wave" title="Gravitational wave">
        gravitational wave
       </a>
       observatory (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Gravitational_wave_Observatory" title="New Gravitational wave Observatory">
        New Gravitational wave Observatory
       </a>
       (NGO)).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In July 2015, Airbus Defence and Space was selected as the Prime Contractor to design and build the probe.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plan">
        Plan
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jupiter_Icy_Moons_Explorer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plan">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A proposed timeline is launch in 2022 on an
       <a href="/wiki/Ariane_6" title="Ariane 6">
        Ariane 6
       </a>
       carrier rocket and arrival at the Jupiter system in 2030.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-l1_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-l1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       By 2033 the spacecraft should enter orbit around Ganymede, after completing various manoeuvres around Jupiter and the other moons.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-l1_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-l1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Proposed instruments include cameras,
       <a href="/wiki/Spectrometer" title="Spectrometer">
        spectrometers
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Magnetometer" title="Magnetometer">
        magnetometers
       </a>
       , and an
       <a href="/wiki/Radioglaciology" title="Radioglaciology">
        ice-penetrating radar
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-l1_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-l1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ice_radar_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ice_radar-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Science_objectives">
        Science objectives
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jupiter_Icy_Moons_Explorer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Science objectives">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Europa_g1_true.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="370" data-file-width="781" height="190" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Europa_g1_true.jpg/400px-Europa_g1_true.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Europa_g1_true.jpg/600px-Europa_g1_true.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/45/Europa_g1_true.jpg 2x" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Europa_g1_true.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <center>
          Section of Europa's icy surface
         </center>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The JUpiter ICy moons Explorer (JUICE) will perform detailed investigations on
       <a href="/wiki/Ganymede_(moon)" title="Ganymede (moon)">
        Ganymede
       </a>
       as a planetary body and evaluate its
       <a href="/wiki/Planetary_habitability" title="Planetary habitability">
        potential to support life
       </a>
       . Investigations of
       <a href="/wiki/Europa_(moon)" title="Europa (moon)">
        Europa
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Callisto_(moon)" title="Callisto (moon)">
        Callisto
       </a>
       will complete a comparative picture of these
       <a href="/wiki/Galilean_moons" title="Galilean moons">
        Galilean moons
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-objectives_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-objectives-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The three moons are thought to harbour internal liquid-water oceans, and so are central to understanding the
       <a href="/wiki/Planetary_habitability" title="Planetary habitability">
        habitability
       </a>
       of icy worlds.
      </p>
      <p>
       The main science objectives for Ganymede, and to a lesser extent for Callisto, are:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-objectives_11-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-objectives-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Characterisation of the ocean layers and detection of putative subsurface water reservoirs;
       </li>
       <li>
        Topographical, geological and compositional mapping of the surface;
       </li>
       <li>
        Study of the physical properties of the icy crusts;
       </li>
       <li>
        Characterisation of the internal mass distribution, dynamics and evolution of the interiors;
       </li>
       <li>
        Investigation of Ganymede's
        <a href="/wiki/Ganymede_(moon)#Atmosphere_and_ionosphere" title="Ganymede (moon)">
         tenuous atmosphere
        </a>
        ;
       </li>
       <li>
        Study of Ganymede's intrinsic
        <a href="/wiki/Ganymede_(moon)#Magnetosphere" title="Ganymede (moon)">
         magnetic field
        </a>
        and its interactions with the
        <a href="/wiki/Magnetosphere_of_Jupiter" title="Magnetosphere of Jupiter">
         Jovian magnetosphere
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       For Europa, the focus is on the chemistry essential to
       <a href="/wiki/Life" title="Life">
        life
       </a>
       , including
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organic_molecule" title="Organic molecule">
        organic molecules
       </a>
       , and on understanding the formation of surface features and the composition of the non-water-ice material. Furthermore, JUICE will provide the first subsurface sounding of the moon, including the first determination of the minimal thickness of the icy crust over the most recently active regions.
      </p>
      <p>
       More distant spatially resolved observations will also be carried for several minor irregular satellites and volcanic moon Io.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Spacecraft">
        Spacecraft

      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_conditions_and_design">
        Mission conditions and design
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jupiter_Icy_Moons_Explorer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mission conditions and design">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The main spacecraft design drivers are related to the large distance to the Sun, the use of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_array" title="Solar array">
        solar power
       </a>
       , and Jupiter's harsh radiation environment. The orbit insertions at Jupiter and Ganymede and the large number of flyby manoeuvres (more than 25
       <a href="/wiki/Gravity_assist" title="Gravity assist">
        gravity assists
       </a>
       , and two Europa flybys) requires the spacecraft to carry about 3,000 kg (6,600 lb) of chemical propellant.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacecraft_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-spacecraft-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Gravity assists include:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gravity_Assist_13-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gravity_Assist-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Interplanetary transfer (Earth, Venus, Earth, Mars, Earth)
       </li>
       <li>
        Jupiter orbit insertion and apocentre reduction with multiple Ganymede gravity assists
       </li>
       <li>
        Reduction of velocity with Ganymede–Callisto assists
       </li>
       <li>
        Increase inclination with 10–12 Callisto gravity assists
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ganymede_Lander">
        Ganymede Lander
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jupiter_Icy_Moons_Explorer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ganymede Lander">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Space_Research_Institute" title="Russian Space Research Institute">
        Russian Space Research Institute
       </a>
       is currently evaluating a Ganymede lander mission called
       <a href="/wiki/Laplace-P" title="Laplace-P">
        Laplace-P
       </a>
       , with emphasis in
       <a href="/wiki/Astrobiology" title="Astrobiology">
        astrobiology
       </a>
       . Cooperation and a possible synergy with JUICE Ganymede orbital mission is being discussed between ESA and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federal_Space_Agency" title="Russian Federal Space Agency">
        Roscosmos
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laplasp_14-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-laplasp-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Russia has also proposed to power the JUICE spacecraft with a Russian-built
       <a href="/wiki/Radioisotope_thermoelectric_generator" title="Radioisotope thermoelectric generator">
        radioisotope thermoelectric generator
       </a>
       (RTG), replacing solar panels that would be vulnerable to Jupiter's radiation.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laplasp_14-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-laplasp-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

          Radio &amp; Plasma Wave Investigation
         </center>
        </td>
        <td>
         <center>
          <b>
           RPWI
          </b>
         </center>
        </td>
        <td>
         <i>
          RPWI
         </i>
         will characterise the plasma environment and radio emissions around the spacecraft, it is composed of four experiments: GANDALF, MIME, FRODO and JENRAGE.
         <i>
          RPWI
         </i>
         will use four Langmuir probes, each one mounted at the end of its own dedicated boom, and sensitive up to 1.6 MHz to characterize plasma and receivers in the frequency range 80 kHz – 45 MHz to measure radio emissions.
         <ul>
          <li>
           <small>
            Principal investigator: J.-E. Wahlund,
            <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Institute_of_Space_Physics" title="Swedish Institute of Space Physics">
             Swedish Institute of Space Physics
            </a>
            , Sweden
           </small>
          </li>
          <li>
           <small>
            Lead Funding Agency:
            <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SNSB" title="SNSB">
             SNSB
            </a>
            , Sweden
           </small>
          </li>
         </ul>
        </td>
       </tr>
       <tr>
        <td>
         <center>
          Gravity &amp; Geophysics of jupiter and Galilean Moons
         </center>
        </td>
        <td>
         <center>
          <b>
           3GM
          </b>
         </center>
        </td>
        <td>
         3GM instrument, composed of a Ka transponder and an ultrastable oscillator, will measure the gravity field of Ganymede and the extent of internal oceans. It will also study the ionosphere and upper atmosphere of Jupiter and other Jovian moons.
         <ul>
          <li>
           <small>
            Principal investigator: L. Iess,
            <a href="/wiki/Sapienza_University_of_Rome" title="Sapienza University of Rome">
             Sapienza University of Rome
            </a>
            , Italy
           </small>
          </li>
          <li>
           <small>
            Lead Funding Agency:
            <a href="/wiki/Italian_Space_Agency" title="Italian Space Agency">
             ASI
            </a>
            , Italy
           </small>
          </li>
         </ul>
        </td>
       </tr>
       <tr>
        <td>
         <center>
          Planetary Radio Interferometer &amp; Doppler Experiment
         </center>
        </td>
        <td>
         <center>
          <b>
           PRIDE
          </b>
         </center>
        </td>
        <td>
         <i>
          PRIDE
         </i>
         experiment will generate specific signals transmitted by
         <i>
          JUICE
         </i>
         antenna and received by
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Very_Long_Baseline_Interferometry" title="Very Long Baseline Interferometry">
          Very Long Baseline Interferometry
         </a>
         to perform precision measurements of the gravity fields and of Jupiter and the icy moons .
         <ul>
          <li>
           <small>
            Principal investigator: L. Gurvits,
            <a href="/wiki/Joint_Institute_for_VLBI_in_Europe" title="Joint Institute for VLBI in Europe">
             Joint Institute for VLBI in Europe
            </a>
            , The Netherlands
           </small>
          </li>

           <small>

             NWO

            and
