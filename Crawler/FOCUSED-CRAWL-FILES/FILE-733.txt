      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Once in a Very Blue Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is
       <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">
        singer-songwriter
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Nanci_Griffith" title="Nanci Griffith">
        Nanci Griffith
       </a>
       's third album. The sounds have become a little more
       <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">
        country
       </a>
       and a little less
       <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">
        folk
       </a>
       than her previous albums. Her first two albums were backed sparsely with instrumentation, but starting with this album, the whole complement of country-styled instrumentalists can be heard. Noted country musicians performing on the album include
       <a href="/wiki/Banjo" title="Banjo">
        banjo
       </a>
       player
       <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9la_Fleck" title="Béla Fleck">
        Béla Fleck
       </a>
       and champion
       <a href="/wiki/Fiddle" title="Fiddle">
        fiddle
       </a>
       player
       <a href="/wiki/Mark_O%27Connor" title="Mark O'Connor">
        Mark O'Connor
       </a>
       . The title song was covered by
       <a href="/wiki/Dolly_Parton" title="Dolly Parton">
        Dolly Parton
       </a>
       , who included her version on her
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Real_Love_(Dolly_Parton_album)" title="Real Love (Dolly Parton album)">
         Real Love
        </a>
       </i>
       album in 1985.
      </p>
      <p>
       The track order was different on MCA vinyl MCG 6054 with track 11 being omitted.
      </p>
