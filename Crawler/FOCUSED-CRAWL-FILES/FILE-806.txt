      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carme_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Carme did not receive its present name until 1975;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       before then, it was simply known as
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         Jupiter XI
        </span>
       </b>
       . It was sometimes called "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan_(mythology)" title="Pan (mythology)">
        Pan
       </a>
       "
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaposchkin_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gaposchkin-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       between 1955 and 1975 (
       <a href="/wiki/Pan_(moon)" title="Pan (moon)">
        Pan
       </a>
       is now the name of a satellite of
       <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">
        Saturn
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       It gives its name to the
       <a href="/wiki/Carme_group" title="Carme group">
        Carme group
       </a>
       , made up of irregular retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23 and 24 Gm and at an inclination of about 165°. Its orbital elements are as of January 2000.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jacobson_2000_2-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Jacobson_2000-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They are continuously changing due to
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        solar
       </a>
       and planetary perturbations.
      </p>
