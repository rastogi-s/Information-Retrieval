      <p>
       <b>
        Telesto
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="'t' in 'tie'">
            t
           </span>
           <span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">
            ə
           </span>
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'l' in 'lie'">
            l
           </span>
           <span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">
            ɛ
           </span>
           <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
            s
           </span>
           <span title="'t' in 'tie'">
            t
           </span>
           <span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">
            oʊ
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         tə-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          LES
         </span>
         -toh
        </i>
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">
        Greek
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="el">
        Τελεστώ
       </span>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">
        moon
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">
        Saturn
       </a>
       . It was discovered by
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_A._Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bradford A. Smith (page does not exist)">
        Smith
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Harold_Reitsema" title="Harold Reitsema">
        Reitsema
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_M._Larson" title="Stephen M. Larson">
        Larson
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=John_W._Fountain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="John W. Fountain (page does not exist)">
        Fountain
       </a>
       in 1980 from ground-based observations, and was provisionally designated
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         S/1980 S 13
        </span>
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3466_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3466-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the following months, several other apparitions were observed:
       <span class="nowrap">
        S/1980 S 24
       </span>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3484_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3484-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <span class="nowrap">
        S/1980 S 33
       </span>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3605_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3605-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and
       <span class="nowrap">
        S/1981 S 1
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3593_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIAUC_3593-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1983 it was officially named after
       <a href="/wiki/Telesto_(mythology)" title="Telesto (mythology)">
        Telesto
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">
        Greek mythology
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mythology_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-mythology-8">
         [a]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is also designated as
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         Saturn XIII
        </span>
       </b>
       (13) or
       <b>
        Tethys B
       </b>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Telesto is co-orbital with
       <a href="/wiki/Tethys_(moon)" title="Tethys (moon)">
        Tethys
       </a>
       , residing in Tethys' leading
       <a href="/wiki/Lagrangian_point" title="Lagrangian point">
        Lagrangian point
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Lagrangian_point#L4_and_L5" title="Lagrangian point">
        L
        <sub>
         4
        </sub>
       </a>
       ). This relationship was first identified by
       <a href="#CITEREFSeidelmann_Harrington_et_al._1981">
        Seidelmann
        <i>
         et al.
        </i>
       </a>
       in 1981.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESeidelmann_Harrington_et_al._1981_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESeidelmann_Harrington_et_al._1981-9">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Another moon,
       <a href="/wiki/Calypso_(moon)" title="Calypso (moon)">
        Calypso
       </a>
       , resides in the other (trailing) Lagrangian point of Tethys, 60 degrees in the other direction from Tethys. The Saturnian system has two additional
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trojan_moon" title="Trojan moon">
        trojan moons
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Exploration">
        Exploration
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Telesto_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Exploration">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cassini-Huygens_Mission" title="Cassini-Huygens Mission">
         Cassini
        </a>
       </i>
       probe performed a distant flyby of Telesto on October 11, 2005. The resulting images show that its surface is surprisingly smooth, devoid of small
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact craters
       </a>
       .
      </p>
