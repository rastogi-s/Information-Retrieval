      <p>
       <b>
        Chibiusa
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja">
         ちびうさ
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Chibiusa or "Chibi-usa"
        </i>
        , renamed "Rini Tsukino" in some English adaptations)
       </span>
       is a fictional character in the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon" title="Sailor Moon">
         Sailor Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       manga series created by
       <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">
        Naoko Takeuchi
       </a>
       . She is introduced in Chapter 13, "Conclusion and Commencement, Petite Étrangere", first published in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Nakayoshi" title="Nakayoshi">
         Nakayoshi
        </a>
       </i>
       on February 3, 1993. She is a small child from the 30th century who travels to the past to seek help from the Sailor Soldiers. She later returns, a few years older, in order to train as a Soldier herself—
       <b>
        Sailor Chibi Moon
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja">
         セーラーちびムーン
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Sērā Chibi Mūn
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       , translated as "Sailor Mini Moon" in the
       <a href="/wiki/DIC_Entertainment" title="DIC Entertainment">
        DIC
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloverway" title="Cloverway">
        Cloverway
       </a>
       English adaptations.
      </p>
      <p>
       Chibiusa's birth name is
       <b>
        Usagi Small Lady Serenity
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja">
         うさぎ
         <ruby>
          <rb>
           S
          </rb>
          <rp>
           (
          </rp>
          <rt>
           スモール
          </rt>
          <rp>
           )
          </rp>
         </ruby>
         <ruby>
          <rb>
           L
          </rb>
          <rp>
           (
          </rp>
          <rt>
           レディ
          </rt>
          <rp>
           )
          </rp>
         </ruby>
         セレニティ
        </span>
        ,
        <i>
         Usagi Sumōru Redi Sereniti
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Takeuchi_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Takeuchi-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       She is adopted as a member of her mother's family, using the alias
       <b>
        Usagi Tsukino
       </b>
       , in the 20th century. She is given her nickname to differentiate from the older
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)" title="Sailor Moon (character)">
        Usagi Tsukino
       </a>
       , Sailor Moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The nickname is a combination of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Chibi_(term)" title="Chibi (term)">
         chibi
        </a>
       </i>
       (meaning small person or small child) and her
       <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">
        given name
       </a>
       , Usagi.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Profile">
        Profile
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chibiusa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Profile">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Author
       <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">
        Naoko Takeuchi
       </a>
       describes Chibiusa as a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precocious" title="Precocious">
        precocious
       </a>
       brat who likes pulling pranks.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-materials_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-materials-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       She is the daughter of
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)" title="Sailor Moon (character)">
        Neo-Queen Serenity
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Mask" title="Tuxedo Mask">
        King Endymion
       </a>
       . Her real name is the same as her mother's, but is called
       <i>
        Chibiusa
       </i>
       in Japanese to avoid confusion between the two. Her name in several
       <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">
        English-language
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(English_versions)" title="Sailor Moon (English versions)">
        versions of Sailor Moon
       </a>
       ,
       <i>
        Rini
       </i>
       , is a diminutive of "Serena", the name of her mother. She is known for having
       <a href="/wiki/Pink" title="Pink">
        pink
       </a>
       hair put into a unique style that resembles rabbit ears on top of her head. Chibiusa's hair is apparently truly
       <a href="/wiki/Pink" title="Pink">
        pink
       </a>
       , and not just colored that way for stylistic purposes; its color is stated in the manga's first Picture Diary
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol5picturediary_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-vol5picturediary-4">
         [4]

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Age">
        Age
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [

        </a>

         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Exposure to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silver_Crystal" title="Silver Crystal">
        Silver Crystal
       </a>
       renders all of Crystal Tokyo's inhabitants functionally
       <a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">
        immortal
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and her frequent
       <a href="/wiki/Time_travel" title="Time travel">
        time traveling
       </a>
       further complicates approximation of her age.
      </p>
      <p>
       Chibiusa is 5 years old approximate in both civil registration and physical appearance; however, she possesses a degree of
       <a href="/wiki/Maturity_(psychological)" title="Maturity (psychological)">
        intellectual maturity
       </a>
       that is normally non-existent in a 5-year-old girl. In act 20 of the manga, it's stated that she is chronologically 900 years old, which lends credibility to her apparent wisdom, maturity, and rather serious personality over her teenage biological mother Usagi (in the 20th century). Her youthful appearance attributed to the fact that her physical body inexplicably halted its natural aging process when she was five. The reason for this is assumed to be caused by having not "awakened" as a Sailor Guardian, which eventually occurred when she first became Sailor Chibi Moon.
      </p>
      <p>
       The candles on a
       <a href="/wiki/Birthday_cake" title="Birthday cake">
        birthday cake
       </a>
       in one memory indicate that she is about five years old when first introduced.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode85_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode85-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       By the time of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Sailor_Moon_Super_S_episodes" title="List of Sailor Moon Super S episodes">
        <i>
         Dream
        </i>
        story arc
       </a>
       , she claims to be 902.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hammer_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-hammer-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Crystal" title="Sailor Moon Crystal">
         Sailor Moon Crystal
        </a>
       </i>
       , it is stated clearly that she is 900 years old. She seems to be the equivalent of a 7 or 8 year old when she returns later. In the manga, after the
       <i>
        Infinity Dream
       </i>
       story arc, its mentioned she is in second grade; in the
       <i>
        Stars
       </i>
       story arc, she is in sixth grade; in the
       <i>
        Sailor Moon Parallel
       </i>
       story arc, she is in the ninth grade.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">
        Relationships
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

        </span>

      <p>
       Chibiusa's affections come into conflict. Despite being
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-aware" title="Self-aware">
        self-aware
       </a>
       of the fact that
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)" title="Sailor Moon (character)">
        Usagi Tsukino
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Mask" title="Tuxedo Mask">
        Mamoru Chiba
       </a>
       are her biological parents, she does not immediately identify them as such, instead suspiciously viewing them as strangers who look like them.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode104_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode104-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There is evidence that Chibiusa has a hard time identifying Usagi and Mamoru as being her parents since she sees such a sharp contrast in personality between their present and future selves. However, it is worth mentioning their forms King Endymion and Neo Queen Serenity are far in the future, leaving enough time to mature. She also reacts differently toward Usagi and Mamoru than she does towards King Endymion and Neo-Queen Serenity, being more respectful to the latter but more spontaneous to the former;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode104_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode104-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       it is not clear if she prefers one set over the other. However, she always calls her teenage father Mamoru as Mamo-chan (in the 20th century), an affectionate nickname by Usagi, and
       <a href="/wiki/Electra_complex" title="Electra complex">
        has an innocent crush on him
       </a>
       , much to Usagi's fury.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode104_9-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode104-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the anime, she often picks on Usagi for being clumsy more.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode104_9-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode104-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Super_S:_The_Movie" title="Sailor Moon Super S: The Movie">
         Sailor Moon Super S: The Movie
        </a>
       </i>
       , she describes Usagi as "
       <a href="/wiki/Blonde_stereotype" title="Blonde stereotype">
        a klutz, a ditz, and a crybaby
       </a>
       ;" however, there are many instances where Usagi and Chibiusa show their love and concern for one another, such as when Sailor Moon breaks down crying after
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mistress_9" title="Mistress 9">
        Mistress 9
       </a>
       takes Chibiusa's pure heart crystal,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode123_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode123-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       or when she dives off a balcony to save her.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode166_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode166-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Nevertheless, the two often bicker, often behaving more like sisters than mother and daughter;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode104_9-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode104-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       however, in the manga, although both bicker towards one another, Chibiusa is shown to have an admiration for Usagi. At times, a mother and daughter relationship is established between the two, shown in various everyday actions such as Usagi holding Chibiusa's hand to cross the street, etc. In Sailor Moon Crystal, Usagi talks to Chibiusa as in a mix of motherly and sister fashion.
      </p>
      <p>
       Before Usagi's discovery of Chibiusa's real identity, Chibiusa was kept under surveillance by Usagi- believing that Chibiusa may possibly be an enemy. Throughout the progression of the
       <i>
        Black Moon
       </i>
       Arc, Usagi slowly comes to understand Chibiusa's motives and reasons for her actions- until her feelings towards Chibiusa is replaced from anguish and jealousy, to love and sympathy. A contrast in the beginning and end of
       <i>
        Sailor Moon Crystal'
       </i>
       s
       <i>
        Black Moon
       </i>
       Arc is shown, in which Usagi expresses her rage upon Chibiusa's arrival, whilst in the latter as the season ends Usagi is shown to be tearfully enjoying Chibiusa's return.
      </p>

         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>

        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>

       <a href="/wiki/Perfectionism_(psychology)" title="Perfectionism (psychology)">
        perfectionist
       </a>
       and starts off trying to do everything alone and for herself. This view evolves as she realizes what maturity really is and what it really means to be an adult.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the original anime, she is more judgmental and does not mature as much as her manga counterpart. But she does in Sailor Moon Crystal and gets along better with Usagi.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sailor_Chibi_Moon">

        </span>
       </span>

      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ChibiusaManga.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="230" data-file-width="273" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/63/ChibiusaManga.jpg/220px-ChibiusaManga.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/63/ChibiusaManga.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">

          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ChibiusaManga.jpg" title="Enlarge">

        </div>

      <p>
       <b>
        Sailor Chibi Moon
       </b>
       is Chibiusa's identity as a Sailor Soldier. Her
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_fuku" title="Sailor fuku">
        uniforms
       </a>
       resemble Sailor Moon's, colored primarily in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Variations_of_pink#Cherry_blossom_pink" title="Variations of pink">
        cherry blossom pink
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Red" title="Red">
        red
       </a>
       , with a prominent motif of small hearts, joined later by crescent moons and stars. Due to her small stature, certain details of her uniforms are slightly abbreviated. She is given various titles throughout the series, including Princess Soldier,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act35_15-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-act35-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Apprentice Soldier, and Soldier in Training.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode103_25-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode103-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As the daughter of Sailor Moon and Tuxedo Mask, she exhibits traits of both parents in battle. Her personality is no different from when she is a civilian, although certain powers are unavailable to her in that form.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the manga Sailor Chibi Moon's powers are significant, able to destroy and kill enemies. She attacks in tandem with Sailor Moon, often combining attacks with her, and combining one with Tuxedo Mask. Late in the series, it is heavily implied that she will marry her own prince in the future,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_minor_Sailor_Moon_characters#Pegasus/Helios" title="List of minor Sailor Moon characters">
        Helios
       </a>
       . She also develops and matures as a Sailor Soldier throughout the manga, assisted by her own guardians, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazoness_Quartet" title="Amazoness Quartet">
        Sailor Quartet
       </a>
       , and eventually saving Sailor Moon's life.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the anime she is made into more of a comic character that has little to no effect on her enemies. She uses mainly distraction techniques and plays a side role, instead of a role that is directly useful to the Sailor Soldiers. While her powers are specifically based on Sailor Moon's in words, they are more like that of her father's — distractions rather than effective attacks. She links her powers with her parents' in the movies, but not in the TV series. It is clear, though, that she does have some true raw power, as seen in
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Sailor_Moon_episodes" title="List of Sailor Moon episodes">
        Episode 81
       </a>
       , and in the final battle sequences where she uses the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silver_Crystal" title="Silver Crystal">
        Silver Crystal
       </a>
       . However, when she uses her powers as a regular character this rarely comes into play.
      </p>
      <p>
       Her powers in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Musicals" title="Sailor Moon Musicals">
        musicals
       </a>
       are based on both her anime and manga incarnations. In
       <i>
        Last Dracul
       </i>
       she heads to the enemy hideout alone only to have her attacks fail; In the revised version of
       <i>
        Transylvania no Mori
       </i>
       she laments that a phrase from a game similar to Red light Green light is more useful than her most powerful attack when dealing with the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazoness_Quartet" title="Amazoness Quartet">
        Sammael Quartet
       </a>
       . Conversely, in the same musicals she is able to combine her powers with
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)" title="Sailor Moon (character)">
        Sailor Moon
       </a>
       for multiple powerful attacks that damage/heal enemies. It is only in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Supers" title="Sailor Moon Supers">
        fourth season
       </a>
       of the anime that she gains a more relevant role as Pegasus gives her a new compact to become Super Sailor Chibi Moon for the first time, and she becomes the only one able to summon him to give assistance to Sailor Moon in performing the attacks that defeat the enemies' minions.
      </p>
      <p>
       As she grows stronger, Sailor Chibi Moon gains additional power, and at key points her uniform changes to reflect this. The first change is first seen in act 33 of the manga, when the Holy Chalice of the future allows her to turn into
       <b>
        Super Sailor Chibi Moon
       </b>
       (or
       <b>
        Super Sailor Mini Moon
       </b>
       in the English anime). Later,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_minor_Sailor_Moon_characters#Helios/Pegasus" title="List of minor Sailor Moon characters">
        Pegasus
       </a>
       gives her a compact which lets her transform without the Grail. In episode 128 of the anime, this gift is what causes her to become Super Sailor Chibi Moon for the first time. A third, manga-only form appears in act 42, unnamed but analogous to Eternal Sailor Moon (
       <i>
        sans
       </i>
       wings).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Act42_26-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Act42-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>

      <p>

        Princess Usagi Small Lady Serenity
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja">

         <ruby>

           S
          </rb>
          <rp>

          </rp>
          <rt>

          </rt>
          <rp>
           )
          </rp>
         </ruby>
         <ruby>

           L
          </rb>
          <rp>
           (
          </rp>
          <rt>
           レディ
          </rt>
          <rp>
           )
          </rp>
         </ruby>
         セレニティ
        </span>

      <p>
       In the manga, this form appears more frequently and receives more explanation. It is the name given to her by her parents so she often uses this to identify herself in the future. The only other character to use it, besides the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_family" title="Royal family">
        royal family
       </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cats_(Sailor_Moon)#Diana" title="Cats (Sailor Moon)">

       <i>
        Artbooks IV
       </i>

       <i>
        Materials Collection
       </i>

      <p>
       In the anime, Chibiusa changes into her princess form when she needs more power than her Soldier or civilian forms allow, similar to how her parents can become Prince Endymion and Princess Serenity when they need more power. In this form, she wears a dress and jewelry identical to that of Princess Serenity,
       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/albeit" title="wikt:albeit">
        albeit
       </a>

        </a>
       </sup>

       <i>
        SuperS
       </i>
       climax. She does this after Usagi, as Serenity, grabs her while they are falling, and the two of them collide with Pegasus. It is unknown if she grew the wings on her own or with Usagi's or Pegasus' help. The Chibiusa of the future, Small Lady, is also shown in her princess dress when she is not transformed, so this form is not exclusive to power-ups.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnepisode104_9-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-jnepisode104-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       </b>

        </i>

       </span>

       <a href="/wiki/Organdy" title="Organdy">
        organdy
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Satin" title="Satin">

       <i>

       </i>

Black Lady later confronts the Sailor Guardians directly to take their Silver Crystal, discarding her birthright while summoning additional chucks of Malefic Back Crystal to speed up 30th Century Earth's destruction. But when Sailor Pluto sacrificed herself to prevent Demand from accidently destroying the universe with the two Silver Crystals, Black Lady emotionally broke down upon remembering her friendship with Pluto. Black Lady's anguish crying awaken the future Silver Crystal's power, shattering Wiseman's spell over her as she reverts to her original form and awakens as Sailor Chibi Moon.

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Development">
        Development
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Adding in Usagi's child to the Sailor Moon storyline, as well as her name, Chibiusa for a
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chibi_(Japanese_term)" title="Chibi (Japanese term)">
         chibi
        </a>
       </i>
       (little) Usagi, was the idea of Naoko Takeuchi's editor,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fumio_Osano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fumio Osano (page does not exist)">
        Fumio Osano
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52">
        <a href="#cite_note-52">
         [52]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Takeuchi has stated that she did not think much about how Chibiusa's hair would look, besides it having odango like Usagi's hair and being pink; and that she used a Chibiusa doll for reference on the hair.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-piccol2_53-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-piccol2-53">
         [53]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The first appearance of Chibiusa as a Sailor Soldier is in a side story, a year and a half before Sailor Chibi Moon made her debut in the manga. In the first Picture Diary, Chibiusa is rescued by Sailors Moon and Venus and dreams that night about becoming a "soldier of love and justice" herself. She calls herself "Sailor Chibiusa Moon" and appears in a uniform slightly different from the one she wears later, most notably including a cape.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54">
        <a href="#cite_note-54">
         [54]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Takeuchi struggled with a name for Black Lady, initially calling her "the Black Queen" and retaining her childlike appearance.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-materials_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-materials-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Takeuchi also noted that the leg-slit on Black Lady's dress was even more revealing in the anime than in the manga, something which shocked her as she had wondered if the slit was going too far in her drawings of Black Lady.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-piccol2_53-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-piccol2-53">
         [53]
        </a>
       </sup>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_influence">
        Reception and influence
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chibiusa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Reception and influence">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Japanese official
       <i>
        Sailor Moon
       </i>
       character popularity poll listed Chibiusa, Sailor Chibi Moon, Princess Lady Serenity, and Black Lady as separate entities. In 1993, with fifty choices, Chibiusa was the most popular character, Black Lady was the thirteenth most popular, and Luna-P, an item of Chibiusa's, was the seventeenth most popular character.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59">
        <a href="#cite_note-59">
         [59]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 1994, with fifty one choices, Sailor Chibi Moon was the second most popular character, Chibiusa was the eighth most popular, and Luna-P was the twenty sixth most popular character.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60">
        <a href="#cite_note-60">
         [60]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In early 1996, with fifty one choices, Sailor Chibi Moon was again the second most popular character, Chibiusa was the eleventh most popular, and Princess Lady Serenity, an older form of Small Lady, was the twenty fourth most popular character.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61">
        <a href="#cite_note-61">
         [61]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Despite this, Chibiusa has been described by Jonathan Clements and Helen McCarthy as "unpopular" even among Japanese audiences, at least in the anime.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyc338_62-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-encyc338-62">
         [62]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
