   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Barcelona Moon Team
   </h1>

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Barcelona Moon Team
       </b>
       is a Spanish team led by
       <a href="/wiki/Galactic_Suite_Design" title="Galactic Suite Design">
        Galactic Suite Design
       </a>
       , which is participating in the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Barcelona_Moon_Team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Galactic_Suite_Design" title="Galactic Suite Design">
        Galactic Suite Design
       </a>
       (GSD) is the lead company in a consortium of companies fielding the Barcelona Moon Team in the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize
       </a>
       (GLXP) competition.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired20120809_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-wired20120809-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       GSD does so through a filial company, Galactic Suite Moon Race. BMT is the official candidate to the GLXP.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glxp111110_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-glxp111110-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The team is a multidisciplinary joint venture bringing together Spanish entrepreneurial, industrial and academic capabilities. The team also includes the Centre of Aerospace Technology in Barcelona (
       <a href="/wiki/Centre_de_Technologia_Aerospacial" title="Centre de Technologia Aerospacial">
        CTAE
       </a>
       ), the
       <a href="/wiki/Polytechnic_University_of_Catalonia" title="Polytechnic University of Catalonia">
        Polytechnic University of Catalonia
       </a>
       (UPC) or the international engineering advisory firm
       <a href="/wiki/Altran" title="Altran">
        Altran
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Altran211010_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Altran211010-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       BMT wants to promote a widening involvement of private initiative in the development of space technology and industry, also including sectors such as exploration and tourism.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glxp111110_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-glxp111110-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Barcelona Moon Team GXLP mission was scheduled to launch aboard a
       <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
        Chinese
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Long_March_2C" title="Long March 2C">
        Long March 2C
       </a>
       in June 2015.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired20120809_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-wired20120809-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iaNews20120809_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-iaNews20120809-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The China Great Wall Industry Corporation (CGWIC) was planned to be launching team Barcelona Moon in June 2015.
      </p>
      <p>
       But later, the team withdrew from the competition.
      </p>
