      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       (styled as
       <i>
        <b>
         Moon.
        </b>
       </i>
       ) is a Japanese
       <a href="/wiki/Eroge" title="Eroge">
        adult
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Visual_novel" title="Visual novel">
        visual novel
       </a>
       developed by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tactics_(company)" title="Tactics (company)">
        Tactics
       </a>
       , a brand of
       <a href="/wiki/Nexton" title="Nexton">
        Nexton
       </a>
       , released on November 21, 1997 playable on
       <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">
        Windows
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">
        PCs
       </a>
       . The game was described by the development team as a "Reaching the Heart
       <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game" title="Adventure game">
        AVG
       </a>
       "
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         心に届くAVG
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Kokoro ni Todoku AVG
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-original-website_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-original-website-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The story follows the protagonist Ikumi Amasawa, a girl who joins an organization called Fargo in the hopes of discovering why and how her mother died, who was a member of the same group. The
       <a href="/wiki/Gameplay" title="Gameplay">
        gameplay
       </a>
       in
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       follows a
       <a href="/wiki/Nonlinear_gameplay#Branching_storylines" title="Nonlinear gameplay">
        branching plot line
       </a>
       which offers pre-determined scenarios with courses of interaction, and focuses on the three female main characters. The game ranked twice in the national top 50 for best-selling PC games sold in Japan.
      </p>
      <p>
       Much of the staff that created the game later became the founding members of the visual novel brand
       <a href="/wiki/Key_(company)" title="Key (company)">
        Key
       </a>
       .
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       was the starting point for Key's origins, and was the first time the principal Key team was formed. A novel based on the game written by
       <a href="/wiki/Midori_Tateyama" title="Midori Tateyama">
        Midori Tateyama
       </a>
       was released in July 1998 by
       <a href="/wiki/Movic" title="Movic">
        Movic
       </a>
       . The game's original soundtrack was released bundled with
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/D%C5%8Dsei" title="Dōsei">
         Dōsei
        </a>
        '
       </i>
       s soundtrack in August 2000 at
       <a href="/wiki/Comiket" title="Comiket">
        Comiket 58
       </a>
       ;
       <i>
        Dōsei
       </i>
       was Tactics' first game.
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       has been referenced in other media not directly related to the game, such as in Tactics' third game
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/One:_Kagayaku_Kisetsu_e" title="One: Kagayaku Kisetsu e">
         One: Kagayaku Kisetsu e
        </a>
       </i>
       , and in the second
       <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">
        anime
       </a>
       adaptation of Key's first game
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Kanon_(visual_novel)" title="Kanon (visual novel)">
         Kanon
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon-in-One_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon-in-One-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon-in-Kanon_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon-in-Kanon-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">
        Gameplay
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Moon._original_screenshot.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="273" data-file-width="364" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/27/Moon._original_screenshot.jpg/200px-Moon._original_screenshot.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/27/Moon._original_screenshot.jpg/300px-Moon._original_screenshot.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/27/Moon._original_screenshot.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Moon._original_screenshot.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Average dialogue and narrative in
         <i>
          Moon
         </i>
         depicting the main character Ikumi talking to Haruka.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">
        drama
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Horror_fiction" title="Horror fiction">
        horror
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Visual_novel" title="Visual novel">
        visual novel
       </a>
       in which the player assumes the role of Ikumi Amasawa. Much of its
       <a href="/wiki/Gameplay" title="Gameplay">
        gameplay
       </a>
       is spent on reading the story's
       <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">
        narrative
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Dialogue" title="Dialogue">
        dialogue
       </a>
       .
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       follows a
       <a href="/wiki/Nonlinear_gameplay#Branching_storylines" title="Nonlinear gameplay">
        branching plot line
       </a>
       with multiple endings, and depending on the decisions that the player makes during the game, the plot will progress in a specific direction.
      </p>
      <p>
       There are three main plot lines that the player will have the chance to experience, one for each of the heroines in the story. Throughout gameplay, the player is given multiple options to choose from, and text progression pauses at these points until a choice is made. To view all plot lines in their entirety, the player will have to replay the game multiple times and choose different choices to further the plot to an alternate direction. The player has to manually navigate through the corridors of Fargo to explore the surroundings.
      </p>
      <p>
       The game is split into 20 sub-parts each given a title in English written mostly in white where a portion of the title is displayed in red, and each covers a single day.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There are seven endings to the game: a single true end, two normal ends, and four bad ends.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the original game, a
       <a href="/wiki/Omake" title="Omake">
        bonus
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">
        role-playing game
       </a>
       became available on the title screen after the game was completed at least once. This was later removed from the full-voice DVD edition of the game. There are scenes in
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       with sexual
       <a href="/wiki/Computer_graphics" title="Computer graphics">
        CGs
       </a>
       depicting a given heroine having sex.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_and_characters">
        Plot and characters
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot and characters">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">
        's
       </span>
       story revolves around a mysterious organization known as Fargo. The protagonist of the story is a young girl named Ikumi Amasawa
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         天沢郁未
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Amasawa Ikumi
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         voiced by:
        </i>
        Ruru)
       </span>
       who discovers that Fargo was involved with the death of her mother Miyoko Amasawa
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         天沢未夜子
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Amasawa Miyoko
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       six years before the story begins.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-original-website_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-original-website-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Miyoko, who would often make special cream stew for her daughter, was a member of Fargo. Following this revelation, Ikumi joins Fargo to discover how and why her mother died while also slowly uncovering the group's disturbing nature of harnessing psychic abilities through torture.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon_DVD_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon_DVD-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Ikumi ultimately wants to take out her revenge against Fargo for killing her mother.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-original-website_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-original-website-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ikumi had been one of ten girls her age that gathered at a seminar for people who wanted to join Fargo; she had been ordered to get on a truck which would take the small group to Fargo, and on the ride, Ikumi meets two other girls with similar goals in mind as her.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-original-website_1-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-original-website-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       One of the girls is named Haruka Mima
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         巳間晴香
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Mima Haruka
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         voiced by:
        </i>
        Aya)
       </span>
       who joined Fargo in order to save her older brother. She starts to work with Ikumi for the sake of their mutual goals.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon_DVD_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon_DVD-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The second girl is named Yui Nakura
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         名倉由依
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Nakura Yui
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         voiced by:
        </i>
        <a href="/wiki/Miya_Serizono" title="Miya Serizono">
         Miya Serizono
        </a>
        )
       </span>
       who is searching for her older sister Yuri Nakura
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         名倉友里
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Nakura Yuri
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         voiced by:
        </i>
        Komugi Nishida)
       </span>
       who joined Fargo in order to obtain the 'invisible power', a kind of
       <a href="/wiki/Extrasensory_perception" title="Extrasensory perception">
        extrasensory perception
       </a>
       . Yui has an optimistic personality which hides a gloomy past of which she has no memory of. Each of the girls have a strong will to complete their respective purposes, and they help each other along the way.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon_DVD_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon_DVD-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       At Fargo, Ikumi meets others who are affiliated with the group. One such person is Yōko Kanuma
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         鹿沼葉子
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Kanuma Yōko
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         voiced by:
        </i>
        Satomi Kodama)
       </span>
       , a modest believer in Fargo's cause; something happened in her past related to her mother. At Fargo, Ikumi lives with a boy known as "Shōnen"
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         少年
        </span>
        , lit.
        <i>
         Boy
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         Young man
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         voiced by:
        </i>
        Arashi Tsunami)
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon_DVD_6-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon_DVD-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He helps Ikumi in her search for the answers regarding her mother; it is never revealed what his true name is. Others include Takatsuki
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         高槻
        </span>
        ,
        <i>
         voiced by:
        </i>
        Matsuri Yūatono)
       </span>
       , a man in Fargo with an arrogant attitude, and Tsuki
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         月
        </span>
        , lit.
        <i>
         Moon
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       , the symbol of Fargo and the mastermind behind the entire operation; he is the main antagonist.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon_DVD_6-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon_DVD-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_release">
        Development and release
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development and release">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">
        's
       </span>
       production was headed by YET11, the pseudonym of Tsutomu Yoshizawa.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erogamescape_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-erogamescape-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Planning for
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       was headed by
       <a href="/wiki/Jun_Maeda" title="Jun Maeda">
        Jun Maeda
       </a>
       , who also worked on the game's scenario with fellow writer
       <a href="/wiki/Naoki_Hisaya" title="Naoki Hisaya">
        Naoki Hisaya
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erogamescape_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-erogamescape-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Art direction and character design was provided by
       <a href="/wiki/Itaru_Hinoue" title="Itaru Hinoue">
        Itaru Hinoue
       </a>
       , while the
       <a href="/wiki/Computer_graphics" title="Computer graphics">
        computer graphics
       </a>
       in the game was supplemented by Miracle Mikipon and Shinory. The game's soundtrack was primarily
       <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">
        composed
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Orito" title="Shinji Orito">
        Shinji Orito
       </a>
       , with two tracks composed by YET11, and one track each composed by Maeda and Ishisan.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erogamescape_7-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-erogamescape-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soundtrack_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-soundtrack-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Excluding YET11 and Ishisan, the staff that created the game later became the founding members of the visual novel brand
       <a href="/wiki/Key_(company)" title="Key (company)">
        Key
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       was first released on November 21, 1997 playable on a
       <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">
        Windows
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">
        PC
       </a>
       as a
       <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">
        CD-ROM
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-original-website_1-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-original-website-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Nexton released
       <i>
        Moon Renewal
       </i>
       on August 21, 1998 with an improved
       <a href="/wiki/Game_engine" title="Game engine">
        game engine
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       AI System published the game under the title
       <i>
        Moon Memorial Selection
       </i>
       on September 14, 2000 for a cheaper price.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Nexton released
       <i>
        Moon DVD LimitedEdition
       </i>
       on July 12, 2002 with added support for
       <a href="/wiki/Windows_98" title="Windows 98">
        Windows 98
       </a>
       /
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windows_Me" title="Windows Me">
        Me
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Windows_2000" title="Windows 2000">
        2000
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Windows_XP" title="Windows XP">
        XP
       </a>
       as a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DVD-ROM" title="DVD-ROM">
        DVD-ROM
       </a>
       ; this release was also called
       <i>
        Moon DVD Final Version
       </i>
       by Nexton.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon_DVD_6-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon_DVD-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The DVD edition, and subsequent releases, also included full voice acting, two
       <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">
        animated
       </a>
       sequences at the beginning of the game, and improved graphics.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon_DVD_6-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon_DVD-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A CD-ROM version of the DVD edition titled
       <i>
        Moon CD LimitedEdition
       </i>
       was released on three CDs on September 20, 2002.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comshop-CDLE_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-comshop-CDLE-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Nexton released
       <i>
        Moon DVDPG Edition
       </i>
       was released in Japan on January 30, 2003 playable as a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DVD_game" title="DVD game">
        DVD game
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Nexton later re-released the DVD edition with updated support for
       <a href="/wiki/Windows_XP" title="Windows XP">
        Windows XP
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Windows_Vista" title="Windows Vista">
        Vista
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Windows_7" title="Windows 7">
        7
       </a>
       on April 2, 2010.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Related_media">

       <i>

       </i>

       <i>

       . The album contains a single CD and was released on August 10, 2000 at
       <a href="/wiki/Comiket" title="Comiket">

       </a>

       <i>
        Dōsei
       </i>
       and the latter 16 are from
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       .

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
        Reception
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(visual_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       According to a national ranking of how well
       <a href="/wiki/Bish%C5%8Djo_game" title="Bishōjo game">
        bishōjo games
       </a>
       sold nationally in Japan, the
       <i>
        Moon DVD
       </i>
       Windows release made the ranking of the top 50 games once at number 44 during the first two weeks of July 2002.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rank44_17-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-rank44-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        Moon Limited
       </i>
       also made it on the list only once, achieving the ranking of 48 in late September 2002.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rank48_18-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-rank48-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Ikumi Amasawa appeared in the
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eternal_Fighter_Zero" title="Eternal Fighter Zero">
         Eternal Fighter Zero
        </a>
       </i>
       <a href="/wiki/D%C5%8Djin_soft" title="Dōjin soft">
        dōjin games
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Twilight_Frontier" title="Twilight Frontier">
        Twilight Frontier
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Four characters from
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       can be seen as background characters during scenes in the cafeteria from
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/One:_Kagayaku_Kisetsu_e" title="One: Kagayaku Kisetsu e">
         One: Kagayaku Kisetsu e
        </a>
       </i>
       ; the characters are: Ikumi, Yōko Kanuma, Haruka Mima, and Yui Nakura.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon-in-One_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon-in-One-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There is a reference to
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       in the sixth episode of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Animation" title="Kyoto Animation">
        Kyoto Animation
       </a>
       version of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Kanon_(visual_novel)" title="Kanon (visual novel)">
         Kanon
        </a>
       </i>
       , which can be seen as the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_poster" title="Film poster">
        film poster
       </a>
       of a horror film that Ayu and Yuichi go to see.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moon-in-Kanon_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moon-in-Kanon-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
