        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ijiraq_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Orbit">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="400" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg/300px-TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg/450px-TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg/600px-TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg.png 2x" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Irregular prograde groups of satellites of Saturn:
         <a href="/wiki/Saturn%27s_Inuit_group_of_satellites" title="Saturn's Inuit group of satellites">
          Inuit
         </a>
         (blue) and
         <a href="/wiki/Saturn%27s_Gallic_group_of_satellites" title="Saturn's Gallic group of satellites">
          Gallic
         </a>
         (red). The eccentricity of the orbits is represented by the yellow segments extending from the
         <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
          pericentre
         </a>
         to the
         <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
          apocentre
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Ijiraq orbits Saturn at an average distance of 11.1 Gm in 451 days on an orbit very similar to
       <a href="/wiki/Kiviuq_(moon)" title="Kiviuq (moon)">
        Kiviuq
       </a>
       's. Ijiraq is believed to be in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kozai_resonance" title="Kozai resonance">
        Kozai resonance
       </a>
       : its orbit is cyclically reducing the inclination while increasing the eccentricity and vice versa. The orbital
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argument_of_pericenter" title="Argument of pericenter">
        argument of pericenter
       </a>
       oscillates around 90° with an amplitude of 60°.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nesvorny2003_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Nesvorny2003-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div style="clear:left;">
      </div>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_characteristics">
        Physical characteristics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ijiraq_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Physical characteristics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       While Ijiraq is a member of the
       <a href="/wiki/Saturn%27s_Inuit_group_of_satellites" title="Saturn's Inuit group of satellites">
        Inuit group
       </a>
       of irregular satellites,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gladman2001_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gladman2001-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       recent observations revealed that it is distinctively redder than
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paaliaq_(moon)" title="Paaliaq (moon)">
        Paaliaq
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siarnaq_(moon)" title="Siarnaq (moon)">
        Siarnaq
       </a>
       and Kiviuq. Its
       <a href="/wiki/Spectral_slope" title="Spectral slope">
        spectral slope
       </a>
       (a measure of body
       <a href="/wiki/Reflectance" title="Reflectance">
        reflectance
       </a>
       in function of the
       <a href="/wiki/Wavelength" title="Wavelength">
        wavelength
       </a>
       ) is twice as steep as that of other Inuit-group satellites (20% per 100 nm), typical for red
       <a href="/wiki/Trans-Neptunian_object" title="Trans-Neptunian object">
        trans-Neptunian objects
       </a>
       like
       <a href="/wiki/90377_Sedna" title="90377 Sedna">
        Sedna
       </a>
       but unknown for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irregular_satellite" title="Irregular satellite">
        irregular satellites
       </a>
       . In addition, the Ijiraupian (Ijiraqan) spectrum lacks the weak absorption near 0.7 μm, attributed to a possible water hydration, found in the other three.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grav2006_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grav2006-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
        Name
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ijiraq_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Name">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Ijiraq was named in 2003 after the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Ijiraq_(mythology)" title="Ijiraq (mythology)">
         ijiraq
        </a>
       </i>
       , a creature of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inuit_mythology" title="Inuit mythology">
        Inuit mythology
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbat.eps.harvard.edu_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-cbat.eps.harvard.edu-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Kavelaars, an astronomer at
       <a href="/wiki/McMaster_University" title="McMaster University">
        McMaster University
       </a>
       , suggested this name to help astronomical nomenclature to get out of its
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greco-Roman" title="Greco-Roman">
        Greco-Romano
       </a>
       -
       <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">
        Renaissance
       </a>
       rut. He spent several months trying to find names that were both multi-cultural and
       <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">
        Canadian
       </a>
       , consulting
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amerindian" title="Amerindian">
        Amerindian
       </a>
       scholars without finding a name that seemed appropriate. In March 2001, he was reading an Inuit tale to his children and had a revelation. The
       <i>
        ijiraq
       </i>
       plays at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hide_and_seek" title="Hide and seek">
        hide-and-seek
       </a>
       , which is what these small moons of Saturn do: they are hard to find, and cold like the Canadian arctic (the team of discoverers includes Canadians,
       <a href="/wiki/Norwegians" title="Norwegians">
        Norwegians
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Icelanders" title="Icelanders">
        Icelanders
       </a>
       —nordicity is their common trait). Kavelaars contacted the author of the tale,
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kusugak" title="Michael Kusugak">
        Michael Kusugak
       </a>
       , to get his assent, and the latter also suggested the names for
       <a href="/wiki/Kiviuq_(moon)" title="Kiviuq (moon)">
        Kiviuq
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/90377_Sedna" title="90377 Sedna">
        90377 Sedna
       </a>
       .
      </p>
