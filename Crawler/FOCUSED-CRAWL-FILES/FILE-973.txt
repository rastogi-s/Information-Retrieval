      <p>
       <b>
        (45) Eugenia I Petit-Prince
       </b>
       is the larger, outer
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asteroid_moon" title="Asteroid moon">
        moon
       </a>
       of asteroid
       <a href="/wiki/45_Eugenia" title="45 Eugenia">
        45 Eugenia
       </a>
       . It was discovered in 1998 by astronomers at the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada-France-Hawaii_Telescope" title="Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope">
        Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope
       </a>
       on
       <a href="/wiki/Mauna_Kea" title="Mauna Kea">
        Mauna Kea
       </a>
       , Hawaii. Initially, it received the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provisional_designation" title="Provisional designation">
        provisional designation
       </a>
       <b>
        S/1998 (45) 1
       </b>
       . Petit-Prince was the first
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asteroid_moon" title="Asteroid moon">
        asteroid moon
       </a>
       to be discovered with a ground-based telescope. Previously, the only known moon of an asteroid was
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dactyl_(moon)" title="Dactyl (moon)">
        Dactyl
       </a>
       , discovered by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galileo_probe" title="Galileo probe">
        Galileo space probe
       </a>
       , around
       <a href="/wiki/243_Ida" title="243 Ida">
        243 Ida
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">
        Characteristics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Petit-Prince_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Petit-Prince is 13 km in diameter, compared to 45 Eugenia's 214 km. It takes five days to complete an orbit around Eugenia.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
        Name
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Petit-Prince_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The discoverers chose the name in honour of
       <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A9nie_de_Montijo" title="Eugénie de Montijo">
        Empress Eugénie
       </a>
       's son, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Eugene,_Prince_Imperial" title="Napoleon Eugene, Prince Imperial">
        Prince Imperial
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, they also intended an allusion to the children's book
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Little_Prince" title="The Little Prince">
         The Little Prince
        </a>
       </i>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Antoine_de_Saint-Exup%C3%A9ry" title="Antoine de Saint-Exupéry">
        Antoine de Saint-Exupéry
       </a>
       , which is about a prince who lives on an asteroid.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-William_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-William-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In their submission of the name to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAU" title="IAU">
        IAU
       </a>
       , the discovers justified the double meaning by arguing for similarities between the Prince Imperial and the Little Prince:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        "Both princes were young and adventurous, and had little fear of danger. Both were of rather small stature. They both left the confines of their cozy little worlds (asteroid
        <a href="/wiki/46610_B%C3%A9sixdouze" title="46610 Bésixdouze">
         B612
        </a>
        for the Little Prince and
        <a href="/wiki/Chislehurst" title="Chislehurst">
         Chislehurst
        </a>
        for the Prince Imperial). They both then undertook long journeys to end up in Africa, whereupon they both meet rather violent deaths ... And in both cases they lay alone for one night each after "death" and then "returned" back home..."
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-William_7-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-William-7">
          [7]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </dd>
      </dl>
