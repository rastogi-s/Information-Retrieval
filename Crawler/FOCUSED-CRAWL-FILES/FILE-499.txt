      <p>
       <b>
        Russell
       </b>
       is the
       <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">
        lava
       </a>
       -flooded remains of a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       . It is located in the western part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Oceanus_Procellarum" title="Oceanus Procellarum">
        Oceanus Procellarum
       </a>
       , close to the western lunar limb. As a result, it appears oblong-shaped due to foreshortening.
      </p>
      <p>
       The south-southwestern rim of Russell overlaps the larger rim of the lava-flooded crater
       <a href="/wiki/Struve_(crater)" title="Struve (crater)">
        Struve
       </a>
       , and together the two crater rims form a figure-8 outline with a wide gap where they are joined. To the east of Russell is
       <a href="/wiki/Briggs_(crater)" title="Briggs (crater)">
        Briggs
       </a>
       , and to the southeast, adjacent to
       <a href="/wiki/Struve_(crater)" title="Struve (crater)">
        Struve
       </a>
       , is the lava-flooded remains of a crater called
       <a href="/wiki/Eddington_(crater)" title="Eddington (crater)">
        Eddington
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The rim of Russell is heavily worn and irregular in form, with multiple crater impacts overlying the wall. The largest of these is Briggs A on the eastern rim. To the north of Russell are the flooded remnants of several smaller craters. Russell's lava-flooded floor is flat and level with the surrounding
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_mare" title="Lunar mare">
        mare
       </a>
       . It lacks a central peak.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the past this crater was sometimes designated as Otto Struve A, or just assumed to be part of the larger Struve. The crater Eddington to the southeast has also been designated as Otto Struve A on old lunar maps.
      </p>
