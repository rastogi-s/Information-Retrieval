      <p>
       <b>
        LUNAR-A
       </b>
       is a cancelled
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japanese
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">
        spacecraft
       </a>
       project that was originally scheduled to be launched in August 2004. After many delays (primarily due to potential thruster faults),
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the project was eventually cancelled in January 2007.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was planned to be launched on a Japanese
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M-5_rocket" title="M-5 rocket">
        M-V
       </a>
       rocket from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Space_Center" title="Kagoshima Space Center">
        Kagoshima Space Center
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The vehicle would have been cylindrical, with a diameter of 2.2 m and a height of 1.7 m. It would have had four
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_module" title="Photovoltaic module">
        solar panels
       </a>
       and was engineered to be spin-stabilized. Plans called for it to enter an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elliptical_orbit" title="Elliptical orbit">
        elliptical orbit
       </a>
       around the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       , and deploy two penetrators at an altitude of 40 km on opposite sides of the lunar body. The penetrators were to have been braked by a small rocket at an altitude of 25 km, then free fall to the surface. They were designed to withstand a collision speed of 330 meters per second to deeply penetrate the lunar
       <a href="/wiki/Regolith" title="Regolith">
        regolith
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Once the penetrators deployed, the LUNAR-A spacecraft was mission-planned to maneuver to an orbital altitude of 200 km above the lunar surface. The craft was to have carried a monochromatic imaging camera with a resolution of 30 m.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
