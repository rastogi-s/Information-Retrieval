      <p>
       "
       <b>
        Moon/Blossom
       </b>
       " is Japanese singer
       <a href="/wiki/Ayumi_Hamasaki" title="Ayumi Hamasaki">
        Ayumi Hamasaki
       </a>
       's forty-eighth (forty-ninth overall) single, released on July 14, 2010.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-announcement_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-announcement-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The first song on the single "Moon" is used to promote
       <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">
        Honda
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Honda_Zest" title="Honda Zest">
        Zest Spark
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Kei_car" title="Kei car">
        Kei car
       </a>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       while the second song "Blossom" is used to promote
       <a href="/wiki/Kiwifruit_industry_in_New_Zealand" title="Kiwifruit industry in New Zealand">
        Zespri
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Kiwifruit" title="Kiwifruit">
        kiwifruit
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The song "Moon" was first released as a Chaku-Uta sample for members of Ayu's official fanclub
       <i>
        Team Ayu
       </i>
       with the release of the album
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Rock_%27n%27_Roll_Circus" title="Rock 'n' Roll Circus">
         Rock 'n' Roll Circus
        </a>
       </i>
       ; the announcement of the single was publicly released first on Twitter, first by Ayu's producer
       <a href="/wiki/Max_Matsuura" title="Max Matsuura">
        Max Matsuura
       </a>
       and then relayed into the "Ayu News" Twitter account, which was officially managed by
       <a href="/wiki/Avex_Group" title="Avex Group">
        Avex Group
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       This single is her first of three singles in the "road to fiftieth (50th) single", which would be followed by
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Crossroad_(song)" title="Crossroad (song)">
         Crossroad
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/L_(Ayumi_Hamasaki_EP)" title="L (Ayumi Hamasaki EP)">
         L
        </a>
       </i>
       , and it is expected that there will be a preview for the 49th single in Chaku-Uta form, though that can only be accessible through a serial number that will be released in limited first press editions of the release, with the forty-ninth (49th) and fiftieth singles also having its own serial numbers for users to access special content, which include never-before-released pictures sent to Japanese cellphones as well as PC wallpapers, though that site is not in operation until as late as late 2010/early 2011.
      </p>
      <p>
       A month before its release, the music video for Blossom was cancelled, and as such, the other CD/DVD version (AVCD-31892/B) was cancelled.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-announcement_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-announcement-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The music video for Blossom was later released on her 49th single, Crossroad.
      </p>
      <p>
       The single is certified Gold for shipment of 100,000 copies
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">
        Music video
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon/Blossom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music video">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A 2:56 minute clip for
       <b>
        Moon
       </b>
       was released on June 9, 2010 in
       <a class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0AF8AFhvwjQ" rel="nofollow">
        Ayumi Hamasaki's YouTube channel
       </a>
       . The full
       <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">
        PV
       </a>
       was released on July 13, 2010, a day before the single was available in stores. The video featured Ayumi singing in a European aristocratic room setting. As the video progresses a strange, black, tar-like liquid begins dropping in the room, on random furniture. The video is also inter-cut with scenes of Ayumi smiling while holding one of her pet dogs, in her second verse it shown ayumi with the black liquid falling down all over her face, near chest and going to her hands. Depicting sorrow or despair, presumably of the dog shown towards the end of the video.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        On
        <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">
         YouTube
        </a>
        , the music video of Moon now has approximately 1,400,000 views. The full music video runtime of moon is 05:50. The music video of Moon was directed by
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Masashi_Muto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Masashi Muto (page does not exist)">
         Masashi Muto
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The music video of
       <i>
        Blossom
       </i>
       was cancelled, but a music video appeared for Crossroad.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Ayumi does not appear in the video, but it features
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Jaejoong" title="Kim Jaejoong">
         Kim Jaejoong
        </a>
        (of
        <a href="/wiki/JYJ" title="JYJ">
         JYJ
        </a>
        ). The MV's plot starts out with Jaejoong enjoying time with his girlfriend at the beach, etc. However, in the middle of the video, Jaejoong visits to a doctor, and finds out that he is diagnosed with terminal cancer (3rd stage) and has only two weeks left to survive. Later, Jaejoong goes to meet his girlfriend to celebrate the birthday. Then, when Jaejoong's girlfriend is bringing the cake he faints. The ending scene shows Jaejoong's girlfriend a few weeks later, alone, (which means that he died the day he fainted) looking at a painting of them both, what Jaejoong was trying to paint in the beginning of the MV. The paint was her and Jaejoong as an angel. There is a director's cut of the MV as well. The running time of "Blossom" is 6:53. This music video was not uploaded to Ayumi Hamasaki's YouTube account.
       </li>
      </ul>
