      <p>
       <b>
        Lunar Lion
       </b>
       is a team of
       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_University" title="Pennsylvania State University">
        Penn State
       </a>
       students and engineers from the Penn State
       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_University_Applied_Research_Laboratory" title="Pennsylvania State University Applied Research Laboratory">
        Applied Research Laboratory
       </a>
       . The team hopes to develop and land their spacecraft, the
       <b>
        Lunar Lion
       </b>
       on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . The team is led by Michael Paul, a space systems engineer at
       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_University_Applied_Research_Laboratory" title="Pennsylvania State University Applied Research Laboratory">
        Penn State’s Applied Research Laboratory
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The team, which is privately funded, has secured its first $2.5 million, most of which was internal funding, to get the project started. They expected to raise a significant amount through fund raising and individual contributions through their website, but have fallen short of their goals.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The team was formerly a competitor in the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize competition
       </a>
       , but has since withdrawn.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Spacecraft">
        Spacecraft
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Penn_State_Lunar_Lion_Team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Spacecraft">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar Lion
       </b>
       is the
       <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">
        spacecraft
       </a>
       currently being developed to reach the Moon. The craft is planned to be sent on a commercial launch vehicle which will send it into space and place it on a
       <a href="/wiki/Trajectory" title="Trajectory">
        trajectory
       </a>
       to the Moon. It is then expected to make a controlled descent to the lunar surface where it will transmit high-resolution photographic images and video to the mission operations center at
       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_University" title="Pennsylvania State University">
        Penn State
       </a>
       . The craft will then take off and fly a short distance to a second landing site while streaming the event live.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Testing_and_development">
        Testing and development
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Penn_State_Lunar_Lion_Team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Testing and development">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phase_0">
        Phase 0
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Penn_State_Lunar_Lion_Team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Phase 0">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       At the end of October 2013, the team successfully finished
       <b>
        Phase 0
       </b>
       , a series of tests that validated the rocket-testing procedure. The phase included the development of contingency plans, safety plans, and thorough rocket testing procedure. As part of this phase, full rocket firing was simulated and investigated. Test plans must meet the Environmental Health and Safety Standards.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-first-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phase_1">
        Phase 1
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Penn_State_Lunar_Lion_Team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Phase 1">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Phase 1 included testing of combustible liquid fuel, a
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       -provided pencil thruster, and custom thrusters developed at Penn State.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-first-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phase_2">
        Phase 2
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Penn_State_Lunar_Lion_Team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Phase 2">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The initial liquid bipropellant part of this phase was completed towards the end of summer 2014. The team is now characterizing and testing
       <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_peroxide" title="Hydrogen peroxide">
        hydrogen peroxide
       </a>
       monopropellant thrusters. These are used on the first flight vehicle the team has constructed, a prototype called Puma. Each of the four 100 lbf engines is currently being characterized in order to generate a thrust profile which will be used on Puma's flight testing. Puma was developed for constrained vertical flight, with guide cables restricting motion to a single axis. Static testing of Puma began during the summer of 2016, and a constrained flight test is scheduled for the fall semester. As of 10/22/2016, Puma made its first flight to an altitude of 3.5 feet, soft landing under its own power.
      </p>
