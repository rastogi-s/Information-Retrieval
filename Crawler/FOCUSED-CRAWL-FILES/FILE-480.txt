      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Dovedale by Moonlight
        </b>
       </i>
       is one of five paintings by
       <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Wright_of_Derby" title="Joseph Wright of Derby">
        Joseph Wright of Derby
       </a>
       which uses the picturesque valley of
       <a href="/wiki/Dovedale" title="Dovedale">
        Dovedale
       </a>
       as its subject. These paintings were sometimes made as pairs with one showing the view by day and the other by moonlight. Wright admitted that he had not observed this scene directly, "Moon lights &amp; fire lights are but a sort of work with me for I cant with impunity go out at night and study the former, &amp; the latter I have seen but once, and at a time too, when I thought not of painting such effects."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oberlin2_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-oberlin2-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
        Description
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dovedale_by_Moonlight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">

        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="375" data-file-width="600" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg/220px-Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg/330px-Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg/440px-Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Study_for_a_painting_of_Dovedale.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         A preparatory sketch for these paintings (now in
         <a href="/wiki/Derby_Museum_and_Art_Gallery" title="Derby Museum and Art Gallery">
          Derby Museum and Art Gallery
         </a>
         ). The unusual trees are said to be
         <i>
          after
         </i>
         <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Cozens" title="Alexander Cozens">
          Alexander Cozens
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Dovedale" title="Dovedale">
        Dovedale
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Dovedale" title="Dovedale">
        Dovedale
       </a>
       is a very popular
       <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">
        dale
       </a>
       in Staffordshire and Wright's home county of
       <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">
        Derbyshire
       </a>
       . Admired in Wright's time, it has been owned by the
       <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">
        National Trust
       </a>
       since 1924 and made available to the many
       <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">
        Peak District
       </a>
       visitors.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Dovedale is named for the
       <a href="/wiki/River_Dove,_Central_England" title="River Dove, Central England">
        River Dove
       </a>
       and it is 3 miles (5 km) long.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peak_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-peak-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Wright's paintings appear to be from nature, but in a letter of 1787 he said that he had observed moonlight and firelight only once at night and this was some time before he decided to create this series of paintings.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oberlin2_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-oberlin2-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However his preparatory sketches show that he was using a mixture of studies and chance. The unusual reversal of light and shade in the trees in the sketch of Dovedale is said to be after using techniques created by the Russian born
       <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Cozens" title="Alexander Cozens">
        Alexander Cozens
       </a>
       . Wright owned paintings by Cozens who taught his students to paint landscapes. He told them to create blots on paper and then use these as inspiration for the composition.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tate_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-tate-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dovedale_by_Moonlight&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are no known Derbyshire landscapes by Wright that are dateable before 1786. One of the problems is that many of Wright's paintings are dated by consulting Wright's accounts in his notebook. In the case of his English landscapes this has proved challenging. It is known that some of the Dovedale pictures are some of his earliest English landscapes. Wright frequently repeated a popular subject in his paintings as he did with his
       <a href="/wiki/Virgil%27s_Tomb_(Joseph_Wright_paintings)" title="Virgil's Tomb (Joseph Wright paintings)">
        Virgil's Tomb
       </a>
       pictures and his
       <a href="/wiki/The_Blacksmith%27s_Shop" title="The Blacksmith's Shop">
        Blacksmith's Shop
       </a>
       paintings. In this case he made five very similar paintings. The version at Oberlin College was painted in 1785 to 1786 and sold to
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Miller%3F_Mundy_(fl._1786)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Edward Miller? Mundy (fl. 1786) (page does not exist)">
        Edward Mundy
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Shipley_Hall" title="Shipley Hall">
        Shipley Hall
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oberlin_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-oberlin-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The college obtained it in 1951. There is a matching painting to this which is a similar view but in daylight. That painting is still in Derbyshire at
       <a href="/wiki/Parwich_Hall" title="Parwich Hall">
        Parwich Hall
       </a>
       which is in a small village close to Dovedale. Wright also did a daylight view of Dovedale in 1787 which he gave to the industrialist
       <a href="/wiki/Josiah_Wedgwood" title="Josiah Wedgwood">
        Josiah Wedgwood
       </a>
       out of respect for his patronage on the arts. This painting is now in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wedgwood_Museum" title="Wedgwood Museum">
        Wedgwood Museum
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jw_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jw-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Two other paintings of Dovedale were sold to
       <a href="/wiki/Sir_Brooke_Boothby,_6th_Baronet" title="Sir Brooke Boothby, 6th Baronet">
        Sir Brooke Boothby bt.
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Ashbourne_Hall" title="Ashbourne Hall">
        Ashbourne Hall
       </a>
       , who had helped Wright when he put on the first one-man exhibition in London.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liver_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-liver-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Boothby also purchased two views of nearby Matlock, two paintings of bridges in Rome as well as an unusual 1781 portrait of himself lying by a brook holding a book by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rousseau" title="Rousseau">
        Rousseau
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
