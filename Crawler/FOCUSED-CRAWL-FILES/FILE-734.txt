      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Traveling Wilburys Vol. 3
        </b>
       </i>
       is the second and final album by the
       <a href="/wiki/Traveling_Wilburys" title="Traveling Wilburys">
        Traveling Wilburys
       </a>
       , a group consisting of
       <a href="/wiki/George_Harrison" title="George Harrison">
        George Harrison
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Lynne" title="Jeff Lynne">
        Jeff Lynne
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">
        Bob Dylan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tom_Petty" title="Tom Petty">
        Tom Petty
       </a>
       . It was released in October 1990 as the follow-up to their 1988 debut,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Traveling_Wilburys_Vol._1" title="Traveling Wilburys Vol. 1">
         Traveling Wilburys Vol. 1
        </a>
       </i>
       . The band members again adopted pseudonyms for their contributions, using new names from the fictitious Wilbury brothers.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Traveling_Wilburys_Vol._3&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Though it was their second release, the album was mischievously titled
       <i>
        Vol. 3
       </i>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/George_Harrison" title="George Harrison">
        George Harrison
       </a>
       . According to
       <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Lynne" title="Jeff Lynne">
        Jeff Lynne
       </a>
       , "That was George's idea. He said, 'Let's confuse the buggers.'"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       As the dynamics within the band had shifted with
       <a href="/wiki/Roy_Orbison" title="Roy Orbison">
        Roy Orbison
       </a>
       's death, the four remaining members all adopted new Wilbury pseudonyms: Spike (
       <a href="/wiki/George_Harrison" title="George Harrison">
        George Harrison
       </a>
       ), Clayton (
       <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Lynne" title="Jeff Lynne">
        Jeff Lynne
       </a>
       ), Muddy (
       <a href="/wiki/Tom_Petty" title="Tom Petty">
        Tom Petty
       </a>
       ) and Boo (
       <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">
        Bob Dylan
       </a>
       ). With Harrison and Lynne producing again, the sessions were undertaken in the spring of 1990. An additional track, a cover of "
       <a href="/wiki/Nobody%27s_Child_(song)" title="Nobody's Child (song)">
        Nobody's Child
       </a>
       ", was recorded and released in June 1990 as a charity single in aid of
       <a href="/wiki/Olivia_Harrison" title="Olivia Harrison">
        Olivia Harrison
       </a>
       's Romanian Angel Appeal. The song was also the title track of a multi-artist fundraising album compiled by the Harrisons,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Nobody%27s_Child:_Romanian_Angel_Appeal" title="Nobody's Child: Romanian Angel Appeal">
         Nobody's Child: Romanian Angel Appeal
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <i>
        Traveling Wilburys Vol. 3
       </i>
       was dedicated to the memory of "Lefty Wilbury" (Roy Orbison).
      </p>
      <p>
       Released in October 1990, the album was less positively received than
       <i>
        Vol. 1
       </i>
       , yet still saw a fair measure of success. In the United States, "She's My Baby" (with guest guitarist
       <a href="/wiki/Gary_Moore" title="Gary Moore">
        Gary Moore
       </a>
       ) and "Inside Out" became radio hits, charting at number 2 album rock and number 16 album rock, respectively. The album peaked at number 14 in the UK and number 11 in the US, where it was certified platinum by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">
        RIAA
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Comparing the two Wilburys albums, a reviewer in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">
         The New York Times
        </a>
       </i>
       wrote in November 1990: "The superstar pop group stays close to 50's and 60's rock roots, drawing on blues, doo-wop, rockabilly and
       <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Holly" title="Buddy Holly">
        Buddy Holly
       </a>
       . But their second album is faster, jokier, lighter and meaner than the first."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Muze_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Muze-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">
         Rolling Stone
        </a>
       </i>
       described the blending of the four participants' musical styles as "seemingly effortless", and said that the album showed they continued to enjoy their collaboration.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Muze_8-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Muze-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the years following
       <i>
        Vol. 3
       </i>
       , there was speculation about further Wilbury releases. Since Harrison was viewed as the de facto leader of the group, his death in November 2001 ended the possibility of any future projects.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       When Harrison’s distribution deal with
       <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">
        Warner Bros.
       </a>
       expired in 1995, ownership of his
       <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Records" title="Dark Horse Records">
        Dark Horse Records
       </a>
       catalog and the two Wilburys albums reverted to him, and the albums went
       <a href="/wiki/Out_of_print" title="Out of print">
        out of print
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On June 12, 2007,
       <i>
        Vol. 1
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        Vol. 3
       </i>
       were reissued by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhino_Records" title="Rhino Records">
        Rhino Records
       </a>
       as
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Traveling_Wilburys_Collection" title="The Traveling Wilburys Collection">
         The Traveling Wilburys Collection
        </a>
       </i>
       , packaged together with bonus tracks and a DVD.
      </p>
