   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Lunar dome
   </h1>

      <p>
       A
       <b>
        lunar dome
       </b>
       is a type of
       <a href="/wiki/Shield_volcano" title="Shield volcano">
        shield volcano
       </a>
       that is found on the surface of the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . They are typically formed by highly viscous, possibly
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silica" title="Silica">
        silica
       </a>
       -rich
       <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">
        lava
       </a>
       , erupting from localized vents followed by relatively slow cooling. Lunar domes are wide, rounded, circular features with a gentle slope rising in elevation a few hundred meters to the midpoint. They are typically 8–12 km in diameter, but can be up to 20 km across. Some of the domes contain a small craterlet at the peak.
      </p>
      <p>
       Some of the domes have been shown to consist of the same materials as the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_maria" title="Lunar maria">
        lunar maria
       </a>
       . Thus they could be created by some mechanism that differs from the mare-forming flows. It is thought that these domes are formed from a smaller
       <a href="/wiki/Magma_chamber" title="Magma chamber">
        magma chamber
       </a>
       that is closer to the surface than is the case for a mare. This results in a lower pressure, and so the lava flows more slowly. The
       <a href="/wiki/Magma" title="Magma">
        magma
       </a>
       wells up through a crack in the surface, but the flow eventually concentrates through one primary vent. This concentration can then result in a vent crater at the peak of the dome.
      </p>
      <p>
       The cluster of lunar domes at the
       <a href="/wiki/Marius_Hills" title="Marius Hills">
        Marius Hills
       </a>
       was considered as a possible landing site of
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_15" title="Apollo 15">
        Apollo 15
       </a>
       . There are concentrations of lunar domes near the craters
       <a href="/wiki/Hortensius_(crater)" title="Hortensius (crater)">
        Hortensius
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/T._Mayer_(crater)" title="T. Mayer (crater)">
        T. Mayer
       </a>
       , across the top of
       <a href="/wiki/Mons_R%C3%BCmker" title="Mons Rümker">
        Mons Rümker
       </a>
       , and in
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Fecunditatis" title="Mare Fecunditatis">
        Mare Fecunditatis
       </a>
       . Solitary lunar domes are also found, including
       <a href="/wiki/Kies_(crater)" title="Kies (crater)">
        Kies Pi
       </a>
       (π),
       <a href="/wiki/Milichius_(crater)" title="Milichius (crater)">
        Milichius Pi
       </a>
       (π),
       <a href="/wiki/Mons_Gruithuisen_Gamma" title="Mons Gruithuisen Gamma">
        Mons Gruithuisen Gamma
       </a>
       (γ) and Delta (δ), and domes near the craters
       <a href="/wiki/Gambart_(crater)" title="Gambart (crater)">
        Gambart C
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Beer_(lunar_crater)" title="Beer (lunar crater)">
        Beer
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Capuanus_(crater)" title="Capuanus (crater)">
        Capuanus
       </a>
       . Omega Cauchy (ω) and Tau Cauchy (τ) form a pair of domes near the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Cauchy_(crater)" title="Cauchy (crater)">
        Cauchy
       </a>
       . Likewise near
       <a href="/wiki/Arago_(lunar_crater)" title="Arago (lunar crater)">
        Arago
       </a>
       are the domes Arago Alpha (α) and Arago Beta (β). There are two domes south of
       <a href="/wiki/Mons_Esam" title="Mons Esam">
        Mons Esam
       </a>
       .
      </p>
