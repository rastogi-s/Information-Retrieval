      <p>
       <b>
        Maraldi
       </b>
       is a worn, eroded
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        crater
       </a>
       on the western edge of the
       <a href="/wiki/Sinus_Amoris" title="Sinus Amoris">
        Sinus Amoris
       </a>
       , in the northeast part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . 35 km to the south is the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Gardner_(crater)" title="Gardner (crater)">
        Gardner
       </a>
       , to the west-southwest is the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Vitruvius_(crater)" title="Vitruvius (crater)">
        Vitruvius
       </a>
       , and to the northwest lies the worn
       <a href="/wiki/Littrow_(crater)" title="Littrow (crater)">
        Littrow crater
       </a>
       . Just to the northeast of the crater is the dome-like
       <a href="/wiki/Mons_Maraldi" title="Mons Maraldi">
        Mons Maraldi
       </a>
       rise.
      </p>
      <p>
       The crater is named after two Italian astronomers
       <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Domenico_Maraldi" title="Giovanni Domenico Maraldi">
        Giovanni Domenico
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giacomo_Filippo_Maraldi" title="Giacomo Filippo Maraldi">
        Jacques Philippe (or Giacomo Filippo)
       </a>
       Maraldi.
      </p>
      <p>
       Maraldi has a very worn outer wall that is deeply incised and has the appearance of a circular range of peaks rather than a crater rim. The interior has been flooded with
       <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">
        basaltic
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">
        lava
       </a>
       , leaving a flat surface with a low
       <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
        albedo
       </a>
       . There is a low ridge just to the northwest of the midpoint, and several tiny craters mark the floor surface.
      </p>
      <p>
       The eastern part of the rim attaches with the satellite crater Maraldi F.
      </p>
