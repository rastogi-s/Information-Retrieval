      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar X
       </b>
       (also known as the
       <b>
        Werner X
       </b>
       ) is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clair-obscur" title="Clair-obscur">
        clair-obscur
       </a>
       effect in which light and shadow creates the appearance of a letter 'X' on the rim of the
       <a href="/wiki/Blanchinus_(crater)" title="Blanchinus (crater)">
        Blanchinus
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/La_Caille_(crater)" title="La Caille (crater)">
        La Caille
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Purbach_(crater)" title="Purbach (crater)">
        Purbach
       </a>
       craters.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The X is visible only for a few hours before the first quarter,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cosmic_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-cosmic-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chapma_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-chapma-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       slightly below the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_terminator" title="Lunar terminator">
        lunar terminator
       </a>
       . Near to the X, the Lunar V is also visible, formed by
       <a href="/wiki/Ukert_(crater)" title="Ukert (crater)">
        Ukert crater
       </a>
       and several other small craters.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cosmic_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-cosmic-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
