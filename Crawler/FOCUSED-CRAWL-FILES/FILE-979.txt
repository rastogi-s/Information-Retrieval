      <p>
       <b>
        88611 Teharonhiawako
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Trans-Neptunian_object" title="Trans-Neptunian object">
        trans-Neptunian object
       </a>
       and a member of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kuiper_belt" title="Kuiper belt">
        Kuiper belt
       </a>
       , measuring about 220 km in diameter. It is a
       <a href="/wiki/Minor-planet_moon" title="Minor-planet moon">
        binary object
       </a>
       , with a large companion named
       <b>
        Sawiskera
       </b>
       (pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">
         [zaɰískɛɺa]
        </a>
       </span>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Mohawk_language" title="Mohawk language">
        Mohawk
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       formally designed
       <b>
        (88611) Teharonhiawako I Sawiskera
       </b>
       ), which at 126 km in diameter is about two-thirds the size of its primary.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Teharonhiawako_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Teharonhiawako-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Teharonhiawako was discovered on August 20, 2001, by the
       <a href="/wiki/Deep_Ecliptic_Survey" title="Deep Ecliptic Survey">
        Deep Ecliptic Survey
       </a>
       , and Sawiskera was identified a month later. The primary is named after
       <a href="/wiki/Iroquois_mythology" title="Iroquois mythology">
        Teharonhia:wako
       </a>
       , a god of
       <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">
        maize
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Iroquois_mythology" title="Iroquois mythology">
        Iroquois
       </a>
       creation myth, while the secondary is named after his evil twin brother
       <a href="/wiki/Iroquois_mythology" title="Iroquois mythology">
        Sawiskera
       </a>
       . The objects were named in 2007.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Teharonhiawako_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Teharonhiawako-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Sawiskera's orbit has the following parameters: semi-major-axis—
       <span class="nowrap">
        27670 ± 120 km
       </span>
       , period—
       <span class="nowrap">
        828.76 ± 0.22 days
       </span>
       , eccentricity—
       <span class="nowrap">
        0.2494 ± 0.0021
       </span>
       and inclination—
       <span class="nowrap">
        144.42 ± 0.35°
       </span>
       (retrograde). The total system mass is about 2.4 × 10
       <sup>
        18
       </sup>
       kg.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grundy2011_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grundy2011-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
