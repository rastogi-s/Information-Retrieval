   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    <i>
     Moongate
    </i>
    (book)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Moongate: Suppressed Findings of the U.S. Space Program, The NASA-Military Cover-Up
        </b>
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is a 1982 book by American engineer William L. Brian II.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fine_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Fine-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Eisen" title="Jonathan Eisen">
        Jonathan Eisen
       </a>
       wrote in his 1999 book
       <i>
        Suppressed Inventions
       </i>
       that Brian asserts in
       <i>
        Moongate
       </i>
       that the Moon has a weighty atmosphere and gravity, so "a top secret antigravity propulsion system" was required to land on and take off from the Moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note--3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The book alleges a
       <a href="/wiki/Cover-up" title="Cover-up">
        cover-up
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       for hiding facts about the Moon's having alien intelligence.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vankin_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Vankin-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Brian asserts that in the 1960s, NASA discovered that the Moon's
       <a href="/wiki/Gravitational_field" title="Gravitational field">
        gravitational field
       </a>
       was 64 percent as powerful as the Earth's.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vankin_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Vankin-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He said this is significant because it would mean
       <a href="/wiki/Newton%27s_law_of_universal_gravitation" title="Newton's law of universal gravitation">
        Newton's law of universal gravitation
       </a>
       is incorrect. It would also indicate that the Moon could maintain an
       <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">
        atmosphere
       </a>
       , allowing for life to exist.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vankin_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Vankin-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reviews">
        Reviews
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moongate_(book)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reviews">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Roger_D._Launius" title="Roger D. Launius">
        Roger D. Launius
       </a>
       and J. D. Hunley of
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       called the book "a sensationalistic exposé". They cited the title of Chapter 10, "Evidence of Extraterrestrial Interference in the Space Program", as suggesting "the highly speculative and tenuous tenor of the book".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LauniusHunley_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-LauniusHunley-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Vankin" title="Jonathan Vankin">
        Jonathan Vankin
       </a>
       and John Whalen wrote in their 2004 book
       <i>
        The 80 Greatest Conspiracies of All Time
       </i>
       that the book "sketched out a ... planet-shaking, NASA-scamming history of the solar system" and that "Brian's theories echo another wing of aerospace conspiracy conjecture, the insanely sweeping '
       <a href="/wiki/Alternative_3" title="Alternative 3">
        Alternative 3
       </a>
       ' plot".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vankin_4-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Vankin-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
