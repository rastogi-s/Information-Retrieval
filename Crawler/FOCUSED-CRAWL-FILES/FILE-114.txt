      <p>
       <b>
        Planets in
        <a href="/wiki/Astrology" title="Astrology">
         astrology
        </a>
       </b>
       have a meaning different from the modern
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">
        astronomical
       </a>
       understanding of
       <a href="/wiki/Definition_of_planet" title="Definition of planet">
        what a planet is
       </a>
       . Before the
       <a href="/wiki/Scientific_revolution" title="Scientific revolution">
        age
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Telescope" title="Telescope">
        telescopes
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Night_sky" title="Night sky">
        night sky
       </a>
       was thought to consist of two very similar components:
       <a href="/wiki/Fixed_stars" title="Fixed stars">
        fixed stars
       </a>
       , which remained motionless in relation to each other, and "wandering stars" (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language" title="Ancient Greek language">
        Ancient Greek
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="grc" xml:lang="grc">
        ἀστέρες πλανῆται
       </span>
       <i>
        asteres planetai
       </i>
       ), which moved relative to the fixed stars over the course of the year.
      </p>
      <p>
       To the
       <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">
        Greeks
       </a>
       and the other earliest astronomers, this group consisted of the five
       <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">
        planets
       </a>
       visible to the
       <a href="/wiki/Naked_eye" title="Naked eye">
        naked eye
       </a>
       and excluded
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       . Although strictly, the term
       <i>
        planet
       </i>
       applied only to those five
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_object" title="Astronomical object">
        objects
       </a>
       , the term was latterly broadened, particularly in the
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">
        Middle Ages
       </a>
       , to include the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       (sometimes referred to as "Lights"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ), making a total of seven planets. Astrologers retain this definition today.
      </p>
      <p>
       To ancient astrologers, the planets represented the will of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gods" title="Gods">
        gods
       </a>
       and their direct influence upon human affairs. To modern astrologers, the planets represent basic drives or urges in the unconscious,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       or energy flow regulators representing dimensions of experience.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They express themselves with different qualities in the twelve
       <a href="/wiki/Astrological_sign" title="Astrological sign">
        signs
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Zodiac" title="Zodiac">
        zodiac
       </a>
       and in the twelve
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astrological_house" title="Astrological house">
        houses
       </a>
       . The planets are also related to each other in the form of
       <a href="/wiki/Astrological_aspect" title="Astrological aspect">
        aspects
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Modern astrologers differ on the source of the planets' influence. Hone writes that the planets exert it directly through
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gravitation" title="Gravitation">
        gravitation
       </a>
       or another, unknown influence.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Others hold that the planets have no direct influence in themselves, but are mirrors of basic organizing principles in the
       <a href="/wiki/Universe" title="Universe">
        universe
       </a>
       . In other words, the basic patterns of the universe repeat themselves everywhere, in
       <a href="/wiki/Fractal" title="Fractal">
        fractal
       </a>
       -like fashion, and "as above, so below".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Therefore, the patterns that the planets make in the sky reflect the ebb and flow of basic human impulses. The planets are also associated, especially in the Chinese tradition, with the basic forces of nature.
      </p>
      <p>
       Listed below are the specific meanings and domains associated with the astrological planets since ancient times, with the main focus on the Western astrological tradition. The planets in
       <a href="/wiki/Hindu_astrology" title="Hindu astrology">
        Hindu astrology
       </a>
       are known as the
       <a href="/wiki/Navagraha" title="Navagraha">
        Navagraha
       </a>
       or "nine realms". In
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astrology" title="Chinese astrology">
        Chinese astrology
       </a>
       , the planets are associated with the life forces of yin and yang and the five
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wu_xing" title="Wu xing">
        elements
       </a>
       , which play an important role in the Chinese form of geomancy known as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feng_Shui" title="Feng Shui">
        Feng Shui
       </a>
       . Astrologers differ on the signs associated with each planet's exaltation.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">

       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="5893" data-file-width="7046" height="184" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg/220px-Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg/330px-Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg/440px-Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cellarius_ptolemaic_system.jpg" title="Enlarge">

         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ptolemaic_system" title="Ptolemaic system">

      <p>
       Treatises on the Ptolemaic planets and their influence on people born "under their reign" appear in
       <a href="/wiki/Block_book" title="Block book">

       form, so-called "planet books" or

       <a href="/wiki/Titan_(mythology)" title="Titan (mythology)">
        Titan

        Cronus

        Rhea

       <a href="/wiki/Crius" title="Crius">
        Crius
       </a>

        Dione
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Hyperion_(mythology)" title="Hyperion (mythology)">
        Hyperion
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Theia" title="Theia">
        Theia
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Atlas_(mythology)" title="Atlas (mythology)">
        Atlas
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Phoebe_(mythology)" title="Phoebe (mythology)">

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Classical_planets">
        Classical planets
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planets_in_astrology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Classical planets">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The seven
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classical_planets" title="Classical planets">
        classical planets
       </a>
       are those easily seen with the naked eye, and were thus known to ancient astrologers. They are the Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter and Saturn. Sometimes, the Sun and Moon were referred to as "the lights" or the "
       <a href="/wiki/Luminary_(astrology)" title="Luminary (astrology)">
        luminaries
       </a>
       ".
       <a href="/wiki/4_Vesta" title="4 Vesta">
        Vesta
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">
        Uranus
       </a>
       can also just be seen with the naked eye, though no ancient culture appears to have taken note of them. The astrological descriptions attached to the seven classical planets have been preserved since ancient times. Astrologers call the seven classical planets "the seven personal and social planets", because they are said to represent the basic human drives of every individual. The personal planets are the Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus and Mars. The social or transpersonal planets are Jupiter and Saturn. Jupiter and Saturn are often called the first of the "transpersonal" or "transcendent" planets as they represent a transition from the inner personal planets to the outer modern, impersonal planets. The outer modern planets Uranus, Neptune and Pluto are often called the collective or transcendental planets.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The following is a list of the planets and their associated characteristics.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       , the god of light). The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system, around which the Earth and other planets revolve and provides us with heat and light. The arc that the Sun travels in every year, rising and setting in a slightly different place each day, is therefore in reality a reflection of the Earth's own orbit around the Sun. This arc is larger the farther north or south from the equator latitude, giving a more extreme difference between day and night and between seasons during the year. The Sun travels through the twelve signs of the zodiac on its annual journey, spending about a month in each. The Sun's position on a person's birthday therefore determines what is usually called his or her "sun" sign. However, the sun sign allotment varies between Western (sign change around 22-23 of every month) and Hindu astrology (sign change around 14-15 of every month) due the different systems of planetary calculations, following the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_zodiac" title="Tropical zodiac">
        tropical
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sidereal_zodiac" title="Sidereal zodiac">
        sidereal
       </a>
       definitions respectively.
      </p>

       Astrologically speaking, the Sun is usually thought to represent the conscious
       <a href="/wiki/Id,_ego_and_super-ego#Ego" title="Id, ego and super-ego">
        ego
       </a>
       , the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self_(psychology)" title="Self (psychology)">
        self

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mesopotamian_Religion" title="Mesopotamian Religion">

       </a>
       and described in the
       <a href="/wiki/Epic_of_Gilgamesh" title="Epic of Gilgamesh">

       <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Manilius" title="Marcus Manilius">

       <i>
        Astronomica
       </i>
       , described the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       , or
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sol_(mythology)" title="Sol (mythology)">
         Sol
        </a>
       </i>
       , as benign and favorable. In medicine, the Sun is associated with the heart, circulatory system,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and the thymus. In
       <a href="/wiki/Ayurveda" title="Ayurveda">

       <b>

       </b>

       <b>

       </b>

      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astrology" title="Chinese astrology">

       </a>
       , the Sun represents
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feng_Shui#Yin_and_Yang" title="Feng Shui">
        Yang
       </a>
       , the active, assertive masculine life principle.

Mercury is so close to the Sun that only a brief period exists after the Sun has set where it can be seen with the naked eye, before following the Sun beyond the horizon.

The 1st-century poet
       <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Manilius" title="Marcus Manilius">

      <p>
       In modern astrology, Mercury is regarded as the ruler of the third house; traditionally, it had the joy in the first house. Mercury is the messenger of the gods in mythology. It is the planet of day-to-day expression and relationships. Mercury's action is to take things apart and put them back together again. It is an opportunistic planet, decidedly unemotional and curious.
      </p>

       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Venus_(mythology)" title="Venus (mythology)">
        Venus
       </a>

Her cults may represent the religiously legitimate charm and seduction of the divine by mortals, in contrast to the formal, contractual relations between most members of Rome's official pantheon and the state, and the unofficial, illicit manipulation of divine forces through magic. The ambivalence of her function is suggested in the etymological relationship of the root *venes- with Latin venenum (poison, venom), in the sense of "a charm, magic philtre".

      <p>
       Venus orbits the Sun in 225 days, spending about 18.75 days in each sign of the zodiac. Venus is the second-brightest object in the night sky, the Moon being the brightest. It is usually beheld as a twin planet to Earth.
      </p>
      <p>
       Astrologically speaking, Venus is associated with the principles of harmony, resilience, beauty, refinement, solidarity, affections, equality, and the urge to sympathize and unite with others. It is involved with the desire for pleasure, comfort and ease. It governs romantic relations, marriage and business partnerships, sex (the origin of the words 'venery' and 'venereal'), the arts, fashion and social life. The 1st-century poet Marcus Manilius described Venus as generous and fecund and the lesser
       <a href="/wiki/Benefic_planet" title="Benefic planet">
        benefic
       </a>
       .
      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/Mars_(mythology)" title="Mars (mythology)">
        Mars
       </a>
       is the Roman god of war and bloodshed, whose symbol is a spear and shield. Both the soil of Mars and the hemoglobin of human blood are rich in iron and because of this they share its distinct deep red color.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Henbestibid_25-0">

       <a href="/wiki/Scorpio_(astrology)" title="Scorpio (astrology)">

       </a>

      <p>
       Mars orbits the Sun in 687 days, spending about 57.25 days in each sign of the zodiac. It is also the first planet that orbits outside of Earth's orbit, making it the first planet that does not set along with the Sun. Mars has two permanent polar ice caps. During a pole's winter, it lies in continuous darkness, chilling the surface and causing the deposition of 25–30% of the atmosphere into slabs of CO
       <sub>
        2
       </sub>
       ice (dry ice).
      </p>
      <p>
       Astrologically speaking, Mars is associated with confidence and self-assertion, aggression, sexuality, energy, strength, ambition and impulsiveness. Mars governs sports, competitions and physical activities in general. The 1st-century poet Manilius, described the planet as ardent and as the lesser
       <a href="/wiki/Malefic_planet" title="Malefic planet">
        malefic
       </a>
       . In medicine, Mars presides over the
       <a href="/wiki/Sex_organ" title="Sex organ">
        genitals
       </a>
       , the muscular system, the gonads and adrenal glands. It was traditionally held to be hot and excessively dry and ruled the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choleric" title="Choleric">
        choleric
       </a>
       humor. It was associated with fever, accidents, trauma, pain and surgery.
      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter_(mythology)" title="Jupiter (mythology)">
        Jupiter
       </a>
       is the ruler of the gods and their guardian and protector, and his symbol is the thunderbolt. The Romans believed that Jupiter granted them supremacy because they had honored him more than any other people had. Jupiter was "the fount of the auspices upon which the relationship of the city with the gods rested." He personified the divine authority of Rome's highest offices, internal organization, and external relations. His image in the Republican and Imperial Capitol bore regalia associated with Rome's ancient kings and the highest consular and Imperial honours. Jupiter co-ruler
       <a href="/wiki/Pisces_(astrology)" title="Pisces (astrology)">

      <p>
       In the same way, the planet Jupiter is the king of the other planets, a giant in size with spectacular, brightly colored clouds and intense storms.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Some astronomers believe that it plays an important protecting role in using its massive gravity to capture or expel from the solar system many comets and asteroids that would otherwise threaten Earth and the inner planets.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Jupiter takes 11.9 years to orbit the Sun, spending almost an earth year (361 days) in each sign of the zodiac. Furthermore, Jupiter is usually the fourth-brightest object in the sky (after the Sun, the Moon and Venus).
      </p>
      <p>
       Astrologically speaking, Jupiter is associated with the principles of growth, expansion, prosperity, and good fortune. Jupiter governs long distance and foreign travel, big business and wealth, higher education, religion, and the law. It is also associated with the urge for freedom and exploration, as well with gambling and merrymaking.
      </p>

It concerns a person's sense of duty, discipline, responsibility, including their physical and emotional endurance during hardships. Saturn also represents the part of a person's concern with long-term planning or foresight.

      <p>
       Before the discovery of
       <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">
        Uranus
       </a>
       , Saturn was regarded as the ruling planet of
       <a href="/wiki/Aquarius_(astrology)" title="Aquarius (astrology)">
        Aquarius
       </a>
       alongside
       <a href="/wiki/Capricorn_(astrology)" title="Capricorn (astrology)">
        Capricorn
       </a>
       of course, which is the preceding sign. Many traditional types of astrologers refer
       <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">
        Saturn
       </a>
       as the planetary ruler for both
       <a href="/wiki/Capricorn_(astrology)" title="Capricorn (astrology)">
        Capricorn
       </a>
       and Aquarius. In modern astrology, it is the primary native ruler of the tenth house. Traditionally however, Saturn ruled both the first and eighth houses, and had its joy in the twelfth house of mischief and bad luck.
      </p>
      <p>
       Saturn is associated with Saturday, which was named after the deity Saturn. Dante Alighieri associated Saturn with the liberal art of
       <i>
        astronomia
       </i>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronomy_and_astrology" title="Astronomy and astrology">
        astronomy and astrology
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       In Chinese astrology, Saturn is ruled by the element
       <a href="/wiki/Earth_(classical_element)" title="Earth (classical element)">
        earth
       </a>
       , which is warm, generous, and co-operative. In
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_astrology" title="Indian astrology">
        Indian astrology
       </a>
       , Saturn is called
       <a href="/wiki/Shani" title="Shani">
        Shani
       </a>
       or "Sani", representing a noteworthy career and longevity. He is also the bringer of obstacles and hardship.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_planets">
        Modern planets
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planets_in_astrology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Modern planets">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Since the invention of the telescope, Western astrology has incorporated Uranus, Neptune, Ceres, Pluto, and other bodies into its methodology. The Indian and Chinese astrologies have tended to retain the ancient seven-planet system. Meanings have had to be assigned to them by modern astrologers, usually according to the major events that occurred in the world at the time of their discovery. As these astrologers are usually Western, the social and historical events they describe have an inevitable Western emphasis. Astrologers consider the "extra-Saturnian" planets to be "impersonal" or
       <a href="/wiki/Generational_planet" title="Generational planet">
        generational
       </a>
       planets, meaning their effects are felt more across whole generations of society. Their effects in individuals depend upon how strongly they feature in that individual's birth-chart. The following are their characteristics as accepted by most astrologers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
        <a href="#cite_note-31">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">

       </a>
       (
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Uranus%27s_astrological_symbol.svg">
        <img alt="Uranus's astrological symbol.svg" data-file-height="50" data-file-width="50" height="22" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Uranus%27s_astrological_symbol.svg/22px-Uranus%27s_astrological_symbol.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Uranus%27s_astrological_symbol.svg/33px-Uranus%27s_astrological_symbol.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Uranus%27s_astrological_symbol.svg/44px-Uranus%27s_astrological_symbol.svg.png 2x" width="22"/>

       <a href="/wiki/Aquarius_(astrology)" title="Aquarius (astrology)">

       </a>
       and is exalted in
       <a href="/wiki/Scorpio_(astrology)" title="Scorpio (astrology)">
        Scorpio
       </a>
       . In Greek mythology, Uranus is the personification of the sky. The planet Uranus is very unusual among the planets in that it rotates on its side, so that it presents each of its poles to the Sun in turn during its orbit; causing both hemispheres to alternate between being bathed in light and lying in total darkness over the course of the orbit.
      </p>
      <p>
       Uranus takes 84 years to orbit the Sun, spending about 7 years in each sign of the zodiac. Uranus was discovered to be a planet only in 1781 by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_William_Herschel" title="Sir William Herschel">
        Sir William Herschel
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Astrological interpretations associate Uranus with the principles of ingenuity, new or unconventional ideas, individuality, discoveries, electricity, inventions, democracy, and revolutions. Uranus, among all planets, most governs genius.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Uranus2.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1720" data-file-width="1720" height="100" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Uranus2.jpg/100px-Uranus2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Uranus2.jpg/150px-Uranus2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Uranus2.jpg/200px-Uranus2.jpg 2x" width="100"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">

          </a>
         </div>

         <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">
          Uranus
         </a>

       </div>

      <p>
       Uranus governs societies, clubs, and any group based on humanitarian or progressive ideals. Uranus, the planet of sudden and unexpected changes, rules freedom and originality. In society, it rules radical ideas and people, as well as revolutionary events that upset established structures. Uranus is also associated with Wednesday, alongside Mercury (since Uranus is in the higher octave of Mercury).
      </p>
      <p>
       In art and literature, the discovery of Uranus coincided with the Romantic movement, which emphasized individuality and freedom of creative expression. Additionally, it is often linked to an individual's animal spirit. When it comes to medicine, Uranus is believed to be particularly associated with the sympathetic nervous system, mental disorders, breakdowns and hysteria, spasms, and cramps. Uranus is considered by modern astrologers to be the primary native ruler of the eleventh house.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
        <a href="#cite_note-32">
         [32]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

        </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hypnotism" title="Hypnotism">
        hypnotism
       </a>
       . In political terms, Neptune was linked to the rise of nationalist movements throughout Europe in countries like Germany, Italy, Hungary, Ireland, and Serbia, seeking independence for their nations inspired by an idealized past of legend. It was also linked to the rise of socialism and the beginnings of the welfare state. Neptune coincided with the utopian ideals of Communism, when Marx and Engels first published '
       <a href="/wiki/The_Communist_Manifesto" title="The Communist Manifesto">

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Pluto">
        Pluto
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Pluto" title="Pluto">
        Pluto
       </a>
       (
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pluto%27s_astrological_symbol.svg">
        <img alt="Pluto's astrological symbol.svg" data-file-height="50" data-file-width="50" height="22" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Pluto%27s_astrological_symbol.svg/22px-Pluto%27s_astrological_symbol.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Pluto%27s_astrological_symbol.svg/33px-Pluto%27s_astrological_symbol.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Pluto%27s_astrological_symbol.svg/44px-Pluto%27s_astrological_symbol.svg.png 2x" width="22"/>
       </a>
       ) is the ruling planet of
       <a href="/wiki/Scorpio_(astrology)" title="Scorpio (astrology)">
        Scorpio
       </a>
       and is exalted in
       <a href="/wiki/Pisces_(astrology)" title="Pisces (astrology)">
        Pisces
       </a>
       . In Roman mythology, Pluto is the god of the underworld and of wealth. The
       <a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">
        alchemical
       </a>
       symbol was given to Pluto on its discovery, three centuries after alchemical practices had all but disappeared. The alchemical symbol can therefore be read as spirit over mind, transcending matter. The symbols were chosen given the close association with Mars which has a similar symbol.
      </p>
      <p>
       Pluto takes 248 years to make a full circuit of the zodiac, but its progress is highly variable: it spends between 15 and 26 years in each sign.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1800" data-file-width="1800" height="100" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg/100px-Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg/150px-Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg/200px-Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg 2x" width="100"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nh-pluto-in-true-color_2x_JPEG.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Pluto as captured by the New Horizons craft on July 14, 2015, in near true color.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Astrologically speaking, Pluto is called "the great renewer", and is considered to represent the part of a person that destroys in order to renew, through bringing buried, but intense needs and drives to the surface, and expressing them, even at the expense of the existing order. A commonly used keyword for Pluto is "transformation".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theastrocodex.com_37-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-theastrocodex.com-37">
         [37]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is associated with power and personal mastery, and the need to cooperate and share with another, if each is not to be destroyed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
        <a href="#cite_note-38">
         [38]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Pluto governs big business and wealth, mining, surgery and detective work, and any enterprise that involves digging under the surface to bring the truth to light. Pluto is also associated with Tuesday, alongside Mars.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Its entry in Cancer in 1913, the sign in which it was later discovered, coincided with World War I. It is also associated with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_armament" title="Nuclear armament">
        nuclear armament
       </a>
       , which had its genesis in the research of the 1930s and 40s. Later on, it gave rise to the polarized nuclear stand off of the Cold War, with the mass consumer societies of the United States and other democracies facing the totalitarian state of the USSR. The discovery of Pluto also occurred just after the birth of modern psychoanalysis, when Freud and Jung began to explore the depths of the unconscious.
      </p>
      <p>
       In real life events and culture, Pluto has been a major astrological aspect. When it comes to art, movements like
       <a href="/wiki/Cubism" title="Cubism">
        Cubism
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">
        Surrealism
       </a>
       began to de-construct the "normal" view of the world. In medicine, Pluto is seen to be associated with regenerative forces in the body involving cell formation and the reproductive system.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theastrocodex.com_37-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-theastrocodex.com-37">
         [37]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The majority of traditional astrologers do not use Pluto as a ruling planet, but do use the planet for interpretation and predictive work, obliquely making reference to projections of influences from higher to lower dimensional spaces.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theastrocodex.com_37-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-theastrocodex.com-37">
         [37]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Pluto is considered by modern astrologers to be the primary native ruler of the eighth house.
      </p>

        </a>

          Martial Reymond
         </a>
         , early 17th century)
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Ceres_(dwarf_planet)" title="Ceres (dwarf planet)">
        Ceres
       </a>
       (
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ceres_symbol.svg">
        <img alt="Ceres symbol.svg" data-file-height="50" data-file-width="50" height="19" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Ceres_symbol.svg/19px-Ceres_symbol.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Ceres_symbol.svg/29px-Ceres_symbol.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Ceres_symbol.svg/38px-Ceres_symbol.svg.png 2x" width="19"/>
       </a>
       ) is the smallest identified
       <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_planet" title="Dwarf planet">
        dwarf planet
       </a>
       in the Solar System, but is significantly the largest object in the
       <a href="/wiki/Asteroid_belt" title="Asteroid belt">
        asteroid belt
       </a>
       . It was discovered on 1 January 1801 by Giuseppe Piazzi, and is named after Ceres, the Roman goddess of growing plants, the harvest, and of motherly love. It was the first asteroid discovered, taking up about one-third of the entire mass of its asteroid belt.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
        <a href="#cite_note-39">
         [39]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The classification of Ceres has changed more than once and has been the subject of some disagreement.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
        <a href="#cite_note-40">
         [40]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Johann_Elert_Bode" title="Johann Elert Bode">
        Johann Elert Bode
       </a>
       believed Ceres to be the "missing planet" he had proposed to exist between
       <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">
        Mars
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
        Jupiter
       </a>
       , at a distance of 419 million km (2.8
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_unit" title="Astronomical unit">
        AU
       </a>
       ) from the Sun. Ceres was assigned a planetary symbol, and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Solar_System_bodies_formerly_regarded_as_planets" title="List of Solar System bodies formerly regarded as planets">
        remained listed as a planet
       </a>
       in astronomy books and tables for about half a century. The 2006 debate surrounding Pluto and what constitutes a
       <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">
        planet
       </a>
       led to Ceres being considered for reclassification as a planet, but in the end Ceres and Pluto were classified as the first members of the new
       <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_planet" title="Dwarf planet">
        dwarf planet
       </a>
       category.
      </p>
      <p>
       Ceres passes through the zodiac every 4 years and 7 months, passing through a little more than 2½ signs every year.
      </p>
      <p>
       In mythology, Ceres is the Roman equivalent of the Greek goddess
       <a href="/wiki/Demeter" title="Demeter">
        Demeter
       </a>
       , and is the goddess of agriculture. The goddess (and metaphorically the planet) is also associated with the reproductive issues of an adult woman, as well as pregnancy and other major transitions in a woman's life, including the nine months of gestation time, family bonds and relationships. In old opinion, Ceres is the ruling planet of
       <a href="/wiki/Virgo_(astrology)" title="Virgo (astrology)">
        Virgo
       </a>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       but as more knowledge about the planet's character has been revealed, majority of modern astrologers opinion, denote Ceres being the ruler for
       <a href="/wiki/Taurus_(astrology)" title="Taurus (astrology)">
        Taurus
       </a>
       , however, Ceres is exalted in
       <a href="/wiki/Virgo_(astrology)" title="Virgo (astrology)">
        Virgo
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
        <a href="#cite_note-41">
         [41]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In an updated revision, Virgo is ruled by
       <a href="/wiki/2060_Chiron" title="2060 Chiron">
        Chiron
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
        <a href="#cite_note-42">
         [42]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Although a mother, Ceres is also the archetype of a virgin goddess. Ceres epitomizes independent women who are often unmarried (since, according to myth, Ceres is an unmarried goddess who chose to become a mother without a husband or partner.) While the moon represents our ideal of "motherhood", Ceres would represent how our real and nature motherhood should be.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martin,_Helena_p333-334_43-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Martin,_Helena_p333-334-43">
         [43]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PIA19547-Ceres-DwarfPlanet-Dawn-RC3-AnimationFrame25-20150504.jpg">

        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PIA19547-Ceres-DwarfPlanet-Dawn-RC3-AnimationFrame25-20150504.jpg" title="Enlarge">

         The dwarf planet
         <a href="/wiki/Ceres_(dwarf_planet)" title="Ceres (dwarf planet)">

         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Ceres, as the Goddess who has control over nature's resources and cycles, may astrologically be considered the planet of the Environment. Returning to mythology, an early environmental villain is the figure of
       <a href="/wiki/Erysichthon_of_Thessaly" title="Erysichthon of Thessaly">
        Erysichthon
       </a>
       , the tearer up of the earth, who cut down trees in a grove sacred to Ceres-Demeter, for which he was punished by the goddess with fearful hunger. In this sense Ceres became an emerging archetype in the awareness of recent
       <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">
        climate change
       </a>
       , and is entering our collective consciousness as a need to take care of our natural and irreplaceable resources in the 21st century. Ceres represents a leap towards a future of ecological responsibility and knowledge. As an indicator for environmental or community activism, Ceres would represent for some astrologers the wave of the future.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
        <a href="#cite_note-44">
         [44]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Planetary_traditions_compared">
        Planetary traditions compared
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planets_in_astrology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Planetary traditions compared">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_astrological_traditions" title="List of astrological traditions">
        List of astrological traditions
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The three most popular Eurasian traditions,
       <a href="/wiki/Western_astrology" title="Western astrology">
        Western astrology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astrology" title="Chinese astrology">
        Chinese astrology
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindu_Astrology" title="Hindu Astrology">
        Hindu Astrology
       </a>
       , accordingly share a large amount of common themes in their
       <a href="/wiki/Zodiac" title="Zodiac">
        zodiacs
       </a>
       and concepts of planetary meanings.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       This could fallaciously inflect that the three have an ancient common origin, whereas in fact the three developed mutually over millennia by diffusion, assimilation, scholarship, and trade across the whole of
       <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">
        Eurasia
       </a>
       and Africa.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Western and Hindu zodiacs essentially correspond to twelve similar
       <a href="/wiki/Archetype" title="Archetype">
        archetypes
       </a>
       , despite differences in tone, emphasis, motifs, and
       <a href="/wiki/Right_ascension" title="Right ascension">
        right ascension
       </a>
       of their constellations, as do the twelve signs of the
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_zodiac" title="Chinese zodiac">
        Chinese zodiac
       </a>
       ; however, both Western and Hindu astrology are based on four elements: fire, earth, air, water; whereas the Chinese is based on five: metal, water, wood, fire, and earth. Chinese elemental conceptions of the planets clearly correlate to their Western and Hindu counterparts in the case of Mars (
       <a href="/wiki/Fire_(classical_element)" title="Fire (classical element)">
        Fire
       </a>
       ), Saturn (
       <a href="/wiki/Earth_(classical_element)" title="Earth (classical element)">
        Earth
       </a>
       ), and Jupiter (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wood_(classical_element)" title="Wood (classical element)">
        Wood
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The Chinese linkage of Mercury with
       <a href="/wiki/Water_(classical_element)" title="Water (classical element)">
        Water
       </a>
       is alien to Western astrology, but this combination shares the water themes, much of what is coined "mercurial" in Western thought, such as intellect, reason and communication.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Chinese association of Venus to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metal_(classical_element)" title="Metal (classical element)">
        Metal
       </a>
       appears at first fundamentally different from Western notions of love and romance. In Babylonian mythology, her equivalent was Ishtar, goddess of both love and war. The Chinese metal representative is unyielding and forceful, set in their ways and taciturn; yet Venus' Western element is air, which is logical and sanguine. Metal is also sophisticated, and enjoys the good things in life. In Western astrology, Venus rules both
       <a href="/wiki/Libra_(astrology)" title="Libra (astrology)">
        Libra
       </a>
       , which is sophisticated, logical, and romantic, and
       <a href="/wiki/Taurus_(astrology)" title="Taurus (astrology)">
        Taurus
       </a>
       , which is reserved, sensual, and unyielding. Some Western astrologers believe that metal is better associated with the qualities of the planet Saturn, arguing that metal equates to air in the western system, and that Saturn is linked to air in Vedic astrology.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
        <a href="#cite_note-45">
         [45]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">
        <a href="#cite_note-46">
         [46]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The cycle of the five Chinese elements operate completely differently from the Western cycle of four. This discrepancy between elements can be clarified by their
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feng_Shui" title="Feng Shui">
        geomancy
       </a>
       , which is locked with Chinese astrology. On the Feng Shui compass, the five elements and the five visible planets are placed on the cardinal directions and center point, with Mercury-Water to the north, Jupiter-Wood to the east, Mars-Fire to the south, Venus-Metal to the west, and Saturn-Earth in the center.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2010)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       This also suggests that Western air best corresponds to metal, while Venus rules the west in both traditions. Wood in Chinese philosophy describes characteristics found in the Western element of fire.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_solar_system_bodies">
        Other solar system bodies
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asteroids_in_astrology" title="Asteroids in astrology">
        Asteroids in astrology
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Some
       <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">
        asteroids
       </a>
       such as
       <a href="/wiki/2_Pallas" title="2 Pallas">
        Pallas
       </a>
       (
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pallas_symbol.svg">
        <img alt="Pallas symbol.svg" data-file-height="50" data-file-width="50" height="22" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Pallas_symbol.svg/22px-Pallas_symbol.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Pallas_symbol.svg/33px-Pallas_symbol.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Pallas_symbol.svg/44px-Pallas_symbol.svg.png 2x" width="22"/>
       </a>
       ) and
       <a href="/wiki/4_Vesta" title="4 Vesta">
        Vesta
       </a>
       (
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vesta_symbol.svg">
        <img alt="Vesta symbol.svg" data-file-height="50" data-file-width="50" height="22" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Vesta_symbol.svg/22px-Vesta_symbol.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Vesta_symbol.svg/33px-Vesta_symbol.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Vesta_symbol.svg/44px-Vesta_symbol.svg.png 2x" width="22"/>
       </a>
       ), as well as dwarf planet
       <a href="/wiki/Ceres_(dwarf_planet)" title="Ceres (dwarf planet)">
        Ceres
       </a>
       , can sometimes be seen with binoculars (Vesta even with the naked eye), but these were not recognized as planetary, and perhaps not even noticed, until the early 19th century.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In the early 19th century,
       <a href="/wiki/Ceres_(dwarf_planet)" title="Ceres (dwarf planet)">
        Ceres
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/3_Juno" title="3 Juno">
        Juno
       </a>
       (
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Juno_symbol.svg">
        <img alt="Juno symbol.svg" data-file-height="50" data-file-width="50" height="22" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Juno_symbol.svg/22px-Juno_symbol.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Juno_symbol.svg/33px-Juno_symbol.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Juno_symbol.svg/44px-Juno_symbol.svg.png 2x" width="22"/>
       </a>
       ), and the other two aforementioned asteroids were for a time regarded as planets. Although asteroids have been known to both astronomers and astrologers for more than 200 years, they are often ignored by astrologers. The tradition of some astrologers casting charts with minor planets originates with these asteroids. Since the discovery of
       <a href="/wiki/2060_Chiron" title="2060 Chiron">

       </a>
       ) in the 1970s, some astrologers have been casting the new "planet", although astronomers consider it a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centaur_(astronomy)" title="Centaur (astronomy)">
        centaur
       </a>
       (a kind of intermediate object between comet and asteroid).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">
        <a href="#cite_note-47">
         [47]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the 21st century, several new planet-sized bodies, including
       <a href="/wiki/90377_Sedna" title="90377 Sedna">
        Sedna
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/50000_Quaoar" title="50000 Quaoar">
        Quaoar
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haumea_(dwarf_planet)" title="Haumea (dwarf planet)">
        Haumea
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Eris_(dwarf_planet)" title="Eris (dwarf planet)">
        Eris
       </a>
       , have been discovered, but not yet incorporated into mainstream astrological predictions, although some more avant-garde groups have attempted to incorporate them.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">
        <a href="#cite_note-48">
         [48]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49">
        <a href="#cite_note-49">
         [49]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">
        Comets
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Nova" title="Nova">
        novae
       </a>
       have been observed and discussed for several thousand years. Comets in particular were portents of great interest to ancient people and given various astrological interpretations. Both phenomena are rarely visible to the naked-eye, and are ignored by most modern astrologers.}
      </p>
      <p>
       The near-earth asteroid
       <a href="/wiki/3753_Cruithne" title="3753 Cruithne">
        Cruithne
       </a>
       is thought to influence the zodiac and some personal horoscopes.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">

          </span>

        </i>

       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hypothetical_planets">
        Hypothetical planets
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planets_in_astrology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Hypothetical planets">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Some astrologers have hypothesized about the existence of unseen or undiscovered planets. In 1918, astrologer
       <a href="/wiki/Sepharial" title="Sepharial">
        Sepharial
       </a>
       proposed the existence of Earth's "Dark Moon"
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lilith_(hypothetical_moon)" title="Lilith (hypothetical moon)">
        Lilith
       </a>
       , and since then, some astrologers have been using it in their charts; though the same name is also (and now, more commonly) used in astrology to refer to the axis of the actual Moon's orbit. The 20th-century German school of astrology known as Uranian astrology also claimed that many undiscovered planets existed beyond the orbit of Neptune, giving them names such as Cupido, Hades, Zeus, Kronos, Apollon, Admetos, Vulcanus, and Poseidon, and charting their supposed orbits. These orbits have not coincided, however, with more recent discoveries by astronomers of objects beyond Neptune.
      </p>
      <p>
       Other astrologers have focused on the theory that in time, all twelve signs of the zodiac will each have their own ruler, so that another two planets have yet to be discovered; namely the "true" rulers of Taurus and Virgo. The names of the planets mentioned in this regard by some are Vulcan (ruler of Virgo) and
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo" title="Apollo">
        Apollo
       </a>
       , the Roman god of the Sun (ruler of Taurus).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50">
        <a href="#cite_note-50">
         [50]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Another version of this theory states that the modern planets discovered so far correspond to the elements known to the ancients—air (Uranus, god of the heavens), water (Neptune, god of the sea), and fire (Pluto, god of the underworld)—which leaves the elements earth and
       <a href="/wiki/Aether_(classical_element)" title="Aether (classical element)">
        ether
       </a>
       (the fifth element of the fiery upper air). In other words, it is claimed that the two planets to be discovered will be named after an
       <a href="/wiki/Earth_(classical_element)" title="Earth (classical element)">
        earth
       </a>
       god or goddess (such as the
       <a href="/wiki/Horae" title="Horae">
        Horae
       </a>
       ), and after

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ruling_planets_of_the_astrological_signs_and_houses">
        Ruling planets of the astrological signs and houses
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Planets_in_astrology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Ruling planets of the astrological signs and houses">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Domicile_(astrology)" title="Domicile (astrology)">
        Domicile (astrology)
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In Western astrology, the symbolism associated with the planets also relates to the zodiac signs and houses of the horoscope in their various
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruling_planet" title="Ruling planet">
        rulerships
       </a>
       . For instance, the description of Mars is masculine, impulsive, and active. Aries is ruled by Mars and has a similar description, representing an active, masculine
       <a href="/wiki/Archetype" title="Archetype">
        archetype
       </a>
       . Similarly, the first house is also ruled by Mars, and deals with a person's physical health and strength, and the manner in which they project themselves.
      </p>
