      <p>
       <b>
        Zvezda moonbase
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
        Russian
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">
        звезда
       </span>
       ,
       <i>
        "star"
       </i>
       ), also called
       <b>
        DLB Lunar Base
       </b>
       was a
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet
       </a>
       plan and project from 1962 to 1974 to construct a crewed
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moonbase" title="Moonbase">
        moonbase
       </a>
       as successor to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_manned_lunar_programs" title="Soviet manned lunar programs">
        N1-L3 crewed lunar expedition program
       </a>
       . Zvezda moonbase was canceled with the rest of the Soviet crewed lunar programs.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Implementation">
        Implementation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvezda_(moonbase)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Implementation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The base's main habitation module would first be delivered to the Moon. Later automatic spacecraft, including the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunokhod" title="Lunokhod">
        Lunokhod
       </a>
       rover, would be delivered, followed by a human crew and more modules. For the safety of the crew, the habitation modules might have been covered with
       <a href="/wiki/Regolith" title="Regolith">
        regolith
       </a>
       . In order to allow for exploration or repositioning of the base, the habitation modules may have been installed on wheel chassis, and multiple docked to form a movable train. Energy for the base would have been provided by
       <a href="/wiki/Atomic_battery" title="Atomic battery">
        atomic batteries
       </a>
       and a
       <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_reactor" title="Nuclear reactor">
        nuclear reactor
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_Barmin_City.gif">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="240" data-file-width="320" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Lunar_Barmin_City.gif/220px-Lunar_Barmin_City.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/85/Lunar_Barmin_City.gif 1.5x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_Barmin_City.gif" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         3D representation of the proposed base
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
          <a href="#cite_note-1">
           [1]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The moonbase project was ordered by the Soviet space chief
       <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Korolev" title="Sergei Korolev">
        Korolyov
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Barmin" title="Vladimir Barmin">
        Barmin
       </a>
       's Spetcmash bureau. The project was named
       <i>
        DLB Lunar Base
       </i>
       in technical specifications and
       <i>
        Zvezda
       </i>
       in government documents. Unofficially, the project was called
       <i>
        Barmingrad
       </i>
       (Barmin's city) by its designers.
      </p>
      <p>
       Several aspects of lunar colonization were studied:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        the aims of the base;
       </li>
       <li>
        a concept for construction;
       </li>
       <li>
        stages of realization;
       </li>
       <li>
        construction and scientific equipment;
       </li>
       <li>
        possible military supplies.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Parameters">
        Parameters
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvezda_(moonbase)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Parameters">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The main unit of the base was the habitation module.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#What_information_to_include" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="A more detailed citation is required. (August 2013)">
           full citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The plan was for the base to consist of 9 such modules, each delivered separately. The modules had control, laboratory, life, medical, dining, store and other specific aims. Each module was to be 8.6 meters in full length and 3.3 meters in diameter, with 18 tons in full weight. The modules were to be 4.5 meters in initial length, and grow to full size by an expanded mechanism and air compression after delivery.
      </p>
      <p>
       The train had an 8-ton mass and would be operated by 4 cosmonauts and consist of tug, life, energy and drilling modules. Chassis of each module had 8 wheels with separate drive, each as implemented on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunokhod" title="Lunokhod">
        Lunokhods
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Modules of the base and train had 3 layers of protection from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micrometeorites" title="Micrometeorites">
        micrometeorites
       </a>
       , heat and ultraviolet rays. Between the external and internal metal layers was a special styrofoam.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2013)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The train had a manipulator arm which would have enabled the collection of samples of soil without the use of
       <a href="/wiki/Space_suit" title="Space suit">
        space suits
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Water would have been extracted from the lunar soil by a chemical reaction. The
       <a href="/wiki/Lavochkin" title="Lavochkin">
        Lavochkin
       </a>
       bureau developed but did not test a small version of such a unit.
      </p>
      <p>
       The base would have had a crew of 9 to 12.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Realization">
        Realization
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zvezda_(moonbase)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Realization">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Zvezda project was not a separate program but part of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_manned_lunar_programs" title="Soviet manned lunar programs">
        N1-L3 expedition program
       </a>
       for the 1970s. The first cosmonaut landings were to be done with the
       <a href="/wiki/LK_(spacecraft)" title="LK (spacecraft)">
        LK Moon landing module
       </a>
       of L3 spacecraft.
      </p>
      <p>
       The common project
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2013)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       was fulfilled to 1971. Barmin had met with defense minister
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dmitriy_Ustinov" title="Dmitriy Ustinov">
        Ustinov
       </a>
       , who was responsible for the Soviet space program. After more than 6 hours of negotiations Ustinov agreed to support the project.
      </p>
      <p>
       Under the project, detailed drawings and models of modules were developed.
      </p>
      <p>
       The realization of the project depended on key parts of the N1-L3 program - the
       <a href="/wiki/N1_(rocket)" title="N1 (rocket)">
        N-1
       </a>
       superheavy launcher, all 4 launches of which failed between 1969 and 1972. Before the cancellation of the Soviet crewed Moon program, the new project N1F-L3M was proposed for expeditions from 1979 onwards. These would have had a longer duration than the Apollo flights and may have led to the successor creation of the Zvezda moonbase in 1980s. Also, the new
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LEK_Lunar_Expeditionary_Complex" title="LEK Lunar Expeditionary Complex">
        LEK Lunar Expeditionary Complex
       </a>
       for transportation to Moon and moonbase was proposed then.
      </p>
      <p>
       The new Soviet space chief
       <a href="/wiki/Valentin_Glushko" title="Valentin Glushko">
        Glushko
       </a>
       canceled the N1-L3 program in 1974 but proposed the new Vulkan-LEK project for expeditions to the Moon, based on a new superheavy launcher developed by his bureau, but this project was not realized either.
      </p>
      <p>
       According to accounts of the project from developers
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (August 2013)">
           who?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       , Moon expeditions and the construction of a Moon base would have required nearly 50 billion rubles (80 billion US dollars in 1997
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ). The Soviet budget was overloaded with defense expenses and could not carry out such a program.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       All crewed Soviet lunar programs, including a Zvezda moonbase, had been classified as top secret and were only published in the
       <a href="/wiki/Glasnost" title="Glasnost">
        glasnost
       </a>
       epoch since 1990. Before this time, the Soviet Union officially denied the existence of these programs but confirmed the existence of near-Earth crewed orbital stations and Moon exploration by robotic spacecraft.
      </p>
