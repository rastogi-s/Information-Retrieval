      <p>
       <b>
        Lunar tourism
       </b>
       may be possible in the future if trips to the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       are made affordable to the public. Some
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_tourism_startup_companies" title="Space tourism startup companies">
        space tourism startup companies
       </a>
       are planning to offer
       <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">
        tourism
       </a>
       on or around the Moon, and estimate this to be possible sometime between 2018 and 2043.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SpaceX-2017-02-27_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SpaceX-2017-02-27-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Companies">
        Companies
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourism_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Companies">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Space tourism
       <a href="/wiki/Company" title="Company">
        companies
       </a>
       which have announced they are pursuing lunar tourism include
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Adventures" title="Space Adventures">
        Space Adventures
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-economist1_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-economist1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Excalibur_Almaz" title="Excalibur Almaz">
        Excalibur Almaz
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-economist1_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-economist1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Galactic" title="Virgin Galactic">
        Virgin Galactic
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX" title="SpaceX">
        SpaceX
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SpaceX-2017-02-27_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-SpaceX-2017-02-27-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Types">
        Types
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:332px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Circumlunar-free-return-trajectory.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="499" data-file-width="951" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Circumlunar-free-return-trajectory.png/330px-Circumlunar-free-return-trajectory.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Circumlunar-free-return-trajectory.png/495px-Circumlunar-free-return-trajectory.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Circumlunar-free-return-trajectory.png/660px-Circumlunar-free-return-trajectory.png 2x" width="330"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">

      <p>
       Tourist flights would be of three types: flyby in a
       <a href="/wiki/Circumlunar_trajectory" title="Circumlunar trajectory">
        circumlunar trajectory
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_orbit" title="Lunar orbit">
        lunar orbit
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Moon_landing" title="Moon landing">
        lunar landing
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">

           citation needed
          </span>

        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cost">
        Cost
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourism_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cost">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Some of the space tourism start-up companies have declared their cost for each tourist for a tour to the Moon.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Circumlunar flyby:
        <a href="/wiki/Space_Adventures" title="Space Adventures">
         Space Adventures
        </a>
        is charging $150 million per seat, a price that includes months of ground-based training, although this is only a fly-by mission, and will not land on the Moon.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-economist1_3-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-economist1-3">
          [3]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <a href="/wiki/Excalibur_Almaz" title="Excalibur Almaz">
         Excalibur Almaz
        </a>
        had the same price tag but never managed to send their capsule to space.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-4">
          [4]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        Lunar orbit:
       </li>
       <li>
        Lunar landing: The
        <a href="/wiki/Golden_Spike_Company" title="Golden Spike Company">
         Golden Spike Company
        </a>
        was planning to charge $750 million per seat for future lunar landing tourism.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popsci1_5-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-popsci1-5">
          [5]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
         <a href="#cite_note-6">
          [6]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Protection_of_lunar_landmarks">
        Protection of lunar landmarks
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourism_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Protection of lunar landmarks">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2382" data-file-width="2961" height="177" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg/220px-Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg/330px-Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg/440px-Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Apollo_11_bootprint_-_GPN-2001-000014.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/Buzz_Aldrin" title="Buzz Aldrin">
          Buzz Aldrin
         </a>
         's boot print on the lunar surface at
         <a href="/wiki/Tranquility_Base" title="Tranquility Base">
          Tranquility Base
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The site of the first human landing on an
       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/extraterrestrial" title="wikt:extraterrestrial">
        extraterrestrial
       </a>
       body,
       <a href="/wiki/Tranquility_Base" title="Tranquility Base">
        Tranquility Base
       </a>
       , has been determined to have cultural and historic significance by the U.S. states of
       <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">
        California
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">
        New Mexico
       </a>
       , which have listed it on their
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heritage_register" title="Heritage register">
        heritage registers
       </a>
       , since their laws require only that listed sites have some association with the state. Despite the location of
       <a href="/wiki/Christopher_C._Kraft_Jr._Mission_Control_Center" title="Christopher C. Kraft Jr. Mission Control Center">
        Mission Control
       </a>
       in Houston, Texas has not granted similar status to the site, as its
       <a href="/wiki/Historic_preservation" title="Historic preservation">
        historic preservation
       </a>
       laws limit such designations to properties located within the state.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_lunar_preservation_story_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NYT_lunar_preservation_story-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The U.S.
       <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">
        National Park Service
       </a>
       has declined to grant it
       <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark" title="National Historic Landmark">
        National Historic Landmark
       </a>
       status, because the
       <a href="/wiki/Outer_Space_Treaty" title="Outer Space Treaty">
        Outer Space Treaty
       </a>
       prohibits any nation from claiming sovereignty over any extraterrestrial body. It has not been proposed as a
       <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">
        World Heritage Site
       </a>
       since the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">
        United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization
       </a>
       (UNESCO), which oversees that program, limits nations to submitting sites within their own borders.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_lunar_preservation_story_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-NYT_lunar_preservation_story-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Interest in affording historical
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_landing" title="Lunar landing">
        lunar landing
       </a>
       sites some formal protection grew in the early 21st century with the announcement of the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize
       </a>
       for private corporations to successfully build spacecraft and reach the Moon; a $1 million bonus was offered for any competitor that visited a historic site on the Moon. One team, led by
       <a href="/wiki/Astrobotic_Technology" title="Astrobotic Technology">
        Astrobotic Technology
       </a>
       , announced it would attempt to land a craft at Tranquility Base. Although it canceled those plans, the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize#Objections_to_the_Heritage_Bonus_Prizes" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        ensuing controversy
       </a>
       led NASA to request that any other missions to the Moon, private or governmental, human or robotic, keep a distance of at least 75 meters (246 ft) from the site.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_lunar_preservation_story_8-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-NYT_lunar_preservation_story-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_missions">
        Proposed missions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tourism_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Proposed missions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The company
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Adventures" title="Space Adventures">
        Space Adventures
       </a>
       has announced a planned mission, titled
       <a href="/wiki/DSE-Alpha" title="DSE-Alpha">
        DSE-Alpha
       </a>
       , to take two tourists within 100 kilometers (54 nautical miles) of the lunar surface, using a
       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_(spacecraft)" title="Soyuz (spacecraft)">
        Soyuz spacecraft
       </a>
       piloted by a professional cosmonaut.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_7-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The trip would last around a week.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-economist1_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-economist1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Excalibur_Almaz" title="Excalibur Almaz">
        Excalibur Almaz
       </a>
       proposed to take three tourists in a flyby around the Moon, using modified
       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">
        Almaz
       </a>
       space station modules, in a low-energy trajectory flyby around the Moon. The trip would last around 6 months.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-economist1_3-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-economist1-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, their equipment was never launched and is to be converted into an educational exhibit.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In February 2017,
       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX" title="SpaceX">
        SpaceX
       </a>
       announced it had accepted deposits for a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SpaceX_Lunar_Tourism_Mission" title="SpaceX Lunar Tourism Mission">
        week-long flyby mission
       </a>
       to the Moon, set for late 2018, in a
       <a href="/wiki/Dragon_2" title="Dragon 2">
        Dragon 2
       </a>
       capsule carrying two Moon tourists, to be launched via
       <a href="/wiki/Falcon_Heavy" title="Falcon Heavy">
        Falcon Heavy
       </a>
       rocket.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC-2017-02-27_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-CBC-2017-02-27-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
