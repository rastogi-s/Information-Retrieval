      <p>
       <b>
        Hou Yi
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">
        Chinese
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">
        后羿
       </span>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">
        pinyin
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">
         Hòu Yì
        </span>
       </i>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">
        Wade–Giles
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">
         <i>
          <b>
           Hou I
          </b>
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       ) was a mythological Chinese
       <a href="/wiki/Archery" title="Archery">
        archer
       </a>
       . He was also known as
       <b>
        Shen Yi
       </b>
       and simply as
       <b>
        Yi
       </b>
       (羿). He is sometimes portrayed as a god of archery descended from heaven to aid mankind. His wife,
       <a href="/wiki/Chang%27e" title="Chang'e">
        Chang'e
       </a>
       , was a lunar deity.
      </p>
      <p>
       In Chinese lore, when 10 Suns rose from Earth and scorched the fields, turning the world into a wasteland, Houyi shot down 9 of the 10 Suns, leaving the last one alive.
      </p>
      <p>
       A Houyi—usually conflated with the legendary figure in ancient sources—was also a tribal leader of prehistoric China who according to the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Bamboo_Annals" title="Bamboo Annals">
         Bamboo Annals
        </a>
       </i>
       attacked the
       <a href="/wiki/Xia_dynasty" title="Xia dynasty">
        Xia
       </a>
       during the first year of the reign of
       <span class="nowrap">
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Taikang" title="King Taikang">
         King Taikang
        </a>
       </span>
       and occupied his capital
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zhenxun" title="Zhenxun">
        Zhenxun
       </a>
       while Taikang was hunting beyond the
       <a href="/wiki/Luo_River_(Shaanxi)" title="Luo River (Shaanxi)">
        Luo River
       </a>
       . Houyi was deposed by his lieutenant
       <a href="/wiki/Han_Zhuo" title="Han Zhuo">
        Han Zhuo
       </a>
       in the eighth year of the reign of Taikang's nephew
       <span class="nowrap">
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Xiang" title="King Xiang">
         King Xiang
        </a>
       </span>
       .
      </p>
