   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Partial/total eclipses on the Moon
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">

    </div>

         A view of the Earth from the Moon during its partial eclipses, a part of the light from the sun is blocked by the Earth
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <b>
        Partial/total
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_eclipses" title="Solar eclipses">
         Solar eclipses
        </a>
        on the
        <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
         Moon
        </a>
       </b>
       are caused when the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       passes in front of the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       , blocking a part its light in one part and the whole light in the other. Viewers on Earth will see a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partial_lunar_eclipse" title="Partial lunar eclipse">
        partial lunar eclipse
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ref1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       During its eclipses, the Earth will be in
       <b>
        new
       </b>
       phase, completely dark, except for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earthshine" title="Earthshine">
        moonshine
       </a>
       and sunlight refracted through the earth's atmosphere, visible as a ring of light.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The next partial-total solar eclipse on the Moon will be partial-total on August 7, 2017 with the southern portion of the Southern Hemisphere seeing totality.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
        Description
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Partial/total_eclipses_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Seen on Earth as a partial lunar eclipse, unlike the Earth where a total eclipse only occurs in a small spot while the remainder of a solar eclipse are total, on the Moon, a part is shown as partial and a part is shown is total as the center of the Earth's shadow's coverage totality is more larger than the Moon on Earth. In some eclipses, about 15-20% of a surface of the moon covered by the center of the Earth's shadow sees totality where the Earth eclipses the sun, some eclipses has about 75-80% of the surface covered by the center of the Earth's shadow while the remainder visilbly as partial, some has 50/50 covered by the center of the Earth's shadow and visibly as partial.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Length">
        Length
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Partial/total_eclipses_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Length">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The shortest can be for around 2 hours and the longest can be up to 5–6 hours.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref1_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ref1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
