      <p>
       <b>
        Cupid
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
            k
           </span>
           <span title="/juː/: 'u' in 'cute'">
            juː
           </span>
           <span title="'p' in 'pie'">
            p
           </span>
           <span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">
            ɪ
           </span>
           <span title="'d' in 'dye'">
            d
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          KEW
         </span>
         -pid
        </i>
       </a>
       ) is an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inner_satellite" title="Inner satellite">
        inner satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">
        Uranus
       </a>
       . It was discovered by
       <a href="/wiki/Mark_R._Showalter" title="Mark R. Showalter">
        Mark R. Showalter
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jack_J._Lissauer" title="Jack J. Lissauer">
        Jack J. Lissauer
       </a>
       in 2003 using the
       <a href="/wiki/Hubble_Space_Telescope" title="Hubble Space Telescope">
        Hubble Space Telescope
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAUC_8209_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-IAUC_8209-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was named after a character in
       <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">
        William Shakespeare
       </a>
       's play
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Timon_of_Athens" title="Timon of Athens">
         Timon of Athens
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       It is the smallest of inner Uranian satellites, crudely estimated to be only about 18 km in diameter. This and the dark surface made it too dim to be detected by the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Voyager_2" title="Voyager 2">
         Voyager 2
        </a>
       </i>
       cameras during its Uranus flyby in 1986.
      </p>
      <p>
       The orbit of Cupid differs only by 863 km from the orbit of the larger moon
       <a href="/wiki/Belinda_(moon)" title="Belinda (moon)">
        Belinda
       </a>
       . Unlike
       <a href="/wiki/Mab_(moon)" title="Mab (moon)">
        Mab
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Perdita_(moon)" title="Perdita (moon)">
        Perdita
       </a>
       , Uranian satellites also discovered in 2003, it does not seem to be
       <a href="/wiki/Perturbation_(astronomy)" title="Perturbation (astronomy)">
        perturbed
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Showalter_Lissauer_2006_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Showalter_Lissauer_2006-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Following its discovery, Cupid was given the temporary designation
       <b>
        S/2003 U 2
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAUC_8209_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-IAUC_8209-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is also designated
       <b>
        Uranus XXVII
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       It should not be confused with the
       <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">
        asteroid
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/763_Cupido" title="763 Cupido">
        763 Cupido
       </a>
       .
      </p>
