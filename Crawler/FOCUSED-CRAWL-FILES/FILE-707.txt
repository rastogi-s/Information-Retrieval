      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Moon Tycoon
        </b>
       </i>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/City-building_game" title="City-building game">
        city-building
       </a>
       computer game released in 2001 by
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anarchy_Enterprises&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anarchy Enterprises (page does not exist)">
        Anarchy Enterprises
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Unique_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Unique Entertainment (page does not exist)">
        Unique Entertainment
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moby_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-moby-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is based on the creation of a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_colony" title="Lunar colony">
        lunar colony
       </a>
       or city. Anarchy Enterprises described it as the "first 3-D Sim game ever", and noted that it has similarities to
       <a href="/wiki/SimCity" title="SimCity">
        SimCity
       </a>
       (which at the time was 2-dimensional).
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">
        Gameplay
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_Tycoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Moon Tycoon has three campaigns to play through, each with its own story-line and placed around the year 2021.
      </p>
      <p>
       Campaign 1: Earth is in a global energy crisis, and the only thing that can stop it is a precious ore called Helium 3 that can only be found on the moon.
      </p>
      <p>
       Campaign 2: The energy crisis is over, but competition begins to heat up as corporations battle for lunar domination as space borne epidemics, discontent over living conditions and space disasters become more frequent.
      </p>
      <p>
       Campaign 3: Mankind makes yet another leap as it begins the next stage of space exploration: the colonization of the asteroid belt beyond Mars.
      </p>
      <p>
       Each campaign has ten levels to play through each with varied levels of difficulty. All three campaigns make up a larger story, so they can only be played in order and starting with the first. There is also a sandbox mode; where you can set the starting money, terrain type, ore quantity and average building life; it also lets you select the moon or asteroid.
      </p>
      <p>
       The game is played on the Play Area, on which completed and under-construction structures are displayed. Players also use this screen to begin construction of buildings and alter the terrain. The play area is raised up from the surrounding view and can also have a grid.
      </p>
      <p>
       To the right of the Play Area is the Building Menus, Colony Management, Disasters, and Options.
      </p>
      <p>
       The building menu display the various types of structures available for construction. These buildings are divided into different categories: Housing and Medical, Tourism, Research, Rewards (earned by different objectives), utilities, and Industry.
      </p>

      <p>
       The spiritual sequel,
       <i>
        Deep Sea Tycoon
       </i>
       , often confused with
       <i>
        Atlantis Underwater Tycoon
       </i>
       , is a
       <a href="/wiki/2003_in_video_gaming" title="2003 in video gaming">
        2003
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">
        video game
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">
        developed
       </a>
       by Anarchy Enterprises and
       <a href="/wiki/Video_game_publisher" title="Video game publisher">
        published
       </a>
       by Unique Entertainment.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mobygames_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-mobygames-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This game involves you building an underwater city in
       <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">
        3D
       </a>
       . It has a sequel,
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Deep_Sea_Tycoon_2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Deep Sea Tycoon 2 (page does not exist)">
         Deep Sea Tycoon 2
        </a>
       </i>
       , developed by Pixel after Pixel.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay_2">
        Gameplay
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_Tycoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Gameplay">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The gameplay of
       <i>
        Deep Sea Tycoon
       </i>
       involves choosing one of 24 different characters and creating an underwater city filled with different buildings and features, from Mermaid Palaces to Seafood Restaurants. Through the character they choose and the choices they make in their city, the player can become a ruthless oil-tycoon, or something similar, or an
       <a href="/wiki/Environmentalist" title="Environmentalist">
        environmentalist
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The gameplay is similar to
       <a class="mw-selflink selflink">
        Moon Tycoon
       </a>
       . The player creates buildings on a flat piece of land, that must be connected to other buildings by a "tunnel". The only major differences is the scenario, and the types of buildings each game uses.
      </p>
