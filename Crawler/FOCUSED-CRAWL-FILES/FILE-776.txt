      <p>
       <b>
        Thomas Boric
       </b>
       (born May 11, 1961)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obs_1-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-obs-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is an Yugoslav retired
       <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">
        professional wrestler
       </a>
       better known by his
       <a href="/wiki/Ring_name" title="Ring name">
        ring name
       </a>
       <b>
        Paul Diamond
       </b>
       . He is best known for being one half of the tag team
       <a href="/wiki/Badd_Company" title="Badd Company">
        Badd Company
       </a>
       with
       <a href="/wiki/Pat_Tanaka" title="Pat Tanaka">
        Pat Tanaka
       </a>
       and for his time in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">
        World Wrestling Federation
       </a>
       as
       <b>
        Kato
       </b>
       , one half of
       <a href="/wiki/The_Orient_Express" title="The Orient Express">
        The Orient Express
       </a>
       , also with Tanaka. Before he was a professional wrestler, Boric was a professional
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">
        soccer
       </a>
       player.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
        Early life
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Boric was born in
       <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">
        Zagreb
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">
        Croatia
       </a>
       in 1961 and grew up wanting to follow in his father’s footsteps and become a
       <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">
        soccer
       </a>
       player. When Boric was 13 years old the family emigrated from Croatia to
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       , settling down in
       <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">
        Winnipeg
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">
        Manitoba
       </a>
       . Tom got a full soccer scholarship to
       <a href="/wiki/Old_Dominion_University" title="Old Dominion University">
        Old Dominion University
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">
        Norfolk, Virginia
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canoe-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_soccer_career">
        Professional soccer career
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional soccer career">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       After only three semesters at college, Boric was selected as the 6th overall pick of the 1981 NASL draft to play
       <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">
        goalkeeper
       </a>
       for the recently formed
       <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Boomers" title="Calgary Boomers">
        Calgary Boomers
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League_(1968%E2%80%931984)" title="North American Soccer League (1968–1984)">
        North American Soccer League
       </a>
       . After the Boomers folded in 1981, Boric moved on to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rowdies_(1975-1993)" title="Tampa Bay Rowdies (1975-1993)">
        Tampa Bay Rowdies
       </a>
       whom he played for until the NASL closed for good in 1984.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canoe-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       After his professional soccer career ended, Boric began training for a professional wrestling career at a
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Area" title="Tampa Bay Area">
        Tampa Bay
       </a>
       wrestling school run by
       <a href="/wiki/Boris_Malenko" title="Boris Malenko">
        Boris Malenko
       </a>
       along with his two sons
       <a href="/wiki/Joe_Malenko" title="Joe Malenko">
        Joe Malenko
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Dean_Malenko" title="Dean Malenko">
        Dean Malenko
       </a>
       who all had a hand in training him.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canoe-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">
        Professional wrestling career
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span id="Texas-All_Star_Wrestling_.281985.E2.80.931986.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Texas-All_Star_Wrestling_(1985–1986)">
        Texas-All Star Wrestling (1985–1986)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Texas-All Star Wrestling (1985–1986)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Force_(wrestling)" title="American Force (wrestling)">
        American Force (wrestling)
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       After training, Boric began wrestling as "Paul Diamond" in 1985. His first break came in the Texas All-Star Wrestling promotion in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Antonio,_Texas" title="San Antonio, Texas">
        San Antonio, Texas
       </a>
       . He initially formed a tag team called
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Force_(wrestling)" title="American Force (wrestling)">
        American Breed
       </a>
       with
       <a href="/wiki/Nick_Kiniski" title="Nick Kiniski">
        Nick Kiniski
       </a>
       (son of legendary wrestler
       <a href="/wiki/Gene_Kiniski" title="Gene Kiniski">
        Gene Kiniski
       </a>
       ) in Fred Barend's
       <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Championship_Wrestling" title="Southwest Championship Wrestling">
        Texas All-Star Wrestling
       </a>
       . Neither Diamond nor Kiniski were actually American, (Diamond is Croatian/Canadian while Kiniski is Canadian) so the team name was quite ironic.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The team won the
       <a href="/wiki/Texas_All-Star_USA_Tag_Team_Championship" title="Texas All-Star USA Tag Team Championship">
        TASW Tag Team Championship
       </a>
       from the Maoris (Tudui and Wakahi) on May 25, 1985 and held them until
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alberto_Madril" title="Alberto Madril">
        Al Madrill
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Chavo_Guerrero_Sr." title="Chavo Guerrero Sr.">
        Chavo Guerrero
       </a>
       took the titles from them on June 5, 1985.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The titles would be vacated later that same month when Chavo Guerrero refused to wrestle in a rematch against Diamond and Kiniski because Chavo’s father
       <a href="/wiki/Gory_Guerrero" title="Gory Guerrero">
        Gory Guerrero
       </a>
       was the special referee chosen for the match. Madrill teamed up with Black Gorman instead but lost the match. After the match, Madrill attacked Chavo Guerrero splitting the team and forcing the titles to be held up.
      </p>
      <p>
       When Chavo won the rights to name a new partner he chose to give the titles to ”American Breed” instead, only now American Breed consisted of Paul Diamond and a rookie named
       <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Michaels" title="Shawn Michaels">
        Shawn Michaels
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HBK_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-HBK-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       as Kiniski had left the promotion a few weeks prior.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Diamond and Michaels lost and then regained the titles from the “Masked Hoods” (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aldo_Ortiz" title="Aldo Ortiz">
        Ricky Santana
       </a>
       and Tony Torres)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       before being beaten for the titles on January 27, 1986 by Al Madrill and Magnificent Zulu.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Both Michaels and Diamond began to add more to their wrestling skills, thanks in part to fellow wrestler
       <a href="/wiki/Chris_Adams_(wrestler)" title="Chris Adams (wrestler)">
        Chris Adams
       </a>
       , who split his time between
       <a href="/wiki/World_Class_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Class Championship Wrestling">
        World Class Championship Wrestling
       </a>
       and Texas All-Star at the time.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Continental_Wrestling_Association_.281986.E2.80.931988.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Continental_Wrestling_Association_(1986–1988)">
        Continental Wrestling Association (1986–1988)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Continental Wrestling Association (1986–1988)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Badd_Company" title="Badd Company">
        Badd Company
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In 1986, Diamond moved on to
       <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">
        Memphis, Tennessee
       </a>
       where he worked for
       <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lawler" title="Jerry Lawler">
        Jerry Lawler
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Jarrett" title="Jerry Jarrett">
        Jerry Jarrett
       </a>
       ’s
       <a href="/wiki/Continental_Wrestling_Association" title="Continental Wrestling Association">
        Continental Wrestling Association
       </a>
       . In the fall of 1986 Diamond teamed up with a young
       <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Jarrett" title="Jeff Jarrett">
        Jeff Jarrett
       </a>
       to win the
       <a href="/wiki/CWA/AWA_International_Tag_Team_Championship" title="CWA/AWA International Tag Team Championship">
        CWA/AWA International Tag Team Championship
       </a>
       from
       <a href="/wiki/Tarzan_Goto" title="Tarzan Goto">
        Tarzan Goto
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Akio_Sato_(wrestler)" title="Akio Sato (wrestler)">
        Akio Sato
       </a>
       on November 3, 1986. The team lost the titles back to Goto and Sato less than 2 weeks later.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In late 1986, the tag team specialist Diamond was teamed up with
       <a href="/wiki/Pat_Tanaka" title="Pat Tanaka">
        Pat Tanaka
       </a>
       , who was also seen as a tag team specialist, to form
       <a href="/wiki/Badd_Company" title="Badd Company">
        Badd Company
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tag_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Tag-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They held the federation's tag team titles four times and the two won the CWA/AWA International Tag Team Championship, beating Tarzan Goto and Akio Sato on December 15, 1986. The first reign was short lived as
       <a href="/wiki/The_Bushwhackers" title="The Bushwhackers">
        The Sheepherders
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Luke_Williams_(wrestler)" title="Luke Williams (wrestler)">
        Luke Williams
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Butch_Miller_(wrestler)" title="Butch Miller (wrestler)">
        Butch Miller
       </a>
       ) defeated the young duo on January 10, 1987.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Badd Company quickly regained the titles only to lose them to Tarzan Goto and Akio Sato on February 5, 1987.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The third run with the tag team titles came on May 9, 1987 when the team beat
       <a href="/wiki/Mark_Starr" title="Mark Starr">
        Mark Starr
       </a>
       in a handicap match,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       but lost them back to Mark Starr and his new tag team partner Billy Joe Travis. Badd Company had one last run with the International tag team titles as they won the vacant titles on May 25, 1987
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and held them until July 6, 1987 where they lost the titles to
       <a href="/wiki/Bill_Dundee" title="Bill Dundee">
        Bill Dundee
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Johnson" title="Rocky Johnson">
        Rocky Johnson
       </a>
       (who were the last International tag team champions).
      </p>
      <p>
       Badd Company also won the CWA’s main tag team titles in Memphis, when they defeated Jeff Jarrett and Billy Joe Travis for the
       <a href="/wiki/AWA_Southern_Tag_Team_Championship" title="AWA Southern Tag Team Championship">
        AWA Southern Tag Team Championship
       </a>
       on August 8, 1987.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Tanaka and Diamond soon lost them as
       <a href="/wiki/The_Nasty_Boys" title="The Nasty Boys">
        the Nasty Boys
       </a>
       took the gold from them just over a month later.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="American_Wrestling_Association_.281988.E2.80.931990.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="American_Wrestling_Association_(1988–1990)">
        American Wrestling Association (1988–1990)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: American Wrestling Association (1988–1990)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       After working as a team for almost a year, Badd Company moved on from the CWA to the
       <a href="/wiki/American_Wrestling_Association" title="American Wrestling Association">
        American Wrestling Association
       </a>
       . In the AWA they were given identical ring gear, and were accompanied by flamboyant manager
       <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Dallas_Page" title="Diamond Dallas Page">
        Diamond Dallas Page
       </a>
       , who always had a number of valets, known as the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diamond_Exchange#Diamond_Dolls" title="Diamond Exchange">
        Diamond Dolls
       </a>
       , with him.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tag_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Tag-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The team's first feud was against
       <a href="/wiki/The_Rockers" title="The Rockers">
        The Midnight Rockers
       </a>
       , whom they defeated for the
       <a href="/wiki/AWA_World_Tag_Team_Championship" title="AWA World Tag Team Championship">
        AWA World Tag Team Championship
       </a>
       on March 19, 1988.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       During their year long title reign, Badd Company would feud heavily with
       <a href="/wiki/Chavo_Guerrero_Sr." title="Chavo Guerrero Sr.">
        Chavo
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Mando_Guerrero" title="Mando Guerrero">
        Mando Guerrero
       </a>
       and the team known as the
       <b>
        Top Guns
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Rice" title="Ricky Rice">
        Ricky Rice
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Derrick_Dukes" title="Derrick Dukes">
        Derrick Dukes
       </a>
       ). They teamed with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Debra_Miceli" title="Debra Miceli">
        Madusa Miceli
       </a>
       to face the team of the Top Guns and
       <a href="/wiki/Wendi_Richter" title="Wendi Richter">
        Wendi Richter
       </a>
       at the first AWA PPV,
       <a href="/wiki/SuperClash#SuperClash_III" title="SuperClash">
        SuperClash III
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SC3_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SC3-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Both Badd Company’s Tag Team Title and Wendi Richter’s
       <a href="/wiki/AWA_World_Women%27s_Championship" title="AWA World Women's Championship">
        AWA World Women's Championship
       </a>
       were on the line, but since Richter pinned Miceli, Badd Company remained the champions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Diamond and Tanaka’s reign ended on March 25, 1989 as they lost to “the Olympians" (
       <a href="/wiki/Brad_Rheingans" title="Brad Rheingans">
        Brad Rheingans
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ken_Patera" title="Ken Patera">
        Ken Patera
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Shortly after losing their titles they split from Page, and had a short feud against each other before wrestling in singles competition until early 1990. Just before moving to the WWF, Diamond teamed with The Trooper
       <a href="/wiki/The_Patriot_(wrestler)" title="The Patriot (wrestler)">
        Del Wilkes
       </a>
       for a short run at the AWA Tag Team Titles against The Destruction Crew (Mike Enos and
       <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Bloom" title="Wayne Bloom">
        Wayne Bloom
       </a>
       ). Diamond and Trooper actually defeated The Destruction Crew, but were not awarded the titles when it was determined that Diamond pinned the wrong person.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="World_Wrestling_Federation_.281990.E2.80.931993.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Federation_(1990–1993)">
        World Wrestling Federation (1990–1993)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Federation (1990–1993)">

      </h3>
      <h4>
       <span id="Debut_and_The_Orient_Express_.281990.E2.80.931992.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Debut_and_The_Orient_Express_(1990–1992)">
        Debut and The Orient Express (1990–1992)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Debut and The Orient Express (1990–1992)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/The_Orient_Express" title="The Orient Express">
        The Orient Express
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Diamond signed with the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">
        World Wrestling Federation
       </a>
       in 1990, working mainly on the lower card against
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dustin_Rhodes" title="Dustin Rhodes">
        Dustin Rhodes
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Hillbilly_Jim" title="Hillbilly Jim">
        Hillbilly Jim
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Al_Perez" title="Al Perez">
        Al Perez
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       usually in a losing role. Orient Express member
       <a href="/wiki/Akio_Sato_(wrestler)" title="Akio Sato (wrestler)">
        Akio Sato
       </a>
       decided to take a position in the Titan (WWF) front office focusing on the WWF television rights sold to Japan TV networks following the 1990 Survivor Series. Following Survivor Series 1990 the "C-team" house shows were no longer taking place and many wrestlers left television following the event. The move left Pat Tanaka without a partner in
       <a href="/wiki/The_Orient_Express" title="The Orient Express">
        The Orient Express
       </a>
       . The WWF decided to reunite the team known as
       <a href="/wiki/Badd_Company" title="Badd Company">
        Badd Company
       </a>
       , only this time with Paul Diamond wearing a mask to hide the fact that he was not
       <a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">
        Asian
       </a>
       and using the name of Kato,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tag_8-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Tag-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       During this time, the team had a very well received match with their old enemies the Rockers at the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(1991)" title="Royal Rumble (1991)">
        1991 Royal Rumble
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and another acclaimed match against the
       <a href="/wiki/The_Hart_Foundation#The_New_Foundation" title="The Hart Foundation">
        New Foundation
       </a>
       at the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(1992)" title="Royal Rumble (1992)">
        1992 Royal Rumble
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       which were the only PPV appearances for the New Orient Express.
      </p>
      <p>
       Sato briefly rejoined the team in 1991 to team with Tanaka and Kato for a series of 6-man tag-team matches on WWF house shows,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       but left the WWF after only a handful of matches. Tanaka would leave the WWF in early 1992.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span id="Max_Moon_.281992-1993.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Max_Moon_(1992-1993)">
        Max Moon (1992-1993)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Max Moon (1992-1993)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       In the fall of 1992, Diamond got a brand new
       <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Gimmick" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">
        gimmick
       </a>
       in "Maximillian Moon" ("Max Moon" for short). Diamond wasn’t originally intended to play Max Moon. The gimmick was created for
       <a href="/wiki/Konnan" title="Konnan">
        Konnan
       </a>
       , but Konnan left the WWF after a backstage disagreement. Since Diamond fit the costume, he was chosen to take over the character.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WrestleCrap_18-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-WrestleCrap-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The suit was a very elaborate powder blue bodysuit with markings that were supposed to look like a
       <a href="/wiki/Printed_circuit_board" title="Printed circuit board">
        circuit board
       </a>
       and white protruding rings around his arms making him look rather "outlandish".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The elaborate outfit also came with two wrist devices that shot out fireworks and a
       <a href="/wiki/Jet_pack" title="Jet pack">
        jet pack
       </a>
       that was supposed to make Max Moon look like a man of the future.
      </p>
      <p>
       Diamond (as Max Moon) would wrestle and defeat
       <a href="/wiki/Terry_Taylor" title="Terry Taylor">
        Terry Taylor
       </a>
       in a series of house show matches during the rest of 1992, but otherwise, his biggest victories came against
       <a href="/wiki/Rick_Martel" title="Rick Martel">
        Rick Martel
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barry_Darsow" title="Barry Darsow">
        Repo Man
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
       both by disqualification. On the December 7, 1992 episode of
       <a href="/wiki/WWF_Prime_Time_Wrestling" title="WWF Prime Time Wrestling">
        WWF Prime Time Wrestling
       </a>
       , Max Moon teamed with High Energy (
       <a href="/wiki/Owen_Hart" title="Owen Hart">
        Owen Hart
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Koko_B._Ware" title="Koko B. Ware">
        Koko B. Ware
       </a>
       ) in a six-man tag team match against
       <a href="/wiki/The_Beverly_Brothers" title="The Beverly Brothers">
        The Beverly Brothers
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Lanny_Poffo" title="Lanny Poffo">
        The Genius
       </a>
       . Max Moon and High Energy won the match when Moon pinned The Genius.
      </p>
      <p>
       On January 11, 1993, Max Moon wrestled and lost an
       <a href="/wiki/WWE_Intercontinental_Championship" title="WWE Intercontinental Championship">
        Intercontinental Championship
       </a>
       match to former tag team partner/longtime rival Shawn Michaels on the very first
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">
         Monday Night Raw
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The loss to Michaels was a sign of things to come for the Max Moon gimmick as he began losing to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Mid-carder" title="List of professional wrestling terms">
        midcarders
       </a>
       such as
       <a href="/wiki/Steve_Keirn" title="Steve Keirn">
        Skinner
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Max Moon gimmick only appeared at one PPV, participating in the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(1993)#Royal_Rumble_entrances_and_eliminations" title="Royal Rumble (1993)">
        30 Man Elimination match
       </a>
       at the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(1993)" title="Royal Rumble (1993)">
        1993 Royal Rumble
       </a>
       , where he was in the ring less than 2 minutes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In late February, Paul Diamond’s contract with the WWF expired and was not renewed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canoe-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="NWA_Eastern.2FExtreme_Championship_Wrestling_.281993.E2.80.931994.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="NWA_Eastern/Extreme_Championship_Wrestling_(1993–1994)">
        NWA Eastern/Extreme Championship Wrestling (1993–1994)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: NWA Eastern/Extreme Championship Wrestling (1993–1994)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Diamond and Pat Tanaka reunited as “Badd Company" to work for
       <a href="/wiki/Extreme_Championship_Wrestling" title="Extreme Championship Wrestling">
        Eastern Championship Wrestling
       </a>
       (the forerunner to “Extreme Championship Wrestling") in late 1993. The team made their debut at
       <i>
        NWA Bloodfest: Part 1
       </i>
       on October 1, 1993 where they beat "Bad Breed" (
       <a href="/wiki/Ian_Rotten" title="Ian Rotten">
        Ian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Axl_Rotten" title="Axl Rotten">
        Axl Rotten
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prowrestlinghistory.com_25-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-prowrestlinghistory.com-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Tanaka and Diamond got a shot at the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ECW_Tag_Team_Championship" title="ECW Tag Team Championship">
        ECW Tag Team Champions
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Tony_Stetson" title="Tony Stetson">
        Tony Stetson
       </a>
       and Johnny Hotbody later in the night, which Badd Company lost.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prowrestlinghistory.com_25-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-prowrestlinghistory.com-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The next night at
       <i>
        NWA Bloodfest: Part 2
       </i>
       , Badd Company fought against
       <a href="/wiki/The_Public_Enemy_(professional_wrestling)" title="The Public Enemy (professional wrestling)">
        Public Enemy
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Rocco_Rock" title="Rocco Rock">
        Rocco Rock
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Grunge" title="Johnny Grunge">
        Johnny Grunge
       </a>
       ) in one of their very early matches.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http_26-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-http-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Later in the show, Badd Company and the Public Enemy would clash again, this time in a
       <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Multi-competitor_match_variations" title="Professional wrestling match types">
        Three way
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Cages" title="Professional wrestling match types">
        steel cage match
       </a>
       that also included Bad Breed. This time, Public Enemy was victorious.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http_26-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-http-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       At
       <i>
        Terror at Tabor
       </i>
       on November 12, 1993, Badd Company beat the makeshift team of Don E. Allen and
       <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Hughes" title="Curtis Hughes">
        Mr. Hughes
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_27-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       After the match, Badd Company called out Public Enemy resulting in an impromptu match between Paul Diamond and Rocco Rock which Diamond won.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_27-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Public Enemy quickly got very brutal revenge on Badd Company as they defeated the duo in a “South Philly hood" match the very next day at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ECW_November_to_Remember#1993" title="ECW November to Remember">
        November to Remember
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
       At
       <i>
        Holiday Hell 1993
       </i>
       on December 26, Pat Tanaka defeated Rocco Rock in a “Body Count" match.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       As 1993 turned to 1994, Badd Company set their sights on ECW Tag Team Champions
       <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Sullivan_(wrestler)" title="Kevin Sullivan (wrestler)">
        Kevin Sullivan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tazz" title="Tazz">
        The Tasmaniac
       </a>
       ; they defeated them twice in non-title matches.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_30-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_30-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
       At "The Night the Line was Crossed" on February 5, 1994, Tanaka and his one night only partner
       <a href="/wiki/Ed_Farhat" title="Ed Farhat">
        The Sheik
       </a>
       defeated Sullivan and The Tasmaniac. Unfortunately, the team had been stripped of the titles the night before.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
        <a href="#cite_note-31">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
       When Tanaka was rejoined by Diamond, the team lost to the (once again) Tag Team Champions when the gold was finally on the line.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
        <a href="#cite_note-32">
         [32]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On March 27, 1994, Badd Company wrestled its last match for ECW, a house show victory over
       <a href="/wiki/Rockin%27_Rebel" title="Rockin' Rebel">
        Rockin' Rebel
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Gary_Wolfe_(wrestler)" title="Gary Wolfe (wrestler)">
        Pitbull #1
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="World_Championship_Wrestling_.281994.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="World_Championship_Wrestling_(1994)">
        World Championship Wrestling (1994)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: World Championship Wrestling (1994)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       By the time Badd Company wrestled its last match for ECW, Tanaka and Diamond had already signed with
       <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Championship Wrestling">
        World Championship Wrestling
       </a>
       ; Tanaka had even made his on screen debut as "Tanaka-San".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
        <a href="#cite_note-33">
         [33]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Paul Diamond showed up with the Kato mask on using the name "Haito" (sometimes spelled "Hyeeto") only days after their last ECW match.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
        <a href="#cite_note-34">
         [34]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The duo reprised their "Orient Express" gimmick but could not use the name since the WWF owned the trademark to it. The two men wrestled a couple of matches together
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Graham_Cawthon_35-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Graham_Cawthon-35">
         [35]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Graham_Cawthon_35-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Graham_Cawthon-35">
         [35]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
        <a href="#cite_note-36">
         [36]
        </a>
       </sup>
       but they never achieved any notoriety in WCW. Badd Company / The Orient Express finally split up for good by the end of 1994.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tag_8-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Tag-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Independent_circuit_.281994.E2.80.932001.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Independent_circuit_(1994–2001)">
        Independent circuit (1994–2001)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Diamond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Independent circuit (1994–2001)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Diamond worked for Jerry Lawler's promotions in Memphis after working for WCW, first for the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Wrestling_Association" title="United States Wrestling Association">
        United States Wrestling Association
       </a>
       where he held the
       <a href="/wiki/USWA_World_Tag_Team_Championship" title="USWA World Tag Team Championship">
        USWA World Tag Team Championship
       </a>
       alongside
       <a href="/wiki/Steve_Doll" title="Steve Doll">
        Steven Dunn
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titles_6-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-Titles-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       After the USWA, he worked for “Music City Wrestling” until a torn triceps muscle put him out of action for six months in 1998.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe_3-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canoe-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Diamond had set up an agreement to join Shawn Michaels’
       <a href="/wiki/Texas_Wrestling_Academy" title="Texas Wrestling Academy">
        Texas Wrestling Academy
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe_3-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canoe-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Diamond also wrestled for the Texas Wrestling Alliance under the name of "Venom", holding the TWA title twice
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
        <a href="#cite_note-37">
         [37]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and also being involved in Shawn Michaels return to wrestling after a 1998 back injury that was thought to have put a permanent end to his wrestling career. When Michaels handed the wrestling academy to head trainer Rudy Gonzalez, Diamond and his family moved to Tampa then to Winnipeg where Diamond opened up a wrestling school called "Paul Diamond Hard Knocks Pro Wrestling Academy," while helping out his terminally ill father.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canoe_3-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canoe-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Diamond semi-retired from active competition in 2001 to focus on the wrestling academy; his only active involvement in recent years has been
       <b>
        Rocket City Wrestling
       </b>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Alabama" title="Huntsville, Alabama">
        Huntsville, Alabama
       </a>
       wrestling sometimes twice, as Diamond and as Max Moon
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
        <a href="#cite_note-38">
         [38]
        </a>
       </sup>
       while also being involved in the booking.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_wrestling">
        In wrestling
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
