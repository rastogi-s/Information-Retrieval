      <p>
       <b>
        Portia
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'p' in 'pie'">
            p
           </span>
           <span title="/ɔːr/: 'ar' in 'war'">
            ɔːr
           </span>
           <span title="/ʃ/: 'sh' in 'shy'">
            ʃ
           </span>
           <span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">
            ə
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          POR
         </span>
         -shə
        </i>
       </a>
       ) is an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inner_satellite" title="Inner satellite">
        inner satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">
        Uranus
       </a>
       . It was discovered from the images taken by
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Voyager_2" title="Voyager 2">
         Voyager 2
        </a>
       </i>
       on 3 January 1986, and was given the temporary designation
       <b>
        S/1986 U 1
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAUC_4164_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-IAUC_4164-7">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The moon is named after
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portia_(Merchant_of_Venice)" title="Portia (Merchant of Venice)">
        Portia
       </a>
       , the heroine of
       <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">
        William Shakespeare
       </a>
       's play
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Merchant_of_Venice" title="The Merchant of Venice">
         The Merchant of Venice
        </a>
       </i>
       . It is also designated
       <b>
        Uranus XII
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer-8">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Portia is the second-largest inner satellite of Uranus after
       <a href="/wiki/Puck_(moon)" title="Puck (moon)">
        Puck
       </a>
       . The Portian orbit, which lies inside Uranus'
       <a href="/wiki/Synchronous_orbit" title="Synchronous orbit">
        synchronous orbital radius
       </a>
       , is slowly decaying due to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal_deceleration" title="Tidal deceleration">
        tidal deceleration
       </a>
       . The moon will one day either break up into a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planetary_ring" title="Planetary ring">
        planetary ring
       </a>
       or hit Uranus.
      </p>
      <p>
       It heads a group of satellites called the
       <b>
        Portia Group
       </b>
       , which includes
       <a href="/wiki/Bianca_(moon)" title="Bianca (moon)">
        Bianca
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cressida_(moon)" title="Cressida (moon)">
        Cressida
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Desdemona_(moon)" title="Desdemona (moon)">
        Desdemona
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Juliet_(moon)" title="Juliet (moon)">
        Juliet
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Rosalind_(moon)" title="Rosalind (moon)">
        Rosalind
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cupid_(moon)" title="Cupid (moon)">
        Cupid
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Belinda_(moon)" title="Belinda (moon)">
        Belinda
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Perdita_(moon)" title="Perdita (moon)">
        Perdita
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       These satellites have similar orbits and photometric properties.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Little is known about Portia beyond its size of about 140 km,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karkoschka,_Voyager_2001_3-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karkoschka,_Voyager_2001-3">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       orbit,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jacobson_1998_1-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Jacobson_1998-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and geometric
       <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
        albedo
       </a>
       of about 0.08.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001_6-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the Voyager 2 images, Portia appears as an elongated object whose major axis points towards Uranus. The ratio of axes of the Portia's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prolate_spheroid" title="Prolate spheroid">
        prolate spheroid
       </a>
       is 0.8 ± 0.1.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karkoschka,_Voyager_2001_3-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karkoschka,_Voyager_2001-3">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Its surface is grey in color.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karkoschka,_Voyager_2001_3-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karkoschka,_Voyager_2001-3">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Observations with
       <a href="/wiki/Hubble_Space_Telescope" title="Hubble Space Telescope">
        Hubble Space Telescope
       </a>
       and large terrestrial telescopes found water ice absorption features in the spectrum of Portia.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001_6-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karkoschka,_Hubble_2001-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dumas_Smith_et_al._2003_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dumas_Smith_et_al._2003-9">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
