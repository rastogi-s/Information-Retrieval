      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       a song recorded by Japanese singer
       <a href="/wiki/Koda_Kumi" title="Koda Kumi">
        Koda Kumi
       </a>
       . Much like her past summer single
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Freaky_(song)" title="Freaky (song)">
         Freaky
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/4_Hot_Wave" title="4 Hot Wave">
         4 Hot Wave
        </a>
        ,
       </i>
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       carried four songs. It charted at #2 on
       <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">
        Oricon
       </a>
       and stayed on the charts for nineteen weeks.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was released in CD and CD+DVD editions, with the limited editions of both versions carrying an alternate rendition of "Moon Crying."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Information">
        Information
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Information">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Moon
       </i>
       is
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japanese
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">
        singer-songwriter
       </a>
       Kumi Koda's fortieth single. It charted at #2 on the weekly
       <a href="/wiki/Oricon_Singles_Chart" title="Oricon Singles Chart">
        Oricon Singles Charts
       </a>
       and remained on the charts for nineteen weeks.
      </p>
      <p>
       The single became Kumi's first to be released after the controversy in late January of that same year. On January 30, Kumi made an inflammatory statement on
       <a href="/wiki/All_Night_Nippon" title="All Night Nippon">
        All Night Nippon
       </a>
       when asked about whether or not she wanted her then-manager to have children, whereas her manager had just gotten married. She had responded, "When women turn 35, their
       <a href="/wiki/Amniotic_fluid" title="Amniotic fluid">
        amniotic fluid
       </a>
       goes rotten, so I hope they have a child before then."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Her website had been shut down with a letter from Kumi posted on the page, apologizing for her remark. Due to the controversy, it was speculated that any following singles would not chart well, if she chose to continue releasing music.
      </p>
      <p>
       Kumi had gone to say how the song's lyrics and overall tone were written as an apology and to reflect how much she had regretted the statement.
      </p>
      <p>
       The single continued on with the theme of releasing a summer single with four songs, following
       <i>
        4 hot wave
       </i>
       (2006) and
       <i>
        Freaky
       </i>
       (2007). However, although the single carried four different musical tracks, it was only a double
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-side" title="A-side">
        a-side
       </a>
       with
       <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">
        music videos
       </a>
       only being released for "Moon Crying" and "That Ain't Cool."
      </p>
      <p>
       For "Moon Crying," Kumi worked closely with musical
       <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">
        composer
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H-wonder" title="H-wonder">
        h-wonder
       </a>
       . The two had worked together several times in the past, beginning with her debut single
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Take_Back" title="Take Back">
         Take Back
        </a>
       </i>
       in 2000.
      </p>
      <p>
       "That Ain't Cool" featured
       <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">
        American
       </a>
       singer-songwriter
       <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">
        Fergie
       </a>
       from the
       <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">
        hip-hop
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">
        pop
       </a>
       group
       <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">
        The Black Eyed Peas
       </a>
       . Although both Kumi and Fergie were listed as the writers for the song, the song was mainly written by Fergie, who presented the song as a gift to Kumi upon meeting the artist.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]

        </i>

       Limited editions contained an alternate piano arrangements of "Moon Crying" on the CD and a live version of "Moon Crying," which was performed on May 15, 2008 during her

      <h2>
       <span id="Post_Controversy_.26_KODA_REKI">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Post_Controversy_&amp;_KODA_REKI">
        Post Controversy &amp; KODA REKI
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post Controversy &amp; KODA REKI">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        "It’s scary to talk in front of people. It’s scary to stand on stage. I want to quit being Koda Kumi."
       </p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        —
        <cite>
         Koda Kumi, KODA REKI
        </cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       In
       <i>
        KODA REKI,
       </i>
       Kumi's published
       <a href="/wiki/Biography" title="Biography">
        biography
       </a>
       , she said how she still cries when she thinks about the comment on All Night Nippon and hurting "many Japanese women." She goes on to say how it was that moment in her career when she realized how immature and unprofessional she was, not realizing how many people listened to her words due to her fame. She admitted how she had cancelled several rehearsals for
       <i>
        Kingdom's
       </i>
       corresponding tour,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Live_Tour_2008:_Kingdom" title="Live Tour 2008: Kingdom">
         Live Tour 2008 ~Kingdom~
        </a>
        ,
       </i>
       due to the severe anxiety she would get when she believed that she would walk out onto the stage to perform, only to have an empty arena.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music_videos">
        Music videos
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music videos">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       "Moon Crying" carried a very somber tone about missing a deceased loved one and not knowing how to move on. In the video, Kumi's departed lover comes to her as a spirit, trying to tell her it's okay to move on. She becomes angry, but ultimately accepts his death.
      </p>
      <p>
       "That Ain't Cool feat. Fergie" was directed by
       <a href="/wiki/Music_video_director" title="Music video director">
        music video director
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Choreography" title="Choreography">
        choreographer
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Fatima_Robinson" title="Fatima Robinson">
        Fatima Robinson
       </a>
       and was shot in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">
        Atlanta, Georgia
       </a>
       . It premiered on
       <a href="/wiki/MTV_Japan" title="MTV Japan">
        MTV Japan
       </a>
       on May 29, 2008 and carried a theme of two women (played by Kumi and Fergie) finding out that their lovers had been cheating on them.
      </p>
      <p>
       An "album version" was placed on Kumi's seventh
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">
        studio album
       </a>
       ,
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trick_(Kumi_Koda_album)" title="Trick (Kumi Koda album)">
         Trick
        </a>
        ,
       </i>
       and featured a complete story line with Kumi and Fergie seeking retribution on the man that had been dating both of them without either woman realizing.
      </p>
