      <p>
       <b>
        Ignignokt
       </b>
       and
       <b>
        Err
       </b>
       (known collectively as
       <b>
        The Mooninites
       </b>
       ) are fictional characters on the
       <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">
        Adult Swim
       </a>
       animated television series
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force">
         Aqua Teen Hunger Force
        </a>
       </i>
       (also known by various
       <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force#Alternative_titles" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force">
        alternative titles
       </a>
       ). Ignignokt and Err are both
       <a href="/wiki/Two-dimensional_space" title="Two-dimensional space">
        two dimensional
       </a>
       alien villains from the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       who frequently visit Earth and cause senseless destruction and chaos for the main protagonists. Ignignokt is the larger of the two who frequently appears as more calm and patient, whereas Err appears more temperamental and hyper. Both characters were created by series creators
       <a href="/wiki/Dave_Willis" title="Dave Willis">
        Dave Willis
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Matt_Maiellaro" title="Matt Maiellaro">
        Matt Maiellaro
       </a>
       . Willis provides the voice of Ignignokt and Maiellaro provides the voice of Err.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Mooninites were also the main protagonists in a
       <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">
        television pilot
       </a>
       for a potential
       <i>
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force
       </i>
       spin-off entitled
       <i>
        Spacecataz
       </i>
       , which was never picked up for a full series or aired on television, outside of clips during
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_open" title="Cold open">
        cold openings
       </a>
       during the
       <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_(season_3)" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force (season 3)">
        third season
       </a>
       . The Mooninites also received national controversial attention on January 31, 2007 during the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Boston_bomb_scare" title="2007 Boston bomb scare">
        2007 Boston bomb scare
       </a>
       when
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LED" title="LED">
        LED
       </a>
       displays resembling them were placed in various locations in
       <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">
        Boston
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">
        Massachusetts
       </a>
       and were mistaken for bombs causing a panic. The duo has received a positive reception from reviewers at
       <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">
        IGN
       </a>
       , and have appeared in various forms of media relating to the series including the 2007 series film adaption
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_Colon_Movie_Film_for_Theaters" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force Colon Movie Film for Theaters">
         Aqua Teen Hunger Force Colon Movie Film for Theaters
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Role_in_Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force">
        Role in
        <i>
         Aqua Teen Hunger Force
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mooninites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Role in Aqua Teen Hunger Force">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Quad_Glacier.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="282" data-file-width="353" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a6/Quad_Glacier.png/250px-Quad_Glacier.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/a/a6/Quad_Glacier.png 1.5x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Quad_Glacier.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The Mooninites after forming the Quad Glacier.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Both of The Mooninites live in the center of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       , which they frequently boast about.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Besides Ignignokt and Err, other Mooninites are not commonly seen with the exception of Ignignokt's uncle Cliff and a giant mooninite monster named "The Gorgotron".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Remoonedcite_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Remoonedcite-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoonMasterepisode_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-MoonMasterepisode-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Despite the apparent lack of mooninites on the moon a human female police officer patrols the surface of the Moon, as seen in "Jumpy George".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Mooninites periodically visit earth in order to harass
       <a href="/wiki/Master_Shake" title="Master Shake">
        Master Shake
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Meatwad" title="Meatwad">
        Meatwad
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Frylock" title="Frylock">
        Frylock
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Carl_Brutananadilewski" title="Carl Brutananadilewski">
        Carl Brutananadilewski
       </a>
       , arriving in a purple-and-blue spaceship. They particularly target Meatwad to take advantage of his childlike naivete, often encouraging Meatwad to participate in bad behavior such as shoplifting, burglary or smoking cigarettes and
       <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">
        marijuana
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moonajuana_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moonajuana-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RevengeoftheMooninitesepisode_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-RevengeoftheMooninitesepisode-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Frylock, who sees right through their schemes, has a very condescending view of The Mooninites and often strives to keep them away from Meatwad, himself, and their property. Master Shake is more welcoming to them than Frylock, and only slightly less gullible than Meatwad when it comes to their lies. The Mooninites seem to welcome Master Shake to a degree by giving him shoplifted items as gifts, inviting him to help cash a stolen government aid check, and offering him marijuana, although they frequently try to trick Shake so they can steal from him as well. Carl is often the primary victim in The Mooninites' plans and schemes. They often end up stealing or destroying his possessions, causing damage to his home or car, and physically assaulting him in some cases. Most encounters The Mooninites have with the main protagonists often end with them rushing to their ship and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finger_(gesture)" title="Finger (gesture)">
        shooting the bird
       </a>
       to them from
       <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">
        outer space
       </a>
       as they return home, as a
       <a href="/wiki/Running_gag" title="Running gag">
        running gag
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Both of the Mooninites are equipped with a small
       <a href="/wiki/Raygun" title="Raygun">
        laser gun
       </a>
       that fires a small, slow-moving square bullet (despite Ignignokt's insistence on how fast it goes). Err and Ignignokt also possess the ability to lock their heads together to create the "Quad Laser", which results in them firing four laser guns at once, although this only results in them firing a much larger and much slower-moving projectile.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In "The Last One", the Mooninites created an even larger weapon consisting of multiple copies of themselves formed into the shape of a giant gun which they call "The Quad Glazer" (although they argue over whether it's actually called "The Quad Glacier"). The bullet fired was even more massive and seemed to not move at all, and the sheer size of the gun caused Err to break his legs under the sheer weight of the weapon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheLastOnecite_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-TheLastOnecite-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They also have the ability to jump very high (but only on the Moon) and spin around in place flashing bright colors.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode_1-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Little is known about their family lives, however, it's revealed that both Ignignokt and Err come from broken homes and neither had a father in their lives while growing up. Err went on to explain that his father was an alcoholic who abandoned him when he was three, and his mother later abandoned him at a summer camp, causing him to slip into a deep depression, and his frequent bad behavior is a desperate cry for attention. Ignignokt didn’t go into as much detail but did express some sorrow that he didn’t learn to "steal or drink the right way" from his father.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moonajuana_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moonajuana-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In "Remooned" it is revealed Ignignokt has an uncle named Cliff, who suffers from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Post-traumatic_stress_disorder" title="Post-traumatic stress disorder">
        post-traumatic stress disorder
       </a>
       as a result of serving in the "Lunar Wars" and requires
       <a href="/wiki/Home_care" title="Home care">
        home care
       </a>
       as a result.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Remoonedcite_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Remoonedcite-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">
        Characters
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mooninites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Mooninites were both created and designed by series creators
       <a href="/wiki/Dave_Willis" title="Dave Willis">
        Dave Willis
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Matt_Maiellaro" title="Matt Maiellaro">
        Matt Maiellaro
       </a>
       and first appeared in the
       <i>
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force
       </i>
       <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_(season_1)" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force (season 1)">
        season one
       </a>
       episode "Mayhem of The Mooninites", an episode in which they were centric. The episode first aired in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       on October 14, 2001 on
       <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">
        Cartoon Network’s
       </a>
       late night programming block
       <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">
        Adult Swim
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IgnignoktGuide_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-IgnignoktGuide-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ErrGuide_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ErrGuide-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Willis himself provides the voice of Ignignokt and Err is voiced by Maiellaro, whose voice is digitally altered for the role.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitecommentary_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitecommentary-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       When appearing in the series Err is never written into the script as all of his dialog and actions are completely
       <a href="/wiki/Improvisation" title="Improvisation">
        improvised
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The duo were originally created as
       <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">
        ghosts
       </a>
       from the 1982 video game
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/E.T._the_Extra-Terrestrial_(video_game)" title="E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (video game)">
         E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial
        </a>
       </i>
       , an
       <a href="/wiki/Atari,_Inc." title="Atari, Inc.">
        Atari
       </a>
       video game adaption to the science fiction
       <a href="/wiki/E.T._the_Extra-Terrestrial" title="E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial">
        film of the same name
       </a>
       . The protagonists house was to be revealed to be built on top an ancient Indian burial ground filled with old copies of the game. This premise was based on the
       <a href="/wiki/Atari_video_game_burial" title="Atari video game burial">
        Atari video game burial
       </a>
       , when a surplus of over a million copies of the game, along with several other Atari games, were buried underground in
       <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">
        New Mexico
       </a>
       in 1983.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitecommentary_10-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitecommentary-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They are both
       <a href="/wiki/Recurring_character" title="Recurring character">
        recurring characters
       </a>
       of the series, who both have appeared more than any other characters besides the main protagonists. They are usually the main focus in the episodes where they appear, and also make several brief cameos in certain episodes as well. A person from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standards_and_practices" title="Standards and practices">
        standards and practices
       </a>
       at the network originally saw issue with Ignignokt and Err smoking
       <a href="/wiki/Cigarette" title="Cigarette">
        cigarettes
       </a>
       , Willis and Maiellaro argued that they are negative characters and that them smoking would depict smoking cigarettes as a negative action. Willis and Maiellaro claimed that they would remove the smoking cigarettes aspect of the characters from the script, however, never followed through on their claim.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitecommentary_10-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitecommentary-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Design">
        Design
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mooninites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Design">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Mooninites appear as
       <a href="/wiki/Two-dimensional_space" title="Two-dimensional space">
        two dimensional
       </a>
       pixelated figures with crude eyes, eyebrows, mouths, arms and legs, all composed from basic blue rectangles.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGNTen_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-IGNTen-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Neither Ignignokt nor Err can open their mouths to speak but rather the size of the rectangle used for their mouths changes size to the rhythm of what they are saying. Ignignokt is colored with a solid shade of light green, and significantly larger than Err and has on somewhat more complex shape with several perfectly symmetrical grooves across his head.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IgnignoktGuide_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-IgnignoktGuide-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Err is light purple and square shaped with a second rectangle on his head, which fits perfectly in the groove on Ignignokt's head, which is used to form the Quad Laser and the Quad Glacier.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode_1-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-MayhemoftheMooninitesepisode-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheLastOnecite_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-TheLastOnecite-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ErrGuide_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ErrGuide-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Neither Ignignokt or Err wear clothing on a normal basis, but can be seen wearing them on occasion.
      </p>
      <p>
       Err’s facial expression is set as angry and is never seen to change, even during an emotional monologue about his childhood in "Moonajuana",
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moonajuana_5-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moonajuana-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       whereas Ignignokt's facial expression is predominantly set as calm and relaxed, although unlike Err his facial expression occasionally changes to express anger.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Remoonedcite_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Remoonedcite-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Ignignokt's facial expressions never skew from calm or angry, however, his plain rectangular mouth reverts to the shape of a
       <a href="/wiki/Smile" title="Smile">
        smile
       </a>
       briefly during the pilot episode of
       <i>
        Spacecataz
       </i>
       , his mouth in the shape of a smile is never seen again outside of this one-time isolated appearance. In "Deleted Scenes" they are both
       <a href="/wiki/Hypnosis" title="Hypnosis">
        hypnotized
       </a>
       and their eyes become spinning circular spirals, a very rare alteration of their eyes. However, their facial expressions remain the same. Ignignokt has the ability to extend the size of his middle finger, and also the ability to increase the size of his entire hand immensely in order to be more prominent when
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finger_(gesture)" title="Finger (gesture)">
        giving the bird
       </a>
       to Earth.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Remoonedcite_2-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Remoonedcite-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RevengeoftheMooninitesepisode_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-RevengeoftheMooninitesepisode-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Spacecataz_spin-off_pilot">
        <i>
         Spacecataz
        </i>
        spin-off pilot
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mooninites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spacecataz spin-off pilot">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_(season_3)" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force (season 3)">
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force (season 3)
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Both Ignignokt and Err served as the main protagonist for a
       <i>
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force
       </i>
       <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">
        spin-off
       </a>
       entitled
       <i>
        Spacecataz
       </i>
       . A
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilot_episode" title="Pilot episode">
        pilot episode
       </a>
       for the spin-off was produced, however, Adult Swim did not pick it up for a full series as executives couldn't see it going anywhere with all the characters dying towards the end of the pilot, despite the parent series having a long history of
       <a href="/wiki/Canon_(fiction)" title="Canon (fiction)">
        non-canon
       </a>
       plot twists and storylines.
       <i>
        Spacecataz
       </i>
       was created by Willis and Maiellaro, and intended it to begin with a five episode first season. The plot revolves around both of The Mooninites and
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_characters#Oglethorpe_and_Emory" title="List of Aqua Teen Hunger Force characters">
        The Plutonians
       </a>
       , another villainous duo featured in
       <i>
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force
       </i>
       , having an ongoing back and forth mean-spirited string of practical jokes between each other. This follows up the hostility between both groups depicted in
       <i>
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The pilot episode was never aired on television but was released on the
       <i>
        Volume Four
       </i>
       DVD set on December 6, 2005.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDV4_14-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-DVDV4-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The pilot was also broken down into several parts and used as
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_open" title="Cold open">
        cold openings
       </a>
       throughout the
       <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_(season_3)" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force (season 3)">
        third season
       </a>
       of
       <i>
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force;
       </i>
       replacing short bits from
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_characters#Dr._Weird" title="List of Aqua Teen Hunger Force characters">
        Dr. Weird
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_characters#Steve" title="List of Aqua Teen Hunger Force characters">
        Steve
       </a>
       , which were used throughout the first two seasons.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Connection_to_the_2007_Boston_bomb_scare">
        Connection to the 2007 Boston bomb scare
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mooninites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Connection to the 2007 Boston bomb scare">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Further information:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Boston_bomb_scare" title="2007 Boston bomb scare">
        2007 Boston bomb scare
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ATHF_LED_in_Cambridge.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="368" data-file-width="364" height="253" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/ATHF_LED_in_Cambridge.jpg/250px-ATHF_LED_in_Cambridge.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5c/ATHF_LED_in_Cambridge.jpg 1.5x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ATHF_LED_in_Cambridge.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         An LED display of Ignignokt which amongst others, were placed in various locations in
         <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">
          Boston
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">
          Massachusetts
         </a>
         and were mistaken for bombs, causing a panic on January 31, 2007 during the
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Boston_bomb_scare" title="2007 Boston bomb scare">
          2007 Boston bomb scare
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Mooninites were subject to National attention during the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Boston_bomb_scare" title="2007 Boston bomb scare">
        2007 Boston bomb scare
       </a>
       on January 31, 2007. During the incident
       <a href="/wiki/Light-emitting_diode" title="Light-emitting diode">
        LED
       </a>
       displays depicting Ignignokt and Err were placed in eleven different cities: Boston, New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, Atlanta, Seattle, Portland, Denver, Austin, San Francisco, and Philadelphia. In Boston, the authorities considered the LEDs suspicious, prompting the closure of major roads and waterways for investigation.
       <a href="/wiki/Turner_Broadcasting_System" title="Turner Broadcasting System">
        Turner Broadcasting System
       </a>
       later admitted placing the LEDs and apologized for the misunderstanding. In spite of the uproar, Berdovsky and Stevens mocked the media and critics in interviews while facing charges for "placing a hoax device and disorderly conduct".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Subsequently, all criminal charges were dropped in exchange for Berdovsky and Stevens apologizing during their court date and accepting a plea bargain which consisted of community service at the Spaulding Rehabilitation Center.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Turner Broadcasting has paid the
       <a href="/wiki/Boston_Police_Department" title="Boston Police Department">
        Boston Police Department
       </a>
       one million dollars to cover the investigation's cost and an additional million for good will.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resign_17-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-resign-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This action was designed to settle criminal and civil claims, while the general manager of Cartoon Network stepped down because of the incident. Of the 10 cities in which the
       <a href="/wiki/Lite-Brite" title="Lite-Brite">
        Lite-Brite
       </a>
       -like LED displays were placed, only Boston saw them as a matter of concern.
      </p>
      <p>
       Willis and Maiellaro responded to the incident by satirizing it in an episode entitled "Boston", the episode was meant to serve as the premiere of the
       <a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force_(season_5)" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force (season 5)">
        fifth season
       </a>
       in 2008 but was pulled by Turner Legal to avoid further controversy surrounding the incident. Subsequently, “Boston” has never been aired on television or formally released to the public in any format legally.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bostonepisodeinterview_19-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Bostonepisodeinterview-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Willis and Maiellaro also worked a stealthily hidden image of Err dressed as
       <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden" title="Osama bin Laden">
        Osama bin Laden
       </a>
       onto the artwork for the
       <i>
        Aqua Teen Hunger Force Volume Five
       </i>
       <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">
        DVD
       </a>
       set.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
