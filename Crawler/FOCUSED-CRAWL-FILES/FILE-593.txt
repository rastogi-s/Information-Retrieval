      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         MoonRise
        </b>
       </i>
       is a robotic mission concept to the south pole of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . It was proposed in 2017 for
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/New_Frontiers_program" title="New Frontiers program">
        New Frontiers program
       </a>
       mission 4, but it was not selected. If funded and launched by another NASA opportunity, it would focus on the giant
       <a href="/wiki/South_Pole%E2%80%93Aitken_basin" title="South Pole–Aitken basin">
        South Pole–Aitken basin
       </a>
       (SPA basin) on the
       <a href="/wiki/Far_side_of_the_Moon" title="Far side of the Moon">
        far side of the Moon
       </a>
       between the Moon's South Pole and Aitken Crater, 16° south of the Moon's equator. This basin measures nearly 2,500 kilometres (1,600 mi) in diameter and 12 kilometres (7.5 mi) in depth. This region is the oldest, deepest, observable
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Impact_basin" title="Impact basin">
        impact basin
       </a>
       on the Moon and provides a window into the deep
       <a href="/wiki/Internal_structure_of_the_Moon" title="Internal structure of the Moon">
        crust of the Moon
       </a>
       and its history as a result. The basin is also among the largest recognized impact structures in the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
        Solar System
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-factsheet_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-factsheet-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        MoonRise
       </i>
       was not selected for the third New Frontiers program mission 3, losing out to the
       <a href="/wiki/OSIRIS-REx" title="OSIRIS-REx">
        OSIRIS-REx
       </a>
       asteroid sample return mission,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfnow20110525_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-sfnow20110525-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and it lost again in the 2017 competition for New Frontiers program mission 4.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibtimes20171220_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ibtimes20171220-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       <i>
        MoonRise
       </i>
       has the following objectives:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-factsheet_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-factsheet-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Determine the impact chronology of the SPA basin
       </li>
       <li>
        Investigate processes associated with the formation of large impact basins
       </li>
       <li>
        Investigate the materials excavated from the deeper crust, and possibly the mantle, of the Moon within the SPA basin
       </li>
       <li>
        Determine rock types, distribution of thorium, and implications for the Moon's thermal evolution
       </li>
       <li>
        Sample and analyze basaltic rock and volcanic glass, which record the composition and chemical evolution of the Moon's far-side mantle beneath the SPA basin
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Future_prospects">
        Future prospects
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MoonRise&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Future prospects">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        MoonRise
       </i>
       received Phase A funding out of the New Frontiers program.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The study was one of three concepts to get
       <span style="white-space: nowrap">
        US$3.3 million
       </span>
       funding in 2010 to further developed the mission for the final selection, which was a
       <span style="white-space: nowrap">
        US$650 million
       </span>
       mission to launch in the late 2010s.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wustl20091231_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-wustl20091231-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The three semi-finalists were
       <i>
        MoonRise
       </i>
       , the OSIRIS-REx sample return mission, and the Venus In Situ Explorer mission.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wustl20091231_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-wustl20091231-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Although
       <i>
        MoonRise
       </i>
       was passed over in favor of OSIRIS-REx in the 2011 selection, a South Pole–Aitken basin sample return mission remains part of the 2013-2022
       <a href="/wiki/Planetary_Science_Decadal_Survey" title="Planetary Science Decadal Survey">
        Planetary Science Decadal Survey
       </a>
       's recommendation for potential New Frontiers missions,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-decadal.survey.2_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-decadal.survey.2-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and NASA's Planetary Science Division has expressed support for the Decadal Survey's recommendations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSD.DS.response_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-PSD.DS.response-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
