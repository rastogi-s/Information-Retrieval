   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Bebhionn (moon)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Bebhionn
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="'b' in 'buy'">
            b
           </span>
           <span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">
            ɛ
           </span>
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'v' in 'vie'">
            v
           </span>
           <span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">
            iː
           </span>
           <span title="'n' in 'nigh'">
            n
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         be-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          VEEN
         </span>
        </i>
       </a>
       ) or
       <b>
        Saturn XXXVII
       </b>
       (37) (provisional designation
       <b>
        S/2004 S 11
       </b>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">
        natural satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">
        Saturn
       </a>
       . Its discovery was announced by
       <a href="/wiki/Scott_S._Sheppard" title="Scott S. Sheppard">
        Scott S. Sheppard
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/David_C._Jewitt" title="David C. Jewitt">
        David C. Jewitt
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jan_Kleyna" title="Jan Kleyna">
        Jan Kleyna
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Brian_G._Marsden" title="Brian G. Marsden">
        Brian G. Marsden
       </a>
       on 4 May 2005 from observations taken between 12 December 2004 and 9 March 2005.
      </p>
      <p>
       Bebhionn is about 6 kilometres in diameter and orbits Saturn at an average distance of 16,898 Mm in 820.130 days at an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">
        inclination
       </a>
       of 41° to the
       <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">
        ecliptic
       </a>
       (18° to Saturn's equator) and with an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eccentricity_(orbit)" title="Eccentricity (orbit)">
        eccentricity
       </a>
       of 0.333. The rotation period was measured by the
       <a class="external text" href="http://ciclops.org/" rel="nofollow">
        ISS camera
       </a>
       of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cassini-Huygens" title="Cassini-Huygens">
        Cassini spacecraft
       </a>
       to ~16 hours.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DenkMottola2011_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-DenkMottola2011-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       It was named in April 2007 after
       <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9binn" title="Bébinn">
        Bébinn
       </a>
       , an early
       <a href="/wiki/Irish_mythology" title="Irish mythology">
        Irish mythology
       </a>
       goddess of birth, who was renowned for her beauty.
      </p>

       <br/>
      </p>
