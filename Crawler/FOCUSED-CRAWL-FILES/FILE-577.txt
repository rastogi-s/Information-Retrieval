      <p>
       <b>
        Cislunar Explorers
       </b>
       is a pair of spacecraft that will show the viability of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_electrolysis" title="Water electrolysis">
        water electrolysis
       </a>
       propulsion and interplanetary optical navigation to orbit the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gunter_CisExpl_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gunter_CisExpl-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Both spacecraft will launch mated together as two L-shaped 3-Unit
       <a href="/wiki/CubeSat" title="CubeSat">
        CubeSats
       </a>
       , which fit together as a 6-Unit CubeSat of about 10×20×30 cm.
      </p>
      <p>
       The technology demonstrator spacecraft pair is being developed at
       <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">
        Cornell University
       </a>
       in New York, by a team of researchers, graduate students, and undergraduates.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wordpress_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Wordpress-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It will be one of thirteen CubeSats to be carried with the
       <a href="/wiki/Exploration_Mission_1" title="Exploration Mission 1">
        Orion EM-1 mission
       </a>
       into a
       <a href="/wiki/Heliocentric_orbit" title="Heliocentric orbit">
        heliocentric orbit
       </a>
       in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cislunar_space" title="Cislunar space">
        cislunar space
       </a>
       on the maiden flight of the
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Launch_System" title="Space Launch System">
        Space Launch System
       </a>
       scheduled to launch in 2019.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Water_powered">
        Water powered
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cislunar_Explorers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Water powered">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The two spacecraft feature an unusual
       <a href="/wiki/Electrolysis_of_water" title="Electrolysis of water">
        water electrolysis
       </a>
       propulsion system that splits the bond between hydrogen and oxygen, producing combustible gaseous mixture that can be used as engine propellant.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spotlight_2017_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Spotlight_2017-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This propulsion system will be used to enter lunar orbit. The mission designers comment that if this water-based propulsion technnology is successful, it may enable
       <i>
        in-situ
       </i>
       resources for refueling landers for commercial or science purposes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spotlight_2017_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Spotlight_2017-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Such a spacecraft could refuel at space-bound water sources, like asteroids, instead of bringing all the needed fuel along with it from Earth.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewin_2016_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lewin_2016-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Optical_navigation">
        Optical navigation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cislunar_Explorers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Optical navigation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Since the purpose of the
       <i>
        Cislunar Explorers
       </i>
       is to test a novel propulsion system, they will simply be injected in "any lunar orbit" and maintain it for as long as possible.
       <i>
        Cislunar Explorers
       </i>
       will navigate completely autonomously, with minimal control from Earth.
       <i>
        Cislunar Explorers
       </i>
       will each use commercial cameras that enable them to view the Earth, the Moon, and the Sun. By computing the sizes of each of these objects and their locations relative to one another, the two spacecraft will deduce their locations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewin_2016_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lewin_2016-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
