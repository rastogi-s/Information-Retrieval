      <p>
       A
       <b>
        total
        <a href="/wiki/Lunar_eclipse" title="Lunar eclipse">
         lunar eclipse
        </a>
       </b>
       took place on May 16, 2003, the first of two total lunar eclipses in 2003, the other being on
       <a href="/wiki/November_2003_lunar_eclipse" title="November 2003 lunar eclipse">
        November 9, 2003
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This lunar eclipse is
       <a href="#Lunar_year_series">
        first of a
        <i>
         tetrad
        </i>
       </a>
       , four total lunar eclipses in series. The previous series was in 1985 and 1986, starting with a
       <a href="/wiki/May_1985_lunar_eclipse" title="May 1985 lunar eclipse">
        May 1985 lunar eclipse
       </a>
       . The next one was in
       <a href="/wiki/Template:Lunar_eclipse_set_2013-2016" title="Template:Lunar eclipse set 2013-2016">
        2014 and 2015
       </a>
       , starting with the April 15, 2014 lunar eclipse.
      </p>

      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Metonic_series">
        Metonic series
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=May_2003_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Metonic series">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       This eclipse is the second of four
       <a href="/wiki/Metonic_cycle" title="Metonic cycle">
        Metonic cycle
       </a>
       lunar eclipses on the same date, May 15–16, each separated by 19 years.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Metonic_cycle" title="Metonic cycle">
        Metonic cycle
       </a>
       repeats nearly exactly every 19 years and represents a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saros_cycle" title="Saros cycle">
        Saros cycle
       </a>
       plus one lunar year. Because it occurs on the same calendar date, the earth's shadow will be in nearly the same location relative to the background stars.
      </p>
