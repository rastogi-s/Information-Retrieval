      <p>
       <b>
        Pettit
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       that lies near the western limb of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . In this location the crater is viewed nearly from the side by observers on
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       , and visibility can be significantly affected by
       <a href="/wiki/Libration" title="Libration">
        libration
       </a>
       effects.
      </p>
      <p>
       The crater lies among the rugged features of the
       <a href="/wiki/Montes_Rook" title="Montes Rook">
        Montes Rook
       </a>
       , the inner ring of mountains that surround the
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Orientale" title="Mare Orientale">
        Mare Orientale
       </a>
       impact basin. It is nearly a twin of the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Nicholson_(lunar_crater)" title="Nicholson (lunar crater)">
        Nicholson
       </a>
       which lies less than one crater diameter to the northeast. Another similar formation is
       <a href="/wiki/Wright_(lunar_crater)" title="Wright (lunar crater)">
        Wright
       </a>
       almost due south.
      </p>
      <p>
       The rim of this crater has something of the appearance of a rounded hexagon, with slightly flattened rims to the east and west. The rim is sharp-edged, and the inner wall material has slumped down to lie in a pile around the base. The interior floor is a rugged jumble, with a small central peak and hills to the north and west. Beyond the rim lies rugged terrain to the north and west, while the ground becomes less jumbled to the southeast beyond the edge of the mountain foothills.
      </p>
      <p>
       This formation should not be mistaken for ths similarly named crater
       <a href="/wiki/Petit_(crater)" title="Petit (crater)">
        Petit
       </a>
       , a small feature on the eastern half of the Moon's near side.
      </p>
