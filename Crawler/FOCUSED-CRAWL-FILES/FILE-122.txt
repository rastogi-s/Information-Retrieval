   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Lunar and Planetary Science Conference
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar and Planetary Science Conference
       </b>
       (LPSC), jointly sponsored by the
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute" title="Lunar and Planetary Institute">
        Lunar and Planetary Institute
       </a>
       (LPI) and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NASA_Johnson_Space_Center" title="NASA Johnson Space Center">
        NASA Johnson Space Center
       </a>
       (JSC), brings together international specialists in
       <a href="/wiki/Petrology" title="Petrology">
        petrology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Geochemistry" title="Geochemistry">
        geochemistry
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Geophysics" title="Geophysics">
        geophysics
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">
        geology
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">
        astronomy
       </a>
       to present the latest results of research in
       <a href="/wiki/Planetary_science" title="Planetary science">
        planetary science
       </a>
       . Since its beginning in 1970, the LPSC has been a significant focal point for planetary science research, with more than 1700 planetary scientists and students attending from all over the world.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Science_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG/200px-Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG/300px-Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG/400px-Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_Sample_Processing_Facility_NASA_JSC_DSCN0199.JPG" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Lunar Samples at the Johnson Space Center
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In a speech delivered at the Manned Spacecraft Center (MSC) in Houston, Texas in March 1968, President
       <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson" title="Lyndon B. Johnson">
        Lyndon B. Johnson
       </a>
       announced the formation of the Lunar Science Institute (LSI).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The creation of the LSI was the culmination of meetings and events involving the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Aeronautics_and_Space_Administration" title="National Aeronautics and Space Administration">
        National Aeronautics and Space Administration
       </a>
       , the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Science" title="National Academy of Science">
        National Academy of Science
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Universities_Research_Association" title="Universities Research Association">
        Universities Research Association
       </a>
       , and several major universities. Initially operated by the National Academy of Sciences, the
       <a href="/wiki/Universities_Space_Research_Association" title="Universities Space Research Association">
        Universities Space Research Association
       </a>
       took over the management of the Lunar Science Institute on December 11, 1969.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A program of visiting university-based scientists was established, the first symposium was organized, and the first lecture of the LSI seminar series was presented. The first science conference, known as the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">
        Apollo 11
       </a>
       Lunar Science Conference, was held in Houston from January 5–8, 1970. During the early days of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollo_Program" title="Apollo Program">
        Apollo Program
       </a>
       , meetings focused on the study of the lunar samples. In 1978, the name of the conference was changed from the Lunar Science Conference to the Lunar and Planetary Science Conference
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       to reflect the expanded scope of research that included planetary science. After four decades, this conference continues to draw planetary scientists and researchers from around the world.
      </p>
      <p>
       The conference has been held every year since 1970. The first three were held in January, and from 1973 on have been held in March. The most recent conference was held March 20–24, 2017, in
       <a href="/wiki/The_Woodlands,_Texas" title="The Woodlands, Texas">
        The Woodlands, Texas
       </a>
       , 31 miles north of
       <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">
        Houston
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
        Texas
       </a>
       . The first eight conferences were titled
       <i>
        Lunar Science Conference
       </i>
       , and the name changed to the current
       <i>
        Lunar and Planetary Science Conference
       </i>
       in 1978.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="LPSC_today">
        LPSC today
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        </span>

       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PIA13655_Mars_Noctis_Vista.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="100" data-file-width="288" height="76" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/PIA13655_Mars_Noctis_Vista.jpg/220px-PIA13655_Mars_Noctis_Vista.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/71/PIA13655_Mars_Noctis_Vista.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/>

        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PIA13655_Mars_Noctis_Vista.jpg" title="Enlarge">

         <br/>
         2001 Mars Odyssey

      <p>
       The schedule for the five-day conference consists of parallel sessions of contributed presentations that emphasize planetary processes and those related to planetary bodies. Contributed abstracts are peer-reviewed, and selections for presentation are based on the overall relevance of the subject matter to the conference and the quality of the science. The conference features five full days of oral and poster sessions covering a variety of topics in planetary science research. A number of special sessions and events are also scheduled. Every year special sessions are held covering a variety of topics, including recent events in planetary science and current planetary missions and their results. Special sessions are scheduled throughout the week of the conference and throughout the event venue.
      </p>
      <p>
       Highlighted events of the conference include a welcome event, a briefing from the Planetary Sciences Division of NASA's
       <a href="/wiki/Science_Mission_Directorate" title="Science Mission Directorate">
        Science Mission Directorate
       </a>
       , plenary sessions, education/public outreach presentations, peripheral team meetings and workshops, and networking opportunities. Special events for
       <a href="/wiki/Women_in_science" title="Women in science">
        women in science
       </a>
       and students are also featured. Every ten years a
       <a href="/wiki/Planetary_Science_Decadal_Survey" title="Planetary Science Decadal Survey">
        Planetary Science Decadal Survey
       </a>
       Briefing is held as part of the conference. This decadal survey "shall broadly canvas the field of space- and ground-based planetary science to determine the current state of knowledge and then identify the most important scientific questions expected to face the community...In addition, the survey and report shall address relevant programmatic and implementation issues of interest to NASA and the National Science Foundation (NSF)."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
