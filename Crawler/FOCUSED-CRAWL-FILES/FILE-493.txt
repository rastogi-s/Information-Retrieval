   </h1>

      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Blue Origin
        <i>
         Blue Moon
        </i>
       </b>
       is a proposed
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_lander" title="Lunar lander">
        lunar lander
       </a>
       designed by
       <a href="/wiki/Blue_Origin" title="Blue Origin">
        Blue Origin
       </a>
       for use for cargo deliveries to the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       .
       <i>
        Blue Moon
       </i>
       is an unmanned lunar
       <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">
        spacecraft
       </a>
       that is projected to provide an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">
        Amazon.com
       </a>
       -like delivery service to the Moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INverse-2017-04-07_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-INverse-2017-04-07-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WashPost-2017-03-02_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-WashPost-2017-03-02-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Blue Moon derives from the vertical landing technology used in Blue Origin's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Origin_New_Shepard" title="Blue Origin New Shepard">
        New Shepard
       </a>
       sub-orbital space tourist rocket.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MotleyFool-2017-03-06_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-MotleyFool-2017-03-06-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The lander platform was first revealed in March 2017.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopMech-2017-03-06_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-PopMech-2017-03-06-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AWST-2017-03-03_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-AWST-2017-03-03-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The proposed lander will be optimized to use NASA's
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Launch_System" title="Space Launch System">
        Space Launch System
       </a>
       (SLS) rocket, but also usable with Blue Origin's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Origin_New_Glenn" title="Blue Origin New Glenn">
        New Glenn
       </a>
       and ULA's
       <a href="/wiki/Atlas_V" title="Atlas V">
        Atlas V
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SN-2017-04-06_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SN-2017-04-06-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and upcoming
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vulcan_rocket" title="Vulcan rocket">
        Vulcan rocket
       </a>
       . The lander would be capable of delivering 10,000 lb (4,500 kg) to the surface of the Moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AWST-2017-03-03_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-AWST-2017-03-03-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is anticipated that the platform will be used to support NASA activities in cis-lunar space.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SN-2017-04-06_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-SN-2017-04-06-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The lander is also projected to be able to return with payloads of ice from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shackleton_Crater" title="Shackleton Crater">
        Shackleton Crater
       </a>
       to support space activities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SpaceflightInsider-2017-04-12_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SpaceflightInsider-2017-04-12-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The first projected mission for the craft would be a
       <a href="/wiki/2020_Blue_Origin_lunar_south_polar_landing_mission" title="2020 Blue Origin lunar south polar landing mission">
        2020 lunar south polar landing mission
       </a>
       . It is projected that a series of landings would be used to build up materiel for the foundation of a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moon_colony" title="Moon colony">
        colony on the Moon
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INverse-2017-04-07_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-INverse-2017-04-07-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WashPost-2017-03-02_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-WashPost-2017-03-02-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This may be used to support a Blue Origin polar lunar colony.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GW-2017-05-20_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GW-2017-05-20-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
