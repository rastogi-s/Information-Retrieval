     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Thai lunar calendar
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
        Thai
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        ปฏิทินจันทรคติ
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        <i>
         patithin chanthrakhati
        </i>
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        pronounced
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [pà.tì.tʰīn tɕān.tʰrá.kʰā.tìʔ]
        </a>
       </span>
       , literally,
       <i>
        Specific days according to lunar norms
       </i>
       ), or
       <b>
        <a href="/wiki/Tai_peoples" title="Tai peoples">
         Tai
        </a>
        calendar
       </b>
       , is a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunisolar_calendar" title="Lunisolar calendar">
        lunisolar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_calendar" title="Buddhist calendar">
        Buddhist calendar
       </a>
       . It is used for calculating lunar-regulated holy days. Based on the
       <i>
        SuriyaYatra
       </i>
       , with likely influence from the traditional Hindu
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Surya_Siddhanta" title="Surya Siddhanta">
         Surya Siddhanta
        </a>
       </i>
       , it has its own unique structure that does not require the Surya Siddhanta to calculate. Lunisolar calendars combine
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_calendar" title="Lunar calendar">
        lunar
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_calendar" title="Solar calendar">
        solar calendars
       </a>
       for a nominal
       <a href="/wiki/Year" title="Year">
        year
       </a>
       of 12
       <a href="/wiki/Month" title="Month">
        months
       </a>
       . An extra day or an extra 30-day month is
       <a href="/wiki/Intercalation_(timekeeping)" title="Intercalation (timekeeping)">
        intercalated
       </a>
       at regular intervals.
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_v._religious_calendar">
        Legal v. religious calendar
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Legal v. religious calendar">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_solar_calendar" title="Thai solar calendar">
        Thai solar calendar
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
        Thai
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        ปฏิทินสุริยคติ
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        patithin suriyakhati
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [pà.tì.tʰin sù.rī.já.kʰā.tìʔ]
        </a>
       </span>
       ), Thailand's version of the
       <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">
        Gregorian calendar
       </a>
       , replaced the
       <i>
        patithin chanthrakhati
       </i>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era#Dionysian_.22Common_Era.22" title="Calendar era">
        AD
       </a>
       1888 / 2431
       <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era#Religious" title="Calendar era">
        BE
       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Civil_law_(legal_system)" title="Civil law (legal system)">
        legal
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">
        commercial
       </a>
       purposes. In both calendars, the four principal
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_phase" title="Lunar phase">
        lunar phases
       </a>
       determine
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddhist" title="Buddhist">
        Buddhist
       </a>
       Sabbaths (
       <a href="/wiki/Uposatha" title="Uposatha">
        Uposatha
       </a>
       ), obligatory holy days for observant Buddhists. Significant days also include feast days.
      </p>
      <p>
       Note that the Thai and the Chinese lunar calendars do not directly correspond.
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_Chinese" title="Thai Chinese">
        Thai Chinese
       </a>
       likewise observe their Sabbaths and
       <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_holidays" title="Traditional Chinese holidays">
        traditional Chinese holidays
       </a>
       according to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_terms" title="Solar terms">
        solar terms
       </a>
       , two of which correspond to one lunar phase. These also move with respect to the solar calendar, and so it is common for Thai calendars to incorporate both Thai and
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_calendar" title="Chinese calendar">
        Chinese
       </a>
       lunar calendar-based events.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mundane_astrology" title="Mundane astrology">
        Mundane astrology
       </a>
       also figures prominently in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thai_culture" title="Thai culture">
        Thai culture
       </a>
       , so modern Thai birth certificates include lunar calendar dates and the appropriate Thai Zodiacal animal year-name for Thai
       <a href="/wiki/Hora_(astrology)" title="Hora (astrology)">
        Hora
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
        Thai
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        โหราศาสตร์
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        horasat
       </span>
       ). The Thai Zodiac is similar to the Chinese, though the
       <a href="/wiki/Dragon_(zodiac)" title="Dragon (zodiac)">
        Dragon
       </a>
       is replaced by the Naga or large snake (งูใหญ่), and in Northern Thailand the
       <a href="/wiki/Pig_(zodiac)" title="Pig (zodiac)">
        Pig
       </a>
       is occasionally replaced with an Elephant.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Years">
        Years
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Years">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Further information:
       <a href="/wiki/Chula_Sakarat" title="Chula Sakarat">
        Chula Sakarat
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_phase" title="Lunar phase">
        Lunar phase
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_term" title="Solar term">
        Solar term
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       To keep the years in sync with the seasons, Thai lunar years may add a day to the 7th month or repeat the 8th month. Therefore, years may have one of three lengths – 354, 355 or 384 days – yet retain a
       <a href="/wiki/Real_versus_nominal_value" title="Real versus nominal value">
        nominal
       </a>
       length of twelve months.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The 354-day-long years consist of 12 "normal months", and such a year is called a "normal-month year" (
        <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
         Thai
        </a>
        :
        <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
         (ปี) ปกติมาส
        </span>
        ,
        <small>
         <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
          RTGS
         </a>
         :
        </small>
        <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
         (pi) pakatimat
        </span>
        ,
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
          [(pī) pà.kà.ti.mâːt]
         </a>
        </span>
        ).
       </li>
       <li>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="New_year">
        New year
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New year">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Thai lunar calendar does not mark the beginning of a new year when it starts a new 1-to-12 count, which occurs most frequently in December.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:August2004_1-2.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="293" data-file-width="630" height="102" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/August2004_1-2.jpg/220px-August2004_1-2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/August2004_1-2.jpg/330px-August2004_1-2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/August2004_1-2.jpg/440px-August2004_1-2.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:August2004_1-2.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         August 1 and 2, 2004. Sunday, a holiday, on the left, and Monday, observed as the compensatory day, on the right
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Thai solar calendar determines a
       <a href="/wiki/Person" title="Person">
        person
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Age_of_majority" title="Age of majority">
        legal age
       </a>
       and the dates of
       <a href="/wiki/Secularity" title="Secularity">
        secular
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Holiday" title="Holiday">
        holidays
       </a>
       , including the civil
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_year" title="New year">
        new year
       </a>
       and the three days of the
       <a href="/wiki/Songkran_(Thailand)" title="Songkran (Thailand)">
        traditional Thai New Year
       </a>
       , which begin the next Twelve-year animal cycle. Should the holidays fall on a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Workweek" title="Workweek">
        weekend
       </a>
       , it also accommodates these as well as some of the principal lunar festivals with a compensatory day off (
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
        Thai
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        วันชดเชย
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        wan chotchoei
       </span>
       ).
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Months">
        Months
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Months">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the modern Thai calendar, months (
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
        Thai
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        เดือน
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        duean
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [dɯ̄an]
        </a>
       </span>
       , meaning "month" or "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunation" title="Lunation">
        Lunation
       </a>
       ") are defined by lunar cycles. Successive months (or lunations) are numbered from 1 to 12 within the Thai year. As in other
       <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_calendar" title="Buddhist calendar">
        Buddhist calendars
       </a>
       , these months have names that derive from
       <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_calendar#Names_of_the_months" title="Buddhist calendar">
        Sanskrit
       </a>
       , but for the most part are only known by Thai astrologers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diller_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Diller-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Two successive lunations take slightly more than 59 days. The Thai lunar calendar approximates this interval with "normal-month" pairs (
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        ปกติมาส
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        pakatimat
       </span>
       ) that are alternately 29 and 30 days long. 29-day "
       <a href="/wiki/Month#Calendrical_consequences" title="Month">
        hollow months
       </a>
       " (
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        เดือนขาด
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        duean khat
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [dɯ̄an kʰàːt]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) are odd-numbered (
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        เดือนคี่
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        duean khi
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [dɯːan.kʰî]
        </a>
       </span>
       ); 30-day "full months" (
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        เดือนถ้วน
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        duean thuan
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [dɯ̄an tʰûan]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) are even-numbered (
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        เดือนคู่
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
         RTGS
        </a>
        :
       </small>
       <span lang="th-Latn" style="font-style:inherit" title="Thai-language transliteration" xml:lang="th-Latn">
        duean khu
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [dɯ̄an kʰûː]
        </a>
       </span>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       To keep the beginning of the month in sync with the new moon, from time to time either the normally "hollow" Month 7 takes an extra day, or an extra "full" Month 8 follows a normal "full" Month 8.
      </p>
      <p>
       Months 1 and 2 are named in
       <a href="/wiki/Archaism" title="Archaism">
        archaic
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_numerals#Alternate_numbers" title="Thai numerals">
        alternate numbers
       </a>
       , with the remainder being named in modern numbers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diller_quote_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Diller_quote-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Months_1_.E2.80.93_6">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Months_1_–_6">
        Months 1 – 6
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Months 1 – 6">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Month 1, "duean ai" (
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        เดือนอ้าย
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [dɯ̄an ʔâːj]
        </a>
       </span>
       ), begins the cycle of counting the months anew, most frequently in December, but does not signify the beginning of a new year.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diller_quote_2_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Diller_quote_2-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <b>
        ai
       </b>
       , an
       <a href="/wiki/Archaism" title="Archaism">
        archaic
       </a>
       word in Thai but not in other dialects, means
       <i>
        first
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ORID_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ORID-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       An odd-numbered hollow month, it is 29 days long.
      </p>
      <p>
       Month 2, "duean yi", (
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        เดือนยี่
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [dɯ̄an jîː]
        </a>
       </span>
       , from archaic ญี่ meaning
       <i>
        2
       </i>
       )
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ORID_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ORID-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is an even-numbered full month.
      </p>
      <p>
       Months 3–6, "duean 3–6", use the modern reading of
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_numerals" title="Thai numerals">
        Thai numerals
       </a>
       , as do all remaining months. Months 3–6, alternate between 29-day hollow months and 30-day full months.
      </p>

       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        อธิกวาร
       </span>
       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">

        </a>

       </small>

       </span>

       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">

       </span>

       <small>
        <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">

      </p>

      </p>

       Months 9–12, "duean 9–12", complete the lunar cycle.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Month_divisions">
        Month divisions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Month divisions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Months divide into two periods designated by whether they are
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_phase#Names_of_lunar_phases" title="Lunar phase">
        waxing or waning
       </a>
       :
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Waxing :
        <i>
         khang khuen
        </i>
        (
        <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
         ข้างขึ้น
        </span>
        ), the period from new moon to full moon, is always 15 days long.
       </li>
       <li>
        Waning :
        <i>
         khang raem
        </i>
        (
        <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
         ข้างแรม
        </span>
        ), the period from full moon to new moon, which is 14 days long in hollow months, except when Month 7 adds an extra day, and 15 days long in full months.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Weeks">
        Weeks
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Weeks">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A
       <a href="/wiki/Week" title="Week">
        week
       </a>
       is called
       <i>
        Sapda/Sappada
       </i>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">
        Thai
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        สัปดาห์
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai_and_Lao" title="Help:IPA/Thai and Lao">
         [sàp.dāː, sàp.pà.dāː]
        </a>
       </span>
       ). The term is defined by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_Thailand#The_Royal_Institute_Dictionary" title="Royal Institute of Thailand">
        Royal Institute Dictionary
       </a>
       (RID) as a 7-day period beginning on Sunday and ending Saturday.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       When referring to lunations, however, it is the 7-, 8- or (rarely) 9-day interval between
       <a href="/wiki/Quartile" title="Quartile">
        quartile
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_phase" title="Lunar phase">
        lunar phases
       </a>
       ; that is, from one
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        วันพระ
       </span>
       to the next.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Days">
        Days
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Days">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       While
       <a href="/wiki/Thai_solar_calendar#Weekdays" title="Thai solar calendar">
        solar-calendar weekdays
       </a>
       have names, lunar-calendar days number sequentially from 1 to 14 or 15 in two segments depending on whether the moon is waxing or waning. For example, "raem 15 kham duean 12
       <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
        แรม ๑๕ ค่ำ เดือน ๑๒
       </span>
       " means "Waning of the 15th Night of the 12th Lunar Month".
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Kham
        <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">
         ค่ำ
        </span>
       </i>
       , evening, is considered to be the evening of the common day that begins and ends at midnight, rather than of a day that begins and ends at dusk. Past practice may have been different.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Thai_year_vocabulary">
        Thai year vocabulary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thai_lunar_calendar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Thai year vocabulary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Thai orthography spells most native words phonetically, though there is no definitive system for
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_General_System_of_Transcription" title="Royal Thai General System of Transcription">
        transcription
       </a>
       into Roman letters. Here, native Thai words are immediately followed by a vocabulary entry in this pattern:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         Phonetic
        </i>
        Thai (Thai phonetic respelling, if different) [Comment] definition; variant definitions.
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       Example:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         Thai
        </i>
        ไทย (ไท) [Archaic] free, frank; Thai race, language,
        <a href="/wiki/Thai_alphabet" title="Thai alphabet">
         alphabet
        </a>
        ;
        <a href="/wiki/Thaification" title="Thaification">
         citizen of Thailand
        </a>
        .
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">
        Sanskrit
       </a>
       loan words follow different rules [the way English grammatical rules vary for words of Greek and Latin origin ('ph-' in 'phonetic' being pronounced /f/, for example.)] Entered below in order of first appearance, these vocabulary entries are in this pattern:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Sanskrit
        </i>
        สันสกฤต (สันสะกฺริด /san-sa-krit/)
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <p>
       Literally means "self-made" or "self-done", or "cultured" in a modern usage (which implies the language of cultured persons); Sanskrit alphabet,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanskrit_language" title="Sanskrit language">
        language
       </a>
       , writing; [presumed] compound of
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         san
        </i>
        สัน (-/son/) derived from the word, "saṃ" meaning "self, together, with"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         skrit
        </i>
        สกฤต (สะกฺริต /sa-krit/) derived from the word "(s)kar" meaning "do or make".
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Chanthrakhati
        </i>
        จันทรคติ (จันทฺระคะติ)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        "Lunar norms", Lunar Calendar; compound of
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Chanthra-
        </i>
        จันทร- (จันทฺระ) :
        <i>
         Chan
        </i>
        จันทร์ (จัน) moon, lunar +
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Kati
        </i>
        คติ (คะติ) : ways, principles, norms
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Patithin
        </i>
        ปฏิทิน (ปะติทิน)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        Calendar; compound of
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Pati-
        </i>
        ปฏิ- (ปะติ-) : anti-, re-, for, specific +
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         -thin
        </i>
        (-ทิน) : [from Sanskrit
        <i>
         dina
        </i>
        ] : day.
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         patithin
        </i>
        means for days, specific days or fixed days
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Patitin Chanthakhati
        </i>
        ปฏิทินจันทรคติ (ปะติทินจันทระคะติ)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        "Specific days according to lunar norms", Lunar Calendar
       </dd>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Suriyakhati
        </i>
        สุริยคติ (สุริยะคะติ)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        <i>
         Solar norms
        </i>
        , Solar Calendar; compound of
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Suriya
        </i>
        สุริย or สุริยะ :
        <i>
         Athit
        </i>
        อาทิตย์, the sun, Sol +
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Khati
        </i>
        คติ (คะติ) : ways, principles, norms
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Prokkatimat
        </i>
        ปรกติมาส (ปฺรกกะติมาด)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        normal month; compound of
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Prokkati
        </i>
        ปรกติ (ปฺรกกะติ) :
        <i>
         pakati
        </i>
        ปกติ (ปะกะติ) ordinary, usual, normal +
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Mat
        </i>
        มาส (มาด) :
        <i>
         duean
        </i>
        (เดือน) month.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Athikamat
        </i>
        อธิกมาส (อะทิกะมาด)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        month added in leap-month lunar years
       </dd>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Athikawan
        </i>
        อธิกวาร (อะทิกะวาน)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        day added in leap-day lunar years; compound of
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Athika
        </i>
        (Sanskrit:
        <i>
         adhika
        </i>
        ) : additional +
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         -wan
        </i>
        วาร (Sanskrit:
        <i>
         vāra
        </i>
        ) :
        <i>
         wan
        </i>
        วัน day.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         Athikasurathin
        </i>
        อธิกสุรทิน (อะทิกะสุระทิน)
       </dt>
       <dd>
        day added to February in a solar leap year.
       </dd>
      </dl>
