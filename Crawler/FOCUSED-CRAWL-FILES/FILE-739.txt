      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Blue Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is the second book in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Immortals_(books)" title="The Immortals (books)">
        The Immortals
       </a>
       series by author
       <a href="/wiki/Alyson_No%C3%ABl" title="Alyson Noël">
        Alyson Noël
       </a>
       released in July 2009.
       <i>
        Blue Moon
       </i>
       had spent 12 weeks on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times_Bestsellers" title="New York Times Bestsellers">
        New York Times Bestsellers
       </a>
       list for children's books as of October 11, 2009.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">
        Summary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Moon_(No%C3%ABl_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Just as Ever is learning everything she can about her new abilities as an immortal, initiated into the dark, seductive world by her beloved Damen, something terrible is happening to him. As Ever’s powers are increasing, Damen’s are fading—stricken by a mysterious illness that threatens his memory, his identity, his life.
      </p>
      <p>
       Desperate to save him, Ever travels to the mystical dimension of Summerland, uncovering not only the secrets of Damen’s past—the brutal, tortured history he hoped to keep hidden—but also an ancient text revealing the workings of time. With the approaching blue moon heralding her only window for travel, Ever is forced to decide between turning back the clock and saving her family from the accident that claimed them—or staying in the present and saving Damen, who grows weaker each day...
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
