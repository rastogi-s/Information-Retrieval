      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         A Man on the Moon: The Voyages of the Apollo Astronauts
        </b>
       </i>
       is a book by
       <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Chaikin" title="Andrew Chaikin">
        Andrew Chaikin
       </a>
       , first published in 1994. It describes the voyages of the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo program
       </a>
       astronauts in detail, from Apollo 8 to 17.
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         “A decade in the making, this book is based on hundreds of hours of in-depth interviews with each of the twenty-four moon voyagers, as well as those who contributed their brain power, training and teamwork on Earth."
         <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.andrewchaikin.com/books/a-man-on-the-moon/" rel="nofollow">
          [1]
         </a>
        </i>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       This book formed the basis of the television miniseries
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon_(TV_miniseries)" title="From the Earth to the Moon (TV miniseries)">
         From the Earth to the Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       . It was released in paperback in 2007 by
       <a href="/wiki/Penguin_Books" title="Penguin Books">
        Penguin Books
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">
        ISBN
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/978-0-14-311235-8" title="Special:BookSources/978-0-14-311235-8">
        978-0-14-311235-8
       </a>
       .
      </p>
