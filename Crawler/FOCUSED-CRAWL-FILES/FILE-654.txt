      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         From the Earth to the Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is a 12-part 1998
       <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">
        HBO
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">
        television miniseries
       </a>
       co-produced by
       <a href="/wiki/Ron_Howard" title="Ron Howard">
        Ron Howard
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Brian_Grazer" title="Brian Grazer">
        Brian Grazer
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">
        Tom Hanks
       </a>
       , and Michael Bostick, telling the story of the landmark
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
         Apollo
        </a>
       </i>
       expeditions to the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       during the 1960s and early 1970s in
       <a href="/wiki/Docudrama" title="Docudrama">
        docudrama
       </a>
       format. Largely based on
       <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Chaikin" title="Andrew Chaikin">
        Andrew Chaikin
       </a>
       's book,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/A_Man_on_the_Moon" title="A Man on the Moon">
         A Man on the Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       , the series is known for its accurate telling of the story of Apollo and the outstanding
       <a href="/wiki/Special_effect" title="Special effect">
        special effects
       </a>
       under visual director Ernest D. Farino.
      </p>
      <p>
       The series takes its title from, but is not based upon, the famous
       <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne" title="Jules Verne">
        Jules Verne
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">
        science fiction
       </a>
       novel
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon" title="From the Earth to the Moon">
         From the Earth to the Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       . Hanks appears in every episode, introducing each of the first eleven. The last episode is represented in a pseudo-
       <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">
        documentary
       </a>
       format narrated by
       <a href="/wiki/Blythe_Danner" title="Blythe Danner">
        Blythe Danner
       </a>
       , interspersed with a reenactment of the making of
       <a href="/wiki/Georges_M%C3%A9li%C3%A8s" title="Georges Méliès">
        Georges Méliès
       </a>
       ' film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/A_Trip_to_the_Moon" title="A Trip to the Moon">
         Le Voyage dans la Lune
        </a>
       </i>
       . Hanks narrates and appears in these scenes as Méliès' assistant.
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">
        Cast
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon_(miniseries)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cast">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon_characters" title="List of From the Earth to the Moon characters">
        List of From the Earth to the Moon characters
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The miniseries has a fairly large cast, driven in part by the fact that it portrays 30 of the
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Apollo_astronauts" title="List of Apollo astronauts">
        32 astronauts who flew, or were preparing to fly, the twelve missions
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo program
       </a>
       . (The only two Apollo astronauts not portrayed by credited actors are
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_13" title="Apollo 13">
        Apollo 13
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Command/Service_Module" title="Apollo Command/Service Module">
        Command Module
       </a>
       pilot
       <a href="/wiki/Jack_Swigert" title="Jack Swigert">
        Jack Swigert
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17" title="Apollo 17">
        Apollo 17
       </a>
       Command Module pilot
       <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Evans_(astronaut)" title="Ronald Evans (astronaut)">
        Ronald Evans
       </a>
       , who had a brief appearance in the liftoff scene of
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17" title="Apollo 17">
        Apollo 17
       </a>
       in the final episode.) Members of many of the astronauts' families, and other
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       and non-NASA personnel, are also portrayed.
      </p>
      <p>
       Several fictional (or fictionalized) characters are also included, notably television newscaster Emmett Seaborn (
       <a href="/wiki/Lane_Smith" title="Lane Smith">
        Lane Smith
       </a>
       ) who appears in nine of the twelve episodes.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes">
        Episodes
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon_(miniseries)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Episodes">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The twelve episodes, each directed by different individuals, use a variety of viewpoints and themes, while sequentially covering the Mercury, Gemini and Apollo programs.
       <a href="/wiki/Lane_Smith" title="Lane Smith">
        Lane Smith
       </a>
       portrays Emmett Seaborn, a seasoned reporter for a fictional television network, who covers the U.S. space program from its earliest days, providing continuity for most of the episodes.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Integration_with_existing_films">
        Integration with existing films
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon_(miniseries)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Integration with existing films">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The miniseries, concentrating on the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo
       </a>
       space program, was produced with an intent not to repeat other dramatic portrayals of events of the space race.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Project_Mercury" title="Project Mercury">
        Project Mercury
       </a>
       , which was portrayed in the film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Right_Stuff_(film)" title="The Right Stuff (film)">
         The Right Stuff
        </a>
       </i>
       , was briefly summarized in the first episode. Miniseries producers Hanks, Howard and Grazer, who had previously produced
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Apollo_13_(film)" title="Apollo 13 (film)">
         Apollo 13
        </a>
       </i>
       , shot the episode "We Interrupt This Program" from the perspective of the media covering that flight, as the film had already covered the story from the point of view of the crew and the mission control team.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Production_information">
        Production information
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon_(miniseries)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production information">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Many of the actors had opportunity to interact and form friendships with the real life astronauts they were portraying.
       <a href="/wiki/Brett_Cullen" title="Brett Cullen">
        Brett Cullen
       </a>
       , who played
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_9" title="Apollo 9">
        Apollo 9
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Command/Service_Module#Command_Module_(CM)" title="Apollo Command/Service Module">
        Command Module
       </a>
       pilot and
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_15" title="Apollo 15">
        Apollo 15
       </a>
       commander
       <a href="/wiki/David_Scott" title="David Scott">
        David Scott
       </a>
       , was invited to the Scott family home each time an episode he appeared in was first televised. Two short clips from the final scenes of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Apollo_13_(film)" title="Apollo 13 (film)">
         Apollo 13
        </a>
       </i>
       were used in "That's All There Is"; a splashdown sequence, and a view of the recovery ship
       <a href="/wiki/USS_Iwo_Jima_(LPH-2)" title="USS Iwo Jima (LPH-2)">
        USS
        <i>
         Iwo Jima
        </i>
       </a>
       (portrayed by
       <a href="/wiki/USS_New_Orleans_(LPH-11)" title="USS New Orleans (LPH-11)">
        USS
        <i>
         New Orleans
        </i>
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       The original series was shot in 1.33:1 aspect ratio, intended to be viewed on standard television sets. The series was released on DVD as a 4-disc set. With the proliferation of widescreen flat-panel TV sets the series was remastered in 1.78:1 aspect ratio and re-released in 2005 as a 5-disc DVD box set. New framing causes loss of top and bottom parts of the frames from the original movie. This is not always noticeable because of careful transfer process, but in some scenes important details are lost. For example, in Disc 1, when the Gemini 8 / Agena assembly is tumbling around the sky with a stuck thruster, the thruster is not visible in the new widescreen version as it is cut off by the top of the frame. Some captions have also been compromised.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Parts of the mini-series were filmed at the Disney-MGM Studios (now
       <a href="/wiki/Disney%27s_Hollywood_Studios" title="Disney's Hollywood Studios">
        Disney's Hollywood Studios
       </a>
       ) in Orlando, Florida. Scenes of the moonwalks were shot inside the blimp hangars on the former Marine base in Tustin, California. Approximately half the area inside was converted to the Moon's surface, with the remainder used to hold production trailers. To simulate lunar
       <a href="/wiki/Surface_gravity" title="Surface gravity">
        surface gravity
       </a>
       , weather balloons filled with helium were attached to the backs of the actors playing the astronauts in the lunar
       <a href="/wiki/Extravehicular_activity" title="Extravehicular activity">
        extravehicular activity
       </a>
       scenes, effectively reducing their Earth-bound weights to one-sixth.
      </p>
      <p>
       The score of "Spider" prominently features an imitation of the main title theme from the 1963
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        World War II
       </a>
       movie
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Great_Escape_(film)" title="The Great Escape (film)">
         The Great Escape
        </a>
       </i>
       , and Tom Kelly jokes about having a crew digging a tunnel out of the Grumman plant. The episode also featured a real
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Lunar_Module" title="Apollo Lunar Module">
        Apollo Lunar Module
       </a>
       (LM-13), which had been built for the Apollo 18 mission but was never used due to budget cuts.
       <a href="/wiki/Blythe_Danner" title="Blythe Danner">
        Blythe Danner
       </a>
       , who narrated the final episode, had previously worked on location at the
       <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Space_Center" title="Johnson Space Center">
        Johnson Space Center
       </a>
       for the 1976 movie
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Futureworld" title="Futureworld">
         Futureworld
        </a>
       </i>
       , filmed in the same buildings where Apollo moonwalkers had recently trained.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">
        Awards
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=From_the_Earth_to_the_Moon_(miniseries)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The series won 3 Emmy awards for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Miniseries" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Miniseries">
        Outstanding Miniseries
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Casting_for_a_Miniseries,_Movie,_or_a_Special" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Casting for a Miniseries, Movie, or a Special">
        Outstanding Casting for a Miniseries or a Movie
       </a>
       and Outstanding Hairstyling for a Miniseries, Movie or a Special. In addition, the series won a 1999
       <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">
        Golden Globe Award
       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Miniseries or Television Film">
        Best Mini-Series or Motion Picture Made for TV
       </a>
       .
      </p>
