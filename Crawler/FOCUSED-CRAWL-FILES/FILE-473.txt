   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Lunar limb
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        lunar limb
       </b>
       is the edge of the visible surface (disc) of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       as viewed from
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Libration" title="Libration">
        Libration
       </a>
       of the Moon, with its irregular surface, leads to small changes in its profile; this complicates the task of precisely calculating
       <a href="/wiki/Eclipse" title="Eclipse">
        eclipse
       </a>
       times and durations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, data from the mapping of the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Geology_if_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Geology if the Moon (page does not exist)">
        lunar surface
       </a>
       allows
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">
        astronomers
       </a>
       to predict the lunar profile for any given time with a high degree of certainty.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The irregularity of the lunar limb is the cause of
       <a href="/wiki/Baily%27s_beads" title="Baily's beads">
        Baily's beads
       </a>
       ‍—‌
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collimation" title="Collimation">
        collimated
       </a>
       rays of
       <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">
        sunlight
       </a>
       that shine through in some places, and not in others, during a
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">
        solar eclipse
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The contrast of a brightly illuminated limb against a black sky makes it a popular target when testing
       <a href="/wiki/Telescope" title="Telescope">
        telescope
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Binoculars" title="Binoculars">
        binocular
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Optics" title="Optics">
        optics
       </a>
       .
      </p>
