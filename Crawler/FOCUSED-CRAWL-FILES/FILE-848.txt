      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        SSERVI
       </b>
       ), originally the
       <b>
        NASA Lunar Science Institute
       </b>
       , is an organization, established by
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       in 2008, that supplemented and extended existing NASA
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_science" title="Lunar science">
        lunar science
       </a>
       programs. Supported by the NASA
       <a href="/wiki/Science_Mission_Directorate" title="Science Mission Directorate">
        Science Mission Directorate
       </a>
       (SMD) and the Exploration Systems Mission Directorate (ESMD), the NLSI was managed by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NASA_Ames_Research_Center" title="NASA Ames Research Center">
        NASA Ames Research Center
       </a>
       and was modeled on the
       <a href="/wiki/NASA_Astrobiology_Institute" title="NASA Astrobiology Institute">
        NASA Astrobiology Institute
       </a>
       (NAI) with dispersed teams across the nation working together to help lead the agency's research activities related to NASA's lunar exploration goals. Competitively selected team investigations focused on one or more aspects of lunar science investigations of the Moon (including
       <a href="/wiki/Moon_rock" title="Moon rock">
        lunar samples
       </a>
       ), from the Moon, and on the Moon.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2013 the LSI became the Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute and expanded its scope to include
       <a href="/wiki/Near-Earth_object" title="Near-Earth object">
        Near-Earth asteroids
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Phobos_(moon)" title="Phobos (moon)">
        Phobos
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Deimos_(moon)" title="Deimos (moon)">
        Deimos
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Teams">
        Teams
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Solar_System_Exploration_Research_Virtual_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Teams">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Lunar Science Institute is composed of seven teams across the nation, each with a different focus for lunar science. The teams are listed below.
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        The Moon as the Cornerstone to the Terrestrial Planets: The Formative Years
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       PI:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carle_Pieters" title="Carle Pieters">
        Carle Pieters
       </a>
       , Brown University
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Scientific and Exploration Potential of the Lunar Poles
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       PI:
       <a href="/wiki/Ben_Bussey" title="Ben Bussey">
        Ben Bussey
       </a>
       , APL / JHU
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Impact Processes in the Origin and Evolution of the Moon: New Sample-Driven Perspectives
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       PI: David Kring, LPI
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Dynamic Response of the Environment at the Moon (DREAM)
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       PI: Bill Farrell, NASA Goddard
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Center for Lunar Origin and Evolution (CLOE)
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       PI: Bill Bottke, SwRI
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Lunar University Node for Astrophysics Research (LUNAR)
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       PI: Jack Burns, Univ. of Colorado / CASA
      </p>
      <p>
       <b>
        Colorado Center for Lunar Dust and Atmospheric Studies (CCLDAS)
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       PI: Mihaly Horanyi, Univ. of Colorado / LASP
      </p>
