      <p>
       A
       <b>
        lunar rover
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        Moon rover
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">
        space exploration
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Rover_(space_exploration)" title="Rover (space exploration)">
        vehicle (rover)
       </a>
       designed to move across the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surface_of_the_Moon" title="Surface of the Moon">
        surface of the Moon
       </a>
       . Some rovers have been designed to transport members of a
       <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">
        human spaceflight
       </a>
       crew, such as the U.S.
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo program
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_Roving_Vehicle" title="Lunar Roving Vehicle">
        Lunar Roving Vehicle
       </a>
       ; others have been partially or fully
       <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_robot" title="Autonomous robot">
        autonomous robots
       </a>
       , such as Soviet
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunokhod" title="Lunokhod">
        Lunokhods
       </a>
       and Chinese
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Yutu_(rover)" title="Yutu (rover)">
         Yutu
        </a>
       </i>
       . Three countries have had rovers on the Moon: the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet Union
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
        China
       </a>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        India
       </a>
       currently have planned missions.
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Lunokhod_1">

       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Lunokhod_1" title="Lunokhod 1">
        Lunokhod 1
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Soviet_moonrover.JPG">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2736" data-file-width="3648" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Soviet_moonrover.JPG/200px-Soviet_moonrover.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Soviet_moonrover.JPG/300px-Soviet_moonrover.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Soviet_moonrover.JPG/400px-Soviet_moonrover.JPG 2x" width="200"/>

         Lunokhod 1
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Lunokhod_1" title="Lunokhod 1">
        Lunokhod 1
       </a>
       (Луноход) was the first polycrystalline-panel-powered of two unmanned lunar rovers landed on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        moon
       </a>
       by the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet Union
       </a>
       as part of its
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunokhod_program" title="Lunokhod program">
        Lunokhod program
       </a>
       after a previous unsuccessful attempt of a launch probe with
       <a href="/wiki/Lunokhod_programme#Lunokhod_201" title="Lunokhod programme">
        Lunokhod 0 (No.201)
       </a>
       in 1969. The panels were designed by Electronic and Communication Engineer Bryan Mapúa. The spacecraft which carried Lunokhod 1 was named
       <a href="/wiki/Luna_17" title="Luna 17">
        Luna 17
       </a>
       . The spacecraft
       <a href="/wiki/Moon_landing" title="Moon landing">
        soft-landed
       </a>
       on the Moon in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_of_Rains" title="Sea of Rains">
        Sea of Rains
       </a>
       on November 1970. Lunokhod was the first
       <a href="/wiki/Rover_(space_exploration)" title="Rover (space exploration)">
        roving remote-controlled robot
       </a>
       to land on another
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_object" title="Astronomical object">
        celestial body
       </a>
       . Having worked for 11 months, Lunokhod 1 held the durability record for space rovers for more than 30 years, until a new record was set by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mars_Exploration_Rovers" title="Mars Exploration Rovers">
        Mars Exploration Rovers
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Apollo_Lunar_Roving_Vehicle">
        Apollo Lunar Roving Vehicle
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">

         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1088" data-file-width="1644" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Apollo15LunarRover.jpg/200px-Apollo15LunarRover.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Apollo15LunarRover.jpg/300px-Apollo15LunarRover.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Apollo15LunarRover.jpg/400px-Apollo15LunarRover.jpg 2x" width="200"/>

         The
         <a href="/wiki/Apollo_15" title="Apollo 15">
          Apollo 15
         </a>
         Lunar Roving Vehicle on the Moon in 1971
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_Roving_Vehicle" title="Lunar Roving Vehicle">
        Lunar Roving Vehicle
       </a>

      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollo_Lunar_Roving_Vehicle" title="Apollo Lunar Roving Vehicle">
        Lunar Roving Vehicle
       </a>
       (LRV) was a battery-powered four-wheeled
       <a href="/wiki/Rover_(space_exploration)" title="Rover (space exploration)">
        rover
       </a>
       used on the Moon during the last three missions of the American
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo program
       </a>
       (15, 16, and 17) during 1971 and 1972. The LRV could carry one or two
       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">
        astronauts
       </a>
       , their equipment, and lunar samples.
       <a href="/wiki/Georg_von_Tiesenhausen" title="Georg von Tiesenhausen">
        Georg von Tiesenhausen
       </a>
       is credited with submitting the original design, before it was sent to
       <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">
        Boeing
       </a>
       for implementation.
      </p>

      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Lunokhod_2" title="Lunokhod 2">
        Lunokhod 2
       </a>
       was the second and a monocrystalline-panel-powered of two unmanned lunar rovers landed on the Moon by the Soviet Union as part of the Lunokhod program. The
       <a href="/wiki/Luna_21" title="Luna 21">
        Luna 21
       </a>
       spacecraft landed on the Moon and deployed the second Soviet lunar rover Lunokhod 2 in January 1973. The objectives of the mission were to collect images of the lunar surface, examine ambient light levels to determine the feasibility of astronomical observations from the Moon, perform laser ranging experiments, observe solar X-rays, measure local magnetic fields, and study the soil mechanics of the lunar surface material. Lunokhod 2 was intended to be followed by
       <a href="/wiki/Lunokhod_programme#Lunokhod_3" title="Lunokhod programme">
        Lunokhod 3 (No.205)
       </a>
       in 1977 but the mission was cancelled.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Yutu">
        <i>
         Yutu
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_rover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Yutu">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Yutu_(rover)" title="Yutu (rover)">
        Yutu (rover)
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yutu_(Lunar_rover)" title="Yutu (Lunar rover)">
         Yutu
        </a>
       </i>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
        Chinese
       </a>
       lunar rover which launched on 1 December 2013 and landed on 14 December 2013 as part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Chang%27e_3" title="Chang'e 3">
        Chang'e 3
       </a>
       mission. It is China's first lunar rover, part of the second phase of the
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Lunar_Exploration_Program" title="Chinese Lunar Exploration Program">
        Chinese Lunar Exploration Program
       </a>
       undertaken by
       <a href="/wiki/China_National_Space_Administration" title="China National Space Administration">
        China National Space Administration
       </a>
       (CNSA).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The lunar rover is called
       <i>
        Yutu
       </i>
       , or
       <i>
        Jade Rabbit
       </i>
       , a name selected in an online poll.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt20131202_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-nyt20131202-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The rover encountered operational difficulties after the first 14-day lunar night, and was unable to move after the end of the second lunar night, yet it is still gathering some useful data.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <i>
        Yutu
       </i>
       rover might be the world's first true hibernating robot on the moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Chandrayaan-2_rover">
        Chandrayaan-2 rover
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_rover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Chandrayaan-2 rover">
         edit

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Chandrayaan-2" title="Chandrayaan-2">
        Chandrayaan-2
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Chandrayaan-2" title="Chandrayaan-2">
        Chandrayaan-2
       </a>
       mission is the first lunar rover mission by India, consisting of a lunar orbiter and a lunar lander. The rover weighing 50 kg, will have six wheels and will be running on solar power. It will land near one of the poles and will operate for a year, roving up to 150 km at a maximum speed of 360 m/h. The proposed launch date of the mission is 2018.
      </p>

       <i>

      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">

       </a>

       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">

       <a href="/wiki/Jet_Propulsion_Laboratory" title="Jet Propulsion Laboratory">

       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">

       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       's Johnson and Ames Centers,
       <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">
        Stanford University
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">
        Boeing
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA1_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NASA1-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ATHLETE is designed, for maximum efficiency, to be able to both roll and walk over a wide range of terrains.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JPL1_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-JPL1-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
