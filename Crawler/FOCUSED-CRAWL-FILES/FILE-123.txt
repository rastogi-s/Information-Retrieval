      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Earth, Moon, and Planets
        </b>
       </i>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">
        peer-reviewed
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">
        scientific journal
       </a>
       , published approximately ten times per year by
       <a href="/wiki/Springer_Science%2BBusiness_Media" title="Springer Science+Business Media">
        Springer Science+Business Media
       </a>
       . It was established in 1969 under the title
       <i>
        The Moon
       </i>
       , was known as
       <i>
        The Moon and the Planets
       </i>
       from 1978 to 1983, and was first published under the current title in February 1984. The
       <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">
        editor-in-chief
       </a>
       is Murthy S. Gudipati (
       <a href="/wiki/California_Institute_of_Technology" title="California Institute of Technology">
        Caltech
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jet_Propulsion_Laboratory" title="Jet Propulsion Laboratory">
        JPL
       </a>
       ). The journal's main focus is on research about the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
        Solar System
       </a>
       . Besides original research articles,
       <i>
        Earth, Moon, and Planets
       </i>
       publishes conference proceedings, review articles, book reviews, and special issues.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edit_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-edit-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cassi_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-cassi-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aims_and_scope">
        Aims and scope
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth,_Moon,_and_Planets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Aims and scope">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Earth, Moon, and Planets
       </i>
       focuses on
       <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">
        original research
       </a>
       articles on
       <a href="/wiki/Star_formation" title="Star formation">
        formation of stars
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Nebular_hypothesis" title="Nebular hypothesis">
        planets
       </a>
       , evolution of the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
        Solar System
       </a>
       including its origin, and the evolution of extra-solar systems including their origins. The focus also includes
       <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">
        asteroids
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">
        comets
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Meteoroid" title="Meteoroid">
        meteoroids
       </a>
       , and near-Earth objects, Earth impact hazards, the Solar System-Earth relationship, and related topics. Research coverage encompasses
       <a href="/wiki/Physical_property" title="Physical property">
        physical
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Chemical_property" title="Chemical property">
        chemical properties
       </a>
       of the above-mentioned celestial bodies, and their related chaotic behavior.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
