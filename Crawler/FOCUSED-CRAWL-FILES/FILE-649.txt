      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is a 2001
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       written, produced and directed by
       <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">
        Nashville
       </a>
       -based
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">
        filmmaker
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Bart_Sibrel" title="Bart Sibrel">
        Bart Sibrel
       </a>
       . Sibrel is a critic of the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo program
       </a>
       and proponent of the
       <a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_theory" title="Conspiracy theory">
        conspiracy theory
       </a>
       that the six
       <a href="/wiki/Moon_landing" title="Moon landing">
        Apollo Moon landing
       </a>
       missions between 1969 and 1972 were
       <a href="/wiki/Moon_landing_conspiracy_theories" title="Moon landing conspiracy theories">
        elaborate hoaxes
       </a>
       perpetrated by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_government" title="United States government">
        United States government
       </a>
       , including
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Sibrel presents assertions of an alleged
       <a href="/wiki/Hoax" title="Hoax">
        hoax
       </a>
       , including: supposed
       <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">
        photographic
       </a>
       anomalies; disasters such as the destruction of
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_1" title="Apollo 1">
        Apollo 1
       </a>
       in which crew members
       <a href="/wiki/Roger_B._Chaffee" title="Roger B. Chaffee">
        Roger B. Chaffee
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Gus_Grissom" title="Gus Grissom">
        Gus Grissom
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ed_White_(astronaut)" title="Ed White (astronaut)">
        Ed White
       </a>
       died; technical difficulties experienced in the 1950s and 1960s; and the problems of traversing the
       <a href="/wiki/Van_Allen_radiation_belt" title="Van Allen radiation belt">
        Van Allen radiation belts
       </a>
       . Sibrel proposes that the most condemning evidence is a piece of footage that was inadvertently
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       sent to him by NASA when requesting photographs and video of the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">
        Apollo 11
       </a>
       mission. Sibrel believes that the footage shows Apollo astronauts
       <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armstrong" title="Neil Armstrong">
        Neil Armstrong
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Buzz_Aldrin" title="Buzz Aldrin">
        Buzz Aldrin
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(astronaut)" title="Michael Collins (astronaut)">
        Michael Collins
       </a>
       attempting to create the
       <a href="/wiki/Illusion" title="Illusion">
        illusion
       </a>
       that they were 130,000 miles (210,000 km) from
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       (or roughly halfway to the Moon) when, he claims, they were only in a
       <a href="/wiki/Low_Earth_orbit" title="Low Earth orbit">
        low Earth orbit
       </a>
       . Sibrel views this clip as "
       <a href="/wiki/Smoking_gun" title="Smoking gun">
        smoking gun
       </a>
       " proof that the Moon landings were staged.
      </p>
      <p>
       The film's name was taken from the title of the book
       <i>
        A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Moon
       </i>
       by Bob Ward, a 1969 book of humorous anecdotes by people involved in the space program.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The film is narrated by
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">
        British
       </a>
       stage actress Anne Tonelson. Bart Sibrel himself makes no appearance in the film. However, he does appear prominently in the similarly toned 2001
       <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">
        Fox
       </a>
       television network special,
       <i>
        Conspiracy Theory: Did We Land on the Moon?
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       Bart Sibrel believes that there were numerous insurmountable scientific and technical problems which made it improbable that men could land on the Moon and return to Earth safely. Further, he believes that certain supposed anomalies and inconsistencies in NASA's records of the landings point to a hoax, and that the
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Race" title="Space Race">
        Space Race
       </a>
       was actually a race to develop armaments. He claims that NASA perpetrated a
       <a href="/wiki/Fraud" title="Fraud">
        fraud
       </a>
       because of the perception that if the United States could land men on the Moon before the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet Union
       </a>
       , it would be a major victory in the
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">
        Cold War
       </a>
       , since the Soviets had been the first to achieve a successful space launch (
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">
         Sputnik 1
        </a>
       </i>
       in 1957), the first manned space flight (
       <a href="/wiki/Vostok_1" title="Vostok 1">
        Vostok 1
       </a>
       in 1961), and the first
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">
        spacewalk
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Voskhod_2" title="Voskhod 2">
        Voskhod 2
       </a>
       in 1965).
      </p>
      <p>
       The 47-minute film primarily focuses on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Van_Allen_radiation_belts" title="Van Allen radiation belts">
        Van Allen radiation belt
       </a>
       , areas of intense radiation circling the Earth, as a major reason that Sibrel believes it was impossible for a manned spacecraft to land on the Moon.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms">
        Criticisms
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Funny_Thing_Happened_on_the_Way_to_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criticisms">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Jim McDade, writing in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Birmingham_News" title="The Birmingham News">
         The Birmingham News
        </a>
       </i>
       , characterized
       <i>
        A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Moon
       </i>
       as "full of falsehoods, innuendo, strident accusations, half-truths, flawed logic and premature conclusions."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McDadereview_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-McDadereview-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       According to McDade, the "only thing new and weird" in the film is that the claim that video views of Earth were actually filmed through a small hole to give the impression that Apollo 11 was not in low earth orbit. "Bart has misinterpreted things that are immediately obvious to anyone who has extensively read Apollo history and documentation or anyone who has ever been inside an
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Command/Service_Module" title="Apollo Command/Service Module">
        Apollo Command Module
       </a>
       or accurate mockup," says McDade.
      </p>
