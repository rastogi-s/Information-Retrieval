      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Moon Patrol
        </b>
       </i>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         ムーンパトロール
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Mūn Patorōru
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       is an
       <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">
        arcade game
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Irem" title="Irem">
        Irem
       </a>
       released in 1982. It was licensed to
       <a href="/wiki/WMS_Industries" title="WMS Industries">
        Williams
       </a>
       for distribution in North America.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        Moon Patrol
       </i>
       is widely credited for the introduction of
       <a href="/wiki/Parallax_scrolling" title="Parallax scrolling">
        parallax scrolling
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Side-scrolling_video_game" title="Side-scrolling video game">
        side-scrolling video games
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parallax_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-parallax-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Taito" title="Taito">
        Taito
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Jungle_Hunt" title="Jungle Hunt">
         Jungle Hunt
        </a>
       </i>
       side-scroller, released the same year as
       <i>
        Moon Patrol
       </i>
       , also features parallax scrolling.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">
        Gameplay
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_Patrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Moon_patrol.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="248" data-file-width="240" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1d/Moon_patrol.png/170px-Moon_patrol.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/1/1d/Moon_patrol.png 1.5x" width="170"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Moon_patrol.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Gameplay screenshot
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The player takes the role of a Luna City
       <a href="/wiki/Police_officer" title="Police officer">
        police officer
       </a>
       assigned to Sector Nine, the home of the "toughest thugs in the galaxy". The player controls a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_Roving_Vehicle" title="Lunar Roving Vehicle">
        moon buggy
       </a>
       that travels over the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        moon
       </a>
       's surface, viewing it from the side as it moves towards the right side of the screen. While driving it, obstacles such as craters and mines must be avoided, and various enemies such as
       <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_flying_object" title="Unidentified flying object">
        UFOs
       </a>
       from above and tanks on the ground must be shot down.
      </p>
      <p>
       Gameplay is within a number of courses, and each is divided into 26 checkpoints, named after the letters of the
       <a href="/wiki/English_alphabet" title="English alphabet">
        English alphabet
       </a>
       . Of these, the five major checkpoints—
       <i>
        E
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        J
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        O
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        T
       </i>
       and,
       <i>
        Z
       </i>
       —denote a new "stage" with a new background and theme; for example, the third stage starting at J introduces mines. The top portion of the screen shows a timeline-style map of the course, with the five major checkpoints clearly marked. Above the map is an indicator of the current checkpoint, the time spent in the stage, and three indicator lights: the top light indicates upcoming enemy aerial attacks, the middle one indicates an upcoming minefield, and the bottom one indicates enemies approaching from behind.
      </p>
      <p>
       At the end of a stage, that time spent is compared to the average, and bonus points are awarded accordingly, at 1,000 plus 100 per second bettered; completing an entire course gives an additional 5,000 points plus 100 per second bettered. There are two unique courses: the "Beginner Course" and the "Champion Course". The Champion Course "loops" forever, and each loop is numbered for convenience, up to 3.
      </p>
      <div style="clear:left;">
      </div>
      <p>
       The music during gameplay is a repeating
       <a href="/wiki/Twelve-bar_blues" title="Twelve-bar blues">
        twelve-bar
       </a>
       melody that appears to pay homage to the bass line of James Brown's "Soulful Christmas."
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (December 2017)">
           original research?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Extra lives are given at 10,000 points, and per 20,000 scored up to 50,000; thereafter, no more lives. Game ends if last patrol car is destroyed. Can be continued, but points scored from one game cannot carry over. 999,990 points is the maximum high score that can be achieved; players can exceed this score, but game keeps last 6 digits.
      </p>

        MSX
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBM_PC" title="IBM PC">
        IBM PC
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TI-99/4A" title="TI-99/4A">
        TI-99/4A
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tandy_Color_Computer" title="Tandy Color Computer">
        Tandy Color Computer
       </a>
       . Many of these were published by Atari's
       <a href="/wiki/Atarisoft" title="Atarisoft">
        Atarisoft
       </a>
       label.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was included in the retro compilations
       <i>
        Arcade Hits: Moon Patrol &amp; Spy Hunter
       </i>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy_Color" title="Game Boy Color">
        Game Boy Color
       </a>
       and
       <i>
        Midway Presents Arcade's Greatest Hits: The Midway Collection 2
       </i>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Dreamcast" title="Dreamcast">
        Dreamcast
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation">
        PlayStation
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windows" title="Windows">
        Windows
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2006
       <a href="/wiki/Bandai" title="Bandai">
        Bandai
       </a>
       released an enhanced version as
       <i>
        Moon Patrol EX
       </i>
       for cell phones.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_legacy">
        Reception and legacy
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_Patrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception and legacy">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>

      <p>
       <i>
        Moon Patrol
       </i>
       received a Certificate of Merit in the category of "1984 Best Science Fiction/ Fantasy Videogame" at the 5th annual
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arkie_Awards" title="Arkie Awards">
        Arkie Awards
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        :
        <span>
         42
        </span>
       </sup>
       <i>
        Arcade Express
       </i>
       reviewed the arcade version in January 1983 and scored it 8 out of 10.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Scott Mace of
       <i>
        InfoWorld
       </i>
       stated that
       <i>
        Moon Patrol
       </i>
       for the Commodore 64 was his favorite Atarisoft game, with good use of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SID_chip" title="SID chip">
        the computer's sound
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mace19840409_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-mace19840409-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Clones">
        Clones
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_Patrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Clones">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       A bootleg version called
       <i>
        Moon Ranger
       </i>
       was released in the arcades the same year.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moonranger_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-moonranger-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Home clones include
       <i>
        Desert Patrol
       </i>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/TRS-80_Color_Computer" title="TRS-80 Color Computer">
        TRS-80 Color Computer
       </a>
       (1983),
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        Gas Hog
       </i>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/Atari_2600" title="Atari 2600">
        Atari 2600
       </a>
       (1983),
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        Lunar Rover Patrol
       </i>
       for the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dragon_32" title="Dragon 32">
        Dragon 32
       </a>
       (1983),
       <i>
        Moon Buggy
       </i>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">
        Commodore 64
       </a>
       (1983),
       <i>
        Moon Alert
       </i>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/ZX_Spectrum" title="ZX Spectrum">
        ZX Spectrum
       </a>
       (1984),
       <i>
        Luna Rover
       </i>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/ZX_Spectrum" title="ZX Spectrum">
        ZX Spectrum
       </a>
       (1985),
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        Moon Control
       </i>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/Amstrad_CPC" title="Amstrad CPC">
        Amstrad CPC
       </a>
       (1985),
       <i>
        Moonrider
       </i>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/MSX" title="MSX">
        MSX
       </a>
       (1986), and
       <i>
        Overlander
       </i>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/Amiga" title="Amiga">
        Amiga
       </a>
       (1993).
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Battle_Through_Time" title="Battle Through Time">
         Battle Through Time
        </a>
       </i>
       (1984) for the Commodore 64 re-themed the gameplay to be about major wars of the twentieth century.
      </p>
      <p>
       Open-source clones named
       <i>
        moon-buggy
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/ASCII" title="ASCII">
         Ascii
        </a>
        Patrol
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       run in Unix-like terminals.
      </p>
