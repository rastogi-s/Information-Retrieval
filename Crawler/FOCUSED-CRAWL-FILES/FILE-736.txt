      <p>
       <b>
        2013 CNBLUE Blue Moon World Tour
       </b>
       was the 2013 live
       <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">
        concert tour
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">
        South Korean
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_band" title="Rock band">
        rock band
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/CNBLUE" title="CNBLUE">
        CNBLUE
       </a>
       in support of their fifth EP,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Re:Blue" title="Re:Blue">
         Re:Blue
        </a>
       </i>
       , which was released on 14 January 2013.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The world tour commenced, at the conclusion of promotional activities in
       <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">
        South Korea
       </a>
       , with one show in
       <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">
        Taiwan
       </a>
       in April 2013
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and scheduled concerts for
       <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">
        Singapore
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">
        Thailand
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">
        Hong Kong
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">
        South Korea
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
        Australia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">
        Malaysia
       </a>
       , thru August 2013.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Subsequent concert dates added
       <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
        China
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
        Indonesia
       </a>
       ;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and extended the tour into January 2014, for concerts in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
        Mexico
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
        Peru
       </a>
       , and the final concert in
       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
        Chile
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       for a total of 20 concerts in 14 countries and 18 cities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
        Background
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Moon_World_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The tour was announced on 23 January 2013 by
       <a href="/wiki/FNC_Entertainment" title="FNC Entertainment">
        FNC Entertainment
       </a>
       , with the official poster released on 28 February.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This marked the band's first world live tour following their joint concert with labelmates
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FT_Island" title="FT Island">
        FT Island
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
        Los Angeles
       </a>
       , USA in May 2012 and exclusive concert at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IndigO2" title="IndigO2">
        IndigO2
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
        London
       </a>
       in September 2012.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In March 2013
       <a href="/wiki/FNC_Entertainment" title="FNC Entertainment">
        FNC Entertainment
       </a>
       announced they were in negotiations for concerts in China, namely
       <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">
        Beijing
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">
        Shanghai
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">
        South America
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       which would make the band the first South Korean rock band to hold a world tour of this kind.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Concerts">
        Concerts
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Moon_World_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Concerts">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian" title="Australian">
        Australian
       </a>
       concerts marked the first time the band performed solo in
       <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">
        Sydney
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">
        Melbourne
       </a>
       , also the first Korean band to do so.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The tickets for the 11 May Hong Kong concert went on sale 11 March and the 7,000 tickets were sold out within five minutes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       An additional show on 10 May was added and tickets went on sale on 20 March.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       An extra show in Malaysia was added after tickets for the Singapore concert were sold out.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The first concert of the world tour in
       <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">
        Taipei
       </a>
       was played to an audience of 11,000 people, where the band played 24 tracks. During a press conference held on 5 April, leader
       <a href="/wiki/Jung_Yong-hwa" title="Jung Yong-hwa">
        Jung Yong-hwa
       </a>
       performed an acoustic version of his self-composed lead track "I′m Sorry" from EP Re:Blue.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The two concerts in Hong Kong were played to an audience of 15,000 people, where the band celebrated
       <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jong-hyun" title="Lee Jong-hyun">
        Lee Jong-hyun
       </a>
       ′s birthday on the second and last day of the concerts.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">

       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
        New York City
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Times_Square" title="Times Square">
        Times Square
       </a>
       .

       <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">
        East Coast
       </a>
       . The 21 January date coincided with a major snowstorm, and the band worried about fans waiting outside in lines.

       <a href="/wiki/DramaFever" title="DramaFever">
        DramaFever
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">
        Korean drama
       </a>
       or k-drama for
       <a href="/wiki/Television_in_South_Korea" title="Television in South Korea">
        Television in South Korea
       </a>
       , and noted Jung Yong-hwa's "flawless English."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>

       <a href="/wiki/Billboard_K-Town" title="Billboard K-Town">
        Billboard K-Town
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jung-shin" title="Lee Jung-shin">
        Lee Jung-shin
       </a>
       said that the strong chart showing and sales of EP Re:Blue gave them the opportunity for the world tour and the trip to the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        U.S.
       </a>
       Jung Yong-hwa, who wore a guitar strap with an American flag design, said the band had dreamed of playing New York City for a long time and was glad it was happening. The band discussed their comparisons to America's
       <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">
        Maroon 5
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jason_Mraz" title="Jason Mraz">
        Jason Mraz
       </a>
       ; and influences of
       <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">
        Bruno Mars
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Kings_of_Leon" title="Kings of Leon">
        Kings of Leon
       </a>
       , as they are recording new music.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_22-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Billboard-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The band had a photo shoot and interview session with the city's
       <a href="/wiki/MTV_K" title="MTV K">
        MTV K
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTVK_23-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-MTVK-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and did additional photo shoots in Times Square and on the
       <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Bridge" title="Brooklyn Bridge">
        Brooklyn Bridge
       </a>
       , which were used for a
       <a href="/wiki/Teaser_campaign" title="Teaser campaign">
        teaser campaign
       </a>
       for their fifth mini album
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop_(CNBLUE_album)" title="Can't Stop (CNBLUE album)">
        Can't Stop
       </a>
       .

The panelists note the band's love of American music, their similarity to Maroon 5, and their greatest inspirations,
       <a href="/wiki/Jon_Bon_Jovi" title="Jon Bon Jovi">

       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
