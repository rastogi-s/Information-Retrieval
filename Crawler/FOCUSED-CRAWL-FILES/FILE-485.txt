   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Feast of the Hunters' Moon
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

      <p>
       <b>
        Feast of the Hunters’ Moon
       </b>
       is a weekend festival and historical reenactment held each fall on the first weekend of October since 1968, at the present-day site of
       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Ouiatenon" title="Fort Ouiatenon">
        Fort Ouiatenon
       </a>
       , a replica 18th century French military and trading post near
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Lafayette" title="West Lafayette">
        West Lafayette
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">
        Indiana
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Site">
        Site
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Feast_of_the_Hunters%27_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Site">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1872" data-file-width="2532" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg/220px-Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg/330px-Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg/440px-Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fort_Ouiatenon_blockhouse_front.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/Blockhouse" title="Blockhouse">
          Blockhouse
         </a>
         replica at Fort Ouiatenon
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Feast is held on the grounds of the Historic Fort Ouiatenon Park, on the
       <a href="/wiki/Wabash_River" title="Wabash River">
        Wabash River
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JCLocation_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-JCLocation-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Blockhouse" title="Blockhouse">
        blockhouse
       </a>
       is a replica of the original
       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Ouiatenon" title="Fort Ouiatenon">
        Fort Ouiatenon
       </a>
       , which was the first fortified European settlement in what is now called
       <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">
        Indiana
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fort_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-fort-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The fort served as a French
       <a href="/wiki/Trading_post" title="Trading post">
        trading post
       </a>
       and was located approximately one mile downriver from the replica.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ouiatenon_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ouiatenon-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Program">
        Program
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Feast_of_the_Hunters%27_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Program">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       During the festival, participants reenact the annual fall gathering of the French and Native Americans which took place at Fort Ouiatenon in the mid-18th century.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChicagoSunTimes1_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ChicagoSunTimes1-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JournalCcourier20110923_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-JournalCcourier20110923-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Participants dress in the garb of the 18th century French soldiers, settlers, and Native Americans who lived in this region.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gisler_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gisler-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Borsky_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Borsky-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Food vendors sell traditional period foods such as
       <a href="/wiki/Rabbit_stew" title="Rabbit stew">
        rabbit stew
       </a>
       , voyageur stew, and
       <a href="/wiki/Venison" title="Venison">
        venison
       </a>
       sausage.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JournalCcourier20110923_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-JournalCcourier20110923-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Borsky_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Borsky-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The program also includes music, marching, dancing and renenacted
       <a href="/wiki/Military_exercise" title="Military exercise">
        military maneuvers
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JournalCcourier20110923_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-JournalCcourier20110923-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_performances">
        Musical performances
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Feast_of_the_Hunters%27_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical performances">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       As part of the event, various musical acts perform, including
       <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">
        Native American
       </a>
       drummers, historical
       <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">
        folk music
       </a>
       performers,
       <a href="/wiki/French_folk_music" title="French folk music">
        French folksingers
       </a>
       and period
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Fife_and_Drum_Corps" title="Ancient Fife and Drum Corps">
        fife and drum corps
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gisler_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gisler-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Living_history_presentations">
        Living history presentations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Feast_of_the_Hunters%27_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Living history presentations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The event includes
       <a href="/wiki/Historical_reenactment" title="Historical reenactment">
        historical reenactments
       </a>
       featuring period characters from the 1700s to educate visitors about the lives and culture of the period.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OutdoorIndiana_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-OutdoorIndiana-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       One
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historical_interpreter" title="Historical interpreter">
        historical interpreter
       </a>
       presented a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delaware_Indian" title="Delaware Indian">
        Delaware Indian
       </a>
       who first served as a scout for the French and whose people lived along the Wabash River in the 1700s and co-existed peacefully with the French at the outpost.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThisIsOurLand_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ThisIsOurLand-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Others play
       <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">
        traders
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gunsmiths" title="Gunsmiths">
        gunsmiths
       </a>
       , such as "Pierre Rolletof" of French
       <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Scots_people" title="Ulster Scots people">
        Scots-Irish
       </a>
       descent who traveled along the Wabash River trading a range of items, including guns, and also
       <a href="/wiki/Gunsmith" title="Gunsmith">
        gunsmithed
       </a>
       as he traveled.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ItsAChallengingLife_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ItsAChallengingLife-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Other reenactors have demonstrated various period trades and crafts, including a chairmaker who built
       <a href="/wiki/Windsor_chair" title="Windsor chair">
        Windsor chairs
       </a>
       to order, spending 40–45 hours per chair.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simplicity_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Simplicity-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Size_and_production">
        Size and production
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Feast_of_the_Hunters%27_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Size and production">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In 2004, the annual event drew over 8,000 participants and over 60,000 spectators.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gisler_7-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gisler-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Tippecanoe_County,_Indiana" title="Tippecanoe County, Indiana">
        Tippecanoe County
       </a>
       Historical Association, cooperating with the Tippecanoe County Parks Department, presents the event on a weekend in late September or early October.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The annual event ran for its 44th year in 2011.2017 marks its 50th anniversary and the 300th anniversary of the fort.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-opener_14-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-opener-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
