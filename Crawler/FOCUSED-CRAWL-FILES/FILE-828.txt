      <p>
       <b>
        Dawes
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       , named after the British astronomer
       <a href="/wiki/William_Rutter_Dawes" title="William Rutter Dawes">
        William Rutter Dawes
       </a>
       , and which is located in the wide straight between
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Serenitatis" title="Mare Serenitatis">
        Mare Serenitatis
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mare_Tranquilitatis" title="Mare Tranquilitatis">
        Mare Tranquilitatis
       </a>
       . About 70 km southwest is the larger crater
       <a href="/wiki/Plinius_(crater)" title="Plinius (crater)">
        Plinius
       </a>
       , other nearby prominent craters include
       <a href="/wiki/Beketov_(crater)" title="Beketov (crater)">
        Beketov
       </a>
       nearly 75 km to the east and nearly to the southeast is
       <a href="/wiki/Jansen_(crater)" title="Jansen (crater)">
        Jansen
       </a>
       . To the northeast is the
       <a href="/wiki/Mons_Argaeus" title="Mons Argaeus">
        Mons Argaeus
       </a>
       mountain rise.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
        Description
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dawes_(lunar_crater)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       This is a circular crater with a sharp rim that has a slightly flattened oval perimeter. It has a slight central rise, and a somewhat darker floor that is nearly covered in overlapping swirl-like deposits. Much of the deposits are slumped or fall-back material. The inner walls are steep and free from impact erosion. Northeast of Dawes is a rille known as
       <b>
        Rima Dawes
       </b>
       , it lies next to Catena Brigitte to the northeast. The rille name was approved by the IAU in 1985.
      </p>
      <p>
       Detailed examination of this crater have located what appear to be alcoves and channels along the inner rim. It is hypothesized that
       <a href="/wiki/Micrometeorite" title="Micrometeorite">
        micrometeorite
       </a>
       impacts along the rim trigger dry landslides, which produce a
       <a href="/wiki/Gully" title="Gully">
        gully
       </a>
       -like appearance. A similar phenomenon may be responsible for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martian_Gullies" title="Martian Gullies">
        gully-like features
       </a>
       along the inner rim of some
       <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">
        Martian
       </a>
       craters.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
