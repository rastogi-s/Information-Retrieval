      <p>
       <b>
        Suess
       </b>
       is a small
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       on the
       <a href="/wiki/Oceanus_Procellarum" title="Oceanus Procellarum">
        Oceanus Procellarum
       </a>
       . It is a circular, cup-shaped feature with a higher
       <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
        albedo
       </a>
       than the surroundings. The closest significant crater is
       <a href="/wiki/Reiner_(crater)" title="Reiner (crater)">
        Reiner
       </a>
       , about 150 kilometers to the west-northwest. The
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_mare" title="Lunar mare">
        lunar mare
       </a>
       that surrounds Suess is marked by the rays radiating from the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Kepler_(lunar_crater)" title="Kepler (lunar crater)">
        Kepler
       </a>
       to the east-northeast.
      </p>
      <p>
       The long, sinuous
       <a href="/wiki/Rille" title="Rille">
        rille
       </a>
       named Rima Suess begins about 30 kilometers to the east of Suess, and winds its way in a generally north-northwesterly direction for a length of almost 200 kilometers.
      </p>
