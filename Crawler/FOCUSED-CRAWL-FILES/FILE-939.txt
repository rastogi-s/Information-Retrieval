      <p>
       <b>
        Puck
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="'p' in 'pie'">
            p
           </span>
           <span title="/ʌ/: 'u' in 'cut'">
            ʌ
           </span>
           <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
            k
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          PUK
         </span>
        </i>
       </a>
       ) is an
       <a href="/wiki/Inner_moon" title="Inner moon">
        inner moon
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">
        Uranus
       </a>
       . It was discovered in December 1985 by the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Voyager_2" title="Voyager 2">
         Voyager 2
        </a>
       </i>
       spacecraft.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The name
       <i>
        Puck
       </i>
       follows the convention of naming Uranus's moons after characters from
       <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">
        Shakespeare
       </a>
       . The orbit of Puck lies between the
       <a href="/wiki/Rings_of_Uranus" title="Rings of Uranus">
        rings of Uranus
       </a>
       and the first of Uranus's large moons,
       <a href="/wiki/Miranda_(moon)" title="Miranda (moon)">
        Miranda
       </a>
       . Puck is approximately spherical in shape and has diameter of about 162 km.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001-3">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It has a dark, heavily cratered surface, which shows spectral signs of water ice.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDumas_Smith_et_al.2003_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDumas_Smith_et_al.2003-7">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_and_naming">
        Discovery and naming
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puck_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Discovery and naming">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Puck—the largest inner moon of Uranus—was discovered from the images taken by
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Voyager_2" title="Voyager 2">
         Voyager 2
        </a>
       </i>
       on 30 December 1985. It was given the temporary designation
       <b>
        S/1985 U 1
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIAUC_4159_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIAUC_4159-8">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The moon was later named after the character Puck who appears in Shakespeare's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night's Dream">
         A Midsummer Night's Dream
        </a>
       </i>
       , a little
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sprite_(creature)" title="Sprite (creature)">
        sprite
       </a>
       who travels around the globe at night with the
       <a href="/wiki/Fairy" title="Fairy">
        fairies
       </a>
       . In
       <a href="/wiki/Celtic_mythology" title="Celtic mythology">
        Celtic mythology
       </a>
       and English folklore, a
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Puck_(mythology)" title="Puck (mythology)">
         Puck
        </a>
       </i>
       is a mischievous sprite, imagined as an evil demon by Christians.
      </p>
      <p>
       It is also designated
       <b>
        Uranus XV
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEUSGS:_Planet_and_Satellite_Names_and_Discoverers_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEUSGS:_Planet_and_Satellite_Names_and_Discoverers-9">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_characteristics">
        Physical characteristics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Puck_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Physical characteristics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Puck_map_(Stooke).jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="320" height="440" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5f/Puck_map_%28Stooke%29.jpg/220px-Puck_map_%28Stooke%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/5/5f/Puck_map_%28Stooke%29.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Puck_map_(Stooke).jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Map of Puck
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Puck is the largest small
       <a href="/wiki/Inner_moon" title="Inner moon">
        inner moon
       </a>
       of Uranus, which orbits inside the orbit of Miranda. It is intermediate in size between
       <a href="/wiki/Portia_(moon)" title="Portia (moon)">
        Portia
       </a>
       (the second-largest inner moon) and
       <a href="/wiki/Miranda_(moon)" title="Miranda (moon)">
        Miranda
       </a>
       (the smallest of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moons_of_Uranus#Large_moons" title="Moons of Uranus">
        five large classical moons
       </a>
       ). Puck's orbit is located between the
       <a href="/wiki/Rings_of_Uranus" title="Rings of Uranus">
        rings of Uranus
       </a>
       and Miranda. Little is known about Puck aside from its orbit,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJacobson1998_1-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJacobson1998-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       radius of about 81 km,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001_3-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001-3">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and geometric
       <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
        albedo
       </a>
       in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visible_light" title="Visible light">
        visible light
       </a>
       of approximately 0.11.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Hubble2001_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Hubble2001-4">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Of the moons discovered by the
       <i>
        Voyager 2
       </i>
       imaging team, only Puck was discovered early enough that the probe could be programmed to image it in some detail.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Images showed that Puck has a shape of a slightly
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prolate" title="Prolate">
        prolate
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Spheroid" title="Spheroid">
        spheroid
       </a>
       (ratio between axes is 0.97 ± 0.04).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001_3-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001-3">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Its surface is heavily cratered
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThomas_Veverka_et_al.1987_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThomas_Veverka_et_al.1987-5">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and is grey in color.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001_3-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Voyager2001-3">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There are three named
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        craters
       </a>
       on the surface of Puck, the largest being about 45 km in diameter.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Observations with the
       <a href="/wiki/Hubble_Space_Telescope" title="Hubble Space Telescope">
        Hubble Space Telescope
       </a>
       and large terrestrial telescopes found water-ice absorption features in the spectrum of Puck.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Hubble2001_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKarkoschka,_Hubble2001-4">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDumas_Smith_et_al.2003_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDumas_Smith_et_al.2003-7">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Nothing is known about the internal structure of Puck. It is probably made of a mixture of
       <a href="/wiki/Ice" title="Ice">
        water ice
       </a>
       with the dark material similar to that found in the rings.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDumas_Smith_et_al.2003_7-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDumas_Smith_et_al.2003-7">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This dark material is probably made of
       <a href="/wiki/Rock_(geology)" title="Rock (geology)">
        rocks
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Radiation" title="Radiation">
        radiation
       </a>
       -processed
       <a href="/wiki/Organic_compound" title="Organic compound">
        organics
       </a>
       . The absence of craters with bright rays implies that Puck is not differentiated, meaning that ice and non-ice components have not separated from each other into a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Core_(geology)" title="Core (geology)">
        core
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Mantle_(geology)" title="Mantle (geology)">
        mantle
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986_6-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmith_Soderblom_et_al.1986-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
