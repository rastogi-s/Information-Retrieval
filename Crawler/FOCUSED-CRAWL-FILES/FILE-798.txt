   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Thelxinoe (moon)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Thelxinoe
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/θ/: 'th' in 'thigh'">
            θ
           </span>
           <span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">
            ɛ
           </span>
           <span title="'l' in 'lie'">
            l
           </span>
           <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
            k
           </span>
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
            s
           </span>
           <span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">
            ɪ
           </span>
           <span title="'n' in 'nigh'">
            n
           </span>
           <span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">
            oʊ
           </span>
           <span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">
            ˌ
           </span>
           <span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">
            iː
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         thelk-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          SIN
         </span>
         -o-ee
        </i>
       </a>
       ; Greek:
       <i>
        Θελξινόη
       </i>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         Jupiter XLII
        </span>
       </b>
       , is a
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">
        natural satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
        Jupiter
       </a>
       . It was discovered by a team of astronomers from the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii" title="University of Hawaii">
        University of Hawaii
       </a>
       led by
       <a href="/wiki/Scott_S._Sheppard" title="Scott S. Sheppard">
        Scott S. Sheppard
       </a>
       in 2004 from pictures taken in 2003, and originally received the temporary designation
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         S/2003 J 22
        </span>
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Thelxinoe is about 2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 20,454 Mm in 597.607 days, at an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">
        inclination
       </a>
       of 151° to the
       <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">
        ecliptic
       </a>
       (153° to Jupiter's equator), in a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retrograde_motion" title="Retrograde motion">
        retrograde
       </a>
       direction and with an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eccentricity_(orbit)" title="Eccentricity (orbit)">
        eccentricity
       </a>
       of 0.2685.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was named in March 2005 after
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thelxinoe" title="Thelxinoe">
        Thelxinoe
       </a>
       , one of the four original
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muse" title="Muse">
        Muses
       </a>
       according to some Greek writers, and a daughter of
       <a href="/wiki/Zeus" title="Zeus">
        Zeus
       </a>
       (Jupiter) by
       <a href="/wiki/Mnemosyne" title="Mnemosyne">
        Mnemosyne
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Thelxinoe belongs to the
       <a href="/wiki/Ananke_group" title="Ananke group">
        Ananke group
       </a>
       , retrograde irregular moons that orbit Jupiter between 19.3 and 22.7 Gm, at inclinations of roughly 150°.
      </p>
