      <p>
       <b>
        Cheese Factories on the Moon: Why Earmarks are Good for American Democracy
       </b>
       is a book by American political scientists Scott A. Frisch and Sean Q Kelly. The title of the book was inspired by a quote by conservative
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Republican_Party" title="U.S. Republican Party">
        Republican
       </a>
       and former
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">
        Senator
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Phil_Gramm" title="Phil Gramm">
        Phil Gramm
       </a>
       , who said:
      </p>
      <table class="cquote pullquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
       <tr>
        <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
         “
        </td>
        <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
         If we should vote next week on whether to begin producing cheese in a factory on the moon, I almost certainly would oppose it...On the other hand, if the government decided to institute the policy, it would be my objective to see that a
         <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
          Texas
         </a>
         contractor builds this celestial cheese plant, that the milk comes from Texas cows, and that the Earth distribution center is located in Texas."
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
          <a href="#cite_note-1">
           [1]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </td>
        <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
         ”
        </td>
       </tr>
      </table>
      <p>
       The book focuses on
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">
        congressional
       </a>
       appropriations
       <a href="/wiki/Earmark_(politics)" title="Earmark (politics)">
        earmarks
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Herdt_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Herdt-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
