      <p>
       "
       <b>
        Moon
       </b>
       " is a song by Icelandic artist
       <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">
        Björk
       </a>
       . It is the first track on her album
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Biophilia_(album)" title="Biophilia (album)">
         Biophilia
        </a>
       </i>
       and was released as the fourth and final single before the release of the album on September 6, 2011
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       . Each song in the album features a theme related to nature. In "Moon", Björk explores the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_cycle" title="Lunar cycle">
        lunar cycles
       </a>
       and the effect they have on Earth.
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_composition">
        Background and composition
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(Bj%C3%B6rk_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and composition">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The song "Moon" is based on four different
       <a href="/wiki/Cycle_(music)" title="Cycle (music)">
        sequences
       </a>
       played by four different harpists:
       <a href="/wiki/Zeena_Parkins" title="Zeena Parkins">
        Zeena Parkins
       </a>
       , Shelley Burgon, Sara Cutler, Carol Emanuel. These sequences repeat throughout the song, resembling the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_cycle" title="Lunar cycle">
        lunar cycles
       </a>
       . The song has 17/8
       <a href="/wiki/Time_signature" title="Time signature">
        time signature
       </a>
       . The lyrics to "Moon" deals with themes such as
       <a href="/wiki/Reincarnation" title="Reincarnation">
        rebirth
       </a>
       , start over and
       <a href="/wiki/Mythology" title="Mythology">
        mythology
       </a>
       , making reference to the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       as "adrenalin pearls placed in the gods' mouths".
      </p>
      <p>
       About the song, Björk explained: "With each
       <a href="/wiki/New_moon" title="New moon">
        new moon
       </a>
       we complete a cycle and are offered renewal —to take risks, to connect with other people, to love, to give. The symbolism of the moon as the realm of imagination, melancholy, and regeneration is expressed in the song"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">

       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The music video for "Moon" premiered on September 23, 2011, in Björk's official YouTube page
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       . In the video, Björk's image is alternated with images of the moon, resembling the moon's phases. The singer wears the same orange wig and brown custom that is seen in the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Biophilia_(album)" title="Biophilia (album)">
         Biophilia
        </a>
       </i>
       album cover.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video_2">
        Music video
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(Bj%C3%B6rk_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The video for "Moon" was directed by
       <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">
        Björk
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/M/M_Paris" title="M/M Paris">
        M/M Paris
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inez_van_Lamsweerde_and_Vinoodh_Matadin" title="Inez van Lamsweerde and Vinoodh Matadin">
        Inez &amp; Vinoodh
       </a>
       and James Merry and recorded spontaneously during the photo sessions for
       <i>
        Biophilia
       </i>
       . Björk sings the song with the orange
       <a href="/wiki/Crystal" title="Crystal">
        crystal
       </a>
       that also features on the
       <i>
        Biophilia
       </i>
       album and singles cover, in one take. Images from the 'Moon'
       <a href="/wiki/Application_software" title="Application software">
        app
       </a>
       appear superposed throughout the video.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="App">
        App
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_(Bj%C3%B6rk_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: App">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The 'Moon'
       <a href="/wiki/Application_software" title="Application software">
        app
       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">
        iPad
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">
        iPhone
       </a>
       was developed by 18-year-old programmer
       <a href="/wiki/Max_Weisel" title="Max Weisel">
        Max Weisel
       </a>
       , and is a musical sequencer which features 17 moons (representing each pulse of a time bar in the song). By changing the position of each moon, one changes the note it produces, thus forming a new melody each time which can be played with Björk's voice. The app also allows one to play the original song
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       .
      </p>
