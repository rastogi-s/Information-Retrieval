   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    A Trip to the Moon (attraction)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

      <p>
       <b>
        A Trip to the Moon
       </b>
       was a pioneering early
       <a href="/wiki/Dark_ride" title="Dark ride">
        dark ride
       </a>
       , best known as the flagship and namesake of Coney Island's
       <a href="/wiki/Luna_Park,_Coney_Island_(1903)" title="Luna Park, Coney Island (1903)">
        Luna Park
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Trip_to_the_Moon_(attraction)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Le_voyage_dans_la_lune_(operetta)" title="Le voyage dans la lune (operetta)">
        Le voyage dans la lune (operetta)
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/A_Trip_to_the_Moon" title="A Trip to the Moon">
        A Trip to the Moon
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       A Trip to the Moon was originally designed by
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frederic_Thompson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frederic Thompson (page does not exist)">
        Frederic Thompson
       </a>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/Pan-American_Exposition" title="Pan-American Exposition">
        Pan-American Exposition
       </a>
       of 1901 in
       <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">
        Buffalo, New York
       </a>
       . Tickets for the popular ride were
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._dollar" title="U.S. dollar">
        US$
       </a>
       0.50 ($15.00 in 2017 dollars
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflation-US_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ) at the time, twice the price of other attractions at the exposition. It was experienced by over 400,000 people before it closed on November 2, 1901.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was the first electrically powered mechanical "dark ride" and one of the first space rides.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       After the exposition, Thompson and his partner
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Elmer_Skip_Dundy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Elmer Skip Dundy (page does not exist)">
        Elmer Skip Dundy
       </a>
       brought the attraction to Tilyou's
       <a href="/wiki/Steeplechase_Park" title="Steeplechase Park">
        Steeplechase Park
       </a>
       , then later established it as the anchor of their newly opened
       <a href="/wiki/Luna_Park,_Coney_Island_(1903)" title="Luna Park, Coney Island (1903)">
        Luna Park
       </a>
       in 1903.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Experience">
        Experience
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Trip_to_the_Moon_(attraction)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Experience">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The first version of the ride involved a simulated trip for thirty passengers from the fairgrounds to the Moon aboard the
       <a href="/wiki/Airship" title="Airship">
        airship
       </a>
       -
       <a href="/wiki/Ornithopter" title="Ornithopter">
        ornithopter
       </a>
       <i>
        Luna
       </i>
       , with visions displayed of
       <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Falls" title="Niagara Falls">
        Niagara Falls
       </a>
       , the North American continent and the Earth's disc. The passengers then left the craft to walk around a cavernous
       <a href="/wiki/Papier-m%C3%A2ch%C3%A9" title="Papier-mâché">
        papier-mâché
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_the_Moon" title="Geology of the Moon">
        lunar surface
       </a>
       peopled by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Costumed_characters" title="Costumed characters">
        costumed characters
       </a>
       playing
       <a href="/wiki/The_First_Men_in_the_Moon" title="The First Men in the Moon">
        Selenites
       </a>
       . There they visited the palace of the
       <a href="/wiki/Man_in_the_Moon" title="Man in the Moon">
        Man in the Moon
       </a>
       and his dancing "moon maidens", before finally leaving the attraction through a
       <a href="/wiki/Mooncalf" title="Mooncalf">
        Mooncalf
       </a>
       's mouth. After it was brought to Coney Island's Luna Park, the ride was revamped in a new building at a cost of $52,000. The ride's centerpiece was a ship called Luna III, enlarged to accommodate more passengers. Unlike its original Buffalo incarnation, the new version of the ride passed over a panorama of Coney Island and Manhattan's skyscrapers before rising into the clouds.
      </p>
