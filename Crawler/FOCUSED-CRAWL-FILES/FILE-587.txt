      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar Orbital Station
       </b>
       is a proposed
       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
        Russian
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Space_station" title="Space station">
        space station
       </a>
       in orbit around the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The design was revealed in 2007 at a conference at the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gagarin_Cosmonaut_Training_Center" title="Gagarin Cosmonaut Training Center">
        Gagarin Cosmonaut Training Center
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Star_City,_Russia" title="Star City, Russia">
        Star City
       </a>
       . It is one of the two parts of the planned Russian lunar infrastructure, the other part being a base on the surface of the Moon. LOS will have six docking ports, high-power
       <a href="/wiki/Antenna_(radio)" title="Antenna (radio)">
        antenna
       </a>
       for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communications" title="Communications">
        communications
       </a>
       , maneuvering and
       <a href="/wiki/Attitude_control" title="Attitude control">
        attitude control
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Engines" title="Engines">
        engines
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_panels" title="Solar panels">
        solar panels
       </a>
       and a
       <a href="/wiki/Robotic_arm" title="Robotic arm">
        robotic arm
       </a>
       , similar to the one developed by the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">
        European Space Agency
       </a>
       for the Russian segment of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISS" title="ISS">
        ISS
       </a>
       . The station would be launched atop a super heavy version of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angara_rocket" title="Angara rocket">
        Angara rocket
       </a>
       . It is expected to be built after 2030.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
