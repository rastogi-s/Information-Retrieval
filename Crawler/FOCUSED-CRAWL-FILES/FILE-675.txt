      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">
        fantasy
       </a>
       author
       <a href="/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien" title="J. R. R. Tolkien">
        J. R. R. Tolkien
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">
        Middle-earth
       </a>
       fame included Earth's
       <b>
        <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
         sun
        </a>
       </b>
       and
       <b>
        <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
         moon
        </a>
       </b>
       for the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle-earth_cosmology" title="Middle-earth cosmology">
        cosmology
       </a>
       of his myths of
       <a href="/wiki/Arda_(Tolkien)" title="Arda (Tolkien)">
        Arda
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       These
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_object" title="Astronomical object">
        astronomical bodies
       </a>
       appear in various versions of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Silmarillion" title="The Silmarillion">
         The Silmarillion
        </a>
       </i>
       , a history of a world, called Middle-earth populated by
       <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">
        Elves
       </a>
       and other fantastic creatures as well as
       <a href="/wiki/Man_(Middle-earth)" title="Man (Middle-earth)">
        Men
       </a>
       . A version of
       <i>
        The Silmarillion
       </i>
       , edited by the author's son and literary executor Christopher Tolkien, was posthumously published in 1977. However the Sun and Moon already appear in the author's writings dating from the 1920s.
      </p>
      <p>
       The sun and moon in
       <a href="/wiki/Tolkien%27s_legendarium" title="Tolkien's legendarium">
        Tolkien's legendarium
       </a>
       were described in the
       <i>
        <b>
         Narsilion
        </b>
       </i>
       , the "Song of the Sun and Moon".
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sun">
        Sun
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_and_Moon_(Middle-earth)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sun">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The published Silmarillion states that the Sun was created by the
       <a href="/wiki/Vala_(Middle-earth)" title="Vala (Middle-earth)">
        Vala
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Aul%C3%AB" title="Aulë">
        Aulë
       </a>
       ; he and his people made a vessel to hold the radiance of the last fruit of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurelin" title="Laurelin">
        Laurelin
       </a>
       . The vessel of the sun was guided by
       <a href="/wiki/Arien" title="Arien">
        Arien
       </a>
       , a Maia.
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        […] and Anar the Fire-golden, fruit of Laurelin, they named the Sun. But the Noldor named [it] Vasa, the Heart of Fire, that awakens and consumes; for the Sun was set as a sign for the awakening of Men and the waning of the Elves […]
       </p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        —
        <cite>
         <a href="/wiki/The_Silmarillion" title="The Silmarillion">
          The Silmarillion
         </a>
         , 99
        </cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Names of the Sun amongst the
       <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">
        Elves
       </a>
       included
       <i>
        Anar
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        The Fire-golden
       </i>
       , a name given to it by the
       <a href="/wiki/Vanyar" title="Vanyar">
        Vanyar
       </a>
       ;
       <i>
        Anor
       </i>
       , the common name for the Sun in
       <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">
        Sindarin
       </a>
       , as seen in
       <i>
        Minas Anor
       </i>
       (later
       <a href="/wiki/Minas_Tirith" title="Minas Tirith">
        Minas Tirith
       </a>
       ) and the
       <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">
        Gondorian
       </a>
       province of
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/An%C3%B3rien" title="Anórien">
         Anórien
        </a>
       </i>
       ; and
       <i>
        Vása
       </i>
       , or
       <i>
        Heart of Fire
       </i>
       , a name given to the Sun by the
       <a href="/wiki/Noldor" title="Noldor">
        Noldor
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       A poetic name for the Sun was
       <i>
        The Daystar
       </i>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Gollum" title="Gollum">
        Gollum
       </a>
       referred to it as
       <i>
        The Yellow Face
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Sun was seen by the Elves as made on behalf of
       <a href="/wiki/Man_(Middle-earth)" title="Man (Middle-earth)">
        Men
       </a>
       , and they valued the
       <a href="#Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       more highly.
       <a href="/wiki/Morgoth" title="Morgoth">
        Morgoth
       </a>
       's creatures, the
       <a href="/wiki/Orc_(Middle-earth)" title="Orc (Middle-earth)">
        Orcs
       </a>
       , feared the Sun, and with the exception of the
       <a href="/wiki/Uruk-hai" title="Uruk-hai">
        Uruk-hai
       </a>
       , they did not travel while it was in the sky.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Troll_(Middle-earth)" title="Troll (Middle-earth)">
        Trolls
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">
        Middle-earth
       </a>
       feared the Sun even more, and with great reason: they turned to stone under its light. Only the later
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olog-hai" title="Olog-hai">
        Olog-hai
       </a>
       were able to move under the Sun.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Moon">
        Moon
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_and_Moon_(Middle-earth)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Moon">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Maia_(Middle-earth)" title="Maia (Middle-earth)">
        Maia
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Tilion" title="Tilion">
        Tilion
       </a>
       was chosen to guide the vessel of the Moon.
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        Isil was first wrought and made ready, and first rose into the realm of the stars, and was the elder of the new lights, as was Telperion of the Trees.
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Names of the Moon amongst the
       <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">
        Elves
       </a>
       included
       <i>
        Isil
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        The Sheen
       </i>
       , a name given to it by the
       <a href="/wiki/Vanyar" title="Vanyar">
        Vanyar
       </a>
       ;
       <i>
        Ithil
       </i>
       , the common name for the Moon in
       <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">
        Sindarin
       </a>
       , as seen in
       <i>
        Minas Ithil
       </i>
       (later
       <a href="/wiki/Minas_Morgul" title="Minas Morgul">
        Minas Morgul
       </a>
       ) and the
       <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">
        Gondorian
       </a>
       province of
       <a href="/wiki/Ithilien" title="Ithilien">
        Ithilien
       </a>
       ; and
       <i>
        Rána
       </i>
       , or
       <i>
        The Wayward
       </i>
       , a name given to the Moon by the
       <a href="/wiki/Noldor" title="Noldor">
        Noldor
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       A poetic name for the Moon was
       <i>
        The Silver Flower
       </i>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Gollum" title="Gollum">
        Gollum
       </a>
       referred to it as
       <i>
        The White Face
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Moon was more highly valued than the
       <a href="#Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       by the Elves, both because it came from the Elder Tree, and because it rose first.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">
        Other versions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_and_Moon_(Middle-earth)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other versions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the early versions of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Silmarillion" title="The Silmarillion">
         The Silmarillion
        </a>
       </i>
       as described in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Book_of_Lost_Tales" title="The Book of Lost Tales">
         The Book of Lost Tales 1
        </a>
       </i>
       , a part of
       <a href="/wiki/The_History_of_Middle-earth" title="The History of Middle-earth">
        The History of Middle-earth
       </a>
       series, the Sun was described in great detail as an immense island of fire. The Moon was described as an immense island of crystal. It was also said there that the youth
       <a href="/wiki/Tilion" title="Tilion">
        Tilion
       </a>
       , who guided the Moon, was said to secretly be in love with Arien, the maiden who guided the Sun, and that because he steered the Moon too close to the Sun the Moon was burned, causing the darker spots on the Moon (the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_maria" title="Lunar maria">
        Lunar maria
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2007)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Aulë devised
       <i>
        vírin
       </i>
       , a crystalline material from which he made a cup wherein the flower was set. The markings on the moon are caused as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%B3rien_(Vala)" title="Lórien (Vala)">
        Lórien
       </a>
       tried to pluck the "Rose of Silpion". The withered bough breaks, and the flower falls to the ground, and "a petal was crushed and tarnished" (HoME 1, p. 214). In a later version, the fruit of Laurelin also fell to the ground, when Aulë stumbled and its weight was too great for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tulkas" title="Tulkas">
        Tulkas
       </a>
       to bear alone (HoME 1, pp. 207, 215, 226; HoME 4, p. 57)
      </p>
      <p>
       In writings
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2007)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       not included in the Silmarillion tradition, Morgoth at one point was infatuated with Arien, and wanted to claim her as his wife: he is at one point even described as ravishing her, so she abandoned her body and 'died': the Sun after this for a while left its course, burning a large part of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arda_(Middle-earth)" title="Arda (Middle-earth)">
        Arda
       </a>
       the world (apparently creating the deserts of Far
       <a href="/wiki/Harad" title="Harad">
        Harad
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       In writings
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2007)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       which are older than the material from which the published
       <i>
        Silmarillion
       </i>
       was drawn, the Moon was described at one point as being created by
       <a href="/wiki/Morgoth" title="Morgoth">
        Morgoth
       </a>
       as a mockery of Arda the world, but this notion was abandoned.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/Round_World_version_of_the_Silmarillion" title="Round World version of the Silmarillion">
        Round World
       </a>
       version of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legendarium" title="Legendarium">
        legendarium
       </a>
       , the Sun and the Moon were not the fruit of the Two Trees, but actually preceded the creation of the Trees. Instead, the Trees preserved the light of the Sun before it was tainted by Melkor when he ravished
       <a href="/wiki/Arien" title="Arien">
        Arien
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2007)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Narsil">
        Narsil
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_and_Moon_(Middle-earth)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Narsil">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The name of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_weapons_and_armour#Narsil" title="List of Middle-earth weapons and armour">
        Narsil
       </a>
       , the sword of King
       <a href="/wiki/Elendil" title="Elendil">
        Elendil
       </a>
       (later reforged for
       <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">
        Aragorn
       </a>
       as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/And%C3%BAril" title="Andúril">
        Andúril
       </a>
       ), contains the elements
       <i>
        nar
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        thil,
       </i>
       "fire" and "white light" respectively in
       <a href="/wiki/Quenya" title="Quenya">
        Quenya
       </a>
       , referring to the Sun and Moon.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Man_in_the_Moon">
        The Man in the Moon
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_and_Moon_(Middle-earth)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Man in the Moon">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Man_in_the_Moon" title="Man in the Moon">
        Man in the Moon
       </a>
       is even described in those writings, as being an old Elf who secretly hid on the island of the Moon, and built a
       <a href="/wiki/Minaret" title="Minaret">
        minaret
       </a>
       there. This is alluded to further in Tolkien's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Roverandom" title="Roverandom">
         Roverandom
        </a>
       </i>
       , in which the Man in the Moon also lives in a minaret. Combined with the
       <a href="/wiki/Elf_(Middle-earth)" title="Elf (Middle-earth)">
        Elven
       </a>
       lore, as presented in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legendarium" title="Legendarium">
        legendarium
       </a>
       of
       <i>
        The Silmarillion
       </i>
       , the Man in the Moon of the
       <a href="/wiki/Hobbit" title="Hobbit">
        Hobbits
       </a>
       ' tales must have his origins in the legend of
       <a href="/wiki/Tilion" title="Tilion">
        Tilion
       </a>
       the Maia. In
       <a href="/wiki/The_Book_of_Lost_Tales" title="The Book of Lost Tales">
        The Book of Lost Tales
       </a>
       , his name is given as Uolë Kúvion, but the tale of how he came to live there was never fully told.
      </p>
