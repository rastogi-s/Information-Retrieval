     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Precession" title="Precession">
        Precession
       </a>
       is the change in orientation of an axis of rotation with respect to a
       <a href="/wiki/Plane_of_reference" title="Plane of reference">
        reference plane
       </a>
       . The
       <a href="/wiki/Orbit_of_the_Moon" title="Orbit of the Moon">
        orbit of the Moon
       </a>
       undergoes two important types of precessional motion.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_standstill.GIF">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="471" data-file-width="363" height="221" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Lunar_standstill.GIF/170px-Lunar_standstill.GIF" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Lunar_standstill.GIF/255px-Lunar_standstill.GIF 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Lunar_standstill.GIF/340px-Lunar_standstill.GIF 2x" width="170"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_standstill.GIF" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/Lunar_standstill" title="Lunar standstill">
          Lunar standstill
         </a>
         : every 18.6 years, the
         <a href="/wiki/Declination" title="Declination">
          declination
         </a>
         range of the
         <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
          Moon
         </a>
         reaches a maximum or minimum.
        </div>
       </div>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Nodal_precession">
        Nodal precession
        <span id="Nodal">
        </span>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_precession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nodal precession">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Nodal_precession" title="Nodal precession">
        Nodal precession
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="476" data-file-width="815" height="128" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg/220px-Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg/330px-Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg/440px-Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_eclipse_diagram-en.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_nodes" title="Lunar nodes">
          lunar nodes
         </a>
         are the points where the
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moon%27s_orbit" title="Moon's orbit">
          Moon's orbit
         </a>
         intersects the
         <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">
          ecliptic
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The first type of lunar precession is that of the plane of the
       <a href="/wiki/Orbit_of_the_Moon" title="Orbit of the Moon">
        Moon's orbit
       </a>
       . The period of the lunar
       <a href="/wiki/Nodal_precession" title="Nodal precession">
        nodal precession
       </a>
       is defined as the time it takes the ascending
       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_node" title="Orbital node">
        node
       </a>
       to move through 360° relative to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vernal_equinox" title="Vernal equinox">
        vernal equinox
       </a>
       . It is about 18.6 years and the direction of motion is westward, i.e. in the direction opposite to the Earth's orbit around the Sun if seen from the celestial north. This is the reason that a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Draconic_month" title="Draconic month">
        draconic month
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Nodal_period" title="Nodal period">
        nodal period
       </a>
       (the period the Moon takes to return to the same node in its orbit) is shorter than the sidereal month. After one nodal precession period, the number of draconic months exceeds the number of sidereal months by exactly one. This period is about 6,793 days (18.60 years).
      </p>
      <p>
       As a result of this nodal precession, the time for the Sun to return to the same lunar node, the
       <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_cycle" title="Eclipse cycle">
        eclipse year
       </a>
       , is about 18.6377 days shorter than a
       <a href="/wiki/Sidereal_year" title="Sidereal year">
        sidereal year
       </a>
       . The number of solar orbits (years) during one lunar nodal precession period equals the period of orbit (one year) divided by this difference, minus one.
       <sup>
        365.2422
       </sup>
       <i>
        ∕
       </i>
       <sub>
        18.6377
       </sub>
       − 1
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Moon_apsidal_precession.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1257" data-file-width="1446" height="191" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Moon_apsidal_precession.png/220px-Moon_apsidal_precession.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Moon_apsidal_precession.png/330px-Moon_apsidal_precession.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Moon_apsidal_precession.png/440px-Moon_apsidal_precession.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Moon_apsidal_precession.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/Apsidal_precession" title="Apsidal precession">
          Apsidal precession
         </a>
         occurs when the direction of the
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_axis" title="Major axis">
          major axis
         </a>
         of the Moon's
         <a href="/wiki/Elliptic_orbit" title="Elliptic orbit">
          elliptic orbit
         </a>
         rotates once every 8.85 years.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Apsidal_precession">
        Apsidal precession
        <span id="Apsidal">
        </span>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_precession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Apsidal precession">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Apsidal_precession" title="Apsidal precession">
        Apsidal precession
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The second kind of precession of the
       <a href="/wiki/Orbit_of_the_Moon" title="Orbit of the Moon">
        Moon's orbit
       </a>
       is that of the major axis of the Moon's
       <a href="/wiki/Elliptic_orbit" title="Elliptic orbit">
        elliptic orbit
       </a>
       (the line of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apsides" title="Apsides">
        apsides
       </a>
       from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perigee" title="Perigee">
        perigee
       </a>
       to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apogee" title="Apogee">
        apogee
       </a>
       ), which
       <a href="/wiki/Precession" title="Precession">
        precesses
       </a>
       eastward by 360° in approximately 8.85 years. This is the reason that an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anomalistic_month" title="Anomalistic month">
        anomalistic month
       </a>
       (the period the Moon moves from the perigee to the apogee and to the perigee again) is longer than the
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_month#Sidereal_month" title="Lunar month">
        sidereal month
       </a>
       (the period the Moon takes to completes one orbit with respect to the fixed stars). This
       <a href="/wiki/Apsidal_precession" title="Apsidal precession">
        apsidal precession
       </a>
       completes one rotation in the same time as the number of sidereal months exceeds the number of anomalistic months by exactly one, after about 3,233 days (8.85 years).
      </p>
