   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Regular moon
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       In astronomy, a
       <b>
        regular moon
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">
        natural satellite
       </a>
       following a relatively close and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prograde_orbit" title="Prograde orbit">
        prograde orbit
       </a>
       with little
       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_inclination" title="Orbital inclination">
        orbital inclination
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_eccentricity" title="Orbital eccentricity">
        eccentricity
       </a>
       . They are believed to have formed in orbit about their
       <a href="/wiki/Primary_(astronomy)" title="Primary (astronomy)">
        primary
       </a>
       , as opposed to
       <a href="/wiki/Irregular_moon" title="Irregular moon">
        irregular moons
       </a>
       , which were captured.
      </p>
      <p>
       There are at least
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_natural_satellites" title="List of natural satellites">
        57 regular satellites of the eight planets
       </a>
       :
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        one at Earth
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Moons_of_Jupiter" title="Moons of Jupiter">
        eight at Jupiter
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Moons_of_Saturn" title="Moons of Saturn">
        23 named regular moons at Saturn
       </a>
       (not counting hundreds or thousands of
       <a href="/wiki/Moonlet" title="Moonlet">
        moonlets
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Moons_of_Uranus" title="Moons of Uranus">
        18 known at Uranus
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Moons_of_Neptune" title="Moons of Neptune">
        7 small regular moons at Neptune
       </a>
       (Neptune's largest moon
       <a href="/wiki/Triton_(moon)" title="Triton (moon)">
        Triton
       </a>
       appears to have been captured). It is thought that
       <a href="/wiki/Moons_of_Pluto" title="Moons of Pluto">
        Pluto's five moons
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Moons_of_Haumea" title="Moons of Haumea">
        Haumea's two
       </a>
       were formed in orbit about those
       <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_planet" title="Dwarf planet">
        dwarf planets
       </a>
       out of
       <a href="/wiki/Collisional_family" title="Collisional family">
        debris created in giant collisions
       </a>
       .
      </p>
