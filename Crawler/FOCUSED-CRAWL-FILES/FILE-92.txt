      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar and Planetary Institute
       </b>
       (LPI) is a scientific research institute dedicated to study of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">
        solar system
       </a>
       , its formation, evolution, and current state. The Institute is part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Universities_Space_Research_Association" title="Universities Space Research Association">
        Universities Space Research Association
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (USRA) and is supported by the
       <a href="/wiki/Science_Mission_Directorate" title="Science Mission Directorate">
        Science Mission Directorate
       </a>
       of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Aeronautics_and_Space_Administration" title="National Aeronautics and Space Administration">
        National Aeronautics and Space Administration
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (NASA). Located at 3600 Bay Area Boulevard in Houston, Texas, the LPI maintains an extensive collection of lunar and planetary data, carries out education and public outreach programs, and offers meeting coordination and publishing services. The LPI sponsors and organizes several workshops and conferences throughout the year, including the
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_and_Planetary_Science_Conference" title="Lunar and Planetary Science Conference">
        Lunar and Planetary Science Conference
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (LPSC) held in March in the Houston area.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In his March 1968 speech at the Manned Spacecraft Center (MSC) in Houston, Texas, President
       <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson" title="Lyndon B. Johnson">
        Lyndon B. Johnson
       </a>
       announced the formation of the Lunar Science Institute (LSI).
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="725" data-file-width="750" height="213" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg/220px-LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg/330px-LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg/440px-LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LBJ_announces_Lunar_Science_Institute_in_1968.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Johnson at Manned Spacecraft Center in 1968
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <i>
         "We will welcome here all who are interested in the sciences of space. We will strengthen the cooperation between NASA and our universities. And we will set new patterns of scientific cooperation which will have profound effects on man's knowledge of his universe."
        </i>
        -- President Lyndon B. Johnson, March 1, 1968
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-4">
          [4]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       "The institute will provide a base for outside scientists, encouraging them to visit the Manned Spacecraft Center and use its laboratories, lunar photographs, and (ultimately) its rock samples. LSI is viewed as a major potential stimulus to lunar science at MSC and elsewhere."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       This announcement was the culmination of meetings and events involving NASA, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_National_Academy_of_Sciences" title="United States National Academy of Sciences">
        National Academy of Sciences
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Universities_Research_Association" title="Universities Research Association">
        Universities Research Association
       </a>
       and several major universities. Initially operated by the National Academy of Sciences, USRA took over the management of the Lunar Science Institute on December 11, 1969.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_(AS08-14-2506).jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3306" data-file-width="3997" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_%28AS08-14-2506%29.jpg/220px-Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_%28AS08-14-2506%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_%28AS08-14-2506%29.jpg/330px-Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_%28AS08-14-2506%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_%28AS08-14-2506%29.jpg/440px-Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_%28AS08-14-2506%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Apollo_8_Image_of_the_Moon_(AS08-14-2506).jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Moon from Apollo 8
         <br/>
         December 22, 1968
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Rubey" title="William Rubey">
        Dr. William W. Rubey
       </a>
       was appointed the first director of the Lunar Science Institute. Dr. Rubey led the institute through the transition to the new management operation under USRA. A program of visiting university-based scientists was established, the first symposium was organized, and the first lecture of the LSI seminar series was presented. The Lunar Science Institute was formally dedicated on January 4, 1970, at the former West Mansion on NASA Road 1 near the Manned Spacecraft Center.
      </p>
      <p>
       Dr. Thomas R. McGetchin was appointed director in 1977. Under his leadership, Dr. McGetchin expanded the focus of the Lunar Science Institute to include the study of the entire solar system, and the name was changed to the Lunar and Planetary Institute.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1991, under the leadership of Dr. David C. Black, the LPI moved into a new facility. This new building combined several USRA divisions and operations into one location. The building, dedicated in January 1992, provided more office, meeting, computer and library space and improved USRA-Houston operations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The LPI continues to operate in the USRA-Houston building under the direction of Dr.
       <a href="/wiki/Louise_Prockter" title="Louise Prockter">
        Louise Prockter
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="General_focus">
        General focus
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: General focus">

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Research topics of the LPI include the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Formation_and_evolution_of_the_solar_system" title="Formation and evolution of the solar system">
        formation and evolution of the solar system
       </a>
       , petrology and geochemistry of planetary materials and volatiles, planetary interiors,
       <a href="/wiki/Volcanism" title="Volcanism">
        volcanism
       </a>
       , tectonism, and impact cratering. Research interests range from
       <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(planet)" title="Mercury (planet)">
        Mercury
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/wiki/Pluto" title="Pluto">
        Pluto
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Icy_moon" title="Icy moon">
        icy moons
       </a>
       of the solar system.
      </p>

      <p>
       The LPI currently maintains a staff of resident scientists as well as visiting scientists, postdoctoral fellows, and graduate fellows. Resident scientists provide planetary science expertise necessary for the LPI to achieve its goals and maintain their scientific proficiency through peer-reviewed activities.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/nlsi/" rel="nofollow">
        Center for Lunar Science and Exploration
       </a>
       , a collaborative effort of the Lunar and Planetary Institute and the
       <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Space_Center" title="Johnson Space Center">
        Johnson Space Center
       </a>
       and an integral part of the Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute (SSERVI) (formerly the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NASA_Lunar_Science_Institute" title="NASA Lunar Science Institute">
        NASA Lunar Science Institute
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ), was established in 2009. The Center is designed to develop a core, multi-institutional lunar science program, provide scientific and technical expertise to NASA, support the development of a lunar science community, and develop lunar science education and outreach programs.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Analysis_groups">
        Analysis groups
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Analysis groups">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The LPI provides support for a number of NASA's community analysis groups, including:
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/captem/" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Curation and Analysis Planning Team for Extraterrestrial Materials
        </b>
       </a>
       (CAPTEM) - responsible for the care and distribution of all extraterrestrial samples collected by NASA including the Apollo lunar samples, materials from sample return missions, and solid materials of the solar system
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/leag/" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Lunar Exploration Analysis Group
        </b>
       </a>
       (LEAG) - responsible for analyzing scientific, technical, commercial, and operational issues associated with lunar exploration in response to requests by NASA
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://mepag.jpl.nasa.gov" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Mars Exploration Program Analysis Group
        </b>
       </a>
       (MEPAG) - responsible for providing science input for planning and prioritizing future
       <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">
        Mars
       </a>
       exploration activities for the next several decades
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/opag" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Outer Planets Assessment Group
        </b>
       </a>
       (OPAG) - responsible for identifying the scientific priorities and pathways for exploration in the outer solar system
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/osewg" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Optimizing Science and Exploration Working Group
        </b>
       </a>
       (OSEWG) - responsible for guiding exploration and science investigations during lunar sortie and outpost missions, with extensibility to future Mars missions
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/sbag" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Small Bodies Assessment Group
        </b>
       </a>
       (SBAG) - responsible for identifying scientific priorities and opportunities for the exploration of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asteroids" title="Asteroids">
        asteroids
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comets" title="Comets">
        comets
       </a>
       , interplanetary dust, small satellites, and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trans-Neptunian_objects" title="Trans-Neptunian objects">
        trans-Neptunian objects
       </a>
       and for providing scientific input on the utility of asteroids and comets in support of human space activities
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/vexag" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Venus Exploration Analysis Group
        </b>
       </a>
       (VEXAG) - responsible for identifying scientific priorities and strategy for the exploration of
       <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">
        Venus
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/mapsit" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Mapping and Planetary Spatial Infrastructure Team
        </b>
       </a>
       (MAPSIT) - ensuring that planetary data are usable for any purpose, now and in the future
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Summer_intern_programs">
        Summer intern programs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Summer intern programs">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The LPI hosts a
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/lpiintern" rel="nofollow">
        Summer Intern Program
       </a>
       providing undergraduates an opportunity to participate in cutting-edge research in the planetary sciences. LPI Summer Interns work one-on-one with scientists at the LPI or at the Johnson Space Center to complete research projects of current interest. The Summer Intern Program allows participants to experience a real research environment, to learn from leading planetary scientists, and to preview careers in research.
      </p>
      <p>
       From 2008 through 2013, the LPI also hosted a
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/lunar_intern" rel="nofollow">
        Lunar Exploration Summer Intern Program
       </a>
       designed to evaluate possible landing sites for robotic and human exploration missions. Interns worked with LPI scientific staff and other collaborators. The program was open to graduate students in geology, planetary science, and related fields, and undergraduates with at least 50 semester hours of credit. In 2015, a new
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/exploration_intern" rel="nofollow">
        Exploration Science Summer Intern Program
       </a>
       was established, building on the success of the previous program, but with a broader scope that includes both the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       and near-Earth asteroids.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Meetings">
        Meetings
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Meetings">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The LPI organizes and sponsors a number of planetary science workshops and conferences throughout the year in both domestic and international locations, including the annual Lunar and Planetary Science Conference. This important five-day meeting held in the Houston area in March brings together international specialists in petrology, geochemistry, geophysics, geology, and astronomy to present scientific findings in planetary science. The LPSC dates back to the days of the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo program
       </a>
       and the early meetings focusing on the study of the lunar samples. After four decades, this conference continues to thrive, drawing planetary scientists and researchers from around the world.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_public_outreach">
        Education and public outreach
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education and public outreach">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <p>
       The LPI has a long tradition of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_science" title="Space science">
        space science
       </a>
       education and public outreach through a number of programs and resources. This effort serves a wide variety of audiences, including K-12 students and educators, undergraduate, graduate, and postgraduate students, and the public in formal and informal venues and on local, regional, and national levels.
      </p>
      <p>
       These programs and resources include the following:
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/education/explore" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         <i>
          Explore!
         </i>
         Fun with Science
        </b>
       </a>
       - a program designed to bring space science into libraries and informal learning environments
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/education/skyFest" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         SkyFest
        </b>
       </a>
       - a series of free programs open to all ages that offers night sky viewing opportunities and hands-on activities about four times a year
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/education/lectures" rel="nofollow">
        <b>
         Cosmic Explorations: A Speakers Series
        </b>
       </a>
       - a series of free public lectures presented by international experts in space science (past lectures are made available online at the LPI website)
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Library">
        Library
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_and_Planetary_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Library">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/library/" rel="nofollow">
        LPI library
       </a>
       contains more than 60,000 cataloged books, documents, maps, films and videos, and print and electronic journals and newsletters. The subject emphasis of the collection is planetary science and geology, with limited collection development extending into the secondary support field of computer science remote sensing. There is an ongoing effort to scan and make available to the scientific community and the general public a number of out-of-print planetary science books, NASA documents and images, and related works. (These publications are copyright-free or made available with permission.)
      </p>
      <p>
       This collection is a NASA
       <a href="/wiki/Regional_Planetary_Image_Facility" title="Regional Planetary Image Facility">
        Regional Planetary Image Facility
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (RPIF) and includes photographs, maps, and other data from planetary missions including Apollo,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_Orbiter" title="Lunar Orbiter">
        Lunar Orbiter
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Clementine_(spacecraft)" title="Clementine (spacecraft)">
        Clementine
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mars_Pathfinder" title="Mars Pathfinder">
        Mars Pathfinder
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Voyager_1" title="Voyager 1">
        Voyager 1
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Voyager_2" title="Voyager 2">
        Voyager 2
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magellan_probe" title="Magellan probe">
        Magellan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Galileo_(spacecraft)" title="Galileo (spacecraft)">
        Galileo
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Mars_Global_Surveyor" title="Mars Global Surveyor">
        Mars Global Surveyor
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The library maintains a weblog called
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/library/whats_new/" rel="nofollow">
        What's New
       </a>
       that features new acquisitions in the library collection, tips on using library services and resources, and news about what is happening in the library and RPIF at the Lunar and Planetary Institute.
      </p>
