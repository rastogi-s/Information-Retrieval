      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Moon for Sale
        </b>
       </i>
       is a 2007 British
       <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">
        documentary film
       </a>
       directed and produced by Nick Davidson. The film premiered on
       <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">
        BBC Two
       </a>
       on April 10, 2007 and has played worldwide since. The primary focus of the documentary is on the value of the Moon to scientists and collectors. Dr.
       <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Schmitt" title="Harrison Schmitt">
        Harrison Schmitt
       </a>
       , an
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17" title="Apollo 17">
        Apollo 17
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">
        astronaut
       </a>
       , is seen discussing harvesting
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helium_3" title="Helium 3">
        Helium 3
       </a>
       on the moon to meet Earth's energy needs. Also discussed in the documentary is the black market industry in stolen Apollo-era
       <a href="/wiki/Moon_rock" title="Moon rock">
        moon rocks
       </a>
       , which sell for as much as $5 million a
       <a href="/wiki/Gram" title="Gram">
        gram
       </a>
       . Specifically, the viewer is introduced to a
       <a href="/wiki/Sting_operation" title="Sting operation">
        sting operation
       </a>
       known as Operation Lunar Eclipse, where for the first time a moon rock, the
       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">
        Honduras
       </a>
       Apollo 17 Goodwill Moon Rock, is recovered by US federal agents.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
