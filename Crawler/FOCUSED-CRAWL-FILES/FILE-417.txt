      <p>
       A
       <b>
        total
        <a href="/wiki/Lunar_eclipse" title="Lunar eclipse">
         lunar eclipse
        </a>
       </b>
       took place on July 6, 1982.
      </p>
      <p>
       The moon passed through the
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_central_lunar_eclipses" title="List of central lunar eclipses">
        center of the Earth's shadow
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Visibility">
        Visibility
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=July_1982_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Visibility">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       It was seen completely over North and South America, seen rising over Australia, and setting over Western Africa.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lunar_eclipse_from_moon-1982Jul06.png">
        <img alt="Lunar eclipse from moon-1982Jul06.png" data-file-height="831" data-file-width="867" height="307" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Lunar_eclipse_from_moon-1982Jul06.png/320px-Lunar_eclipse_from_moon-1982Jul06.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Lunar_eclipse_from_moon-1982Jul06.png/480px-Lunar_eclipse_from_moon-1982Jul06.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Lunar_eclipse_from_moon-1982Jul06.png/640px-Lunar_eclipse_from_moon-1982Jul06.png 2x" width="320"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Related_eclipses">
        Related eclipses
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=July_1982_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Related eclipses">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are seven eclipses in 1982, the maximum possible, including 4 partial solar eclipses:
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_January_25,_1982" title="Solar eclipse of January 25, 1982">
        January 25
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_July_20,_1982" title="Solar eclipse of July 20, 1982">
        July 20
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_June_21,_1982" title="Solar eclipse of June 21, 1982">
        June 21
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_December_15,_1982" title="Solar eclipse of December 15, 1982">
        December 15
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Inex_series">
        Inex series
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=July_1982_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Inex series">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Inex" title="Inex">
        inex series
       </a>
       repeats eclipses 20 days short of 29 years, repeating on average every 10571.95 days. This period is equal to 358 lunations (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synodic_month" title="Synodic month">
        synodic months
       </a>
       ) and 388.5
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Draconic_month" title="Draconic month">
        draconic months
       </a>
       . Saros series increment by one on successive Inex events and repeat at alternate ascending and descending lunar nodes.
      </p>
      <p>
       This period is 383.6734
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anomalistic_month" title="Anomalistic month">
        anomalistic months
       </a>
       (the period of the Moon's elliptical orbital precession). Despite the average 0.05 time-of-day shift between subsequent events, the variation of the Moon in its elliptical orbit at each event causes the actual eclipse time to vary significantly.
      </p>
      <p>
       All events in this series listed below and more are total lunar eclipses.
      </p>
