   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Odyssey Moon
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

      <p>
       On 6 December 2007,
       <b>
        Odyssey Moon
       </b>
       was the first team to register for the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize
       </a>
       competition,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space1_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-space1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       an event that hopes to rekindle the efforts of humans to return to the moon. The competition is referred to as "Moon 2.0" and is composed of other private organizations like Odyssey Moon Limited, the commercial lunar enterprise that makes up this team.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-space-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Each team will be competing for a $20 million first prize, a $5 million second prize, and additional $5 million in (potential) bonuses.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xprize_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-xprize-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2328" data-file-width="3098" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg/220px-MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg/330px-MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg/440px-MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MCSB_-_LADEE.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Odyssey Moon will utilize the basic design of this NASA hover test vehicle.
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfn_4-0">
          <a href="#cite_note-sfn-4">
           [4]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Odyssey Moon Limited is based on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Isle_of_Man" title="The Isle of Man">
        The Isle of Man
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-space-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and is the design of
       <a href="/wiki/Robert_D._Richards" title="Robert D. Richards">
        Robert D. Richards
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xprize_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-xprize-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       His goals include developing the first commercial enterprise that utilizes the energy and resources on the moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       To achieve this end, the team enlisted the part-time consultant services of
       <a href="/wiki/Alan_Stern" title="Alan Stern">
        Alan Stern
       </a>
       , NASA's former top-rank planetary scientist.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On 22 September 2008, another veteran of NASA joined Odyssey Moon.
       <a href="/wiki/Jay_F._Honeycutt" title="Jay F. Honeycutt">
        Jay F. Honeycutt
       </a>
       was named president and will be responsible for all programs and commercial launch operations. He brings a great deal of expertise in managing large scale engineering operations. His experience at NASA was diverse. He was director of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">
        Kennedy Space Center
       </a>
       for several years and was director of Shuttle Management and Operations for more than five years. Outside NASA, another part of his forty years of professional experience was as president of
       <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin" title="Lockheed Martin">
        Lockheed Martin
       </a>
       Space Operations from 1997-2004.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jay_pr_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jay_pr-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
          <span title="The text near this tag is dated.">
           needs update
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The team's goals are to build and deploy a robotic lander that will deliver exploration as well as scientific payloads to the moon. The new lander/spacecraft has been dubbed "MoonOne (M-1)".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       These efforts have been contracted to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MacDonald_Dettwiler" title="MacDonald Dettwiler">
        MacDonald Dettwiler
       </a>
       , a Canadian corporation with a successful history of providing technical space solutions for several
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       projects including the
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">
        Space Shuttle
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">
        International Space Station
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-space-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
          <span title="The text near this tag is dated.">
           needs update
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Planetary_Society" title="The Planetary Society">
        The Planetary Society
       </a>
       , an international space interest group co-founded by
       <a href="/wiki/Carl_Sagan" title="Carl Sagan">
        Carl Sagan
       </a>
       , joined Odyssey Moon's efforts in 2007, specifically with public outreach and coordination between public and private organizations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space1_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-space1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Colin_Pillinger" title="Colin Pillinger">
        Colin Pillinger
       </a>
       , a scientist with a background in studying meteorites, led the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">
        European Space Agency
       </a>
       's failed
       <a href="/wiki/Beagle_2" title="Beagle 2">
        Beagle 2
       </a>
       Mars lander project in 2003. In 2009 he was in discussion with Odyssey Moon regarding the use of an identical version of Beagle's most powerful instrument on their lander.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfn_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-sfn-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
          <span title="The text near this tag is dated.">
           needs update
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
