      <p>
       <b>
        Usagi Tsukino
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         月野 うさぎ
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Tsukino Usagi
        </i>
        , renamed "Serena Tsukino", "Bunny Tsukino" in some English adaptations)
       </span>
       , better known as
       <b>
        Sailor Moon
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         セーラームーン
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Sērā Mūn
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       , is a fictional
       <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">
        superheroine
       </a>
       who is the
       <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">
        protagonist
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Title_role" title="Title role">
        titular character
       </a>
       of the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon" title="Sailor Moon">
         Sailor Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       manga series written by
       <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">
        Naoko Takeuchi
       </a>
       . She is introduced in chapter #1, "Usagi – Sailor Moon" (originally published in Japan's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Nakayoshi" title="Nakayoshi">
         Nakayoshi
        </a>
       </i>
       magazine on December 28, 1991), as a carefree schoolgirl who can transform into Sailor Moon, the
       <a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">
        de facto
       </a>
       leader of the Sailor Soldiers. Initially believing herself to be an Earthling, she is later revealed to be the princess of the Moon Kingdom, and she subsequently discovers her birth name,
       <b>
        Princess Serenity
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         プリンセス・セレニティ
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Purinsesu Sereniti
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <i>
        Sailor Moon
       </i>
       , Usagi meets
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM_Luna" title="SM Luna">
        Luna
       </a>
       , a magical talking black cat that is searching for the Moon Princess. Luna reveals that Usagi is destined to save Earth from the forces of evil and gives her a brooch to transform into Sailor Moon. She asks Usagi to form the Sailor Soldiers, find their princess and protect the "
       <a href="#Silver_Crystal">
        Silver Crystal
       </a>
       ". As Usagi matures, she becomes a powerful warrior and protects her adopted home planet, Earth, from villains who wish to harm it. Usagi is depicted as carefree and cheerful when at ease but promptly serious and strategic-minded when fighting.
      </p>
      <p>
       As the protagonist, Usagi appears in every episode, film, television special, and
       <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">
        original video animation
       </a>
       (OVA) of the anime adaptations,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(anime)" title="Sailor Moon (anime)">
         Sailor Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Crystal" title="Sailor Moon Crystal">
         Sailor Moon Crystal
        </a>
       </i>
       ; as well as the live action adaptation,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Pretty_Guardian_Sailor_Moon_(live-action_series)" title="Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon (live-action series)">
         Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       . She also
       <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">
        cameos
       </a>
       in the sister series
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Codename:_Sailor_V" title="Codename: Sailor V">
         Codename: Sailor V
        </a>
       </i>
       . She has been the subject of parodies and has appeared in special events. Most Western audiences were introduced to Usagi appearing in the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(anime)" title="Sailor Moon (anime)">
         Sailor Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       anime, which is an adaptation of the manga series. Usagi's critical reception has been largely positive and she is recognized as one of the most important and popular female superheroes of all time.

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">
        Appearances
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Appearances">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Usagi is first introduced as living the life of a normal teenage
       <a href="/wiki/Student" title="Student">
        schoolgirl
       </a>
       in 20th-century
       <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">
        Tokyo
       </a>
       . Although well-meaning, she is an underachieving, accident-prone
       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/crybaby" title="wikt:crybaby">
        crybaby
       </a>
       . One day, Usagi encounters a mysterious cat, who later reveals herself to be
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luna_(Sailor_Moon)" title="Luna (Sailor Moon)">
        Luna
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/Wise_old_man" title="Wise old man">
        mentor archetype
       </a>
       who introduces Usagi to her new heroic role. Luna gives Usagi a magical brooch and explains how to use it to transform into Sailor Moon (the Soldier of Love and Justice); she tells Usagi that she is a Sailor Soldier who must fight for peace and find the rest of the Sailor Soldiers, as well as their princess. Usagi is a reluctant heroine at first,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grigsby_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grigsby-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       she grows more confident and mature over time. As Sailor Moon, she sets out to fight the villains from her
       <a href="/wiki/Reincarnation" title="Reincarnation">
        past life
       </a>
       and to protect the Earth using the Silver Crystal. This provides most of the conflict in both the manga and the anime.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Usagi_Manga.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="347" data-file-width="287" height="206" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6d/Usagi_Manga.jpg/170px-Usagi_Manga.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6d/Usagi_Manga.jpg/255px-Usagi_Manga.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/6d/Usagi_Manga.jpg 2x" width="170"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Usagi_Manga.jpg" title="Enlarge">

      <p>
       Usagi lives in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azabu-J%C5%ABban,_Minato,_Tokyo" title="Azabu-Jūban, Minato, Tokyo">
        Azabu Jūban
       </a>
       with her mother, Ikuko Tsukino; her father, Kenji Tsukino; and her brother,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_minor_Sailor_Moon_characters#Shingo_Tsukino" title="List of minor Sailor Moon characters">
        Shingo Tsukino
       </a>
       . These names reflect those of
       <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">
        Naoko Takeuchi
       </a>
       's real-life family members.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Out of all the Sailor Soldiers, only Usagi and
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Venus" title="Sailor Venus">
        Minako Aino
       </a>
       live in a conventional
       <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_family" title="Nuclear family">
        nuclear family
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (April 2016)">
           original research?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       and Usagi is the only one known to have a sibling.
      </p>
      <p>
       Usagi has a boyfriend named
       <a href="/wiki/Tuxedo_Mask" title="Tuxedo Mask">
        Mamoru Chiba
       </a>
       (also known as Tuxedo Mask). Mamoru and Usagi's relationship is a significant part of Usagi's
       <a href="/wiki/Personal_life" title="Personal life">
        personal life
       </a>
       , as well as the series as a whole. Mamoru and Usagi date for a long time in the series and the love they share helps her through many challenges. In the anime adaptation, Mamoru gives Usagi a heart-shaped promise ring just before he leaves for America. The ring represents a promise to Usagi that they will eventually marry.
      </p>
      <p>
       Once she forms the Sailor Soldiers, Usagi learns that she comes from a race belonging to the Silver Millennium, and that her mother sent her to Earth to be reincarnated. In the second series, Usagi learns that she will give birth to a daughter (
       <a href="/wiki/Chibiusa" title="Chibiusa">
        Chibiusa
       </a>
       ) by her boyfriend and future husband. She also discovers that she will become a "Sovereign of the Earth", known as Neo-Queen Serenity, by the 30th century.
      </p>
      <p>
       Usagi loves sweet foods and they easily they distract her.
       <a href="/wiki/Ice_cream" title="Ice cream">
        Ice cream
       </a>
       is listed as a hobby of hers in the
       <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">
        manga
       </a>
       , and her favorite subject is listed as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_and_consumer_science" title="Family and consumer science">
        home economics
       </a>
       . She is said to dislike
       <a href="/wiki/Carrot" title="Carrot">
        carrots
       </a>
       , and is poor with both English and
       <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">
        mathematics
       </a>
       . Usagi is shown to be a genuine friend. She also has a talent for brown-nosing when needed, and for crying to get what she wants. She is
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dental_phobia" title="Dental phobia">
        afraid of dentists
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">
        ghosts
       </a>
       and lightning, and her greatest dream is to be a
       <a href="/wiki/Bride" title="Bride">
        bride
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol_10_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-vol_10-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       She is apparently a member of the Manga Drawing Club at school,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act35_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-act35-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (April 2016)">
           page needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       though her skill level varies widely when shown in the anime.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [note 1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       She stands 150 cm tall.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animeI_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-animeI-12">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (April 2016)">
           page needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the manga and anime, Mamoru refers to her as
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Dango" title="Dango">
         odango
        </a>
       </i>
       (a kind of rice dumpling), based on her distinctive hairstyle. At first, this is always accompanied with the suffix
       <i>
        ‑atama
       </i>
       , meaning "head", but this is gradually dropped. Usagi hates the name at first, but it develops into a sign of affection as they become close. Later in the series
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Uranus" title="Sailor Uranus">
        Haruka
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Star_Fighter" title="Sailor Star Fighter">
        Seiya
       </a>
       , other important male (or androgynous) figures in her life, adopt the name as well. Since there is no North American equivalent to
       <i>
        odango
       </i>
       , the original English adaptation almost always used the phrases "meatball head" or "moon face".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the Tokyopop Manga adaptation, Mamoru calls Usagi "buns", which is an approximation of
       <i>
        odango
       </i>
       and is short for "Bunny". In the Viz Media English adaptation, she is referred to as "bun head".
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">
        Variations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Usagi's character is inconsistent between versions the series. In the manga she starts out as a crybaby, but quickly matures and learns to make decisions for herself.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol1_14-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-vol1-14">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act52_15-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-act52-15">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The series often portrays Usagi as lazy rather than lacking intelligence, such as when she passes her high school exams without trouble when threatened with separation from her friends.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act35_10-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-act35-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(anime)" title="Sailor Moon (anime)">
        original anime
       </a>
       often portrays Usagi as more childlike.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       She often bickers with her daughter Chibiusa, but can show just as much caring as her manga counterpart.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (November 2016)">
           original research?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       She does evolve during the course of the series, but other than the last few episodes of each story arc, she generally lacks the maturity she has in the manga.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act52_15-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-act52-15">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the live-action series, Usagi differs slightly from her manga and anime counterparts. She is more outgoing and extroverted, and makes friends very easily. This immediately puts her personality in conflict with the other Sailor Soldiers, each of whom is solitary to some degree. She rarely uses
       <a href="/wiki/Formal_grammar" title="Formal grammar">
        formal grammar
       </a>
       with those of her age (though she does with adults), and refers to everyone as "
       <i>
        given name
       </i>
       -chan" (which is very informal and a way of expressing closeness). She teases Ami when Ami continues calling her "Tsukino-san" (a formal way of speaking to classmates), saying that it is as if they are not friends.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Every time a new Sailor Soldier appears, Usagi immediately tries to make friends, even though almost all of them resist. However, Usagi eventually makes the other Sailor Soldiers realize that they are stronger together than alone. Usagi also has a habit of forcing her interests on the people that she makes friends with. This is prominent in her relationship with Rei, where she repeatedly tries to get her to sing.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       Being a character with a long lifetime (spanning the ancient Silver Millennium era and 30th century), as well as multiple incarnations, special powers and transformations, Usagi has various aliases such as Princess Serenity, Sailor Moon, Princess Sailor Moon, Super Sailor Moon, Eternal Sailor Moon, and Neo-Queen Serenity.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sailor_Moon">
        Sailor Moon
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sailor Moon">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The series often refers to Usagi's Sailor Soldier identity, Sailor Moon, as the "Soldier of Love and Justice", and once as the "Soldier of Mystery".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (April 2016)">
           page needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Throughout most of the series, Sailor Moon wears a white and blue
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_fuku" title="Sailor fuku">
         sailor fuku
        </a>
       </i>
       uniform; white and reddish‑pink gloves and boots; and crescent‑moon earrings. She also wears red hairpieces and white
       <a href="/wiki/Barrette" title="Barrette">
        barrettes
       </a>
       resembling feathers, both of which can be used for minor attacks. Her personality is no different from when she is a civilian, though her Sailor Moon form has certain powers.
      </p>
      <p>
       The names for Sailor Moon's attacks center around the moon, love, mystery and light. Starting out as a frightened, reluctant girl often in need of help, she gradually accepts her full identity. She eventually becomes the most powerful Sailor Soldier in the galaxy, but her capacity for caring for others is shown to be more powerful still.
      </p>
      <p>
       Salior Moon's appearance and title change at key points when she grows stronger or gains additional powers. The first change takes place during the third major story arc – act 30 of the manga and episode 111 of the anime – when she obtains the Holy Grail and becomes
       <i>
        Super Sailor Moon
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">

          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In this form, her costume becomes more ornate and her powers are increased. At first she is unable to take this form without the Grail, but she later gains this ability permanently. This happens when Pegasus grants both her and Sailor Chibi Moon new transformation brooches – in arc 34 of the manga and in episode 130 of the anime. However, in this "Super" version, her white back bow is shorter than in the Holy Grail version.
      </p>
      <p>
       Sailor Moon receives her third and final form at the end of the fourth major story arc, as the combined power of the other Sailor Soldiers transforms her into
       <i>
        Eternal Sailor Moon
       </i>
       , whom
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diana_(Sailor_Moon)#Diana" title="Diana (Sailor Moon)">

        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Her uniform is radically altered, including the addition of two pairs of angelic wings on her back which replace her back bow.
      </p>
      <p>
       The plot of
       <i>
        Sailor Moon
       </i>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Princess_Serenity">
        Princess Serenity
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Princess Serenity">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PrincessSerenity.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="338" data-file-width="295" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/23/PrincessSerenity.jpg/170px-PrincessSerenity.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/23/PrincessSerenity.jpg/255px-PrincessSerenity.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/23/PrincessSerenity.jpg 2x" width="170"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PrincessSerenity.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Princess Serenity as seen in the manga. Unlike the anime, Serenity is almost always pictured with silver hair [in this version]
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Princess Serenity
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         プリンセス・セレニティ
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Purinsesu Sereniti
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       is a past incarnation of Sailor Moon that lived in the Moon Kingdom during the age of Silver Millennium. She was the daughter of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Serenity" title="Queen Serenity">
        Queen Serenity
       </a>
       , who ruled Silver Millennium and watched over the Earth. Princess Serenity's guardians and closest friends were
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Mercury" title="Sailor Mercury">
        Sailor Mercury
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Mars" title="Sailor Mars">
        Sailor Mars
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Jupiter" title="Sailor Jupiter">
        Sailor Jupiter
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Venus" title="Sailor Venus">
        Sailor Venus
       </a>
       , who were princesses of their own respective planets that sometimes lived on the moon. On one of her visits to Earth, she met and fell in love with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Endymion" title="Prince Endymion">
        Endymion
       </a>
       , the crown prince of Earth.
      </p>
      <p>
       During the attack that caused the Moon Kingdom's downfall, Prince Endymion died protecting Serenity. In the manga she then commits suicide out of grief, while in the anime Queen Metaria killed them both. Serenity's mother, the Queen, was able to seal away the evil that had created the attack, but everyone involved was killed. Before her own death, the Queen used the Silver Crystal to give her daughter (and others) another chance at life, hoping that Serenity and Endymion would be able to find happiness together. In the live-action series, it is Princess Serenity herself who destroys the Moon Kingdom when Endymion was killed during the war.
      </p>
      <p>
       Serenity reincarnates as Usagi Tsukino in the 20th century. Usagi occasionally takes the form of Princess Serenity during the series, often at climactic moments when more strength is needed than Sailor Moon can usually access. Usagi discovers her identity as a princess in act 9 of the manga, episode 34 of the anime, and act 25 of the
       <a href="/wiki/Live_action" title="Live action">
        live-action
       </a>
       series.
      </p>
      <p>
       While Takeuchi draws Usagi with white, yellow, and even pink hair, Serenity almost always has white hair. In the anime, both characters are always blond. In the live-action series, Serenity has black hair and brown eyes, just like Usagi, and she wears her hair straight down rather than in pigtails. This makes her identity more ambiguous before the storyline reveals her to be Usagi.
      </p>
      <p>
       At climactic moments, Serenity sometimes gains a pair of functioning angelic wings. She does this during the final battles of
       <i>
        SuperS
       </i>
       , after she jumps off a tower to save Chibiusa and the two of them collide with Pegasus while falling. It also happens in
       <i>
        Sailor Stars
       </i>
       during the fight with the fully possessed Galaxia when she grabs the Sword of Sealing. It remains unclear if this power comes from her past life, if it belongs to Usagi herself, or if it came from Pegasus and the Sword of Sealing.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Princess_Sailor_Moon">
        Princess Sailor Moon
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Princess Sailor Moon">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:128px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Princesssailormoon.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="190" data-file-width="126" height="190" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/78/Princesssailormoon.jpg" width="126"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Princesssailormoon.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Princess Sailor Moon in
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Pretty_Guardian_Sailor_Moon_(live-action_series)" title="Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon (live-action series)">
           Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon
          </a>
         </i>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Princess Sailor Moon is a powerful combination of Sailor Moon and Princess Serenity that only exists in the live-action series. She is introduced when Usagi is possessed by the spirit of her former self. She originally appears after Queen Beryl takes the shitennou hostage in exchange for Mamoru. Sailor Moon transforms into Princess Sailor Moon and stops Queen Beryl using her sword.
      </p>
      <p>
       Princess Sailor Moon is not the same person as Usagi and they have different personalities. Princess Sailor Moon shows no remorse for the fate of the Four Kings of Heaven and she refers to Mamoru as "Endymion" rather than using his civilian name. She is always angry, and has no misgivings about causing death or destruction. In one act, Usagi's friend Naru accidentally gets too close to Usagi and has to go to the hospital as a result. It is also shown that Princess Serenity has full control of the upgrade. Though smiling in most promotional material for the series, Princess Sailor Moon does not smile in the series itself until the end, after reconciling with Usagi.
      </p>
      <p>
       During a confrontation with her current self as Princess Sailor Moon, Serenity tells Usagi that she would have no qualms about destroying the world if Endymion were taken from her again. Usagi pleads with Serenity not to overuse her powers, but Serenity refuses. Afraid that she will eventually destroy the world, Usagi tires to suppress her powers. Usagi's internal conflict forces her to undergo endurance training to keep her powers and Princess Serenity persona at bay. Usagi initially succeeds but avoiding negative thoughts. However, when she is forced to kill a possessed Mamoru, Serenity overcomes Usagi's resistance and transforms into Princess Sailor Moon. Serenity even summons her own minions to fight the other Sailor Guardians to prevent them from stopping her. Princess Sailor Moon successfully destroys the world once again, but Serenity eventually realizes the extent to which she is responsible for this.
      </p>
      <p>
       Princess Sailor Moon has a sword that can deflect enemy attacks or unleash devastating projectiles. The sword also doubles as a harp with invisible strings that Princess Sailor Moon plays while mourning her lost prince. The harp's main power is the ability to heal people and the land. Other than the healing powers, the exact effect of playing the harp is unclear, but it often causes her Silver Crystal to feed the power of Queen Metaria, accelerating the devastation of the planet.
      </p>
      <p>
       As with other characters unique to the live-action series, Takeuchi designed Princess Sailor Moon's outfit. Her sailor outfit is considerably more elaborate than Sailor Moon's, and included pearls on her gloves and lace on her skirt.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="Please supply an ISBN for this book.">
           ISBN missing
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In fact, there a very similar design on the back cover of
       <i>
        Sailor Moon: Short Stories
       </i>
       volume 1.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Neo-Queen_Serenity">
        Neo-Queen Serenity
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Neo-Queen Serenity">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       During the second major
       <a href="/wiki/Story_arc" title="Story arc">
        story arc
       </a>
       , it is revealed that Usagi, as Serenity, will eventually become the
       <a href="/wiki/Queen_regnant" title="Queen regnant">
        queen regnant
       </a>
       of a new Silver Millennium called Crystal Tokyo, in the 30th century. She is first seen in this future form in act 16 of the manga and episode 68 of the anime. Usagi learns that she will be given the title "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sovereign" title="Sovereign">
        Sovereign
       </a>
       of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth_(planet)" title="Earth (planet)">
        Earth
       </a>
       ", and Mamoru will become
       <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">
        King
       </a>
       Endymion alongside her. It is stated in the anime that she becomes Neo-Queen Serenity after warding off a second Ice Age, though the specifics of this are never discussed.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       This incarnation is shown to be more mature than the present day Usagi, though she is still childish in some ways. For example, in episode 104, Chibiusa gives the Sailor Soldiers a letter from the future, in which the Queen asks them to train her, but the letter is simplistic and contains almost no
       <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">
        kanji
       </a>
       . In episode 146, Diana says that the King and Queen would sometimes play sick to get out of things. Letters she sends though the Door of Space-Time to Chibiusa are sometimes signed with a drawing of herself (and sometimes King Endymion) instead of a name.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the manga, Neo-Queen Serenity tells the present-day Sailor Soldiers that after she became queen, she lost her power as a Sailor Soldier. In the second arc of the anime she does not transform (into Sailor Moon) even when the others do. However, she is seen showing great powers in a flashback when the King Endymion of the future describes the great feats of Neo-Queen Serenity during the time she brought about peace.
      </p>
      <p>
       She wears an altered version of the dress she wore as a princess. The shoulder pieces are omitted and a large, wing-shaped bow replaces the smaller one of the princess outfit. In the manga, Neo-Queen Serenity's dress is similar to her past form's outfit. She also wears a crown and new earrings. The crescent moon is always visible on her forehead, just as it is with her princess form. Her face and facial expressions are drawn to look more mature than the 20th century Usagi, but her
       <a href="/wiki/Bun_(hairstyle)#Double_bun" title="Bun (hairstyle)">
        iconic hairstyle
       </a>
       is retained.
      </p>
      <p>
       This form is the one that Chibiusa considers as truly being her mother, while she sees the Usagi of the past as a sister figure.
      </p>

       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">

        ]

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Silver_Crystal">
        Silver Crystal
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Silver Crystal">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <i>
        Legendary Silver Crystal
       </i>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         「幻の銀水晶」
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Maboroshi no Ginzuishō
        </i>
        , lit. "Phantom Silver Crystal")
       </span>
       is a magical crystal that only the members of the Moon dynasty can use. The first English-dubbed anime sometimes calls it the
       <i>
        Imperium Silver Crystal
       </i>
       as well as various other names.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Crystal possesses tremendous power, capable of reviving an entire world from ruin. However, the strain of using such power often costs the user her life, as the power derives from the
       <a href="/wiki/Energy_(esotericism)" title="Energy (esotericism)">
        life force
       </a>
       of the Moon dynasty.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (March 2017)">
           original research?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The anime shows this happening three times. The first time is in a flashback with Queen Serenity; the second time when Usagi defeats Queen Metaria at the end of season one; and finally in the
       <i>
        R
       </i>
       movie. It is shown as the source of Queen Serenity's power during the age of Silver Millennium, with Usagi Tsukino and Chibiusa each going on to inherit the Crystal in some form. However, it is also shown in the
       <i>
        S
       </i>
       movie that the power of all the Senshi working in unison allows Sailor Moon to use the Crystal's full strength without the result being fatal (although she was still exhausted afterwards from using it).
      </p>
      <p>
       Both the anime, manga and second anime series commonly portray the Silver Crystal as possibly the single most powerful artifact in the universe, able to focus the energy of its wielder to perform magnificent feats. However, several artifacts rival it in strength, including the Black Crystal of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_Phantom" title="Death Phantom">
        Death Phantom
       </a>
       , and the Saffer Crystal of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Galaxia" title="Sailor Galaxia">
        Sailor Galaxia
       </a>
       . In the fifth series of the anime, the crystal also appears to double as the Star Seed of Sailor Moon, which was hinted at in the
       <i>
        R
       </i>
       movie, and the manga implies that it is her Sailor Crystal. It takes on a multitude of shapes, including round, diamond, rose, heart, star, and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lotus_(plant)" title="Lotus (plant)">
        lotus
       </a>
       , and it turns pink while stored within the brooches of Sailor Moon and Sailor Chibi Moon.
      </p>
      <p>
       Because Chibiusa comes from the future – having eventually inherited the Silver Crystal from Usagi – two versions of it exist in the series. After the first and second story arcs, the owners of the crystals keep them in their respective transformation brooches and only remove them in times of urgent need.
      </p>
      <p>
       The original anime features seven
       <a href="/wiki/Rainbow" title="Rainbow">
        rainbow
       </a>
       crystals that had the
       <a href="/wiki/Dark_Kingdom#Seven_Great_Youma" title="Dark Kingdom">
        Seven Great Monsters
       </a>
       (the most powerful monsters in the
       <a href="/wiki/Dark_Kingdom" title="Dark Kingdom">
        Dark Kingdom
       </a>
       ) sealed into them. They were sealed within seven separate shards of the Silver Crystal using Queen Serenity's power. They were then carried to Earth where they were reincarnated centuries later, with no memories of their prior existences. All seven rainbow crystals (and, as in the manga, one of Usagi's tears) are needed to recombine to form the Silver Crystal.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Conception_and_creation">
        Conception and creation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Conception and creation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Usagi and
       <i>
        Sailor Moon
       </i>
       series evolved from Naoko Takeuchi's earlier one-shot series called
       <i>
        Codename: Sailor V
       </i>
       . In Takeuchi's first proposal for the
       <i>
        Sailor Moon
       </i>
       series, each of the five heroines had a completely unique outfit. It was eventually decided that they would instead wear uniforms based on a single theme, whose design was closest to Sailor Moon's original costume concept. Sailor Moon's original had some small differences, including color changes, an exposed midriff, and ribbons around the gloves and boots. She also had a mask, which did appear in a few chapters of the manga before being discarded.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (April 2016)">
           page needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       These aspects of Sailor Moon's costume are shown in multiple pieces of early artwork, along with a gun and cloak, which were also parts of the original concept.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-devel_26-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-devel-26">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Takeuchi based Usagi's signature hairstyle on a "good luck charm" she had during her studies as a university student. Takeuchi would put her own hair up in
       <i>
        odango
       </i>
       before difficult classes or exams.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (April 2016)">
           page needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Sailor Moon has pink hair in the initial sketches, but by the intermediate stages of development, Takeuchi planned to have the character's hair be blond in civilian form and change to silver when she transformed. Her editor, Fumio Osano, told her that silver hair would be too plain for cover art.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-devel_26-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-devel-26">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Despite this, stylistic use of differently colored hair does sometimes appear in later artwork, and the concept of the heroines' hair changing color when transformed is used in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Pretty_Guardian_Sailor_Moon_(live-action_series)" title="Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon (live-action series)">
         Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Of all the Sailor Soldiers, Usagi's personality is closest to Takeuchi's own personality at the time
       <i>
        Sailor Moon
       </i>
       was created.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">
        kanji
       </a>
       of Usagi's surname translate as "moon"
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         月
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         tsuki
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       and "field"
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         野
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         no
        </i>
        )
       </span>
       . Her given name is in
       <a href="/wiki/Hiragana" title="Hiragana">
        hiragana
       </a>
       <i>
        usagi
       </i>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         うさぎ
        </span>
        )
       </span>
       and so its meaning is not inherent, but the word
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">
         兎
        </span>
        )
       </span>
       means "rabbit" and this is used as a pun frequently throughout the series, including her
       <a href="/wiki/Bun_(hairstyle)" title="Bun (hairstyle)">
        hairstyle
       </a>
       and possessions. Her name is structured as a
       <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">
        pun
       </a>
       , as the syllable "no" indicates a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Possessive_case" title="Possessive case">
        possessive
       </a>
       , so her name can also be understood as "Rabbit of the Moon." This derives from a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_folktale" title="Japanese folktale">
        Japanese folktale
       </a>
       about the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon_rabbit" title="Moon rabbit">
        rabbit
       </a>
       which is said to be visible in the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       's face, much like the Western
       <a href="/wiki/Man_in_the_Moon" title="Man in the Moon">
        Man in the Moon
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The English-language manga – along with other localisations – gives her the nickname "Bunny" to partially preserve this pun. "Usagi" is not a common given name in Japan.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grigsby_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grigsby-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_legacy">
        Reception and legacy
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Moon_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Reception and legacy">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Sheila Rose Browning describes Sailor Moon as "one of the most popular and well-known manga characters in Japan".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
        <a href="#cite_note-41">
         [38]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Usagi influenced the hairstyle and personality of
       <a href="/wiki/Misato_Katsuragi" title="Misato Katsuragi">
        Misato Katsuragi
       </a>
       from
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion">
         Neon Genesis Evangelion
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
        <a href="#cite_note-42">
         [39]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and of Gruier Serenity's anime version from
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Bodacious_Space_Pirates" title="Bodacious Space Pirates">
         Bodacious Space Pirates
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
        <a href="#cite_note-43">
         [40]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Sailor Moon was ranked 9th on
       <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">
        IGN
       </a>
       's "Top 25 Anime Characters of All Time "list.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
        <a href="#cite_note-44">
         [41]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Rebecca Silverman, writing about the 2011 re-release of the
       <i>
        Sailor Moon
       </i>
       manga, felt that Usagi's initial hesitancy about whether she is good enough to be Sailor Moon added authenticity to her claim of being an "ordinary girl". Silverman states that along with
       <a href="/wiki/Itazura_na_Kiss" title="Itazura na Kiss">
        Itazura na Kiss
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Marmalade_Boy" title="Marmalade Boy">
        Marmalade Boy
       </a>
       , Usagi gave rise to an "unintelligent heroine" character type, but feels that even in the first volume, Usagi's determination sets her apart.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
        <a href="#cite_note-45">
         [42]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
