      <p>
       <b>
        Synergy Moon
       </b>
       is one of five finalist teams, out of an original 33 entrants,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       competing for the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize
       </a>
       —a challenge to land the first privately funded
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_rover" title="Lunar rover">
        rover
       </a>
       on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Competition guidelines require the rover to travel 500 metres and transmit images, video, data, an sms and an email back to
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Teams have until 31 March 2018 to launch their missions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2018-ext_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-2018-ext-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       With working groups on in over 15 countries and on 6 continents, Team Synergy Moon promotes international cooperation in space exploration and development.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Synergy_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Team Synergy Moon was formed in 2010 when Kevin Myrick,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       founder and CEO of
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.interplanetaryventures.org" rel="nofollow">
        InterPlanetary Ventures
       </a>
       , and Nebojša Stanojević,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       founder of The Human Synergy Moon Project, decided to merge their ventures and partner with
       <a href="/wiki/Interorbital_Systems" title="Interorbital Systems">
        Interorbital Systems
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       to create a single entrant for the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Team Synergy Moon received verification of its launch agreement from the
       <a href="/wiki/X_Prize_Foundation" title="X Prize Foundation">
        X Prize Foundation
       </a>
       on August 30, 2016.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In December 2016 Google Lunar X Prize entrants
       <a class="external text" href="http://lunar.xprize.org/teams/stellar" rel="nofollow">
        Team Stellar
       </a>
       (Croatia / Australia),
       <a class="external text" href="http://lunar.xprize.org/teams/omega-envoy" rel="nofollow">
        Team Omega Envoy
       </a>
       (USA),
       <a class="external text" href="http://lunar.xprize.org/teams/spacemeta" rel="nofollow">
        Team Space META
       </a>
       (Brasil) and
       <a class="external text" href="http://lunar.xprize.org/teams/independence-x" rel="nofollow">
        Team Independence-X
       </a>
       (Malaysia) partnered with Team Synergy Moon
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       to become Synergy Space Explorers
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_vehicle_and_rover">
        Launch vehicle and rover
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Synergy_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Launch vehicle and rover">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Team Synergy Moon plans to use an
       <a href="/wiki/Interorbital_Systems" title="Interorbital Systems">
        Interorbital Systems
       </a>
       Neptune N-8 LUNA launch vehicle
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       to deploy its Tesla Surveyor rover on the surface of the Moon during the second half of 2017.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
          <span title="The text near this tag is dated.">
           needs update
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The launch is planned from an open-ocean location off the California coast.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
          <span title="The text near this tag is dated.">
           needs update
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Competitor_teams">
        Competitor teams
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Synergy_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Competitor teams">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Team Synergy Moon is the only team in the
       <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
        Google Lunar X Prize
       </a>
       competition that includes members drawn from multiple countries, rather than from one, single country.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The five qualifying teams
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       are:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Synergy Moon (International); Launch agreement verified 30 August 2016.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
         <a href="#cite_note-18">
          [18]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/SpaceIL" title="SpaceIL">
         SpaceIL
        </a>
        (Israel); Launch agreement verified October 7, 2015.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
         <a href="#cite_note-19">
          [19]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Moon_Express" title="Moon Express">
         Moon Express
        </a>
        (USA); Launch agreement verified December 8, 2015.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
         <a href="#cite_note-20">
          [20]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_Indus" title="Team Indus">
         TeamIndus
        </a>
        (India)
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
         <a href="#cite_note-21">
          [21]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Hakuto" title="Hakuto">
         HAKUTO
        </a>
        (Japan) - which has signed a rideshare partnership with TeamIndus to carry its rover to the Moon.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
         <a href="#cite_note-22">
          [22]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
