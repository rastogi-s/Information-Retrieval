      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         The Case of the Missing Moon Rocks
        </b>
       </i>
       is a non-fiction book by Joe Kloc, a former contributing editor for
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seed_Magazine" title="Seed Magazine">
         Seed Magazine
        </a>
       </i>
       . It describes the efforts of both
       <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Gutheinz" title="Joseph Gutheinz">
        Joseph Gutheinz
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/NASA_Office_of_Inspector_General" title="NASA Office of Inspector General">
        NASA Office of Inspector General
       </a>
       Senior
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Agent" title="Special Agent">
        Special Agent
       </a>
       turned college professor and his students to locate and find up to 79 missing
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">
        Apollo 11
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17" title="Apollo 17">
        17
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moon_rocks" title="Moon rocks">
        moon rocks
       </a>
       and plaques that the United States government gave away to 135 nations of the world, all 50 states and its territories.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       It begins by telling the story of Operation Lunar Eclipse, the first successful
       <a href="/wiki/Sting_operation" title="Sting operation">
        sting operation
       </a>
       to recover a piece of the moon brought back by
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        American
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronauts" title="Astronauts">
        astronauts
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/Sting_operation" title="Sting operation">
        sting operation
       </a>
       the professor led and went undercover in, while still an agent. The sting operation successfully recovered the Honduras Apollo 17 Goodwill Moon Rock that was in the possession of Florida businessman Alan H. Rosen. This operation was funded in part with the financial assistance of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H._Ross_Perot" title="H. Ross Perot">
        H. Ross Perot
       </a>
       , billionaire and former Presidential candidate.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On October 1, 2012, Gutheinz gave a major speech on Operation Lunar Eclipse and the Moon Rock Project before the Engineering Colloquium at NASA's
       <a href="/wiki/Goddard_Space_Flight_Center" title="Goddard Space Flight Center">
        Goddard Space Flight Center
       </a>
       . The speech was entitled "Finding the Missing Moon Rocks", and is preserved on a video at the Space Flight Center Library.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In that speech Joe Gutheinz was critical of NASA and the U.S. Government for not turning over the Cyprus Apollo 17 Goodwill Moon Rock to Cyprus, which was also a major topic in Joe Kloc’s novel. On May 16, 2013 news reports first broke that bowing from pressure from Cyprus the United States Government would give Cyprus its Apollo 17 Goodwill Moon Rock.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
