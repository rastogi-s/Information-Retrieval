      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar Explorers Society
       </b>
       is an organisation dedicated to achieving permanent presence of humanity on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . The Society is open to all people in the world with an interest in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_exploration" title="Lunar exploration">
        Lunar exploration
       </a>
       . It hopes to bring the best of humanity to the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       , and to bring the benefits of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       to all people on
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       has not been visited by man since 1972, and though the first manned exploration efforts provided a huge scientific return, no further human
       <a href="/wiki/Exploration_of_the_Moon" title="Exploration of the Moon">
        Exploration of the Moon
       </a>
       has been done. However, several robotic
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_exploration" title="Lunar exploration">
        Lunar exploration
       </a>
       missions have been conducted since the beginning of the 1990s. These missions fuelled the desire to return to the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       among many Lunar enthusiast, and this was the background for the establishment of the
       <b>
        Lunar Explorers Society
       </b>
       in 2000. The founding members saw the need for an organisation where the members could share knowledge, join forces and pursue their ultimate goal: To establish a permanent human presence on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       to the benefit of all people on
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">
        Objectives
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_Explorers_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Objectives">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        To support the establishment of a permanent human presence on the
        <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
         Moon
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        To promote international cooperation between scientists working with Lunar exploration by providing a neutral platform for their discussions
       </li>
       <li>
        To raise awareness of what could be achieved by returning to the Moon through educational and outreach activities
       </li>
       <li>
        To promote the peaceful and fair use of the resources available on the
        <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
         Moon
        </a>
        , to the benefit of mankind
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Young_Lunar_Explorers_Award">
        The Young Lunar Explorers Award
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_Explorers_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Young Lunar Explorers Award">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Young Lunar Explorers Award is presented to a candidate who has been instrumental in promoting lunar exploration among young lunar explorers once per year, at the annual International Conference on the Exploration and Utilisation of the Moon (ICEUM). The awards have been given to the following winners:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        2008: The
        <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_Prize" title="Google Lunar X Prize">
         Google Lunar X Prize
        </a>
        Foundation
       </li>
       <li>
        2007: The Lunar Explorers Society
       </li>
       <li>
        2005: The
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSETI" title="SSETI">
         SSETI
        </a>
        Express team
       </li>
       <li>
        2004:
        <a href="/wiki/International_Space_University" title="International Space University">
         International Space University
        </a>
        (ISU)
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_Explorers_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Lunar Explorers Society
       </b>
       was founded 14 July 2000, during the 4th International Conference on the Exploration and Utilisation of the Moon (ICEUM4). There,
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.lunarexplorers.nl/images/ICEUM-4group-medium.jpg" rel="nofollow">
        the 156 ICEUM4 participants
       </a>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template">
        <span style="white-space: nowrap;">
         [
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot">
           <span title=" Dead link since January 2018">
            permanent dead link
           </span>
          </a>
         </i>
         ]
        </span>
       </sup>
       signed the founding declaration of the Lunar Explorers Society. The International Lunar Exploration Working Group (ILEWG) has supported the Lunar Explorers Society since the beginning
      </p>
