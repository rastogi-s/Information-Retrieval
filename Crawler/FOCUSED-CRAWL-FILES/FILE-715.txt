      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Saturnian_system">
        Saturnian system
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturn%27s_moons_in_fiction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Saturnian system">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Some works of fiction refer to several of Saturn's moons, or to no specific moon.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Animorphs" title="Animorphs">
          Animorphs
         </a>
        </i>
        novel series. The creature called the
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Veleek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Veleek (page does not exist)">
         Veleek
        </a>
        was originally found by the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeerks" title="Yeerks">
         Yeerks
        </a>
        on one of Saturn's moons.
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Exosquad" title="Exosquad">
          Exosquad
         </a>
        </i>
        animated TV series. Saturn's moons initially were home of the Pirate Clans before the discovery of the planet Chaos.
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Warhammer_40,000" title="Warhammer 40,000">
          Warhammer 40,000
         </a>
        </i>
        game universe (1987-) places the headquarters and other facilities of the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordo_Malleus" title="Ordo Malleus">
         Ordo Malleus
        </a>
        branch of
        <a href="/wiki/Inquisition_(Warhammer_40,000)" title="Inquisition (Warhammer 40,000)">
         Inquisition
        </a>
        in Saturn's moons, including the main base of their most famous subordinates — the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grey_Knights" title="Grey Knights">
         Grey Knights
        </a>
        chapter of
        <a href="/wiki/Space_Marines_(Warhammer_40,000)" title="Space Marines (Warhammer 40,000)">
         Space Marines
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starhunter_2300" title="Starhunter 2300">
          Starhunter 2300
         </a>
        </i>
        Canadian sci-fi TV series. Many episodes of this series take place in part of the Saturn system, designated as "Saturn Federation." The spaceship carrying the main characters is the Trans-Utopian, which travels frequently between Saturn, Jupiter and Mars. Various moons of Saturn are mentioned, including Titan and Iapetus. There is also a large space station orbiting Saturn at which the Trans-Utopian docks, called "Ring-Shepherd."
       </li>
       <li>
        In the novel
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/The_True_Meaning_of_Smekday" title="The True Meaning of Smekday">
          The True Meaning of Smekday
         </a>
        </i>
        , the Boov, after leaving Earth, colonize one of Saturn's moons. It is "a bit of a fixer-upper", though.
       </li>
       <li>
        The Daedalus Series, by Michael J. Martinez, including novels
        <i>
         The Daedalus Incident
        </i>
        (2013),
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <i>
         The Enceladus Crisis
        </i>
        (2014),
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
        and
        <i>
         The Venusian Gambit
        </i>
        (2015), incorporate an advanced civilization native to the Saturnian rings and moons, especially
        <a href="/wiki/Titan_(moon)" title="Titan (moon)">
         Titan
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Mimas_(moon)" title="Mimas (moon)">
         Mimas
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/wiki/Enceladus" title="Enceladus">
         Enceladus
        </a>
        . In the books, Saturn is referred to as Great Xanath.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Janus">
        Janus
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturn%27s_moons_in_fiction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Janus">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="floatright">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Janus_2006_closeup_by_Cassini.jpg" title="Janus">
        <img alt="Janus" data-file-height="250" data-file-width="300" height="67" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Janus_2006_closeup_by_Cassini.jpg/80px-Janus_2006_closeup_by_Cassini.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Janus_2006_closeup_by_Cassini.jpg/120px-Janus_2006_closeup_by_Cassini.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Janus_2006_closeup_by_Cassini.jpg/160px-Janus_2006_closeup_by_Cassini.jpg 2x" width="80"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Janus_(moon)" title="Janus (moon)">
        Janus
       </a>
       is a small inner moon of Saturn notable for having an orbit nearly identical to the orbit of the moon
       <a href="/wiki/Epimetheus_(moon)" title="Epimetheus (moon)">
        Epimetheus
       </a>
       ; the two swap orbital positions every four years.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Janus features prominently in
        <a href="/wiki/Alastair_Reynolds" title="Alastair Reynolds">
         Alastair Reynolds
        </a>
        ' science fiction novel
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Pushing_Ice" title="Pushing Ice">
          Pushing Ice
         </a>
        </i>
        (2005). In the novel, Janus is revealed to be a shell containing an
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life_in_popular_culture" title="Extraterrestrial life in popular culture">
         extraterrestrial
        </a>
        spacecraft which unexpectedly departs the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">
         solar system
        </a>
        in the direction of a giant structure orbiting the star
        <a href="/wiki/Spica" title="Spica">
         Spica
        </a>
        . The novel tells the story of the crew of a human mining ship who establish a colony on the moon as it leaves the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">
         solar system
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mimas">
        Mimas
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturn%27s_moons_in_fiction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mimas">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="floatright">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mimas_shape.jpg">
        <img alt="Mimas shape.jpg" data-file-height="274" data-file-width="296" height="74" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Mimas_shape.jpg/80px-Mimas_shape.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Mimas_shape.jpg/120px-Mimas_shape.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Mimas_shape.jpg/160px-Mimas_shape.jpg 2x" width="80"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Mimas_(moon)" title="Mimas (moon)">
        Mimas
       </a>
       is a small,
       <a href="/wiki/Icy_moon" title="Icy moon">
        icy moon
       </a>
       orbiting close to Saturn, notable for being scarred by
       <a href="/wiki/Herschel_(Mimantean_crater)" title="Herschel (Mimantean crater)">
        Herschel crater
       </a>
       , whose diameter is very wide compared to the total circumference of the moon.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Asimov" title="Isaac Asimov">
         Isaac Asimov
        </a>
        's 1958 novel
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Starr_and_the_Rings_of_Saturn" title="Lucky Starr and the Rings of Saturn">
          Lucky Starr and the Rings of Saturn
         </a>
        </i>
        the hero, knowing that Mimas is almost entirely made of ice, guides his ship on a collision course towards it, melts its surface with his weapons, and thus hides the ship in order to escape from his enemies, the people from the Sirius System.
       </li>
       <li>
        In "Zero Summer," a short story by
        <a href="/wiki/Caitl%C3%ADn_R._Kiernan" title="Caitlín R. Kiernan">
         Caitlín R. Kiernan
        </a>
        , Mimas is the destination of a starship manned by two androids.
       </li>
       <li>
        Mimas is a recurring location in the
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Red_Dwarf" title="Red Dwarf">
          Red Dwarf
         </a>
        </i>
        TV sitcom and its spinoff novels. In the episode "
        <a href="/wiki/Dimension_Jump_(Red_Dwarf_episode)" title="Dimension Jump (Red Dwarf episode)">
         Dimension Jump
        </a>
        ", Mimas hosts a Space Corps test base for test pilot
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Dwarf_characters#Ace_Rimmer" title="Red Dwarf characters">
         Arnold J. 'Ace' Rimmer
        </a>
        , while the first novel,
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infinity_Welcomes_Careful_Drivers" title="Infinity Welcomes Careful Drivers">
          Infinity Welcomes Careful Drivers
         </a>
        </i>
        , begins with
        <a href="/wiki/Dave_Lister" title="Dave Lister">
         Dave Lister
        </a>
        stranded in a seedy city on Mimas. Both the book and the series refer to the local delicacy "Mimean Bladderfish".
       </li>
       <li>
        "
        <a href="/wiki/The_First_Duty" title="The First Duty">
         The First Duty
        </a>
        " (1992), episode of
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Next_Generation" title="Star Trek: The Next Generation">
          Star Trek: The Next Generation
         </a>
        </i>
        . Mimas is the site of an evacuation station to which four
        <a href="/wiki/Starfleet" title="Starfleet">
         Starfleet
        </a>
        cadets, including
        <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Crusher" title="Wesley Crusher">
         Wesley Crusher
        </a>
        , transport after their vessels collide.
       </li>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Grant_Callin" title="Grant Callin">
         Grant Callin
        </a>
        's science fiction novel
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Saturnalia_(Callin_novel)" title="Saturnalia (Callin novel)">
          Saturnalia
         </a>
        </i>
        , Mimas is one of the locations of an alien artifact. It is also the site of human colonization and mining called "M-Base".
       </li>
       <li>

       </span>

      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Iapetus_(moon)" title="Iapetus (moon)">
        Iapetus
       </a>
       is a large moon orbiting further from Saturn than any of its other large satellites. Half of its surface is very bright while the other half is extremely dark. Investigations since 2004 have also noted its irregular shape, immense impact basins, and a high mountainous ridge on the equator.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Sturgeon" title="Theodore Sturgeon">
         Theodore Sturgeon
        </a>
        's short story "
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Comedian%27s_Children&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Comedian's Children (page does not exist)">
         The Comedian's Children
        </a>
        " (1958), a manned expedition to Iapetus in 2034 creates a public craze for black/white designs and "bi-colored gimcrackery", but is later linked to
        <i>
         iapetitis
        </i>
        , a disease in children where one side of the body turns white and paralyzed, the other black.
       </li>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Arthur_C._Clarke" title="Arthur C. Clarke">
         Arthur C. Clarke
        </a>
        's novel
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey_(novel)" title="2001: A Space Odyssey (novel)">
          2001: A Space Odyssey
         </a>
        </i>
        (1968), astronaut Dave Bowman finds an enigmatic alien monolith waiting for him on the surface of Iapetus (referred to as "Japetus" throughout). Iapetus's two tone coloration is caused by a vast white ellipse on the moon's surface, with the monolith appearing as a black dot in its exact center. When the
        <i>
         Voyager
        </i>
        space probes arrived at Iapetus thirteen years later, they discovered that there was indeed a black region within the moon's brighter hemisphere. Clarke reports that
        <a href="/wiki/Carl_Sagan" title="Carl Sagan">
         Carl Sagan
        </a>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phoebe">
        Phoebe
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturn%27s_moons_in_fiction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Phoebe">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Phoebe_(moon)" title="Phoebe (moon)">
        Phoebe
       </a>
       is a retrograde orbiting moon in the outer Saturn system. It is the largest of the Saturnian
       <a href="/wiki/Irregular_moon" title="Irregular moon">
        irregular moons
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_S.A._Corey" title="James S.A. Corey">
         James S.A. Corey
        </a>
        's
        <i>
         Expanse
        </i>
        series, Phoebe is actually an alien biological warhead that was accidentally captured by Saturn on its way to reprogram protozoan life on Earth. The extraction and use as a weapon of the protomolecular payload of Phoebe drives the primary conflicts of the series. After the protomolecule is used against
        <a href="/wiki/433_Eros" title="433 Eros">
         433 Eros
        </a>
        , Phoebe is destroyed via thermonuclear bombardment by the Martian space navy to keep anyone else from obtaining samples, and to prevent it from getting loose in the system.
       </li>
      </ul>
