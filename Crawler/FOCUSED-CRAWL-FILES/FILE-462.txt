      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         To The Moon and Beyond
        </b>
       </i>
       is the title of a special
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motion_picture" title="Motion picture">
        motion picture
       </a>
       produced for and shown at the
       <a href="/wiki/1964_New_York_World%27s_Fair" title="1964 New York World's Fair">
        1964/1965 New York World's Fair
       </a>
       . It depicted traveling from Earth out to an overall view of the universe and back again, zooming down to the atomic scale. It was filmed in a
       <a href="/wiki/Cinerama" title="Cinerama">
        Cinerama
       </a>
       process using a camera with a single
       <a href="/wiki/Fisheye_lens" title="Fisheye lens">
        fisheye lens
       </a>
       and projected onto a dome screen.
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
        Description
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=To_the_Moon_and_Beyond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The film was made in a format called "The New CINERAMA - 360 Process" It was shown in a 96 foot high "Moon Dome" that was part of Transportation and Travel building (Pavilion No. 123) in the Transportation section of the Fair and was presented by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KLM_Royal_Dutch_Airlines" title="KLM Royal Dutch Airlines">
        KLM Royal Dutch Airlines
       </a>
       . The film was narrated by
       <a href="/wiki/Rod_Serling" title="Rod Serling">
        Rod Serling
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Text from ad in the World's Fair Guide Book:
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        YOUR MOST MEMORABLE EXPERIENCE
       </p>
       <p>
        AT THE WORLD'S FAIR! THE NEW CINERAMA-360 PROCESS TAKES YOU...
        <br/>
        TO THE MOON AND BEYOND
        <br/>
        YOU will be propelled on the most fantastic, incredible voyage through billions of miles of space . . . from its utmost outer reaches . . . back to the Earth itself, and into the center of the minutest atom. All through the magic of Cinerama!
        <br/>
        AT THE TRANSPORTATION &amp; TRAVEL PAVILION 3 SHOWINGS EVERY HOUR
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
        Production
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=To_the_Moon_and_Beyond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The film was created using Cinerama 360º - a process that recorded on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/70mm_film" title="70mm film">
        70mm film
       </a>
       at 18 fps using a fish-eye
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wide_angle_lens" title="Wide angle lens">
        wide angle lens
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was projected in a domed theater using a similar wide angle projector. The film was made by Graphic Films Corporation, a company run by former
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">
        Disney
       </a>
       animator
       <a href="/wiki/Lester_Novros" title="Lester Novros">
        Lester Novros
       </a>
       who had been making technical films for NASA, the US Air Force, and various aerospace clients.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Influences_on_2001:_A_Space_Odyssey">
        Influences on
        <i>
         2001: A Space Odyssey
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=To_the_Moon_and_Beyond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Influences on 2001: A Space Odyssey">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Kubrick" title="Stanley Kubrick">
        Stanley Kubrick
       </a>
       saw the film at the Fair and was so impressed by the its
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_effects" title="Special effects">
        special effects
       </a>
       and accurate depiction of scientifically based material he hired Graphic Films as a design consultant on a film he already had in pre-production,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey" title="2001: A Space Odyssey">
         2001: A Space Odyssey
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Graphic Films' Lester Novros,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Con_Pederson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Con Pederson (page does not exist)">
        Con Pederson
       </a>
       , and background artist
       <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Trumbull" title="Douglas Trumbull">
        Douglas Trumbull
       </a>
       would air-mail research based concept sketches and notes covering the mechanics and physics of space travel to Kubrick in England during pre-production. They would go on to create
       <a href="/wiki/Storyboard" title="Storyboard">
        storyboards
       </a>
       for a portion of the space flight sequences seen in the film.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Trumbull would eventually leave Graphic Films to become a special effects supervisor on
       <i>
        2001
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
