   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Harpalyke (moon)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Harpalyke
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="'h' in 'hi'">
            h
           </span>
           <span title="/ɑːr/: 'ar' in 'far'">
            ɑːr
           </span>
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'p' in 'pie'">
            p
           </span>
           <span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">
            æ
           </span>
           <span title="'l' in 'lie'">
            l
           </span>
           <span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">
            ə
           </span>
           <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
            k
           </span>
           <span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">
            i
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         har-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          PAL
         </span>
         -ə-kee
        </i>
       </a>
       ; Greek:
       <i>
        Αρπαλύκη
       </i>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         Jupiter XXII
        </span>
       </b>
       , is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prograde_and_retrograde_motion" title="Prograde and retrograde motion">
        retrograde
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irregular_satellite" title="Irregular satellite">
        irregular satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
        Jupiter
       </a>
       . It was discovered by a team of astronomers from the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii" title="University of Hawaii">
        University of Hawaii
       </a>
       led by
       <a href="/wiki/Scott_S._Sheppard" title="Scott S. Sheppard">
        Scott S. Sheppard
       </a>
       in 2000, and given the temporary designation
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         S/2000 J 5
        </span>
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In August 2003, the moon was named
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       after
       <a href="/wiki/Harpalyce_(mythology)" title="Harpalyce (mythology)">
        Harpalyke
       </a>
       , the incestuous daughter of
       <a href="/wiki/Clymenus" title="Clymenus">
        Clymenus
       </a>
       , who in some accounts was also a lover of
       <a href="/wiki/Zeus" title="Zeus">
        Zeus
       </a>
       (Jupiter).
      </p>
      <p>
       Harpalyke belongs to the
       <a href="/wiki/Ananke_group" title="Ananke group">
        Ananke group
       </a>
       , believed to be the remnants of a break-up of a captured heliocentric asteroid.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SheppardJewitt2003_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SheppardJewitt2003-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nesvorny2003_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Nesvorny2003-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is about 4 kilometres in diameter
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SheppardJewittPorco2004_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SheppardJewittPorco2004-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and appears grey (
       <a href="/wiki/Color_index" title="Color index">
        color index
       </a>
       R-V=0.43), similar to
       <a href="/wiki/C-type_asteroid" title="C-type asteroid">
        C-type asteroids
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grav2003_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grav2003-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The satellite orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 21,064 Mm in 624.542 Earth days, at an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">
        inclination
       </a>
       of 147° to the
       <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">
        ecliptic
       </a>
       (147° to Jupiter's equator) with an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eccentricity_(orbit)" title="Eccentricity (orbit)">
        eccentricity
       </a>
       of 0.2441.
      </p>
