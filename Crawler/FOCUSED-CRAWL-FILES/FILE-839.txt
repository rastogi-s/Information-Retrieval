      <p>
       <b>
        Zooniverse
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Citizen_science" title="Citizen science">
        citizen science
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">
        web portal
       </a>
       owned and operated by the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Citizen_Science_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Citizen Science Alliance (page does not exist)">
        Citizen Science Alliance
       </a>
       . It is home to some of the internet's largest, most popular and most successful citizen science projects.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESERO_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ESERO-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The organization grew from the original
       <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_Zoo" title="Galaxy Zoo">
        Galaxy Zoo
       </a>
       project and now hosts dozens of projects which allow volunteers to participate in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crowdsource" title="Crowdsource">
        crowdsourced
       </a>
       scientific research. It has headquarters at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxford_University" title="Oxford University">
        Oxford University
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Adler_Planetarium" title="Adler Planetarium">
        Adler Planetarium
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chicago_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Chicago-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Unlike many early internet-based citizen science projects (such as
       <a href="/wiki/SETI@home" title="SETI@home">
        SETI@home
       </a>
       ) which used spare computer processing power to analyse data, known as
       <a href="/wiki/Volunteer_computing" title="Volunteer computing">
        volunteer computing
       </a>
       , Zooniverse projects require the active participation of human volunteers to complete research tasks. Projects have been drawn from disciplines including
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">
        astronomy
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">
        ecology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cell_biology" title="Cell biology">
        cell biology
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">
        humanities
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Climate_science" title="Climate science">
        climate science
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       As of 14 February 2014
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Zooniverse&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       , the Zooniverse community consisted of more than 1 million registered volunteers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UT_ZooStats_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-UT_ZooStats-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The volunteers are often collectively referred to as "Zooites".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GalaxyBabe_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GalaxyBabe-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finkbeiner_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Finkbeiner-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The data collected from the various projects has led to the publication of more than 100 scientific papers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A daily news website called 'The Daily Zooniverse' provides information on the different projects under the Zooniverse umbrella, and has a presence on
       <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">
        social media
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Citizen_Science_Alliance">
        Citizen Science Alliance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zooniverse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Citizen Science Alliance">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Zooniverse is hosted by the Citizen Science Alliance, which is governed by a board of directors from seven institutions in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
        United Kingdom
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       . The partners are the
       <a href="/wiki/Adler_Planetarium" title="Adler Planetarium">
        Adler Planetarium
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Johns_Hopkins_University" title="Johns Hopkins University">
        Johns Hopkins University
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota" title="University of Minnesota">
        University of Minnesota
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/National_Maritime_Museum" title="National Maritime Museum">
        National Maritime Museum
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nottingham" title="University of Nottingham">
        University of Nottingham
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxford_University" title="Oxford University">
        Oxford University
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Vizzuality" title="Vizzuality">
        Vizzuality
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
