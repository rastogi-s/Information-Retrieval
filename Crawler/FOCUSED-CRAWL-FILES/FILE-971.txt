      <p>
       <b>
        Laomedeia
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">

           <span title="'m' in 'my'">

           </span>
          </span>
          , -
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'d' in 'dye'">
            d
           </span>
           <span title="/aɪ/: 'i' in 'tide'">

           </span>
           <span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">

           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          LAY
         </span>
         -oh-mə-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          DEE
         </span>
         -ə
        </i>
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          LAY
         </span>
         -oh-mə-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          DY
         </span>
         -ə
        </i>
       </a>
       ; Greek:
       <i>
        Λαομήδεια
       </i>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        Neptune XII
       </b>
       , is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prograde_and_retrograde_motion" title="Prograde and retrograde motion">
        prograde
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irregular_satellite" title="Irregular satellite">
        irregular satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Neptune" title="Neptune">
        Neptune
       </a>
       . It was discovered by
       <a href="/wiki/Matthew_J._Holman" title="Matthew J. Holman">
        Matthew J. Holman
       </a>
       , et al. on August 13, 2002.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HolmanKavelaarsGrav2004_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-HolmanKavelaarsGrav2004-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Before the announcement of its name on February 3, 2007 (IAUC 8802), it was known as
       <b>
        S/2002 N 3
       </b>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       It orbits Neptune at a distance of about 23,571,000 km and is about 42 kilometers in diameter (assuming albedo of 0.04).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SheppardJewittKleyna2006_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-SheppardJewittKleyna2006-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is named after Laomedeia, one of the 50
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nereids" title="Nereids">
        Nereids
       </a>
       .
      </p>
