      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         In the Shadow of the Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is a 2007 British documentary film about the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       '
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        manned missions to the Moon
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sundance_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Sundance-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It premiered at the
       <a href="/wiki/2007_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2007 Sundance Film Festival">
        2007 Sundance Film Festival
       </a>
       , where it won the World Cinema Audience Award. In March 2008, it was the first film to win the
       <a href="/wiki/Sir_Arthur_Clarke_Award" title="Sir Arthur Clarke Award">
        Sir Arthur Clarke Award
       </a>
       for Best Film Presentation. It was given a
       <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">
        limited release
       </a>
       in the United States on 7 September 2007, and in Canada on 19 October 2007.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb-release_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-imdb-release-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was released on DVD in the United States on 22 February 2008, and 31 March 2008, in the United Kingdom.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is also notable for giving
       <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Edwards_(director)" title="Gareth Edwards (director)">
        Gareth Edwards
       </a>
       an early credit in visual effects.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
        Plot
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=In_the_Shadow_of_the_Moon_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The film follows the manned missions to the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       made by the United States in the late 1960s and early 1970s. The documentary reviews both the footage and media available to the public at the time of the missions, as well as
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       films and materials which had not been opened in over 30 years. All of this has been sourced and
       <a href="/wiki/Remaster" title="Remaster">
        remastered
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/High-definition_video" title="High-definition video">
        HD
       </a>
       by the stock footage company
       <a href="/wiki/Footagevault" title="Footagevault">
        Footagevault
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="Most of this footage was transferred to ATSC HDTV 1080p by Mark Gray at Spacecraft Films (July 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Augmenting the archival audio and video are contemporary interviews with some surviving
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo
       </a>
       era
       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">
        astronauts
       </a>
       , including
       <a href="/wiki/Alan_Bean" title="Alan Bean">
        Al Bean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(astronaut)" title="Michael Collins (astronaut)">
        Michael Collins
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Buzz_Aldrin" title="Buzz Aldrin">
        Buzz Aldrin
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/John_Young_(astronaut)" title="John Young (astronaut)">
        John Young
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/David_Scott" title="David Scott">
        David Scott
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlie_Duke" title="Charlie Duke">
        Charlie Duke
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Eugene_Cernan" title="Eugene Cernan">
        Eugene Cernan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Schmitt" title="Harrison Schmitt">
        Harrison Schmitt
       </a>
       . The former astronauts have the only speaking roles in the movie, although occasional supplementary information is presented on screen with text and archival television footage presents the words of journalists such as
       <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bergman" title="Jules Bergman">
        Jules Bergman
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Walter_Cronkite" title="Walter Cronkite">
        Walter Cronkite
       </a>
       . The only surviving moon walker at the time not to participate was
       <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armstrong" title="Neil Armstrong">
        Neil Armstrong
       </a>
       , the first person to set foot on the Moon.
      </p>
      <p>
       The documentary shares its name with a
       <a href="/wiki/In_the_Shadow_of_the_Moon_(book)" title="In the Shadow of the Moon (book)">
        book
       </a>
       by space historians
       <a href="/wiki/Colin_Burgess_(author)" title="Colin Burgess (author)">
        Colin Burgess
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Francis_French" title="Francis French">
        Francis French
       </a>
       , and both include many original interviews with Apollo lunar astronauts. The documentary offers a view of the Apollo program that is complementary to the book and is neither a source nor a tie-in.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Participants">
        Participants
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=In_the_Shadow_of_the_Moon_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Participants">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Ten former astronauts appear in
       <i>
        In the Shadow of the Moon
       </i>
       . During the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollo_Program" title="Apollo Program">
        Apollo Program
       </a>
       , twenty-four men reached the vicinity of the Moon. Of the twelve who actually set foot on the lunar surface, eight appear on camera (Neil Armstrong did not participate, while
       <a href="/wiki/Pete_Conrad" title="Pete Conrad">
        Pete Conrad
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/James_Irwin" title="James Irwin">
        James Irwin
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shepard" title="Alan Shepard">
        Alan Shepard
       </a>
       had died). Two other astronauts who are featured in
       <i>
        In the Shadow of the Moon
       </i>
       orbited or looped around the Moon without landing (
       <a href="/wiki/Jim_Lovell" title="Jim Lovell">
        Jim Lovell
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(astronaut)" title="Michael Collins (astronaut)">
        Michael Collins
       </a>
       ). All manned Apollo flights with the exception of
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_7" title="Apollo 7">
        Apollo 7
       </a>
       , which was an earth-orbit mission, were represented in the film. The film is presented by
       <a href="/wiki/Ron_Howard" title="Ron Howard">
        Ron Howard
       </a>
       , but there is no narration in the film.
      </p>

       <li>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
        Reception
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=In_the_Shadow_of_the_Moon_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Critics gave the film very positive reviews.
       <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
        Metacritic
       </a>
       reported
       <i>
        In the Shadow of the Moon
       </i>
       had an average score of 84 out of 100, based on 34 reviews, classifying the film's reception as "Universal Acclaim."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">
        Rotten Tomatoes
       </a>
       gave the film a 94% rating based on 111 reviews. Furthermore, 4,884 users of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_Movie_Database" title="Internet Movie Database">
        IMDb
       </a>
       gave the film 8.1/10 points.
      </p>
      <p>
       Roger Ebert called it "spellbinding".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">
         Entertainment Weekly
        </a>
       </i>
       rated the movie an A.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">
         Los Angeles Times
        </a>
       </i>
       called the film a "fresh and compelling film, made with intelligence and emotion"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Meanwhile,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">
         The Hollywood Reporter
        </a>
       </i>
       concluded that "The value of this film, not just to moviegoers today but to future generations, is simply enormous."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On 23 June 2008 the Independent Investigations Group (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IIG" title="IIG">
        IIG
       </a>
       ) honoured
       <i>
        In the Shadow of the Moon
       </i>
       for promoting
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_skepticism" title="Scientific skepticism">
        scientific skepticism
       </a>
       in media; the award was received by producer Duncan Copp.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
