      <p>
       <b>
        Montes Riphaeus
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latin
       </a>
       for "Riphaeus Mountains") is an irregular range of
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">
        mountains
       </a>
       that lie along the west-northwestern edge of
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Cognitum" title="Mare Cognitum">
        Mare Cognitum
       </a>
       , on the southeastern edge of
       <a href="/wiki/Oceanus_Procellarum" title="Oceanus Procellarum">
        Oceanus Procellarum
       </a>
       . The range trends generally from north-northeast to south-southwest. It includes a number of slender ridge lines with valleys flooded by intruding flows of
       <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">
        lava
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This range is located at selenographic
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coordinate" title="Coordinate">
        coordinates
       </a>
       7.7° S, 28.1° W. It has a diameter of 189 km, although it is typically only about 30–50 km wide. The nearest feature of note is
       <a href="/wiki/Euclides_(crater)" title="Euclides (crater)">
        Euclides
       </a>
       , a small but prominent crater to the west. About 100 km to the north is the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Lansberg_(crater)" title="Lansberg (crater)">
        Lansberg
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The range is called after the
       <a href="/wiki/Riphean_Mountains" title="Riphean Mountains">
        Riphean Mountains
       </a>
       in the geography of
       <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">
        classical antiquity
       </a>
       .
      </p>
