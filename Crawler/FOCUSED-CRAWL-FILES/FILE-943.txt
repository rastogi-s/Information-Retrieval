      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Orbit">
        Orbit
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Margaret_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Orbit">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:292px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1000" data-file-width="1000" height="290" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg/290px-TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg/435px-TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg/580px-TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg.png 2x" width="290"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_URANUS.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Irregular satellites of Uranus
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Margaret stands out as the only
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prograde_and_retrograde_motion" title="Prograde and retrograde motion">
        prograde
       </a>
       irregular satellite of Uranus. The diagram illustrates the orbital parameters of Margaret, unique among the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irregular_satellite" title="Irregular satellite">
        irregular satellites
       </a>
       of Uranus, with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">
        inclination
       </a>
       on the vertical axis and the
       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_eccentricity" title="Orbital eccentricity">
        eccentricity
       </a>
       of the orbits represented by the segments extending from the
       <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
        pericentre
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
        apocentre
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Margaret's inclination of 57° is close to the limit of stability. The intermediate inclinations 60 &lt; i &lt; 140 are devoid of known moons due to the
       <a href="/wiki/Kozai_mechanism" title="Kozai mechanism">
        Kozai instability
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESheppardJewittKleyna2005524–525_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESheppardJewittKleyna2005524–525-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In this instability region, solar
       <a href="/wiki/Perturbation_(astronomy)" title="Perturbation (astronomy)">
        perturbations
       </a>
       at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apoapse" title="Apoapse">
        apoapse
       </a>
       cause the moons in this region to acquire large eccentricities that lead to collisions or ejection over 10 million to a billion years. Margaret's
       <a href="/wiki/Apsidal_precession" title="Apsidal precession">
        periapsis precession
       </a>
       period (P
       <sub>
        w
       </sub>
       ) is almost 1.6 million years long.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JPL_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-JPL-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Margaret itself may be ejected from the Uranian system in the far future.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Margaret_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Margaret-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2008, Margaret's current eccentricity was 0.7979.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAU-MPC_nses_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-IAU-MPC_nses-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This temporarily gives Margaret the most eccentric orbit of any moon in the Solar System, though
       <a href="/wiki/Nereid_(moon)" title="Nereid (moon)">
        Nereid
       </a>
       's mean eccentricity is greater.
      </p>
