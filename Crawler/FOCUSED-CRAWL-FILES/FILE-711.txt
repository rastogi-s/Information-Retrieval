      <p>
       The comic book stories published by
       <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">
        Marvel Comics
       </a>
       since the 1940s have featured several noteworthy concepts besides its fictional characters, such as unique places and artifacts. There follows a list of those features.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Places">
        Places
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Features_of_the_Marvel_Universe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Places">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Certain places feature prominently in the Marvel Universe, some real-life, others fictional and unique to the setting; fictional places may appear in conjunction with, or even within, real-world locales. A majority of dystopian cities have been used for their characters since the creation of Marvel Comics in the Marvel Universe.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_City">
        New York City
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Most of the action of Marvel Comics takes place in
       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
        New York City
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       New York is the site of many places important to superheroes:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Avengers_Mansion" title="Avengers Mansion">

        </a>

         Avengers
        </a>
        .

        <a href="/wiki/Baxter_Building" title="Baxter Building">
         Baxter Building
        </a>

        <a href="/wiki/Four_Freedoms_Plaza" title="Four Freedoms Plaza">
         Four Freedoms Plaza
        </a>

        <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four" title="Fantastic Four">
         Fantastic Four
        </a>
        .

       <li>
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Daily_Bugle" title="Daily Bugle">

         </a>

        <a href="/wiki/Spider-Man" title="Spider-Man">

        </a>

        <a href="/wiki/J._Jonah_Jameson" title="J. Jonah Jameson">

        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Fisk_Towers" title="Fisk Towers">
         Fisk Towers
        </a>
        – a fictional skyscraper owned by
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingpin_(comics)" title="Kingpin (comics)">
         Kingpin
        </a>

       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Hell%27s_Kitchen,_Manhattan" title="Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan">
         Hell's Kitchen
        </a>

        <a href="/wiki/Defenders_(comics)" title="Defenders (comics)">
         Defenders
        </a>
        .

       <li>
        The Raft, a fictional
        <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">
         prison
        </a>
        for superpowered villains, located on
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryker%27s_Island" title="Ryker's Island">

        </a>

        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riker%27s_Island" title="Riker's Island">

        <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">
         Colorado
        </a>

        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Strange%27s_Sanctum_Sanctorum" title="Doctor Strange's Sanctum Sanctorum">
         Sanctum Sanctorum
        </a>
        – fictional abode of
        <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Strange" title="Doctor Strange">
         Doctor Strange
        </a>
        located in
        <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Village" title="Greenwich Village">
         Greenwich Village
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Stark_Tower" title="Stark Tower">
         Stark Tower
        </a>
        – fictional skyscraper of the
        <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">
         Avengers
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oscorp_Tower" title="Oscorp Tower">
         Oscorp Tower
        </a>
        – fictional skyscraper owned by
        <a href="/wiki/Norman_Osborn" title="Norman Osborn">
         Norman Osborn
        </a>
        . Now the headquarters for
        <a href="/wiki/Alchemax" title="Alchemax">
         Alchemax
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/District_X" title="District X">
         Mutant Town
        </a>
        – a ghetto-like neighborhood of New York primarily populated by
        <a href="/wiki/Mutant_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Mutant (Marvel Comics)">
         mutants
        </a>
        . Since the
        <a href="/wiki/Decimation_(comics)" title="Decimation (comics)">
         Decimation
        </a>

        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bowery" title="The Bowery">
         The Bowery
        </a>

        <i>
         Fantastic Four
        </i>

        <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four" title="Fantastic Four">
         Fantastic Four
        </a>

        <a href="/wiki/Human_Torch" title="Human Torch">

        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Namor_the_Sub-Mariner" title="Namor the Sub-Mariner">

        <a href="/wiki/Retrograde_amnesia" title="Retrograde amnesia">
         retrograde amnesia
        </a>
        about his identity.
       </li>

A seventh gem was discovered in another dimension. This gem, called the Ego gem, contained the essence of the entity Nemesis, whose self-destruction created the gems.

        <b>

        </b>

The protector of the crystal is singular in all universes, with the same memories in each, which suggests that the reality immediately surrounding the crystal is anchored in place.

        <a href="/wiki/Silver_Surfer" title="Silver Surfer">

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Drugs">
        Drugs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Features_of_the_Marvel_Universe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Drugs">

        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_comic_book_drugs#Marvel_Comics" title="List of comic book drugs">
        List of comic book drugs § Marvel Comics
       </a>

      <ul>
       <li>
        Goblin Serum (Oz Formula) – The chemical formula that gave
        <a href="/wiki/Green_Goblin" title="Green Goblin">
         Green Goblin
        </a>
        his powers.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pym_Particles" title="Pym Particles">
         Growth Pills
        </a>
        – Capsules containing the size-altering
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pym_Particles" title="Pym Particles">
         Pym Particles
        </a>
        that allow
        <a href="/wiki/Bill_Foster_(comics)" title="Bill Foster (comics)">
         Giant Man
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Pym" title="Henry Pym">

        </a>

        <a href="/wiki/Wasp_(comics)" title="Wasp (comics)">
         Wasp
        </a>
        to change their size.
       </li>
       <li>
        Extremis – A techno-organic virus created in an attempt to recreate the Super-Soldier Serum that gave Captain America his powers.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Red_Skull" title="Red Skull">
         Red Skull
        </a>
        's Dust of Death – A red powder which kills a victim within seconds of skin contact. The powder causes the skin of the victim's head to shrivel, tighten, and take on a red discoloration, while causing the hair to fall out. Hence the victim's head resembles a "red skull".
       </li>
       <li>
        Super Soldier Serum – An experimental military drug that enhances physical abilities and gave several superheroes their powers, most notably
        <a href="/wiki/Captain_America" title="Captain America">
         Captain America
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Terrigen Mists – A mutagenic catalyst discovered and used by the
        <a href="/wiki/Inhumans" title="Inhumans">
         Inhumans
        </a>
        that can grant superpowers, but leaves many subjects with deformities and amnesia.
       </li>
