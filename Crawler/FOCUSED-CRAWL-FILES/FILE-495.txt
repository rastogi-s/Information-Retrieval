      <p>
       <b>
        Lunar Crater National Natural Landmark
       </b>
       is a volcanic field landmark located 70 miles (110 km) east-northeast of
       <a href="/wiki/Tonopah,_Nevada" title="Tonopah, Nevada">
        Tonopah
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Nye_County,_Nevada" title="Nye County, Nevada">
        Nye County
       </a>
       , in central
       <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">
        Nevada
       </a>
       . It was designated a
       <a href="/wiki/National_Natural_Landmark" title="National Natural Landmark">
        National Natural Landmark
       </a>
       in 1973.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Volcanic_features">
        Volcanic features
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunar_Crater_National_Natural_Landmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Volcanic features">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A 400-acre (1.6 km
       <sup>
        2
       </sup>
       ) crater that is thought to have been formed by several
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volcanic" title="Volcanic">
        volcanic
       </a>
       explosions, and is one of two
       <a href="/wiki/Maar" title="Maar">
        maars
       </a>
       in the volcanic field of the
       <a href="/wiki/Pancake_Range" title="Pancake Range">
        Pancake Range
       </a>
       .
      </p>
