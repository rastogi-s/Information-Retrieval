   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Eurydome (moon)
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
     Jump to:
     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation
     </a>

     <a href="#p-search">

     </a>
    </div>
    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Eurydome
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/j/: 'y' in 'yes'">
            j
           </span>
           <span title="/ʊəˈr/: 'ur' in 'plurality'">
            ʊəˈr
           </span>
           <span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">
            ɪ
           </span>
           <span title="'d' in 'dye'">
            d
           </span>
           <span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">
            ə
           </span>
           <span title="'m' in 'my'">
            m
           </span>
           <span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">
            i
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         yoor-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          ID
         </span>
         -ə-mee
        </i>
       </a>
       ; Greek:
       <i>
        Ευριδόμη
       </i>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         Jupiter XXXII
        </span>
       </b>
       , is a
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">
        natural satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
        Jupiter
       </a>
       . It was discovered by a team of astronomers from the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii" title="University of Hawaii">
        University of Hawaii
       </a>
       led by
       <a href="/wiki/Scott_S._Sheppard" title="Scott S. Sheppard">
        Scott S. Sheppard
       </a>
       in 2001, and given the temporary designation
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         S/2001 J 4
        </span>
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Eurydome is about 3 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 23,231 Mm in 723.359 days, at an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">
        inclination
       </a>
       of 149° to the
       <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">
        ecliptic
       </a>
       (147° to Jupiter's equator), in a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retrograde_motion" title="Retrograde motion">
        retrograde
       </a>
       direction and with an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eccentricity_(orbit)" title="Eccentricity (orbit)">
        eccentricity
       </a>
       of 0.3770.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was named in August 2003 after
       <a href="/wiki/Eurydome" title="Eurydome">
        Eurydome
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">
        Greek mythology
       </a>
       , who is sometimes described as the mother of the
       <a href="/wiki/Charites" title="Charites">
        Graces
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Zeus" title="Zeus">
        Zeus
       </a>
       (Jupiter).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Eurydome belongs to the
       <a href="/wiki/Pasiphae_group" title="Pasiphae group">
        Pasiphae group
       </a>
       , irregular retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at distances ranging between 22.8 and 24.1 Gm, and with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">
        inclinations
       </a>
       ranging between 144.5° and 158.3°.
      </p>
