       <i>
        <b>
         Blue Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is the eighth in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anita_Blake:_Vampire_Hunter_(series)" title="Anita Blake: Vampire Hunter (series)">

       </a>
       series of horror/mystery/erotica novels by
       <a href="/wiki/Laurell_K._Hamilton" title="Laurell K. Hamilton">
        Laurell K. Hamilton
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_introduction">
        Plot introduction
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Moon_(Hamilton_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot introduction">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Blue Moon
       </i>
       continues the adventures of
       <a href="/wiki/Anita_Blake" title="Anita Blake">
        Anita Blake
       </a>
       . In this novel, Anita travels to Myerton,
       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">
        Tennessee
       </a>
       to help clear her ex-boyfriend
       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Zeeman" title="Richard Zeeman">
        Richard
       </a>
       of rape allegations. In the process, Anita and her various allies are drawn into a supernatural conflict with the Master Vampire of that city, as well as the criminal scheme that Richard's frame-up was meant to conceal. As with the other later novels in the series,
       <i>
        Blue Moon
       </i>
       blends elements of
       <a href="/wiki/Supernatural_fiction" title="Supernatural fiction">
        supernatural
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hardboiled" title="Hardboiled">
        hardboiled
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Detective_fiction" title="Detective fiction">
        detective
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Erotic_literature" title="Erotic literature">
        erotic
       </a>
       fiction.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="Explanation_of_the_novel.27s_title">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Explanation_of_the_novel's_title">
        Explanation of the novel's title
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Moon_(Hamilton_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Explanation of the novel's title">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       As with previous novels, "Blue Moon" refers to a location within the novel itself. In this case, the "Blue Moon cabins" are a set of rental cabins owned by
       <b>
        Verne
       </b>
       , the local
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shapeshifters_(Anita_Blake_mythology)#Ulfric" title="Shapeshifters (Anita Blake mythology)">
        Ulfric
       </a>
       . Within the novel, the term "
       <a href="/wiki/Blue_moon#Calendar_blue_moons" title="Blue moon">
        blue moon
       </a>
       " is also used to refer to an upcoming full moon, the second in that calendar month, which is a rare occurrence.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">
        Plot summary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Moon_(Hamilton_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Plot summary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Blue Moon
       </i>
       takes place in the apparently fictional town of Myerton,
       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">
        Tennessee
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Zeeman" title="Richard Zeeman">
        Richard
       </a>
       , still recovering from
       <a href="/wiki/Anita_Blake" title="Anita Blake">
        Anita's
       </a>
       rejection in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Killing_Dance_(novel)" title="The Killing Dance (novel)">
        <i>
         The Killing Dance
        </i>
       </a>
       , has been in Myerton for some time, studying a local group of
       <a href="/wiki/Anita_Blake_mythology#Trolls" title="Anita Blake mythology">
        trolls
       </a>
       as part of the requirements for his master's degree, and auditioning the women of the local werewolf tribe as possible
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shapeshifters_(Anita_Blake_mythology)#Lupa" title="Shapeshifters (Anita Blake mythology)">
        lupas
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The plot begins when Anita receives a call from Richard's brother
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Anita_Blake:_Vampire_Hunter_characters#Daniel_Zeeman" title="List of Anita Blake: Vampire Hunter characters">
        Daniel
       </a>
       . Daniel explains that Richard has been arrested for an alleged rape, and is refusing to hire a lawyer. Anita leaves for Myerton, over the objection of the local
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vampires_(Anita_Blake_mythology)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vampires (Anita Blake mythology) (page does not exist)">
        Master of the City
       </a>
       , with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undead_characters_of_Anita_Blake:_Vampire_Hunter#Asher" title="Undead characters of Anita Blake: Vampire Hunter">
        Asher
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undead_characters_of_Anita_Blake:_Vampire_Hunter#Damian" title="Undead characters of Anita Blake: Vampire Hunter">
        Damian
       </a>
       , and most of the
       <a href='/wiki/List_of_Anita_Blake:_Vampire_Hunter_characters#Wereleopards_aka_"The_Pard"' title="List of Anita Blake: Vampire Hunter characters">
        wereleopards
       </a>
       as backup.
      </p>
      <p>
       Once there, Anita must simultaneously attempt to uncover why local police have framed Richard and deal with
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Anita_Blake:_Vampire_Hunter_characters#Colin" title="List of Anita Blake: Vampire Hunter characters">
        Colin
       </a>
       , the local Master of the City, who views her arrival as an act of war.
      </p>
      <p>
       Ultimately, Anita destroys most of Colin's vampires by activating the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shapeshifters_(Anita_Blake_mythology)#Lupanar" title="Shapeshifters (Anita Blake mythology)">
        lupanar
       </a>
       of the local werewolf clan, rendering it holy ground, and kills Colin herself by shooting Colin's human servant,
       <b>
        Nikki
       </b>
       . In the course of her various rituals, Anita ends up
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Having_sex_with" title="Having sex with">
        having sex with
       </a>
       Richard, and they agree that Anita will begin
       <a href="/wiki/Dating" title="Dating">
        dating
       </a>
       both Richard and Jean-Claude. Anita also learns that Richard had discussed her with Jean-Claude and had obliquely asked Jean-Claude whether he would accept Anita taking Richard as a
       <a href="/wiki/Sexual_partner" title="Sexual partner">
        lover
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Anita meets Marianne, a Wiccan practitioner who works with Verne's pack. Marianne advises Anita on building the wereleopards into a coherent group. Anita also has a long talk with Damien, and discovers that Jean-Claude gains power from
       <a href="/wiki/Lust" title="Lust">
        lust
       </a>
       and sex. Not only can he feed on the patrons of his strip club Guilty Pleasures, but on sex with Anita, or Anita having sex with Richard.
      </p>
      <p>
       She and Richard discover that the rape charges were an effort by art collector
       <b>
        Frank Niley
       </b>
       to drive Richard's study project from the area, allowing them to acquire some contested land and complete Niley's search for the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spear_of_Destiny" title="Spear of Destiny">
        Spear of Destiny
       </a>
       . In desperation, Niley kidnaps and brutalizes Richard's mother and brother, causing Anita to cross another moral line, torturing Niley's messenger and killing everyone responsible.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Characters_in_Blue_Moon">
        Characters in
        <i>

       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <h2>
       <span id="Allusions.2Freferences_to_other_works">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Allusions/references_to_other_works">
        Allusions/references to other works
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Moon_(Hamilton_novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Allusions/references to other works">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <b>
         Frank Niley
        </b>
        and his entourage appear to be inspired by
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/The_Maltese_Falcon_(1941_film)" title="The Maltese Falcon (1941 film)">
          The Maltese Falcon
         </a>
        </i>
        . Niley's appearance and demeanor closely resemble
        <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Greenstreet" title="Sydney Greenstreet">
         Sydney Greenstreet
        </a>
        's portrayal of "Kaspar Gutman" from the 1941 film adaptation of that novel, while Howard's physical description closely resembles that of
        <a href="/wiki/Peter_Lorre" title="Peter Lorre">
         Peter Lorre
        </a>
        , who played Gutman's associate, "Joel Cairo" in the film. Similarly, Niley's futile quest for the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spear_of_Destiny" title="Spear of Destiny">
         Spear of Destiny
        </a>
        resembles Gutman's chase after the eponymous falcon.
       </li>
      </ul>
