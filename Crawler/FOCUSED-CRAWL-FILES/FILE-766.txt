    The Moon (Tarot card)
   </h1>

      <p>
       <b>
        The Moon (XVIII)
       </b>
       is the eighteenth
       <a href="/wiki/Trump_(card_games)" title="Trump (card games)">
        trump
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Major_Arcana" title="Major Arcana">
        Major Arcana
       </a>
       card in most traditional
       <a href="/wiki/Tarot" title="Tarot">
        Tarot
       </a>
       decks. It is used in
       <a href="/wiki/Tarot_card_games" title="Tarot card games">
        game playing
       </a>
       as well as in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tarot_reading" title="Tarot reading">
        divination
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:215px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jean_Dodal_Tarot_trump_18.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="395" data-file-width="213" height="395" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/50/Jean_Dodal_Tarot_trump_18.jpg" width="213"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jean_Dodal_Tarot_trump_18.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         An original card from the tarot deck of
         <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Dodal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jean Dodal (page does not exist)">
          Jean Dodal
         </a>
         of
         <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">
          Lyon
         </a>
         , a classic "
         <a href="/wiki/Tarot_of_Marseilles" title="Tarot of Marseilles">
          Tarot of Marseilles
         </a>
         " deck. The deck dates from 1701-1715.
        </div>
       </div>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
        Description
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Moon_(Tarot_card)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The card depicts a night scene. Two large, foreboding pillars are shown. A wolf and a domesticated dog howl at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_moon" title="The moon">
        the moon
       </a>
       . A
       <a href="/wiki/Crayfish" title="Crayfish">
        crayfish
       </a>
       appears in the water. The Moon has "sixteen chief and sixteen secondary rays" and "[is] shedding the moisture of fertilizing
       <a href="/wiki/Dew" title="Dew">
        dew
       </a>
       in great drops" (totaling 15 in the Rider-Waite deck) which are all
       <a href="/wiki/Yodh" title="Yodh">
        Yodh
       </a>
       -shaped.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The figure in the moon is
       <a href="/wiki/Frown" title="Frown">
        frowning
       </a>
       , reflecting displeasure.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Interpretation">
        Interpretation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Moon_(Tarot_card)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Interpretation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       According to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_Edward_Waite" title="Arthur Edward Waite">
        Waite
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Pictorial_Key_to_the_Tarot#XVIII._The_Moon" title="wikisource:The Pictorial Key to the Tarot">
         The Pictorial Key to the Tarot
        </a>
       </i>
       , "The card represents life of the
       <a href="/wiki/Imagination" title="Imagination">
        imagination
       </a>
       apart from life of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spirit" title="Spirit">
        spirit
       </a>
       ... The dog and wolf are the
       <a href="/wiki/Fear" title="Fear">
        fears
       </a>
       of the natural
       <a href="/wiki/Mind" title="Mind">
        mind
       </a>
       in the presence of that place of exit, when there is only reflected light to guide it... The
       <a href="/wiki/Intellect" title="Intellect">
        intellectual
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">
        light
       </a>
       is a reflection and beyond it is the unknown mystery which it cannot reveal." Additionally, "It illuminates our animal nature" and according to Waite, "the message is 'Peace, be still; and it may be that there shall come a calm upon the animal nature, while the abyss beneath shall cease from giving up a form.'"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_decks">
        Alternative decks
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Moon_(Tarot_card)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alternative decks">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In the "Flemish Deck" by Vandenborre, the moon shows a woman seated in the right-hand corner with a tree in the left hand corner. The moon is directly above her. She is shown with a
        <a href="/wiki/Distaff" title="Distaff">
         distaff
        </a>
        in her right hand and spinning thread with her left hand.
       </li>
       <li>
        In one of the old Italian Tarot decks, instead of the above scene there is an Astrologer plotting a horoscope while the moon shines in from a window.
       </li>
       <li>
        In Mythic Tarot decks, the moon is depicted by
        <a href="/wiki/Artemis" title="Artemis">
         Artemis
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Selene" title="Selene">
         Selene
        </a>
        or
        <a href="/wiki/Hecate" title="Hecate">
         Hecate
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
