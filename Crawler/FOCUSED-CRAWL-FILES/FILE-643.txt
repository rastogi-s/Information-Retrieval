      <p>
       <b>
        Gill
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       that is located near the southeastern limb of the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . Due to its proximity to the edge of the Moon as seen from the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       , this crater is viewed nearly from the side and it can become hidden from sight due to
       <a href="/wiki/Libration" title="Libration">
        libration
       </a>
       . The crater lies to the southwest of the irregular
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Australe" title="Mare Australe">
        Mare Australe
       </a>
       , and southeast of the prominent crater
       <a href="/wiki/Pont%C3%A9coulant_(crater)" title="Pontécoulant (crater)">
        Pontécoulant
       </a>
       . To the southwest of Gill is the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Helmholtz_(lunar_crater)" title="Helmholtz (lunar crater)">
        Helmholtz
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This is an old, eroded crater formation with an outer rim that is uneven from a history of impacts. A joined pair of small craters lie along the northern rim, and Gill A intrudes slightly into the western outer rim. The interior floor is relatively level, and is marked by several craterlets.
      </p>
      <p>
       On June 11, 2009, the Japanese
       <a href="/wiki/SELENE" title="SELENE">
        SELENE
       </a>
       lunar orbiter spacecraft was deliberately crashed into the surface of the Moon to the southeast of Gill. The impact site was at selenographic coordinates 65.5 S, 80.4 E.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The flash from the impact was successfully observed from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_Abu_Observatory" title="Mount Abu Observatory">
        Mount Abu Observatory
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Guru_Shikhar" title="Guru Shikhar">
        Guru Shikhar
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        India
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and by the
       <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Australian_Telescope" title="Anglo-Australian Telescope">
        Anglo-Australian Telescope
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
