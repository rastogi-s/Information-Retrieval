      <p>
       <b>
        Albiorix
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">
            ˌ
           </span>
           <span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">
            æ
           </span>
           <span title="'l' in 'lie'">
            l
           </span>
           <span title="'b' in 'buy'">
            b
           </span>
           <span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">
            i
           </span>
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="/ɒr/: 'or' in 'moral'">
            ɒr
           </span>
           <span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">
            ɪ
           </span>
           <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
            k
           </span>
           <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
            s
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          AL
         </span>
         -bee-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          ORR
         </span>
         -iks
        </i>
       </a>
       ) is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retrograde_motion" title="Retrograde motion">
        prograde
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irregular_satellite" title="Irregular satellite">
        irregular
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">
        satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">
        Saturn
       </a>
       . It was discovered by
       <a href="/wiki/Matthew_J._Holman" title="Matthew J. Holman">
        Holman
       </a>
       and colleagues in 2000, and given the temporary designation
       <b>
        S/2000 S 11
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gladman2001_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gladman2001-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Albiorix is the largest member of the
       <a href="/wiki/Saturn%27s_Gallic_group_of_satellites" title="Saturn's Gallic group of satellites">
        Gallic group
       </a>
       of irregular satellites.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was named in August 2003
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       for Albiorix, "a Gallic giant who was considered to be the king of the world."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The name is known from an inscription found near the French town of
       <a href="/wiki/Sablet" title="Sablet">
        Sablet
       </a>
       which identifies him with the Roman god
       <a href="/wiki/Mars_(mythology)" title="Mars (mythology)">
        Mars
       </a>
       (an
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Interpretatio_graeca" title="Interpretatio graeca">
         interpretatio romana
        </a>
       </i>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="400" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg/300px-TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg/450px-TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg/600px-TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg.png 2x" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_SATURN_PRO_GROUPS.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Irregular prograde groups of satellites of Saturn: Gallic (red) and Inuit (blue)
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Albiorix orbits Saturn at a distance of about 16 million km and its diameter is estimated at 32 kilometers, assuming an
       <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
        albedo
       </a>
       of 0.04. The rotation period was measured by the
       <a class="external text" href="http://ciclops.org/" rel="nofollow">
        ISS camera
       </a>
       of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cassini-Huygens" title="Cassini-Huygens">
        Cassini spacecraft
       </a>
       to 13 hours and 19 minutes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DenkMottola2011_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-DenkMottola2011-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The diagram illustrates the Albiorigian orbit in relation to other prograde irregular satellites of Saturn. The eccentricity of the orbits is represented by the yellow segments extending from the
       <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
        pericentre
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
        apocentre
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Given the similarity of the orbital elements and the homogeneity of the physical characteristics with other members of the Gallic group, it was suggested that these satellites could have a common origin in the break-up of a larger moon.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grav2003_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grav2003-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gladman2001_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gladman2001-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Varying colours revealed recently suggest a possibility of a large crater, leading to an alternative hypothesis that
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erriapus_(moon)" title="Erriapus (moon)">
        Erriapus
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tarvos_(moon)" title="Tarvos (moon)">
        Tarvos
       </a>
       could be fragments of Albiorix following a near-break-up collision with another body.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grav2006_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grav2006-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
