      <p>
       A
       <b>
        partial
        <a href="/wiki/Lunar_eclipse" title="Lunar eclipse">
         lunar eclipse
        </a>
       </b>
       took place on August 7/8, 2017, the second of two lunar eclipses in 2017. The Moon was only slightly covered by the Earth's umbral shadow at maximum eclipse.
      </p>
      <p>
       The moon inside the umbral shadow was a subtle red, but hard to see in contrast to the much brighter moon in the outer penumbral shadow.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_August_21,_2017" title="Solar eclipse of August 21, 2017">
        solar eclipse of August 21, 2017
       </a>
       occurred fourteen days later, in the same
       <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_season" title="Eclipse season">
        eclipse season
       </a>
       . It was the first total solar eclipse visible in the contiguous
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       since the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_February_26,_1979" title="Solar eclipse of February 26, 1979">
        solar eclipse of February 26, 1979
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
