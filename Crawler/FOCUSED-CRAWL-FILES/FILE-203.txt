      <p>
       A
       <b>
        partial
        <a href="/wiki/Lunar_eclipse" title="Lunar eclipse">
         lunar eclipse
        </a>
       </b>
       took place on August 16, 2008, the second of two lunar eclipses in 2008, with the first being a total eclipse on
       <a href="/wiki/February_2008_lunar_eclipse" title="February 2008 lunar eclipse">
        February 20, 2008
       </a>
       . The next lunar eclipse was a penumbral eclipse occurring on
       <a href="/wiki/February_2009_lunar_eclipse" title="February 2009 lunar eclipse">
        February 9, 2009
       </a>
       , while the next total lunar eclipse occurred on
       <a href="/wiki/December_2010_lunar_eclipse" title="December 2010 lunar eclipse">
        December 21, 2010
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <p>
       Parts of Australia saw it begin before sunrise, while parts of South America saw it end just after sunset. The eclipse is also seen in the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       and other parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       at moonset. Parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">
        Middle East
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       saw it when it is visible.
      </p>
      <p>
       The penumbral eclipse began at 18:23
       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">
        UTC
       </a>
       , with the partial eclipse beginning at 19:36. The time of greatest eclipse was 21:10. The partial eclipse ended at 22:44, and the penumbral eclipse will ended at 23:57.
      </p>
      <p>
       The planet
       <a href="/wiki/Neptune" title="Neptune">
        Neptune
       </a>
       was 2 days past
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Opposition_(astronomy)" title="Opposition (astronomy)">
        opposition
       </a>
       , visible in
       <a href="/wiki/Binoculars" title="Binoculars">
        binoculars
       </a>
       as an 8th
       <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">
        magnitude
       </a>
       "star" just two degrees west and slightly south of the moon.
      </p>

        </a>

         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Lunar
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saros_cycle" title="Saros cycle">
        saros series
       </a>
       138 has 19 total eclipses between
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_21st_century_lunar_eclipses" title="List of 21st century lunar eclipses">
        September 7, 2044
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_25th_century_lunar_eclipses" title="List of 25th century lunar eclipses">
        June 8, 2495
       </a>
       . The longest eclipse will be on January 7, 2243, and last for 102 minutes.
      </p>
      <p>
       Partial eclipses will occur between
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_20th_century_lunar_eclipses" title="List of 20th century lunar eclipses">
        June 13, 1900
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_lunar_eclipses" title="List of lunar eclipses">
        August 13, 2603
       </a>
       . Penumbral eclipses will occur between
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_16th_century_lunar_eclipses" title="List of 16th century lunar eclipses">
        October 5, 1503
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_lunar_eclipses" title="List of lunar eclipses">
        March 30, 2982
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Metonic_cycle_.2819_years.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Metonic_cycle_(19_years)">
        Metonic cycle (19 years)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=August_2008_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Metonic cycle (19 years)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Metonic_cycle" title="Metonic cycle">
        Metonic cycle
       </a>
       repeats nearly exactly every 19 years and represents a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saros_cycle" title="Saros cycle">
        Saros cycle
       </a>
       plus one lunar year. Because it occurs on the same calendar date, the earth's shadow will in nearly the same location relative to the background stars.
      </p>
