      <p>
       <b>
        Arago
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       located in the western part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Tranquillitatis" title="Mare Tranquillitatis">
        Mare Tranquillitatis
       </a>
       which is named after
       <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Arago" title="François Arago">
        François Arago
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GPN_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GPN-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Nearly 45 km southwest lies the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Manners_(crater)" title="Manners (crater)">
        Manners
       </a>
       , and beyond are
       <a href="/wiki/Dionysius_(crater)" title="Dionysius (crater)">
        Dionysius
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Ritter_(crater)" title="Ritter (crater)">
        Ritter
       </a>
       –
       <a href="/wiki/Sabine_(crater)" title="Sabine (crater)">
        Sabine
       </a>
       crater pair. To the southeast is the large
       <a href="/wiki/Lamont_(lunar_crater)" title="Lamont (lunar crater)">
        Lamont
       </a>
       formation that has been submerged by the
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_mare" title="Lunar mare">
        mare
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Its diameter is 26 km long and is 1,800 meters deep. The area is about 500 km² and the perimeter is over 75 km.
      </p>
      <p>
       From that location the Earth would appear in the lunar sky at over 6 degrees from the top and it is seen more than 21 degrees towards the west, sometimes due to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lunar_libration" title="Lunar libration">
        librations
       </a>
       , it appears about 7 degrees in different directions.
      </p>
      <p>
       The rim of Arago has a bulge in the western wall. There is a central ridge that runs towards the northern wall. The surface of the mare nearby is marked by
       <a href="/wiki/Wrinkle_ridge" title="Wrinkle ridge">
        wrinkle ridges
       </a>
       , most notably to the east and southeast. To the north is a large
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_dome" title="Lunar dome">
        lunar dome
       </a>
       designated Arago Alpha (α). A similar-sized lunar dome is located an equal distance to the west, designated Arago Beta (β).
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
