   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Moon, South Dakota
   </h1>

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Moon
       </b>
       is an extinct town in
       <a href="/wiki/Pennington_County,_South_Dakota" title="Pennington County, South Dakota">
        Pennington County
       </a>
       , in the
       <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">
        U.S. state
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">
        South Dakota
       </a>
       . The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GNIS" title="GNIS">
        GNIS
       </a>
       classifies it as a populated place.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon,_South_Dakota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Moon was laid out in 1910, and named in honor of Jack Moon, its first inhabitant.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A post office called Moon was established in 1911, and remained in operation until 1953.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
