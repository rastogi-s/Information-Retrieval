      <p>
       A
       <b>
        total
        <a href="/wiki/Lunar_eclipse" title="Lunar eclipse">
         lunar eclipse
        </a>
       </b>
       took place on June 15, 2011. It was the first of two such eclipses in 2011. The second occurred on
       <a href="/wiki/December_2011_lunar_eclipse" title="December 2011 lunar eclipse">
        December 10, 2011
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This was a relatively rare
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_central_lunar_eclipses" title="List of central lunar eclipses">
        central lunar eclipse
       </a>
       , in which the center point of Earth's shadow passes across the moon. The last time a lunar eclipse was closer to the center of the earth's shadow was on
       <a href="/wiki/July_2000_lunar_eclipse" title="July 2000 lunar eclipse">
        July 16, 2000
       </a>
       . The next central total lunar eclipse will be on
       <a href="/wiki/July_2018_lunar_eclipse" title="July 2018 lunar eclipse">
        July 27, 2018
       </a>
       over
       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">
        South America
       </a>
       , western
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       , and setting over eastern
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       .
      </p>

        Visibility and viewing
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <p>
       In western Asia,
       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
        Australia
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       , the lunar eclipse was visible just before sunrise.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was very visible in the clear and cloudless night sky throughout eastern and southeast Asia.
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">
        far eastern Russia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       witnessed the whole event even in the late stages (as in partial lunar eclipse). The
       <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">
        Americas
       </a>
       (including North and northwestern South America) missed the eclipse completely (except in most areas) because it occurred at
       <a href="/wiki/Orbit_of_the_Moon" title="Orbit of the Moon">
        moonset
       </a>
       .
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Related_eclipses">
        Related eclipses
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       It was preceded by the partial
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_January_4,_2011" title="Solar eclipse of January 4, 2011">
        solar eclipse of January 4, 2011
       </a>
       , and the partial
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_June_1,_2011" title="Solar eclipse of June 1, 2011">
        solar eclipse of June 1, 2011
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Semester_series">
        Semester series
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=June_2011_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Semester series">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       This eclipse is the center of nine lunar eclipses in a short-lived series. Each eclipse in the series repeats after one semester (6 lunations or 177 days) occurring at alternating nodes.

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tritos_series">
        Tritos series
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=June_2011_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tritos series">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Tritos" title="Tritos">
        tritos
       </a>
       series repeats 31 days short of 11 years at alternating nodes. Sequential events have incremental
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saros_cycle" title="Saros cycle">
        Saros cycle
       </a>
       indices.
      </p>
      <p>
       This series produces 23 total eclipses between June 22, 1880 and August 9, 2120.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Inex_series">
        Inex series
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=June_2011_lunar_eclipse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Inex series">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Inex" title="Inex">
        inex series
       </a>
       repeats eclipses 20 days short of 29 years, repeating on average every 10571.95 days. This period is equal to 358 lunations (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synodic_month" title="Synodic month">
        synodic months
       </a>
       ) and 388.5
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Draconic_month" title="Draconic month">
        draconic months
       </a>
       . Saros series increment by one on successive Inex events and repeat at alternate ascending and descending lunar nodes.
      </p>
      <p>
       This period is 383.6734
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anomalistic_month" title="Anomalistic month">
        anomalistic months
       </a>
       (the period of the Moon's elliptical orbital precession). Despite the average 0.05 time-of-day shift between subsequent events, the variation of the Moon in its elliptical orbit at each event causes the actual eclipse time to vary significantly.
      </p>
      <p>
       All events in this series listed below and more are total lunar eclipses.
      </p>
