      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Moon Shot: The Inside Story of America's Race to the Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       is a book written by
       <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Seven" title="Mercury Seven">
        Mercury Seven
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">
        astronaut
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shepard" title="Alan Shepard">
        Alan Shepard
       </a>
       , with
       <a href="/wiki/NBC_News" title="NBC News">
        NBC News
       </a>
       correspondent
       <a href="/wiki/Jay_Barbree" title="Jay Barbree">
        Jay Barbree
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">
        Associated Press
       </a>
       space writer Howard Benedict.
       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">
        Astronaut
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Deke_Slayton" title="Deke Slayton">
        Donald K. "Deke" Slayton
       </a>
       is also listed as an author, although he died before the project was completed and was an author in name only. The book was published in 1994. It was turned into a television
       <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">
        miniseries
       </a>
       that aired on
       <a href="/wiki/Turner_Broadcasting_System" title="Turner Broadcasting System">
        TBS
       </a>
       in the United States in
       <a href="/wiki/1994_in_television" title="1994 in television">
        1994
       </a>
       . The miniseries was narrated by
       <a href="/wiki/Barry_Corbin" title="Barry Corbin">
        Barry Corbin
       </a>
       (as Slayton) and featured interviews with several American astronauts as well as a few Russian cosmonauts. Slayton died before the miniseries completed production in 1993 and the miniseries is dedicated to his memory.
      </p>
