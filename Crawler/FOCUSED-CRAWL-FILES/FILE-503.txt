      <p>
       <b>
        Secchi
       </b>
       is a small
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       formation on the northwest edge of
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Fecunditatis" title="Mare Fecunditatis">
        Mare Fecunditatis
       </a>
       and is named after a 19th-century Italian astronomer
       <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Secchi" title="Angelo Secchi">
        Angelo Secchi
       </a>
       . To the northeast is the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Taruntius_(crater)" title="Taruntius (crater)">
        Taruntius
       </a>
       and nearly south is
       <a href="/wiki/Lubbock_(crater)" title="Lubbock (crater)">
        Lubbock
       </a>
       . The western rim is joined with a section of the minor
       <a href="/wiki/Montes_Secchi" title="Montes Secchi">
        Montes Secchi
       </a>
       range. The rim of this crater has been opened in the northern and southern ends, leaving two curved ridges facing each other across the crater floor. To the south is a pair of
       <a href="/wiki/Rille" title="Rille">
        rilles
       </a>
       designated the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_features_on_the_Moon" title="List of features on the Moon">
        Rimae Secchi
       </a>
       . These lie near the edge of the
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_mare" title="Lunar mare">
        mare
       </a>
       , and have a combined length of about 40 kilometers.
      </p>
