      <p>
       The
       <b>
        SpaceX lunar tourism mission
       </b>
       (unofficial name) is a planned private mission to fly two
       <a href="/wiki/Space_tourism" title="Space tourism">
        space tourists
       </a>
       around the moon aboard
       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX" title="SpaceX">
        SpaceX
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Dragon_2" title="Dragon 2">
        Dragon 2
       </a>
       spacecraft. SpaceX aims for a launch sometime in 2018, after the Dragon 2 is tested and flown under
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Commercial_Crew_Development" title="Commercial Crew Development">
        Commercial Crew Program
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Space X lunar tourism mission could fall around the 50th anniversary of the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_8" title="Apollo 8">
        Apollo 8
       </a>
       flight, which flew
       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">
        astronauts
       </a>
       around the moon for the first time, as part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
        Apollo program
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:2-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The announcement for this mission was made on February 27, 2017.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
        Overview
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=SpaceX_lunar_tourism_mission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       It is a
       <a href="/wiki/Tourism_on_the_Moon" title="Tourism on the Moon">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Space_tourism" title="Space tourism">
        space
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Private_spaceflight" title="Private spaceflight">
        tourist
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_mission" title="Space mission">
        mission
       </a>
       to be flown by
       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX" title="SpaceX">
        SpaceX
       </a>
       aboard the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crew_Dragon" title="Crew Dragon">
        Crew Dragon
       </a>
       , launched via
       <a href="/wiki/Falcon_Heavy" title="Falcon Heavy">
        Falcon Heavy
       </a>
       rocket, to fly on a free-return trajectory around the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       , scheduled for late 2018. It is also to serve as a development mission in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mars_Colonial_Transporter" title="Mars Colonial Transporter">
        SpaceX's further plans to colonize Mars
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SnT-2017-03-01_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SnT-2017-03-01-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A 2018 mission would mark the 50th anniversary of the first manned Moon mission,
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_8" title="Apollo 8">
        Apollo 8
       </a>
       , of 1968. It would be the first manned Moon mission since
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_17" title="Apollo 17">
        Apollo 17
       </a>
       of 1972, some 46 years earlier.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC-2017-03-08_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-CBC-2017-03-08-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SpaceX-2017-02-27_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SpaceX-2017-02-27-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA-17-010_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-17-010-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_8" title="Apollo 8">
        Apollo 8
       </a>
       mission was a similar mission during NASA's Project Apollo, which orbited the Moon without landing. However Apollo 8 entered orbit, while the SpaceX mission will not. The only manned spacecraft to have performed this maneuver is
       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_13" title="Apollo 13">
        Apollo 13
       </a>
       , which was forced to use a free-return trajectory in order to return to earth after an accident that prevented it from using its thrusters to enter orbit around the moon. The original
       <a href="/wiki/Exploration_Mission_1" title="Exploration Mission 1">
        Exploration Mission 1
       </a>
       (EM-1) and
       <a href="/wiki/Exploration_Mission_2" title="Exploration Mission 2">
        EM-2
       </a>
       for the NASA
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orion_MPCV" title="Orion MPCV">
        Orion MPCV
       </a>
       /
       <a href="/wiki/Space_Launch_System" title="Space Launch System">
        SLS
       </a>
       combo were also free-return loop-around-the-Moon missions, very similar to this SpaceX mission. Since the 2017 Trump-era revision, both EM-1 and EM-2 missions will enter lunar orbit.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheVerge-2017-03-03_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-TheVerge-2017-03-03-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nerdist-2017-02-27_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Nerdist-2017-02-27-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSMonitor-2017-02-25_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-CSMonitor-2017-02-25-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Several have pointed out that the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dragon_V2" title="Dragon V2">
        Dragon V2
       </a>
       crew capsule was still under development and that the Falcon Heavy had yet to fly at the time of announcement, and the schedule was very aggressive. The capsule would also need modifications to handle the differing mission profile from crew transfer to Earthly space stations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SciAm-2017-03-04_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SciAm-2017-03-04-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Launch">
        Launch
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=SpaceX_lunar_tourism_mission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Launch">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A
       <a href="/wiki/Falcon_Heavy" title="Falcon Heavy">
        Falcon Heavy
       </a>
       can be used to put the Dragon 2 in a weeklong cruise around the moon. SpaceX has planned the launch from
       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center_Launch_Complex_39" title="Kennedy Space Center Launch Complex 39">
        Pad 39A
       </a>
       at
       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">
        Kennedy Space Center
       </a>
       . The Dragon 2 will go on an automated cruise around the moon, although the crew will be trained for emergencies.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:3-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Crew">
        Crew
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=SpaceX_lunar_tourism_mission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Crew">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       SpaceX has not disclosed the names of the crew that they are planning to fly on the mission. SpaceX says that two private citizens have paid a "significant deposit."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Although SpaceX hasn't shared how much the tourists will pay,
       <a href="/wiki/Elon_Musk" title="Elon Musk">
        Elon Musk
       </a>
       , CEO of SpaceX, did reveal that the cost will be "comparable" to the cost of a crewed mission to the
       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">
        International Space Station
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Concerns_by_non-participating_parties">
        Concerns by non-participating parties
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=SpaceX_lunar_tourism_mission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Concerns by non-participating parties">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In May 2017,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RSC_Energia" title="RSC Energia">
        RSC Energia
       </a>
       general director
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Solntsev&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vladimir Solntsev (page does not exist)">
        Vladimir Solntsev
       </a>
       questioned whether SpaceX could meet its schedule, and thought it more likely that it would be 2020 or later, and not 2018, that SpaceX could launch. He also was questioned whether hardware designed for missions to the
       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">
        International Space Station
       </a>
       and launch aboard
       <a href="/wiki/Falcon_9" title="Falcon 9">
        Falcon 9
       </a>
       rockets could be used for a lunar mission and launched aboard Falcon Heavy. RSC Energia is a
       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
        Russian
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Competition" title="Competition">
        competitor
       </a>
       of SpaceX.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DailyMail-2017-05-18_13-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-DailyMail-2017-05-18-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
