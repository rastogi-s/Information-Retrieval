      <p>
       <b>
        Rabbit in the Moon
       </b>
       is an American
       <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">
        electronic music
       </a>
       group. Their style draws from
       <a href="/wiki/Psychedelic_trance" title="Psychedelic trance">
        psychedelic trance
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">
        house music
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Breakbeat" title="Breakbeat">
        breakbeat
       </a>
       , along with other diverse influences.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rabbit_in_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Formed in 1992 in
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">
        Tampa, Florida
       </a>
       , the group consisted of performer/singer Bunny and producer David Christophere. Former member DJ Monk left the group in 2002.
      </p>
      <p>
       After several singles released on Monk and David Christophere's
       <a href="/wiki/Hallucination_Recordings" title="Hallucination Recordings">
        Hallucination Recordings
       </a>
       label under the name Anarch-E, their first success as producers came in 1993 with the release of the "Phases of an Out of Body Experience" single on Hardkiss Records. As remixers, Rabbit in the Moon has reworked songs by artists such as
       <a href="/wiki/Garbage_(band)" title="Garbage (band)">
        Garbage
       </a>
       ("
       <a href="/wiki/Queer_(song)" title="Queer (song)">
        Queer
       </a>
       " and "
       <a href="/wiki/Milk_(song)" title="Milk (song)">
        Milk
       </a>
       "),
       <a href="/wiki/Tori_Amos" title="Tori Amos">
        Tori Amos
       </a>
       ("Precious Things"),
       <a href="/wiki/Sarah_McLachlan" title="Sarah McLachlan">
        Sarah McLachlan
       </a>
       ("Fear", "Possession"),
       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_(band)" title="Orbital (band)">
        Orbital
       </a>
       ("Are We Here?"),
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smashing_Pumpkins" title="Smashing Pumpkins">
        Smashing Pumpkins
       </a>
       ("
       <a href="/wiki/The_End_Is_the_Beginning_Is_the_End" title="The End Is the Beginning Is the End">
        The End Is the Beginning Is the End
       </a>
       "),
       <a href="/wiki/Goldie" title="Goldie">
        Goldie
       </a>
       ("
       <a href="/wiki/Inner_City_Life" title="Inner City Life">
        Inner City Life
       </a>
       "),
       <a href="/wiki/White_Zombie_(band)" title="White Zombie (band)">
        White Zombie
       </a>
       ("
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blood,_Milk_and_Sky" title="Blood, Milk and Sky">
        Blood, Milk and Sky
       </a>
       ") and
       <a href="/wiki/Delerium" title="Delerium">
        Delerium
       </a>
       ("Euphoria").
      </p>
      <p>
       Rabbit in the Moon has also collaborated with
       <a href="/wiki/Humate" title="Humate">
        Humate
       </a>
       and in 1994 they released the
       <i>
        Hemispheres E.P.
       </i>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Throughout their career, Rabbit in the Moon toured extensively in the United States playing shows at venues and raves.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <i>
        Rabbit in the Moon Remixes Volume 2
       </i>
       CD came with a liner note stating that there was a new album to be released in summer of 2001. The album never materialized. Because of Monk's departure, questions were raised as to whether the band was breaking up, but this was not the case.
      </p>
      <p>
       A new song called "Time Bomb" surfaced in 2003, with David Christophere and Bunny as the only producers on the track.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2005, they made the song "Came and Conkered", inspired by and featured in the soundtrack of the 2005 video game
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Conker:_Live_%26_Reloaded" title="Conker: Live &amp; Reloaded">
         Conker: Live &amp; Reloaded
        </a>
       </i>
       . They were asked by
       <a href="/wiki/Nile_Rodgers" title="Nile Rodgers">
        Nile Rodgers
       </a>
       to create music for the Xbox remake of the game.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Early in 2007, a countdown to a new album appeared on the band's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MySpace" title="MySpace">
        MySpace
       </a>
       page, putting the release date in April 2007. The album was ultimately released on July 10, 2007. It features two discs: one, a collection of past singles and newer material in audio CD format; the other, a collection of music videos and live performances from various festivals and concerts in DVD format. The record is called "Decade", and was released on Contagious Musiq / Southbeat Records.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2008, on the second night of the Annual World DJ Festival in Seoul, South Korea, frontman Bunny was injured when a glass bottle thrown by someone in the crowd hit his left eye, lacerating his retina, lens, and skin, and severely damaging his vision. Friends and fans throughout the electronic dance music community rallied to raise money to help him pay for his medical expenses.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       As of 2010
       <sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">
        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Rabbit_in_the_Moon&amp;action=edit">
         [update]
        </a>
       </sup>
       , Rabbit in the Moon announced on their MySpace page that they were working on a new double album.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On March 27, 2010, Rabbit in the Moon performed at the Ultra Music Festival in Miami. David Christophere was not seen at this show, fueling rumors he left the band and of an imminent band break up.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       David Christophere's last Rabbit in the Moon show was New Year's Eve 2009.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The two have not performed a show together since. Bunny has been performing in several countries under the title BUNNY or Bunny Live.
      </p>
      <p>
       David Christophere continues to produce music and DJ from his home base in Los Angeles.
      </p>
      <p>
       On December 16, 2015, the
       <a href="/wiki/Ultra_Music_Festival" title="Ultra Music Festival">
        Ultra Music Festival
       </a>
       of Miami announced on their lineup of 2016 edition the appearance of Rabbit in the Moon.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Since their show at Ultra 2015 Rabbit in the Moon has been releasing new music and playing one off festival gigs. They performed at
       <a href="/wiki/Electric_Daisy_Carnival" title="Electric Daisy Carnival">
        Electric Daisy Carnival
       </a>
       in Orlando in November, 2016.
      </p>
