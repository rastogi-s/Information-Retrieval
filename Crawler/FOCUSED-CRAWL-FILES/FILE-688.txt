   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Nothing Ever Happens on the Moon
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">

      <p>
       <b>
        "Nothing Ever Happens on the Moon"
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">
        science fiction
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Short_story" title="Short story">
        short story
       </a>
       written by
       <a href="/wiki/Robert_A._Heinlein" title="Robert A. Heinlein">
        Robert A. Heinlein
       </a>
       and published in April and May 1949
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Boys%27_Life" title="Boys' Life">
         Boys' Life
        </a>
       </i>
       , a magazine of the
       <a href="/wiki/Boy_Scouts_of_America" title="Boy Scouts of America">
        Boy Scouts of America
       </a>
       , who jointly hold copyright with Heinlein, dated 1976.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EU_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-EU-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The story is about a boy who tries to become an
       <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Scout_(Boy_Scouts_of_America)" title="Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America)">
        Eagle Scout
       </a>
       on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Though the story is not usually counted among the Future History stories, Heinlein wrote in a foreword in
       <i>
        Expanded Universe
       </i>
       that "This story is compatible with the so-called 'Future History' series. It is also part of my continuing post-War-II attempt to leave the SF-pulp field and spread out."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EU_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-EU-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Heinlein wrote a "teleplay" or treatment, date uncertain but possibly sometime in 1959. It was not published or produced.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The short story is approximately 30 pages (13,000 words
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ) as collected in
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expanded_Universe_(Heinlein)" title="Expanded Universe (Heinlein)">
         Expanded Universe
        </a>
       </i>
       (1980).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The story was again collected in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Off_the_Main_Sequence" title="Off the Main Sequence">
         Off the Main Sequence
        </a>
       </i>
       (2005).
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Setting">
        Setting
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nothing_Ever_Happens_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Setting">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The story is set on the Moon, in the mid 21st century or later.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       During the adventure, Heinlein weaves many explanations of problems, details and consequences of surviving on the moon under harsh lunar conditions, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Vacuum" title="Vacuum">
        vacuum
       </a>
       , temperature extremes, the extended lunar "day" and reduced
       <a href="/wiki/Gravity" title="Gravity">
        gravity
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">
        Plot summary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nothing_Ever_Happens_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot summary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Bruce is an
       <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Scout_(Boy_Scouts_of_America)" title="Eagle Scout (Boy Scouts of America)">
        Eagle Scout
       </a>
       from Colorado on his way to Venus with a three-week layover on the Moon. He hopes to qualify as an Eagle Scout (Luna) and then go on to become an Eagle Scout (Venus), the first triple Eagle. Shortly after his arrival on the Moon things get off to a rocky start when he gets a hard time from the troop he's assigned to, because nobody, not even the scoutmaster, believes he can make the grade. But quickly he is taken good care of by another scout, Sam, as the troop suits up for a hike. After some amount of bounding interrupted with a short section where they ski over powdery dust, the troop builds a pressurized shelter for some sleep.
      </p>
      <p>
       The next day they hike to Base Camp, which are some caves roughly but adequately furnished with an oxygen-producing hydroponic garden, solar panels, and sealed pressure. From here Sam and Bruce go on a two-person hike across rough terrain, up a cliff face and down another.
      </p>
      <p>
       Disaster strikes when the pitons break loose and Sam is injured in a fall. Bruce rigs a makeshift toboggan to haul Sam and they set out across the plain. A second catastrophe sees them falling into one of numerous dust-filled sinkholes, trapping them in a cavern. At first Sam loses hope, but Bruce is steadfast. After trying to find a way out through the cave and trying to shift the dustpile to expose the hole they fell through, nothing works. Their air is running out and as Bruce gets discouraged it is Sam's turn to bolster their courage. Bruce tries again to find a route out of the caverns and finally gets them onto the surface where they repeat "M'aidez" (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayday_(distress_signal)" title="Mayday (distress signal)">
        Mayday
       </a>
       ) into their radio as their air fails and they are found by the troop.
      </p>
      <p>
       As Bruce is recuperating, the scoutmaster tells him that they will get him ready for the examination in two weeks time, whatever it takes.
      </p>
