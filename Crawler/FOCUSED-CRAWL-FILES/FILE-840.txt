      <p>
       <b>
        Beer
       </b>
       is a relatively small
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_craters" title="Lunar craters">
        lunar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">
        impact crater
       </a>
       located on the
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Imbrium" title="Mare Imbrium">
        Mare Imbrium
       </a>
       , to the east of the crater
       <a href="/wiki/Timocharis_(crater)" title="Timocharis (crater)">
        Timocharis
       </a>
       . Just to the northwest is the matching twin
       <a href="/wiki/Feuill%C3%A9e_(crater)" title="Feuillée (crater)">
        Feuillée
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Beer is a circular, cup-shaped crater with a sharp-edged rim that has not been significantly eroded. The interior has a higher
       <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
        albedo
       </a>
       than the surrounding
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_mare" title="Lunar mare">
        lunar mare
       </a>
       , which is usually an indication of a relatively young crater. A string of craterlets arc away from the rim to the southwest, known as Fossa Archimedes.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_mare" title="Lunar mare">
        mare
       </a>
       to the east has a higher
       <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">
        albedo
       </a>
       than the surrounding surface, and this lighter-
       <a href="/wiki/Hue" title="Hue">
        hued
       </a>
       surface reaches to the base of the
       <a href="/wiki/Montes_Archimedes" title="Montes Archimedes">
        Montes Archimedes
       </a>
       . To the southeast of Beer is a
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_dome" title="Lunar dome">
        lunar dome
       </a>
       that is of comparable diameter to the crater.
      </p>
