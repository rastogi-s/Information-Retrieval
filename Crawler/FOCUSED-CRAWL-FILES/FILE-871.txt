      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Scarab
        </b>
       </i>
       is a new generation
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_rover" title="Lunar rover">
        lunar rover
       </a>
       designed to explore the lunar south pole.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        Scarab
       </i>
       is capable of autonomously traversing in dark polar craters. It uses laser mapping to navigate and carries a science payload. The science payload is capable of taking a 1m core sample and analyzing it for water and gasses. Scarab is also used to test varying mobility techniques and next generation lunar wheels. It is being developed by the
       <a href="/wiki/Robotics_Institute" title="Robotics Institute">
        Robotics Institute
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">
        Carnegie Mellon University
       </a>
       , supported by
       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
        NASA
       </a>
       .
      </p>
