      <p>
       <b>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Total_eclipse" title="Total eclipse">
         Total solar eclipses
        </a>
        on the
        <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
         Moon
        </a>
       </b>
       are caused when the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       passes in front of the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       , blocking much its light. Viewers on Earth will see a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Total_lunar_eclipse" title="Total lunar eclipse">
        total lunar eclipse
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The next total solar eclipse on the Moon will be in January 2018.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Length">
        Length
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Total_eclipses_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Length">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Unlike the Earth which sees its eclipse for up to 2 1/2 hours the longest in one area as the Moon's shadow touches for over 5 1/2 hours the longest. Some of the eclipses on the Moon lasts for about 5 hours in one area the longest as the Earth's shadow touches for over 6 hours the longest.
      </p>
      <p>
       Some eclipses are longer when the Earth is slightly closer (on Earth, the Moon is seen with its apparent diameter is larger than the sun's), some eclipses are shorter when the Earth is slightly further (on Earth, the Moon is seen with its apparent diameter is smaller than the sun's).
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="Center_of_the_Earth.27s_shadow">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Center_of_the_Earth's_shadow">
        Center of the Earth's shadow
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Total_eclipses_on_the_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Center of the Earth's shadow">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Unlike the Earth which receives the smallest portion being inside the center of the moon's shadow during total solar eclipses. During its total eclipses on the Moon, the center of the Earth's shadow covers the whole Nearside part of the Moon and lasts much longer than on Earth with up to around 2.8 hours.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Some total-partial eclipses have a most, half or a part of the Moon being in the center of the Earth's shadow.
      </p>
      <p>
       Unlike the Earth which it umbral shadow shows black, as the Moon has no atmosphere, the surface appears red because the only sunlight available is refracted through the Earth's atmosphere on the edges of the earth as it is shown in the sky in a painting by
       <a href="/wiki/Lucien_Rudaux" title="Lucien Rudaux">
        Lucien Rudaux
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rudaux's_painting_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Rudaux's_painting-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
