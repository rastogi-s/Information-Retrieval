      <p>
       <b>
        "The Moon"
       </b>
       was the
       <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">
        Romanian
       </a>
       entry in the
       <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2000" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2000">
        Eurovision Song Contest 2000
       </a>
       , performed in
       <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">
        English
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Taxi_(Romanian_band)" title="Taxi (Romanian band)">
        Taxi
       </a>
       . This was the first Romanian entry not to feature any lyrics in
       <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">
        Romanian
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The song is a ballad which gains in tempo as it develops. Lead singer
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Teodorescu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dan Teodorescu (page does not exist)">
        Dan Teodorescu
       </a>
       addresses his lover, telling her that she has been able to look into his soul and find the humanity within it. He sings that as a result, "the wind is playing with the stars of my night" and "the moon is playing on and on and on with my heart". Musically, the song is accentuated by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan_pipes" title="Pan pipes">
        pan pipes
       </a>
       , played by
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Georgiana_Pan%C4%83&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Georgiana Pană (page does not exist)">
        Georgiana Pană
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The song was performed sixth on the night (following
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Mestari" title="Sofia Mestari">
        Sofia Mestari
       </a>
       with "
       <a href="/wiki/On_aura_le_ciel" title="On aura le ciel">
        On aura le ciel
       </a>
       " and preceding
       <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">
        Malta
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Claudette_Pace" title="Claudette Pace">
        Claudette Pace
       </a>
       with "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Desire_(Eurovision_song)" title="Desire (Eurovision song)">
        Desire
       </a>
       "). At the close of voting, it had received 25 points, placing 17th in a field of 24, Romania's best result at the time.
      </p>
      <p>
       The result was not, however, good enough to qualify Romania for the
       <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2001" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2001">
        2001 Contest
       </a>
       . As a "passive participant" (a country broadcasting but not entering that year's Contest), Romania was re-admitted the following year. Thus, the song was succeeded as
       <a href="/wiki/Romania_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Romania in the Eurovision Song Contest">
        Romanian representative
       </a>
       at the
       <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2002" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2002">
        2002 Contest
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Monica_Anghel" title="Monica Anghel">
        Monica Anghel
       </a>
       &amp;
       <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Pavel" title="Marcel Pavel">
        Marcel Pavel
       </a>
       with "
       <a href="/wiki/Tell_Me_Why_(Monica_Anghel_and_Marcel_Pavel_song)" title="Tell Me Why (Monica Anghel and Marcel Pavel song)">
        Tell Me Why
       </a>
       ".
      </p>
