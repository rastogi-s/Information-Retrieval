      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Agreement Governing the Activities of States on the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies
       </b>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       better known as the
       <b>
        Moon Treaty
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        Moon Agreement
       </b>
       , is an international treaty that turns
       <a href="/wiki/Jurisdiction" title="Jurisdiction">
        jurisdiction
       </a>
       of all celestial bodies (including the orbits around such bodies) over to the international community. Thus, all activities must conform to international law, including the
       <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" title="United Nations Charter">
        United Nations Charter
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In practice it is a failed treaty because it has not been ratified by any state that engages in self-launched
       <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">
        manned space exploration
       </a>
       or has plans to do so (e.g. the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       , the larger part of the member states of the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">
        European Space Agency
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
        Russia
       </a>
       (former
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet Union
       </a>
       ),
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">
        People's Republic of China
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japan
       </a>
       ) since its creation in 1979, and thus has a negligible effect on actual spaceflight. As of January 2018, it had been ratified by 18 states.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untdb_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-untdb-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Provisions">
        Provisions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moon_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Provisions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       As a follow-on to the
       <a href="/wiki/Outer_Space_Treaty" title="Outer Space Treaty">
        Outer Space Treaty
       </a>
       , the Moon Treaty intended to establish a
       <a href="/wiki/Regime" title="Regime">
        regime
       </a>
       for the use of the Moon and other
       <a href="/wiki/Astronomical_object" title="Astronomical object">
        celestial bodies
       </a>
       similar to the one established for the
       <a href="/wiki/Seabed" title="Seabed">
        sea floor
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">
        United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea
       </a>
       . The treaty would apply to the Moon and to other
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celestial_bodies" title="Celestial bodies">
        celestial bodies
       </a>
       within the
       <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">
        Solar System
       </a>
       , other than
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       , including
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orbits" title="Orbits">
        orbits
       </a>
       around or other trajectories to or around them.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The treaty makes a declaration that the Moon should be used for the benefit of all states and all peoples of the international community. It also expresses a desire to prevent the Moon from becoming a source of international conflict. To those ends the treaty does the following:
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Bans any military use of celestial bodies, including weapon testing or as military bases.
       </li>
       <li>
        Bans all exploration and uses of celestial bodies without the approval or benefit of other states under the
        <a href="/wiki/Common_heritage_of_mankind" title="Common heritage of mankind">
         common heritage of mankind
        </a>
        principle (article 11).
       </li>
       <li>
        Requires that the
        <a href="/wiki/Secretary-General_of_the_United_Nations" title="Secretary-General of the United Nations">
         Secretary-General
        </a>
        must be notified of all celestial activities (and discoveries developed thanks to those activities).
       </li>
       <li>
        Declares all states have an equal right to conduct research on celestial bodies.
       </li>
       <li>
        Declares that for any samples obtained during research activities, the state that obtained them must consider making part of it available to all countries/scientific communities for research.
       </li>
       <li>
        Bans altering the environment of celestial bodies and requires that states must take measures to prevent accidental contamination.
       </li>
       <li>
        Bans any state from claiming
        <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">
         sovereignty
        </a>
        over any territory of celestial bodies.
       </li>
       <li>
        Bans any ownership of any extraterrestrial property by any organization or person, unless that organization is international and governmental.
       </li>
       <li>
        Requires an
        <a href="/wiki/International_regime" title="International regime">
         international regime
        </a>
        be set up to ensure safe and orderly development and management of the resources and sharing of the benefits from them.
       </li>
      </ul>

         Participation in the Moon Treaty
         <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column">
          <span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #00aa00; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">
          </span>
          Parties
         </div>
         <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column">
          <span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #ffff00; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">
          </span>
          Signatories
         </div>
         <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column">
          <span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #ff1111; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">

          Non-parties
         </div>

      <p>
       The treaty was finalized in 1979 and entered into force for the
       <a href="/wiki/Ratification" title="Ratification">
        ratifying
       </a>
       parties in 1984. As of January 2018, 18 states are parties to the treaty,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untdb_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-untdb-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       seven of which ratified the agreement and the rest acceded.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untdb_1-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-untdb-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNOOSA_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-UNOOSA-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Four additional states have signed but not ratified the treaty.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untdb_1-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-untdb-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNOOSA_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-UNOOSA-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/L5_Society" title="L5 Society">
        L5 Society
       </a>
       and others successfully opposed ratification of the treaty by the United States Senate.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oneilchilds_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-oneilchilds-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The objection to the treaty by the spacefaring nations is held to be the requirement that extracted resources (and the technology used to that end) must be shared with other nations. The similar regime in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">
        United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea
       </a>
       is believed to impede the development of such industries on the seabed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
