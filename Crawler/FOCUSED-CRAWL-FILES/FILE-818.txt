      <p>
       <b>
        Pasiphae
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="'p' in 'pie'">
            p
           </span>
           <span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">
            ə
           </span>
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
            s
           </span>
           <span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">
            ɪ
           </span>
           <span title="'f' in 'find'">
            f
           </span>
           <span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">
            ə
           </span>
           <span title="/./: syllable break">
            .
           </span>
           <span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">
            iː
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key">
        <i title="English pronunciation respelling">
         pə-
         <span style="font-size:90%">
          SIF
         </span>
         -ə-ee
        </i>
       </a>
       ;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       [
       <i>
        Πασιφάη
       </i>
       ]
       <span class="error" style="font-size:100%; font-style:normal;">
        error: {{lang-xx}}: text has italic markup (
        <a href="/wiki/Category:Lang_and_lang-xx_template_errors" title="Category:Lang and lang-xx template errors">
         help
        </a>
        )
       </span>
       ; formerly
       <b>
        Pasiphaë
       </b>
       )
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retrograde_motion" title="Retrograde motion">
        retrograde
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irregular_satellite" title="Irregular satellite">
        irregular satellite
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">
        Jupiter
       </a>
       . It was discovered in 1908 by
       <a href="/wiki/Philibert_Jacques_Melotte" title="Philibert Jacques Melotte">
        Philibert Jacques Melotte
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melotte_1908_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Melotte_1908-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harvard_1908_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Harvard_1908-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and later named after the mythological
       <a href="/wiki/Pasipha%C3%AB" title="Pasiphaë">
        Pasiphaë
       </a>
       , wife of
       <a href="/wiki/Minos" title="Minos">
        Minos
       </a>
       and mother of the
       <a href="/wiki/Minotaur" title="Minotaur">
        Minotaur
       </a>
       from
       <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">
        Greek legend
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       It was first spotted on a plate taken at the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Greenwich_Observatory" title="Royal Greenwich Observatory">
        Royal Greenwich Observatory
       </a>
       on the night of February 28, 1908. Inspection of previous plates found it as far back as January 27. It received the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provisional_designation" title="Provisional designation">
        provisional designation
       </a>
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         1908 CJ
        </span>
       </b>
       , as it was not clear whether it was an asteroid or a moon of Jupiter. The recognition of the latter case came by April 10.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-discovery_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-discovery-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Pasiphae did not receive its present name until 1975;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       before then, it was simply known as
       <b>
        <span class="nowrap">
         Jupiter VIII
        </span>
       </b>
       . It was sometimes called "Poseidon"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaposchkin_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gaposchkin-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       between 1955 and 1975.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Orbit">
        Orbit
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pasiphae_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Orbit">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="400" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg/300px-TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg/450px-TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg/600px-TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg.png 2x" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TheIrregulars_JUPITER_retro.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Retrograde irregular satellites of Jupiter.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Pasiphae orbits Jupiter on a high eccentricity and high inclination retrograde orbit. It gives its name to the
       <a href="/wiki/Pasiphae_group" title="Pasiphae group">
        Pasiphae group
       </a>
       , irregular retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at distances ranging between 22.8 and 24.1 million km, and with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inclination" title="Inclination">
        inclinations
       </a>
       ranging between 144.5° and 158.3°.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SheppardJewittPorco2004_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SheppardJewittPorco2004-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The orbital elements are as of January 2000.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jacobson_2000_1-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-Jacobson_2000-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They are continuously changing due to
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        solar
       </a>
       and planetary perturbations. The diagram illustrates its orbit in relation to other retrograde irregular satellites of Jupiter. The eccentricity of selected orbits is represented by the yellow segments (extending from the
       <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
        pericentre
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">
        apocentre
       </a>
       ). The outermost regular satellite
       <a href="/wiki/Callisto_(moon)" title="Callisto (moon)">
        Callisto
       </a>
       is located for reference.
      </p>
      <p>
       Pasiphae is also known to be in a
       <a href="/wiki/Secular_resonance" title="Secular resonance">
        secular resonance
       </a>
       with Jupiter (tying the longitude of its perijove with the longitude of perihelion of Jupiter).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nesvorny2004_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Nesvorny2004-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_characteristics">
        Physical characteristics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pasiphae_(moon)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Physical characteristics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       With diameter estimated at 58 km Pasiphae is the largest retrograde and third largest irregular satellite after
       <a href="/wiki/Himalia_(moon)" title="Himalia (moon)">
        Himalia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Elara_(moon)" title="Elara (moon)">
        Elara
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Spectroscopical measurements in
       <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">
        infrared
       </a>
       indicate that Pasiphae is a
       <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_spectrum" title="Electromagnetic spectrum">
        spectrally
       </a>
       featureless object, consistent with the suspected
       <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">
        asteroidal
       </a>
       origin of the object. Pasiphae is believed to be a fragment from a captured
       <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">
        asteroid
       </a>
       along with other Pasiphae group satellites.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SheppardJewitt2003_14-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SheppardJewitt2003-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the visual spectrum the satellite appears grey (
       <a href="/wiki/Color_index" title="Color index">
        colour indices
       </a>
       B-V=0.74, R-V=0.38) similar to
       <a href="/wiki/C-type_asteroid" title="C-type asteroid">
        C-type asteroids
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grav2003_15-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Grav2003-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
