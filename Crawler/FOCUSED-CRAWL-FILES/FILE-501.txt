      <p>
       <b>
        Mont Blanc
       </b>
       is a mountain in the
       <a href="/wiki/Montes_Alpes" title="Montes Alpes">
        Montes Alpes
       </a>
       range on the
       <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">
        Moon
       </a>
       . It is located on the western edge of the range, near the shore of
       <a href="/wiki/Mare_Imbrium" title="Mare Imbrium">
        Mare Imbrium
       </a>
       , at
       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">
        <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Mont_Blanc_(Moon)&amp;params=45.48_N_0.42_E_globe:Moon">
         <span class="geo-nondefault">
          <span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           <span class="latitude">
            45°29′N
           </span>
           <span class="longitude">
            0°25′E
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="geo-multi-punct">
          ﻿ / ﻿
         </span>
         <span class="geo-default">
          <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           45.48°N 0.42°E
          </span>
          <span style="display:none">
           ﻿ /
           <span class="geo">
            45.48; 0.42
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       . Its width is about 25 kilometers; the height is 3.7–3.8 km above adjacent plains of Mare Imbrium and 1.12 km above lunar level of zero elevation (a sphere with radius 1737.4 km).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JMARS_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-JMARS-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The name of
       <a href="/wiki/Mont_Blanc" title="Mont Blanc">
        Mont Blanc
       </a>
       , the highest mountain of terrestrial
       <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">
        Alps
       </a>
       , was proposed for this mountain by
       <a href="/wiki/Johann_Hieronymus_Schr%C3%B6ter" title="Johann Hieronymus Schröter">
        Johann Hieronymus Schröter
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blagg_1935_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Blagg_1935-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was approved by
       <a href="/wiki/International_Astronomical_Union" title="International Astronomical Union">
        International Astronomical Union
       </a>
       in 1935.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blagg_1935_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Blagg_1935-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazetteer_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-gazetteer-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is the only summit of Montes Alpes with proper name and the only extraterrestrial mountain, whose international name contains French word "Mont" instead of Latin "
       <a href="/wiki/Mons_(planetary_nomenclature)" title="Mons (planetary nomenclature)">
        Mons
       </a>
       ".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Despite statements that lunar Mont Blanc, like terrestrial one, is a highest mountain of its Alps,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atlas_2012_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Atlas_2012-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       measurements of
       <a href="/wiki/Lunar_Reconnaissance_Orbiter" title="Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter">
        Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter
       </a>
       show that it is only third, being 600 meters lower than the highest one and about 100 m lower than the second.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JMARS_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-JMARS-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
