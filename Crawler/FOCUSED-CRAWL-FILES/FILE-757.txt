      <p>
       <b>
        Kyoko Fukada
       </b>
       <span style="font-weight: normal">
        (
        <span lang="ja">
         深田 恭子
        </span>
        <span style="display:none">
         ,
        </span>
        <i>
         Fukada Kyōko
        </i>
        , born November 2, 1982)
       </span>
       is a Japanese actress and singer. In Japan, her name is sometimes
       <a href="/wiki/Japanese_abbreviated_and_contracted_words" title="Japanese abbreviated and contracted words">
        contracted
       </a>
       to the nickname
       <b>
        Fukakyon
       </b>
       . She won the award for Best Actress at the
       <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Film_Festival" title="Yokohama Film Festival">
        Yokohama Film Festival
       </a>
       for
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Kamikaze_Girls" title="Kamikaze Girls">
         Kamikaze Girls
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
        Biography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kyoko_Fukada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Fukada Kyoko was born and raised in Tokyo, Japan. She has a younger sister. Fukada debuted in 1997 with her first appearance in the television drama
       <i>
        Sore ga Kotae da!
       </i>
       as Kazune. Fukada appeared in her first movie
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Ring_2" title="Ring 2">
         Ring 2
        </a>
       </i>
       as Kanae Sawaguchi in 1999 and in later in School Day of the Dead, released in 2000. That same year, she graduated from
       <a href="/wiki/Horikoshi_High_School" title="Horikoshi High School">
        Horikoshi High School
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2002, she made an appearance in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Dolls_(2002_film)" title="Dolls (2002 film)">
         Dolls
        </a>
       </i>
       as Haruna. Fukada is also a
       <a href="/wiki/J-pop" title="J-pop">
        J-pop
       </a>
       singer and her debut single "The Last Fruit" led to her album
       <i>
        Dear
       </i>
       , both released in 1999.
      </p>
