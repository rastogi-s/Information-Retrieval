      <p>
       <b>
        Uruguayan Portuguese
       </b>
       (
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         <i>
          português uruguaio
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <small>
        locally
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">
         [poɾtuˈɣes uɾuˈɣwajo]
        </a>
       </span>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
          <i>
           fronteiriço
          </i>
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       (
       <small>
        locally
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">
         [fɾõteˈɾiso]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) and
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
          <i>
           portunhol riverense
          </i>
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       (
       <small>
        locally
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">
         [poɾtuˈɲɔɫ riveˈɾẽse]
        </a>
       </span>
       ), is a variety of
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       with influences from
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       . It is spoken in northern
       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
        Uruguay
       </a>
       , near the Brazilian border, mainly in the region of the twin cities of
       <a href="/wiki/Rivera" title="Rivera">
        Rivera
       </a>
       (Uruguay) and
       <a href="/wiki/Santana_do_Livramento" title="Santana do Livramento">
        Santana do Livramento
       </a>
       (Brazil). This section of the frontier is called
       <i>
        Frontera de la Paz
       </i>
       (Border of Peace), because there is no legal obstacle to crossing the
       <a href="/wiki/Border" title="Border">
        border
       </a>
       between the two countries.
      </p>
      <p>
       The varieties of Uruguayan Portuguese share many similarities with the countryside dialects of the southern Brazilian state of
       <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">
        Rio Grande do Sul
       </a>
       , such as the
       <a href="/wiki/Denasalization" title="Denasalization">
        denasalization
       </a>
       of final unstressed
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nasal_vowels" title="Nasal vowels">
        nasal vowels
       </a>
       , replacement of
       <a href="/wiki/Palatal_lateral_approximant" title="Palatal lateral approximant">
        lateral palatal
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʎ/
       </span>
       with
       <a href="/wiki/Semivowel" title="Semivowel">
        semivowel
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /j/
       </span>
       , failure to apply the raising rule to final unstressed
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /e/
       </span>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alveolar_trill" title="Alveolar trill">
        alveolar trill
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /r/
       </span>
       instead of the
       <a href="/wiki/Guttural_R" title="Guttural R">
        guttural R
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Lateral_consonant" title="Lateral consonant">
        lateral
       </a>
       realization of coda
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /l/
       </span>
       instead of
       <a href="/wiki/L-vocalization" title="L-vocalization">
        L-vocalization
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Recent changes in Uruguayan Portuguese include the urbanization of this variety, acquiring characteristics from urban
       <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Portuguese" title="Brazilian Portuguese">
        Brazilian Portuguese
       </a>
       such as distinction between
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʎ/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /j/
       </span>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Affrication" title="Affrication">
        affrication
       </a>
       of
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /t/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /d/
       </span>
       before
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /i/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ĩ/
       </span>
       , and other features of
       <a href="/wiki/Television_in_Brazil" title="Television in Brazil">
        Brazilian broadcast media
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Uruguayan_Portuguese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The origin of Portuguese in Uruguay can be traced back to the time of the dominion of the kingdoms of
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
        Portugal
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Brazil" title="Empire of Brazil">
        Empire of Brazil
       </a>
       . In those times, the ownership of those lands was not very well defined, passing back and forth from the hands of one crown to the other. Before its independence after the
       <a href="/wiki/Cisplatine_War" title="Cisplatine War">
        Cisplatine War
       </a>
       in 1828, Uruguay was one of the provinces of the
       <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Brazil" title="Empire of Brazil">
        Empire of Brazil
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Portuguese was the only language spoken throughout northern Uruguay until the end of the 19th century. To assure the homogeneity of the newly formed country, the government made an effort to impose the Spanish language into
       <a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">
        lusophone
       </a>
       communities through educational policies and language planning, and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bilingualism" title="Bilingualism">
        bilingualism
       </a>
       became widespread and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diglossic" title="Diglossic">
        diglossic
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The varieties of Uruguayan Portuguese vary in
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect_continuum" title="Dialect continuum">
        dialect continuum
       </a>
       which range from
       <a href="/wiki/Rioplatense_Spanish" title="Rioplatense Spanish">
        Rioplatense Spanish
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Portuguese" title="Brazilian Portuguese">
        Brazilian Portuguese
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Nevertheless, it has one variant which is the most used, and could be taken as a case study: this variant is geographically located on the area having the cities of Rivera and Santana do Livramento as its center, and expanding over a strip of several kilometers parallel to the border, including territory of both nations.
      </p>

      <p>
       The Riverense language does not possess a formally defined orthography, but in this article an orthography of Portuñol will be presented in order to enable its
       <a href="/wiki/Phoneme" title="Phoneme">
        phonemes
       </a>
       to be represented in the most accurate and consistent possible way, highlighting the
       <a href="/wiki/Phonology" title="Phonology">
        phonologic
       </a>
       features of this language variety. It should be noted that not all Portuñol-speaking persons use the same pronunciation for the same words (as is the case with most languages). Nevertheless, the script that is chosen is very representative of the most frequent and distinctive features.
      </p>
      <p>
       The chosen representation is the closest to the one that would be used if we tried to transcribe the phonemes to the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       (because this is the language taught to Uruguayans, which is the nationality of the majority of speakers of this dialect), except for the phonemes that can't be represented through the Spanish alphabet, like, for example the
       <a href="/wiki/Nasal_vowel" title="Nasal vowel">
        nasal vowels
       </a>
       .
      </p>
