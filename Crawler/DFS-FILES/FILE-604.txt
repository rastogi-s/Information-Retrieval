      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         La Solidaridad
        </b>
       </i>
       (The Solidarity) was an organization created in Spain on December 13, 1888. Composed of Filipino liberals exiled in 1872 and students attending
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       's universities, the organization aimed to increase Spanish awareness of the needs of its colony, the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       , and to propagate a closer relationship between the colony and Spain.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Headed by
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Rizal" title="José Rizal">
        José Rizal
       </a>
       's cousin, Galicano Apacible, it also issued a newspaper of the same name which was published in
       <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">
        Barcelona
       </a>
       , Spain on February 15, 1889. It was edited by
       <a href="/wiki/Graciano_L%C3%B3pez_Jaena" title="Graciano López Jaena">
        Graciano López Jaena
       </a>
       and later on by
       <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_H._del_Pilar" title="Marcelo H. del Pilar">
        Marcelo H. del Pilar
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solidaridad_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solidaridad-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The social, cultural, and economic conditions of the colonial Philippines was published in
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       . Speeches of the Spanish liberals about the Philippines was also featured in the newspaper.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_solidaridad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       was established to express the goal of the
       <a href="/wiki/Propaganda_Movement" title="Propaganda Movement">
        Propaganda Movement
       </a>
       towards achieving assimilation with Spain.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schumacher_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Schumacher-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The first issue of
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       came out on February 15, 1889. A fortnightly and a bi-weekly newspaper,
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       serves as the principal organ of the Reform Movement in Spain.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solidaridad_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solidaridad-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Comite de Propaganda
       </i>
       in the Philippines funded the publication of the
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       . The editorship for the newspaper was first offered to Rizal. However, he refused because during that time he was annotating
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Morga" title="Antonio de Morga">
        Antonio de Morga
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas
       </i>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
        London
       </a>
       . After Rizal,
       <a href="/wiki/Graciano_L%C3%B3pez_Jaena" title="Graciano López Jaena">
        Graciano López Jaena
       </a>
       was offered for the editorship of
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       and he accepted.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solidaridad_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solidaridad-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On April 25, 1889,
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       published the letter entitled "The aspirations of the Filipinos" which was written by the
       <i>
        Asociación Hispano-Filipina de Madrid
       </i>
       (English:
       <span lang="en" xml:lang="en">
        Hispanic Filipino Association of Madrid
       </span>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schumacher_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Schumacher-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It pursued desires for:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Representation in the
        <a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">
         Cortes
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        Abolition of censure
       </li>
       <li>
        An expressed and definite prohibition of the existing practices of exiling residents by purely administrative order, and without a writ of execution from the courts of justice.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       On December 15, 1889, Marcelo H. del Pilar replaced Graciano López Jaena as the editor of
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solidaridad_2-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Solidaridad-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schumacher_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Schumacher-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Under his editorship, the aims of the newspaper expanded. His articles caught the attention of Spanish leaders and ministers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schumacher_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Schumacher-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Using propaganda, it pursued desires for:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        That the Philippines be a province of Spain
       </li>
       <li>
        Representation Filipino priests instead of Spanish friars —
        <a href="/wiki/Augustinians" title="Augustinians">
         Augustinians
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">
         Dominicans
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/wiki/Franciscans" title="Franciscans">
         Franciscans
        </a>
        — in parishes and remote
        <i>
         sitios
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        Freedom of assembly and speech
       </li>
       <li>
        Equal rights before the law (for both Filipino and Spanish plaintiffs)
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       After years of publication from 1889 to 1895, funding of the
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       became scarce.
       <i>
        Comite de Propaganda'
       </i>
       s contribution to the newspaper stopped and del Pilar funded the newspaper almost on his own. Penniless in Spain, del Pilar stopped the publication of
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       on November 15, 1895, with 7 volumes and 160 issues. In del Pilar's farewell editorial, he said :
      </p>
      <table class="cquote pullquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
       <tr>
        <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
         “
        </td>
        <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
         <i>
          We are persuaded that no sacrifices are too little to win the rights and the liberty of a nation that is oppressed by slavery.
         </i>
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
          <a href="#cite_note-5">
           [5]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </td>
        <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
         ”
        </td>
       </tr>
      </table>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_contributors">
        Notable contributors
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=La_solidaridad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notable contributors">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Several writers contributed to
       <i>
        La Solidaridad
       </i>
       over its six years of existence, like
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Luna" title="Antonio Luna">
        Antonio Luna
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anastacio_Carpio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anastacio Carpio (page does not exist)">
        Anastacio Carpio
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mariano_Ponce" title="Mariano Ponce">
        Mariano Ponce
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Ma._Regidor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Antonio Ma. Regidor (page does not exist)">
        Antonio M.
        <sup>
         <u>
          a
         </u>
        </sup>
        Regidor
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Maria_Panganiban" title="Jose Maria Panganiban">
        Jose Maria Panganiban
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Isabelo_de_los_Reyes" title="Isabelo de los Reyes">
        Isabelo de los Reyes
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eduardo_de_Lete&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eduardo de Lete (page does not exist)">
        Eduardo de Lete
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Alejandrino" title="José Alejandrino">
        José Alejandrino
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Paterno" title="Pedro Paterno">
        Pedro Paterno
       </a>
       . One of the most prolific contributors though was Rizal's confidant
       <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Blumentritt" title="Ferdinand Blumentritt">
        Ferdinand Blumentritt
       </a>
       , whose impassioned defense of the Filipino interests was said to have been inspirational to the other writers and the readers of the newspaper alike.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schumacher_3-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Schumacher-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
