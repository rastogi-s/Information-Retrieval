      <p>
       <b>
        Argentia
       </b>
       is a Canadian commercial seaport and industrial Park located in the Town of
       <a href="/wiki/Placentia,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Placentia, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Placentia, Newfoundland and Labrador
       </a>
       . It is situated on the southwest coast of the Avalon Peninsula and defined by a triangular shaped
       <a href="/wiki/Headlands_and_bays" title="Headlands and bays">
        headland
       </a>
       which reaches northward out into Placentia Bay creating a natural harbour 3 kilometres in length.
      </p>
      <p>
       Originally settled by the French in the 1630s that fishing settlement was called Petit Plaisance, meaning "Pleasant Little Place". The name was retained in English (Little Placentia) when the French lost control of the area following the Treaty of Utrecht in 1713. The census of 1706 records 149 individuals in 14 habitations. The community adopted its present name (unofficially in 1895 and officially in 1901) for the presence of silver ore near Broad Cove Point on the east side of the harbour.
      </p>
      <p>
       The name "Argentia" is Latin, meaning "Land of Silver" and was chosen by Father John St. John, the parish priest at Holy Rosary Parish from September 18, 1895 to February 11, 1911. The Silver Cliff Mine operated until the early 1920s but was never profitable. Through most of the 19th century, the fishery was the lifeblood of the community; the Commission of government built a herring factory at Argentia in 1936.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The first church and school were established by Father Pelagius Nowlan in 1835. He was from Ireland and moved to Newfoundland as a missionary priest. In 1836, population was made up 484 people in 76 houses.
      </p>

      <p>
       Construction started on a branch line to nearby
       <a href="/wiki/Placentia,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Placentia, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Placentia
       </a>
       from the Harbour Grace Railway mainline near
       <a href="/wiki/Whitbourne,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Whitbourne, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Whitbourne
       </a>
       (what would later become part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_Railway" title="Newfoundland Railway">
        Newfoundland Railway
       </a>
       ) on October 14, 1886 and the 26 miles of track were completed by October 1888. This line became known as the "Placentia Branch" and it served as a key route to Placentia and the nearby port and anchorage of Little Placentia where coastal ferries would run to
       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_outport" title="Newfoundland outport">
        outports
       </a>
       along the south coast of the island.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Newfoundland Railway chose
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Channel-Port_aux_Basques,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Channel-Port aux Basques, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Port aux Basques
       </a>
       to be its western terminus in 1893 and a new ferry intended for service to
       <a href="/wiki/North_Sydney,_Nova_Scotia" title="North Sydney, Nova Scotia">
        North Sydney
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">
        Nova Scotia
       </a>
       was built in
       <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">
        Scotland
       </a>
       . In October, 1897 the new vessel named the SS
       <i>
        Bruce
       </i>
       arrived but the docks at Port aux Basques had not been completed. As a result, from October until June, 1898 (when it reverted to Port aux Basques), the
       <i>
        Bruce
       </i>
       operated first from Placentia and then from Little Placentia to North Sydney.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Second_World_War">
        Second World War
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second World War">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       War between
       <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
        Britain
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">
        Nazi Germany
       </a>
       was declared on September 3, 1939 in the aftermath of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitler" title="Hitler">
        Hitler
       </a>
       's invasion of
       <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">
        Poland
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Argentia was selected in 1940 to be the location of the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">
        United States Navy
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Argentia" title="Naval Station Argentia">
        Naval Station Argentia
       </a>
       being built under the U.S.-British destroyers for bases exchange (which preceded the introduction of Lend Lease in 1941) which saw fifty obsolescent US destroyers given to Britain in exchange for control of selected lands controlled by Britain in the
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">
        Western Hemisphere
       </a>
       . The Argentia site was selected due to its proximity to Europe, the relatively ice free nature of Placentia Bay, the safe navigational access channel, the sheltered harbour with secure deepwater anchorages nearby at
       <a href="/wiki/Fox_Harbour,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Fox Harbour, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Fox Harbour
       </a>
       and Ship Harbour, as well as the local topography for an airfield and the existing railway line.
      </p>
      <p>
       The base was urgently needed as part of the trans-
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">
        Atlantic
       </a>
       supply line which joined
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       to Britain, in order to provide
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-submarine" title="Anti-submarine">
        anti-submarine
       </a>
       patrols to protect shipping from the
       <a href="/wiki/Kriegsmarine" title="Kriegsmarine">
        German
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">
        U-boat
       </a>
       fleet.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Lend-lease_arrangement">
        Lend-lease arrangement
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lend-lease arrangement">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The land beneath the village was traded to the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       for construction of the base under the
       <a href="/wiki/Destroyers_for_Bases_Agreement" title="Destroyers for Bases Agreement">
        Destroyers for Bases Agreement
       </a>
       and the residents of Argentia and
       <a href="/wiki/Marquise,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Marquise, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Marquise
       </a>
       received the following notices:
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        In exercise of the powers conferred upon me by the Defence (requisition of land) Regulations, made under the Emergency Powers Defence Act 1940, on the 28th day of December AD 1940, I do authorize all persons who shall be engaged by the
        <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">
         United States Government
        </a>
        or its agents and contractors on the construction for that government of any naval, military or air works at Argentia to do any work on any land or place any thing in, on, or over any land upon the Argentia Peninsula, insofar as it shall be necessary for any such person so to do for the carrying out of any such work of construction including any preliminary work in relation thereto.
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Provided, however, that this present authority shall not be valid to authorize the demolition, pulling down or destruction of any building or erection upon any such land, or the doing of any act which renders any such building or erection intangible.
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Signed, Wilfrid Woods, Commissioner for Public Utilities
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        "The Defence (requisition of land) Regulations made under the Emergency Powers Defence Act 1940 on the 28th day of December, A.D., 1940.
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        I have to notify you that the lands and buildings lately belonging to and occupied by you at Argentia, for which said lands and buildings payment has been awarded, are required for occupation by the Government of Newfoundland not later than ________. Take notice, therefore, that the said premises must be completely vacated by you and peaceably yielded up to the Government of Newfoundland, its servants, agents, on or before the date mentioned.
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Signed: WW Woods, Commissioner for Public Utilities"
       </i>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Relocation">
        Relocation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Relocation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="955" height="131" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg/260px-Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg/390px-Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg/520px-Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg 2x" width="260"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Carlb-placentia-nfld-2002.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         When Argentia village was demolished during the Second World War and its people were moved mostly to nearby Placentia
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Most people relocated to the nearby villages of
       <a href="/wiki/Freshwater,_Placentia_Bay,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Freshwater, Placentia Bay, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Freshwater
       </a>
       or Placentia. However what little had been paid as compensation (usually no more than a few thousand dollars for homeowners in Argentia) proved inadequate for building equivalent new homes due to severe wartime shortages of labour and materials.
      </p>
      <p>
       Those buried in the three local graveyards were exhumed and reburied in a new
       <a href="/wiki/Cemetery" title="Cemetery">
        cemetery
       </a>
       constructed by the US forces at the insistence of the local parish priest, Father A.J. Dee, who had also raised objections to the wartime delays in finding new housing for Argentia's living residents who were being forced to leave the village. The abandoned homes were ultimately burned or levelled by bulldozers.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_flag" title="US flag">
        US flag
       </a>
       was raised in Argentia on February 13, 1941.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Naval_Station_Argentia">
        Naval Station Argentia
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Naval Station Argentia">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Argentia" title="Naval Station Argentia">
        Naval Station Argentia
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Throughout 1940–1941 the U.S. Navy constructed an airfield and navy base and built an extension to the Newfoundland Railway to service their facilities, owing to the condition of local roads. The navy base construction in particular was a priority with
       <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Argentia" title="Naval Station Argentia">
        Navy Operating Base Argentia
       </a>
       being officially commissioned on July 15, 1941.
      </p>

         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="610" data-file-width="740" height="206" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4d/HMS_Prince_of_wales_argentia.jpg/250px-HMS_Prince_of_wales_argentia.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4d/HMS_Prince_of_wales_argentia.jpg/375px-HMS_Prince_of_wales_argentia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4d/HMS_Prince_of_wales_argentia.jpg/500px-HMS_Prince_of_wales_argentia.jpg 2x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HMS_Prince_of_wales_argentia.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/HMS_Prince_of_Wales_(53)" title="HMS Prince of Wales (53)">
          HMS
          <i>
           Prince of Wales
          </i>
         </a>
         in Ship Harbour, Newfoundland for the
         <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Charter" title="Atlantic Charter">
          Atlantic Charter
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The reason for the rush was made clear on August 7, 1941 when the heavy cruiser
       <a href="/wiki/USS_Augusta_(CA-31)" title="USS Augusta (CA-31)">
        USS
        <i>
         Augusta
        </i>
        (CA-31)
       </a>
       carrying U.S. President
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt">
        Franklin D. Roosevelt
       </a>
       arrived at an anchorage outside Argentia near Ship Harbour. Roosevelt inspected the base construction progress and did some fishing from
       <i>
        Augusta
       </i>
       over the next two days.
       <i>
        Augusta
       </i>
       was joined by the British warship
       <a href="/wiki/HMS_Prince_of_Wales_(53)" title="HMS Prince of Wales (53)">
        HMS
        <i>
         Prince of Wales
        </i>
       </a>
       carrying British Prime Minister
       <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">
        Winston Churchill
       </a>
       on August 9, 1941. While in the Ship Harbour anchorage from August 9–12, the chiefs of staff of Britain and the U.S. met to discuss the war strategies and logistics once the U.S. joined the war. The two leaders and their aides also negotiated the wording of a press release that they called a "joint statement". Though not drafted as a legal agreement requiring signatures, Roosevelt and Churchill did sign their own draft markups of the joint statement which was issued as a press release on August 14, 1941, in Washington, D.C. and was issued simultaneously in London, England. Several days later the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Daily_Herald_(UK_newspaper)" title="Daily Herald (UK newspaper)">
         Daily Herald
        </a>
       </i>
       , a London newspaper, would characterize the contents of that press release as the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Charter" title="Atlantic Charter">
        Atlantic Charter
       </a>
       . statement.
      </p>
      <p>
       On August 28, 1941 Naval Station Argentia was officially commissioned by the U.S. Navy. Argentia would prove to be an important base in the U.S. war effort; by 1943 with the U.S. fully involved in the Second World War, Argentia saw upwards of 10,000 U.S. personnel passing through on the way to the European Theatre. An adjoining
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">
        United States Army
       </a>
       base was established as Fort McAndrew to provide anti-aircraft artillery protection for the navy base and naval air station. In 1946 Fort McAndrew became part of the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">
        United States Army Air Forces
       </a>
       and was renamed McAndrew Air Force Base in 1948.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cold_War">
        Cold War
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cold War">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       With
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victory_in_Europe" title="Victory in Europe">
        VE
       </a>
       in 1945, Argentia saw a drop in personnel but by the start of the
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">
        Cold War
       </a>
       in 1947–1948, personnel numbers rose to 7,000. By the end of the
       <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">
        Korean War
       </a>
       in 1953, Argentia saw a total of 8,500 personnel posted in the area.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1955 McAndrew AFB was deactivated and turned over to the U.S. Navy as the U.S. Air Force moved its personnel to more remote and northern locations along the coast of Newfoundland and Labrador to build radar stations which would become part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Pinetree_Line" title="Pinetree Line">
        Pinetree Line
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Distant_Early_Warning_Line" title="Distant Early Warning Line">
        DEW Line
       </a>
       systems. In the 1960s
       <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Argentia" title="Naval Station Argentia">
        Naval Station Argentia
       </a>
       became a key "node" in the U.S. Navy's
       <a href="/wiki/SOSUS" title="SOSUS">
        SOSUS
       </a>
       underwater
       <a href="/wiki/Hydrophone" title="Hydrophone">
        hydrophone
       </a>
       system. As such, the base was the target for several
       <a href="/wiki/Espionage" title="Espionage">
        espionage
       </a>
       attempts by the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet Union
       </a>
       . By 1969 the total U.S. Navy and
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">
        Marine Corps
       </a>
       contingents had dropped to 3,000 and to 1,000 by 1971.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Closure_and_abandonment">
        Closure and abandonment
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Closure and abandonment">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       As facilities and structures closed, assets were transferred to the Government of Canada under the terms of the U.S.-Britain lend-lease program; Newfoundland having become a Canadian province in 1949. In 1973 Naval Air Station Argentia was closed and by 1975 the entire north side of the base was out of U.S. hands. In 1994 Naval Operating Base Argentia, was officially decommissioned and the site was transferred to the Government of Canada. From 1994 to 2007, Government of Canada carried out a $106 million environmental remediation program at Argentia which included the removal of many building structures, and the clean up, removal or safe containment of various hazardous materials on the land and in the water about the site. Concurrently, by 2001, the site was turned over to a private, not-for-profit organization to manage and lead the redevelopment of Argentia.
      </p>
      <p>
       With the military base now closed, Argentia has no permanent residents and is zoned as an industrial area in support of its redevelopment as a seaport and industrial park for the Town Of Placentia.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Redevelopment">
        Redevelopment
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Redevelopment">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Argentia became part of Town of Placentia in 1993, along with Freshwater, Dunville, Jerseyside and townside Placentia.
      </p>
      <p>
       By the end of 2001, Government of Canada had negotiated land transfer agreements to return the lands which formerly comprised the U.S. Naval Base to local ownership and control.
       <b>
        Port of Argentia
       </b>
       , is the port and property authority for the industrial area and is utilizing these former naval base properties to lead the redevelopment of the site to revitalize the local economy and quality of life in the region.
      </p>
      <p>
       In June 2002,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CVRD_Inco" title="CVRD Inco">
        Inco
       </a>
       announced an agreement with the Government of
       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Newfoundland and Labrador
       </a>
       on a three phase plan to develop the
       <a href="/wiki/Voisey%27s_Bay" title="Voisey's Bay">
        Voisey's Bay
       </a>
       nickel deposit. The $1 billion initial phase of the Voisey's Bay agreement provided for mine and mill infrastructure development at Voisey's Bay, and a research and development program in hydrometallurgical processing which would include a demonstration plant to be built at Argentia. the demonstration plant at Argentia was constructed and operated from 2004 to 2007. The demonstration plant was an initial step toward the ultimate development of a commercial hydrometallurgical processing facility to be constructed and operated in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Harbour,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Long Harbour, Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Long Harbour, Newfoundland
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In October, 2013, Husky Energy and Government of Newfoundland &amp; Labrador announced an agreement to amend the White Rose Development. White Rose is the name of an oil field offshore Newfoundland. The announcement included the news that this oil resource could be exploited by installing a gravity based 'wellhead platform' structure over the western section of the oil field on the ocean floor in approximately 120 metres of water. To support this billion dollar wellhead platform construction project, Husky announced that it would be establishing a graving dock facility on the Northside Peninsula at Argentia to support the construction of the concrete base of the wellhead platform. Construction of the graving dock began in late 2013 and was finished in early 2015. However, in December 2014, Husky announced that, due to declining oil prices, the decision to construct the Concrete Gravity Base (CGS) had been deferred.
      </p>

      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Airport">
        Airport
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Airport">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The former U.S. Navy Air Station airfield on the Northside Peninsula has not been active for air traffic since 1973 other than for the Air Cadet Gliding Program.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ferry_terminal">
        Ferry terminal
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Ferry terminal">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Argentia_airphoto.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="730" data-file-width="790" height="231" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Argentia_airphoto.jpg/250px-Argentia_airphoto.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Argentia_airphoto.jpg/375px-Argentia_airphoto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Argentia_airphoto.jpg/500px-Argentia_airphoto.jpg 2x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Argentia_airphoto.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Naval Station Argentia
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       By the mid-1960s roads were upgraded between Argentia and the newly opened
       <a href="/wiki/Trans-Canada_Highway" title="Trans-Canada Highway">
        Trans-Canada Highway
       </a>
       at Whitbourne. In 1967 a new
       <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">
        ferry
       </a>
       terminal was opened by
       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_National_Railway" title="Canadian National Railway">
        Canadian National Railway
       </a>
       and the
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MV_Ambrose_Shea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MV Ambrose Shea (page does not exist)">
         Ambrose Shea
        </a>
       </i>
       became the first seasonal ferry to call at the port, largely carrying tourists bound for the Avalon Peninsula (19 hours crossing time) from
       <a href="/wiki/North_Sydney,_Nova_Scotia" title="North Sydney, Nova Scotia">
        North Sydney, Nova Scotia
       </a>
       . In the 1980s the terminal was upgraded by
       <a href="/wiki/CN_Marine" title="CN Marine">
        CN Marine
       </a>
       and in 1989 the company's successor,
       <a href="/wiki/Marine_Atlantic" title="Marine Atlantic">
        Marine Atlantic
       </a>
       , welcomed the
       <a href="/wiki/MV_Joseph_and_Clara_Smallwood" title="MV Joseph and Clara Smallwood">
        MV
        <i>
         Joseph and Clara Smallwood
        </i>
       </a>
       superferry (14 hours crossing time) on the Argentia summer run.
      </p>
