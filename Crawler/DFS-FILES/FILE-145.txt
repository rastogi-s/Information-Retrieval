      <p>
       <b>
        North east down
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        NED
       </b>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        local tangent plane
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        LTP
       </b>
       ), is a geographical coordinate system for representing
       <a href="/wiki/State_vector_(geographical)" title="State vector (geographical)">
        state vectors
       </a>
       that is commonly used in
       <a href="/wiki/Aviation" title="Aviation">
        aviation
       </a>
       . It consists of three numbers: one represents the position along the northern axis, one along the eastern axis, and one represents vertical position. Down is chosen as opposed to up in order to comply with the
       <a href="/wiki/Right-hand_rule" title="Right-hand rule">
        right-hand rule
       </a>
       . The origin of this coordinate system is usually chosen to be the aircraft's center of gravity.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       NED coordinates are similar to
       <a href="/wiki/ECEF" title="ECEF">
        ECEF
       </a>
       in that they're Cartesian, however they can be more convenient due to the relatively small numbers involved, and also because of the intuitive axes. NED and ECEF coordinates can be related with the following formula:
