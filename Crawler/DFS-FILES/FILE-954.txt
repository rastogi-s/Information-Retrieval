      <p>
       <b>
        Moselle Romance
       </b>
       is an extinct
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance language
       </a>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Disputed statement">
          <span title="This claim has reliable sources with contradicting facts (October 2017)">
           disputed
          </span>
         </a>
         <span class="metadata">
          –
          <a href="/wiki/Talk:Moselle_Romance#Does_Moselle_Romance_exist?" title="Talk:Moselle Romance">
           discuss
          </a>
         </span>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       that developed after the fall of the
       <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">
        Roman Empire
       </a>
       along the
       <a href="/wiki/Moselle" title="Moselle">
        Moselle
       </a>
       river in modern-day
       <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
        Germany
       </a>
       , near the border with
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       . Despite heavy Germanic influence, it persisted in isolated pockets until the 11th century.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_background">
        Historical background
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moselle_Romance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical background">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LocatieMoezel.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="447" data-file-width="597" height="262" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/LocatieMoezel.png/350px-LocatieMoezel.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/LocatieMoezel.png/525px-LocatieMoezel.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1f/LocatieMoezel.png 2x" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LocatieMoezel.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The
         <a href="/wiki/Moselle_Valley" title="Moselle Valley">
          Moselle Valley
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       After
       <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">
        Julius Caesar
       </a>
       conquered
       <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">
        Gaul
       </a>
       in 50 BC, a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Roman" title="Gallo-Roman">
        Gallo-Roman
       </a>
       culture gradually developed in what is today France, southern Belgium,
       <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">
        Luxembourg
       </a>
       , and the region between
       <a href="/wiki/Trier" title="Trier">
        Trier
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">
        Koblenz
       </a>
       . By contrast, the adjacent province of
       <a href="/wiki/Germania_Inferior" title="Germania Inferior">
        Germania Inferior
       </a>
       and part of
       <a href="/wiki/Germania_Superior" title="Germania Superior">
        Germania Superior
       </a>
       retained a Germanic character throughout the Imperial period.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Emergence_of_Moselle_Romance">
        Emergence of Moselle Romance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moselle_Romance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Emergence of Moselle Romance">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Angles" title="Angles">
        Angles
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Saxons" title="Saxons">
        Saxons
       </a>
       , en route to
       <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
        England
       </a>
       from their ancestral lands in the western
       <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">
        Netherlands
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
        Germany
       </a>
       , carved a path through
       <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">
        Holland
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">
        Flanders
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/County_of_Brabant" title="County of Brabant">
        Brabant
       </a>
       and sent the local
       <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">
        Franks
       </a>
       fleeing southeast along the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ourthe_River" title="Ourthe River">
        Ourthe
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauer_River" title="Sauer River">
        Sauer
       </a>
       rivers to the region around
       <a href="/wiki/Metz" title="Metz">
        Metz
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Upper_Moselle" title="Upper Moselle">
        Upper Moselle
       </a>
       . This drove a sixty-kilometer wedge between the Gallo-Romans in the Trier-Koblenz area and their linguistic brethren in the rest of
       <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">
        Gaul
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Judging by the archeological evidence, these newly arrived Franks practiced farming and animal husbandry about
       <a href="/wiki/Bitburg" title="Bitburg">
        Bitburg
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Gutland_(Luxembourg)" title="Gutland (Luxembourg)">
        Gutland
       </a>
       , the Middle and Upper Saar, and the Moselle Valley – strongly preferring these last two over the others.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       According to linguist Alberto Varvaro the linguistic frontier between German and Latin populations around the 13th century was similar to the present language frontier, but only a few years before there still was a "remaining area of neolatin speakers" in the valleys of the Mosella river (near old Roman
       <a href="/wiki/Trier" title="Trier">
        Treviri
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Decline">
        Decline
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moselle_Romance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Decline">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The local Gallo-Roman placenames suggest that the left bank of the Moselle was Germanized following the 8th century, but the right bank remained Romance-speaking into at least the 10th century. Said names include
       <a href="/wiki/Maring-Noviand" title="Maring-Noviand">
        Maring-Noviand
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Osann-Monzel" title="Osann-Monzel">
        Osann-Monzel
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Longuich" title="Longuich">
        Longuich
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Riol" title="Riol">
        Riol
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hatzenport" title="Hatzenport">
        Hatzenport
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Longkamp" title="Longkamp">
        Longkamp
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Treis-Karden" title="Treis-Karden">
        Karden
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Kr%C3%B6v" title="Kröv">
        Kröv
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Alf,_Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Alf, Rhineland-Palatinate">
        Alf
       </a>
       . This being a wine-growing region, a number of viticultural terms from Moselle Romance have survived in the local German dialect.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sample_text">
        Sample text
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Moselle_Romance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sample text">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The following
       <a href="/wiki/Late_Latin" title="Late Latin">
        Late Latin
       </a>
       inscription from the sixth century shows influence from Moselle Romance:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Hoc tetolo fecet Montana, conlux sua, Mauricio, qui visit con elo annus dodece; et portavit annus qarranta; trasit die VIII K(a)l(endas) Iunias.
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        "For Mauricius his wife Montana who lived with him for twelve years made this gravestone; he was forty years old and died on the 25th of May."
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
