      <p>
       <b>
        Extremaduran
       </b>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endonym" title="Endonym">
        autonym
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        estremeñu
       </i>
       ,
       <small>
        IPA:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Astur-Leonese" title="Help:IPA/Astur-Leonese">
         [eʰtːɾeˈmeɲʊ]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romance_language" title="Romance language">
        Romance linguistic variety
       </a>
       , spoken by several hundred thousand people in
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       , in an area covering the north-western part of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_community" title="Autonomous community">
        autonomous community
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Extremadura" title="Extremadura">
        Extremadura
       </a>
       and adjoining areas in the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salamanca" title="Province of Salamanca">
        province of Salamanca
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ethnologue_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ethnologue-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-proel_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-proel-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is difficult to establish the exact boundary between Extremaduran and the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       dialects spoken in most of Extremadura.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Dialects">
        Dialects
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremaduran_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Dialects">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The linguistic varieties of Extremadura are usually classified in three main branches: Northern or "High" (
       <i>
        artu estremeñu
       </i>
       ), Central or "Middle" (
       <i>
        meyu estremeñu
       </i>
       ), and Southern or "Low" (
       <i>
        baju estremeñu
       </i>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-proel_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-proel-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The northern one is usually considered to be the language proper,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and is spoken in the north-west of the autonomous region of Extremadura, and the south-west of Salamanca, a province of the autonomous region of
       <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">
        Castile and León
       </a>
       . The central and southern ones are spoken in the rest of Extremadura, and are not different enough from standard
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       to be considered anything but dialects of it, since at least the 18th century.
      </p>
      <p>
       Northern Extremaduran is also spoken in a few villages of southern Salamanca, being known there as the "
       <a href="/wiki/Palra_d%27El_Rebollal" title="Palra d'El Rebollal">
        palra d'El Rebollal
       </a>
       ", which is now almost extinct.
      </p>

      <p>
       After the union of the kingdoms of León and Castile (into the "Crown of Castile and León"),
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
          <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2014)">
           when?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       the Castilian language slowly replaced Latin as the official language of the institutions, thus relegating Old Leonese to a sign of poverty and ignorance of those who spoke it. Only in
       <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">
        Asturias
       </a>
       , where the language was born, were people conscious of speaking a language different from
       <a href="/wiki/Castilian_Spanish" title="Castilian Spanish">
        Castilian
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       but even there only some authors used it in their writings.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was probably the cultural upheaval of Spanish-speaking
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">
        Salamanca's University
       </a>
       that was the cause of the rapid Castilianisation of the eastern parts of this province, thus dividing the Astur-Leonese domain between Asturian, Leonese and the Extremaduran in the south of the old Leonese kingdom.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The late 19th century saw the first serious attempt to write in Extremaduran, until then an oral language,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       with the poet
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Gabriel_y_Gal%C3%A1n" title="José María Gabriel y Galán">
        José María Gabriel y Galán
       </a>
       . Born in Salamanca, he lived most of his life in the north of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%A1ceres_(province)" title="Cáceres (province)">
        Cáceres
       </a>
       , Extremadura. He wrote in a local variant of Extremaduran, full of dialectal remains, but always with an eye on Spanish usage.
      </p>
      <p>
       After that, localisms are the pattern in the attempts to defend the Extremaduran language to the extent that today only a few people are trying to revive the language and make northern Extremadura a bilingual region,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       whereas the government and official institutions seem to think the best solution is for northwestern Extremadurans to speak a Castilian dialect without any kind of protection.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There are also attempts to transform the southern Castilian dialects ("
       <a href="/wiki/Cast%C3%BAo" title="Castúo">
        castúo
       </a>
       ", as some people named them using the word which appeared in
       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Chamizo_Trigueros" title="Luis Chamizo Trigueros">
        Luis Chamizo Trigueros
       </a>
       's poems) into a language, which makes it even harder to defend High Extremaduran, considered more frequently a "real" language and makes it easier for the administration to reject co-officiality and the normalisation of Extremaduran.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is in serious danger of extinction, with only the oldest people speaking it at present, while most of the Extremaduran population ignores the language, since the majority of Extremadurans, and even its own speakers, regard it as a poorly spoken Spanish.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2013, the people of
       <a href="/wiki/Serradilla" title="Serradilla">
        Serradilla
       </a>
       created the first feature film in Extremaduran,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Territoriu_de_bandolerus" title="Territoriu de bandolerus">
         Territoriu de bandolerus
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">
        Phonology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremaduran_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Phonology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Features related to
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astur-Leonese" title="Astur-Leonese">
         Astur-Leonese
        </a>
        :
        <ul>
         <li>
          Post-tonic
          <i>
           o
          </i>
          becomes
          <i>
           u
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           oru
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈoɾu]
          </span>
          'gold'.
         </li>
         <li>
          Post-tonic
          <i>
           e
          </i>
          becomes
          <i>
           i
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           calli
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈkaʎi]
          </span>
          or
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈkaʝi]
          </span>
          'street'.
         </li>
         <li>
          Latin word-final
          <i>
           e
          </i>
          , chiefly after
          <i>
           d
          </i>
          , is not lost, e.g.
          <i>
           redi
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈreði]
          </span>
          'net'.
         </li>
         <li>
          Some cases of
          <a href="/wiki/Palatalization_(sound_change)" title="Palatalization (sound change)">
           palatalization
          </a>
          of word-initial
          <i>
           n
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           ñíu
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈɲiu]
          </span>
          'nest'.
         </li>
         <li>
          Conservation of the consonantic group
          <i>
           mb
          </i>
          in intermediate position, e.g.
          <i>
           lambel
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [lamˈbel]
          </span>
          'to lick'.
         </li>
         <li>
          Frequent conservation of word-initial
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [
           <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_glottal_fricative" title="Voiceless glottal fricative">
            h
           </a>
           ]
          </span>
          derived from a Latin
          <i>
           f-
          </i>
          . This consonant is lost in most
          <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
           Spanish
          </a>
          varieties, but is common with much of Andalusia, e.g.
          <i>
           higu
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈhiɣu]
          </span>
          'fig'.
         </li>
         <li>
          Occasional conservation of word-initial
          <i>
           f
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           fogal
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [foˈɣal]
          </span>
          'home, hearth'.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        Features related to southern peninsular
        <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
         Spanish
        </a>
        :
        <ul>
         <li>
          General loss of intervocalic
          <i>
           d
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           mieu
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈmjeu]
          </span>
          'fear'.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Debuccalization" title="Debuccalization">
           Debuccalization
          </a>
          of post-vocalic /s/, /ks/ and /θ/ into
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ʰ]
          </span>
          (
          <i>
           s-aspiration
          </i>
          ), e.g.
          <i>
           estal
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ɛʰtˈtal]
          </span>
          'to be'.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        Other features:
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Infinitive" title="Infinitive">
           Infinitives
          </a>
          in
          <i>
           -l
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           dil
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈdil]
          </span>
          'to go'.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Metathesis_(linguistics)" title="Metathesis (linguistics)">
           Metathesis
          </a>
          of the consonant cluster
          <i>
           rl
          </i>
          into
          <i>
           lr
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           chalral
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [tʃalˈral]
          </span>
          'to talk'.
         </li>
         <li>
          Occasional interchange of the
          <a href="/wiki/Liquid_consonant" title="Liquid consonant">
           liquid
          </a>
          consonants
          <i>
           l/r
          </i>
          , e.g.
          <i>
           craru
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ˈkɾaɾu]
          </span>
          'clear'.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ismael_Carmona_García's_dictionary_12-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-Ismael_Carmona_García's_dictionary-12">
            [12]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </li>
         <li>
          Preservation of some old
          <a href="/wiki/Voice_(phonetics)" title="Voice (phonetics)">
           voiced
          </a>
          fricatives, such as some instances of
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [
           <a href="/wiki/Voiced_dental_fricative" title="Voiced dental fricative">
            ð
           </a>
           ]
          </span>
          corresponding to
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [z]
          </span>
          in Portuguese or
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [
           <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_dental_fricative" title="Voiceless dental fricative">
            θ
           </a>
           ]
          </span>
          corresponding to
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [s]
          </span>
          in Portuguese (both corresponding to /θ/ in Spanish). This feature is an
          <a href="/wiki/Archaism" title="Archaism">
           archaism
          </a>
          preserved from
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Spanish" title="Old Spanish">
           Old Spanish
          </a>
          or Old
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astur-Leonese" title="Astur-Leonese">
           Astur-Leonese
          </a>
          , as it happens only when it is
          <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">
           etymologically
          </a>
          justified. When a voiced fricative appears, one also does in languages such as
          <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
           Catalan
          </a>
          or
          <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
           Portuguese
          </a>
          : Extremaduran
          <i>
           tristeza
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [triʰtˈteða]
          </span>
          'sadness' (still voiced in Portuguese
          <i>
           tristeza
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [tɾiʃˈtezɐ]
          </span>
          , voice lost in
          <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
           Spanish
          </a>
          <i>
           tristeza
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [trisˈteθa]
          </span>
          ), but Extremaduran
          <i>
           cabeça
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [kaˈβeθa]
          </span>
          'head' (voiceless also in Portuguese
          <i>
           cabeça
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [kɐˈβesɐ]
          </span>
          , Spanish
          <i>
           cabeza
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [kaˈβeθa]
          </span>
          ). The feature is dying out quite fast but is found all over the High Extremaduran speaking area.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">
        Morphology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremaduran_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Morphology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Anteposition of the
        <a href="/wiki/Article_(grammar)" title="Article (grammar)">
         article
        </a>
        before the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Possessive_pronoun" title="Possessive pronoun">
         possessive pronoun
        </a>
        , as in
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Spanish" title="Old Spanish">
         Old Spanish
        </a>
        or in many
        <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
         Romance languages
        </a>
        such as
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonese_language" title="Leonese language">
         Leonese
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
         Portuguese
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
         Catalan
        </a>
        or
        <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
         Italian
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Anteposition of the particle
        <i>
         lu
        </i>
        (or
        <i>
         lo
        </i>
        ), in some
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interrogative_sentence" title="Interrogative sentence">
         interrogative sentences
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Use of
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diminutives" title="Diminutives">
         diminutives
        </a>
        <i>
         inu
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         ina
        </i>
        , as heritage from
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonese_language" title="Leonese language">
         Leonese
        </a>
        (as in
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
         Portuguese
        </a>
        ).
       </li>
       <li>
        Occasional formation of
        <a href="/wiki/Gerund" title="Gerund">
         gerund
        </a>
        , derived from a form of the verb in
        <a href="/wiki/Past_tense" title="Past tense">
         past tense
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Usage of a
        <a href="/wiki/Vocative_case" title="Vocative case">
         vocative-exclamative case
        </a>
        . When nouns are in the
        <a href="/wiki/Vocative_case" title="Vocative case">
         vocative
        </a>
        , the closing of post-tonic vowels (
        <i>
         e
        </i>
        into
        <i>
         i
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         o
        </i>
        into
        <i>
         u
        </i>
        ) disappears and those vowels open.
        <i>
         El Ramiru quíi venil
        </i>
        (Ramiro wants to come), but
        <i>
         Ramiro, ven pacá
        </i>
        (Ramiro, come here!).
        <i>
         Sé quién lo vidu, Pepi
        </i>
        (I know who saw it,
        <i>
         Pepe did
        </i>
        ), but
        <i>
         Sé quién lo vidu, Pepe
        </i>
        (I know who saw it,
        <i>
         Pepe
        </i>
        ). This is a charasteristic shared with the
        <a href="/wiki/Fala_language" title="Fala language">
         Fala language
        </a>
        . Extremaduran and the Fala language are actually the only western Romance languages with a distinct form of vocative case for nouns formed with a change in the ending.
       </li>
       <li>
        Usage of the preposition
        <i>
         a
        </i>
        with the verbs
        <i>
         andal
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         estal
        </i>
        indicating static temporal location, contrasting with the usage of
        <i>
         en
        </i>
        .
        <i>
         Está a Caçris
        </i>
        "He's in
        <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1ceres,_Spain" title="Cáceres, Spain">
         Cáceres
        </a>
        (for a few days),
        <i>
         está en Caçris
        </i>
        "He's in Cáceres",
        <i>
         Está pa Caçris
        </i>
        "He's around Cáceres".
       </li>
       <li>
        A very frequent usage of deictic forms to which enclitic pronouns can be added at the end. They can be used in the middle of a sentence:
        <i>
         Velaquí
        </i>
        la mi casa (Here is my house),
        <i>
         velallilu
        </i>
        (there he is), Paquí se curtivan
        <i>
         velaquí
        </i>
        lechugas, millu... (Look, lettuce, corn and so on is grown here).
       </li>
       <li>
        Usage of reduplicated forms of plural pronouns with a reciprocal sense (
        <i>
         ellus y ellus
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         vujotrus y vujotrus
        </i>
        ...):
        <i>
         Estaban brucheandu ellus y ellus
        </i>
        : They were wrestling with each other.
       </li>
      </ul>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Organizations">
        Organizations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremaduran_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Organizations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There is a regional organization in Extremadura, OSCEC Estremaúra,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       that tries to defend the language, one journal (Belsana) and one cultural newspaper, Iventia,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       written in the new unified Extremaduran and the old dialect "
       <a href="/wiki/Palra_d%27El_Rebollal" title="Palra d'El Rebollal">
        palra d'El Rebollal
       </a>
       ".
      </p>
