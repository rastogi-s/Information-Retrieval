      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Royal Spanish Academy
       </b>
       (Spanish:
       <b>
        Real Academia Española
       </b>
       , generally abbreviated as
       <b>
        RAE
       </b>
       ), is the official royal institution responsible for overseeing the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       . It is based in
       <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">
        Madrid
       </a>
       , Spain, but is affiliated with national language academies in 22 other
       <a href="/wiki/Hispanophone" title="Hispanophone">
        hispanophone
       </a>
       (Spanish-speaking) nations through the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_of_Spanish_Language_Academies" title="Association of Spanish Language Academies">
        Association of Academies of the Spanish Language
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The RAE's emblem is a fiery crucible, and its
       <a href="/wiki/Motto" title="Motto">
        motto
       </a>
       is "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <i>
          Limpia, fija y da esplendor
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       " ("Cleans, fixes, and gives splendor").
      </p>
      <p>
       The RAE dedicates itself to
       <a href="/wiki/Language_planning" title="Language planning">
        language planning
       </a>
       by applying
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_prescription" title="Linguistic prescription">
        linguistic prescription
       </a>
       aimed at promoting linguistic unity within and between the various territories, to ensure a common standard
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       in accordance with Article 1 of its founding charter: "... to ensure that the changes the Spanish language undergoes [...] do not break the essential unity it maintains throughout the hispanic world."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The proposed language guidelines are shown in a number of works. The priorities are the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dictionary_of_the_Spanish_language" title="Dictionary of the Spanish language">
        Dictionary of the Spanish Language
       </a>
       formerly known as DRAE (from the Spanish:
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Diccionario de la lengua española de la Real Academia Española
        </span>
       </i>
       ), which has 23 published editions since 1780, and its grammar, last edited in October 2014. The Academy has a formal procedure for admitting words to its publications.
      </p>
      <p>
       The headquarters, opened in 1894, is located at Calle Felipe IV, 4, in the
       <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Madrid" title="Districts of Madrid">
        ward of Jerónimos
       </a>
       , next to the
       <a href="/wiki/Museo_del_Prado" title="Museo del Prado">
        Museo del Prado
       </a>
       . The Center for the Studies of the Royal Spanish Academy, opened in 2007, is located at Calle Serrano 187–189.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Spanish_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="672" data-file-width="439" height="306" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg/200px-Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg/300px-Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg/400px-Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Estatutos_rae_1715big.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/Title_page" title="Title page">
          Title page
         </a>
         of
         <i>
          <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
           Fundación y estatútos de la Real Académia Españóla
          </span>
         </i>
         (Foundation and statutes of the Royal Spanish Academy) (1715)
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Royal Spanish Academy was founded in 1713, modeled after the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusca_Academy" title="Crusca Academy">
        Crusca Academy
       </a>
       (1582), of Italy, and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_academy" title="French academy">
        French Academy
       </a>
       (1635), of France, with the purpose "to fix the voices and vocabularies of the Castilian language with propriety, elegance, and purity". King
       <a href="/wiki/Philip_V_of_Spain" title="Philip V of Spain">
        Philip V
       </a>
       approved its constitution on 3 October 1714, placing it under the Crown's protection.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Its aristocratic founder,
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fern%C3%A1ndez_Pacheco" title="Juan Manuel Fernández Pacheco">
          Juan Manuel Fernández Pacheco
         </a>
        </span>
       </i>
       , Marquis of
       <a href="/wiki/Villena" title="Villena">
        Villena
       </a>
       and Duke of
       <a href="/wiki/Escalona" title="Escalona">
        Escalona
       </a>
       , described its aims as "to assure that Spanish speakers will always be able to read
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cervantes" title="Cervantes">
        Cervantes
       </a>
       " – by exercising a progressive up-to-date maintenance of the formal language.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The RAE began establishing rules for the orthography of Spanish beginning in 1741 with the first edition of the
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Ortographía
        </span>
       </i>
       (spelled
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Ortografía
        </span>
       </i>
       from the second edition onwards). The proposals of the Academy became the official norm in Spain by royal decree in 1844, and they were also gradually adopted by the Spanish-speaking countries in the Americas. Several reforms were introduced in the
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Nuevas Normas de Prosodia y Ortografía
        </span>
       </i>
       (1959, New Norms of Prosody and Orthography), and since then the rules have undergone continued adjustment, in consultation with the other national language academies. The current rules and practical recommendations are presented in the latest edition of the
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Ortografía
        </span>
       </i>
       (1999, Orhtography).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1994, the RAE ruled that the Spanish consonants
       <i>
        "CH"
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         ché
        </span>
       </i>
       ) and
       <i>
        "LL"
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         elle
        </span>
       </i>
       ) would hence be alphabetized under "C" and under "L", respectively, and not as separate, discrete letters, as in the past. The RAE eliminated monosyllabic accented vowels where the accent did not serve in changing the word's meaning, examples include: "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/dio#Spanish" title="wikt:dio">
          dio
         </a>
        </span>
        "
       </i>
       ("gave"), "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/vio#Spanish" title="wikt:vio">
          vio
         </a>
        </span>
        "
       </i>
       ("saw"), both had an acutely accented vowel "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%C3%B3#Spanish" title="wikt:ó">
          ó
         </a>
        </span>
        "
       </i>
       ; yet the monosyllabic word "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/s%C3%A9#Spanish" title="wikt:sé">
          sé
         </a>
        </span>
        "
       </i>
       ("I know", the first person, singular, present of "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/saber#Spanish" title="wikt:saber">
          saber
         </a>
        </span>
        "
       </i>
       , "to know"; and the singular imperative of "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ser#Spanish" title="wikt:ser">
          ser
         </a>
        </span>
        "
       </i>
       , "to be") retains its acutely accented vowel in order to differentiate it from the reflexive pronoun "
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/se#Spanish" title="wikt:se">
          se
         </a>
        </span>
        "
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Fundamentals">
        Fundamentals
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Spanish_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fundamentals">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Article 1 of the statutes of the Royal Spanish Academy says the following:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        [...RAE][…]tiene como misión principal velar porque los cambios que experimente la lengua española en su constante adaptación a las necesidades de sus hablantes no quiebren la esencial unidad que mantiene en todo el ámbito hispánico. Debe cuidar igualmente de que esta evolución conserve el genio propio de la lengua, tal como este ha ido consolidándose con el correr de los siglos, así como de establecer y difundir los criterios de propiedad y corrección, y de contribuir a su esplendor. Para alcanzar dichos fines, estudiará e impulsará los estudios sobre la historia y sobre el presente del español, divulgará los escritos literarios, especialmente clásicos, y no literarios que juzgue importantes para el conocimiento de tales cuestiones, y procurará mantener vivo el recuerdo de quienes, en España o en América, han cultivado con gloria nuestra lengua. Como miembro de la Asociación de Academias de la Lengua Española, mantendrá especial relación con las academias correspondientes y asociadas.
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Which, translated into English, reads:
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        [...] [RAE][…] has as its primary mission to ensure that the changes experienced by the Spanish language in its constant adaptation to the needs of its speakers don’t break the essential unity that maintains in all the Hispanic world. It must equally care that this evolution conserves the genius proper of the language, as it has been consolidating with the centuries, as well as establishing and spreading the criteria of propriety and correction, and of contributing to its splendor. To achieve these ends, it will study and promote the studies about history and about the present of Spanish, it will spread the literary writings, especially classics, and non-literary which it deems important for the knowledge of such matters, and it will attempt to keep alive the memory of those who, in Spain or in the Americas, have cultivated our language with glory. As member of the Association of Academies of the Spanish Language, it will keep a special relation with the corresponding and associated academies.
       </p>
      </blockquote>

      <p>
       Members of the Academy are known as
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Académicos de número
        </span>
       </i>
       (English:
       <span lang="en" xml:lang="en">
        Academic Numerary
       </span>
       ), chosen from among prestigious persons in the arts and sciences, including several
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Spanish_language_authors" title="List of Spanish language authors">
        Spanish-language authors
       </a>
       , known as The Immortals (Spanish:
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Los Inmortales
        </span>
       </i>
       ), similarly to their
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_academy" title="French academy">
        French Academy
       </a>
       counterparts. The numeraries (Spanish:
       <i>
        Números)
       </i>
       are elected for life by the other academicians. Each academician holds a seat labeled with a letter from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_alphabet" title="Spanish alphabet">
        Spanish alphabet
       </a>
       , although upper and lower case letters are separate seats.
      </p>

      <dl>
       <dt>
        Joint publications of the RAE and the Association of Academies of the Spanish Language
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Diccionario de la lengua española (Dictionary of the Spanish Language). The 1st edition was published in 1780, the 22nd edition in 2001 and the 23rd edition in 2014,
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
         <a href="#cite_note-5">
          [5]
         </a>
        </sup>
        which since 2001 can be consulted online for free as of October 2017
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
         <a href="#cite_note-6">
          [6]
         </a>
        </sup>
        and was published in Spain and other Spanish-speaking countries to mark the tricentennial of the founding of the RAE).
        <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
         [
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
           <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
            citation needed
           </span>
          </a>
         </i>
         ]
        </sup>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The
          <i>
           Diccionario esencial de la lengua española
          </i>
          (Essential Dictionary of the Spanish Language) was published in 2006 as a compendium of the 22nd edition of the Dictionary of the Spanish Language.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
           <a href="#cite_note-7">
            [7]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ortograf%C3%ADa_de_la_lengua_espa%C3%B1ola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ortografía de la lengua española (page does not exist)">
         Ortografía de la lengua española
        </a>
        (Orthography of the Spanish Language). The 1st edition was published in 1741 and the latest edition in 2010. The edition of 1999 was the first spelling book to cover the whole Hispanic world, replacing the
        <i>
         Nuevas normas de prosodia y ortografía
        </i>
        (New Rules for Prosody and Spelling) of 1959.
        <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
         [
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
           <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
            citation needed
           </span>
          </a>
         </i>
         ]
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nueva_gram%C3%A1tica_de_la_lengua_espa%C3%B1ola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nueva gramática de la lengua española (page does not exist)">
         Nueva gramática de la lengua española
        </a>
        (New Grammar of the Spanish Language, 1st edition: 1771, latest edition: 2009).
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
         <a href="#cite_note-8">
          [8]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
         <a href="#cite_note-9">
          [9]
         </a>
        </sup>
        The latest edition is the first grammar to cover the whole Hispanic world, replacing the prior
        <i>
         Gramática de la lengua española
        </i>
        (Grammar of the Spanish Language, 1931) and the
        <i>
         Esbozo de una Nueva gramática de la lengua española
        </i>
        (Outline of a New Grammar of the Spanish Language, 1973). The
        <i>
         Nueva gramática de la lengua española
        </i>
        is available in 3 different versions: The
        <i>
         Edición completa
        </i>
        (Complete Edition) includes 3,800 pages in two volumes to describe morphology and syntax (published December 4, 2009) plus a third volume of phonetics and phonology and a DVD (early 2010).
        <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
         [
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
           <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
            citation needed
           </span>
          </a>
         </i>
         ]
        </sup>
        <ul>
         <li>
          The
          <i>
           Manual
          </i>
          edition is a single 750-page volume, which was presented at the 5th Congress of the Spanish Language which convened virtually in
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valpara%C3%ADso,_Chile" title="Valparaíso, Chile">
           Valparaíso, Chile
          </a>
          , due to the
          <a href="/wiki/2010_Chile_earthquake" title="2010 Chile earthquake">
           earthquake
          </a>
          , and was released on April 23, 2010.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
           <a href="#cite_note-10">
            [10]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </li>
         <li>
          The
          <i>
           Gramática básica
          </i>
          (Basic Grammar) is a 305-page volume directed to people who received secondary education, and which can be adaptable for school use; it was first published in 2011.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
           <a href="#cite_note-11">
            [11]
           </a>
          </sup>
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
           <a href="#cite_note-12">
            [12]
           </a>
          </sup>
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
           <a href="#cite_note-13">
            [13]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </li>
         <li>
          The RAE has also published two other works by individual editors:
          <i>
           Gramática de la lengua española
          </i>
          (Grammar of the Spanish Language, by Emilio Alarcos Llorach, 1994) and
          <i>
           Gramática descriptiva de la lengua española
          </i>
          (Descriptive Grammar of the Spanish Language, 3 volumes, directed by Ignacio Bosque and Violeta Demonte, 1999).
          <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
           [
           <i>
            <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
             <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
              citation needed
             </span>
            </a>
           </i>
           ]
          </sup>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Diccionario_panhisp%C3%A1nico_de_dudas" title="Diccionario panhispánico de dudas">
         Diccionario panhispánico de dudas
        </a>
        (Pan-Hispanic Dictionary of Doubts, 1st edition: 2005). Resolves doubts related to the use of the Spanish language. Can be consulted online since 2006.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
         <a href="#cite_note-14">
          [14]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Diccionario del estudiante
        </i>
        (Student's Dictionary, 1st edition: 2005). Directed to students in secondary education between 12 and 18 years-old.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
         <a href="#cite_note-15">
          [15]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           Diccionario práctico del estudiante
          </i>
          (Student's Practical Dictionary, 1st edition: 2007) is an adapted version for Latin America of the Student's Dictionary.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
           <a href="#cite_note-16">
            [16]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Diccionario de Americanismos
        </i>
        (Dictionary of Americanisms) is a listing of Spanish language terms of the Americas and their meaning. First edition published in 2010.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
         <a href="#cite_note-17">
          [17]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
