      <p>
       <b>
        Georgetown University Press
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/University_press" title="University press">
        university press
       </a>
       affiliated with
       <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_University" title="Georgetown University">
        Georgetown University
       </a>
       that publishes about forty new books a year. The press's major subject areas include
       <a href="/wiki/Bioethics" title="Bioethics">
        bioethics
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_affairs" title="International affairs">
        international affairs
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Language_acquisition" title="Language acquisition">
        languages
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">
        linguistics
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">
        political science
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Public_policy" title="Public policy">
        public policy
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">
        religion
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       It was founded in 1964, and is a member of the
       <a href="/wiki/Association_of_American_University_Presses" title="Association of American University Presses">
        Association of American University Presses
       </a>
       (AAUP) and a founding member of the
       <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Jesuit_University_Presses" title="Association of Jesuit University Presses">
        Association of Jesuit University Presses
       </a>
       (AJUP).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sjup_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-sjup-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The press publishes the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Al-Kitaab_series" title="Al-Kitaab series">
         Al-Kitaab
        </a>
       </i>
       <a href="/wiki/Al-Kitaab_series" title="Al-Kitaab series">
        series
       </a>
       , the most widely used set of Arabic language textbook series in the United States.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It also publishes textbooks and digital materials for other languages including
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" title="Mandarin Chinese">
        Mandarin
       </a>
       Chinese,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraqi_Arabic" title="Iraqi Arabic">
        Iraqi Arabic
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Moroccan_Arabic" title="Moroccan Arabic">
        Moroccan Arabic
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_Arabic" title="Syrian Arabic">
        Syrian Arabic
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tajik_language" title="Tajik language">
        Tajik
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Uzbek_language" title="Uzbek language">
        Uzbek
       </a>
       .
      </p>
