      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Organization of American States
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         Organización de los Estados Americanos
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="pt">
         Organização dos Estados Americanos
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="fr">
         Organisation des États américains
        </span>
       </i>
       ), or the
       <b>
        OAS
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        OEA
       </b>
       , is a continental
       <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">
        organization
       </a>
       that was founded on 30 April 1948, for the purposes of regional solidarity and cooperation among its member states. Headquartered in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       capital
       <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">
        Washington, D.C.
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the OAS's members are the 35 independent states of the
       <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">
        Americas
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       As of 26 May 2015, the
       <a href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Secretary General of the Organization of American States">
        Secretary General of OAS
       </a>
       is
       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Almagro" title="Luis Almagro">
        Luis Almagro
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Miami_Herald_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Miami_Herald-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pan_american_1900.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="816" data-file-width="1024" height="175" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Pan_american_1900.jpg/220px-Pan_american_1900.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Pan_american_1900.jpg/330px-Pan_american_1900.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Pan_american_1900.jpg/440px-Pan_american_1900.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pan_american_1900.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The Pan American Union shortly after its construction in 1910
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The notion of an international union in the
       <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">
        New World
       </a>
       was first put forward by
       <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Simón Bolívar">
        Simón Bolívar
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       who, at the 1826
       <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Panama" title="Congress of Panama">
        Congress of Panama
       </a>
       (still being part of Colombia), proposed creating a league of American republics, with a common military, a mutual defense pact, and a supranational parliamentary assembly. This meeting was attended by representatives of
       <a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">
        Gran Colombia
       </a>
       (comprising the modern-day countries of
       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">
        Colombia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">
        Ecuador
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">
        Panama
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
        Venezuela
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
        Peru
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">
        Bolivia
       </a>
       , The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_Central_America" title="United Provinces of Central America">
        United Provinces of Central America
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
        Mexico
       </a>
       but the grandly titled "Treaty of Union, League, and Perpetual Confederation" was ultimately ratified only by Gran Colombia. Bolívar's dream soon floundered with civil war in Gran Colombia, the disintegration of Central America, and the emergence of
       <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">
        national
       </a>
       rather than New World outlooks in the newly independent American republics. Bolívar's dream of American unity was meant to unify Hispanic American nations against external powers.
      </p>
      <p>
       The pursuit of regional solidarity and cooperation again came to the forefront in 1889–1890, at the First
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Conference_of_American_States" title="International Conference of American States">
        International Conference of American States
       </a>
       . Gathered together in Washington, D.C., 18 nations resolved to found the International Union of American Republics, served by a permanent secretariat called the Commercial Bureau of the American Republics (renamed the International Commercial Bureau at the Second International Conference in 1901–1902). These two bodies, in existence as of 14 April 1890, represent the point of inception to which the OAS and its General Secretariat trace their origins.
      </p>
      <p>
       At the Fourth
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Conference_of_American_States" title="International Conference of American States">
        International Conference of American States
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">
        Buenos Aires
       </a>
       , 1910), the name of the organization was changed to the Union of American Republics and the Bureau became the Pan American Union. The
       <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Union_Building" title="Pan American Union Building">
        Pan American Union Building
       </a>
       was constructed in 1910, on
       <a href="/wiki/Constitution_Avenue" title="Constitution Avenue">
        Constitution Avenue
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Northwest,_Washington,_D.C." title="Northwest, Washington, D.C.">
        Northwest, Washington, D.C.
       </a>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pan_American_Union,_Washington,_DC_in_1943.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="6548" data-file-width="8458" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Pan_American_Union%2C_Washington%2C_DC_in_1943.jpg/220px-Pan_American_Union%2C_Washington%2C_DC_in_1943.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Pan_American_Union%2C_Washington%2C_DC_in_1943.jpg/330px-Pan_American_Union%2C_Washington%2C_DC_in_1943.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Pan_American_Union%2C_Washington%2C_DC_in_1943.jpg/440px-Pan_American_Union%2C_Washington%2C_DC_in_1943.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pan_American_Union,_Washington,_DC_in_1943.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Union_Building" title="Pan American Union Building">
          Pan American Union headquarters building
         </a>
         in Washington, D.C., 1943.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the mid-1930s, U.S. President
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt">
        Franklin Delano Roosevelt
       </a>
       organized an inter-American conference in Buenos Aires. One of the items at the conference was a "
       <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">
        League of Nations
       </a>
       of the Americas", an idea proposed by Colombia, Guatemala, and the Dominican Republic.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       At the subsequent Inter-American Conference for the Maintenance of Peace, 21 nations pledged to remain neutral in the event of a conflict between any two members.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The experience of
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        World War II
       </a>
       convinced hemispheric governments that unilateral action could not ensure the territorial integrity of the American nations in the event of external aggression. To meet the challenges of global conflict in the postwar world and to contain conflicts within the hemisphere, they adopted a system of
       <a href="/wiki/Collective_security" title="Collective security">
        collective security
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Treaty_of_Reciprocal_Assistance" title="Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance">
        Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance
       </a>
       (Rio Treaty) signed in 1947 in
       <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">
        Rio de Janeiro
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Ninth
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Conference_of_American_States" title="International Conference of American States">
        International Conference of American States
       </a>
       was held in
       <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">
        Bogotá
       </a>
       between March and May 1948 and led by
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State" title="United States Secretary of State">
        United States Secretary of State
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/George_Marshall" title="George Marshall">
        George Marshall
       </a>
       , a meeting which led to a pledge by members to fight communism in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_hemisphere" title="Western hemisphere">
        western hemisphere
       </a>
       . This was the event that saw the birth of the OAS as it stands today, with the signature by 21 American countries of the
       <a href="/wiki/Charter_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Charter of the Organization of American States">
        Charter of the Organization of American States
       </a>
       on 30 April 1948 (in effect since December 1951). The meeting also adopted the
       <a href="/wiki/American_Declaration_of_the_Rights_and_Duties_of_Man" title="American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man">
        American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man
       </a>
       , the world's first general human rights instrument.
      </p>
      <p>
       The transition from the Pan American Union to OAS would have been smooth if it had not been for the assassination of Colombian leader
       <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Eli%C3%A9cer_Gait%C3%A1n" title="Jorge Eliécer Gaitán">
        Jorge Eliécer Gaitán
       </a>
       . The Director General of the former,
       <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Lleras_Camargo" title="Alberto Lleras Camargo">
        Alberto Lleras Camargo
       </a>
       , became the Organization's first
       <a href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Secretary General of the Organization of American States">
        Secretary General
       </a>
       . The current Secretary General is former Uruguayan minister of foreign affairs
       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Almagro" title="Luis Almagro">
        Luis Almagro
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Significant milestones in the history of the OAS since the signing of the Charter have included the following:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        1959:
        <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Commission on Human Rights">
         Inter-American Commission on Human Rights
        </a>
        created.
       </li>
       <li>
        1959:
        <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Development_Bank" title="Inter-American Development Bank">
         Inter-American Development Bank
        </a>
        created.
       </li>
       <li>
        1960: First application of the
        <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Treaty_of_Reciprocal_Assistance" title="Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance">
         Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance
        </a>
        against the regimen of
        <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Trujillo" title="Rafael Trujillo">
         Rafael Trujillo
        </a>
        in
        <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">
         Dominican Republic
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        1961: Charter of Punta del Este signed, launching the
        <a href="/wiki/Alliance_for_Progress" title="Alliance for Progress">
         Alliance for Progress
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        1962: OAS suspends
        <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">
         Cuba
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        1969:
        <a href="/wiki/American_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="American Convention on Human Rights">
         American Convention on Human Rights
        </a>
        signed (in force since 1978).
       </li>
       <li>
        1970:
        <a href="/wiki/General_Assembly_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="General Assembly of the Organization of American States">
         OAS General Assembly
        </a>
        established as the Organization's supreme decision-making body.
       </li>
       <li>
        1979:
        <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Court_of_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Court of Human Rights">
         Inter-American Court of Human Rights
        </a>
        created.
       </li>
       <li>
        1991: Adoption of Resolution 1080, which requires the Secretary General to convene the
        <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Council_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Permanent Council of the Organization of American States">
         Permanent Council
        </a>
        within ten days of a
        <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">
         coup d'état
        </a>
        in any member country.
       </li>
       <li>
        1994: First
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summit_of_the_Americas" title="Summit of the Americas">
         Summit of the Americas
        </a>
        (
        <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">
         Miami
        </a>
        ), which resolved to establish a
        <a href="/wiki/Free_Trade_Area_of_the_Americas" title="Free Trade Area of the Americas">
         Free Trade Area of the Americas
        </a>
        by 2005.
       </li>
       <li>
        2001:
        <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Democratic_Charter" title="Inter-American Democratic Charter">
         Inter-American Democratic Charter
        </a>
        adopted.
       </li>
       <li>
        2009: OAS revokes 1962 suspension of
        <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">
         Cuba
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        2009: OAS suspends Honduras due to the coup which ousted president
        <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Zelaya" title="Manuel Zelaya">
         Manuel Zelaya
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        2011: OAS lifts the suspension of Honduras with the return of Manuel Zelaya from exile.
       </li>
       <li>
        2017:
        <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
         Venezuela
        </a>
        announces it will begin the process to leave the OAS in response to what it alleged was OAS interference in
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crisis_in_Bolivarian_Venezuela" title="Crisis in Bolivarian Venezuela">
         Venezuela's political crisis
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Goals_and_purpose">
        Goals and purpose
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Goals and purpose">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the words of Article 1 of the Charter, the goal of the member nations in creating the OAS was "to achieve an order of peace and justice, to promote their solidarity, to strengthen their collaboration, and to defend their sovereignty, their territorial integrity, and their independence." Article 2 then defines eight essential purposes:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        To strengthen the peace and security of the continent.
       </li>
       <li>
        To promote and consolidate representative democracy, with due respect for the principle of nonintervention.
       </li>
       <li>
        To prevent possible causes of difficulties and to ensure the pacific settlement of disputes that may arise among the member states.
       </li>
       <li>
        To provide for common action on the part of those states in the event of aggression.
       </li>
       <li>
        To seek the solution of political, judicial, and economic problems that may arise among them.
       </li>
       <li>
        To promote, by cooperative action, their economic, social, and cultural development.
       </li>
       <li>
        To eradicate extreme poverty, which constitutes an obstacle to the full democratic development of the peoples of the hemisphere.
       </li>
       <li>
        To achieve an effective limitation of conventional weapons that will make it possible to devote the largest amount of resources to the economic and social development of the member states.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Over the course of the 1990s, with the end of the
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">
        Cold War
       </a>
       , the return to democracy in
       <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">
        Latin America
       </a>
       , and the thrust toward
       <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">
        globalization
       </a>
       , the OAS made major efforts to reinvent itself to fit the new context. Its stated priorities now include the following:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Strengthening democracy: Between 1962 and 2002, the Organization sent multinational observation missions to oversee free and fair elections in the member states on more than 100 occasions. The OAS also works to strengthen national and local government and electoral agencies, to promote democratic practices and values, and to help countries detect and defuse official corruption.
       </li>
       <li>
        Working for peace: Special OAS missions have supported peace processes in
        <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">
         Nicaragua
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">
         Suriname
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">
         Haiti
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">
         Guatemala
        </a>
        . The Organization has played a leading part in the removal of landmines deployed in member states and it has led negotiations to resolve the continents' remaining border disputes (
        <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">
         Guatemala
        </a>
        /
        <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">
         Belize
        </a>
        ;
        <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
         Peru
        </a>
        /
        <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">
         Ecuador
        </a>
        ). Work is also underway on the construction of a common inter-American counter-terrorism front.
       </li>
       <li>
        Defending human rights: The agencies of the inter-American human rights system provide a venue for the denunciation and resolution of human rights violations in individual cases. They also monitor and report on the general human rights situation in the member states.
       </li>
       <li>
        Fostering free trade: The OAS is one of the three agencies currently engaged in drafting a treaty aiming to establish an
        <a href="/wiki/Free_Trade_Area_of_the_Americas" title="Free Trade Area of the Americas">
         inter-continental free trade area
        </a>
        from
        <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">
         Alaska
        </a>
        to
        <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">
         Tierra del Fuego
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Fighting the drugs trade: The Inter-American Drug Abuse Control Commission was established in 1986 to coordinate efforts and crossborder cooperation in this area.
       </li>
       <li>
        Promoting sustainable development: The goal of the OAS's Inter-American Council for Integral Development is to promote economic development and combating poverty. OAS technical cooperation programs address such areas as river basin management, the conservation of biodiversity, preservation of cultural diversity, planning for global climate change,
        <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_tourism" title="Sustainable tourism">
         sustainable tourism
        </a>
        , and natural disaster mitigation.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Organizational_structure">
        Organizational structure
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [

         ]

       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Organization of American States is composed of an Organization of American States General Secretariat, the Permanent Council, the Inter-American Council for Integral Development, and a number of committees.
      </p>
      <p>
       The General Secretariat of the Organization of American States consists of six
       <a href="/wiki/Secretariat_(administrative_office)" title="Secretariat (administrative office)">
        secretariats
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Political_Affairs" title="Organization of American States Secretariat for Political Affairs">
         Secretariat for Political Affairs
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        Executive Secretariat for Integral Development
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security" title="Organization of American States Secretariat for Multidimensional Security">
         Secretariat for Multidimensional Security
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        Secretariat for Administration and Finance
       </li>
       <li>
        Secretariat for Legal Affairs
       </li>
       <li>
        Secretariat for External Relations
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The various committees of the Organization of American States include:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The Committee on Juridical and Political Affairs
       </li>
       <li>
        The Committee on Administrative and Budgetary Affairs
       </li>
       <li>
        The Committee on Hemispheric Security
       </li>
       <li>
        The Committee on Inter-American Summits Management and Civil Society Participation in OAS Activities
       </li>
      </ul>

      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/General_Assembly_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="General Assembly of the Organization of American States">
        General Assembly
       </a>
       is the supreme decision-making body of OAS. It convenes once every year in a regular session. In special circumstances, and with the approval of two-thirds of the member states, the
       <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Council_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Permanent Council of the Organization of American States">
        Permanent Council
       </a>
       can convene special sessions.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Organization's member states take turns hosting the General Assembly on a rotating basis. The states are represented at its sessions by their chosen delegates: generally, their ministers of foreign affairs, or their appointed deputies. Each state has one vote, and most matters—except for those for which the Charter or the General Assembly's own rules of procedure specifically require a two-thirds majority—are settled by a simple majority vote.
      </p>
      <p>
       The General Assembly's powers include setting the OAS's general course and policies by means of resolutions and declarations; approving its budget and determining the contributions payable by the member states; approving the reports and previous year's actions of the OAS's specialized agencies; and electing members to serve on those agencies.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Canada_and_the_OAS">
        Canada and the OAS
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Canada and the OAS">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Although
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       obtained independence in its foreign policy from the
       <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">
        United Kingdom
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">
        1931
       </a>
       , it chose not to join the OAS when it was first formed, despite its
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada-United_States_relations" title="Canada-United States relations">
        close relations with the United States
       </a>
       . Canada became a Permanent Observer in the OAS on 2 February 1972. Canada signed the Charter of the Organization of American States on 13 November 1989 and this decision was ratified on 8 January 1990.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2004–2005, Canada was the second largest contributor to the OAS, with an annual assessed contribution representing 12.36 percent of the OAS Regular Budget (US$9.2 million) and an additional C$9 million in voluntary contributions to specific projects.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Shortly after joining as a full member, Canada was instrumental in the creation of the Unit for the Promotion of Democracy, which provides support for the strengthening and consolidation of democratic processes and institutions in OAS member states.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sanctions_against_the_Dominican_Republic">
        Sanctions against the Dominican Republic
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sanctions against the Dominican Republic">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       During the 6th Conference of Foreign Ministers of the Organization of American States (OAS) in
       <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">
        Costa Rica
       </a>
       , from 16 to 20 August 1960, a conviction against the State of the Dominican Republic was agreed to unanimously. The penalty was motivated because the foreign ministers checked the veracity of the claim that the
       <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Trujillo" title="Rafael Trujillo">
        Rafael Trujillo
       </a>
       regime had sponsored an attack against
       <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B3mulo_Betancourt" title="Rómulo Betancourt">
        Rómulo Betancourt
       </a>
       , at that time, constitutional president of Venezuela. The meeting was attended by foreign ministers from 21 American nations, including
       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">
        Cuba
       </a>
       , which at that time had not yet been expelled from the inter-American system.
      </p>
      <p>
       All countries, including the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">
        Haiti
       </a>
       broke off diplomatic relations with the Dominican Republic. Additionally an economic blockade that affected the exports of sugar was applied, which at that time was the pillar of the Dominican economy.
      </p>
      <p>
       It was the first application of the
       <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Treaty_of_Reciprocal_Assistance" title="Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance">
        Inter-American Treaty of Reciprocal Assistance
       </a>
       , which had been adopted at the OAS on July 29, 1960.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Status_of_Cuba">
        Status of Cuba
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Status of Cuba">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Further information:
       <a href="/wiki/Cuban_relations_with_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Cuban relations with the Organization of American States">
        Cuban relations with the Organization of American States
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The current government of Cuba was excluded from participation in the Organization under a decision adopted by the Eighth Meeting of Consultation in
       <a href="/wiki/Punta_del_Este" title="Punta del Este">
        Punta del Este
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
        Uruguay
       </a>
       , on 31 January 1962. The vote was passed by 14 in favor, with one against (Cuba) and six abstentions (Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Ecuador, and Mexico). The operative part of the resolution reads as follows:
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <ol>
        <li>
         That adherence by any member of the Organization of American States to Marxism-Leninism is incompatible with the inter-American system and the alignment of such a government with the communist bloc breaks the unity and solidarity of the continents.
        </li>
        <li>
         That the present Government of Cuba, which has officially identified itself as a Marxist-Leninist government, was incompatible with the principles and objectives of the inter-American system.
        </li>
        <li>
         That this incompatibility excluded the present Government of Cuba from participation in the inter-American system.
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
          <a href="#cite_note-14">
           [12]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </li>
       </ol>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       This meant that the Cuban nation was still technically a member state, but that the current government was denied the right of representation and attendance at meetings and of participation in activities. The OAS's position was that although Cuba's participation was suspended, its obligations under the Charter, the
       <a href="/wiki/American_Declaration_of_the_Rights_and_Duties_of_Man" title="American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man">
        American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man
       </a>
       , etc. still hold: for instance, the
       <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Commission on Human Rights">
        Inter-American Commission on Human Rights
       </a>
       continued to publish reports on Cuba's human rights situation and to hear individual cases involving Cuban nationals. However, this stance was occasionally questioned by other individual member states.
      </p>
      <p>
       Cuba's position was stated in an official note sent to the Organization "merely as a courtesy" by Minister of Foreign Affairs Dr.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Roa" title="Raúl Roa">
        Raúl Roa
       </a>
       on 4 November 1964: "Cuba was arbitrarily excluded... The Organization of American States has no juridical, factual, or moral jurisdiction, nor competence, over a state which it has illegally deprived of its rights."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The reincorporation of Cuba as an active member regularly arose as a topic within the inter-American system –  for instance, it was intimated by the outgoing ambassador of Mexico in 1998
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       – but most observers did not see it as a serious possibility while the present government remained in power. Since 1960, the Cuban administration had repeatedly characterized the OAS as the "Ministry of Colonies" of the United States of America.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On 6 May 2005, President
       <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">
        Fidel Castro
       </a>
       reiterated that the island nation would not "be part of a disgraceful institution that has only humiliated the honor of Latin American nations."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       After Fidel Castro's recent retirement and the ascent of his brother
       <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Castro" title="Raúl Castro">
        Raúl
       </a>
       to power, this official position was reasserted.
       <a href="/wiki/President_of_Venezuela" title="President of Venezuela">
        Venezuelan President
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">
        Hugo Chávez
       </a>
       promised to veto any final declaration of the 2009
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summit_of_the_Americas" title="Summit of the Americas">
        Summit of the Americas
       </a>
       due to Cuba's exclusion.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On 17 April 2009, after a "trading of warm words" between the administrations of U.S. President Barack Obama and Cuban leader Raúl Castro, OAS Secretary General
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Insulza" title="José Miguel Insulza">
        José Miguel Insulza
       </a>
       said he would ask the 2009 General Assembly to annul the 1962 resolution excluding Cuba.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       On 3 June 2009, foreign ministers assembled in
       <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Sula" title="San Pedro Sula">
        San Pedro Sula
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">
        Honduras
       </a>
       , for the OAS's 39th
       <a href="/wiki/General_Assembly_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="General Assembly of the Organization of American States">
        General Assembly
       </a>
       , passed a vote to lift Cuba's suspension from the OAS. The United States had been pressuring the OAS for weeks to condition Cuba's readmission to the group on democratic principles and commitment to human rights. Ecuador's Foreign Minister
       <a href="/wiki/Fander_Falcon%C3%AD" title="Fander Falconí">
        Fander Falconí
       </a>
       said there will be no such conditions. "This is a new proposal, it has no conditions—of any kind," Falconí said. "That suspension was made in the
       <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">
        Cold War
       </a>
       , in the language of the Cold War. What we have done here is fix a historic error."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The suspension was lifted at the end of the General Assembly, but, to be readmitted to the Organization, Cuba will need to comply with all the treaties signed by the Member States, including the Inter-American Democratic Charter of 2001.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A statement issued by the Cuban government on 8 June 2009 stated that while Cuba welcomed the Assembly's gesture, in light of the Organization's historical record "Cuba will not return to the OAS."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Suspension_of_Honduras_.282009.E2.80.932011.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Suspension_of_Honduras_(2009–2011)">
        Suspension of Honduras (2009–2011)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Suspension of Honduras (2009–2011)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1599" data-file-width="2222" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg/200px-Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg/300px-Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg/400px-Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Suspensi%C3%B3n_de_Honduras_de_la_OEA.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Those attending the Extraordinary Assembly of the OAS voted to suspend Honduras.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Following
       <a href="/wiki/2009_Honduran_constitutional_crisis" title="2009 Honduran constitutional crisis">
        the expulsion of its President
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Zelaya" title="Manuel Zelaya">
        Manuel Zelaya
       </a>
       , Honduras' membership of the Organization was suspended unanimously at midnight on 5 July 2009.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras_25-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras-25">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">
         de facto
        </a>
       </i>
       government had already announced it was leaving the OAS hours earlier; this was not, however, taken into account by the OAS, which did not recognize that government as legitimate.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Honduras_leaders_pull_out_of_OAS_26-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Honduras_leaders_pull_out_of_OAS-26">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       An extraordinary meeting had been conducted by the OAS in
       <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">
        Washington, D.C.
       </a>
       , with Zelaya in attendance.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras_25-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras-25">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OAS_expels_Honduras'_membership_over_coup_27-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-OAS_expels_Honduras'_membership_over_coup-27">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Organisation_of_American_States_suspends_Honduras_over_coup_28-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Organisation_of_American_States_suspends_Honduras_over_coup-28">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The suspension of Honduras was approved unanimously with 33 votes (Honduras did not vote).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras_25-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras-25">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Organisation_of_American_States_suspends_Honduras_over_coup_28-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Organisation_of_American_States_suspends_Honduras_over_coup-28">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This was the first suspension carried out by the OAS since that of Cuba in 1962.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras_25-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Americas_group_suspends_Honduras-25">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Organisation_of_American_States_suspends_Honduras_over_coup_28-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Organisation_of_American_States_suspends_Honduras_over_coup-28">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       After Zelaya's return to Honduras in 2011, the country was re-admitted to the Organization on 1 June 2011 with 32 votes in favor and 1 (Ecuador) against. Venezuela expressed some reservations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Status_of_Venezuela">
        Status of Venezuela
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Status of Venezuela">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       On 26 April 2017, Venezuela announced its intention to withdraw from the OAS.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Venezuelan
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Minister_of_Venezuela" title="Foreign Minister of Venezuela">
        Foreign Minister
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Delcy_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Delcy Rodríguez">
        Delcy Rodríguez
       </a>
       said that President
       <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Maduro" title="Nicolás Maduro">
        Nicolás Maduro
       </a>
       planned to publicly renounce Venezuela's membership on 27 April 2017. It would take two years for the country to formally leave. During this period, the country does not plan on participating in the OAS.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
        <a href="#cite_note-31">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Permanent_Observers">
        Permanent Observers
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Permanent Observers">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       As of 31 January 2014, there are 69 permanent observer countries including the four countries with territories in the Americas—Denmark, France, the Netherlands, and the United Kingdom; as well as the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
        European Union
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
        <a href="#cite_note-32">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
        <a href="#cite_note-33">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
        <a href="#cite_note-34">
         [32]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Official_languages">
        Official languages
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Official languages">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="467" data-file-width="700" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG/220px-Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG/330px-Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG/440px-Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Socha_Izabely_I_Katolickej.JPG" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Statue of Isabella I the Catholic in front of the seat of the Organization of American States in Washington D.C.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Organization's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Official_languages" title="Official languages">
        official languages
       </a>
       are
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">
        English
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       . The Charter, the basic instrument governing OAS, makes no reference to the use of official languages. These references are to be found in the Rules of Procedure governing the various OAS bodies. Article 51 of the Rules of Procedure of the General Assembly,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
        <a href="#cite_note-35">
         [33]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the supreme body of the OAS, which meets once a year, states that English, French, Portuguese and Spanish are the four official languages. Article 28 stipulates that a Style Committee shall be set up with representatives of the four official languages to review the General Assembly resolutions and declarations. Article 53 states that proposals shall be presented in the four official languages. The Rules of Procedure and Statutes of other bodies, such as the Inter-American Council for Integral Development (CIDI), the Permanent Executive Committee of the Inter-American Council for Integral Development (CEPCIDI), the
       <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Commission_of_Women" title="Inter-American Commission of Women">
        Inter-American Commission of Women
       </a>
       (CIM), the Inter-American Drug Abuse Control Commission (CICAD), the
       <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Commission on Human Rights">
        Inter-American Commission on Human Rights
       </a>
       (IACHR) and the Inter-American Juridical Committee (CJI), technical bodies of the OAS, also mention the four official languages in which their meetings are to be conducted. Policy is therefore dictated through these instruments that require use of the four official languages at meetings.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
        <a href="#cite_note-36">
         [34]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Although a number of other languages have official status in one or more member states of OAS (
       <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">
        Dutch
       </a>
       in Suriname;
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haitian_Creole_language" title="Haitian Creole language">
        Haitian Creole
       </a>
       alongside French in Haiti;
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quechua_language" title="Quechua language">
        Quechua
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Aymara_language" title="Aymara language">
        Aymara
       </a>
       in Peru, Ecuador and Bolivia;
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guaran%C3%AD_language" title="Guaraní language">
        Guaraní
       </a>
       in Paraguay), they are not official languages of the Organization.
      </p>
