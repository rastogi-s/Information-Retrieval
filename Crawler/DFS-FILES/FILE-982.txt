      <p>
       <b>
        Rhaeto-Romance
       </b>
       , or
       <b>
        Rhaetian
       </b>
       , is a traditional subfamily of the
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       that is spoken in north and north-eastern
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       and in
       <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
        Switzerland
       </a>
       . The name "Rhaeto-Romance" refers to the former
       <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">
        Roman
       </a>
       province of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhaetia" title="Rhaetia">
        Rhaetia
       </a>
       . The linguistic basis of the subfamily is discussed in the so-called
       <a href="/wiki/Questione_Ladina" title="Questione Ladina">
        Questione Ladina
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Varieties">
        Varieties
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhaeto-Romance_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Varieties">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="481" data-file-width="593" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png/220px-Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png/330px-Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png/440px-Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rhaeto-Romance_languages_now_and_1200_years_ago..png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Contraction of the area of the Rhaeto-Romance languages
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The area where Rhaeto-Romance languages (also called Ladin languages in a wider sense, not to be confused with Ladino or
       <a href="/wiki/Judaeo-Spanish" title="Judaeo-Spanish">
        Judaeo-Spanish
       </a>
       ) were spoken during the
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">
        Middle Ages
       </a>
       stretched from
       <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
        Switzerland
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/Julian_Alps" title="Julian Alps">
        Julian Alps
       </a>
       (in modern-day western
       <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">
        Slovenia
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       The Rhaeto-Romance languages can be distinguished into the following varieties:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Romansh_language" title="Romansh language">
         Romansh
        </a>
        :
        <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
         Switzerland
        </a>
        . Romansh has status in Switzerland as a national language with three other national languages; however, its usage is rather limited to the canton of
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graub%C3%BCnden" title="Graubünden">
         Graubünden
        </a>
        (Romansh:
        <i>
         Grischun
        </i>
        ). It is composed of the following dialects:
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puter" title="Puter">
           Puter
          </a>
          (Engadin valley)
         </li>
         <li>
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vallader" title="Vallader">
           Vallader
          </a>
          (Engadin valley)
         </li>
         <li>
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surmiran" title="Surmiran">
           Surmiran
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sursilvan" title="Sursilvan">
           Sursilvan
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sutsilvan" title="Sutsilvan">
           Sutsilvan
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rumantsch_Grischun" title="Rumantsch Grischun">
           Rumantsch Grischun
          </a>
          , the standardized literary language, constructed by the Swiss linguist
          <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Schmid" title="Heinrich Schmid">
           Heinrich Schmid
          </a>
          in 1982.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Ladin_language" title="Ladin language">
         Ladin
        </a>
        :
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Ladin_language" title="Ladin language">
           Dolomitic Ladin
          </a>
          in Italy (in
          <a href="/wiki/Trentino" title="Trentino">
           Trentino
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/South_Tyrol" title="South Tyrol">
           South Tyrol
          </a>
          and the
          <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Belluno" title="Province of Belluno">
           province of Belluno
          </a>
          )
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Friulian_language" title="Friulian language">
         Friulian
        </a>
        :
        <a href="/wiki/Friuli" title="Friuli">
         Friuli
        </a>
        region, Italy
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       A phylogenetic classification
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       using basic lexicon identifies a primary split between Romansh in Switzerland and Ladin in Italy. One secondary split distinguishes Engadinic from the other Romansh varieties in Switzerland. In Italy, another secondary split is evidently caused by the Dolomite mountain range that divides Ladin into a northern and a southern subbranch, with Friulian being grouped closest to the southern branch.
      </p>
      <p>
       In this study, the divergence of the Rhaeto-Romance languages from their reconstructed lexical ancestor is about 7% on average. This would correspond to a time depth of about 500 years if the
       <a href="/wiki/Glottochronology" title="Glottochronology">
        glottochronological
       </a>
       replacement rate of 14% per millennium for Romance were trustworthy. However, the earliest available Romance text from the Alpine area is somewhat older and dates to AD 1200.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
