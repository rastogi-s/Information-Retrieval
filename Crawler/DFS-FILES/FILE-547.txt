      <p>
       The
       <b>
        National Congress of Brazil
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         Congresso Nacional do Brasil
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is the legislative body of
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       's federal government. Unlike the
       <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">
        state
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly" title="Legislative Assembly">
        Legislative Assemblies
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%A2mara_municipal" title="Câmara municipal">
        Municipal Chambers
       </a>
       , the Congress is
       <a href="/wiki/Bicameralism" title="Bicameralism">
        bicameral
       </a>
       , composed of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senate_of_Brazil" title="Senate of Brazil">
        Federal Senate
       </a>
       (the
       <a href="/wiki/Upper_house" title="Upper house">
        upper house
       </a>
       ) and the
       <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_(Brazil)" title="Chamber of Deputies (Brazil)">
        Chamber of Deputies
       </a>
       (the
       <a href="/wiki/Lower_house" title="Lower house">
        lower house
       </a>
       ). The Congress meets annually in its
       <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">
        Brasília
       </a>
       seat from 2 February to 27 July and from 1 August to 22 December.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Senate represents the
       <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">
        26 states
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Federal_District_(Brazil)" title="Federal District (Brazil)">
        Federal District
       </a>
       . Each state and the Federal District has a representation of three Senators, who are elected by popular ballot for a term of eight years. Every four years, renewal of either one third or two-thirds of the Senate (and of the delegations of the States and the Federal District) takes place.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Chamber of Deputies represents the people of each state, and its members are elected for a four-year term by a system of
       <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">
        proportional representation
       </a>
       . Seats are allotted proportionally according to each state's population, with each state eligible for a minimum of 8 seats (least populous) and a maximum of 70 seats (most populous). Unlike the Senate, the whole of the Chamber of Deputies is renewed every four years.
      </p>
      <p>
       Until recently it was common for politicians to switch parties and the proportion of congressional seats held by each party would often change. However, a decision of the
       <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Federal_Court" title="Supreme Federal Court">
        Supreme Federal Court
       </a>
       has ruled that the seats belong to the parties and not to the politicians, and that one can only change parties and retain his seat in a very limited set of cases. Consequently, politicians who abandon the party for which they were elected now face the loss of their Congressional seat.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Each house of the Brazilian Congress elects its President and the other members of its directing board from among its members.
      </p>
      <p>
       The President of the Senate is
       <i>
        ex officio
       </i>
       the President of the National Congress, and in that capacity summons and presides over joint sessions, as well as over the joint services of both Houses. The President of the Chamber is second in the
       <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_presidential_line_of_succession" title="Brazilian presidential line of succession">
        presidential line of succession
       </a>
       while the President of the Senate (and of Congress) is third.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_Senate">
        Federal Senate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Congress_of_Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Federal Senate">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Federal Senate (Portuguese: Senado Federal) is the upper house of the National Congress. Created by the first Constitution of the Brazilian Empire in 1824, it was inspired in United Kingdom's House of Lords, but with the Proclamation of the Republic in 1889 it became closer to the United States Senate.
      </p>
      <p>
       Currently, the Senate comprises 81 seats. Three Senators from each of the 26 states and three Senators from the Federal District are elected on a majority basis to serve eight-year terms. Elections are staggered so that two-thirds of the upper house is up for election at one time and the remaining one-third four years later. When one seat is up for election in each State, each voter casts one vote for the Senate; when two seats are up for election, each voter casts two votes, and the voter cannot give his two votes for the same candidate, but, in elections for the renewal of two-thirds of the Senate, each party can present two candidates for election. The candidate in each State and the Federal District (or the first two candidates, when two thirds of the seats are up for election) who achieve the greatest plurality of votes are elected.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Chamber_of_Deputies">
        Chamber of Deputies
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Congress_of_Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chamber of Deputies">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Chamber of Deputies (Câmara dos Deputados) is the lower house of the National Congress, it is composed of 513 federal deputies, who are elected by a proportional representation of votes to serve a four-year term. Seats are allotted proportionally according to each state's population, with each state eligible for a minimum of 8 seats (least populous) and a maximum of 70 seats (most populous).
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2010, 22 out of the country's 35
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Brazil" title="List of political parties in Brazil">
        political parties
       </a>
       were able to elect at least one representative in the Chamber, while fifteen of them were able to elect at least one Senator.
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         See the
         <a href="#Latest_election">
          Latest election
         </a>
         section for election results table.
        </i>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_building">
        The building
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Congress_of_Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The building">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In early 1900s, the Brazilian National Congress happened to be in separate buildings. The Senate was located near Railway Central Station, beside the Republica Square, at Moncorvo Filho Street, where there is today a Federal University of Rio de Janeiro students' center. The Federal Chamber of Deputies was located at Misericórdia Street, which would later be the location of the State of Rio de Janeiro's local Chamber of Deputies. From the 1930s to early 1960s, the Senate occupied the Monroe Palace, which was demolished in the 1970s to allow the construction of the subway Cinelândia station. The Federal Chamber of Deputies moved to Brasília in early 1960s as well, but for a couple of years temporarily occupied a building near the Municipal Theater.
      </p>
      <p>
       Since the 1960s, the National Congress has been located in
       <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">
        Brasília
       </a>
       . As with most of the city's government buildings, the National Congress building was designed by
       <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Niemeyer" title="Oscar Niemeyer">
        Oscar Niemeyer
       </a>
       in the modern Brazilian style.
      </p>
      <p>
       The semi-sphere on the left is the seat of the Senate, and the semi-sphere on the right is the seat of the Chamber of the Deputies. Between them are two vertical office towers. The Congress also occupies other surrounding office buildings, some of them interconnected by a tunnel.
      </p>
      <p>
       The building is located in the middle of the
       <a href="/wiki/Monumental_Axis" title="Monumental Axis">
        Monumental Axis
       </a>
       , main street of Brasília. In front of it there is a large lawn where demonstrations take place. At the back of it, is the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Pra%C3%A7a_dos_Tr%C3%AAs_Poderes" title="Praça dos Três Poderes">
         Praça dos Três Poderes
        </a>
       </i>
       (Three Powers Plaza), where lies the
       <a href="/wiki/Pal%C3%A1cio_do_Planalto" title="Palácio do Planalto">
        Palácio do Planalto
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Federal_Court" title="Supreme Federal Court">
        Supreme Federal Court
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       On December 6, 2007, the Institute of Historic and Artistic National Heritage (
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         Instituto do Patrimônio Histórico e Artístico Nacional
        </span>
       </i>
       ) decided to declare the building of the National Congress a historical heritage of the Brazilian people. The building is also among the
       <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">
        UNESCO World Heritage Sites
       </a>
       , as part of Brasília's original urban buildings, since 1987.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Legislatures">
        Legislatures
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Congress_of_Brazil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Legislatures">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Legislatures are counted from the first meeting of the Chamber of Deputies and of the Senate, on 6 May 1826, in the imperial era (the Chamber of Deputies met for preparatory sessions from 29 April 1826 onwards to elect its officers and conduct other preliminary business, but the Legislature was formally opened on 6 May). The Chamber of Deputies and the Senate were created by Brazil's first
       <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Brazil" title="Constitution of Brazil">
        Constitution
       </a>
       , the Constitution of the
       <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Brazil" title="Empire of Brazil">
        Empire of Brazil
       </a>
       , adopted in 1824. The previous Constituent and Legislative Assembly of the Empire of Brazil, a unicameral National Assembly, that was convened in 1823 and that was dissolved by
       <a href="/wiki/Pedro_I_of_Brazil" title="Pedro I of Brazil">
        Emperor Pedro I
       </a>
       before adopting a Constitution is not counted among the Legislatures. Thus, the numbering includes only the bicameral Legislatures that existed from 1826 to the present day, and includes only Legislatures elected after the adoption of the first Brazilian Constitution.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the imperial era, the national legislature was named
       <i>
        General Assembly
       </i>
       . It was made up of the Chamber of Deputies and the Senate. Senators were elected for life and the Senate was a permanent institution, whereas the Chamber of Deputies, unless dissolved earlier, was elected every four years. When Brazil became a Republic and a Federal State the model of a bicameral Legislature was retained at the Federal level, but the Parliament was renamed
       <i>
        National Congress
       </i>
       . The National Congress is made up of the Chamber of Deputies and the Federal Senate. Both Houses have fixed terms and cannot be dissolved earlier. Under Brazil's present Constitution, adopted in 1988, Senators are elected to eight-year terms, and Deputies are elected every four years.
      </p>
      <p>
       The numbering of the Legislatures is continuous, including the Legislatures of the imperial General Assembly and of the republican National Congress. The inauguration of a new composition of Chamber of Deputies for a four-year term of office marks the start of a new Legislature.
      </p>
