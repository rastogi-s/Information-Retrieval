      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Philippine Revolutionary Army
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">
        Filipino
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <b>
         Panghimagsikang Hukbo ng Pilipinas/Hukbong Pilipinong Mapaghimagsik
        </b>
       </i>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <b>
         Ejército Revolucionario Filipino
        </b>
       </i>
       ), later renamed
       <b>
        Philippine Republican Army
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">
        Filipino
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Hukbong Katihan ng Republika ng Pilipinas
       </i>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Ejército en la República de la Filipina
       </i>
       ) was founded on March 22, 1897 in
       <a href="/wiki/Cavite" title="Cavite">
        Cavite
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Artemio_Ricarte" title="Artemio Ricarte">
        General Artemio Ricarte
       </a>
       was designated as its first
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General" title="General">
        Captain General
       </a>
       during the
       <a href="/wiki/Tejeros_Convention" title="Tejeros Convention">
        Tejeros Convention
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PH_Army_Hist_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-PH_Army_Hist-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This armed force of General
       <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Aguinaldo" title="Emilio Aguinaldo">
        Emilio Aguinaldo
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Tejeros_Convention" title="Tejeros Convention">
        central revolutionary government
       </a>
       replaced the
       <a href="/wiki/Katipunan" title="Katipunan">
        Katipunan
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Irregular_military" title="Irregular military">
        military force
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dumindin_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dumindin-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Revolutionary_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:157px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1098" data-file-width="442" height="385" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png/155px-1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png/233px-1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png/310px-1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png 2x" width="155"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1901_Sartorial_Arts_Journal_Fashion_Plate_Men%27s_Norfolk_Jacket.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The Army's uniforms were patterned after the
         <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_jacket" title="Norfolk jacket">
          Norfolk jacket
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:247px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="465" data-file-width="633" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg/245px-Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg/368px-Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg/490px-Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg 2x" width="245"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Insurgent_soldiers_in_the_Philippines_1899.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Regular soldiers of the Philippine Revolutionary Army stand at attention for an inspection.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Military_history_of_the_Philippines" title="Military history of the Philippines">
        Military history of the Philippines
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_the_Philippine_Revolutionary_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="History of the Philippine Revolutionary Army (page does not exist)">
        History of the Philippine Revolutionary Army
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_weapons_of_the_Philippine_revolution" title="List of weapons of the Philippine revolution">
        List of weapons of the Philippine revolution
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The revolutionary army used the 1896 edition of the Spanish regular army's
       <i>
        Ordenanza del Ejército
       </i>
       to organize its forces and establish its character as a modern army.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Rules and regulations were laid down for the reorganization of the army, along with the regulation of ranks and the adoption of new fighting methods, new rank insignias,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       and a new standard uniform known as the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Rayadillo" title="Rayadillo">
         rayadillo
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">
        Filipino
       </a>
       artist
       <a href="/wiki/Juan_Luna" title="Juan Luna">
        Juan Luna
       </a>
       is credited with this design.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       His brother, General
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Luna" title="Antonio Luna">
        Antonio Luna
       </a>
       commissioned him with the task and personally paid for the new uniforms.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Juan Luna also designed the collar insignia for the uniforms, distinguishing between the services:
       <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">
        infantry
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">
        cavalry
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">
        artillery
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sapper" title="Sapper">
        sappers
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Medic" title="Medic">
        medics
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       At least one researcher has postulated that Juan Luna may have patterned the tunic after the
       <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
        English
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_jacket" title="Norfolk jacket">
        Norfolk jacket
       </a>
       , since the Filipino version is not a copy of any Spanish-pattern uniform.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-combsfil_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-combsfil-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">
        Infantry
       </a>
       officers wore blue pants with a black stripe down the side, while
       <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">
        Cavalry
       </a>
       officers wore red trousers with black stripes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Orders and circulars were issued covering matters such as building trenches and fortifications, equipping every male aged 15 to 50 with bows and arrows (as well as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolo_knives" title="Bolo knives">
        bolo knives
       </a>
       , though officers wielded European swords), enticing Filipino soldiers in the Spanish army to defect, collecting empty cartridges for refilling, prohibiting unplanned sorties, inventories of captured arms and ammunition, fundraising, purchasing of arms and supplies abroad, unification of military commands, and exhorting the rich to give aid to the soldiers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dumindin_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dumindin-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Aguinaldo, a month after he declared Philippine independence, created a pay scale for officers in the army: Following the board, a brigadier general would receive 600 pesos annually, and a sergeant 72 pesos.
      </p>
      <p>
       When the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine%E2%80%93American_War" title="Philippine–American War">
        Philippine–American War
       </a>
       erupted on February 4, 1899, the Filipino army suffered heavy losses on every sector. Even
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Luna" title="Antonio Luna">
        Antonio Luna
       </a>
       urged
       <a href="/wiki/Apolinario_Mabini" title="Apolinario Mabini">
        Apolinario Mabini
       </a>
       , Aguinaldo's chief adviser, to convince the President that
       <a href="/wiki/Guerrilla_warfare" title="Guerrilla warfare">
        guerrilla warfare
       </a>
       must be announced as early as April 1899. Aguinaldo adopted guerilla tactics on November 13, 1899, dissolving what remained of the regular army and after many of his crack units were decimated in set-piece battles.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Arsenal">
        Arsenal
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Revolutionary_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Arsenal">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The main weapon of the new Filipino army was the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Mauser" title="Spanish Mauser">
        Spanish M93
       </a>
       , also the standard infantry arm of the Spanish, and the
       <a href="/wiki/Remington_Arms" title="Remington Arms">
        Remington Spanish rifle
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dumindin_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dumindin-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Crew-served weapons of the Philippine military included
       <a href="/wiki/Lantaka" title="Lantaka">
        lantakas
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Krupp_gun" title="Krupp gun">
        Krupp guns
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Hontoria" title="José González Hontoria">
        Hontoria guns
       </a>
       , an
       <a href="/wiki/Ord%C3%B3%C3%B1ez_guns" title="Ordóñez guns">
        Ordóñez gun
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hotchkiss_gun" title="Hotchkiss gun">
        Hotchkiss guns
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Nordenfelt_gun" title="Nordenfelt gun">
        Nordenfelt guns
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Maxim_gun" title="Maxim gun">
        Maxim guns
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/M1895_Colt%E2%80%93Browning_machine_gun" title="M1895 Colt–Browning machine gun">
        Colt guns
       </a>
       . Also, there were improvised artillery weapons made of water pipes reinforced with bamboo or timber, which can only fire once or twice.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dumindin_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dumindin-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Recruitment_and_conscription">
        Recruitment and conscription
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Revolutionary_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recruitment and conscription">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       During the revolution against
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Katipunan" title="Katipunan">
        Katipunan
       </a>
       gave leaflets to the people to encourage them to join the revolution. Since the revolutionaries had become regular soldiers at the time of
       <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Aguinaldo" title="Emilio Aguinaldo">
        Emilio Aguinaldo
       </a>
       , they started to recruit males and some females aged 15 and above as a form of national service. A few Spanish and Filipino enlisted personnel and officers of the Spanish Army and Spanish Navy defected to the Revolutionary Army, as well as a number of foreign individuals and American defectors who volunteered to join during the course of the revolution.
      </p>
      <p>
       Conscription in the revolutionary army was in effect in the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Military_service" title="Military service">
        military service
       </a>
       was mandatory at that time by the order of Gen.
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Luna" title="Antonio Luna">
        Antonio Luna
       </a>
       , the Chief Commander of the Army during the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine%E2%80%93American_War" title="Philippine–American War">
        Philippine–American War
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
