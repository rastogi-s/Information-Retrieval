      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Argennina
        </b>
       </i>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Spider" title="Spider">
        spider
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">
        genus
       </a>
       with a single species,
       <i>
        <b>
         Argennina unica
        </b>
       </i>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WSC_g8915_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-WSC_g8915-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       found in the southern United States.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Individuals are around 5 mm (0.20 in) in body length. The
       <a href="/wiki/Spider_anatomy#Cephalothorax" title="Spider anatomy">
        carapace
       </a>
       is a pale yellow brown, sparsely covered with short black hairs, and the
       <a href="/wiki/Opisthosoma" title="Opisthosoma">
        abdomen
       </a>
       is gray to pale yellow with fine pale hairs. The species was described in 1936 by
       <a href="/wiki/Willis_J._Gertsch" title="Willis J. Gertsch">
        Willis J. Gertsch
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Stanley_B._Mulaik" title="Stanley B. Mulaik">
        Stanley Mulaik
       </a>
       , and is known from Texas.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gertsch&amp;Mulaik1936_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Gertsch&amp;Mulaik1936-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chamberlin&amp;Gertsch1958_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Chamberlin&amp;Gertsch1958-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
