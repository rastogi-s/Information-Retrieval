      <p>
       The
       <b>
        30th parallel north
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">
        circle of latitude
       </a>
       that is 30
       <a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">
        degrees
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/True_north" title="True north">
        north
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth's
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equatorial plane
       </a>
       . It stands one-third of the way between the
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equator
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">
        North Pole
       </a>
       and crosses
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       It is the approximate southern border of the
       <a href="/wiki/Horse_latitudes" title="Horse latitudes">
        horse latitudes
       </a>
       in the Northern Hemisphere, meaning that much of the land area touching the 30th parallel is
       <a href="/wiki/Arid" title="Arid">
        arid
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semi-arid" title="Semi-arid">
        semi-arid
       </a>
       . If there is a source of wind from a body of water the area would more likely be
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical">
        subtropical
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        sun
       </a>
       is visible for 14 hours, 5 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">
        summer solstice
       </a>
       and 10 hours, 13 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">
        winter solstice
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On 21 June, the maximum altitude of the sun is 83.83 degrees and 36.17 degrees on 21 December.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        One degree of longitude = 96.49 km or 59.95 mi
       </li>
       <li>
        One minute of longitude = 1.61 km or 1.00 mi
       </li>
       <li>
        One second of longitude = 26.80 m or 87.93 ft
       </li>
      </ul>
