      <p>
       <b>
        Equatorial Spanish
       </b>
       , also called
       <i>
        Coastal Colombian-Ecuadorian
       </i>
       dialect or
       <i>
        Chocoano
       </i>
       , is a dialect of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       spoken mainly in the coastal region of
       <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">
        Ecuador
       </a>
       , as well as in the bordering coastal areas of northern
       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
        Peru
       </a>
       and southern
       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">
        Colombia
       </a>
       . It is considered to be transitional between the
       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Spanish" title="Caribbean Spanish">
        Caribbean dialects
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peruvian_Coast_Spanish" title="Peruvian Coast Spanish">
        Peruvian Coast
       </a>
       varieties. The major influential linguistic centers are
       <a href="/wiki/Guayaquil" title="Guayaquil">
        Guayaquil
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Buenaventura,_Valle_del_Cauca" title="Buenaventura, Valle del Cauca">
        Buenaventura
       </a>
       . There is an important subvariety of this dialect which is spoken by most of the communities of African descent dwelling on the border between coastal Colombia (
       <a href="/wiki/Choc%C3%B3_Department" title="Chocó Department">
        Chocó department
       </a>
       ) and Ecuador (
       <a href="/wiki/Esmeraldas_Province" title="Esmeraldas Province">
        Esmeraldas province
       </a>
       ), and which is said to reflect African influence in terms of
       <a href="/wiki/Intonation_(linguistics)" title="Intonation (linguistics)">
        intonation
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Isochrony" title="Isochrony">
        rhythm
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The particular intonation which identifies the speakers of these regions has been a subject of study. Boyd-Bowman (1953) states that the features that he has observed show clearly a phonetic continuity along the coasts of Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru, in contrast to that of their respective Andean provinces.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Boyd-Bowman observes that the present borders of Ecuador with the neighboring countries do not correspond to natural geographic boundaries, nor to linguistic/cultural boundaries (the same Spanish is spoken on both sides), nor to older political borders, whether of the Incas or of the Spanish colonies.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Lipski (1994) also considers the dialect of northern coastal Peru as a distinct variety of Spanish, based on phonetic features.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_phonological_characteristics">
        Notable phonological characteristics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Equatorial_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Notable phonological characteristics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Equatorial Spanish presents markedly attenuated Caribbean features:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Syllable-final
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /s/
        </span>
        is aspirated or elided (although the middle class tends to avoid doing so in the prevocalic environment).
       </li>
       <li>
        As in the
        <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Spanish" title="Caribbean Spanish">
         Caribbean dialects
        </a>
        , the
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /x/
        </span>
        phoneme is realized as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [h]
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Word-final
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /n/
        </span>
        is realized as velar, and sometimes
        <a href="/wiki/Labialization" title="Labialization">
         bilabialized
        </a>
        , especially among speakers of African descent.
       </li>
       <li>
        In rural areas, there is virtually no difference between
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /l/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɾ/
        </span>
        , but
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deletion_(phonology)" title="Deletion (phonology)">
         elision
        </a>
        is rare.
       </li>
       <li>
        In the area of Chocó, intervocalic
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /d/
        </span>
        is realized as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɾ]
        </span>
        . In this same region the aspirated
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /s/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /k/
        </span>
        <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
         [
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
           <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (June 2015)">
            clarification needed
           </span>
          </a>
         </i>
         ]
        </sup>
        may result in a glottal stop.
       </li>
       <li>
        As in virtually all American dialects,
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seseo" title="Seseo">
          seseo
         </a>
        </i>
        is the norm, meaning that the
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /θ/
        </span>
        phoneme of European Spanish is merged with
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /s/
        </span>
        and pronounced
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [s]
        </span>
        . However, there are rural areas on the coast of Ecuador, for instance
        <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo,_Ecuador" title="San Lorenzo, Ecuador">
         San Lorenzo
        </a>
        in
        <a href="/wiki/Esmeraldas_Province" title="Esmeraldas Province">
         Esmeraldas
        </a>
        , where
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceceo" title="Ceceo">
          ceceo
         </a>
        </i>
        (the same merger, with realization as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [θ]
        </span>
        ) is present. This is one of the very few places in the Americas where the merger is treated in this way. (
        <i>
         Ceceo
        </i>
        is common to large areas of
        <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">
         Andalusia
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
         Spain
        </a>
        .)
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeismo" title="Yeismo">
         Yeismo
        </a>
        (merger of traditional
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ʎ/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ʝ/
        </span>
        , with realization as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Voiced_palatal_fricative#Palatal" title="Voiced palatal fricative">
          ʝ
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        ) is also the general rule here, as it is in most of the American Spanish dialects.
       </li>
      </ul>
