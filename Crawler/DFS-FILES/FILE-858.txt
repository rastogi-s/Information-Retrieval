      <p>
       <b>
        Honduran Spanish
       </b>
       is the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       as spoken in the country of
       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">
        Honduras
       </a>
       in Central America. The
       <a href="/wiki/Voseo" title="Voseo">
        voseo
       </a>
       is routinely used in Honduras.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Local_words">
        Local words
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Honduran_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Local words">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       These words are some slang words used in Honduras. Some may also be used in neighboring
       <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">
        El Salvador
       </a>
       and elsewhere.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Bululo - bread roll
       </li>
       <li>
        Trucha or pulpería - corner shop
       </li>
       <li>
        Relajo - mess
       </li>
       <li>
        Jura or chepo - police patrol
       </li>
       <li>
        Posta - police station
       </li>
       <li>
        Maje - dude
       </li>
       <li>
        Cipote(a) - kid (male, when it ends with "e"; and female, when it ends with "a")
       </li>
       <li>
        Güirro(a) - kid (male, when it ends with "o"; and female, when it ends with "a")
       </li>
       <li>
        Juco(a) - Dirty person (male, when it ends with "o"; and female, when it ends with "a")
       </li>
      </ul>
