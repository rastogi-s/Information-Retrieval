      <p>
       <b>
        Ramón Menéndez Pidal
       </b>
       (
       <small>
        Spanish pronunciation:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">
         [raˈmon meˈnendeθ piˈðal]
        </a>
       </span>
       ; 13 March 1869 - 14 November 1968) was a
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spanish
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philologist" title="Philologist">
        philologist
       </a>
       and historian.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gale_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-gale-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He worked extensively on the history of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       and Spanish
       <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">
        folklore
       </a>
       and folk poetry. One of his main topics was the history and legend of
       <a href="/wiki/El_Cid" title="El Cid">
        El Cid
       </a>
       . He was nominated for a
       <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">
        Nobel Prize
       </a>
       151 times,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the most nominated person, three times more than the second most nominated person,
       <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Malraux" title="André Malraux">
        André Malraux
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
        Biography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C3%B3n_Men%C3%A9ndez_Pidal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       He was born in
       <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coruña">
        A Coruña
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">
        Galicia
       </a>
       ), Spain.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gale02_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-gale02-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       His father, Juan Menéndez Fernández, was a
       <a href="/wiki/Lawyer" title="Lawyer">
        lawyer
       </a>
       and a
       <a href="/wiki/Magistrate" title="Magistrate">
        magistrate
       </a>
       , originally from
       <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">
        Asturias
       </a>
       . His mother was Ramona Pidal, also an Asturian. His older brother,
       <a href="/wiki/Juan_Men%C3%A9ndez_Pidal" title="Juan Menéndez Pidal">
        Juan Menéndez Pidal
       </a>
       , whom he outlived by more than fifty years, was also a literary scholar of folk poetry, expert in the
       <i>
        poesía popular
       </i>
       of Asturias. Another older brother,
       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Men%C3%A9ndez_Pidal" title="Luis Menéndez Pidal">
        Luis Menéndez Pidal
       </a>
       was a notable realist painter.
      </p>
      <p>
       He studied at the
       <a href="/wiki/Complutense_University_of_Madrid" title="Complutense University of Madrid">
        University of Madrid
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gale02_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-gale02-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 1899 he obtained the chair in
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_studies" title="Romance studies">
        Romance studies
       </a>
       in the same university, an appointment that he held until his retirement in 1939. In 1900 he married
       <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Goyri_de_Men%C3%A9ndez_Pidal" title="María Goyri de Menéndez Pidal">
        María Goyri
       </a>
       , who in 1896 became the first Spanish woman to receive a degree in
       <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">
        Philosophy
       </a>
       and later, in 1909, became the first woman to attain a non-medical doctorate at a Spanish university. They spent their honeymoon retracing the geographic locales of the
       <i>
        Poem of the Cid
       </i>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Cantar_de_Mio_Cid" title="Cantar de Mio Cid">
        Cantar de Mio Cid
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       Menéndez Pidal was elected to the Spanish Royal Academy (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Real Academia Española">
        Real Academia Española
       </a>
       ) in 1901, and in 1925 its director. However, he resigned this position in 1939 under pressure from the academics who wanted a Director more acceptable to the Franco regime. However, he was re-elected Director unanimously in December, 1947, and held that post for the rest of his life.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1910, he became the head of the philology section at the Centro de Estudios Históricos, a division of the liberal and Europeanising
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Junta_para_Ampliaci%C3%B3n_de_Estudios&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Junta para Ampliación de Estudios (page does not exist)">
        Junta para Ampliación de Estudios
       </a>
       <span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">
        (
        <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Junta_para_Ampliaci%C3%B3n_de_Estudios" title="es:Junta para Ampliación de Estudios">
         es
        </a>
        )
       </span>
       , which also had sections devoted to medicine, physics, chemistry, and mathematics.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gale02_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-gale02-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 1914 the Centro founded the
       <i>
        Revista de Filología Española
       </i>
       , which would become the premier scholarly journal in the fields of linguistics, Medieval and Renaissance Spanish literature.
      </p>
      <p>
       During the 1920s Menéndez Pidal published in rapid succession a series of major studies:
       <i>
        Poesía juglaresca y juglares
       </i>
       (1924) traced the development of minstrel poetry in medieval Spain.
       <i>
        Orígenes del español
       </i>
       (1926), a landmark in Romance linguistics, retraces the pre-literary phase of the Ibero-Romance dialects, and the "triumph" of Castilian. A ballad collection, designed for the general public,
       <i>
        Flor nueva de romances viejos
       </i>
       (1928) became a best seller, and includes some versions of ballads that Don Ramón had authored himself. Finally,
       <i>
        La España del Cid
       </i>
       (1929) traced the career of the 11th century warrior lord,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rodrigo_D%C3%ADaz_de_Vivar" title="Rodrigo Díaz de Vivar">
        Rodrigo Díaz de Vivar
       </a>
       , in a scholarly biography of some 1000 pages.
      </p>
      <p>
       After the civil War, Don Ramón forcibly became an "independent scholar" and revised much of his earlier work. However, from this period is his sweeping essay "Los españoles en la Historia," a study that traces the struggle between liberals and conservatives in the entire course of Spanish history, he summarised his findings on the ballads in
       <i>
        Romancero Hispánico: Teoría e historia
       </i>
       (1953) a treatise that has not been superseded, and applied his theory of the origins of epic poetry to French literature in
       <i>
        La Chanson de Roland y el neotradicionalismo
       </i>
       (1959).
      </p>
      <p>
       Menéndez Pidal worked for many years on a comprehensive history of the Spanish language, which he could not complete in his lifetime; the two volumes have been published posthumously as "Historia de la lengua española" (200%).
      </p>
      <p>
       He was nominated for a
       <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">
        Nobel Prize
       </a>
       23 times, but never won. In 1956 alone, he received over 160 nominations for the
       <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">
        Nobel Prize in Literature
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
