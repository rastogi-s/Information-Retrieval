       <b>
        Aostan French
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">

       </a>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aostan_French&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Aosta Valley was the first government authority to adopt Modern French as working language in 1536, three years before
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       itself.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       French has been the official language of the
       <a href="/wiki/Aosta_Valley" title="Aosta Valley">
        Aosta Valley
       </a>
       since 1561, when it replaced Latin.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the 1861 census, the first held after the unification of Italy, 93% declared being Francophone; in 1921, the last census with a question about language found that 88% of the population was French-speaking.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The suppression of all French-language schools and institutions and violence against French speakers during the forcible
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italianisation" title="Italianisation">
        Italianisation
       </a>
       campaign of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy#Fascist_regime_.281922.E2.80.931943.29" title="Kingdom of Italy">
        Fascist government
       </a>
       irretrievably damaged the status of French in the region. Italian and French are nowadays the region's
       <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">
        official languages
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and are used for the regional government's acts and laws, though Italian is much more widely spoken in everyday life, and French is mostly used by intellectuals and within cultural events. Though French was re-introduced as an official language after World War II, by 2003 just 0.99% reported speaking standard French natively. French remains widely known as a second language, but it is no longer spoken as part of daily life.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOIF201412_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOIF201412-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2001, 75.41% of the population of Aosta Valley was French-speaking, 96.01% declared to know Italian, 55.77%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franco-Proven%C3%A7al" title="Franco-Provençal">
        Franco-Provençal
       </a>
       , and 50.53% all of them.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       School education is delivered equally in both Italian and French so that everyone who went to school in Aosta Valley can speak French to at least at a medium-high level.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">
        Influences
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aostan_French&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Influences">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Aostan French is characterized by terms adopted from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vald%C3%B4tain" title="Valdôtain">
        patois francoprovençal valdôtain
       </a>
       and sometimes from
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       . In this sense, it is quite similar to
       <a href="/wiki/Savoyard_dialect" title="Savoyard dialect">
        Savoyard dialect
       </a>
       and to
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Valaisan_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Valaisan dialect (page does not exist)">
        valaisan dialect
       </a>
       as spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Valais" title="Valais">
        Valais
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Numerals">
        Numerals
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aostan_French&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Numerals">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Unlike standard French of France, Aostan French uses:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Seventy:
        <i>
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/septante" title="wikt:septante">
          septante
         </a>
        </i>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
         <a href="#cite_note-8">
          [a]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /sɛp.tɑ̃t/
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        Eighty:
        <i>
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/huitante" title="wikt:huitante">
          huitante
         </a>
        </i>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
         <a href="#cite_note-9">
          [b]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɥi.tɑ̃t/
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        Ninety:
        <i>
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/nonante" title="wikt:nonante">
          nonante
         </a>
        </i>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
         <a href="#cite_note-10">
          [c]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /nɔ.nɑ̃t/
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
