    Cordobés Spanish
   </h1>

      <p>
       <b>
        Cordobés Spanish
       </b>
       is a regional accent of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       spoken by the inhabitants of the city of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_(Argentina)" title="Córdoba (Argentina)">
        Córdoba
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       , and its adjacent territories. Known as
       <i>
        Cordobés
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        tonada cordobesa,
       </i>
       this accent is often the subject of humorous references in Argentine media and popular culture.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       This accent is notably different from the local accents in the provinces neighbouring Córdoba. Seemingly unique, Cordobés is thought to derive from the languages spoken by the
       <a href="/wiki/Comeching%C3%B3n" title="Comechingón">
        Comechingones
       </a>
       , the local indigenous people.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Its distinguishing features are the elongation of the sound of the
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel" title="Vowel">
        vowel
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Syllable" title="Syllable">
        syllable
       </a>
       preceding the
       <a href="/wiki/Stress_(linguistics)" title="Stress (linguistics)">
        stressed syllable
       </a>
       , and of the vowel(s) of the stressed syllable in
       <a href="/wiki/Proparoxytone" title="Proparoxytone">
        Proparoxytone
       </a>
       three-syllable words
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Cordobés also tends to elongate the sound of the last syllable in a word.
      </p>
      <p>
       According to Donni de Mirande (1991) and Vidal de Battini (1964), Córdobes Spanish can be heard throughout not only most the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_C%C3%B3rdoba_(Argentina)" title="Province of Córdoba (Argentina)">
        Province of Córdoba
       </a>
       , but also in most of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_San_Luis" title="Province of San Luis">
        Province of San Luis
       </a>
       , given its location and the influence of Cordoba's media and culture.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vidal_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Vidal-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
