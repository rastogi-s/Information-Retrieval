      <p>
       <b>
        Valdôtain
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arpitan" title="Arpitan">
        Arpitan
       </a>
       (Franco-Provençal) spoken in the
       <a href="/wiki/Aosta_Valley" title="Aosta Valley">
        Aosta Valley
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       . It is commonly known (in the local orthography) as
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Patois" title="Patois">
         patoué
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Diffusion">
        Diffusion
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Aosta Valley represents nowadays the only region of the
       <a href="/wiki/Franco-Proven%C3%A7al_language" title="Franco-Provençal language">
        Franco-Provençal
       </a>
       area where this language is still widely spoken as native by all age ranges of the population.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Linguistic_studies">
        Linguistic studies
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vald%C3%B4tain_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Linguistic studies">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Valdôtain has been the subject of detailed study at the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bureau_r%C3%A9gional_pour_l%27ethnologie_et_la_linguistique&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bureau régional pour l'ethnologie et la linguistique (page does not exist)">
        Bureau régional pour l'ethnologie et la linguistique
       </a>
       (BREL) in
       <a href="/wiki/Aosta" title="Aosta">
        Aosta
       </a>
       as well as in the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Centre_d%27%C3%A9tudes_francoproven%C3%A7ales&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Centre d'études francoprovençales (page does not exist)">
        Centre d'études francoprovençales
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Saint-Nicolas,_Aosta_Valley" title="Saint-Nicolas, Aosta Valley">
        Saint-Nicolas
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music">
        Music
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vald%C3%B4tain_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The main modern singers and songwriters in Valdôtain are:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_de_Jyaryot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Louis de Jyaryot (page does not exist)">
         Louis de Jyaryot
        </a>
        , from
        <a href="/wiki/Ayas,_Aosta_Valley" title="Ayas, Aosta Valley">
         Ayas
        </a>
        ;
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maura_Susanna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maura Susanna (page does not exist)">
         Maura Susanna
        </a>
        , from
        <a href="/wiki/Saint-Vincent,_Aosta_Valley" title="Saint-Vincent, Aosta Valley">
         Saint-Vincent
        </a>
        ;
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Magui_B%C3%A9temps&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Magui Bétemps (page does not exist)">
         Magui Bétemps
        </a>
        , from
        <a href="/wiki/Valtournenche" title="Valtournenche">
         Valtournenche
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The traditional Aostan songs in Valdôtain and in French form the core of the activity of the band
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Trouveur_valdot%C3%A8n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trouveur valdotèn (page does not exist)">
        Trouveur valdotèn
       </a>
       .
      </p>

        Un andze vin leur dëre:

        <br/>
        et sat pei de fen en traver
        <br/>

        De ci gran Rei de l'univer;

        <br/>
