      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Gramática de la lengua castellana
        </b>
       </i>
       ("Grammar of the Castilian Language", originally titled in Latin:
       <i>
        <b>
         Grammatica Antonii Nebrissensis
        </b>
       </i>
       ) is a book written by
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Nebrija" title="Antonio de Nebrija">
        Antonio de Nebrija
       </a>
       and published in 1492. It was the first work dedicated to the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       and its rules, and the first
       <a href="/wiki/Grammar" title="Grammar">
        grammar
       </a>
       of a modern European language to be published. When it was presented to
       <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">
        Isabella of Castile
       </a>
       at
       <a href="/wiki/Salamanca" title="Salamanca">
        Salamanca
       </a>
       in the year of its publication, the queen questioned what the merit of such a work might be; Fray
       <a href="/wiki/Hernando_de_Talavera" title="Hernando de Talavera">
        Hernando de Talavera
       </a>
       , bishop of Avila, answered for the author in prophetic words, as Nebrija himself recalls in a letter addressed to the monarch:
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <i>
         After Your Highness has subjected barbarous peoples and nations of varied tongues, with conquest will come the need for them to accept the laws that the conqueror imposes on the conquered, and among them our language; with this work of mine, they will be able to learn it, as we now learn Latin from the Latin Grammar
        </i>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Contents">
        Contents
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gram%C3%A1tica_de_la_lengua_castellana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Contents">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Nebrija divided his study of the language into four books:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Orthography" title="Orthography">
         Orthography
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Prosody_(linguistics)" title="Prosody (linguistics)">
         Prosody
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Syllable" title="Syllable">
         syllables
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">
         Etymology
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Diction" title="Diction">
         diction
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Syntax" title="Syntax">
         Syntax
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       A fifth book was dedicated to the teaching of Castilian as a
       <a href="/wiki/Foreign_language" title="Foreign language">
        foreign language
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The book established ten parts of speech:
       <a href="/wiki/Noun" title="Noun">
        nouns
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Pronoun" title="Pronoun">
        pronouns
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Verb" title="Verb">
        verbs
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Participle" title="Participle">
        participles
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preposition" title="Preposition">
        prepositions
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Adverb" title="Adverb">
        adverbs
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Interjection" title="Interjection">
        interjections
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammatical_conjunction" title="Grammatical conjunction">
        conjunctions
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Gerund" title="Gerund">
        gerunds
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Supine" title="Supine">
        supines
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">
        Impact
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gram%C3%A1tica_de_la_lengua_castellana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Works had previously been published on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">
        Latin
       </a>
       usage, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Valla" title="Lorenzo Valla">
        Lorenzo Valla
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        De Elegantiis Latinae Linguae
       </i>
       (1471), but
       <i>
        Grammatica
       </i>
       was the first book to focus on the study of the rules of a Western European language besides Latin. Following its publication, grammar came to be considered as the discipline concerned with the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rules_of_language" title="Rules of language">
        rules of language
       </a>
       , until the advent of
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">
        linguistics
       </a>
       as a scientific discipline in the 19th century.
      </p>
      <p>
       Other grammars of the Spanish language followed:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Antonio de Nebrija,
        <i>
         Reglas de ortografía
        </i>
        ("Rules of orthography", 1517)
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Vald%C3%A9s" title="Juan de Valdés">
         Juan de Valdés
        </a>
        ,
        <i>
         Diálogo de la Lengua
        </i>
        ("Dialogue on the language", 1535)
       </li>
       <li>
        Andrés Flórez,
        <i>
         Arte para bien leer y escribir
        </i>
        ("The art of reading and writing well", 1552)
       </li>
       <li>
        Martín Cordero,
        <i>
         La manera de escribir en castellano
        </i>
        (1556)
       </li>
       <li>
        Cristóbal de Villalón,
        <i>
         Gramática castellana
        </i>
        ("Castilian grammar", 1558)
       </li>
       <li>
        Gonzalo Correas,
        <i>
         Ortografía castellana
        </i>
        ("Castilian orthography", 1630)
       </li>
       <li>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Real Academia Española">
         Real Academia Española
        </a>
        ,
        <i>
         Gramática de la lengua española
        </i>
        ("Grammar of the Spanish language", 1771)
       </li>
      </ul>
