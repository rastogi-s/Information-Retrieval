      <p>
       The
       <i>
        <b>
         Academia Argentina de Letras
        </b>
       </i>
       is the academy in charge of studying and prescribing the use of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       in Argentina. Since its establishment, on August 13, 1931, it has maintained ties with the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Spanish_Academy" title="Royal Spanish Academy">
        Royal Spanish Academy
       </a>
       and the other Spanish-language academies that are members to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_of_Spanish_Language_Academies" title="Association of Spanish Language Academies">
        Association of Spanish Language Academies
       </a>
       . Since 1999, it has officially been a
       <i>
        correspondent
       </i>
       academy
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2010)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       . It currently comprises 24 full members, elected because of their significant backgrounds on language-related works, or literature studies. They make up the directing body of the academy, and they are capable of electing honorary and correspondent
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2010)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       academic members.
      </p>
