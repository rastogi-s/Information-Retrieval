      <p>
       <b>
        Zulia State
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         Estado Zulia
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <small>
        IPA:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">
         [esˈtaðo ˈsulja]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is one of the 23
       <a href="/wiki/States_of_Venezuela" title="States of Venezuela">
        states
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
        Venezuela
       </a>
       . The state capital is
       <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo" title="Maracaibo">
        Maracaibo
       </a>
       . As of the 2011 census, it has a population of 3,704,404, the largest population among Venezuela's states. It is also one of the few states (if not the only one) in Venezuela in which
       <a href="/wiki/Voseo" title="Voseo">
        voseo
       </a>
       (the use of
       <i>
        vos
       </i>
       as a second person singular pronoun) is widely used.
      </p>
      <p>
       Zulia State is in northwestern Venezuela, bordering
       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Maracaibo" title="Lake Maracaibo">
        Lake Maracaibo
       </a>
       , the largest body of water of its kind in Latin America. Its basin covers one of the largest oil and gas reserves in the
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">
        Western Hemisphere
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Zulia is economically important to the country from its oil and mineral exploitation, but it is also one of the major agricultural areas of Venezuela, highlighting the region's contribution in areas such as livestock, bananas, fruits, meat, and milk.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
        Geography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zulia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Lake
       <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo_Basin" title="Maracaibo Basin">
        Maracaibo Basin
       </a>
       covers the large to the north and west, from the
       <a href="/wiki/Guajira_Peninsula" title="Guajira Peninsula">
        Guajira Peninsula
       </a>
       to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perij%C3%A1_Mountains" title="Perijá Mountains">
        Perijá Mountains
       </a>
       . Venezuela's Andean states of Táchira, Mérida and Trujillo border Zulia State at the southern end of Lake Ma
      </p>
      <p>
       The name
       <i>
        Venezuela
       </i>
       comes from the lake. When Spanish
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conquistadors" title="Conquistadors">
        conquistadors
       </a>
       sailed into the area, they found the indigenous peoples living in communities of huts supported by stilts along the shores of the lake. They were reminded of
       <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">
        Venice
       </a>
       and named the place "Little Venice" or
       <i>
        Venezuela
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nameorig_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-nameorig-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The lake has a number of islands, some of which are populated.
      </p>
      <p>
       Near the mouth of the
       <a href="/wiki/Catatumbo_River" title="Catatumbo River">
        Catatumbo River
       </a>
       , where it empties into Lake Maracaibo, is the famous
       <a href="/wiki/Catatumbo_lightning" title="Catatumbo lightning">
        Catatumbo lightning
       </a>
       (
       <i>
        Relámpago del Catatumbo
       </i>
       )
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slate_catatumbo_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-slate_catatumbo-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       which is represented on the state's flag and coat of arms by lightning bolts.
      </p>

      <p>
       There are several competing theories about the origin of the state's name. One is that Guaimaral, son of the
       <a href="/wiki/Cacique" title="Cacique">
        cacique
       </a>
       Mara, was on pilgrimage in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pamplona,_Colombia" title="Pamplona, Colombia">
        Pamplona
       </a>
       region, where he fell in love with the beautiful Zulia, but she was killed in a battle against the conquerors. Gaimaral sadly returned to his father's domains, naming rivers, towns and regions for his lost love there is little historical proof, but that is the most popular theory.
      </p>
      <p>
       Alternatively, it had been said that the state was named after the
       <a href="/wiki/Zulia_River" title="Zulia River">
        Zulia River
       </a>
       , known in Colombia as Batatas, which leads into the
       <a href="/wiki/Catatumbo_River" title="Catatumbo River">
        Catatumbo River
       </a>
       . Another story says that both names, river and state, came from the princess Zulia, daughter of a famous cacique Cinera. He was an important leader of a tribal federation located in what is now Colombia's
       <a href="/wiki/Norte_de_Santander_Department" title="Norte de Santander Department">
        Norte de Santander Department
       </a>
       , and the princess Zulia was well known for her physical and moral qualities, formidable temper, and bravery.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zulia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Zulia was first seen by Europeans in 1499 during an expedition commanded by
       <a href="/wiki/Alonso_de_Ojeda" title="Alonso de Ojeda">
        Alonso de Ojeda
       </a>
       . During the Spanish colonial period, its lands were part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_Province" title="Venezuela Province">
        Venezuela Province
       </a>
       until 1676, when its lands were added to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida_Province_(Spanish_Empire)" title="Mérida Province (Spanish Empire)">
        Province of Mérida del Espiritu Santo de la Grita
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-histdic_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-histdic-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       becoming the province
       <i>
        Espiritu Santo de Maracaibo
       </i>
       , or
       <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo_Province_(Spanish_Empire)" title="Maracaibo Province (Spanish Empire)">
        Maracaibo Province
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EspSanto_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-EspSanto-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 1789, the province covered the territory of the current Venezuelan states of Zulia,
       <a href="/wiki/Apure" title="Apure">
        Apure
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Barinas_(state)" title="Barinas (state)">
        Barinas
       </a>
       , Táchira, Mérida and Trujillo. In 1810, Mérida and Trujillo were separated as new provinces. The Zulia Province declared independence from Spain on January 28, 1821. During the
       <a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">
        Gran Colombia
       </a>
       period in 1824, it received the name of "
       <a href="/wiki/Zulia_Department" title="Zulia Department">
        Zulia Department
       </a>
       ", honoring the
       <a href="/wiki/Zulia_River" title="Zulia River">
        Zulia River
       </a>
       . With the dissolution of Gran Colombia in 1830, it was named
       <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo_Province_(Venezuela)" title="Maracaibo Province (Venezuela)">
        Maracaibo Province
       </a>
       and was one of the 11
       <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Venezuela" title="Provinces of Venezuela">
        provinces of Venezuela
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Venezuela's federal constitution, April 22, 1864, changed the denomination of "province" to "state", creating the State of Maracaibo on the same territory as the province. At the end of the same year, the state's legislation determined to change the name to the Sovereign State of Zulia, but that lasted only a few months.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-histzuliagov_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-histzuliagov-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 1874, its name again became Zulia State. Federal government orders in 1881 created the combined
       <a href="/wiki/Falc%C3%B3n_Zulia" title="Falcón Zulia">
        Falcón Zulia state
       </a>
       . Its autonomous state status continued until April 1, 1890, when congress legislated the separation of the Falcon-Zulia State. It suffered further territorial changes towards the end of the 19th century, until the current
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delimitation" title="Delimitation">
        delimitation
       </a>
       in 1899 was finally drawn. Since that time the name has remained
       <i>
        Estado Zulia
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">
        Climate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zulia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the northern sector a semi-arid climate prevails, with an irregular pluviometric regime. The annual medial precipitation registered in Maracaibo city is between 358 and 665.99 mm (14.094 and 26.220 in), with a median temperature of 28 °C (82 °F). The precipitation rates increase in the western and eastern regions of Lake Maracaibo, forming a tropical rain forest savanna climate, with annual average temperatures from 27°C to 28°C, and rainfall exceeding 1,000 mm (39 in) registered in Mene Grande. In the southern lake region, increased rainfall conforms to a tropical rain forest climate with an annual average precipitation of 2,556 mm, and surpassing 3,500 mm per year in the heights of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serrania_de_Perija" title="Serrania de Perija">
        Serrania de Perija
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goblinzulia_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-goblinzulia-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">
        Sports
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zulia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sports">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The state is home to the
       <a href="/wiki/Gaiteros_del_Zulia" title="Gaiteros del Zulia">
        Gaiteros del Zulia
       </a>
       , 4-time Champion of the
       <a href="/wiki/Liga_Profesional_de_Baloncesto" title="Liga Profesional de Baloncesto">
        Liga Profesional de Baloncesto
       </a>
       , Venezuela's top professional basketball league. The team plays its home games at the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gimnasio_Pedro_El%C3%ADas_Belisario_Aponte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gimnasio Pedro Elías Belisario Aponte (page does not exist)">
        Gimnasio Pedro Elías Belisario Aponte
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo" title="Maracaibo">
        Maracaibo
       </a>
       .
      </p>
