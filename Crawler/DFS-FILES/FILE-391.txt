      <p>
       The
       <i>
        <b>
         Diccionario panhispánico de dudas
        </b>
       </i>
       (Pan-Hispanic Dictionary of Doubts) or
       <i>
        <b>
         DPD
        </b>
       </i>
       is an elaborate work undertaken by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Real Academia Española">
        Real Academia Española
       </a>
       (RAE – Royal Spanish Academy) and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_of_Spanish_Language_Academies" title="Association of Spanish Language Academies">
        Association of Spanish Language Academies
       </a>
       with the goal of resolving questions related to the proper use of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       . Like other publications of the Academy, such as the
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diccionario_de_la_lengua_espa%C3%B1ola_de_la_Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Diccionario de la lengua española de la Real Academia Española">
         Diccionario de la lengua española de la Real Academia Española
        </a>
       </i>
       , the work follows a
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_prescription" title="Linguistic prescription">
        linguistically prescriptive
       </a>
       philosophy as opposed to a
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_description" title="Linguistic description">
        descriptive
       </a>
       one.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The first edition was published in 2005 and is now being revised to more properly align with principles set forth by the Academy's other publications.
      </p>
      <p>
       The project was begun in response to the 50,000 questions received yearly by the constituent members of the Association of Spanish Language Academies.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       It is composed of:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Dictionary" title="Dictionary">
         dictionary
        </a>
        itself
       </li>
       <li>
        A set of five
        <a href="/wiki/Addendum" title="Addendum">
         appendices
        </a>
        covering
        <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_conjugation" title="Grammatical conjugation">
         conjugation of verbs
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Abbreviation" title="Abbreviation">
         abbreviations
        </a>
        , symbols that can be ordered alphabetically, other symbols, and
        <a href="/wiki/Demonym" title="Demonym">
         demonyms
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        A
        <a href="/wiki/Glossary" title="Glossary">
         glossary
        </a>
        of
        <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">
         linguistic terminology
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        A list of
        <a href="/wiki/Citation" title="Citation">
         works cited
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
