      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Salt Lake City metropolitan area
       </b>
       is the
       <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">
        metropolitan area
       </a>
       centered on the city of
       <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">
        Salt Lake City
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">
        Utah
       </a>
       . The
       <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Management_and_Budget" title="Office of Management and Budget">
        Office of Management and Budget
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">
        United States Census Bureau
       </a>
       currently define the
       <b>
        Salt Lake City, Utah Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </b>
       as comprising two counties:
       <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_County,_Utah" title="Salt Lake County, Utah">
        Salt Lake
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tooele_County,_Utah" title="Tooele County, Utah">
        Tooele
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OMB_13-01_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-OMB_13-01-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2010" title="United States Census, 2010">
        2010 census
       </a>
       , the MSA had a population of 1,087,873. As of July 1, 2014 the U.S. Census Bureau's population estimates division placed the population at 1,153,340, an increase of 65,467 or 6.0 percent since April 2010; out of 381 total MSAs, the Census Bureau ranks it as the 48th largest MSA in the United States in 2014 and the 58th fastest growing since 2010.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2014_Pop_Estimate_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-2014_Pop_Estimate-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Salt Lake City Metropolitan Area and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ogden-Clearfield_Metropolitan_Area" title="Ogden-Clearfield Metropolitan Area">
        Ogden-Clearfield Metropolitan Area
       </a>
       were a single metropolitan area known as the Salt Lake City-Ogden Metropolitan Area until being separated in 2005.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The metropolitan area is part of the
       <b>
        Salt Lake City-Provo-Orem, UT Combined Statistical Area
       </b>
       , which also includes the
       <a href="/wiki/Ogden%E2%80%93Clearfield_metropolitan_area" title="Ogden–Clearfield metropolitan area">
        Ogden–Clearfield metropolitan area
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Provo%E2%80%93Orem_metropolitan_area" title="Provo–Orem metropolitan area">
        Provo–Orem metropolitan area
       </a>
       , the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heber_City,_Utah_micropolitan_area" title="Heber City, Utah micropolitan area">
        Heber City, Utah micropolitan area
       </a>
       , and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summit_Park,_Utah_micropolitan_area" title="Summit Park, Utah micropolitan area">
        Summit Park, Utah micropolitan area
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">
        Demographics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salt_Lake_City_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demographics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       As of the
       <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">
        census
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GR2-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       of 2000, there were 968,858 people, 318,150 households, and 231,606 families residing within the MSA. The racial makeup of the MSA was 86.63%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">
        White
       </a>
       , 1.04%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">
        African American
       </a>
       , 0.90%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">
        Native American
       </a>
       , 2.43%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">
        Asian
       </a>
       , 1.15%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">
        Pacific Islander
       </a>
       , 5.33% from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">
        other races
       </a>
       , and 2.53% from two or more races.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">
        Hispanic
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">
        Latino
       </a>
       of any race were 11.71% of the population.
      </p>
      <p>
       The median income for a household in the MSA was $53,036, and the median income for a family was $59,139. Males had a median income of $40,683 versus $26,302 for females. The
       <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">
        per capita income
       </a>
       for the MSA was $23,426.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Combined_Statistical_Area">
        Combined Statistical Area
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salt_Lake_City_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Combined Statistical Area">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">
         Salt Lake City
        </a>
        –
        <a href="/wiki/Provo,_Utah" title="Provo, Utah">
         Provo
        </a>
        –
        <a href="/wiki/Orem,_Utah" title="Orem, Utah">
         Orem
        </a>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combined_Statistical_Area" title="Combined Statistical Area">
         Combined Statistical Area
        </a>
       </b>
       is made up of ten counties
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OMB_13-01_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-OMB_13-01-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       in northern
       <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">
        Utah
       </a>
       . The statistical area includes three
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_metropolitan_area" title="United States metropolitan area">
        metropolitan areas
       </a>
       and two
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_micropolitan_area" title="United States micropolitan area">
        micropolitan areas
       </a>
       . As of the 2014 estimate, the CSA had a population of 2,423,912.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2014_Pop_Estimate_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-2014_Pop_Estimate-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <b>
         Metropolitan Statistical Areas (MSAs)
        </b>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">
           Salt Lake City
          </a>
          MSA (
          <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_County,_Utah" title="Salt Lake County, Utah">
           Salt Lake
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Tooele_County,_Utah" title="Tooele County, Utah">
           Tooele
          </a>
          counties)
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Ogden%E2%80%93Clearfield_metropolitan_area" title="Ogden–Clearfield metropolitan area">
           Ogden-Clearfield
          </a>
          MSA (
          <a href="/wiki/Box_Elder_County,_Utah" title="Box Elder County, Utah">
           Box Elder
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Davis_County,_Utah" title="Davis County, Utah">
           Davis
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Morgan_County,_Utah" title="Morgan County, Utah">
           Morgan
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="/wiki/Weber_County,_Utah" title="Weber County, Utah">
           Weber
          </a>
          counties)
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Provo%E2%80%93Orem_metropolitan_area" title="Provo–Orem metropolitan area">
           Provo-Orem
          </a>
          MSA (
          <a href="/wiki/Juab_County,_Utah" title="Juab County, Utah">
           Juab
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Utah_County,_Utah" title="Utah County, Utah">
           Utah
          </a>
          counties)
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <b>
         Micropolitan Statistical Areas (μSAs)
        </b>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Heber_City,_Utah" title="Heber City, Utah">
           Heber City
          </a>
          (
          <a href="/wiki/Wasatch_County,_Utah" title="Wasatch County, Utah">
           Wasatch County
          </a>
          )
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Summit_Park,_Utah" title="Summit Park, Utah">
           Summit Park
          </a>
          (
          <a href="/wiki/Summit_County,_Utah" title="Summit County, Utah">
           Summit County
          </a>
          )
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
