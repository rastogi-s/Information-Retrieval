      <p>
       <b>
        Documented Nahuatl words in the Spanish language
       </b>
       (mostly as spoken in Mexico and Mesoamerica) include an extensive list of words that represent (i) animals, (ii) plants, fruit and vegetables, (iii) foods and beverages, and (iv) domestic appliances.
      </p>
      <p>
       Many of these words end with the absolutive suffix "-tl" in Nahuatl. This word ending—thought to be difficult for Spanish speakers to pronounce at the time—evolved in Spanish into a "-te" ending (e.g. axolotl = ajolote). As a rule of thumb, a
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       word for an animal, plant, food or home appliance widely used in Mexico and ending in "-te" is highly likely to have a
       <a href="/wiki/Nahuatl" title="Nahuatl">
        Nahuatl
       </a>
       origin.
      </p>
