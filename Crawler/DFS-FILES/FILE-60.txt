    Geography and cartography in medieval Islam
   </h1>

       <b>
        Medieval Islamic geography
       </b>
       was based on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hellenistic_geography" title="Hellenistic geography">
        Hellenistic geography
       </a>
       and reached its apex with
       <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_al-Idrisi" title="Muhammad al-Idrisi">
        Muhammad al-Idrisi
       </a>
       in the 12th century.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_and_cartography_in_medieval_Islam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       After its beginnings in the 8th century based on Hellenistic geography,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Islamic geography was patronized by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbasid" title="Abbasid">
        Abbasid
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caliph" title="Caliph">
        caliphs
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">
        Baghdad
       </a>
       . Various Islamic scholars contributed to its development, and the most notable include
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Khw%C4%81rizm%C4%AB" title="Al-Khwārizmī">
        Al-Khwārizmī
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ahmed_ibn_Sahl_al-Balkhi" title="Ahmed ibn Sahl al-Balkhi">
        Abū Zayd al-Balkhī
       </a>
       (founder of the "Balkhi school"), and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abu_Rayhan_Biruni" title="Abu Rayhan Biruni">
        Abu Rayhan Biruni
       </a>
       . : )
      </p>
      <p>
       Islamic cartographers inherited Ptolemy's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Almagest" title="Almagest">
         Almagest
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geographia_(Ptolemy)" title="Geographia (Ptolemy)">
        <i>
         Geography
        </i>
       </a>
       in the 9th century. These works stimulated an interest in geography (particularly gazetteers) but were not slavishly followed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEdsonSavage-Smith200461–63_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEdsonSavage-Smith200461–63-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Instead, Arabian and Persian cartography followed
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Khw%C4%81rizm%C4%AB" title="Al-Khwārizmī">
        Al-Khwārizmī
       </a>
       in adopting a rectangular projection, shifting Ptolemy's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Meridian" title="Prime Meridian">
        Prime Meridian
       </a>
       several degrees eastward, and modifying many of Ptolemy's geographical coördinates.
      </p>
      <p>
       Having received Greek writings directly and without Latin intermediation, Arabian and Persian geographers made no use of European-style
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T-O_map" title="T-O map">
        T-O maps
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEdsonSavage-Smith200461–63_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEdsonSavage-Smith200461–63-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Muslim scientists made many of their own contributions to geography and the earth sciences.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2015)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In the 11th century, the Karakhanid Turkic scholar
       <a href="/wiki/Mahmud_al-Kashgari" title="Mahmud al-Kashgari">
        Mahmud al-Kashgari
       </a>
       was the first to draw a unique Islamic
       <a href="/wiki/World_map" title="World map">
        world map
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       where he illuminated the cities and places of the
       <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">
        Turkic peoples
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">
        Central
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Inner_Asia" title="Inner Asia">
        Inner Asia
       </a>
       . He showed the lake
       <a href="/wiki/Issyk-Kul" title="Issyk-Kul">
        Issyk-Kul
       </a>
       (in nowadays
       <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">
        Kyrgyzstan
       </a>
       ) as the center of the world.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">
        Legacy
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_and_cartography_in_medieval_Islam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legacy">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       These medieval developments influenced
       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_geography" title="Chinese geography">
        Chinese geography
       </a>
       under the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">
        Mongol Empire
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miya_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-miya-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs additional references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">
           additional citation(s) needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       They also provided the underpinnings of the cartographic work of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottomans" title="Ottomans">
        Ottoman
       </a>
       cartographer
       <a href="/wiki/Piri_Reis" title="Piri Reis">
        Piri Reis
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
