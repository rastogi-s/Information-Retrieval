       :
       <span lang="und-Xsux" style="font-size:125%;font-family:'Segoe UI Historic','Akkadian','Noto Sans Cuneiform','Noto Sans Sumero-Akkadian Cuneiform';" title="cuneiform text" xml:lang="und-Xsux">
        <span lang="akk" xml:lang="akk">
         𒀫𒊒
        </span>
       </span>
       ,
       <i>
        Ṣurru
       </i>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">
        Greek
       </a>
       : Τύρος,
       <i>
        Týros
       </i>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">
        Turkish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr">
         Sur
        </span>
       </i>
       ;
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">
        Latin
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="la" xml:lang="la">
         Tyrus
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">
        Armenian
       </a>
       :
       <span lang="hy" xml:lang="hy">
        Տիր
       </span>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tir_(god)" title="Tir (god)">
        Tir
       </a>
       ), sometimes
       <a href="/wiki/Romanization" title="Romanization">
        romanized
       </a>
       as
       <b>
        Sour
       </b>
       , is a
       <a href="/wiki/Tyre_District" title="Tyre District">
        district capital
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/South_Governorate" title="South Governorate">
        South Governorate
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">
        Lebanon
       </a>
       . There were approximately 117,000 inhabitants in 2003.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, the government of Lebanon has released only rough estimates of population numbers since 1932, so an accurate statistical accounting is not possible.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Tyre juts out from the coast of the Mediterranean and is located about 80 km (50 mi) south of
       <a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">
        Beirut
       </a>
       . The name of the city means "
       <a href="/wiki/Rock_(geology)" title="Rock (geology)">
        rock
       </a>
       "
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       after the rocky formation on which the town was originally built. The adjective for Tyre is
       <i>
        Tyrian
       </i>
       , and the inhabitants are
       <i>
        Tyrians
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Tyre is an ancient
       <a href="/wiki/Phoenicia" title="Phoenicia">
        Phoenician
       </a>
       city and the legendary birthplace of
       <a href="/wiki/Europa_(mythology)" title="Europa (mythology)">
        Europa
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Dido" title="Dido">
        Dido
       </a>
       (Elissa). Today it is the fourth largest city in Lebanon after
       <a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">
        Beirut
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tripoli,_Lebanon" title="Tripoli, Lebanon">
        Tripoli
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Sidon" title="Sidon">
        Sidon
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and houses one of the nation's major ports. Tourism is a major industry. The city has a number of ancient sites, including its Roman
       <a href="/wiki/Hippodrome" title="Hippodrome">
        Hippodrome
       </a>
       which was added to
       <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">
        UNESCO
       </a>
       's list of
       <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">
        World Heritage Sites
       </a>
       in 1979.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <span class="mw-editsection">

      <p>
       Tyre originally consisted of two distinct
       <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">
        urban
       </a>
       centres, Tyre itself, which was on an island just off shore, and the associated settlement of
       <a href="/wiki/Ushu" title="Ushu">
        Ushu
       </a>
       on the adjacent mainland.
       <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">
        Alexander the Great
       </a>
       connected the island to the mainland by constructing a
       <a href="/wiki/Causeway" title="Causeway">
        causeway
       </a>
       during his
       <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Tyre_(332_BC)" title="Siege of Tyre (332 BC)">
        siege of the city
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       demolishing the old city to reuse its cut stone.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The original island city had two harbours, one on the south side and the other on the north side of the island. It was the two harbours that enabled Tyre to gain the maritime prominence that it did; the harbour on the north side of the island was, in fact, one of the best harbours on the eastern end of the Mediterranean. The harbour on the south side has silted over, but the harbour on the north side (see Tyre harbor photo at top of page right) is still in use.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In ancient times, the island-city of Tyre was heavily fortified and the mainland settlement, originally called
       <a href="/wiki/Ushu" title="Ushu">
        Ushu
       </a>
       (later called Palaetyrus, meaning "Old Tyre," by the ancient
       <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">
        Greeks
       </a>
       ) was actually more like a line of suburbs than any one city and was used primarily as a source of water and timber for the main island city.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Josephus" title="Josephus">
        Josephus
       </a>
       records that the two fought against each other on occasion,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       but most of the time, they supported one another because they both benefited from the island city's wealth from maritime trade and the mainland area's source of timber, water and burial grounds.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Bronze_and_Iron_Ages">
        Bronze and Iron Ages
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bronze and Iron Ages">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Tyre was founded around 2750 BC according to
       <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">
        Herodotus
       </a>
       and was originally built as a walled city upon the mainland.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Tyre's name appears on monuments as early as 1300 BC.
       <a href="/wiki/Philo_of_Byblos" title="Philo of Byblos">
        Philo of Byblos
       </a>
       (in
       <a href="/wiki/Eusebius" title="Eusebius">
        Eusebius
       </a>
       ) quotes the antiquarian authority
       <a href="/wiki/Sanchuniathon" title="Sanchuniathon">
        Sanchuniathon
       </a>
       as stating that it was first occupied by Hypsuranius. Sanchuniathon's work is said to be dedicated to "Abibalus king of Berytus"—possibly the Abibaal who was king of Tyre.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       There are ten
       <a href="/wiki/Amarna_letters" title="Amarna letters">
        Amarna letters
       </a>
       dated 1350 BC from the mayor,
       <a href="/wiki/Abimilku" title="Abimilku">
        Abimilku
       </a>
       , written to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akenaten" title="Akenaten">
        Akenaten
       </a>
       . The subject is often water, wood and the
       <a href="/wiki/Habiru" title="Habiru">
        Habiru
       </a>
       overtaking the countryside of the mainland and how that affected the island-city.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The commerce of the ancient world was gathered into the warehouses of Tyre.
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        Tyrian merchants were the first who ventured to navigate the Mediterranean waters; and they founded their
        <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">
         colonies
        </a>
        on the coasts and neighbouring islands of the
        <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">
         Aegean Sea
        </a>
        , in Greece, on the
        <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">
         northern coast of Africa
        </a>
        , at
        <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">
         Carthage
        </a>
        and other places, in
        <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">
         Sicily
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">
         Corsica
        </a>
        , in Spain at
        <a href="/wiki/Tartessos" title="Tartessos">
         Tartessus
        </a>
        and even beyond the
        <a href="/wiki/Pillars_of_Hercules" title="Pillars of Hercules">
         pillars of Hercules
        </a>
        at Gadeira (
        <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz" title="Cádiz">
         Cádiz
        </a>
        ).
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
         <a href="#cite_note-14">
          [14]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       The city of Tyre was particularly known for the production of a rare and extraordinarily expensive sort of
       <a href="/wiki/Purple" title="Purple">
        purple
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Dye" title="Dye">
        dye
       </a>
       , produced from the
       <a href="/wiki/Murex" title="Murex">
        murex
       </a>
       shellfish, known as
       <a href="/wiki/Tyrian_purple" title="Tyrian purple">
        Tyrian purple
       </a>
       . The colour was, in ancient cultures,
       <a href="/wiki/Sumptuary_law" title="Sumptuary law">
        reserved
       </a>
       for the use of royalty or at least the nobility.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Phoenicians from Tyre settled in houses around
       <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Egypt" title="Memphis, Egypt">
        Memphis in Egypt
       </a>
       , south of the temple of
       <a href="/wiki/Hephaestus" title="Hephaestus">
        Hephaestus
       </a>
       in a district called the Tyrian Camp.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Tyre was often attacked by Egypt and was besieged by Assyrian king
       <a href="/wiki/Shalmaneser_V" title="Shalmaneser V">
        Shalmaneser V
       </a>
       , who was assisted by the Phoenicians of the mainland, for five years. From 586 until 573 BC, the city was besieged by
       <a href="/wiki/Nebuchadnezzar_II" title="Nebuchadnezzar II">
        Nebuchadnezzar II
       </a>
       of Babylon until it agreed to pay a tribute.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Persian_period">
        Persian period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Persian period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire" title="Achaemenid Empire">
        Achaemenid Empire
       </a>
       conquered the city in 539 BC and kept it under its rule until 332 BC.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSTOR_18-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-JSTOR-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hellenistic_period">
        Hellenistic period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hellenistic period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">
        Alexander the Great
       </a>
       laid
       <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Tyre_(332_BC)" title="Siege of Tyre (332 BC)">
        siege
       </a>
       to the city, conquered and razed it in 332 BC.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSTOR_18-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-JSTOR-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 315 BC, Alexander's former general
       <a href="/wiki/Antigonus_I_Monophthalmus" title="Antigonus I Monophthalmus">
        Antigonus
       </a>
       began his own siege of Tyre,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-315_B.C._–_events_and_references_19-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-315_B.C._–_events_and_references-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       taking the city a year later.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 126 BC, Tyre regained its independence from the
       <a href="/wiki/Seleucid_Empire" title="Seleucid Empire">
        Seleucid Empire
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_period">
        Roman period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Roman period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Tyre was allowed to keep much of its independence, as a "
       <a href="/wiki/Civitas_foederata" title="Civitas foederata">
        civitas foederata
       </a>
       ",
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       when the area became a Roman province in 64 BC.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Tyre continued to maintain much of its commercial importance until the
       <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">
        Common Era
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       It is stated in the
       <a href="/wiki/New_Testament" title="New Testament">
        New Testament
       </a>
       that
       <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">
        Jesus
       </a>
       visited the region of Tyre and
       <a href="/wiki/Sidon#The_Biblical_Sidon" title="Sidon">
        Sidon
       </a>
       and healed a
       <a href="/wiki/Gentile" title="Gentile">
        Gentile
       </a>
       (
       <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=Matthew+15:21–15:21&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">
        Matthew 15:21
       </a>
       ;
       <a class="external text" href="http://bible.oremus.org/?passage=Mark+7:24–7:24&amp;version=nrsv" rel="nofollow">
        Mark 7:24
       </a>
       ) and from this region many came forth to hear him preaching (Mark 3:8;
       <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_Luke" title="Gospel of Luke">
        Gospel of Luke
       </a>
       6:17, Matthew 11:21–23). A congregation was founded here soon after the death of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Stephen" title="St. Stephen">
        St. Stephen
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Apostle" title="Paul the Apostle">
        Paul the Apostle
       </a>
       , on his return from his third missionary journey, spent a week in conversation with the disciples there. According to
       <a href="/wiki/Irenaeus" title="Irenaeus">
        Irenaeus
       </a>
       of Lyon in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/On_the_Detection_and_Overthrow_of_the_So-Called_Gnosis" title="On the Detection and Overthrow of the So-Called Gnosis">
         On the Detection and Overthrow of the So-Called Gnosis
        </a>
       </i>
       , the female companion of
       <a href="/wiki/Simon_Magus" title="Simon Magus">
        Simon Magus
       </a>
       came from here.
      </p>
      <p>
       The famous "Arch of Hadrian" and one of the best hippodromes in the region were constructed during the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_empire" title="Roman empire">
        Roman empire
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_period">
        Byzantine period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Byzantine period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In 395 it got part of the Byzantine Empire until in 638 it was occupied by the Arabs.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Muslim_period">
        Early Muslim period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Early Muslim period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/Revolt_of_Tyre_(996%E2%80%93998)" title="Revolt of Tyre (996–998)">
        Revolt of Tyre (996–998)
       </a>
       , the populace of the city rose against
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatimid" title="Fatimid">
        Fatimid
       </a>
       rule, led by an ordinary sailor named 'Allaqa - but were brutally suppressed in May 998. In 1086 it fell into the hands of the
       <a href="/wiki/Seljuk_Empire" title="Seljuk Empire">
        Seljuks
       </a>
       who lost it in 1089 to the Fatimids.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Crusader_period">
        Crusader period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Crusader period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Vassals_of_the_Kingdom_of_Jerusalem" title="Vassals of the Kingdom of Jerusalem">
        Vassals of the Kingdom of Jerusalem
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       After the first failed siege in 1111, Tyre was captured during the
       <a href="/wiki/First_Crusade" title="First Crusade">
        First Crusade
       </a>
       in 1124 and became one of the most important cities of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Jerusalem" title="Kingdom of Jerusalem">
        Kingdom of Jerusalem
       </a>
       . It was part of the royal domain, but there were also autonomous trading colonies there for the Italian merchant cities. The city was the site of the
       <a href="/wiki/See_of_Tyre" title="See of Tyre">
        See of Tyre
       </a>
       , whose archbishop was a suffragan of the
       <a href="/wiki/Latin_Patriarchate_of_Jerusalem" title="Latin Patriarchate of Jerusalem">
        Latin Patriarch of Jerusalem
       </a>
       ; its archbishops often acceded to the Patriarchate. The most notable of the Latin archbishops was the historian
       <a href="/wiki/William_of_Tyre" title="William of Tyre">
        William of Tyre
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       After the loss of the First Crusader Kingdom in the wake of the 1187
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hattin" title="Battle of Hattin">
        Battle of Hattin
       </a>
       and the reconquest of
       <a href="/wiki/Acre,_Israel" title="Acre, Israel">
        Acre
       </a>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Richard_I_of_England" title="Richard I of England">
        Richard I of England
       </a>
       on July 12, 1191, the seat of the kingdom moved there, but coronations were held in Tyre. In the 13th century, Tyre was separated from the royal domain as the
       <a href="/wiki/Vassals_of_the_Kingdom_of_Jerusalem#Lordship_of_Tyre" title="Vassals of the Kingdom of Jerusalem">
        Lordship of Tyre
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mamluk_period">
        Mamluk period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mamluk period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In 1291, Tyre was retaken by the
       <a href="/wiki/Mamluk_Sultanate_(Cairo)" title="Mamluk Sultanate (Cairo)">
        Mamluk Sultanate
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ottoman_period">
        Ottoman period
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ottoman period">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">
        Ottoman Empire
       </a>
       conquered the region in 1516-17 and held it until
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">
        World War I
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_Lebanon">
        Modern Lebanon
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Modern Lebanon">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="600" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg/220px-Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg/330px-Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg/440px-Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tyre_in_Lebanon_marking_as_protected_cultural_property.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         A large sign which marks the ancient city of Tyre as protected cultural property according to the 1954
         <a href="/wiki/Hague_Convention_for_the_Protection_of_Cultural_Property_in_the_Event_of_Armed_Conflict" title="Hague Convention for the Protection of Cultural Property in the Event of Armed Conflict">
          Hague Convention for the Protection of Cultural Property in the Event of Armed Conflict
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The modern state of Lebanon was declared in 1920.
      </p>
      <p>
       The present city of Tyre covers a large part of the original island and has expanded onto and covers most of the causeway, which had increased greatly in width over the centuries because of extensive silt depositions on either side. The part of the original island not covered by the modern city of Tyre is mostly of an archaeological site showcasing remains of the city from ancient times.
      </p>
      <p>
       After numerous attacks and reprisals involving the
       <a href="/wiki/Palestine_Liberation_Organization" title="Palestine Liberation Organization">
        Palestine Liberation Organization
       </a>
       (PLO), including an assassination attempt on Israeli ambassador Argov, Israel invaded, as part of the so-called
       <a href="/wiki/1978_South_Lebanon_conflict" title="1978 South Lebanon conflict">
        1978 South Lebanon conflict
       </a>
       , and Tyre was badly damaged. It was damaged again in the
       <a href="/wiki/1982_Lebanon_War" title="1982 Lebanon War">
        1982 Lebanon War
       </a>
       between
       <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">
        Israel
       </a>
       and the PLO. The city was used as a base by the PLO and was nearly destroyed by Israeli artillery.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       After the 1982 war, the city was the site of an Israeli military post. In late 1982, and again in November 1983, buildings housing Israeli headquarters were
       <a href="/wiki/Tyre_headquarters_bombings" title="Tyre headquarters bombings">
        destroyed by bombs
       </a>
       , causing dozens of deaths in both cases and known in Israel as the First and Second Tyre Catastrophes. The 1983 explosion by a
       <a href="/wiki/Car_bomb" title="Car bomb">
        suicide truck
       </a>
       happened only 10 days after similar attacks, the
       <a href="/wiki/1983_Beirut_barracks_bombings" title="1983 Beirut barracks bombings">
        1983 Beirut barracks bombings
       </a>
       of US Marines and French paratroop barracks. Israel and the US blame
       <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">
        Iran
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Hezbollah" title="Hezbollah">
        Hezbollah
       </a>
       for all explosions, but they have denied any involvement.
      </p>
      <p>
       During Israel's invasion in the
       <a href="/wiki/2006_Lebanon_War" title="2006 Lebanon War">
        2006 Lebanon War
       </a>
       , several rocket-launching sites used by
       <a href="/wiki/Hezbollah" title="Hezbollah">
        Hezbollah
       </a>
       to attack Israel were located in rural areas around the city.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       At least one village near the city was bombed by Israel as well as several sites within the city, causing civilian deaths and adding to the food shortage problem inside Tyre.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Shayetet_13" title="Shayetet 13">
        Shayetet 13
       </a>
       (Israeli naval commandos) also raided Hezbollah targets within the city.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">
        Climate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [

         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In Tyre, the climate is warm and temperate. In winter there is much more rainfall than in summer. The Köppen-Geiger climate classification is Csa. The average annual temperature in Tyre is 20.2 °C (68.4 °F). About 697 mm (27.44 in) of precipitation falls annually.

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Coast_Nature_Reserve">
        The Coast Nature Reserve
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: The Coast Nature Reserve">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Tyre_Coast_Nature_Reserve" title="Tyre Coast Nature Reserve">
        Tyre Coast Nature Reserve
       </a>
       covers over 380 hectares (940 acres) and divided into three zones: the Tourism zone (public beaches, the old city and Souks, the ancient port), the Agricultural and Archaeological zone, and the Conservation zone that includes the Phoenician springs of
       <a href="/wiki/Ras_al-Ain,_Lebanon" title="Ras al-Ain, Lebanon">
        Ras El Ain
       </a>
       . Due to its diverse flora and fauna, the reserve is a designated
       <a href="/wiki/Ramsar_Convention" title="Ramsar Convention">
        Ramsar Site
       </a>
       . It is an important nesting site for migratory birds and the endangered
       <a href="/wiki/Loggerhead_sea_turtle" title="Loggerhead sea turtle">
        Loggerhead
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Green_sea_turtle" title="Green sea turtle">
        green sea turtle
       </a>
       and the shelter of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabian_spiny_mouse" title="Arabian spiny mouse">
        Arabian spiny mouse
       </a>
       and many other important creatures (including
       <a href="/wiki/Lacertidae" title="Lacertidae">
        wall lizards
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Common_pipistrelle" title="Common pipistrelle">
        common pipistrelle
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/European_badger" title="European badger">
        european badger
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_heritage">
        Cultural heritage
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Cultural heritage">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre,_Lebanon.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2608" data-file-width="3880" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre%2C_Lebanon.jpg/220px-Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre%2C_Lebanon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre%2C_Lebanon.jpg/330px-Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre%2C_Lebanon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre%2C_Lebanon.jpg/440px-Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre%2C_Lebanon.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Roman_Hippodrome_in_Tyre,_Lebanon.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Roman Hippodrome in Tyre
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Threats to Tyre's ancient
       <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">
        cultural heritage
       </a>
       include development pressures and the illegal antiquities trade.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
        <a href="#cite_note-31">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A highway, planned for 2011, is expected to be built in areas that are deemed archaeologically sensitive. A small-scale geophysical survey indicated the presence of archaeological remains at proposed construction sites. The sites have not been investigated. Despite the relocation of a proposed traffic interchange, the lack of precise site boundaries confuses the issue of site preservation.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icomos118_32-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-icomos118-32">
         [32]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tyre-109945.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Tyre-109945.jpg/220px-Tyre-109945.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Tyre-109945.jpg/330px-Tyre-109945.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Tyre-109945.jpg/440px-Tyre-109945.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tyre-109945.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Columns with tourists
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The hostilities of the
       <a href="/wiki/2006_Lebanon_War" title="2006 Lebanon War">
        2006 Lebanon War
       </a>
       put the ancient structures of Tyre at risk. This prompted
       <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">
        UNESCO
       </a>
       's Director-General to launch a "Heritage Alert" for the site.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
        <a href="#cite_note-33">
         [33]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Following the cessation of hostilities in September 2006, a visit by conservation experts to Lebanon observed no direct damage to the ancient city of Tyre. However, bombardment had damaged frescoes in a Roman funerary cave at the
       <a href="/wiki/Tyre_Necropolis" title="Tyre Necropolis">
        Tyre Necropolis
       </a>
       . Additional site degradation was also noted, including "the lack of maintenance, the decay of exposed structures due to lack of rainwater regulation and the decay of porous and soft stones".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icomos118_32-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-icomos118-32">
         [32]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Like many of the cities in the
       <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">
        Levant
       </a>
       and in
       <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">
        Lebanon
       </a>
       , the architecture since the
       <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_Civil_War" title="Lebanese Civil War">
        Lebanese Civil War
       </a>
       in the 1970s has been of poor quality, which tend to threaten the cultural heritage in the built environment before the war.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">
        Demographics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyre,_Lebanon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Demographics">
         edit

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The population of Tyre is a predominantly Shia Muslim with a small but noticeable Christian community. However, the city of Tyre is home for more than 60,000 Palestinian refugees who are mainly Sunni Muslim. The Amal Movement and Hezbollah are the most popular parties, representing all of the Shi'a seats in the city as of the 2009 elections.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">

          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In 2010, it was estimated Christians accounted 10% of Tyre's population.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
        <a href="#cite_note-34">
         [34]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
