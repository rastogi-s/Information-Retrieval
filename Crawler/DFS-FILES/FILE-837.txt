      <p>
       <b>
        Edinburgh University Press
       </b>
       is a scholarly
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Publisher" title="Publisher">
        publisher
       </a>
       of academic books and
       <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">
        journals
       </a>
       , based in
       <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">
        Edinburgh
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">
        Scotland
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edinburgh_University_Press&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Edinburgh University Press was founded in the 1940s and became a wholly owned
       <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">
        subsidiary
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">
        University of Edinburgh
       </a>
       in 1992.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Books and journals published by the Press carry the
       <a href="/wiki/Imprimatur" title="Imprimatur">
        imprimatur
       </a>
       of The University of Edinburgh. All proposed publishing projects are appraised and approved by the Press Committee, which consists of academics from the university. Since August 2004, the Press has had Charitable Status.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In November 2013, Edinburgh University Press acquired Dundee University Press for an undisclosed sum, with a stated aim to increase textbook and digital sales, with a particular focus on law.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2.37_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-2.37-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-bbc-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Brodies" title="Brodies">
        Brodies
       </a>
       advised Edinburgh University Press on the terms of the acquisition.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Publishing">
        Publishing
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edinburgh_University_Press&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publishing">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Edinburgh University Press publishes a range of research publications, which include scholarly
       <a href="/wiki/Monograph" title="Monograph">
        monographs
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Reference_work" title="Reference work">
        reference works
       </a>
       , as well as materials which are available on-line. The Press also publishes
       <a href="/wiki/Textbook" title="Textbook">
        textbooks
       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Student" title="Student">
        students
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Lecturer" title="Lecturer">
        lecturers
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The press publishes around 205 books and 42 journals each year.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jstor_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-jstor-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Edinburgh University Press publishes mostly in humanities and social sciences.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ebooks">
        Ebooks
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edinburgh_University_Press&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ebooks">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The press participates in the ebook platforms University Press Scholarship Online (as Edinburgh Scholarship Online),
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Books at
       <a href="/wiki/JSTOR" title="JSTOR">
        JSTOR
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and University Publishing Online,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and also works with a number of ebook aggregators.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Open_access">
        Open access
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edinburgh_University_Press&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Open access">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       EUP supports both
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_open_access" title="Gold open access">
        gold
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_open_access" title="Green open access">
        green open access
       </a>
       publishing,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and is one of 13 publishers to participate in the
       <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_Unlatched" title="Knowledge Unlatched">
        Knowledge Unlatched
       </a>
       pilot, a global library consortium approach to funding
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_access_book" title="Open access book">
        open access books
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Business">
        Business
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edinburgh_University_Press&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Business">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Trustees normally meet five times a year, and are responsible for the conduct of the Edinburgh University Press.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-about-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Edinburgh University Press achieved combined book and journal revenues of over £2.37m for the year ending 31 July 2013, a 7% increase on the previous year.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2.37_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2.37-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Profit before interest was £197,000, which was marginally down on 2012 due to continued investment in editorial and marketing areas.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2.37_2-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-2.37-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
