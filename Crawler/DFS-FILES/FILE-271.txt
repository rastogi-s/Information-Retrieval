      <p>
       The
       <b>
        35th parallel north
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">
        circle of latitude
       </a>
       that is 35
       <a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">
        degrees
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/True_north" title="True north">
        north
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth's
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equatorial plane
       </a>
       . It crosses
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">
        Mediterranean Sea
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       , the parallel defines the southern border of
       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">
        Tennessee
       </a>
       , and the border between
       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">
        North Carolina
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">
        Georgia
       </a>
       , as well as the southernmost point of
       <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">
        Nevada
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        sun
       </a>
       is visible for 14 hours, 31 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">
        summer solstice
       </a>
       and 9 hours, 48 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">
        winter solstice
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
