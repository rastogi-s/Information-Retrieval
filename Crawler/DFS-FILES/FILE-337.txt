      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Latin American Parliament
       </b>
       (
       <i>
        Parlatino
       </i>
       ) is a regional, permanent organization composed by the countries of Latin America and the Caribbean. It is a consultative assembly similar to the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament#Consultative_assembly" title="European Parliament">
        early European Parliament
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Currently the institution is being considered to become the legislative organ of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CELAC" title="CELAC">
        Community of Latin American and Caribbean States
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEacn2011Havana_Hosts_Meeting_..._2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEacn2011Havana_Hosts_Meeting_...-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span id="Origins.2C_mandate.2C_principles_and_purpose">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Origins,_mandate,_principles_and_purpose">
        Origins, mandate, principles and purpose
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_American_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins, mandate, principles and purpose">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Latin American Parliament (
       <i>
        Parlatino
       </i>
       ) was created in 1964. Its current mandate is derived from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Institutionalization" title="Treaty of Institutionalization">
        Treaty of Institutionalization
       </a>
       which was ratified on 16 November 1987. Situated in
       <a href="/wiki/Panama_City" title="Panama City">
        Panama City
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">
        Panama
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the
       <i>
        Parlatino
       </i>
       has 23 member parliaments, each of which sends to it 12 nominated
       <a href="/wiki/Plenipotentiary" title="Plenipotentiary">
        plenipotentiaries
       </a>
       . The plenipotentiaries must represent the views of their parent parliament, and take into consideration the principles of the Parliament which include the defence of democracy and the further intergeneration of Latin America. The purposes of the
       <i>
        Parlatino
       </i>
       are:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        To promote, human rights, and economic and social development;
       </li>
       <li>
        To maintain and foster relations with other geographic parliaments (such as the
        <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">
         European Parliament
        </a>
        , as well as international organisations);
       </li>
       <li>
        To promote self-determination and defence against imperialism and colonialism.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Institutions">
        Institutions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_American_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Institutions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The main institutions of the
       <i>
        Parlatino
       </i>
       are:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unicameral" title="Unicameral">
         unicameral
        </a>
        Plenary Assembly which meets annually;
       </li>
       <li>
        The Board of Directors of the Plenary Assembly which is chaired by the President of the Assembly and oversees the work of the
        <i>
         Parlatino
        </i>
        between the Assembly's sessions.
       </li>
       <li>
        In 2009 there were thirteen permanent committees:
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350_1-3">
         <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEvansSilk2009350-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Cattle-raising and fisheries;
         </li>
         <li>
          Citizen safety, combat and prevention of narcotraffic, terrorism and organized crime;
         </li>
         <li>
          Economic affairs, social debt and regional development;
         </li>
         <li>
          Education, culture, science, technology and communication;
         </li>
         <li>
          Energy and mines;
         </li>
         <li>
          Environment and tourism;
         </li>
         <li>
          Gender equity, childhood and youth;
         </li>
         <li>
          Health;
         </li>
         <li>
          Human rights, justice and prison policies;
         </li>
         <li>
          Indigenous peoples and ethnic groups;
         </li>
         <li>
          Labour, social security and legal affairs;
         </li>
         <li>
          Political, municipal and integration affairs;
         </li>
         <li>
          Utilities and defence of users and consumers.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
