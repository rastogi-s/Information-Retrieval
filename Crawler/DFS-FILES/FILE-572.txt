      <p>
       <b>
        Indianapolis–Carmel–Anderson
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        Indianapolis metropolitan area
       </b>
       is an 11-county
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_metropolitan_areas_of_the_United_States" title="List of metropolitan areas of the United States">
        metropolitan area
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">
        U.S. state
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">
        Indiana
       </a>
       , as defined by the
       <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Management_and_Budget" title="Office of Management and Budget">
        Office of Management and Budget
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The metropolitan area is situated in
       <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Indiana#Central_Indiana" title="Geography of Indiana">
        Central Indiana
       </a>
       , within the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Midwest" title="American Midwest">
        American Midwest
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The metropolitan area is centered on the
       <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">
        capital
       </a>
       and most populous city of Indiana,
       <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">
        Indianapolis
       </a>
       . Indianapolis–Carmel–Anderson is the 34th most populous metropolitan area in the United States, and largest in the state of Indiana. As of 2014, the population was 1,971,274.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Indianapolis also anchors the larger Indianapolis–Carmel–Muncie
       <a href="/wiki/Combined_statistical_area" title="Combined statistical area">
        combined statistical area
       </a>
       (CSA), the 26th most populated, with 2,372,570.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Indianapolis metropolitan area is part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes_Megalopolis" title="Great Lakes Megalopolis">
        Great Lakes Megalopolis
       </a>
       , which contains an estimated 59 million people.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Area_codes">
        Area codes
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Indianapolis_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Area codes">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Area_code_317" title="Area code 317">
        317
       </a>
       area code covered all of northern and central Indiana until 1948, when the
       <a href="/wiki/Area_code_219" title="Area code 219">
        219
       </a>
       area code was created. Central Indiana remained under the 317 banner until 1997, when growth in and around Indianapolis prompted the creation of
       <a href="/wiki/Area_code_765" title="Area code 765">
        765
       </a>
       area code.
      </p>
      <p>
       The 317 area code covers the Indianapolis metropolitan area. The counties covered by 317 are
       <a href="/wiki/Boone_County,_Indiana" title="Boone County, Indiana">
        Boone County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hancock_County,_Indiana" title="Hancock County, Indiana">
        Hancock County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_County,_Indiana" title="Hamilton County, Indiana">
        Hamilton County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hendricks_County,_Indiana" title="Hendricks County, Indiana">
        Hendricks County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Johnson_County,_Indiana" title="Johnson County, Indiana">
        Johnson County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Madison_County,_Indiana" title="Madison County, Indiana">
        Madison County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Morgan_County,_Indiana" title="Morgan County, Indiana">
        Morgan County
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Shelby_County,_Indiana" title="Shelby County, Indiana">
        Shelby County
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       According to the Indiana Office of Utility Consumer Counselor, the 317 area code is expected to run out of numbers in 2017.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Overlay_plan" title="Overlay plan">
        Overlay
       </a>
       area code 463 will be implemented in late 2016, thereby requiring 10-digit dialing.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Interstates">
        Interstates
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Indianapolis_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Interstates">

         ]

       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       The Indianapolis area is a major point on the United States
       <a href="/wiki/Interstate_Highway_System" title="Interstate Highway System">
        Interstate Highway System
       </a>
       , as it is a confluence of four major interstate highways:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <img alt="" data-file-height="601" data-file-width="601" height="20" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/I-65.svg/20px-I-65.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/I-65.svg/30px-I-65.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/I-65.svg/40px-I-65.svg.png 2x" width="20"/>

         <a href="/wiki/Interstate_65_in_Indiana" title="Interstate 65 in Indiana">
          I-65
         </a>
        </span>

        <a href="/wiki/Gary,_Indiana" title="Gary, Indiana">
         Gary, Indiana
        </a>
        to the north and
        <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">
         Louisville, Kentucky
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">
         Nashville, Tennessee
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama" title="Birmingham, Alabama">
         Birmingham, Alabama
        </a>
        to the south.
       </li>
       <li>

      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_notable_roads">
        Other notable roads
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Indianapolis_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Other notable roads">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Other notable roads in the area are:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Avenue" title="Indiana Avenue">
         Indiana Avenue (Indianapolis)
        </a>
        – One of four diagonal streets included in
        <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Ralston" title="Alexander Ralston">
         Alexander Ralston
        </a>
        's 1821 Plat of Indianapolis, the street became a center for the local African American community and now anchors a cultural district of the same name.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Meridian_Street_(Indianapolis)" title="Meridian Street (Indianapolis)">
         Meridian Street (Indianapolis)
        </a>
        – A primary north-south route through
        <a href="/wiki/Marion_County,_Indiana" title="Marion County, Indiana">
         Marion
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_County,_Indiana" title="Hamilton County, Indiana">
         Hamilton
        </a>
        counties, the street serves as the axis separating east addresses from west addresses.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Michigan_Road" title="Michigan Road">
         Michigan Road
        </a>
        – Indiana's first "highway," built in the 1830s and 1840s, running north to
        <a href="/wiki/Michigan_City,_Indiana" title="Michigan City, Indiana">
         Michigan City, Indiana
        </a>
        and south to
        <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Indiana" title="Madison, Indiana">
         Madison, Indiana
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Sam_Jones_Expressway" title="Sam Jones Expressway">
         Sam Jones Expressway (Indianapolis)
        </a>
        – Expressway between I-465 and I-70, connecting south-central Indianapolis with the former terminal of the
        <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_International_Airport" title="Indianapolis International Airport">
         Indianapolis International Airport
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Washington_Street_(Indianapolis)" title="Washington Street (Indianapolis)">
         Washington Street (Indianapolis)
        </a>
        – A primary east–west street through Marion County, the street follows the route of the
        <a href="/wiki/National_Road" title="National Road">
         National Road
        </a>
        for almost all of its length in the city of Indianapolis.
       </li>
      </ul>

       <span id="College_sports_.28Division_I.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="College_sports_(Division_I)">
        College sports (Division I)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <p>
       Headquartered in Indianapolis, the
       <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">
        National Collegiate Athletic Association
       </a>
       (NCAA) is the preeminent collegiate athletic governing body in the U.S. and Canada, regulating athletes of 1,281 institutions; conferences; organizations; and individuals. The NCAA also organizes the athletic programs of many colleges and universities and helps more than 450,000 college student-athletes who compete annually in college sports.

       The Indianapolis metropolitan area hosts several notable sporting events annually, including the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brickyard_400" title="Brickyard 400">
        Brickyard 400
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_of_Indianapolis" title="Grand Prix of Indianapolis">

       </a>
       ,

        Indianapolis 500
       </a>

        IndyCars
       </i>

       <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I" title="NCAA Division I">

       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/1987_Pan_American_Games" title="1987 Pan American Games">
        Pan American Games X
       </a>
       in 1987,
       <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">
        Super Bowl XLVI
       </a>
       in 2012,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and the

       </a>

        Inter Milan
       </a>
       .

         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
