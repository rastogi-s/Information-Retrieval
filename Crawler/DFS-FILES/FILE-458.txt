      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_verbs" title="Spanish verbs">
        Spanish verbs
       </a>
       are a complex area of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_grammar" title="Spanish grammar">
        Spanish grammar
       </a>
       , with many combinations of tenses, aspects and moods (up to fifty conjugated forms per verb). Although
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_conjugation" title="Grammatical conjugation">
        conjugation
       </a>
       rules are relatively straightforward, a large number of verbs are
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irregular_verb" title="Irregular verb">
        irregular
       </a>
       . Among these, some fall into more-or-less defined deviant patterns, whereas others are uniquely irregular. This article summarizes the common irregular patterns.
      </p>
      <p>
       As in all
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       , many irregularities in Spanish verbs can be retraced to
       <a href="/wiki/Latin_grammar" title="Latin grammar">
        Latin grammar
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Stem-vowel_changes">
        Stem-vowel changes
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stem-vowel changes">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are two kinds of changes that can affect stem vowels of some Spanish verbs:
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel_breaking" title="Vowel breaking">
        diphthongization
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Close_vowel" title="Close vowel">
        vowel raising
       </a>
       . Both changes affect
       <i>
        -e-
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        -o-
       </i>
       in the last (or only) syllable of a verb stem. Diphthongization changes
       <i>
        -e-
       </i>
       to
       <i>
        -ie-
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        -o-
       </i>
       to
       <i>
        -ue-
       </i>
       . Vowel raising changes the
       <a href="/wiki/Mid_vowel" title="Mid vowel">
        mid vowels
       </a>
       <i>
        -e-
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -o-
       </i>
       to the corresponding
       <a href="/wiki/Close_vowel" title="Close vowel">
        high vowels
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        -i-
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -u-
       </i>
       respectively. Some verbs, in their various forms, can exhibit both kinds of changes (e.g.
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sentir#Spanish" title="wikt:sentir">
          <i>
           s
           <b>
            e
           </b>
           ntir
          </i>
         </a>
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/siente#Spanish" title="wikt:siente">
          <i>
           s
           <b>
            ie
           </b>
           nte
          </i>
         </a>
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sinti%C3%B3#Spanish" title="wikt:sintió">
          <i>
           s
           <b>
            i
           </b>
           ntió
          </i>
         </a>
        </span>
       </i>
       ;
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/dormir#Spanish" title="wikt:dormir">
          <i>
           d
           <b>
            o
           </b>
           rmir
          </i>
         </a>
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/duerme#Spanish" title="wikt:duerme">
          <i>
           d
           <b>
            ue
           </b>
           rme
          </i>
         </a>
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/durmi%C3%B3#Spanish" title="wikt:durmió">
          <i>
           d
           <b>
            u
           </b>
           rmió
          </i>
         </a>
        </span>
       </i>
       ).
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Diphthongization">
        Diphthongization
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diphthongization">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Some verbs with
       <i>
        -e-
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        -o-
       </i>
       in their stem are inherently diphthongizing, whereas others are not: their identities must be learned individually. In a diphthongizing verb, the change turns
       <i>
        -e-
       </i>
       into
       <i>
        -ie-
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -o-
       </i>
       into
       <i>
        -ue-
       </i>
       when the syllable in question is stressed, which in effect happens only in the singular persons and third-person plural of the present indicative and present subjunctive, and in the imperative (all other tenses and forms are stressed on their endings, not their stems). Note that the dictionary form always has the vowel, not the
       <a href="/wiki/Diphthong" title="Diphthong">
        diphthong
       </a>
       , because, in the infinitive form, the stress is on the ending, not the stem. Exceptionally, the
       <i>
        -u-
       </i>
       of
       <i>
        j
        <b>
         u
        </b>
        gar
       </i>
       and the
       <i>
        -i-
       </i>
       of
       <i>
        adqu
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        rir
       </i>
       also are subject to diphthongization (
       <i>
        juega
       </i>
       , etc.;
       <i>
        adquiere
       </i>
       , etc.).
      </p>
      <p>
       In word-initial position,
       <i>
        *ie-
       </i>
       is written
       <i>
        ye-
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        errar
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        yerro
       </i>
       ) and
       <i>
        *ue-
       </i>
       is written
       <i>
        hue-
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        oler
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        huele
       </i>
       ). Also, the
       <i>
        -ue-
       </i>
       diphthong is written
       <i>
        -üe
       </i>
       - after
       <i>
        g
       </i>
       , with the
       <a href="/wiki/Diaeresis_(diacritic)#Non-silent_vowels" title="Diaeresis (diacritic)">
        diaeresis
       </a>
       to indicate that the letter is not silent (
       <i>
        avergonzarse
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        me avergüenzo
       </i>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       The following examples show that all three conjugations (
       <i>
        -ar
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        -er
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        -ir
       </i>
       verbs) include some diphthongizing verbs (only some tenses and persons are shown, for contrasting purposes):
      </p>

        </i>
        →
        <i>
         yo *reb
         <b>
          ue
         </b>
         so
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         él *reb
         <b>
          ue
         </b>
         sa...
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         yo reb
         <b>
          o
         </b>
         so, él reb
         <b>
          o
         </b>
         sa...
        </i>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vowel_raising">
        Vowel raising
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Vowel raising">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Vowel raising appears only in verbs of the third conjugation (
       <i>
        -ir
       </i>
       verbs), and in this group it affects
       <i>
        d
        <b>
         o
        </b>
        rmir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        m
        <b>
         o
        </b>
        rir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         o
        </b>
        drir
       </i>
       (alternative of the more common
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         u
        </b>
        drir
       </i>
       ) and nearly all verbs which have
       <i>
        -e-
       </i>
       as their last stem vowel (e.g.
       <i>
        s
        <b>
         e
        </b>
        ntir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        rep
        <b>
         e
        </b>
        tir
       </i>
       ); exceptions include
       <i>
        cernir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        discernir
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        concernir
       </i>
       (all three diphthongizing).
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Affected_forms">
        Affected forms
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Affected forms">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       The forms that exhibit the change can be described negatively as those in which the stem vowel is
       <i>
        not
       </i>
       diphthongized and the ending does
       <i>
        not
       </i>
       contain stressed /í/
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       or the /ir/ sequence. In other words, vowel raising affects the forms whose endings do not contain an
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       which is not part of a diphthong, taking into account that diphthongizing overrides vowel raising.
      </p>
      <p>
       In effect, for diphthongizing verbs (e.g.
       <i>
        sentir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        dormir
       </i>
       ), the vowel-raising forms are:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        the first-person and second-person plural of the present subjunctive (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntamos
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntáis
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmamos
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmáis
        </i>
        );
       </li>
       <li>
        the gerund (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntiendo
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmiendo
        </i>
        );
       </li>
       <li>
        the third-person singular and plural of the preterite (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntió
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntieron
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmió
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmieron
        </i>
        );
       </li>
       <li>
        all forms of the imperfect subjunctive (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntiera/s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntiese
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmiera/d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmiese
        </i>
        ...) and of the future subjunctive (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         ntiere
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         d
         <b>
          u
         </b>
         rmiere
        </i>
        ...).
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       For non-diphthongizing verbs (e.g.
       <i>
        pedir
       </i>
       ) it affects these same forms (
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        damos
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        dáis
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        diendo
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        dió
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        dieron
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        diera
       </i>
       ...), plus:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        in the present indicative, all singular forms and the third-person plural (
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         do
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         des
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         de
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         den
        </i>
        );
       </li>
       <li>
        the remaining forms of the present subjunctive (
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         da
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         das
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         dan
        </i>
        );
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <i>
         tú
        </i>
        form of the imperative (
        <i>
         p
         <b>
          i
         </b>
         de
        </i>
        ).
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The forms which do
       <i>
        not
       </i>
       undergo either diphthongizing or vowel raising are:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        the first-person and second-person plural of the present indicative (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntimos
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntís
        </i>
        ), because these forms have stressed /í/ in their endings.
       </li>
       <li>
        the infinitive (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntir
        </i>
        ), past participle (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntido
        </i>
        ), imperfect indicative (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntía
        </i>
        ...) and the
        <i>
         vos
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         vosotros/as
        </i>
        forms of the imperative (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntí
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntid
        </i>
        ), for the same reason.
       </li>
       <li>
        the future (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntiré
        </i>
        ...) and conditional (
        <i>
         s
         <b>
          e
         </b>
         ntiría
        </i>
        ...), whose endings contain the /ir/ sequence.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Affected_verbs">
        Affected verbs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Affected verbs">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Verbs which are diphthongizing
       <i>
        and
       </i>
       vowel-raising include:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        those ending in
        <i>
         -entir
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         sentir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         mentir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         arrepentirse
        </i>
        ...) and
        <i>
         -ertir
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         convertir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         advertir
        </i>
        ...);
       </li>
       <li>
        those ending in
        <i>
         -erir
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         herir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         requerir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         preferir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         digerir
        </i>
        ...), except
        <i>
         aterir
        </i>
        ;
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         venir
        </i>
        and derived verbs (
        <i>
         convenir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         prevenir
        </i>
        ...) in the gerund (
        <i>
         viniendo
        </i>
        ...), as the rest of forms undergo other overriding irregularities.
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         dormir
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         morir
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The diphthongizing
       <i>
        -er
       </i>
       verb
       <i>
        poder
       </i>
       exceptionally undergoes vowel rising in the gerund (
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         u
        </b>
        diendo
       </i>
       ), but the first- and second-person plural of the present subjunctive are regular (
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         o
        </b>
        damos
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        p
        <b>
         o
        </b>
        dáis
       </i>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Non
       </i>
       -diphthongizing vowel-rising verbs include:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        those ending in
        <i>
         -edir
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         medir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         pedir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         despedir
        </i>
        ...),
        <i>
         -etir
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         competir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         repetir
        </i>
        ) and
        <i>
         -egir
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         corregir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         elegir
        </i>
        ...; note forms with
        <i>
         j
        </i>
        before
        <i>
         a/o
        </i>
        such as
        <i>
         corrijo
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         corrija
        </i>
        ).
       </li>
       <li>
        those ending in
        <i>
         -eír
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         reír
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         sonreír
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         freír
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         desleír
        </i>
        ). Double
        <i>
         i
        </i>
        that would result is simplified (
        <i>
         rieron
        </i>
        , not
        <i>
         *riieron
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         *riyeron
        </i>
        ). Note that stressed
        <i>
         i
        </i>
        in contact with
        <i>
         a/e/o
        </i>
        must take an acute accent (
        <i>
         río
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         ríe
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         ría
        </i>
        ) but monosyllabic forms of the preterite do not have it (
        <i>
         rio
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         riais
        </i>
        , but
        <i>
         rió
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         riáis
        </i>
        also valid if you pronounce them in two syllables).
       </li>
       <li>
        those ending in
        <i>
         -eñir
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         teñir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         ceñir
        </i>
        ...). Note that unstressed
        <i>
         i
        </i>
        between
        <i>
         ñ
        </i>
        and a vowel is dropped (
        <i>
         tiñendo
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         tiñó
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         tiñeron
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         tiñera
        </i>
        ...).
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         decir
        </i>
        and derived verbs (
        <i>
         bendecir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         predecir
        </i>
        ...), in the forms that do not undergo other overriding irregularities.
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         embestir
        </i>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         podrir
        </i>
        . Note that the affected forms are equal to those derived from the more usual infinitive
        <i>
         pudrir
        </i>
        , which is regular except in the past participle
        <i>
         podrido
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The vowel-raising verb
       <i>
        erguir
       </i>
       is usually diphthongizing (with
       <i>
        ye-
       </i>
       forms as
       <i>
        yergo
       </i>
       ...), not-diphthongizing forms are however valid but rare (
       <i>
        irgo
       </i>
       ...).
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Diphthongs_and_hiatuses">
        Diphthongs and hiatuses
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Diphthongs and hiatuses">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span id="Cambio_vs._env.C3.ADo">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cambio_vs._envío">
        <i>
         Cambio
        </i>
        vs.
        <i>
         envío
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Cambio vs. envío">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Diphthong" title="Diphthong">
        Diphthongs
       </a>
       in the infinitive may be preserved throughout the conjugation or broken in the forms which are stressed on the stem, depending on whether the
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        u
       </i>
       in contact with
       <i>
        a/e/o
       </i>
       take the stress or not. The stressed vowel is marked bold in the examples:
       <i>
        cambiar
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        c
        <b>
         a
        </b>
        mbio
       </i>
       , but
       <i>
        enviar
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        env
        <b>
         í
        </b>
        o
       </i>
       (requiring an acute accent to indicate the resulting
       <a href="/wiki/Hiatus_(linguistics)" title="Hiatus (linguistics)">
        hiatus
       </a>
       ). The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Real Academia Española">
        Real Academia Española
       </a>
       does not consider either behaviour as irregular, but illustrates each with six "regular" models, one for each possible diphthong in the infinitive:
       <i>
        anunciar, averiguar, bailar, causar, peinar
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        adeudar
       </i>
       for diphthong-keeping verbs and
       <i>
        enviar, actuar, aislar, aunar, descafeinar
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        rehusar
       </i>
       for diphthong-breaking ones. Remember that the presence of a silent
       <i>
        h
       </i>
       does not break a diphthong, so a written accent is needed anyway in
       <i>
        reh
        <b>
         ú
        </b>
        so
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       All verbs ending in
       <i>
        -guar
       </i>
       are diphthong-keeping, as well as
       <i>
        saciar
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        desairar
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        restaurar
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        reinar
       </i>
       . Note that two dipththongs are kept in
       <i>
        desahuciar
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        des
        <b>
         a
        </b>
        hucio
       </i>
       (again the
       <i>
        -h-
       </i>
       makes no difference), which thus follows both the
       <i>
        anunciar
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        causar
       </i>
       models.
      </p>
      <p>
       Diphthong-breaking verbs include
       <i>
        ahincar, aislar, aunar, aullar, maullar, aupar, aliar, vaciar, contrariar, evaluar, habituar, reunir
       </i>
       . The verbs
       <i>
        criar, fiar, guiar, liar
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        piar
       </i>
       are also diphthong-breaking (
       <i>
        cr
        <b>
         í
        </b>
        o, gu
        <b>
         í
        </b>
        e
       </i>
       ), but when the stress falls on the endings the resulting forms are generally considered as monosyllables and thus written without accent:
       <i>
        crie, fie, guiais, lieis...
       </i>
       . In spite of that, you can follow the regular accentuation rules if you pronounce these forms as bisyllabic:
       <i>
        crié, guiáis...
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       For the verbs
       <i>
        licuar
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        adecuar
       </i>
       both options are valid:
       <i>
        adecuo
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        adecúo
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Note that the
       <i>
        ui
       </i>
       diphthong in
       <i>
        cuidar
       </i>
       is kept throughout the conjugation despite the fact of the
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       getting the stress in forms such as
       <i>
        cu
        <b>
         i
        </b>
        do
       </i>
       (written
       <i>
        without
       </i>
       stress mark).
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Verbs_ending_in_-uir_and_-o.C3.ADr">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Verbs_ending_in_-uir_and_-oír">
        Verbs ending in
        <i>
         -uir
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         -oír
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Verbs ending in -uir and -oír">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       All verbs ending in
       <i>
        -uir
       </i>
       (e.g.
       <i>
        construir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        disminuir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        distribuir
       </i>
       ) add a medial
       <i>
        -y-
       </i>
       before all endings
       <i>
        not
       </i>
       starting with
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       :
       <i>
        constru
        <b>
         y
        </b>
        o
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        constru
        <b>
         y
        </b>
        es
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        constru
        <b>
         y
        </b>
        a
       </i>
       ... Taking into account that these verbs also undergo the change of unstressed intervocalic
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       to
       <i>
        y
       </i>
       (see
       <a href="#Orthographic_changes">
        orthographic changes
       </a>
       above), they have many forms containing
       <i>
        y
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This also applies to the forms of
       <i>
        oír
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        desoír
       </i>
       that do not undergo the
       <a href="#G-verbs">
        <i>
         -ig-
        </i>
       </a>
       change:
       <i>
        oyes
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        oye
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        oyen
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       Again, note that some regular forms of
       <i>
        fluir, fruir
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        huir
       </i>
       are written without stress mark if considered monosyllabic, but may bear it if pronounced as bisyllabic:
       <i>
        vosotros huis
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        huís
       </i>
       (present),
       <i>
        yo hui
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        huí
       </i>
       (preterite).
      </p>
      <p>
       Note that logically
       <i>
        argüir
       </i>
       loses the
       <a href="/wiki/Diaeresis_(diacritic)" title="Diaeresis (diacritic)">
        diaeresis
       </a>
       before
       <i>
        y
       </i>
       :
       <i>
        arguyo
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        arguyó
       </i>
       ...
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_common_irregular_patterns">
        Other common irregular patterns
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Other common irregular patterns">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span id="Endings_starting_with_o.2Fa_in_er.2Fir_verbs">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Endings_starting_with_o/a_in_er/ir_verbs">
        Endings starting with
        <i>
         o/a
        </i>
        in
        <i>
         er/ir
        </i>
        verbs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Endings starting with o/a in er/ir verbs">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In
       <i>
        er
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        ir
       </i>
       verbs, the first person singular of the present indicative and the whole present subjunctive are the only forms whose endings start with
       <i>
        o/a
       </i>
       instead of
       <i>
        e/i
       </i>
       . These two different phonetic environments made Latin forms evolve differently in many verbs, leading to irregularities. Note that:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Whenever the first person singular of the present indicative has an irregularity other than diphthongizing, but still ends in
        <i>
         -o
        </i>
        , the whole present subjunctive shares the same irregularity:
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           hacer
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           hago
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           haga
          </i>
          ...
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           lucir
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           luzco
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           luzca
          </i>
          ...
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           caber
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           quepo
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           quepa
          </i>
          ...
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           ver
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           veo
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           vea
          </i>
          ...;
          <i>
           prever
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           preveo
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           prevea
          </i>
          ...
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        When the first person singular of the present indicative does not end in
        <i>
         -o
        </i>
        , the present subjunctive is also irregular, but in a different way:
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           ser
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           soy
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           sea
          </i>
          ...
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           ir
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           voy
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           vaya
          </i>
          ...
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           haber
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           he
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           haya
          </i>
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           saber
          </i>
          &gt;
          <i>
           sé
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           sepa
          </i>
          ...
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="G-verbs">
        G-verbs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: G-verbs">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Before
       <i>
        o
       </i>
       (in the first person singular of the indicative present tense) and
       <i>
        a
       </i>
       (that is, in all persons of the present subjunctive), the so-called
       <i>
        G
       </i>
       -verbs (sometimes "go-verbs" or "yo-go" verbs) add a medial
       <i>
        -g-
       </i>
       after
       <i>
        l
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        n
       </i>
       (also after
       <i>
        s
       </i>
       in
       <i>
        asir
       </i>
       ), add
       <i>
        -ig-
       </i>
       when the root ends in a vowel, or substitute
       <i>
        -g-
       </i>
       for
       <i>
        -c-
       </i>
       . Note that this change
       <i>
        overrides
       </i>
       diphthongization (
       <i>
        tener, venir
       </i>
       ) but
       <i>
        combines
       </i>
       with vowel-raising (
       <i>
        decir
       </i>
       ). Many of these verbs are also irregular in other ways. For example:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         salir: yo salgo, tú sales...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         valer: yo valgo, tú vales...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         poner: yo pongo, tú pones...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         tener: yo tengo, tú tienes...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         venir: yo vengo , tú vienes...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         caer: yo caigo, tú caes...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         traer: yo traigo, tú traes...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         oír: yo oigo, tú oyes...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         hacer: yo hago, tú haces...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         decir: yo digo, tú dices...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         asir: yo asgo, tú ases...
        </i>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="ZC-verbs">
        ZC-verbs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: ZC-verbs">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       This group of verbs—which originated in the Latin
       <a href="/wiki/Inchoative_verb" title="Inchoative verb">
        inchoative verbs
       </a>
       but now includes other verbs as well— substitute
       <i>
        -zc-
       </i>
       for stem-final
       <i>
        -c-
       </i>
       before
       <i>
        o
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        a
       </i>
       . The group includes nearly all verbs ending in
       <i>
        -acer
       </i>
       (except
       <i>
        hacer
       </i>
       and derived verbs),
       <i>
        -ecer
       </i>
       (except
       <i>
        mecer
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        remecer
       </i>
       ),
       <i>
        -ocer
       </i>
       (except
       <i>
        cocer
       </i>
       and derived verbs), and
       <i>
        -ucir
       </i>
       . For example:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         nacer: yo nazco, tú naces...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         crecer: yo crezco, tú creces...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         conocer: yo conozco, tú conoces...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         producir: yo produzco, tú produces...
        </i>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       <i>
        Yacer
       </i>
       may alternatively be conjugated with
       <i>
        -zc-
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        yazco
       </i>
       ),
       <i>
        -g-
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        yago
       </i>
       ) or a compromise
       <i>
        -zg-
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        yazgo
       </i>
       ).
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Irregular_forms_in_the_future.2C_conditional_and_imperative">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Irregular_forms_in_the_future,_conditional_and_imperative">
        Irregular forms in the future, conditional and imperative
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Irregular forms in the future, conditional and imperative">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Some
       <i>
        -er
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -ir
       </i>
       verbs (most
       <i>
        G-
       </i>
       verbs plus
       <i>
        haber
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        saber
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        poder
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        querer
       </i>
       ) also change their stem in the future and conditional tenses. This involves:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Just dropping the infinitive
        <i>
         e
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         haber
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         habr-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         saber
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         sabr-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         poder
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         podr-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         querer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         querr-
        </i>
        ...
       </li>
       <li>
        Dropping the infinitive
        <i>
         e/i
        </i>
        and padding the resulting
        <i>
         *-lr-/*-nr-
        </i>
        with a
        <i>
         -d-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         tener
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         tendr-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         poner
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         pondr-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         venir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         vendr-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         valer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         valdr-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         salir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         saldr-
        </i>
        ...
       </li>
       <li>
        Dropping the infinitive
        <i>
         -ce-
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         -ec-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         hacer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         har-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         deshacer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         deshar-
        </i>
        ...,
        <i>
         decir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         dir-
        </i>
        ...
        <i>
         Predecir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         contradecir
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         desdecir
        </i>
        may share this irregularity (
        <i>
         predir-
        </i>
        ...) or, more commonly, use the regular forms (
        <i>
         predecir-
        </i>
        ). For
        <i>
         bendecir
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         maldecir
        </i>
        only the regular forms are used (
        <i>
         bendecir-
        </i>
        ...).
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Many of these verbs also have shortened
       <i>
        tú
       </i>
       imperative forms:
       <i>
        tener
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        ten
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        contener
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        contén
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        poner
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        pon
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        disponer
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        dispón
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        venir
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        ven
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        salir
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        sal
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        hacer
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        haz
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        decir
       </i>
       →
       <i>
        di
       </i>
       . However, all verbs derived from
       <i>
        decir
       </i>
       are regular in this form:
       <i>
        bendice
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        maldice
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        desdícete
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        predice
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        contradice
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Anomalous_stems_in_the_preterite_and_derived_tenses">
        Anomalous stems in the preterite and derived tenses
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Anomalous stems in the preterite and derived tenses">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Some verbs (including most
       <i>
        G
       </i>
       -verbs) have a completely different stem in the
       <a href="/wiki/Preterite" title="Preterite">
        preterite
       </a>
       . These stems are very old and often are found in
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latin
       </a>
       as well. The same irregular stem is also found in the imperfect subjunctive (both in
       <i>
        -ra
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -se
       </i>
       forms) and the future subjunctive. This stems are anomalous also because:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        they are stressed in the first and third persons singular, ending in unstressed
        <i>
         -e
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         -o
        </i>
        respectively (while in all other cases the preterite gets the stress over the suffix).
       </li>
       <li>
        the rest of the endings are the usual for
        <i>
         -er/-ir
        </i>
        verbs even for the
        <i>
         -ar
        </i>
        verbs
        <i>
         estar
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         andar
        </i>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        in the verbs with
        <i>
         -je
        </i>
        preterite (
        <i>
         conducir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         decir
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         traer
        </i>
        ...) unstressed
        <i>
         i
        </i>
        is dropped between the
        <i>
         j
        </i>
        and a vowel:
        <i>
         ellos trajeron
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         yo trajera
        </i>
        ... Note that this doesn't happen with regular or vowel-raising
        <i>
         -ger/-jer/-gir/-jir
        </i>
        verbs (
        <i>
         proteger
        </i>
        &gt;
        <i>
         protegieron
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         tejer
        </i>
        &gt;
        <i>
         tejieron
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         corregir
        </i>
        &gt;
        <i>
         corrigieron
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         crujir
        </i>
        &gt;
        <i>
         crujieron
        </i>
        ).
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Examples:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>
         estar
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         estuv-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo estuve, tú/vos estuviste(s), él estuvo..., ellos estuvieron; yo estuviera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         andar
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         anduv-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo anduve, tú/vos anduviste(s), él anduvo..., ellos anduvieron; yo anduviera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         tener
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         tuv-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo tuve, tú/vos tuviste(s), él tuvo..., ellos tuvieron; yo tuviera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         haber
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         hub-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo hube, tú/vos hubiste(s), él hubo..., ellos hubieron; yo hubiera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         caber
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         cup-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo cupe, tú/vos cupiste(s), él cupo..., ellos cupieron; yo cupiera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         saber
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         sup-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo supe, tú/vos supiste(s), él supo..., ellos supieron; yo supiera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         venir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         vin-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo vine, tú/vos viniste(s), él vino..., ellos vinieron; yo viniera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         poder
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         pud-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo pude, tú/vos pudiste(s), él pudo..., ellos pudieron; yo pudiera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         poner
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         pus-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo puse, tú/vos pusiste(s), él puso..., ellos pusieron; yo pusiera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         hacer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         hic-/hiz-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo hice, tú/vos hiciste(s), él hizo..., ellos hicieron; yo hiciera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         reducir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         reduj-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo reduje, tu/vos redujiste(s), él redujo.., ellos condujeron; yo condujera...
        </i>
       </dd>
       <dd>
        <i>
         decir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         dij-
        </i>
        :
        <i>
         yo dije, tú/vos dijiste(s), él dijo..., ellos dijeron; yo dijera...
        </i>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Irregular_past_participles">
        Irregular past participles
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_irregular_verbs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Irregular past participles">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       A number of verbs have irregular past participles, sometimes called "strong" because the change is in the root, rather than an ending. This includes verbs which are irregular in many other ways, as
       <i>
        poner
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        decir
       </i>
       , but for some other verbs this is their only irregularity (e.g.
       <i>
        abrir
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        romper
       </i>
       ), while some very irregular verbs (as
       <i>
        ser
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        ir
       </i>
       ) have regular past participles. Examples:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         abrir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         abierto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         morir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         muerto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         volver
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         vuelto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         devolver
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         devuelto
        </i>
        ...
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         romper
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         roto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         escribir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         escrito
        </i>
        ...
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         ver
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         visto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         prever
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         previsto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         poner
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         puesto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         componer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         compuesto
        </i>
        ...
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         hacer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         hecho
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         rehacer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         rehecho
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         decir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         dicho
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         predecir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         predicho
        </i>
        (but
        <i>
         bendecir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         bendecido
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         maldecir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         maldecido
        </i>
        )...
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         pudrir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         podrido
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       There are three verbs that have both a regular and an irregular past participle. Both forms may be used when conjugating the compound tenses and the passive voice with the auxiliary verbs
       <i>
        haber
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        ser
       </i>
       , but the irregular form is generally the only one used as an adjective:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         freír
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         he freído
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         he frito
        </i>
        , but
        <i>
         papas fritas
        </i>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         imprimir
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         he imprimido
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         he impreso
        </i>
        , but
        <i>
         papeles impresos
        </i>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         proveer
        </i>
        →
        <i>
         he proveído
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         he provisto
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         una despensa bien provista
        </i>
        far more usual than
        <i>
         una despensa bien proveída
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       A number of other "strong" past participles, such as
       <i>
        pinto
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        ducho
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        electo
       </i>
       , and a number of others, are obsolete for general use, but are occasionally used in Spain (and to a much lesser extent in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_America" title="Spanish America">
        Spanish America
       </a>
       ) among educated, style-conscious writers, or in linguistic archaisms such as proverbs (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_proverbs" title="Spanish proverbs">
        refranes
       </a>
       ).
      </p>

      <p>
       <span id="ser">
       </span>
       The verbs
       <i>
        ser
       </i>
       (to be) and
       <i>
        ir
       </i>
       (to go) both exhibit irregularities in the
       <a href="/wiki/Present_tense" title="Present tense">
        present
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Imperfect" title="Imperfect">
        imperfect
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Preterite" title="Preterite">
        preterite
       </a>
       forms. Together with
       <i>
        ver
       </i>
       (to see) and
       <i>
        prever
       </i>
       (to foresee), they are the only four verbs with irregular imperfect indicative. Their
       <i>
        tú
       </i>
       imperative forms are
       <i>
        sé
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        ve
       </i>
       (for both
       <i>
        ir
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        ver
       </i>
       , although
       <i>
        mirar
       </i>
       is more common than
       <i>
        ver
       </i>
       in commands) and
       <i>
        prevé
       </i>
       . Their
       <i>
        vos
       </i>
       imperative forms are
       <i>
        sé
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        andá
       </i>
       (the verb
       <i>
        andar
       </i>
       replaces
       <i>
        ir
       </i>
       ),
       <i>
        ve
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        prevé
       </i>
       .

      <p>
       Remember that whenever the preterite is irregular, the imperfect subjunctive (
       <i>
        -ra
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -se
       </i>
       forms) and the dated future subjunctive (
       <i>
        -re
       </i>
       ) share the same irregularity; indeed, these tenses may always be correctly formed by substituting the appropriate endings for the
       <i>
        -ron
       </i>
       ending of the third person plural preterite:
       <i>
        fueron
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        fuera/fuese,...; fuere...
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <span id="estar">
       </span>
       The verbs
       <i>
        dar
       </i>
       (to give) and
       <i>
        estar
       </i>
       (to be) both exhibit irregularities in the present indicative and present subjunctive because their stems cannot be stressed (in
       <i>
        dar
       </i>
       the stem is just
       <i>
        d-
       </i>
       , in
       <i>
        estar
       </i>
       it was originally
       <i>
        st-
       </i>
       ). The form
       <i>
        dé
       </i>
       is so written to distinguish it from the preposition
       <i>
        de
       </i>
       . Both verbs are also irregular in the preterite and derived tenses:
       <i>
        dar
       </i>
       follows the pattern of regular
       <i>
        -er/-ir
       </i>
       verbs, while
       <i>
        estar

       has an

        anomalous preterite stem
