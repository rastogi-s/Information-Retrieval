      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Academia Panameña de la Lengua
       </b>
       (Spanish for
       <i>
        Panamanian Academy of Language
       </i>
       ) is an association of academics and experts on the use of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">
        Panama
       </a>
       . It was founded in
       <a href="/wiki/Panama_City" title="Panama City">
        Panama City
       </a>
       on August 9, 1926. Both former
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_state_of_Panama" title="List of heads of state of Panama">
        presidents
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Joaqu%C3%ADn_Alfaro_Jovan%C3%A9" title="Ricardo Joaquín Alfaro Jované">
        Ricardo Joaquín Alfaro Jované
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ernesto_de_la_Guardia" title="Ernesto de la Guardia">
        Ernesto de la Guardia
       </a>
       were members of the academy. It is a member of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_of_Spanish_Language_Academies" title="Association of Spanish Language Academies">
        Association of Spanish Language Academies
       </a>
       .
      </p>
