      <p>
       <b>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" title="Denver, Colorado">
         Denver
        </a>
       </b>
       is the central city of a
       <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">
        conurbation
       </a>
       region in the
       <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">
        U.S. state
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">
        Colorado
       </a>
       . The conurbation includes one continuous region consisting of the six central counties of Adams, Arapahoe, Broomfield, Denver, Douglas and Jefferson. The Denver region is part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Front_Range_Urban_Corridor" title="Front Range Urban Corridor">
        Front Range Urban Corridor
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Office_of_Management_and_Budget" title="United States Office of Management and Budget">
        United States Office of Management and Budget
       </a>
       has delineated the
       <b>
        Denver–Aurora–Lakewood, CO Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </b>
       consisting of ten Colorado
       <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">
        counties
       </a>
       : the
       <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">
        City and County of Denver
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Arapahoe_County,_Colorado" title="Arapahoe County, Colorado">
        Arapahoe County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Colorado" title="Jefferson County, Colorado">
        Jefferson County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Adams_County,_Colorado" title="Adams County, Colorado">
        Adams County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Douglas_County,_Colorado" title="Douglas County, Colorado">
        Douglas County
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Broomfield,_Colorado" title="Broomfield, Colorado">
        City and County of Broomfield
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Elbert_County,_Colorado" title="Elbert County, Colorado">
        Elbert County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Park_County,_Colorado" title="Park County, Colorado">
        Park County
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Clear_Creek_County,_Colorado" title="Clear Creek County, Colorado">
        Clear Creek County
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Gilpin_County,_Colorado" title="Gilpin County, Colorado">
        Gilpin County
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OMB_13-01_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-OMB_13-01-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">
        United States Census Bureau
       </a>
       estimates that the population was 2,812,732 as of July 1, 2015, an increase of +10.6% since the
       <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">
        2010 United States Census
       </a>
       , and ranking as the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Metropolitan_Statistical_Areas" title="List of Metropolitan Statistical Areas">
        19th most populous
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Metropolitan Statistical Area">
        metropolitan statistical area
       </a>
       of the United States.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopEstCBSA2012_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-PopEstCBSA2012-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Office of Management and Budget also delineated the more extensive
       <a href="/wiki/Denver%E2%80%93Aurora_combined_statistical_area" title="Denver–Aurora combined statistical area">
        Denver–Aurora combined statistical area
       </a>
       comprising the Denver-Aurora-Lakewood Metropolitan Statistical Area, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boulder,_CO_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Boulder, CO Metropolitan Statistical Area">
        Boulder Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </a>
       , and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greeley,_CO_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Greeley, CO Metropolitan Statistical Area">
        Greeley Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OMB_13-01_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-OMB_13-01-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The central part of the metropolitan statistical area (MSA) includes Denver and three immediately adjacent counties: Jefferson County to the west, Adams County to the north and east, and Arapahoe County to the south and east. The continuously urbanized area extends northwest into the
       <a href="/wiki/Broomfield,_Colorado" title="Broomfield, Colorado">
        City and County of Broomfield
       </a>
       , bordering Jefferson and Adams counties, and south into Douglas County, adjoining Arapahoe County. Also included in the federally defined MSA are four rural counties:
       <a href="/wiki/Elbert_County,_Colorado" title="Elbert County, Colorado">
        Elbert County
       </a>
       on the southeastern prairie and
       <a href="/wiki/Clear_Creek_County,_Colorado" title="Clear Creek County, Colorado">
        Clear Creek
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Gilpin_County,_Colorado" title="Gilpin County, Colorado">
        Gilpin
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Park_County,_Colorado" title="Park County, Colorado">
        Park
       </a>
       counties in the
       <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">
        Rocky Mountains
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_cooperation">
        Regional cooperation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Denver_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Regional cooperation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Denver_Regional_Council_of_Governments" title="Denver Regional Council of Governments">
        Denver Regional Council of Governments
       </a>
       (DRCOG, pronounced Doctor Cog) is a regional planning and inter-governmental coordination organization in a nine-county region. The
       <a href="/wiki/Scientific_and_Cultural_Facilities_District" title="Scientific and Cultural Facilities District">
        Scientific and Cultural Facilities District
       </a>
       (SCFD) provides funding for scientific and cultural facilities in a seven-county region including:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Denver_Museum_of_Nature_and_Science" title="Denver Museum of Nature and Science">
         Denver Museum of Nature and Science
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Denver_Zoo" title="Denver Zoo">
         Denver Zoo
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Denver_Art_Museum" title="Denver Art Museum">
         Denver Art Museum
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Denver_Center_for_the_Performing_Arts" title="Denver Center for the Performing Arts">
         Denver Center for the Performing Arts
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Denver_Botanic_Gardens" title="Denver Botanic Gardens">
         Denver Botanic Gardens
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       In addition, the
       <a href="/wiki/Regional_Transportation_District" title="Regional Transportation District">
        Regional Transportation District
       </a>
       (RTD) provides
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mass_transit" title="Mass transit">
        mass transit
       </a>
       , including a
       <a href="/wiki/RTD_Bus_%26_Rail" title="RTD Bus &amp; Rail">
        light rail
       </a>
       system. In 2005 the RTD developed a twelve-year comprehensive plan, called "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fastracks" title="Fastracks">
        FasTracks
       </a>
       ", to build and operate
       <a href="/wiki/Urban_rail_transit" title="Urban rail transit">
        rail transit
       </a>
       lines and expand and improve bus service throughout the region.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">
        Economy
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Denver_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Economy">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The most prosperous parts of the area are in the south, while the most industrialized areas are in the northeast, specifically in the northern part of Denver proper and extending to areas such as
       <a href="/wiki/Commerce_City,_Colorado" title="Commerce City, Colorado">
        Commerce City
       </a>
       in Adams County.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Changes in
       <a href="/wiki/House_price_index" title="House price index">
        house prices
       </a>
       for the area are publicly tracked on a regular basis using the
       <a href="/wiki/Case%E2%80%93Shiller_index" title="Case–Shiller index">
        Case–Shiller index
       </a>
       ; the statistic is published by
       <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">
        Standard &amp; Poor's
       </a>
       and is also a component of S&amp;P's 10-city
       <a href="/wiki/Composite_(finance)" title="Composite (finance)">
        composite index
       </a>
       of the value of the residential real estate market.
      </p>
      <p>
       Electricity is provided by
       <a href="/wiki/Xcel_Energy" title="Xcel Energy">
        Xcel Energy
       </a>
       . Cable television is provided by
       <a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">
        Comcast
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Air_quality">
        Air quality
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Denver_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Air quality">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The center of the metropolitan area sits in a valley, the
       <a href="/wiki/Denver_Basin" title="Denver Basin">
        Denver Basin
       </a>
       , and suffers from
       <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">
        air pollution
       </a>
       known colloquially as the
       <i>
        brown cloud
       </i>
       , building up if the air is stagnant as it often is in the winter. Severity of pollution in this area has varied enormously over the years. In the late 1980s the area was frequently in violation of multiple
       <a href="/wiki/National_Ambient_Air_Quality_Standards" title="National Ambient Air Quality Standards">
        National Ambient Air Quality Standards
       </a>
       established by the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">
        United States Environmental Protection Agency
       </a>
       (EPA). The Regional Air Quality Council (RAQC) was formed in 1989 to create plans to address the problem. Through a variety of measures the area's air quality was improved and in 2002 the EPA designated the area in compliance with all federal health-based air quality standards. Denver was the first major city in the United States to reach compliance with all six of these standards after previously violating five of them.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Since then the EPA introduced a new standard for small particulates and made the existing ozone standard stricter. In 2003 the new ozone standard was frequently exceeded in the area and was occasionally exceeded as far away as
       <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_National_Park" title="Rocky Mountain National Park">
        Rocky Mountain National Park
       </a>
       . The RAQC hopes to implement plans enabling the area to comply with the new standards by 2007.
      </p>
