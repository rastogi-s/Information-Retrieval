      <p>
       The
       <b>
        50th parallel north
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">
        circle of latitude
       </a>
       that is 50
       <a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">
        degrees
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/True_north" title="True north">
        north
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth's
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equatorial plane
       </a>
       . It crosses
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        sun
       </a>
       is visible for 16 hours, 22 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">
        summer solstice
       </a>
       and 8 hours, 4 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">
        winter solstice
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The maximum altitude of the sun on the summer solstice is 63.5 degrees and on the winter solstice it is 16.5 degrees.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude, the average sea surface temperature between 1982 and 2011 was about 8.5°C (47.3°F).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sakhalin_island">
        Sakhalin island
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=50th_parallel_north&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sakhalin island">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       During the
       <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">
        Empire of Japan
       </a>
       era, from 1905 to 1945, the 50th parallel marked the
       <a href="/wiki/Japan%E2%80%93Russia_border" title="Japan–Russia border">
        border
       </a>
       between the
       <a href="/wiki/Karafuto_Prefecture" title="Karafuto Prefecture">
        Karafuto Prefecture
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
        Japan
       </a>
       to the south with the
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">
        Russian Empire
       </a>
       , and later the
       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">
        Soviet Union
       </a>
       , to the north in the island of
       <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin" title="Sakhalin">
        Sakhalin
       </a>
       . The entire island came under Soviet control after
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        World War II
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
