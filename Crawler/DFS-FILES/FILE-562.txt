      <p>
       <b>
        San Antonio–New Braunfels
       </b>
       is an eight-county
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_metropolitan_area" title="United States metropolitan area">
        metropolitan area
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        U.S.
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">
        state
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
        Texas
       </a>
       defined by the
       <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Management_and_Budget" title="Office of Management and Budget">
        Office of Management and Budget
       </a>
       (OMB). Colloquially referred to as
       <span class="nowrap">
        <b>
         Greater San Antonio
        </b>
       </span>
       , the metropolitan area straddles
       <a href="/wiki/South_Texas" title="South Texas">
        South Texas
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Texas" title="Central Texas">
        Central Texas
       </a>
       and is on the southwestern corner of the
       <a href="/wiki/Texas_Triangle" title="Texas Triangle">
        Texas Triangle
       </a>
       . The official 2011 U.S. Census estimate
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GR2-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       showed the metropolitan area's population at 2,194,927—up from a reported 1,711,103 in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2000" title="United States Census, 2000">
        2000
       </a>
       —making it the 24th largest metropolitan area in the United States.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Greater_Austin" title="Greater Austin">
        Austin–Round Rock
       </a>
       lies about 80 miles northeast of Greater San Antonio.
      </p>
      <p>
       San Antonio–New Braunfels is the third-largest metro area in Texas, after
       <span class="nowrap">
        <a href="/wiki/Dallas%E2%80%93Fort_Worth_metroplex" title="Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex">
         Dallas–Fort Worth–Arlington
        </a>
       </span>
       and
       <span class="nowrap">
        <a href="/wiki/Greater_Houston" title="Greater Houston">
         Houston–The Woodlands–Sugar Land
        </a>
       </span>
       . It is also the second fastest-growing large metropolitan area in the state (over 1 million population), after Austin–Round Rock and just ahead of Houston–The Woodlands–Sugar Land.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       Greater San Antonio has a number of communities spread out across several counties and regions. It is centered around the
       <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">

       </a>

        Uptown San Antonio
       </a>

      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/VIA_Metropolitan_Transit" title="VIA Metropolitan Transit">
        VIA Metropolitan Transit
       </a>

      </div>
      <p>
       The City of San Antonio is home to many public institutions. The San Antonio area's largest university is the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_San_Antonio" title="University of Texas at San Antonio">
        University of Texas at San Antonio
       </a>
       (UTSA). Other public institutions include the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_Health_Science_Center_at_San_Antonio" title="University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio">
        University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Texas_A%26M_University%E2%80%93San_Antonio" title="Texas A&amp;M University–San Antonio">
        Texas A&amp;M University–San Antonio
       </a>
       , and the five colleges of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alamo_Community_College_District" title="Alamo Community College District">
        Alamo Community College District
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The city has many private institutions as well, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_the_Lake_University" title="Our Lady of the Lake University">
        Our Lady of the Lake University
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s_University,_Texas" title="St. Mary's University, Texas">
        St. Mary's University
       </a>
       on the inner west side.
       <a href="/wiki/Trinity_University_(Texas)" title="Trinity University (Texas)">
        Trinity University
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Incarnate_Word" title="University of the Incarnate Word">
        University of the Incarnate Word
       </a>
       are in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neighborhoods_of_San_Antonio#Midtown" title="Neighborhoods of San Antonio">
        Midtown
       </a>
       . The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Culinary_Institute_of_America" title="Culinary Institute of America">
        Culinary Institute of America
       </a>
       maintains its third campus in
       <a href="/wiki/Downtown_San_Antonio" title="Downtown San Antonio">
        Downtown
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Texas_Lutheran_University" title="Texas Lutheran University">
        Texas Lutheran University
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Seguin,_Texas" title="Seguin, Texas">
        Seguin
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Howard_Payne_University_at_New_Braunfels" title="Howard Payne University at New Braunfels">
        Howard Payne University at New Braunfels
       </a>
       , now offering classes at a local high school but will soon have a true campus in the Veramendi Development, are the only higher education institutions in the area outside of San Antonio city limits.
      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">

      <p>
       As of the
       <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">
        census
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-GR2-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       of 2000, there were 1,711,703 people, 601,265 households, and 432,131 families residing within the MSA. The racial makeup of the MSA was 71.4%
       <a href="/wiki/White_Americans" title="White Americans">
        White
       </a>
       , 6.2%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">
        African American
       </a>
       , 0.8%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">
        Native American
       </a>
       , 1.4%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">
        Asian
       </a>
       , 0.1%
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">
        Pacific Islander
       </a>
       , 16.6% from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">
        other races
       </a>
       , and 3.4% from two or more races.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">
        Hispanic
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">
        Latino
       </a>
       of any race were 50.4% of the population. The median income for a household in the MSA was $40,764 and the median income for a family was $46,686. Males had a median income of $32,143 versus $24,007 for females. The
       <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">
        per capita income
       </a>
       for the MSA was $18,713.
