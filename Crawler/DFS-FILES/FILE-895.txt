      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese language
       </a>
       is spoken in Asia by small communities either in regions which formerly served as colonies to Portugal, notably
       <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">
        Macau
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">
        East Timor
       </a>
       where the language is official albeit not widely spoken, or of
       <a href="/wiki/Lusophone" title="Lusophone">
        Lusophone
       </a>
       immigrants, notably the
       <a href="/wiki/Brazilians_in_Japan" title="Brazilians in Japan">
        Brazilians in Japan
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <ul>
       <li>
        <b>
         <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">
          Sri Lanka
         </a>
        </b>
        : Formerly known as
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Ceylon" title="Portuguese Ceylon">
         Ceylon
        </a>
        (
        <i>
         Ceilão
        </i>
        in Portuguese), the island's first European visitors were
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">
         Portuguese people
        </a>
        , who gave the island its original name. The island is home to a
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Burghers" title="Portuguese Burghers">
         Portuguese Burgher
        </a>
        minority who speaks
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Portuguese_Creole" title="Sri Lankan Portuguese Creole">
         Sri Lankan Portuguese Creole
        </a>
        . Sri Lanka participated in the
        <a href="/wiki/Lusophony_Games" title="Lusophony Games">
         <i>
          Jogos da Lusofonia
         </i>
        </a>
        in 2006 and 2014.
       </li>
       <li>
        <b>
         <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
          India
         </a>
        </b>
        : The state of
        <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">
         Goa
        </a>
        was a part of the
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Empire" title="Portuguese Empire">
         Portuguese Empire
        </a>
        until 1961. India participated in the
        <a href="/wiki/Lusophony_Games" title="Lusophony Games">
         <i>
          Jogos da Lusofonia
         </i>
        </a>
        in 2006 and 2014. Goa is awaiting the permission of the Indian Government to join the CPLP as an observer.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
        In Goa, most of the relatively few speakers of Portuguese are older people. The Union Territory of
        <a href="/wiki/Daman_and_Diu" title="Daman and Diu">
         Daman and Diu
        </a>
        was also part of
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_India" title="Portuguese India">
         Portuguese India
        </a>
        . As in Goa, the dwindling number of Portuguese speakers are also older people. Daman and Diu are also home to
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indo-Portuguese" title="Indo-Portuguese">
         Indo-Portuguese
        </a>
        Creoles. However, the Portuguese language and culture is undergoing a kind of renaissance in the former Portuguese Indian colonies. There is even talk of making Portuguese co-official alongside Konkani. It is estimated that there are 3% – 5% of fluent speakers of Portuguese in Goa, Daman and Diu. The language is still spoken by about 10,000 people, in 2014, an estimated 1,500 students were learning Portuguese in Goa.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
         <a href="#cite_note-3">
          [3]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <b>
         <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">
          Malaysia
         </a>
        </b>
        and
        <b>
         <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">
          Singapore
         </a>
        </b>
        : The Malaysian state of
        <a href="/wiki/Malacca" title="Malacca">
         Malacca
        </a>
        and city-state of Singapore are homes to the
        <a href="/wiki/Kristang_people" title="Kristang people">
         Gente Kristang
        </a>
        a community of
        <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_(mixed_ancestry)" title="Eurasian (mixed ancestry)">
         Eurasians
        </a>
        who claim Portuguese descent and speak
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papia_Kristang" title="Papia Kristang">
         Papia Kristang
        </a>
        , a Portuguese-lexified Creole. The
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_Settlement,_Malacca" title="Portuguese Settlement, Malacca">
         Portuguese settlement at Malacca
        </a>
        is a source of tourism for the state and the Lusophone heritage is visible in cuisine, architecture and folklore of the Kristang people. Pending approval from the Malaysian Government, Malacca may join the CPLP as an associate observer.
       </li>
       <li>
        <b>
         <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">
          Macau
         </a>
        </b>
        : Portuguese is a co-official language alongside Chinese in the Chinese
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Administrative_Region_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China">
         special administrative region
        </a>
        of
        <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">
         Macau
        </a>
        . It has become the centre for Portuguese learning in Asia and has become the focus through which China relates diplomatically to the member states of CPLP. Macau was the host city for the first Lusophone games in 2006. While the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patu%C3%A1" title="Patuá">
         Macanese Language
        </a>
        is by now critically endangered with less than a hundred speakers, the number of speakers of Portuguese has also decreased since the handover in 1999. But enrollments for private Portuguese classes have tripled, to 1,000, since 2002; that prompted public schools here to offer Portuguese, drawing more than 5,000 students.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-4">
          [4]
         </a>
        </sup>
        It is now estimated that about 3% of the population speak Portuguese as their first language, while 7% professes fluency.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
         <a href="#cite_note-5">
          [5]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <b>
         <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">
          East Timor
         </a>
        </b>
        : The Southeast Asian country added Portuguese as an official language as it gained independence from Indonesia in 2002. According to a 2004 census, 36 percent of respondents said they had "a capability in Portuguese".
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
         <a href="#cite_note-6">
          [6]
         </a>
        </sup>
        The inter-ethnic lingua franca,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tetum" title="Tetum">
         Tetum
        </a>
        has a large number of loanwords derived from Portuguese making the latter relatively easy to learn for speakers of the former.
       </li>
       <li>
        <b>
         <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
          Japan
         </a>
        </b>
        : As of 2005, there were approximately 302,000
        <a href="/wiki/Brazilians_in_Japan" title="Brazilians in Japan">
         Brazilians living in Japan
        </a>
        .
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
         <a href="#cite_note-7">
          [7]
         </a>
        </sup>
        This is the largest immigrant community after the Chinese and Korean communities. This 'return migration' to Japan has resulted in the largest Portuguese-speaking community in Asia.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="CPLP">
        CPLP
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portuguese_language_in_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: CPLP">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Various regions in
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       have expressed interest in participating in the Community of Portuguese-speaking Countries (the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CPLP" title="CPLP">
        CPLP
       </a>
       ). The Malaysian state of Malacca, Macau, and the Indian state of Goa have all applied for observer or associate member status and are awaiting the permission of the Malaysian, Chinese, and Indian governments, respectively. East Timor joined the CPLP shortly after its independence at the turn of the 21st century.
       <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
        Indonesia
       </a>
       has also expressed interest in joining the CPLP.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="Instituto_Cam.C3.B5es">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Instituto_Camões">
        Instituto Camões
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portuguese_language_in_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Instituto Camões">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Cam%C3%B5es" title="Instituto Camões">
        Instituto Camões
       </a>
       maintains language centres in Macau, Goa, Busan and Dili.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Local_norms_and_phonology">
        Local norms and phonology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portuguese_language_in_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Local norms and phonology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In Asia, Standard
       <a href="/wiki/European_Portuguese" title="European Portuguese">
        European Portuguese
       </a>
       (português-padrão) forms the basis for the written and spoken norm, exclusively to East Timor and Macau.
      </p>
