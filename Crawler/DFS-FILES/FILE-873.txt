      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Maracucho Spanish
       </b>
       (also called
       <b>
        maracaibero
       </b>
       ,
       <b>
        marabino
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        zuliano
       </b>
       ) is the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linguistic_variety" title="Linguistic variety">
        variety
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       generally spoken in the
       <a href="/wiki/Zulia" title="Zulia">
        Zulia
       </a>
       state in the northwest of
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
        Venezuela
       </a>
       and the west of the Falcón state (
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mauroa_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mauroa Municipality (page does not exist)">
        Mauroa Municipality
       </a>
       ). Unlike the dialects from
       <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">
        Caracas
       </a>
       or the
       <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">
        Venezuelan Andean region
       </a>
       , the maracaibero is typically
       <a href="/wiki/Voseo" title="Voseo">
        voseo
       </a>
       . Preserves for this combination the shape of the second person plural familiar (
       <i>
        vosotros
       </i>
       ), without
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ap%C3%B3cope&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Apócope (page does not exist)">
        apócope
       </a>
       or
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Linguistic_syncopation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Linguistic syncopation (page does not exist)">
        syncopation
       </a>
       , which distinguishes it from the
       <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Spanish" title="Chilean Spanish">
        Chilean
       </a>
       voseo and the
       <a href="/wiki/Rioplatense_Spanish" title="Rioplatense Spanish">
        Rioplatense
       </a>
       voseo, respectively.
      </p>

      <p>
       Besides the maracaibero is characterized by the use of many words and expressions different to the particular Spanish from Venezuela and an accent markedly different to from other regions of the country. An interesting fact is that the
       <a href="/wiki/Gentile" title="Gentile">
        gentiles
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo" title="Maracaibo">
        Maracaibo
       </a>
       are due to the type of speech that uses each of the people of
       <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">
        Municipality
       </a>
       , the maracucho is characterized (in other states of Venezuela) for being foul-mouthed and sometimes cracked, but this is (depending on which part of the region they are in) The maracaiberos is distinguished by the use of unique phrases in
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
        Venezuela
       </a>
       , and the use of colloquial language, some examples of which are:
       <i>
        ¡Que molleja!
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        ¡A la vaina!
       </i>
       <i>
        Vercía
       </i>
       that indicate astonishment,
       <i>
        Mollejúo
       </i>
       to something big.
      </p>
      <p>
       Are also used expressions such as "Mialma" and "Vergación" for example; "¡Mialma, no sabía eso!" And the "Vergación" when something seems surprising them also to emphasize that if in the size, color, odor and other characteristics on what they refer to, example; "Vergación de grande es tu casa" or "vergación de calor hay" usually it is used by the maracaiberos as these expressions are seen as rude or vulgar.
      </p>
