      <p>
       <b>
        Ambeth R. Ocampo
       </b>
       <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;">
        <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Lakandula" title="Order of Lakandula">
         OL
        </a>
        <a href="/wiki/Ordre_des_Arts_et_des_Lettres" title="Ordre des Arts et des Lettres">
         OAL
        </a>
        <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Civil_Merit" title="Order of Civil Merit">
         OCM
        </a>
       </span>
       (born August 13, 1961) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Filipino
       </a>
       historian, academic, journalist, former cultural administrator and author best known for his writings about Philippines' national hero
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Rizal" title="José Rizal">
        José Rizal
       </a>
       and for
       <i>
        Looking Back
       </i>
       , his bi-weekly editorial page column in the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Daily_Inquirer" title="Philippine Daily Inquirer">
         Philippine Daily Inquirer
        </a>
       </i>
       . He served as the
       <a href="/wiki/National_Historical_Commission_of_the_Philippines" title="National Historical Commission of the Philippines">
        Chairman of the National Historical Commission of the Philippines
       </a>
       (2002-2011) and concurrently Chairman of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Commission_for_Culture_and_the_Arts_(Philippines)" title="National Commission for Culture and the Arts (Philippines)">
        National Commission for Culture and the Arts
       </a>
       in (2005-2007).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Personal_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Personal-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Education">
        Education
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambeth_Ocampo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Ocampo received his Primary and Secondary education at the Jesuit-run
       <a href="/wiki/Ateneo_de_Manila_University" title="Ateneo de Manila University">
        Ateneo de Manila University
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       He subsequently attained his undergraduate and master degrees in Philippine Studies from the
       <a href="/wiki/De_La_Salle_University" title="De La Salle University">
        De La Salle University
       </a>
       in 1989 and 1991. He took graduate courses in the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Philippines_Diliman" title="University of the Philippines Diliman">
        University of the Philippines Diliman
       </a>
       and later read for a doctorate degree in Southeast Asian History at the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">
        University of London
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/School_of_Oriental_and_African_Studies" title="School of Oriental and African Studies">
        School of Oriental and African Studies
       </a>
       (SOAS).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He abandoned his London postgraduate studies in 1993, when he entered the Benedictine Abbey of Our Lady of Montserrat, Manila taking the monastic name Dom. Ignacio Maria, OSB.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Writings">
        Writings
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambeth_Ocampo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Writings">

      </h3>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Looking_Back_column">
        Looking Back column
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambeth_Ocampo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Looking Back column">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Ocampo began writing for
       <i>
        Weekend Magazine
       </i>
       , the Sunday supplement of the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Daily_Express&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philippine Daily Express (page does not exist)">
        Philippine Daily Express
       </a>
       in 1985 and subsequently joined its editorial staff as Associate Editor. His column
       <i>
        Looking Back
       </i>
       first appeared in the
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Daily_Globe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philippine Daily Globe (page does not exist)">
         Philippine Daily Globe
        </a>
       </i>
       from 1987 to 1990 and compilations of these columns saw new life as his two bestselling books, namely, the
       <i>
        Looking Back
       </i>
       series and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Rizal_Without_the_Overcoat" title="Rizal Without the Overcoat">
         Rizal Without the Overcoat
        </a>
       </i>
       that was awarded the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine_National_Book_Awards" title="Philippine National Book Awards">
        National Book Award
       </a>
       for essay in 1990. At the invitation of
       <a href="/wiki/Letty_Jimenez_Magsanoc" title="Letty Jimenez Magsanoc">
        Letty Jimenez Magsanoc
       </a>
       and Eugenia D. Apostol, Ocampo's
       <i>
        Daily Globe
       </i>
       column moved to the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Daily_Inquirer" title="Philippine Daily Inquirer">
         Philippine Daily Inquirer
        </a>
       </i>
       in 1990, where it appeared twice weekly.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Overcoat_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Overcoat-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Since 1986, Ocampo has published over 39 books, as well as other publications, compilations of his essays and writings on Philippine history, arts and culture. Most of his published works focus on the life and works of the Philippine nationalist and martyr,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Rizal" title="Jose Rizal">
        Jose Rizal
       </a>
       with
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Rizal_Without_the_Overcoat" title="Rizal Without the Overcoat">
         Rizal Without the Overcoat
        </a>
       </i>
       going into six editions since its first publication in 1990. He has published monographs on other historical and cultural figures: the writer-artist
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Emilio_Aguilar_Cruz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Emilio Aguilar Cruz (page does not exist)">
        Emilio Aguilar Cruz
       </a>
       , musical composer
       <a href="/wiki/Nicanor_Abelardo" title="Nicanor Abelardo">
        Nicanor Abelardo
       </a>
       , historian
       <a href="/wiki/Teodoro_Agoncillo" title="Teodoro Agoncillo">
        Teodoro Agoncillo
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Teodora_Alonso&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Teodora Alonso (page does not exist)">
        Teodora Alonso
       </a>
       , the mother of Rizal, among others.
      </p>
      <p>
       Ocampo also contributed to works on the foreign relations of the Philippines with
       <a href="/wiki/France%E2%80%93Philippines_relations" title="France–Philippines relations">
        France
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Japan%E2%80%93Philippines_relations" title="Japan–Philippines relations">
        Japan
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-published_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-published-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2016, he was at work on an edition of the diaries of former Philippine President
       <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Marcos" title="Ferdinand Marcos">
        Ferdinand Marcos
       </a>
       and a biography of former Philippine President
       <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Ramos" title="Fidel Ramos">
        Fidel Ramos
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       When Ocampo was appointed chair of the National Historical Institute and later elected chair of the National Commission for Culture and the Arts, then President
       <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal_Arroyo" title="Gloria Macapagal Arroyo">
        Gloria Macapagal Arroyo
       </a>
       declared that she was an ardent reader of his newspaper column, commending his writings because he "makes history so approachable." Some academics have criticized Ocampo for his
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Populist" title="Populist">
        populist
       </a>
       approach towards historiography. In response, Ocampo has since released two compilations of his public lectures
       <i>
        Meaning and History
       </i>
       focused on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Rizal" title="Jose Rizal">
        Jose Rizal
       </a>
       and
       <i>
        Bones of Contention
       </i>
       on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andres_Bonifacio" title="Andres Bonifacio">
        Andres Bonifacio
       </a>
       both published in 2001 with the required bibliographic references and footnotes. Nevertheless, Ocampo is considered as one of the most prominent Philippine historians.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Art_historian_and_curator">

      <p>
       Ocampo has also written several essays and monographs on Philippine art beginning with his first book on Philippine modern impressionist painter Emilio Aguilar Cruz in 1986. Since then Ocampo has worked as independent art curator and has curated several landmark
       <a href="/wiki/Retrospective#Arts_and_popular_culture" title="Retrospective">
        artist retrospectives
       </a>
       and exhibitions on Philippine visual artists including:
       <a href="/wiki/Juan_Luna" title="Juan Luna">
        Juan Luna
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Amorsolo" title="Fernando Amorsolo">
        Fernando C. Amorsolo
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Guillermo_Tolentino" title="Guillermo Tolentino">
        Guillermo E. Tolentino
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Romulo_Galicano" title="Romulo Galicano">
        Romulo Galicano
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arturo_Luz" title="Arturo Luz">
        Arturo R. Luz
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Benedicto_Cabrera" title="Benedicto Cabrera">
        Benedicto Cabrera
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Ateneo_Art_Gallery" title="Ateneo Art Gallery">
        Ateneo Art Gallery
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Ayala_Museum" title="Ayala Museum">
        Ayala Museum
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=BenCab_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="BenCab Museum (page does not exist)">
        BenCab Museum
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Lopez_Museum" title="Lopez Museum">
        Lopez Museum
       </a>
       and the President Elpidio Quirino Foundation.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GunsoftheKatipunan_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GunsoftheKatipunan-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Since 2011, he has delivered public lectures on Philippine history at the
       <a href="/wiki/Ayala_Museum" title="Ayala Museum">
        Ayala Museum
       </a>
       known as
       <i>
        History Comes Alive
       </i>
       to sold-out crowds.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GunsoftheKatipunan_11-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-GunsoftheKatipunan-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Government_service">
        Government service
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambeth_Ocampo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Government service">

       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_administrator">
        Cultural administrator
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambeth_Ocampo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cultural administrator">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       From 1987-1992, Ocampo served as a consultant to the
       <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_the_Philippines" title="National Library of the Philippines">
        National Library of the Philippines
       </a>
       (NLP) with a concurrent capacity as member of the National Committee on Libraries and Information (NCLI) and the National Committee on Monuments and Sites (NCOM) under the Presidential Commission on Culture and the Arts (the present-day
       <a href="/wiki/National_Commission_for_Culture_and_the_Arts" title="National Commission for Culture and the Arts">
        National Commission for Culture and the Arts
       </a>
       (NCCA)). During his term at the National Library he recovered the unpublished manuscripts of Rizal's unfinished novel
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Makamisa" title="Makamisa">
         Makamisa
        </a>
       </i>
       in 1987 and later worked on the bibliographic catalog of papers, writings and documents related to Rizal held in the vault of the National Library.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-National_Library_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-National_Library-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rizaliana_13-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Rizaliana-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He has recently completed a similar bibliographic catalog of the Rizal materials in the
       <a href="/wiki/Lopez_Museum" title="Lopez Museum">
        Lopez Museum and Library
       </a>
       and has been collating material from other sources for a Union Catalog of Rizaliana both in the Philippines and abroad.
      </p>
      <p>
       From 1996-1998, Manila Mayor
       <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Lim" title="Alfredo Lim">
        Alfredo Lim
       </a>
       appointed Ocampo co-Chair, with writer and historian
       <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Guerrero_Nakpil" title="Carmen Guerrero Nakpil">
        Carmen Guerrero Nakpil
       </a>
       of the Manila Historical and Heritage Commission, he was also consulted by the National Centennial Commission Chair Salvador H. Laurel during the celebrations of the centennials of: the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Rizal#Death" title="Jose Rizal">
        martyrdom of Jose Rizal
       </a>
       in 1996 and the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Centennial_of_Philippine_independence&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Centennial of Philippine independence (page does not exist)">
        Centennial
       </a>
       in 1998.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Government_Service_14-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Government_Service-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1999, Ocampo was appointed Board Member of the National Historical Institute by then President
       <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Estrada" title="Joseph Estrada">
        Joseph Estrada
       </a>
       and in 2002, he was appointed Chairman by then President
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal-Arroyo" title="Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo">
        Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo
       </a>
       . As NHI Chair, Ocampo served as a board member in the National Commission for Culture and the Arts (NCCA), and later was elected as its Chairman serving from 2005 to 2007. As NCCA Chairman, Ocampo held cabinet-rank, and lobbied for increased national funding for the arts by providing financial support for the national cultural agencies: the National Library,
       <a href="/wiki/National_Archives_of_the_Philippines" title="National Archives of the Philippines">
        National Archives
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_the_Philippines" title="National Museum of the Philippines">
        National Museum
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Center_of_the_Philippines" title="Cultural Center of the Philippines">
        Cultural Center of the Philippines
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Komisyon_ng_Wikang_Filipino" title="Komisyon ng Wikang Filipino">
        Komisyon ng Wikang Filipino
       </a>
       all attached to the NCCA. During his term, Ocampo focused on
       <a href="/wiki/Cultural_diplomacy" title="Cultural diplomacy">
        cultural diplomacy
       </a>
       as a main function in bridging
       <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_the_Philippines" title="Foreign relations of the Philippines">
        diplomatic relations of the Philippines
       </a>
       . For this the President granted Ocampo full powers to negotiate and sign cultural agreements with
       <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">
        Pakistan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">
        Vietnam
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">
        North Korea
       </a>
       as well as executive programs on culture with
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
        Mexico
       </a>
       , and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">
        People's Republic of China
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       During his term as NHI Chairman, Ocampo weathered criticism over attempts to enforce provisions of the existing Flag and Heraldic Code of the Philippines by reminding artists singing the
       <a href="/wiki/Lupang_Hinirang" title="Lupang Hinirang">
        Philippine national anthem
       </a>
       during international boxing matches of the proper way to sing the anthem, and his controversial decision to paint the Rizal clan house green - to remind Filipinos that the word "rizal" came from "ricial" meaning a green field ready for harvest.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He successfully worked for the passage of Republic Act 10086 restoring the National Historical Institute into the
       <a href="/wiki/National_Historical_Commission_of_the_Philippines" title="National Historical Commission of the Philippines">
        National Historical Commission of the Philippines
       </a>
       (NHCP). Passed in record time, less than a year, President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo signed the measure into law on May 12, 2010. Under the new law, the NHCP was reorganized, expanded, its duties and powers clarified and strengthened.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ocampo also served as Chairman of Incoming State Visits under Office of Presidential Protocol of
       <a href="/wiki/Malaca%C3%B1ang_Palace" title="Malacañang Palace">
        Malacañang Palace
       </a>
       from 2007 until 2011 and also sat as adviser of Numismatic Committee of the
       <a href="/wiki/Bangko_Sentral_ng_Pilipinas" title="Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas">
        Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas
       </a>
       . Along with Deputy Governor Diwa Guinigundo, the committee proposed and redesigned the entire Philippine banknotes in circulation and launched the New Generation Currency series in 2010.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_academia">
        In academia
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambeth_Ocampo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: In academia">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Ocampo is
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associate_Professor" title="Associate Professor">
        Associate Professor
       </a>
       and former Chairman of the Department of History, School of Social Sciences in the
       <a href="/wiki/Ateneo_de_Manila_University" title="Ateneo de Manila University">
        Ateneo de Manila University
       </a>
       , with which he has been connected since childhood. He was Professorial Lecturer in the Department of Filipino and Philippine Literature of the College of Arts and Letters in the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Philippines_Diliman" title="University of the Philippines Diliman">
        University of the Philippines Diliman
       </a>
       from 1989 until 2010 and served on the Board of Regents of the
       <a href="/wiki/Universidad_de_Manila" title="Universidad de Manila">
        Universidad de Manila
       </a>
       (formerly City College of Manila), where he served as its President and Vice-President for Academic Affairs from 1996 to 1998. He has also held previous appointments at the
       <a href="/wiki/De_La_Salle_University" title="De La Salle University">
        De La Salle University
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Far_Eastern_University" title="Far Eastern University">
        Far Eastern University
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/San_Beda_College" title="San Beda College">
        San Beda College
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He has held appointments as Visiting Research Fellow in
       <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_University" title="Kyoto University">
        Kyoto University
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Chulalongkorn_University" title="Chulalongkorn University">
        Chulalongkorn University
       </a>
       , Bangkok, and recently Visiting Professor in
       <a href="/wiki/Sophia_University" title="Sophia University">
        Sophia University
       </a>
       , Tokyo where he taught courses on Philippine history and culture from 2012 to 2014.
      </p>
      <p>
       His personal and official papers, notes and correspondence are deposited in the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Philippines" title="University of the Philippines">
        University of the Philippines
       </a>
       Archives in Diliman,
       <a href="/wiki/Quezon_City" title="Quezon City">
        Quezon City
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       A collector of Filipiniana including books, art, numismatics and other paper ephemera, his extensive library and collection is divided between his home in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Makati_City" title="Makati City">
        Makati City
       </a>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Holy_Angel_University" title="Holy Angel University">
        Holy Angel University
       </a>
       , Angeles,
       <a href="/wiki/Pampanga" title="Pampanga">
        Pampanga
       </a>
       ;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and the Center for Southeast Asian Studies Library in
       <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_University" title="Kyoto University">
        Kyoto University
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="Honors.2C_awards.2C_and_decorations">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Honors,_awards,_and_decorations">
        Honors, awards, and decorations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ambeth_Ocampo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Honors, awards, and decorations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Ocampo has won three
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine_National_Book_Awards" title="Philippine National Book Awards">
        National Book Awards
       </a>
       in these categories: Essay, Literary History, and Bibliography. He also won a Premio Manuel Bernabe awarded by the then Centro Cultural de la Embajada de Espana en Filipinas and a Premio Quijano de Manila from the
       <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Cervantes" title="Instituto Cervantes">
        Instituto Cervantes
       </a>
       Manila. He was elected National Fellow for Essay by the University of the Philippines Creative Writing Center (1995–1996).
      </p>
      <p>
       He was a
       <a href="/wiki/Fulbright_Program" title="Fulbright Program">
        Fulbright Senior Research Scholar
       </a>
       (2000); Research Fellow,
       <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_University" title="Kyoto University">
        Kyoto University
       </a>
       Center for Southeast Asian Studies (2003); Senior Fellow, Asian Public Intellectual Program awarded by the Nippon Foundation (2010) and participated in the Asia Leadership Fellow Program of the Japan Foundation and the International House of Japan in Tokyo (2014). His other awards include: TOYM Ten Outstanding Young Men (History) in 1997, Gawad Balagtas lifetime achievement award (Essay) from the Writers Union of the Philippines, 2006 MetroBank Outstanding Teacher Award,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Gatpuno Villegas Award (Patnubay ng Sining), one of the highest awards given by the City of Manila for Culture (2007).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Background_22-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Background-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Holy Angel University conferred the Juan D. Nepomuceno Award for Research and Scholarship on him in recognition of his research on Pampanga.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       He holds the rank of Commander of the Order of the
       <a href="/wiki/Knights_of_Rizal" title="Knights of Rizal">
        Knights of Rizal
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Civil_Merit" title="Order of Civil Merit">
        Encomienda (Commander) de la Orden del Merito Civil
       </a>
       (Order of Civil Merit) conferred by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Spain" title="Kingdom of Spain">
        Kingdom of Spain
       </a>
       for his contribution to the success of the state visit of President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo in December 2007.
      </p>
      <p>
       In June 2008, he was conferred the rank of Officier in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordre_des_Arts_et_Lettres" title="Ordre des Arts et Lettres">
        Ordre des Arts et Lettres
       </a>
       by the Republic of France for his contributions to the arts and letters as: writer, academic, cultural administrator, and for his support of cultural exchanges between the Philippines and France.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In recognition of his work in cultural administration and his contributions to Philippine history, the
       <a href="/wiki/Polytechnic_University_of_the_Philippines" title="Polytechnic University of the Philippines">
        Polytechnic University of the Philippines
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       conferred on him the degree of Doctor of Public Administration, honoris causa, in December 2008.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2010, he was conferred one of the highest civilian awards of the Philippines,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the
       <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Lakandula" title="Order of Lakandula">
        Order of Lakandula
       </a>
       , Rank of Bayani
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
       for his contributions in cultural administration, the popularization of Philippine history, and for having served as Chairman of the National Historical Institute from 2002 to 2010, and concurrently Chairman of the National Commission for Culture and the Arts 2005–2007, without compensation.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yannievalmero_29-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-yannievalmero-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In December 2013, during President
       <a href="/wiki/Benigno_Aquino_III" title="Benigno Aquino III">
        Benigno Aquino III
       </a>
       's visit to Tokyo, Ocampo received the
       <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Medal_of_Merit_(Philippines)" title="Presidential Medal of Merit (Philippines)">
        Presidential Medal of Merit
       </a>
       whose citation reads, "for his achievements as scholar, teacher, and in recognition of his writings through which he polarized Philippine history, art and culture thus bringing these aspects of our national identity closer to the people."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In September 2016, he was conferred the prestigious
       <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Prize" title="Fukuoka Prize">
        Fukuoka (Academic) Prize
       </a>
       . Ocampo is only the fifth Filipino to be so honoured, and part of the Award Citation reads: "As an outstanding historian and intellectual, Dr. Ambeth R. Ocampo has made a great contribution to academic, cultural and social progress in the Philippines, through his university teaching, his writing for newspapers and magazines, and his service in historic and cultural administration. His clear and accessible explanations of the wider global context in which the country developed during the period of the Spanish and American colonial regimes have helped promote a more open sense of nationalism, and facilitated the advancement of international exchanges both with Asia and with the West."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
        <a href="#cite_note-31">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
