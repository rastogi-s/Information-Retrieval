    Reintegrationism
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Reintegrationism
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
        Galician
       </a>
       and Portuguese:
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         reintegracionismo
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <small>
        Galician:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Galician" title="Help:IPA/Galician">
         [rejnteɣɾaθjoˈnizmo]
        </a>
       </span>
       or
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Galician" title="Help:IPA/Galician">
         [rejnteɣɾasjoˈnizmo]
        </a>
       </span>
       ,
       <small>
        European Portuguese:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">
         [ʁɛĩtɨɣɾɐsiuˈniʒmu]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is the
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">
        linguistic
       </a>
       and cultural movement in
       <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">
        Galicia
       </a>
       which advocates for the unity of
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
        Galician
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       as a single language. In other words, it postulates that Galician and Portuguese languages did not only share a
       <a href="/wiki/Galician-Portuguese" title="Galician-Portuguese">
        common origin
       </a>
       and literary tradition, but that they are in fact
       <a href="/wiki/Diasystem" title="Diasystem">
        variants
       </a>
       of the same language today. According to this, Galicia should re-integrate into the
       <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">
        Community of Portuguese Language Countries
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">
        Controversy
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Reintegrationism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Controversy">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are two main views in Galicia about the Galician language:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <i>
         isolationist
        </i>
        considers Galician and Portuguese to be two distinct languages, although they are closely related. Isolationists favour differentiated rules of writing and spelling between Galician and Portuguese. In this fashion, Galician spelling follows the model of
        <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
         Spanish
        </a>
        <a href="/wiki/Orthography" title="Orthography">
         orthography
        </a>
        and its own traditional conventions, which converge with it in some aspects. This view is held by the majority of public and Government organizations. Its standard norm, the "NOMIGa", is elaborated by the
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Galega" title="Real Academia Galega">
          Real Academia Galega
         </a>
        </i>
        (Royal Galician Academy) and the
        <i>
         <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Instituto_da_L%C3%ADngua_Galega&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Instituto da Língua Galega (page does not exist)">
          Instituto da Língua Galega
         </a>
        </i>
        (Institute for Galician Language).
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        Among other criteria, isolationism argues that since the majority of the Galician population was educated in Spanish only (as official use of Galician was forbidden for centuries, especially during
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spain_under_Franco" title="Spain under Franco">
         Franco's dictatorship
        </a>
        ), it is easier to create a Galician written norm which is closer to Spanish, therefore more "familiar", regardless of other considerations. It is also alleged that since the Spanish language had such a vast influence in modern Galician its contribution should not be simply disregarded. This influence is noticeable in some aspects of Galician
        <a href="/wiki/Phonetics" title="Phonetics">
         phonetics
        </a>
        and colloquial speech.
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <i>
         reintegrationist
        </i>
        view considers Galician to be a variant of a shared
        <a href="/wiki/Galician-Portuguese" title="Galician-Portuguese">
         Galician-Portuguese
        </a>
        linguistic
        <a href="/wiki/Diasystem" title="Diasystem">
         diasystem
        </a>
        , as evidenced by the common origin,
        <a href="/wiki/Grammar" title="Grammar">
         grammar
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Syntax" title="Syntax">
         syntax
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Vocabulary" title="Vocabulary">
         vocabulary
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(linguistics)" title="Morphology (linguistics)">
         morphology
        </a>
        and overall high level of
        <a href="/wiki/Mutual_intelligibility" title="Mutual intelligibility">
         mutual intelligibility
        </a>
        . Therefore, reintegrationists support the use of spelling rules similar to the ones used in Portuguese-speaking countries. This is supported by the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AGAL" title="AGAL">
         AGAL
        </a>
        (Galician Association of the Language),
        <a href="/wiki/Galician_Academy_of_the_Portuguese_Language" title="Galician Academy of the Portuguese Language">
         Galician Academy of the Portuguese Language
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Academia_Brasileira_de_Letras" title="Academia Brasileira de Letras">
         Brazilian Academy of Letters
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sciences_Academy_of_Lisbon" title="Sciences Academy of Lisbon">
         Sciences Academy of Lisbon
        </a>
        , and a number of civic and cultural associations both in Galicia and
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese-speaking_countries" title="Portuguese-speaking countries">
         Portuguese-speaking countries
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        In this fashion, it is argued that Galician would be faithful to its history and
        <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">
         etymology
        </a>
        and subsequently its written norm would be more scientific and precise. Thus, it would allow Galician speakers to have direct access to a world culture and it would also clarify some spelling problems of the isolationist norm (for example in terms of
        <a href="/wiki/Stress_(linguistics)" title="Stress (linguistics)">
         accentuation
        </a>
        ).
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
         <a href="#cite_note-2">
          [2]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        Reintegrationism is a cultural stand as much as a linguistic position. Its supporters believe that Galicia rightfully belongs to
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lusophony" title="Lusophony">
         Lusophony
        </a>
        since, in fact, modern Portuguese originated in what is today Galicia (see
        <a href="/wiki/History_of_Portuguese#Galician-Portuguese_period" title="History of Portuguese">
         History of Portuguese, Galician-Portuguese period
        </a>
        ). They aim for stronger cultural and economic ties with the Portuguese-speaking countries using the common language as a tool for direct communication.
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        From the Portuguese side, a number of relevant linguists and authors such as
        <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Lindley_Cintra" title="Luís Lindley Cintra">
         Luís Lindley Cintra
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Rodrigues_Lapa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Manuel Rodrigues Lapa (page does not exist)">
         Manuel Rodrigues Lapa
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Ven%C3%A2ncio" title="Fernando Venâncio">
         Fernando Venâncio
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Ant%C3%B3nio_Alves_dos_Reis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carlos António Alves dos Reis (page does not exist)">
         Carlos Reis
        </a>
        or
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jo%C3%A3o_Malaca_Casteleiro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="João Malaca Casteleiro (page does not exist)">
         Malaca Casteleiro
        </a>
        have expressed their agreement with the reintegrationist views.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
         <a href="#cite_note-3">
          [3]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Genesis_of_the_debate">
        Genesis of the debate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Reintegrationism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Genesis of the debate">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The divergences between isolationism and reintegrationism can be traced back to the time of the Galician
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Rexurdimento" title="Rexurdimento">
         Rexurdimento
        </a>
       </i>
       (Revival), in the nineteenth century, when Galician began to be systematically written again in Galicia for the first time since the Middle Ages. Up to that time, written Galician was either forbidden or simply dismissed by the Spanish authorities, and certainly not used officially. Hence,
       <i>
        Rexurdimento
       </i>
       Galician writers realized they did not "know" how Galician should be spelled properly. There were three possible options: to infer it from the Medieval forms; to use a Spanish-based spelling, which was already known to all; or to use the Portuguese spelling, feeling that written Portuguese was "what Galician would have been if it had not been forbidden". Authors such as
       <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Daniel_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Castelao" title="Alfonso Daniel Rodríguez Castelao">
        Castelao
       </a>
       , among others, stated that Galician should gradually merge with Portuguese, namely in its written form.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The reality was that until the 1980s Galician was often written using a mix of the three options.
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        The Lusitanian and Galician languages are the same.
       </p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        —
        <cite>
         <a href="/wiki/Benito_Jer%C3%B3nimo_Feij%C3%B3o_y_Montenegro" title="Benito Jerónimo Feijóo y Montenegro">
          Padre Feijóo
         </a>
         ,
         <i>
          Theatro Critico Universal
         </i>
         , 1726
        </cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        There are two opinions on the orthographic norms of our native language: the phonetic ... influenced by the domination of Spanish, and another one ... where etymology is its main and most logical attribute ... because (Portuguese orthography) is the natural orthography of the Galician language, and I cannot understand how there still are not only doubts about this, but even opinions against it ... with no scientific basis.
       </p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        —
        <cite>
         Roberto Blanco Torres,
         <i>
          La unificación ortográfica del idioma gallego
         </i>
         , 1930
        </cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        There is a reason why our language is the same as in Portugal ... Our languages must become the same one again.
       </p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        —
        <cite>
         <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Otero_Pedrayo" title="Ramón Otero Pedrayo">
          Otero Pedrayo
         </a>
         ,
         <i>
          Discursos Parlamentarios
         </i>
         , 1933
        </cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        Galician is a wide-spread and useful language which—with small variations—is spoken in Brazil, Portugal and in the Portuguese colonies.
       </p>
       <div class="templatequotecite">
        —
        <cite>
         <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Daniel_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Castelao" title="Alfonso Daniel Rodríguez Castelao">
          Castelao
         </a>
         ,
         <i>
          Sempre en Galiza
         </i>
         , 1944
        </cite>
       </div>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Yet, with the end of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spain_under_Franco" title="Spain under Franco">
        Franco dictatorship
       </a>
       in 1975, and with the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_transition_to_democracy" title="Spanish transition to democracy">
        Spanish transition to democracy
       </a>
       , Galicia became an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_community" title="Autonomous community">
        autonomous community
       </a>
       with the Statute of Autonomy of 1981, with Galician as its official language (alongside Spanish). Establishing a fixed standard form then became urgent. Claims for Galician-Portuguese linguistic unity had already been produced, as evidenced with the
       <i>
        Manifesto para a supervivência da cultura galega
       </i>
       (Manifesto for the Survival of Galician Culture), first published in 1974.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Still, the first draft of the language norms was produced in 1979 under the guidance of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linguist" title="Linguist">
        linguist
       </a>
       Professor
       <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Carballo_Calero" title="Ricardo Carballo Calero">
        Ricardo Carballo Calero
       </a>
       . These norms recommended a gradual approach to Portuguese, often allowing for a number of different solutions in the case of uncertainty.
      </p>
      <p>
       However, political issues forced the resignation of Carvalho Calero and, consequently, the 1979 pro-reintegrationist norms were revoked. The new official norms and reforms passed from 1982 onwards would be strongly pro-isolationist.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Practicalities">
        Practicalities
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Reintegrationism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practicalities">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Reintegrationism accepts two possibilities for writing Galician: either adopting the standard Portuguese written norm or using a slightly modified norm following the recommendations of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AGAL" title="AGAL">
        AGAL
       </a>
       . In any case, reintegrationism considers that spoken Galician and all of its characteristic words, expressions, and pronunciation should not be radically substituted by standard Portuguese. The main recommendations of reintegrationism when referring to spoken Galician revolve around the avoidance of unnecessary Spanish
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loanwords" title="Loanwords">
        loanwords
       </a>
       , namely
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colloquialisms" title="Colloquialisms">
        colloquialisms
       </a>
       . In writing, the most obvious differences from the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Normas_Ortogr%C3%A1ficas_e_Morfol%C3%B3xicas_do_Idioma_Galego&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Normas Ortográficas e Morfolóxicas do Idioma Galego (page does not exist)">
        official norm (NOMIGa)
       </a>
       are (according to AGAL):
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Use of
        <i>
         nh
        </i>
        instead of the letter
        <i>
         ñ
        </i>
        to represent the
        <a href="/wiki/Palatal_nasal" title="Palatal nasal">
         palatal nasal
        </a>
        sound. For example:
        <i>
         cami
         <b>
          nh
         </b>
         o
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         cami
         <b>
          ñ
         </b>
         o
        </i>
        (way). Use of
        <i>
         mh
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         nh
        </i>
        to represent the
        <a href="/wiki/Velar_nasal" title="Velar nasal">
         velar nasal
        </a>
        sound. For example:
        <i>
         algu
         <b>
          mh
         </b>
         a
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         algu
         <b>
          nh
         </b>
         a
        </i>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Use of the digraph
        <i>
         lh
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         ll
        </i>
        to represent the
        <a href="/wiki/Palatal_lateral_approximant" title="Palatal lateral approximant">
         palatal lateral
        </a>
        sound. For example:
        <i>
         coe
         <b>
          lh
         </b>
         o
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         coe
         <b>
          ll
         </b>
         o
        </i>
        (rabbit)
       </li>
       <li>
        Use of
        <i>
         -çom
        </i>
        /
        <i>
         -ção
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         -ções
        </i>
        instead of the suffix
        <i>
         -ción
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         -cións
        </i>
        . For example:
        <i>
         associa
         <b>
          çom
         </b>
        </i>
        /
        <i>
         associa
         <b>
          ção
         </b>
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         asocia
         <b>
          ción
         </b>
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         associa
         <b>
          ções
         </b>
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         asocia
         <b>
          cións
         </b>
        </i>
        (association, associations)
       </li>
       <li>
        Preference for the use of suffixes
        <i>
         -aria
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         -vel
        </i>
        over
        <i>
         -ería
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         -ble
        </i>
        or even
        <i>
         -bel
        </i>
        . For example:
        <i>
         livr
         <b>
          aria
         </b>
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         libr
         <b>
          ería
         </b>
        </i>
        (bookshop);
        <i>
         incrí
         <b>
          vel
         </b>
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         incri
         <b>
          ble
         </b>
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         incrí
         <b>
          bel
         </b>
        </i>
        (incredible)
       </li>
       <li>
        Use of
        <i>
         ss
        </i>
        between vowels, when appropriate, instead of the simplified
        <i>
         s
        </i>
        for all cases. For example:
        <i>
         a
         <b>
          ss
         </b>
         ociação
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         a
         <b>
          s
         </b>
         ociación
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        Use of either
        <i>
         x
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         j
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         g
        </i>
        preceding
        <i>
         e
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         i
        </i>
        , according to the etymology of the word, instead of
        <i>
         x
        </i>
        for all cases. For example:
        <i>
         ho
         <b>
          j
         </b>
         e
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         ho
         <b>
          x
         </b>
         e
        </i>
        (today),
        <i>
         <b>
          g
         </b>
         eral
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         <b>
          x
         </b>
         eral
        </i>
        (general), but
        <i>
         e
         <b>
          x
         </b>
         ército
        </i>
        as in
        <i>
         e
         <b>
          x
         </b>
         ército
        </i>
        (army)
       </li>
       <li>
        Use of
        <i>
         m
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         n
        </i>
        at the end of a word. For example:
        <i>
         so
         <b>
          m
         </b>
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         so
         <b>
          n
         </b>
        </i>
        (sound)
       </li>
       <li>
        Use of a wider range of accentuation signs instead of the simplified single stroke. For example:
        <i>
         portugu
         <b>
          ê
         </b>
         s
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         portugu
         <b>
          é
         </b>
         s
        </i>
        (Portuguese),
        <i>
         coment
         <b>
          á
         </b>
         rio
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         coment
         <b>
          a
         </b>
         rio
        </i>
        (commentary). Note that the official orthography, being a calque of the Spanish one in that respect, does not cater for any difference between open and closed vowels, since Spanish does not have them.
       </li>
       <li>
        Avoidance of specific lexical choices introduced by
        <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
         Spanish
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Even though the reintegrationist norm does not have official status, it has been recognized in courts of law.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2007)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Furthermore, Galician members of the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">
        European Parliament
       </a>
       (such as
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Posada" title="José Posada">
        José Posada
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camilo_Nogueira" title="Camilo Nogueira">
        Camilo Nogueira
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Xos%C3%A9_Manuel_Beiras" title="Xosé Manuel Beiras">
        Xosé Manuel Beiras
       </a>
       ) have used spoken Galician when addressing the chamber and have used standard Portuguese orthography to encode their Galician speech. In all cases, these interventions and encodings have been accepted by the Parliament as a valid form of Portuguese, that is, an official language of the
       <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
        European Union
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Furthermore, members of Galician reintegrationist associations have been regularly present at meetings of the Community of Portuguese-Speaking Countries. In 2008, Galician delegates were invited as speakers to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_Parliament" title="Portuguese Parliament">
        Portuguese Parliament
       </a>
       when discussing the new
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spelling_reforms_of_Portuguese" title="Spelling reforms of Portuguese">
        spelling norms
       </a>
       for Portuguese language.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The local government of
       <a href="/wiki/Corcubi%C3%B3n" title="Corcubión">
        Corcubión
       </a>
       (a Galician
       <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">
        municipality
       </a>
       ) was the first public institution to officially use the AGAL norm, as seen on
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.corcubion.gal/pt" rel="nofollow">
        its website
       </a>
       , offering options for "Galician" (NOMIGa) and "Galician-Portuguese" (AGAL) – plus English and Spanish.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Common_positions">
        Common positions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Reintegrationism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Common positions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Theoretically, the positions of the standards of
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Normas_Ortogr%C3%A1ficas_e_Morfol%C3%B3xicas_do_Idioma_Galego&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Normas Ortográficas e Morfolóxicas do Idioma Galego (page does not exist)">
        NOMIGa
       </a>
       (official norm) and AGAL are not so different. Although usually accused of having pro-Spanish tendencies, it is stated at the introduction of the NOMIGa that "standard choices must be in harmony with those of other languages, especially to those of Romance languages and especially to those of Portuguese". Furthermore, they value "the contribution of
       <a href="/wiki/European_Portuguese" title="European Portuguese">
        Peninsular
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Portuguese" title="Brazilian Portuguese">
        Brazilian Portuguese
       </a>
       " in the creation of the Galician norm.
      </p>
      <p>
       This being the philosophy behind the "official standard", NOMIGa and AGAL share an initial starting point, but it is often argued that the NOMIGa are far removed from the usual speech of day-to-day and older Galician speakers, in addition to "isolating" Galician (hence the term
       <i>
        isolationist
       </i>
       ) from the rest of Portuguese-speaking areas by using a different writing system. In any case, European and Brazilian Portuguese are usually analyzed by both isolationists and reintegrationists as a primary source from which to extract scientific and technical
       <a href="/wiki/Terminology" title="Terminology">
        terminology
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neologisms" title="Neologisms">
        neologisms
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Political_implications">
        Political implications
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Reintegrationism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Political implications">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       As with many other aspects of Galician society and culture, language is deeply politicized in Galicia. Traditionally, the defence and promotion of Galician language has been linked to
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_nationalism" title="Galician nationalism">
        Galician nationalism
       </a>
       , yet this is often considered a simplification. Likewise, different political groups and parties have adopted different approaches to the "isolationism vs reintegrationism" polemic.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Broadly speaking, pro-independence groups have traditionally expressed a greater support for the reintegrationist norm, while others have adopted the isolationist. In any case, all the linguistic organizations behind both reintegrationism and isolationism have attempted to dissociate themselves from the political debate. For example, AGAL members have often expressed that this is merely a linguistic, hence scientific, discussion, and that it should not become the arena for political fights among the community of Galician speakers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
