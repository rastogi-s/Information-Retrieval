      <p>
       <b>
        Lorrain
       </b>
       is a dialect (often referred to as
       <a href="/wiki/Patois" title="Patois">
        patois
       </a>
       ) spoken by a minority of people in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lorraine_(region)" title="Lorraine (region)">
        Lorraine
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       , small parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Alsace" title="Alsace">
        Alsace
       </a>
       and in
       <a href="/wiki/Gaume" title="Gaume">
        Gaume
       </a>
       in Belgium. It is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Langue_d%27o%C3%AFl" title="Langue d'oïl">
        langue d'oïl
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       It is classified as a
       <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_France" title="Languages of France">
        regional language of France
       </a>
       and has the recognised status of a regional language of
       <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">
        Wallonia
       </a>
       , where it is known as
       <b>
        Gaumais
       </b>
       . It has been influenced by
       <a href="/wiki/Lorraine_Franconian" title="Lorraine Franconian">
        Lorraine Franconian
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxembourgish_language" title="Luxembourgish language">
        Luxembourgish
       </a>
       , Germanic languages spoken in nearby or overlapping areas.
      </p>
