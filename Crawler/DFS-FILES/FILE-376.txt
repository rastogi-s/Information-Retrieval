    List of Spanish words of Indigenous American Indian origin
   </h1>

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       This is a list of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       words that come from
       <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_languages_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous languages of the Americas">
        Indigenous languages of the Americas
       </a>
       . It is further divided into words that come from
       <a href="/wiki/Arawakan_languages" title="Arawakan languages">
        Arawakan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Aymara_language" title="Aymara language">
        Aymara
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carib_languages" title="Carib languages">
        Carib
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mayan_languages" title="Mayan languages">
        Mayan
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nahuatl_language" title="Nahuatl language">
        Nahuatl
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quechua_language" title="Quechua language">
        Quechua
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_language" title="Taíno language">
        Taíno
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tarahumara" title="Tarahumara">
        Tarahumara
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tupi_languages" title="Tupi languages">
        Tupi
       </a>
       , and uncertain (the word is known to be from the Americas, but the exact source language is unclear). Some of these words have alternate
       <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">
        etymologies
       </a>
       and may also appear on a list of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       words from a different
       <a href="/wiki/Language" title="Language">
        language
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Quechua">
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quechua_language" title="Quechua language">
         Quechua
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Spanish_words_of_Indigenous_American_Indian_origin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Quechua">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Quechuan /ˈkɛtʃwən/, also known as runa simi ("people's language"), is a Native South American language family spoken primarily in the Andes, derived from a common ancestral language. It is the most widely spoken language family of the indigenous peoples of the Americas, with a total of probably some 8 million to 10 million speakers
      </p>
