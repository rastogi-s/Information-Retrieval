      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Ilustrados
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_Philippines" title="Spanish language in the Philippines">
        Spanish
       </a>
       for "erudite,"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       "learned,"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       or "enlightened ones"
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LOC_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-LOC-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ) constituted the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">
        Filipino
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Education_in_the_Philippines" title="Education in the Philippines">
        educated
       </a>
       class during the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Philippines" title="Spanish Philippines">
        Spanish colonial period
       </a>
       in the late 19th century.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thomas2012_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Thomas2012-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cullinane1989_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Cullinane1989-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Elsewhere in
       <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">
        New Spain
       </a>
       (of which the Philippines were part), the term
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Gente_de_raz%C3%B3n" title="Gente de razón">
         gente de razón
        </a>
       </i>
       carried a similar meaning.
      </p>
      <p>
       They were the
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_class" title="Middle class">
        middle class
       </a>
       who were educated in
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_Philippines" title="Spanish language in the Philippines">
        Spanish
       </a>
       and exposed to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_liberalism" title="Spanish liberalism">
        Spanish liberal
       </a>
       and European nationalist ideals. The
       <i>
        Ilustrado
       </i>
       class was composed of native-born intellectuals and cut across ethnolinguistic and racial lines—
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">
         Indios
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Criollo_(people)" title="Criollo (people)">
         Insulares
        </a>
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Filipino_mestizo" title="Filipino mestizo">
         mestizos
        </a>
       </i>
       , among others—and sought
       <a href="/wiki/Reform" title="Reform">
        reform
       </a>
       through “a more equitable arrangement of both political and economic power” under Spanish tutelage.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Karnow" title="Stanley Karnow">
        Stanley Karnow
       </a>
       , in his
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/In_Our_Image:_America%27s_Empire_in_the_Philippines" title="In Our Image: America's Empire in the Philippines">
         In Our Image: America's Empire in the Philippines
        </a>
       </i>
       , referred to the
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       as the “rich
       <a href="/wiki/Intelligentsia" title="Intelligentsia">
        Intelligentsia
       </a>
       ” because many were the children of wealthy landowners. They were key figures in the development of
       <a href="/wiki/Filipino_nationalism" title="Filipino nationalism">
        Filipino nationalism
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LOC_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-LOC-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Karnow_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Karnow-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mega_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Mega-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Britannica_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Britannica-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Embassy_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Embassy-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilustrado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="670" height="269" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg/200px-Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg/300px-Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg/400px-Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Filipino_Ilustrados_Jose_Rizal_Marcelo_del_Pilar_Mariano_Ponce.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Three prominent
         <i>
          Ilustrados
         </i>
         in Spain: José Rizal, Marcelo H. del Pilar and Mariano Ponce
         <i>
          (from left to right)
         </i>
         . Photo was taken in Spain in 1890.
        </div>
       </div>

      <p>
       The most prominent
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       were
       <a href="/wiki/Graciano_L%C3%B3pez_Jaena" title="Graciano López Jaena">
        Graciano López Jaena
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_H._del_Pilar" title="Marcelo H. del Pilar">
        Marcelo H. del Pilar
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mariano_Ponce" title="Mariano Ponce">
        Mariano Ponce
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Luna" title="Antonio Luna">
        Antonio Luna
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Rizal" title="José Rizal">
        José Rizal
       </a>
       , the Philippine
       <a href="/wiki/Folk_hero" title="Folk hero">
        national hero
       </a>
       . Rizal’s novels
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noli_Me_Tangere_(novel)" title="Noli Me Tangere (novel)">
         Noli Me Tangere
        </a>
       </i>
       ("Touch Me Not") and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/El_filibusterismo" title="El filibusterismo">
         El filibusterismo
        </a>
       </i>
       ("The Subversive") “exposed to the world the injustices imposed on Filipinos under the Spanish colonial regime”.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Britannica_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Britannica-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SSPXAsia_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-SSPXAsia-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the beginning, Rizal and his fellow
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       preferred not to win
       <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Philippines)" title="Independence Day (Philippines)">
        independence
       </a>
       from
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       , instead they yearned legal equality for both
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peninsulares" title="Peninsulares">
         Peninsulares
        </a>
       </i>
       and natives—
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">
         Indios
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Criollo_(people)" title="Criollo (people)">
         Insulares
        </a>
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Filipino_mestizo" title="Filipino mestizo">
         mestizos
        </a>
       </i>
       , among others—in the economic reforms demanded by the
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       were that “the Philippines be represented in the
       <a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">
        Cortes
       </a>
       and be considered as a
       <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">
        province
       </a>
       of Spain” and “the
       <a href="/wiki/Secular_clergy" title="Secular clergy">
        secularization of the parishes
       </a>
       .”
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Embassy_10-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Embassy-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SSPXAsia_11-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-SSPXAsia-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       However, in 1872, nationalist sentiment grew strongest, when three Filipino
       <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">
        priests
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Burgos" title="José Burgos">
        José Burgos
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mariano_G%C3%B3mez" title="Mariano Gómez">
        Mariano Gómez
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">
        friar
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Jacinto_Zamora" title="Jacinto Zamora">
        Jacinto Zamora
       </a>
       , who had been charged with leading a military
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cavite_Mutiny" title="Cavite Mutiny">
        mutiny
       </a>
       at an
       <a href="/wiki/Arsenal" title="Arsenal">
        arsenal
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Cavite" title="Cavite">
        Cavite
       </a>
       , near
       <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">
        Manila
       </a>
       , were executed by the Spanish authorities. The event and “other repressive acts outraitings and activities, Rizal was executed on December 30, 1896. His execution propelled the
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       . This also prompted unity among the
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Bonifacio" title="Andrés Bonifacio">
        Andrés Bonifacio
       </a>
       ’s radical
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Katipunan" title="Katipunan">
         Katipunan
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Embassy_10-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Embassy-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Philippine policies by the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       reinforced the dominant position of the
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       within Filipino society.
       <a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">
        Friar
       </a>
       estates were sold to the
       <i>
        Ilustrados
       </i>
       and most government positions were offered to them.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Embassy_10-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Embassy-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_personalities">
        Notable personalities
