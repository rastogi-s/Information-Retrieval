    Catalan dialects
   </h1>

      <p>
       The dialects of the
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan language
       </a>
       feature a relative uniformity, especially when compared to other Romance languages;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMoll195847_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMoll195847-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       both in terms of
       <a href="/wiki/Vocabulary" title="Vocabulary">
        vocabulary
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Semantics" title="Semantics">
        semantics
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Syntax" title="Syntax">
        syntax
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">
        morphology
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Phonology" title="Phonology">
        phonology
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana634–635_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana634–635-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Mutual intelligibility between its dialects is very high,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler20051_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler20051-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECosta_CarrerasYates20095_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECosta_CarrerasYates20095-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       estimates ranging from 90% to 95%.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ethnologue_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ethnologue-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The only exception is the isolated idiosyncratic Alguerese dialect.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMoll195847_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMoll195847-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
        Overview
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catalan_dialects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/1861" title="1861">
        1861
       </a>
       , linguist
       <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Mil%C3%A0_i_Fontanals" title="Manuel Milà i Fontanals">
        Manuel Milà i Fontanals
       </a>
       split Catalan into two main dialects: Western and Eastern.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana634–635_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana634–635-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The most obvious phonetic difference lies in the treatment of unstressed a and e, which have merged to /ə/ in Eastern dialects, but remain distinct as /a/ and /e/ in Western dialects.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMoll195847_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMoll195847-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105_8-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There are a few other differences in pronunciation, verbal morphology, and vocabulary.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler20051_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler20051-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Western Catalan comprises the two dialects of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northwestern_Catalan" title="Northwestern Catalan">
        Northwestern Catalan
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencian_language" title="Valencian language">
        Valencian
       </a>
       ; the Eastern block comprises four dialects:
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Catalan" title="Central Catalan">
        Central Catalan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Balearic_dialect" title="Balearic dialect">
        Balearic
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Catalan" title="Northern Catalan">
        Rossellonese
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Algherese_dialect" title="Algherese dialect">
        Alguerese
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105_8-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Each dialect can be further subdivided in several subdialects.
      </p>
      <p>
       There are two spoken standards for the language based on the Eastern and Western dialects respectively:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">
         Catalonia
        </a>
        , the
        <a href="/wiki/Institut_d%27Estudis_Catalans" title="Institut d'Estudis Catalans">
         Institut d'Estudis Catalans (IEC)
        </a>
        regulates the spoken standard based on
        <a href="/wiki/Central_Catalan" title="Central Catalan">
         Central Catalan
        </a>
        , which has the highest number of speakers
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105_8-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105-8">
          [8]
         </a>
        </sup>
        and is spoken in the densely populated regions of the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_Province_(Spain)" title="Barcelona Province (Spain)">
         Barcelona province
        </a>
        , the eastern half of the province of Tarragona, and most of the province of Girona.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105_8-5">
         <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeldhausen20105-8">
          [8]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        In the
        <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">
         Valencian Country
        </a>
        , the
        <a href="/wiki/Acad%C3%A8mia_Valenciana_de_la_Llengua" title="Acadèmia Valenciana de la Llengua">
         Acadèmia Valenciana de la Llengua
        </a>
        adapts the
        <a href="/wiki/Pompeu_Fabra" title="Pompeu Fabra">
         Fabran guidelines
        </a>
        to the Valencian variety, and regulates an alternative spoken standard based on the Southern Valencian subdialect. Despite having fewer speakers than the Central Valencian subdialect,
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a_10-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-a-10">
          [10]
         </a>
        </sup>
        Southern Valencian has been less influenced by
        <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
         Spanish
        </a>
        . It is spoken in the South and North of the
        <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Valencia" title="Province of Valencia">
         València
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Alicante" title="Province of Alicante">
         Alacant
        </a>
        provinces respectively, in cities such as
        <a href="/wiki/Gandia" title="Gandia">
         Gandia
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alcoi" title="Alcoi">
         Alcoi
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/X%C3%A0tiva" title="Xàtiva">
         Xàtiva
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Valencians are only surpassed in number of Catalan-speakers by Catalans themselves, representing approximately a third of the whole Catalan-speaking population.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a_10-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-a-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Therefore, in the context of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencian_language_controversy" title="Valencian language controversy">
        linguistic conflict
       </a>
       , recognition and respect towards the dual standard, as well as the dual Catalan–Valencian denomination,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       pacifies the tense central–periphery relations between Catalonia and the Valencian country.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Pronunciation">
        Pronunciation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catalan_dialects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pronunciation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vowels">
        Vowels
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catalan_dialects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Vowels">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Catalan has inherited the typical vowel system of
       <a href="/wiki/Vulgar_Latin" title="Vulgar Latin">
        Vulgar Latin
       </a>
       , with seven stressed phonemes:
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a ɛ e i ɔ o u/
       </span>
       , a common feature in
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Romance_languages" title="Western Romance languages">
        Western Romance
       </a>
       , except
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">
        Asturian
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">
        Aragonese
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana630_13-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana630-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Balearic_dialect" title="Balearic dialect">
        Balearic
       </a>
       has also instances of stressed /ə/.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200537,53-54_14-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200537,53-54-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Dialects differ in the different degrees of
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel_reduction" title="Vowel reduction">
        vowel reduction
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200537_15-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200537-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and the incidence of the pair /ɛ e/.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538_16-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Catalan" title="Eastern Catalan">
        Eastern Catalan
       </a>
       (except Majorcan), unstressed vowels reduce to three:
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a e ɛ/ → [ə]
       </span>
       ;
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o ɔ u/ → [u]
       </span>
       ;
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /i/
       </span>
       remains distinct.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200554_17-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200554-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       There are a few instances of unreduced [e], [o] in some words.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200554_17-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200554-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alguerese" title="Alguerese">
        Alguerese
       </a>
       has lowered [ə] to [a].
      </p>
      <p>
       In Majorcan, unstressed vowels reduce to four:
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a e ɛ/
       </span>
       follow the Eastern Catalan reduction pattern; however
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o ɔ/
       </span>
       reduce to
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [o]
       </span>
       , with
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /u/
       </span>
       remaining distinct, as in Western Catalan.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553-54_18-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553-54-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Catalan" title="Western Catalan">
        Western Catalan
       </a>
       , unstressed vowels reduce to five:
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /e ɛ/ → [e]
       </span>
       ;
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o ɔ/ → [o]
       </span>
       ;
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a u i/
       </span>
       remain distinct.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553_19-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This reduction pattern, inherited from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proto-Romance" title="Proto-Romance">
        Proto-Romance
       </a>
       , is also found in
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553_19-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Some Western dialects present further reduction or vowel harmony in some cases.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553_19-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200553-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERecasens199675–76,128–129_21-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERecasens199675–76,128–129-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Central, Western, and Balearic differ in the lexical incidence of stressed /e/ and /ɛ/.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538_16-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Usually, words with /ɛ/ in central Catalan correspond to /ə/ in Balearic and /e/ in Western Catalan.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538_16-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Words with /e/ in Balearic almost always have /e/ in central and western Catalan as well.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538_16-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As a result, Western Catalan has a much higher incidence of /e/.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538_16-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWheeler200538-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">
        Morphology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catalan_dialects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Morphology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In verbs, the 1st person present indicative ending is -
       <i>
        e
       </i>
       (∅ in verbs of the 2nd and 3rd conjugation), or -
       <i>
        o
       </i>
       .
       <br/>
       E.g.,
       <i>
        parle
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        tem
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        sent
       </i>
       (Valencian);
       <i>
        parlo
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        temo
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        sento
       </i>
       (North-Western). In verbs, the 1st person present indicative ending is -
       <i>
        o
       </i>
       , -
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       or ∅ in all conjugations.
       <br/>
       E.g.,
       <i>
        parlo
       </i>
       (Central),
       <i>
        parl
       </i>
       (Balearic),
       <i>
        parli
       </i>
       (Northern), ('I speak').
      </p>

      <p>
       In verbs, the inchoative desinences are -
       <i>
        isc
       </i>
       /-
       <i>
        ixo
       </i>
       , -
       <i>
        ix
       </i>
       , -
       <i>
        ixen
       </i>
       , -
       <i>
        isca
       </i>
       . In verbs, the inchoative desinences are -
       <i>
        eixo
       </i>
       , -
       <i>
        eix
       </i>
       , -
       <i>
        eixen
       </i>
       , -
       <i>
        eixi
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In nouns and adjectives, maintenance of
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /n/
       </span>
       of medieval plurals in proparoxytone words.
       <br/>
       E.g.,
       <i>
        hòmens
       </i>
       'men',
       <i>
        jóvens
       </i>
       'youth'. In nouns and adjectives, loss of
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /n/
       </span>
       of medieval plurals in proparoxytone words.
       <br/>
       E.g.,
       <i>
        homes
       </i>
       'men',
       <i>
        joves
       </i>
       'youth'.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vocabulary">
        Vocabulary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catalan_dialects&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vocabulary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Despite its relative lexical unity, the two dialectal blocks of Catalan (Eastern and Western) show some differences in word choices.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana632_24-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana632-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Any lexical divergence within any of the two groups can be explained as an archaism. Also, usually
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Catalan" title="Central Catalan">
        Central Catalan
       </a>
       acts as an innovative element.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana632_24-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEnciclopèdia_Catalana632-24">
