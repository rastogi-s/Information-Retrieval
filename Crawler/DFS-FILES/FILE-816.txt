      <p>
       <b>
        Palenquero
       </b>
       (also
       <i>
        <b>
         palenque
        </b>
       </i>
       ) is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish-based_creole_language" title="Spanish-based creole language">
        Spanish-based creole language
       </a>
       spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">
        Colombia
       </a>
       . Palenquero is the only Spanish-based
       <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">
        creole
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">
        Latin America
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The ethnic group which speaks this creole consisted of only 3,000 people in 1998. Palenquero is spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">
        Colombia
       </a>
       , in the village of
       <a href="/wiki/San_Basilio_de_Palenque" title="San Basilio de Palenque">
        San Basilio de Palenque
       </a>
       which is southeast of
       <a href="/wiki/Cartagena,_Colombia" title="Cartagena, Colombia">
        Cartagena
       </a>
       , and in some neighbourhoods of
       <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">
        Barranquilla
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [

        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:033_Palenquero_Cartagena_Colombia_2013.JPG">

        </a>

         A Palenquero woman selling fruit in the Plaza de San Pedro Claver,
         <a href="/wiki/Cartagena,_Colombia" title="Cartagena, Colombia">

      <p>
       The village was formed by escaped slaves (
       <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">
        Maroons
       </a>
       ) and sometimes
       <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">
        Native Americans
       </a>
       around 1604 under the leadership of Benkos Biojo. Since many slaves had not been subjected to a lot of contact with people of European descent, the
       <i>
        palenqueros
       </i>
       spoke Creole languages constructed from the Spanish language and their own African ones.
      </p>
      <p>
       Spanish speakers are usually unable to understand Palenquero. Ten percent of the population under 25 years of age spoke Palenquero in 1998. It is more commonly spoken by the elderly. There are some influences from
       <a href="/wiki/Kongo_language" title="Kongo language">
        Kongo
       </a>
       , which is spoken in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of Congo">
        Democratic Republic of Congo
       </a>
       . Palenquero words like "
       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ngombe#Palanquero" title="wikt:ngombe">
        ngombe
       </a>
       ", which means cattle, are found in several
       <a href="/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">
        Bantu languages
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sample">
        Sample
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Palenquero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sample">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Lord%27s_Prayer" title="Lord's Prayer">
        Lord's Prayer
       </a>
       in Palenque:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <table class="wikitable">
         <tr>
          <th>
           Palenquero
          </th>
          <th>
           Spanish
          </th>
         </tr>
         <tr>
          <td>
           Tatá sutu lo ke ta riba sielo,
           <br/>
           santifikaro sendá nombre si,
           <br/>
           miní a reino sí,
           <br/>
           <p>
            asé ño boluntá sí,
            <br/>
            aí tiela kumo a sielo.
            <br/>
            Nda suto agué pan ri to ma ría,
            <br/>
            peddona ma fata suto,
            <br/>
            asina kumo suto a se peddoná,
            <br/>
            lo ke se fatá suto.
            <br/>
            Nu rejá sujo kaí andí tentación nu,
            <br/>
            librá suto ri má. Amén.
           </p>
          </td>
          <td>
           Padre nuestro que estás en el cielo,
           <br/>
           santificado sea tu nombre.
           <br/>
           Venga a nosotros tu Reino.
           <br/>
           <p>
            Hágase tu voluntad,
            <br/>
            así en la tierra como en el cielo.
            <br/>
            Danos hoy nuestro pan de cada día.
            <br/>
            perdona nuestras ofensas,
            <br/>
            como también nosotros perdonamos
            <br/>
            a los que nos ofenden.
            <br/>
            no nos dejes caer en la tentación,
            <br/>
            y líbranos del mal. Amén.
           </p>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </table>
       </dd>
      </dl>
