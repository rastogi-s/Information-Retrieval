      <p>
       <b>
        Limousin
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="oc" xml:lang="oc">
         Lemosin
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a dialect of the
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan language
       </a>
       , spoken in the three
       <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">
        departments
       </a>
       of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Limousin_(region)" title="Limousin (region)">
        Limousin
       </a>
       , parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Charente" title="Charente">
        Charente
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Dordogne" title="Dordogne">
        Dordogne
       </a>
       in the southwest of
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The first Occitan documents are in an early form of this dialect, particularly the
       <a href="/wiki/Boecis" title="Boecis">
        Boecis
       </a>
       , written around the year 1000.
      </p>
      <p>
       Limousin is used primarily by people over age 50 in rural communities. All speakers speak
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       as a first or second language. Due to the
       <a href="/wiki/Language_policy_in_France" title="Language policy in France">
        French single language policy
       </a>
       , it is not recognised by the government and might be disappearing. A
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Language_revival" title="Language revival">
        revivalist
       </a>
       movement around the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Felibrige" title="Felibrige">
        Felibrige
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_d%27Estudis_Occitans&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Institut d'Estudis Occitans (page does not exist)">
        Institut d'Estudis Occitans
       </a>
       is active in Limousin (as well as in other parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">
        Occitania
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_from_Languedocien">
        Differences from Languedocien
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Limousin_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Differences from Languedocien">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Most speakers and academics consider Limousin to be a dialect of Occitan. For more detailed information on this question, see the section on
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occitan#Dialects" title="Occitan">
        Occitan dialects
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occitan#Codification" title="Occitan">
        codification
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The following reproduces the first article of the
       <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">
        Universal Declaration of Human Rights
       </a>
       : "All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. They are endowed with reason and conscience and should act towards one another in a spirit of brotherhood."
      </p>
