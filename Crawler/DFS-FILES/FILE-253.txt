      <p>
       The
       <b>
        40th parallel north
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">
        circle of latitude
       </a>
       that is 40
       <a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">
        degrees
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/True_north" title="True north">
        north
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth's
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equatorial plane
       </a>
       . It crosses
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">
        Mediterranean Sea
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude the sun is visible for 15 hours, 1 minute during the
       <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">
        summer solstice
       </a>
       and 9 hours, 20 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">
        winter solstice
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       On 21 June, the maximum altitude of the sun is 73.83 degrees and 26.17 degrees on 21 December.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">
        United States
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=40th_parallel_north&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United States">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:40th_parallel_US.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="593" data-file-width="959" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/40th_parallel_US.svg/220px-40th_parallel_US.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/40th_parallel_US.svg/330px-40th_parallel_US.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/40th_parallel_US.svg/440px-40th_parallel_US.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:40th_parallel_US.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The 40th parallel defines the border between Nebraska and Kansas.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2153" data-file-width="1650" height="287" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg/220px-Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg/330px-Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg/440px-Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Survey_monument_RH00-062_from_SW_3.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Survey marker on the Kansas/Nebraska state line
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The parallel 40° north forms the boundary between the states of
       <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">
        Kansas
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">
        Nebraska
       </a>
       . On 30 May 1854, the
       <a href="/wiki/Kansas%E2%80%93Nebraska_Act" title="Kansas–Nebraska Act">
        Kansas–Nebraska Act
       </a>
       created the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Territory_of_Kansas" title="Territory of Kansas">
        Territory of Kansas
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Territory_of_Nebraska" title="Territory of Nebraska">
        Territory of Nebraska
       </a>
       divided by the parallel 40° north. Both territories were required to determine for themselves whether to permit
       <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">
        slavery
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Bleeding_Kansas" title="Bleeding Kansas">
        Open conflict
       </a>
       between
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave_and_free_states" title="Slave and free states">
        free-state
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave_state" title="Slave state">
        pro-slavery
       </a>
       forces in the Kansas Territory was one of the root causes of the
       <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
        American Civil War
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The parallel 40° north formed the original northern boundary of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_monarchy" title="British monarchy">
        British
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Maryland#History" title="Maryland">
        Colony of Maryland
       </a>
       . A subsequent
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_monarchy" title="British monarchy">
        royal
       </a>
       grant gave the
       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania#History" title="Pennsylvania">
        Colony of Pennsylvania
       </a>
       land north of the 40th parallel but mistakenly assumed it would intersect the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twelve_Mile_Circle" title="Twelve Mile Circle">
        Twelve Mile Circle
       </a>
       , which it does not. Pennsylvania's border was thus unclear and the colony pushed for a border far south of the 40th parallel. The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mason%E2%80%93Dixon_Line" title="Mason–Dixon Line">
        Mason–Dixon Line
       </a>
       was drawn between 1763 and 1767 as the compromise boundary between the overlapping claims of these two colonies.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_(Columbus,_Ohio).jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2355" data-file-width="2354" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_%28Columbus%2C_Ohio%29.jpg/220px-40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_%28Columbus%2C_Ohio%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_%28Columbus%2C_Ohio%29.jpg/330px-40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_%28Columbus%2C_Ohio%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_%28Columbus%2C_Ohio%29.jpg/440px-40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_%28Columbus%2C_Ohio%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:40th_parallel_marker_at_The_Ohio_State_University_(Columbus,_Ohio).jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         40th parallel marker at
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ohio_State_University" title="The Ohio State University">
          The Ohio State University
         </a>
         (
         <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">
          Columbus, Ohio
         </a>
         )
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The parallel 40° north passes through the cities of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">
        Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">
        Columbus, Ohio
       </a>
       ; as well as northern suburbs of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">
        Indianapolis, Indiana
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" title="Denver, Colorado">
        Denver, Colorado
       </a>
       . The parallel goes directly through the
       <a href="/wiki/John_Glenn_Columbus_International_Airport" title="John Glenn Columbus International Airport">
        John Glenn Columbus International Airport
       </a>
       , with runway 10L-28R lying immediately north of the line, runway 10R-28L lying slightly south of it, and the line going through the northernmost edges of the terminal. It also passes through the main campus of The
       <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University" title="Ohio State University">
        Ohio State University
       </a>
       ; specifically, it cuts directly across the Oval greenspace, between University Hall and the William Oxley Thompson Memorial Library
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Stadium" title="Ohio Stadium">
        Ohio Stadium
       </a>
       , one of the
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_stadiums_by_capacity" title="List of stadiums by capacity">
        largest stadiums
       </a>
       in the world, barely misses the parallel 40° north (6 seconds above 40° north)
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Baseline_Road_(Colorado)" title="Baseline Road (Colorado)">
        Baseline Road
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">
        Boulder, Colorado
       </a>
       , traces the parallel 40° north.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Thistle,_Utah" title="Thistle, Utah">
        Thistle, Utah
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/Ghost_town" title="Ghost town">
        ghost town
       </a>
       since 1983, is slightly (30 seconds) below 40° north.
      </p>
