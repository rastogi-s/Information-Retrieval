   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Judaeo-Piedmontese
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Judaeo-Piedmontese (or Lason Akodesh)
       </b>
       was the vernacular language of the
       <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Italy" title="History of the Jews in Italy">
        Italian Jews
       </a>
       living in
       <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">
        Piedmont
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       , from about the 15th century until
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        World War II
       </a>
       . It was based on the
       <a href="/wiki/Piedmontese_language" title="Piedmontese language">
        Piedmontese language
       </a>
       , with many loanwords from ancient
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hebrew" title="Hebrew">
        Hebrew
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Franco-Proven%C3%A7al_language#Geographic_distribution" title="Franco-Provençal language">
        Provençal
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Judaeo-Spanish" title="Judaeo-Spanish">
        Spanish
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Small_vocabulary">
        Small vocabulary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Judaeo-Piedmontese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Small vocabulary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The dialect never had written phonetic rules; the words in this list are written according to the book
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_gran_battaglia_degli_ebrei_di_Moncalvo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La gran battaglia degli ebrei di Moncalvo (page does not exist)">
         La gran battaglia degli ebrei di Moncalvo
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Primo_Levi" title="Primo Levi">
        Primo Levi
       </a>
       's book
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Periodic_Table_(book)" title="The Periodic Table (book)">
         The Periodic Table
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Pronunciation:
      </p>
      <p>
       (kh) as in German "Na
       <b>
        ch
       </b>
       t".
      </p>
      <p>
       (ñ) nasal, as in English "si
       <b>
        ng
       </b>
       "; not to be confused with the Spanish
       <i>
        ñ
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       (ô) as in English "l
       <b>
        oo
       </b>
       m".
      </p>
      <p>
       (u) like the French
       <i>
        u
       </i>
       or the German
       <i>
        ü
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       (sc) like the English
       <i>
        sh
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       (j) as in German "
       <b>
        J
       </b>
       ung" or in English "
       <b>
        y
       </b>
       oung".
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        (a)brakhà - blessing
       </li>
       <li>
        Adonai Eloénô - God, Lord
       </li>
       <li>
        bahalòm - in a dream (used for jokes)
       </li>
       <li>
        barakhùt - blessed
       </li>
       <li>
        barôcabà - Welcome! (literally: Blessed be he who comes!)
       </li>
       <li>
        batacaìn - cemetery
       </li>
       <li>
        beemà - beast
       </li>
       <li>
        berìt - pact, penis (vulgar)
       </li>
       <li>
        Cadòss Barôkhù - God
       </li>
       <li>
        cassèr - community, ghetto
       </li>
       <li>
        ganàv - thief
       </li>
       <li>
        ganavé - to steal
       </li>
       <li>
        ghescér - bridge
       </li>
       <li>
        gôì - non-Jewish man
       </li>
       <li>
        gôià - non-Jewish woman
       </li>
       <li>
        gojìm - non-Jewish people
       </li>
       <li>
        hafassìm - jewels (lit. "stuff")
       </li>
       <li>
        hamòr - donkey
       </li>
       <li>
        hamortà - stupid woman (lit. female of donkey)
       </li>
       <li>
        hasìr - pig
       </li>
       <li>
        hasirùd - rubbish
       </li>
       <li>
        havertà - rough and dissolute woman
       </li>
       <li>
        khakhàm - rabbi (lit. "learned one")
       </li>
       <li>
        khalaviòd - breasts (from Hebrew "halav", milk)
       </li>
       <li>
        khaltrùm - Catholic bigotry
       </li>
       <li>
        khamisà - five
       </li>
       <li>
        khamissidò - slap
       </li>
       <li>
        khanéc - neck (pregnant with meaning, used to swear)
       </li>
       <li>
        khaniké - to hang (kill)
       </li>
       <li>
        khèder - room
       </li>
       <li>
        kinìm - lice
       </li>
       <li>
        lakhtì - (exclamation) go away!
       </li>
       <li>
        Lassòn Acòdesh - Sacred Language
       </li>
       <li>
        macòd - blows
       </li>
       <li>
        maftèkh - key
       </li>
       <li>
        mahané - neck (generic and neutral)
       </li>
       <li>
        mamsér - bastard
       </li>
       <li>
        mañòd - money
       </li>
       <li>
        medà meshônà - accident (lit. "strange death")
       </li>
       <li>
        menaghèm / meraghèl - spy
       </li>
       <li>
        Milca - Queen
       </li>
       <li>
        morénô - rabbi (lit. "our master")
       </li>
       <li>
        nainé - to look at
       </li>
       <li>
        ñarél - non-circumcised
       </li>
       <li>
        nero - evil, bad
       </li>
       <li>
        pakhàt - fear
       </li>
       <li>
        pegartà - dead woman
       </li>
       <li>
        pôñaltà - dirty, shabbily-clothed woman
       </li>
       <li>
        pôñèl - dirty, shabbily-clothed man
       </li>
       <li>
        rabbenù - rabbi
       </li>
       <li>
        rashàn - non-pious
       </li>
       <li>
        rôkhòd - winds
       </li>
       <li>
        ruàkh - wind
       </li>
       <li>
        samdé - to baptize (lit. "destroy")
       </li>
       <li>
        sarfatìm - guards
       </li>
       <li>
        saròd - disgraced
       </li>
       <li>
        scòla - synagogue, temple
       </li>
       <li>
        sefòkh - toddler vomit
       </li>
       <li>
        sôrada - appearance, look
       </li>
       <li>
        sôtià - crazy woman
       </li>
       <li>
        tafùs - prison, jail (it is a Hebrew loanword in Piedmontese jargon.
        <i>
         Ca tafus
        </i>
        meaning "jail", from
        <i>
         ca
        </i>
        house, although the piedmontese word is
        <i>
         përzon
        </i>
        )
       </li>
       <li>
        takhôrìm - haemorroids
       </li>
       <li>
        tanhanè - to argue
       </li>
      </ul>
