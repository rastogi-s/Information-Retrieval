      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Career">
        Career
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Francisco_Moreno_Fern%C3%A1ndez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Moreno Fernández holds a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD">
        PhD
       </a>
       in Hispanic Linguistics and is Professor of Spanish Language in the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alcal%C3%A1" title="University of Alcalá">
        University of Alcalá
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       ). He pursues research in sociolinguistics, dialectology, and
       <a href="/wiki/Applied_linguistics" title="Applied linguistics">
        applied linguistics
       </a>
       . He has been Academic Director of the "
       <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Cervantes" title="Instituto Cervantes">
        Instituto Cervantes
       </a>
       " (2008-2013) and a visiting researcher at the universities of London, New York, (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SUNY" title="SUNY">
        SUNY
       </a>
       – Albany), Québec (Montreal), and Tokyo as well as visiting professor at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%C3%B6teborg_University" title="Göteborg University">
        Göteborg University
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">
        Sweden
       </a>
       ),
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidade_de_Sao_Paulo" title="Universidade de Sao Paulo">
        Universidade de Sao Paulo
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois_at_Chicago" title="University of Illinois at Chicago">
        University of Illinois at Chicago
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Brigham_Young_University" title="Brigham Young University">
        Brigham Young University
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontificia_Universidad_Cat%C3%B3lica_de_Chile" title="Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile">
        Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       He is Full Member of the
       <a href="/wiki/North_American_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language" title="North American Academy of the Spanish Language">
        North American Academy of the Spanish Language
       </a>
       (since 2017), and Corresponding Member of the Cuban Academy of the Language (since 2013), the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Royal_Academy" title="Spanish Royal Academy">
        Spanish Royal Academy
       </a>
       (since 2015), and the Chilean Academy of the Language (since 2017).
      </p>
      <p>
       Dr. Moreno Fernández was Director of the Cervantes institutes at São Paulo (1998–2001) and Chicago (2001–2005). Academic and Research Director of the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Comillas_Foundation" title="Comillas Foundation">
         Comillas Foundation
        </a>
       </i>
       for the study and teaching of Spanish Language (2006–2008). Since 2013 he is Director of the
       <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Cervantes" title="Instituto Cervantes">
        Instituto Cervantes
       </a>
       at
       <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">
        Harvard University
       </a>
       (Observatory of the Spanish Language and Hispanic Cultures in the United States).
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1998, he coordinated the first
       <i>
        Cervantes Institute Yearbook. Spanish in the World
       </i>
       . He has been a columnist in several American journals in Spanish:
       <i>
        La Opinión
       </i>
       (Los Angeles),
       <i>
        Diario. La Prensa
       </i>
       (New York), and
       <i>
        La Raza
       </i>
       (Chicago). Co-editor of
       <i>
        Spanish in Context
       </i>
       . He belongs to the Editorial Board of the journals:
       <i>
        International Journal of the Sociology of Language
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Journal of Linguistic Geography
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Journal of World Languages
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Boletín de Filología de la Universidad de Chile
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Lingüística Española Actual
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Revista Internacional de Lingüística Iberoamericana
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        Oralia
       </i>
       . He is General Editor of the journal
       <i>
        Lengua y migración / Language &amp; Migration
       </i>
       .
      </p>
