      <p>
       <b>
        Murcian
       </b>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endonym" title="Endonym">
        endonym
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        murciano
       </i>
       ) is a variant of
       <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Spanish" title="Peninsular Spanish">
        Peninsular Spanish
       </a>
       , spoken mainly in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_community_of_Spain" title="Autonomous community of Spain">
        autonomous community
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Murcia" title="Region of Murcia">
        Murcia
       </a>
       and the adjacent
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comarca_(Spain)" title="Comarca (Spain)">
         comarcas
        </a>
       </i>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Vega_Baja_del_Segura" title="Vega Baja del Segura">
        Vega Baja del Segura
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Alto_Vinalop%C3%B3" title="Alto Vinalopó">
        Alto Vinalopó
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Alicante" title="Province of Alicante">
        province of Alicante
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">
        Valencia
       </a>
       ), the
       <a href="/wiki/Altiplanicie_de_Almansa" title="Altiplanicie de Almansa">
        corridor of Almansa
       </a>
       in Albacete (Castile-La Mancha). In a greater extent, it may also include some areas that were part of the former
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Murcia" title="Kingdom of Murcia">
        Kingdom of Murcia
       </a>
       , like southeastern Albacete (now part of Castile La Mancha) and parts of Jaén and Almería (now part of Andalusia).
      </p>
      <p>
       The linguistic varieties of Murcian form a dialect continuum with Eastern Andalusian and Manchego Peninsular Spanish.
      </p>
      <p>
       Murcian is considered a separate language of Spanish by some of its native speakers, who call it
       <i>
        llengua murciana
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The term
       <i>
        panocho
       </i>
       is also used to designate the Murcian language, however it mostly refers to the variety spoken in the
       <i>
        comarca
       </i>
       of the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Huerta_de_Murcia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Huerta de Murcia (page does not exist)">
        Huerta de Murcia
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      </p>

      <p>
       Murcian emerged from the mixture of several linguistics varieties that joined in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Murcia" title="Kingdom of Murcia">
        Kingdom of Murcia
       </a>
       after the conquest of the
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">
        Crown of Aragon
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">
        Crown of Castile
       </a>
       between 13th and 14th centuries. The linguistics varieties were mainly
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tudmir" title="Tudmir">
        Tudmir
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance
       </a>
       (a type of
       <a href="/wiki/Mozarabic_language" title="Mozarabic language">
        Andalusi Romance
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_Arabic" title="Andalusian Arabic">
        Arabic
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">
        Aragonese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Old_Spanish_language" title="Old Spanish language">
        Old Castilian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitano
       </a>
       -
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonetic_features_of_Murcian">
        Phonetic features of Murcian
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Murcian_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Phonetic features of Murcian">

       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Murcian dialectal features differ among areas, villages, social classes and individuals in accordance with the communicative situation in which they are involved, this is because of the influence of standard rule. This dialect has similarities and differences with Spanish, Aragonese and Catalan languages.
      </p>

      <p>
       As Eastern Andalusian and Southern Manchego Peninsular dialects, Murcian has 5 tense vowels (roughly the same as standard Spanish):
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [a]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [e]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [i]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [o]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [u]
       </span>
       , plus 3 lax vowels (not present in standard Spanish):
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [æ]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɛ]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɔ]
       </span>
       . Vowels are lowered when in contact with an omitted coda: fricative (
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /θ/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /x/
       </span>
       ) or liquid (
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /l/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /r/
       </span>
       ); additionally a
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel_harmony" title="Vowel harmony">
        vowel harmony
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phonological_process" title="Phonological process">
        process
       </a>
       may take place; e.g.
       <i>
        la casa
       </i>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [la ˈkasa]
       </span>
       ("the house") vs
       <i>
        las casas
       </i>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [læ ˈkːæsæ]
       </span>
       ("the houses"). Notes about the Murcian vowels:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In some cases
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɛ]
        </span>
        approaches
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [æ]
        </span>
        :
        <i>
         seis
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈsæj]
        </span>
        'six'.
        <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
         [
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
           <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">
            citation needed
           </span>
          </a>
         </i>
         ]
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        Some accents use two additional allophones:
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɪ]
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ʊ]
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Vowels are often phonetically nasalised when in contact with a nasal consonant. Nasalisation is particularly strong when in contact with palatals and velars:
        <i>
         munchos
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈmũn̠ʲtɕɔ ~ ˈmũn̠ʲdʑɔ ~ ˈmũn̠ʲɟʝɔ]
        </span>
        ('much, a lot').
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Consonants">
        Consonants
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The consonant system of Murcian is mostly the same as Castilian.
      </p>
      <p>
       Notable features, several shared with other southern dialects of Spanish:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The spirants
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [β ð ɣ]
        </span>
        are pronounced as fricatives or close fricatives more frequently than in Standard Spanish.
        <ul>
         <li>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [β ð ɣ]
          </span>
          can occur word initially and can be geminated and/or devoiced in certain environments.
         </li>
         <li>
          Pronunciation of
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [β]
          </span>
          (and
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ɸ]
          </span>
          ) may alternate with
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [v]
          </span>
          (and
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [f]
          </span>
          ).
          <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
           [
           <i>
            <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">

              clarification needed
             </span>
            </a>
           </i>
           ]
          </sup>
          <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
           [
           <i>
            <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
             <span title="The text in the vicinity of this tag needs examples for clarification (June 2017)">
              examples needed
             </span>
            </a>
           </i>
           ]
          </sup>
         </li>
         <li>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [ð]
          </span>
          elision is very common:
          <i>
           prenná
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [pɾeˈɲa]
          </span>
          ,
          <i>
           mercao
          </i>
          <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
           [meɾˈka̯o]

          .
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>

        Palatalisation of initial
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /l/
        </span>
        :
        <i>
         llengua
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈʎẽŋgwa]
        </span>
        ('tongue').
       </li>
       <li>
        Final
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /l/
        </span>
        is pronounced
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɾ]
        </span>
        before consonants (
        <i>
         sal blanca
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈsæɾ ˈblaŋka]
        </span>
        'white salt'),
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [l]
        </span>
        before vowels (
        <i>
         sal azul
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈsæl aˈθu]
        </span>
        'blue salt'), and it is silent at the end of an utterance:
        <i>
         sal
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈsæ]
        </span>
        ('salt').
       </li>
       <li>
        Final
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɾ/
        </span>

        <i>
         El Palmar
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɛɾ paɾˈmæ]
        </span>
        .
       </li>

        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">

        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
