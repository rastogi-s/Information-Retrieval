      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Philippine Academy of the Spanish Language
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Academia Filipina de la Lengua Española
        </span>
       </i>
       , abbreviated
       <b>
        AFLE
       </b>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Tagalog_language" title="Tagalog language">
        Tagalog
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="tl" xml:lang="tl">
         Akademyang Pilipino ng Lengguwaheng Espanyol
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Language_regulator" title="Language regulator">
        language regulator
       </a>
       for the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Spanish" title="Philippine Spanish">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       . It is one of two Spanish language regulators located in countries where the language does not have an official status nationwide, the other being the
       <a href="/wiki/North_American_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language" title="North American Academy of the Spanish Language">
        North American Academy of the Spanish Language
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-donquijote_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-donquijote-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A founding member of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asociaci%C3%B3n_de_Academias_de_la_Lengua_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Asociación de Academias de la Lengua Española">
        Asociación de Academias de la Lengua Española
       </a>
       (ASALE), the Academy was formerly headquartered in the
       <a href="/wiki/Casino_Espa%C3%B1ol_de_Manila" title="Casino Español de Manila">
        Casino Español de Manila
       </a>
       in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ermita,_Manila" title="Ermita, Manila">
        Ermita
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">
        Manila
       </a>
       before moving to its current headquarters in
       <a href="/wiki/Makati" title="Makati">
        Makati
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
        Background
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pa%C3%ADses_con_academia_de_la_lengua_espa%C3%B1ola.png">

         Countries with institutes that are members of the ASALE.

      <p>
       The Philippine Academy of the Spanish Language was established in
       <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">
        Manila
       </a>
       on July 25, 1924. The eleventh Spanish language academy in the world to be founded, its establishment reflected the preeminent position of Spanish as a language in the Philippines at the time despite already-existing cultural influences coming from the United States.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laregion_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-laregion-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2008,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/El_Pa%C3%ADs" title="El País">
         El País
        </a>
       </i>
       reported that the
       <a href="/wiki/Securities_and_Exchange_Commission_(Philippines)" title="Securities and Exchange Commission (Philippines)">
        Securities and Exchange Commission
       </a>
       revoked the Academy's corporate registration in 2003 due to its non-filing of annual returns.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elpais_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-elpais-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Despite this, the Academy nonetheless is recognized as possibly playing a key role again in revitalizing the Spanish language and promoting Spanish culture in the Philippines,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newprospects_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-newprospects-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       a role that it also played in previous years.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cambridgecore_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-cambridgecore-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Dar%C3%ADo_Villanueva" title="Darío Villanueva">
        Darío Villanueva
       </a>
       , director of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Real Academia Española">
        Real Academia Española
       </a>
       , visited the Philippine Academy of the Spanish Language in July 2017 as part of his official visit to the Philippines. During his visit, where he also presided over a meeting of the Academy's board of directors, he remarked that the Academy served as "the perennial lighthouse of the Spanish language" in the country.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elperiodico_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-elperiodico-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Status_of_Spanish_in_the_Philippines">
        Status of Spanish in the Philippines
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Status of Spanish in the Philippines">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Spanish" title="Philippine Spanish">
        Philippine Spanish
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Spanish" title="Philippine Spanish">
        Philippine Spanish
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         español filipino, castellano filipino
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a variant of standard
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       spoken in the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       , albeit by an extreme minority. The historical variant is very similar to
       <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Spanish" title="Mexican Spanish">
        Mexican Spanish
       </a>
       , because of Mexican and Hispanic American emigration to the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_East_Indies" title="Spanish East Indies">
        Spanish East Indies
       </a>
       over the years.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="De_facto_official_language">
        <i>
         De facto
        </i>
        official language
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: De facto official language">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_Philippines" title="Spanish language in the Philippines">
        Spanish language in the Philippines
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the Philippines, even if the official languages are Filipino and English, it is clear that
       <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Spanish" title="Philippine Spanish">
        Spanish
       </a>
       is considered a
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">
         de facto
        </a>
       </i>
       official language, as Section 7, Article XIV of the present
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1987_Constitution" title="1987 Constitution">
        1987 Philippine Constitution
       </a>
       specifies Spanish (along with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
        Arabic
       </a>
       ) as a language to "be promoted on a voluntary and optional basis".
      </p>
      <p>
       Spanish was the language of government, education and trade throughout the three centuries (333 years) of the Philippines being part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">
        Spanish Empire
       </a>
       and continued to serve as a
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">
         lingua franca
        </a>
       </i>
       until the first half of the 20th century.
      </p>
      <p>
       In December 2007, former
       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Philippines" title="President of the Philippines">
        President
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal-Arroyo" title="Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo">
        Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo
       </a>
       signed a directive in
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       that require the teaching and learning of the Spanish language in the Philippine school system starting in 2008.
      </p>
      <p>
       The presidential decision had immediate results. The Under-Secretary of the
       <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Education_(Philippines)" title="Department of Education (Philippines)">
        Department of Education
       </a>
       , Vilma L. Labrador, circulated a Memorandum (17/XII/2007), on the
       <i>
        "Restoration of the Spanish language in Philippine Education"
       </i>
       . In it, the Department mandates secondary schools to offer basic and advanced Spanish.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Directors">
        Directors
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Philippine_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Directors">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Philippine Academy of the Spanish Language is led by a Board of Directors (
       <i>
        Junta Directiva
       </i>
       ), which includes a director, two honorary directors, a vice-director, a secretary, a treasurer, a coordinator and the organization's librarian. Since August 22, 2016,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elections_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-elections-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       the Academy has been led by the
       <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Augustinian_Recollects" title="Order of Augustinian Recollects">
        Recollect
       </a>
       priest Emmanuel Luis Romanillos.
      </p>
      <p>
       Romanillos, a historian who became an academic of the Academy in 2005 and who previously served as its coordinator prior to becoming director,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elections_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-elections-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       is associate professor of Spanish, Italian and Latin at the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Philippines_Diliman" title="University of the Philippines Diliman">
        University of the Philippines Diliman
       </a>
       , where he has taught for 30 years.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-romanillos_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-romanillos-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
