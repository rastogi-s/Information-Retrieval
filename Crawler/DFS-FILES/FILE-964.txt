      <p>
       <b>
        Monégasque
       </b>
       (natively
       <i>
        Munegascu
       </i>
       ) is a dialect of
       <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_(Romance_language)" title="Ligurian (Romance language)">
        Ligurian
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance_languages" title="Gallo-Romance languages">
        Gallo-Romance language
       </a>
       spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">
        Monaco
       </a>
       as well as nearby in
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Language_family">
        Language family
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mon%C3%A9gasque_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Language family">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Forming a part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Romance_languages" title="Western Romance languages">
        Western Romance
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect_continuum" title="Dialect continuum">
        dialect continuum
       </a>
       , Monégasque shares many features with the
       <a href="/wiki/Genoese_dialect" title="Genoese dialect">
        Genoese dialect
       </a>
       of Ligurian, but differs from its neighbouring
       <a href="/wiki/Intemelio_dialect" title="Intemelio dialect">
        Intemelio
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Mentonasc_dialect" title="Mentonasc dialect">
        Mentonasc
       </a>
       dialects. It has been partially influenced by the
       <a href="/wiki/Ni%C3%A7ard_dialect" title="Niçard dialect">
        Niçard dialect
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       . Niçard is also traditionally spoken in some parts of Monaco, besides Monégasque.
      </p>
      <p>
       Monegasque, along with all other Ligurian language variants, is derived directly from the northern Italian languages of the
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">
        Middle Ages
       </a>
       , and has some influence in vocabulary, grammar and syntax from French and related Gallo-Romance languages.
      </p>
      <p>
       Before the annexation of the
       <a href="/wiki/County_of_Nice" title="County of Nice">
        County of Nice
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       in 1860, the Niçois spoke a dialect very similar to Monégasque.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Speakers">
        Speakers
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mon%C3%A9gasque_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Speakers">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       It is spoken in addition to
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mon%C3%A9gasques" title="Monégasques">
        Monégasques
       </a>
       . Because the Monégasques are only a minority in Monaco, Monégasque was threatened with extinction in the 1970s.
      </p>
      <p>
       However, the language is now being taught in schools, and its continuance is regarded as secured. In the old part of Monaco, the street signs are written in Monégasque as well as French.
      </p>

        Standard Italian
       </a>
       is also a major language in Monaco. Italian nationals make up some 20% of Monaco's 35,000 permanent residents. Italian was the official language of Monaco when it was a
       <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">
        protectorate
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">
        Kingdom of Sardinia
       </a>
       , from 1814 to 1861, leaving a legacy in some Monégasque words.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Indeed, for a long time after the
       <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">
        Renaissance
       </a>
       , Monaco was the most westerly part on the
       <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">
        Mediterranean
       </a>
       coast of the
       <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">
        Republic of Genoa
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       During the
       <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">
        fascist
       </a>
       occupation in 1942–43, the Principality of Monaco was incorporated into
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       and Monégasque was again considered an Italian dialect. After
       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
        World War II
       </a>
       there were nearly 10,000 Italians in
       <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">
        Monte Carlo
       </a>
       , and some of them (descendants of the followers of
       <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Garibaldi" title="Giuseppe Garibaldi">
        Giuseppe Garibaldi
       </a>
       , who were forced to move from Nice to the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">
        Kingdom of Italy
       </a>
       after 1861) even spoke Monégasque fluently.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Orthography">
        Orthography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mon%C3%A9gasque_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Orthography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Monégasque
       <a href="/wiki/Orthography" title="Orthography">
        orthography
       </a>
       generally follows
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       principles, with the following exceptions:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        the
        <b>
         ü
        </b>
        is pronounced
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ʏ]
        </span>
        , as in
        <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">
         German
        </a>
        , or as the French
        <b>
         u
        </b>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <b>
         œ
        </b>
        is pronounced
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [e]
        </span>
        as the French
        <b>
         é
        </b>
        , and not like the French
        <b>
         œu
        </b>
        as in
        <b>
         bœuf
        </b>
        , which is how
        <b>
         œ
        </b>
        is pronounced in Ligurian, which also uses the character
        <b>
         ö
        </b>
        to represent this sound.
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <b>
         ç
        </b>
        is pronounced as in the French ç
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [s]
        </span>
        :
        <i>
         tradiçiùn
        </i>
        comes from the Latin
        <i>
         traditionem
        </i>
        , and not from the Italian
        <i>
         tradizione
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Samples">
        Samples
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mon%C3%A9gasque_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Samples">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Below is an excerpt from the
       <a href="/wiki/Hymne_Mon%C3%A9gasque" title="Hymne Monégasque">
        Monégasque national anthem
       </a>
       , written by
       <a href="/wiki/Louis_Notari" title="Louis Notari">
        Louis Notari
       </a>
       . In addition, there exists an older French version of the anthem whose lyrics have a different meaning. The choice between the two forms is generally subject to occasion and circumstance.
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        Despoei tugiù sciü d'u nostru paise
        <br/>
        Se ride au ventu, u meme pavayùn
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Despoei tugiù a curù russa e gianca
        <br/>
        E stà l'emblema, d'a nostra libertà
        <br/>
       </p>
       Grandi e i piciui, l'an sempre respetà
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       The following is a Monégasque rendering of the
       <a href="/wiki/Hail_Mary" title="Hail Mary">
        Hail Mary
       </a>
       prayer:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        Ave Maria,
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Tüta de graçia
        <br/>
        u Signù è cun tü
        <br/>
        si benedëta tra tüt'ë done
        <br/>
        e Gesü u to Fiyu è benejiu.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Santa Maria, maire de Diu,
        <br/>
        prega per nùi, pecatùi
        <br/>
        aùra e à l'ura d'a nostra morte
        <br/>
       </p>
       AMEN. (Che sice cusci.)
      </blockquote>
