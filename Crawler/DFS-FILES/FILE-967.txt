      <p>
       <b>
        Eastern Lombard
       </b>
       is a group of closely related dialects of
       <a href="/wiki/Lombard_language" title="Lombard language">
        Lombard
       </a>
       , a
       <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Italic_languages" title="Gallo-Italic languages">
        Gallo-Italic language
       </a>
       spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">
        Lombardy
       </a>
       , mainly in the provinces of
       <a href="/wiki/Bergamo" title="Bergamo">
        Bergamo
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Brescia" title="Brescia">
        Brescia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Mantua" title="Mantua">
        Mantua
       </a>
       , in the area around
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crema,_Italy" title="Crema, Italy">
        Crema
       </a>
       and in parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Trentino" title="Trentino">
        Trentino
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Its main variants are
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bergamasque" title="Bergamasque">
        Bergamasque
       </a>
       and Brescian.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In Italian-speaking contexts, Eastern Lombard is often generically called a "
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       ". This is often incorrectly understood as to mean
       <i>
        a dialect of Italian
       </i>
       , which actually is not the case; it's not a dialect but a language. Eastern Lombard and
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       are different languages and have only limited
       <a href="/wiki/Mutual_intelligibility" title="Mutual intelligibility">
        mutual intelligibility
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Eastern Lombard does not have any official status either in
       <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">
        Lombardy
       </a>
       or anywhere else: the only
       <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">
        official language
       </a>
       in Lombardy is
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">
        Classification
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Eastern Lombard is a
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance
       </a>
       language and belongs to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Italic" title="Gallo-Italic">
        Gallo-Italic
       </a>
       branch. Its position on the language family put in evidence that it is genetically closer to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occitan" title="Occitan">
        Occitan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       , etc. than to
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       . Its substratum is Celtic.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_distribution">
        Geographic distribution
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geographic distribution">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Eastern Lombard is primarily spoken in eastern
       <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">
        Lombardy
       </a>
       (Northern
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       ), in the provinces of Bergamo and Brescia, in northern region of the province of
       <a href="/wiki/Mantua" title="Mantua">
        Mantua
       </a>
       and in the area around
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crema,_Italy" title="Crema, Italy">
        Crema
       </a>
       . The varieties spoken in these regions are generally mutually intelligible for speakers of neighboring areas but this is not always true for distant peripheral areas. For instance, an inhabitant of the alpine valleys of Bergamo can hardly be understood by a rural inhabitant of the plains of Mantua. Differences include lexical, grammatical and phonetic aspects.
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">
        Phonology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       The following notes are essentially based on the variety of Eastern Lombard spoken in Brescia. The basic principle are generally valid also for the other varieties but local discrepancies can be found.
      </p>

       Eastern Lombard has 9
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel" title="Vowel">
        vowels
       </a>
       and 20
       <a href="/wiki/Consonant" title="Consonant">
        consonants
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <p>
       The voiced consonants
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /b/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /d/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ɡ/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /v/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /z/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /dʒ/
       </span>
       never occur at the end of a word.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alberti_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Alberti-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This phenomenon, common to other languages (including
       <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">
        German
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">
        Dutch
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">
        Turkish
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">
        Russian
       </a>
       ), is called
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Final_devoicing" title="Final devoicing">
        final devoicing
       </a>
       . The phoneme
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʃ/
       </span>
       only occurs in loanwords, often borrowings from Italian. For example,
       <i>
        scià
       </i>
       , "to ski" (from Italian
       <i>
        sciare
       </i>
       ) is pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʃiˈa/
       </span>
       . The phoneme
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /tʃ/
       </span>
       is pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [j]
       </span>
       before a consonant. This never occurs inside a word as the segment
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /tʃ/
       </span>
       + consonant doesn't exist in Eastern Lombard. However, it does occur when
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /tʃ/
       </span>
       appears word-finally preceding another word which begins with a consonant.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alberti_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Alberti-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       For example:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <i>
             i è nacc vià
            </i>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             [i ɛ ˌnaj ˈvja]
            </span>
            = "they have gone away"
            <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">
             vs
            </span>
            <br/>
            <i>
             i è nacc a spas
            </i>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             [i ɛ ˌnatʃ a ˈspas]
            </span>
            = "they have gone for a leisury walk"
           </li>
          </ul>
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <i>
             töcc du
            </i>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             [tøj ˈdu]
            </span>
            = "both", "each of the two"
            <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">
             vs
            </span>
            <br/>
            <i>
             töcc öndes
            </i>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             [tøtʃ ˈøndes]
            </span>
            = "all of the eleven"
           </li>
          </ul>
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       The approximants
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /j/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /w/
       </span>
       are distinct phonemes from the vocalic sounds
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /i/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /u/
       </span>
       . This can be seen in the following examples:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             /kwat/
            </span>
            = "how much"
            <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">
             vs
            </span>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             /kuˈat/
            </span>
            = "brooded"
           </li>
           <li>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             /pjat/
            </span>
            = "dish"
            <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant:small-caps;">
             vs
            </span>
            <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
             /piˈat/
            </span>
            = "bitten"
            <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cresci_7-1">
             <a href="#cite_note-Cresci-7">
              [7]
             </a>
            </sup>
           </li>
          </ul>
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       Locally, the alveolar fricative
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [s]
       </span>
       is replaced by the glottal fricative
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [h]
       </span>
       . This mainly happens in the prealpine valleys of the provinces of Bergamo and Brescia;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [n 1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       thus
       <i>
        Brèsa
       </i>
       ("Brescia") is pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ˈbrɛhɔ]
       </span>
       instead of
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ˈbrɛsɔ]
       </span>
       . However, even in areas where this phenomenon is the rule, there are some interesting exceptions to take in account. Words like
       <i>
        grasie
       </i>
       ("thanks") are never pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ˈɡrahje]
       </span>
       . At present, the most common pronunciation is
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ˈɡrasje]
       </span>
       but a more genuine outcome (and often preferred by aged people) would be
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ˈɡrahtʃe]
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Massariello_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Massariello-9">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Other examples for this feature:
      </p>

        </span>
       </li>

        </i>

        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [krisˈtja]
        </span>

        </span>
       </li>
       <li>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Assimilation">
        Assimilation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Assimilation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Regressive
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assimilation_(linguistics)" title="Assimilation (linguistics)">
        assimilation
       </a>
       at word boundaries is common in Eastern Lombard. Assimilation can be either complete or partial. Complete assimilation occurs when two occlusive sounds fall in contact. In this case the first occlusive is completely absorbed by the second and the resulting sound has all the features of the second consonant but is notably lengthened. For example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         el ga fa
         <u>
          t p
         </u>
         àla
        </i>
        =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɛl ɡa fa‿ˈpːalɔ]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         l'è trò
         <u>
          p c
         </u>
         alt
        </i>
        ("it's too hot") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˌlɛ trɔ‿ˈkːalt]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         el ga
         <u>
          t b
         </u>
         ianc
        </i>
        ("the white cat") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [el ɡa‿ˈbːjaŋk]
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The same phenomenon occurs when an occlusive consonant precedes a nasal or a liquid consonant. For example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         en ga
         <u>
          t n
         </u>
         égher
        </i>
        ("a black cat") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɛŋ ɡa‿ˈnːeɡɛr]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         l'è trò
         <u>
          p m
         </u>
         is
        </i>
        =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˌlɛ trɔ‿ˈmːis]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         so ché stra
         <u>
          c m
         </u>
         órt
        </i>
        ("I'm dead tired")=
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [so ˌke stra‿ˈmːort]
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Complete assimilation can also occur when an occlusive precedes a fricative. For example:
       <i>
        l'è ni
        <u>
         t v
        </u>
        ért
       </i>
       =
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ˌlɛ ni‿ˈvːert]
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       When a sequence of nasal+occlusive falls in contact with another occlusive or a fricative, the first occlusive is completely elided and the nasal undergoes partial assimilation. In this case no lengthening occurs. For example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         el ga 'l sa
         <u>
          nch b
         </u>
         lö
        </i>
        ("he's got blue blood")=
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɛlˌɡal sam‿ˈblø]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         l'è lo
         <u>
          nc f
         </u>
         és
        </i>
        =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˌlɛ loɱ‿ˈfes]
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       But when an occlusive precedes
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /z/
       </span>
       , assimilation involves both consonants and the result is an affricate sound:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         l'è ni
         <u>
          t z
         </u>
         ó ècc
        </i>
        =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [lɛ ˌni‿dːzo ˈɛtʃ]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         l'è trò
         <u>
          p z
         </u>
         alt
        </i>
        ("it's too yellow") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˌlɛtrɔ‿ˈdːzalt]
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The phoneme
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /n/
       </span>
       can undergo assimilation in
       <a href="/wiki/Place_of_articulation" title="Place of articulation">
        place of articulation
       </a>
       with a following consonant. Thus, the /n/ in
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /nk/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /nɡ/
       </span>
       is a velar
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ŋ]
       </span>
       , the /n/ in
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /nv/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /nf/
       </span>
       is a labiodental
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɱ]
       </span>
       . Within a word, the phoneme
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /n/
       </span>
       is never transcribed before
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /p/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /b/
       </span>
       , where
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /m/
       </span>
       is written instead. Nasal assimilation, including
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /n/
       </span>
       to
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /m/
       </span>
       , also takes place across word boundaries. For example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         e
         <u>
          n c
         </u>
         a
        </i>
        ("a dog") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ɛŋ‿ˈka]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         vàghe
         <u>
          n f
         </u>
         ò
        </i>
        ("hurry up") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˌvaɡeɱ‿ˈfɔ]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         l'a
         <u>
          n p
         </u>
         asàt
        </i>
        ("last year") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˌlam‿paˈsat]
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>

       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a/
       </span>
       in open syllables only, and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /e/
       </span>
       in both open and closed syllables. Other vowels can occur in final syllables in loanwords.
      </p>
      <p>
       Locally, the phoneme
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a/
       </span>
       is pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɔ]
       </span>
       when it appears as last sound of the word in an unstressed syllable
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alberti_6-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Alberti-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (actually slightly more close than cardinal
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɔ]
       </span>
       ). For example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>

         </u>

        </span>

         seteman
         <u>
          a
         </u>
        </i>
        ("week") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [sɛtɛˈmanɔ]
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Unstressed_vowel_system_reduction_and_local_variability">
        Unstressed vowel system reduction and local variability
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Unstressed vowel system reduction and local variability">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Some vowel contrasts are eliminated in unstressed syllables. For example, in the urban Brescian variety,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɔ]
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [o]
       </span>
       no longer contrast. Thus, the word
       <i>
        robà
       </i>
       ("to steal") can be pronounced both
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [roˈba]
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [rɔˈba]
       </span>
       , with almost no difference noticed by speakers. In addition, a further variant
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ruˈba]
       </span>
       is also possible, though in this case, a difference is noticed by speakers but it is considered a local variant and no loss of intelligibility results. The sounds
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [e]
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɛ]
       </span>
       also no longer contrast in unstressed syllables, and therefore the word
       <i>
        vedèl
       </i>
       ("calf") can be pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [veˈdɛl]
       </span>
       or
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [vɛˈdɛl]
       </span>
       . However, when affected by vowel harmony (see
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Lombard_language#Vowel_harmony" title="Eastern Lombard language">
        below
       </a>
       ), the unstressed sounds
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [e]
       </span>
       /
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɛ]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [o]
       </span>
       /
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɔ]
       </span>
       , and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ø]
       </span>
       become
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [i]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [u]
       </span>
       , and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [y]
       </span>
       respectively.
      </p>
      <p>
       In conclusion, it is possible to say that only five contrastive vowel qualities are found in unstressed syllables:
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [o]
       </span>
       /
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɔ]
       </span>
       /
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [(u)]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ø]
       </span>
       /
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [(y)]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [a]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [e]
       </span>
       /
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɛ]
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [i]
       </span>
       (but with the
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [i]
       </span>
       not completely separated from
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [e]
       </span>
       /
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ɛ]
       </span>
       ). Some examples:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         molà
        </i>
        ("to let go, to release")
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [moˈla]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         mölà
        </i>
        ("to grind")
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [møˈla]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         malàt
        </i>
        ("sick, ill")
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [maˈlat]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         pelàt
        </i>
        ("bald")
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [peˈlat]
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Milà
        </i>
        ("Milan")
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [miˈla]
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The situation can differ for other Eastern Lombard varieties, however, and the rules of the unstressed vowel system vary according to the area. For example, in
       <a href="/wiki/Franciacorta" title="Franciacorta">
        Franciacorta
       </a>
       , a province of Brescia, the sounds
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [o]
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ø]
       </span>
       are regularly replaced by
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [u]
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [y]
       </span>
       in pretonic position:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         mulà
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         molà
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Ruàt
        </i>
        ("
        <a href="/wiki/Rovato" title="Rovato">
         Rovato
        </a>
        ") instead of
        <i>
         Roàt
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Üspedalèt
        </i>
        ("
        <a href="/wiki/Ospitaletto" title="Ospitaletto">
         Ospitaletto
        </a>
        ") instead of
        <i>
         Öspedalèt
        </i>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Since in unstressed position these vocalic sounds are not contrastive, these local variants do not compromise reciprocal intelligibility.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vowel_harmony">
        Vowel harmony
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Vowel harmony">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Certain varieties of Eastern Lombard (mostly in Brescian area) exhibit a process of regressive
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel_harmony" title="Vowel harmony">
        vowel harmony
       </a>
       involving the feature of vowel height.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alberti_6-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-Alberti-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       When the stress falls on a close vowel (
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /i/
       </span>
       or
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /u/
       </span>
       ) the preceding vowels shift their height, becoming close as well (
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ɛ/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /e/
       </span>
       become
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [i]
       </span>
       , while
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ɔ/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o/
       </span>
       become
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [u]
       </span>
       ). The vowel
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a/
       </span>
       is not affected by this process and acts as opaque vowel blocking the harmonization process.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sanga_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Sanga-10">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In Camuno, harmonization occurs almost only where the stressed vowel is an
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /i/
       </span>
       and not where it is an
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /u/
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cresci_7-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Cresci-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       This phenomenon affects all the words independent of the word's function.
      </p>
      <p>
       Because the
       <a href="/wiki/Diminutive" title="Diminutive">
        diminutive
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Augmentative" title="Augmentative">
        augmentative
       </a>
       are formed with the suffixes
       <i>
        -ì
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -ù
       </i>
       (feminine
       <i>
        -ìna
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        -ùna
       </i>
       ) respectively, this process is easily observable in nouns:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         cortèl
        </i>
        ("knife")
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           curtilì
          </i>
          ("small knife")
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           curtilù
          </i>
          ("big knife")
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       As already mentioned, the vowel
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a/
       </span>
       acts as opaque vowel which blocks the harmonization process:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         fontàna
        </i>
        ("fountain")
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           fontanì
          </i>
          ("small fountain"),
          <u>
           not
          </u>
          <i>
           funtanì
          </i>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         öspedàl
        </i>
        ("hospital")
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           öspedalì
          </i>
          ("small hospital"),
          <u>
           not
          </u>
          <i>
           üspidalì
          </i>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       But vowels that occur after the
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a/
       </span>
       and before the stressed vowel are still affected:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         mortadèla
        </i>
        ("
        <a href="/wiki/Mortadella" title="Mortadella">
         mortadella
        </a>
        ")
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           mortadilìna
          </i>
          ("small mortadella")
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       In these cases variants like
       <i>
        funtanì
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        üspedalì
       </i>
       (but not
       <i>
        üspidalì
       </i>
       ) or
       <i>
        murtadilìna
       </i>
       are accepted (or locally preferred) but fall under the normal unstressed vowel variability.
      </p>
      <p>
       Verbs are affected by this process in their conjugation, when the inflection contains a stressed
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /i/
       </span>
       (there are no verbal suffixes containing a stressed
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /u/
       </span>
       ). For example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         öler
        </i>
        ("to want")
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           öle
          </i>
          ("I want")
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           ülìt
          </i>
          ("wanted", past participle)
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           ülìf
          </i>
          ("you want", second person plural)
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           ülìef
          </i>
          ("you wanted", second person plural imperfect indicative)
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Adjectives formed with the suffix
       <i>
        -ùs
       </i>
       (feminine
       <i>
        -ùza
       </i>
       ) also exhibit this rule:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         póra
        </i>
        ("fear")
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           purús
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           purúza
          </i>
          ("fearful person")
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Orthography">
        Orthography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Orthography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Since Eastern Lombard is still principally an oral language, a commonly accepted orthography has not been established. While in recent years there has been an increasing production of texts (mainly light comedies and poem collections), each author continues to follow their own spelling rules. The most problematic and controversial issues seem to be the representation of intervocalic
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /z/
       </span>
       (rendered by different authors with ⟨-ss-⟩, ⟨-s-⟩ or ⟨-z-⟩) and final
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /tʃ/
       </span>
       vs.
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /k/
       </span>
       (rendered with ⟨-cc⟩, ⟨-c⟩ or ⟨-ch⟩).
      </p>
      <p>
       This article follows the rules of the
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language#Writing_system" title="Italian language">
        Italian orthography
       </a>
       , with the following exceptions.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vowels_2">
        Vowels
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>

        /ɛ/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o/
       </span>
       from
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ɔ/
       </span>
       in stressed syllables. Furthermore, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umlaut_(diacritic)" title="Umlaut (diacritic)">
        umlaut
       </a>
       is adopted to represent the rounded vowels
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ø/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">

      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Consonants_2">
        Consonants
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Consonants">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Digraph_(orthography)" title="Digraph (orthography)">
        digraph
       </a>
       ⟨-cc⟩ is used at the end of the word to represent the sound
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /tʃ/
       </span>
       (in other positions this sound is rendered by means of the usual Italian orthography rules: ⟨c⟩ before front vowels and ⟨ci⟩ before non-front vowels).
      </p>
      <p>
       A consonant sequence that is peculiar to Lombard is that of a
       <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_alveolar_fricative" title="Voiceless alveolar fricative">
        voiceless alveolar fricative
       </a>
       followed by a
       <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_postalveolar_affricate" title="Voiceless postalveolar affricate">
        voiceless postalveolar affricate
       </a>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [stʃ]
       </span>
       . This article adopts the convention of representing this sound as ⟨s·c⟩, although other texts may follow different traditions (so the same sequence can also be spelled ⟨s'c⟩ or ⟨s-c⟩ or even the ambiguous ⟨sc⟩; some authors use ⟨scc⟩). This sequence, which is absent in Italian, can occur at the beginning of word, as in
       <i>
        s·cèt
       </i>
       ("son, boy")
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /stʃɛt/
       </span>
       ; in the middle, as in
       <i>
        brös·cia
       </i>
       ("brush")
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ˈbrøstʃa/
       </span>
       ; or at the end, as in
       <i>
        giös·cc
       </i>
       ("right, correct", plural)
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ˈdʒøstʃ/
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The sequence
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /zdʒ/
       </span>
       is also present in Eastern Lombard and is represented in this article with the sequence of signs ⟨-sgi-⟩, for example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         bàsgia
        </i>
        ("large bowl") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ˈbazdʒa/
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         sgionfà
        </i>
        ("to inflate") =
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /zdʒonˈfa/
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar">
        Grammar
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Grammar">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Lombard_grammar" title="Eastern Lombard grammar">
        Eastern Lombard grammar
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The grammatical system of Eastern Lombard is similar to other those of other Romance languages. The
       <a href="/wiki/Word_order" title="Word order">
        word order
       </a>
       is
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agent%E2%80%93verb%E2%80%93object" title="Agent–verb–object">
        SVO
       </a>
       (subject–verb–object) and it has a moderate inflection system: verbs are declined for
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_mood" title="Grammatical mood">
        mood
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_tense" title="Grammatical tense">
        tense
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_aspect" title="Grammatical aspect">
        aspect
       </a>
       and agree with their subject in
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_person" title="Grammatical person">
        person
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_number" title="Grammatical number">
        number
       </a>
       . Nouns are classified as either
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_gender" title="Grammatical gender">
        masculine or feminine
       </a>
       and can be marked as singular or plural. Adjectives and pronouns agree with any nouns they modify in gender and number. Eastern Lombard also prefers
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preposition" title="Preposition">
        prepositions
       </a>
       over
       <a href="/wiki/Declension" title="Declension">
        case marking
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">
        Literature
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Literature">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The oldest known text written in Eastern Lombard consists of fragments of a laud known as
       <i>
        Mayor gremeza il mund no pothevela ancor aver
       </i>
       , a manuscript found in
       <a href="/wiki/Bovegno" title="Bovegno">
        Bovegno
       </a>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trompia" title="Trompia">
        Trompia
       </a>
       valley), and dating from the fourteenth century. Today, literary production has increased in volume and mainly consists in light comedies and poem collections (Angelo Canossi is an example for poetry in the Brescian dialect).
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">
        Examples
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Examples">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The following tale is in Brescian:
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="La_m.C3.A8rla">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="La_mèrla">
        <i>
         La mèrla
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: La mèrla">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <i>
        I mèrli 'na ólta i ghìa le pène biànche, ma chèl envéren lé l'éra stàt en bèl envéren e lé, la mèrla, la gà dìt: "Zenér de la màla gràpa, per tò despèt gó i uzilì 'ndela gnàta." A lü, 'l Zenér, gh'è nìt adòs 'n pó de ràbia, e 'l gà dìt: "Spèta, mèrla, che te la faró mé adès a té, e se te sét biànca mé te faró ègner négra." E pò dòpo 'l gà dit amò: "Dù ghe i ó e giü 'n prèstet el töaró e se te sét biànca, mé te faró ní négra." E alùra 'l gà fàt nì fò 'n frèt che se n'ìa mài vést giü compàgn.
       </i>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        Lé la mèrla la saìa piö che fà cói sò uzilì ndèla gnàta, e isé l'è nàda a rifügiàs endèla càpa del camì; dré al camì va sö 'l föm e lùr i uzilì i è déentàcc töcc négher, e quànche i è nicc fò de là, la mèrla la gh'ìa mìa piö le pène biànche, ma la ghe i éra négre. Alùra Zenér, töt sudisfàt, el gà dìt: "Tò mèrla, che te l'ó fàda mé staólta: se te se stàda biànca mé t'ó fàt ní négra e isé te làset lé de seghetà a tiràm en gìr."
       </i>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonetic_transcription">
        Phonetic transcription
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [i ˈmɛrli na ˈoltɔ i ˈɡiɔ le ˌpɛne ˈbjaŋke | ma ˌkɛl ɛɱˌverɛn ˈle lerɔ ˌstat ɛm ˈbɛl ɛɱˌverɛn ɛ ˌle | la ˈmɛrlɔ | la ɡa ˈditː | zeˈner de la ˌmalɔ ˈɡrapɔ | ˌper tɔ deˈspɛt ˌɡo j uziˈli ˌndelɔ ˈɲatɔ | aˈly | lzeˈner | ˌɡɛ nit aˈdɔs em ˌpo de ˈrabja | ˌɛ lː ɡa ˈdit | ˈspɛtɔ | ˌmɛrlɔ | kɛ tɛ la faˌro ˈme aˌdɛs a ˈte | ɛ sɛ tɛ ˌse ˈbːjaŋkɔ ˌme tɛ faro ˌɛɲɛr ˈneɡrɔ | ɛ pɔ ˈdɔpo l ɡaˌdit aˌmɔ | ˌdu ɡɛ ˈj o ɛ dʒy m ˌprɛstet ɛl tøaˈro ɛ sɛ tɛ ˌse ˈbːjaŋkɔ | ˌme tɛ faˌro ni ˈneɡrɔ | ɛ aˈlurɔ l ɡa ˌfa nːi ˌfɔ ɱ ˈfrɛt kɛ sɛ ˌnia mai ˌvez dʒy komˈpaɲ]
       </span>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_she-blackbird">
        The she-blackbird
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The she-blackbird">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Once upon a time blackbirds had white feathers, but in that time winter had been mild and a she-blackbird scorned January saying: "Bad-headed January, in spite of you I have got a brood in my nest." Hearing this, January got angry and he said: "Just wait a bit, you she-blackbird, I will fool you and I will turn you from white into black." Then he said: "I have got two, and I will borrow one,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and I will turn you from white to black." And he brought forth a cold as there had never been before.
      </p>
      <p>
       The she-blackbird did not know how to cope with her brood in the nest, so she sheltered in the hood of a chimney, and the smoke turned all the birds black; so when they came out the blackbirds did not have white feathers anymore, but black ones. And January, very happy, said: "This time it was me that fooled you, blackbird: you were white and I turned you black, this will teach you to stop teasing me."
      </p>
