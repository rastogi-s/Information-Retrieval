      <p>
       <b>
        Campidanese Sardinian
       </b>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-e18_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-e18-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glottolog_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Glottolog-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="sc" xml:lang="sc">
         Sardu Campidanesu
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="it" xml:lang="it">
         Sardo Campidanese
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a standardised
       <a href="/wiki/Variety_(linguistics)" title="Variety (linguistics)">
        variety
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian language
       </a>
       primarily spoken in the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Cagliari" title="Province of Cagliari">
        Province of Cagliari
       </a>
       , Italy.
      </p>
      <p>
       Traditionally the name
       <a href="/wiki/Campidano" title="Campidano">
        Campidano
       </a>
       refers to the fertile area located around the towns of
       <a href="/wiki/Guspini" title="Guspini">
        Guspini
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Villacidro" title="Villacidro">
        Villacidro
       </a>
       . Campidanese and its variants of the dialect can be found across the entire Province of Cagliari and not just the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Medio_Campidano" title="Province of Medio Campidano">
        Province of Medio Campidano
       </a>
       area. Campidanese also extends into parts of the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Nuoro" title="Province of Nuoro">
        Province of Nuoro
       </a>
       , notably the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ogliastra" title="Ogliastra">
        Ogliastra
       </a>
       area and in the southern half of the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Oristano" title="Province of Oristano">
        Province of Oristano
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Oristano" title="Oristano">
        the capital
       </a>
       included. However, it is at this point that the language merges into
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logudorese_language" title="Logudorese language">
        Logudorese
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Campidanese Sardinian is intelligible to those from the central to southern part of Sardinia, where
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logudorese_language" title="Logudorese language">
        Logudorese Sardinian
       </a>
       is spoken,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sardinianvarieties_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Sardinianvarieties-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       but it is not to those from the extreme north of the island, where
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language#Linguistics" title="Sardinian language">
        Corsican–Sardinian dialects
       </a>
       are spoken. Italian speakers can hardly understand Campidanese, like any other dialect of the Sardinian language:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Sardinian is an autonomous linguistic group rather than a
       <a href="/wiki/Regional_Italian" title="Regional Italian">
        dialect of Italian
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       as it is often noted because of its morphological, synctatic, and lexical differences from Italian. In 2009, the provincial administration of Cagliari approved the spelling, phonetics, morphology, and vocabulary rules for Standard Campidanese Sardinian.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Subvariants">
        Subvariants
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Campidanese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Subvariants">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are six main but mutually intelligible subdialects of Campidanese: Arborense, Cagliaritano (
       <i>
        Casteddaiu
       </i>
       ), Meridionale, Ogliastrino, Guspinese and Villacidrese. Cagliaritano is the dialect of Campidanese spoken in the capital, Cagliari; however, it extends to most of the neighbouring towns and villages within a 15 km radius of Cagliari.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vocabulary">
        Vocabulary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Campidanese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Vocabulary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Campidanese has some borrowed words from
       <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">
        Aragonese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       . Since the early 20th century, there has been an increase in lexical borrowing from Italian. That is particularly evident with technological words for which there is no Campidanese equivalent. However, many words that are from Italian have been changed phonetically so that they sound Sardinian. Italian loan words that end in an
       <i>
        o
       </i>
       are often substituted with a
       <i>
        u
       </i>
       . The strong Campidanese accent also changes the sound of the word.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">
        Characteristics
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Campidanese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Characteristics">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Singular nouns descending from 3rd declension Latin nouns ending in "-i" (Campidanese
       <i>
        pisci
       </i>
       vs Logudorese
       <i>
        pische
       </i>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       Plural definite article of "is" (Campidanese
       <i>
        is terras
       </i>
       vs Logudorese
       <i>
        sas terras
       </i>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       Present participle in "-endi" (Campidanese
       <i>
        èssendi
       </i>
       vs Logudorese
       <i>
        èssende
       </i>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       Conservation of the Latin phoneme &lt;qu&gt; and &lt;gu&gt; (/kʷ/ and /gʷ/) in words such as
       <i>
        akua
       </i>
       (water) and
       <i>
        sànguni
       </i>
       (blood).
      </p>
      <p>
       Palatalisation of Latin word-initial /k/ before /e/ and /i/ (Lat.
       <i>
        centum
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        centu
       </i>
       cf. Logudorese
       <i>
        chentu
       </i>
       ). In medial positions, /k/ becomes -/ʒ/- (Lat.
       <i>
        decem
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        dexi
       </i>
       cf. Logudorese
       <i>
        deghe
       </i>
       ) or -/ʃʃ/- (Lat.
       <i>
        piscem
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        pisci
       </i>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       Transformation of /rj/ to /rg/, /nj/ to /ng/, /lj/ to /ll/, and /ti/, /te/ into /tz/
      </p>
      <p>
       Epenthetic /a/ before word-initial /r/ (Lat.
       <i>
        rubeum
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        arrubiu
       </i>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       Metathesis (Logudorese
       <i>
        Carbonia
       </i>
       vs Campidanese
       <i>
        Crabonia
       </i>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       Catalan influence (Words such as
       <i>
        seu
       </i>
       "cathedral" loaned from Catalan)
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_system">
        Writing system
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Campidanese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Writing system">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Campidanese is written using the Latin alphabet. Like Italian, Campidanese does not use w and y. Campidanese also uses the
       <a href="/wiki/Digraph_(orthography)" title="Digraph (orthography)">
        digraphs
       </a>
       <b>
        gh
       </b>
       , representing
       <a href="/wiki/Voiced_velar_stop" title="Voiced velar stop">
        /g/
       </a>
       ,
       <b>
        ch
       </b>
       representing
       <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_velar_stop" title="Voiceless velar stop">
        /k/
       </a>
       before
       <i>
        e
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       vowels,
       <b>
        tz
       </b>
       representing
       <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_alveolar_affricate" title="Voiceless alveolar affricate">
        /ts/
       </a>
       and
       <b>
        x
       </b>
       , representing
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voiced_palato-alveolar_sibilant" title="Voiced palato-alveolar sibilant">
        /ʒ/
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In phonetic syntax, final or intervocalic
       <i>
        t
       </i>
       is pronounced as a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voiced_alveolar_stop" title="Voiced alveolar stop">
        /d/
       </a>
       (es:
       <i>
        issu andat
       </i>
       , meaning "he goes", is pronounced
       <i>
        issu andada
       </i>
       ) and
       <i>
        s
       </i>
       is pronounced as a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voiced_dental_sibilant#Voiced_dental_sibilant" title="Voiced dental sibilant">
        /z̪/
       </a>
       , (es.
       <i>
        sa mesa
       </i>
       , meaning "the table", is pronounced
       <i>
        sa mez̪a
       </i>
       ). In case there are consonants like
       <i>
        s
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        t
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        nt
       </i>
       at the end of the word, a helping vowel is usually added (es.
       <i>
        sa domu
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        is domus(u)
       </i>
       , the house, the houses). If preceded by a consonant, an "i" is inserted before the normally-initial
       <i>
        s
       </i>
       (es:
       <i>
        sa scala
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        is (i)scalas(a)
       </i>
       , the staircase, the staircases). The spelling rules were established by the Province of Cagliari with a deliberation on March 17, 2010.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       </i>
       <i>
        Mancai fessit cincu oras currendu e èssit passau Campidanu, Partiolla, Trexenta e Sarcidanu, non parìat fadiau. Passau su scàmbiu arrallentàt e si poniat a passu de ómini po intrai in stazioni: cun imponénzia, cun importànzia, cun totu is ogus de sa genti puntaus asuba, cumpraxu. Ma candu si firmàt, asutta de is matas de ùlimu prus artas de sa stazioni, e sa genti cumenzàt a si accostiai, su ùrtimu ciuff pariat unu suspiru: fut própiu fadiau.
       </i>
       <i>
        Ma fut prexau su própiu, su trenu de Casteddu: a ananti de sa macchina is cuatru numerus de latoni – 1908 – brilliant che unu sprigu, e su capustatzioni, cun su berretu arrùbiu in conca, fut benendu a ddu arricìri cun grandu arrespetu. Comenti dónnia dì.
       </i>
      </p>
