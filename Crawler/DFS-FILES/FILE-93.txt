      <p>
       <b>
        Tema
       </b>
       is a city on the
       <a href="/wiki/Bight_of_Benin" title="Bight of Benin">
        Bight of Benin
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic
       </a>
       coast of
       <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">
        Ghana
       </a>
       . It is located 25 kilometres (16 mi) east of the capital city;
       <a href="/wiki/Accra" title="Accra">
        Accra
       </a>
       , in the region of
       <a href="/wiki/Greater_Accra_Region" title="Greater Accra Region">
        Greater Accra
       </a>
       , and is the capital of the
       <a href="/wiki/Tema_Metropolis_District" title="Tema Metropolis District">
        Tema Metropolitan District
       </a>
       . As of 2013, Tema is the eleventh most populous settlement in Ghana, with a population of approximately 161,612 people – a marked decrease from its 2005 figure of 209,000.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GW_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GW-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenwich_Meridian" title="Greenwich Meridian">
        Greenwich Meridian
       </a>
       (00
       <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">
        Longitude
       </a>
       ) passes directly through the city.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GHD_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GHD-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Tema is locally nicknamed the "Harbour Town" because of its status as Ghana's largest
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seaport" title="Seaport">
        seaport
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Tema is a city constructed on the site of a small fishing village. Tema was commissioned by Ghana's first president,
       <a href="/wiki/Kwame_Nkrumah" title="Kwame Nkrumah">
        Kwame Nkrumah
       </a>
       , and grew rapidly after the construction of a large harbour in 1961. The Tema
       <a href="/wiki/Metropolis" title="Metropolis">
        metropolis
       </a>
       was designed, planned and developed by the award-winning
       <a href="/wiki/Urban_planner" title="Urban planner">
        urban planner
       </a>
       and Ghana's first
       <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">
        architect
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Theodore_S._Clerk" title="Theodore S. Clerk">
        Theodore S. Clerk
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is now a major trading centre, home to an
       <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">
        oil refinery
       </a>
       and numerous factories, and is linked to Accra by a highway and railway. Tema is one of Ghana's two deep seaports, the other being
       <a href="/wiki/Sekondi-Takoradi" title="Sekondi-Takoradi">
        Sekondi-Takoradi
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GHD_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-GHD-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Tema became an Autonomous Council in 1974 and was elevated to the status of a Metropolitan Assembly in December 1990. Tema metropolitan forms part of the sixteen (16) Metropolis, Municipalities and Districts in the Greater Accra Region. The Metropolitan shares boundaries with Ashaiman Municipal, Adentan Municipal, and ledzokuku Krowor Municipal to the west respectively, to the east with Kpone Katamanso District, to the North with Dangme West District and to the South with the Gulf of Guinea.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Before the
       <a href="/wiki/History_of_Ghana#Independent_Ghana" title="History of Ghana">
        independence
       </a>
       , the government identified a small fishing village called Torman as the site for an ultra modern seaport for the new Ghana. Torman residents also grew the calabash plant (gourd), known as Tor in the local language, hence the name of the village Tor-man (gourd-land) which was corrupted to TEMA.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1952, the government acquired 166 square kilometres (64 sq mi) of land north of the harbour, and entrusted it to the Tema Development Corporation. The "New Town" that was subsequently built on the site was planned as an industrial and residential complex. The villagers of Torman were ejected to make way for the project, and migrated to a new fishing ground around 3 kilometres (1.9 mi) away.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghanaweb.com_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ghanaweb.com-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The main Tema Township was constructed, and the Tema Harbour officially opened, in 1962. Over the following decades, Tema grew into the industrial hub of Ghana, with a carefully constructed road layout featuring landscaping and street lights. It boasted modern recreational centres and other social amenities rare among African cities at the time.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghanaweb.com_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ghanaweb.com-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       President Nkrumah appointed Mr. Theophilus Asiaw Mills as the first District Commissioner.
      </p>
      <p>
       The chiefs and people of Tema Manhean have finally made a historical return to their ancestral home at the frontage of the dilapidated Meridian Hotel building for the first time in 55 years, after they were relocated to their present home at Tema Manhean.
      </p>
      <p>
       Fifty-five years ago, the government of Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, Ghana's first President, in an attempt to develop the Tema into a modern port city, and also to create an industrial city, forcefully relocated the Tema people from their original place of abode to the present Tema Manhean.
      </p>
      <p>
       A large population influx began in the 1960s owing to the town's employment opportunities; but the Tema Development Corporation was unable to construct housing and provide other services to meet the needs of the migrants.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTAL_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-OTAL-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Tema Newtown district was overwhelmed by the sudden population growth, and became the poor cousin of Tema Township, receiving none of the latter's improved housing, geometrically laid roads, or social amenities. Moreover, royalties paid by Tema Newtown's companies to evicted villagers have not been used due to a chieftain dispute. The area's fishing potential was thus not fully exploited.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghanaweb.com_5-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-ghanaweb.com-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The importance of Tema as a port and industrial hub is reflected by the fact that the
       <a href="/wiki/Ghana_Police_Service" title="Ghana Police Service">
        Ghana Police Service
       </a>
       maintains a special policing region devoted entirely to the city.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghanaweb.com_5-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-ghanaweb.com-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">
        Climate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Tema is characterised by a dry
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Equatorial_climate" title="Equatorial climate">
        equatorial climate
       </a>

        <a href="#cite_note-GHD-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Seaport">
        Seaport
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Seaport">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Tema_Harbour" title="Tema Harbour">
        Tema Harbour
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Containerterminal_in_Tema,_Ghana.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2272" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Containerterminal_in_Tema%2C_Ghana.jpg/190px-Containerterminal_in_Tema%2C_Ghana.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Containerterminal_in_Tema%2C_Ghana.jpg/285px-Containerterminal_in_Tema%2C_Ghana.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Containerterminal_in_Tema%2C_Ghana.jpg/380px-Containerterminal_in_Tema%2C_Ghana.jpg 2x" width="190"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Containerterminal_in_Tema,_Ghana.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         A container terminal at Tema Port in 2008.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Tema Port, which was opened in 1962, is the biggest of the two seaports in Ghana. It has a water-enclosed area of 1.7 square kilometres (0.66 sq mi) and a total land area of 3.9 square kilometres (1.5 sq mi). Apart from handling Ghanaian imports and exports, it is also a traffic junction, dealing with transit cargo destined for the landlocked countries of
       <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">
        Burkina Faso
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">
        Mali
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">
        Niger
       </a>
       . The port of Tema handles 80% of Ghana's import and export cargo, including the country's chief export,
       <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_bean" title="Cocoa bean">
        cacao
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The port has 5 kilometres (3.1 mi) of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breakwaters" title="Breakwaters">
        breakwaters
       </a>
       , 12 deepwater berths, an outsize
       <a href="/wiki/Oil_tanker" title="Oil tanker">
        oil tanker
       </a>
       berth, a dockyard, warehouses, and transit sheds. The port has open and covered areas for the storage of cargo, including a 77,200-m² (7.72-hectare) paved area for the storage of containers, steel products and other conventional cargo. The port's container yard is capable of holding over 8,000
       <a href="/wiki/Twenty-foot_equivalent_unit" title="Twenty-foot equivalent unit">
        TEUs
       </a>
       at any given time. The closed storage area, which is about 25,049 m² (2.51 hectares) in area, consists of six sheds with a total storage capacity of 50,000 tonnes of cargo. The port also includes a 100,000-
       <a href="/wiki/Deadweight_tonnage" title="Deadweight tonnage">
        dwt
       </a>
       dry dock and slipway facility.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GPHA_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-GPHA-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The harbour is operated by the
       <a href="/wiki/Ghana_Ports_and_Harbours_Authority" title="Ghana Ports and Harbours Authority">
        Ghana Ports and Harbours Authority
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Fishing_harbour">
        Fishing harbour
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fishing harbour">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Ghana has a long history of fishing. The Tema fishing harbour is at the eastern end of the town's commercial harbour. It comprises the Inner Fishing Harbour, the
       <a href="/wiki/Canoe" title="Canoe">
        Canoe
       </a>
       Basin, the Outer Fishing Harbour, and a commercial area with marketing and cold storage facilities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTAL_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-OTAL-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Inner Fishing Harbour was constructed along the Tema Main Port in 1962 to provide a handling facility for semi-industrial and industrial fishing vessels, and to encourage the development of the local
       <a href="/wiki/Fishing_industry" title="Fishing industry">
        fishing industry
       </a>
       . In 1965, the Outer Fishing Harbour was constructed for bigger industrial vessels such as
       <a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">
        trawlers
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tuna" title="Tuna">
        tuna
       </a>
       vessels, and deep-sea carriers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTAL_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-OTAL-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The trawlers operating in the area are 30–45 m long and are able to land 55–65 tonnes of fish (usually
       <a href="/wiki/Redfish" title="Redfish">
        redfish
       </a>
       and club and scad
       <a href="/wiki/Mackerel" title="Mackerel">
        mackerel
       </a>
       ) per fishing trip. The tuna vessels range from smaller boats around 45–50 m in length, able to land 200–250 tonnes of catch, to larger versions with a length of 50–65 m and the ability to land up to 650 tonnes per fishing trip.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTAL_6-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-OTAL-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The largest fishing vessels, the deep-sea carriers (with lengths of 90–105 m), are mostly
       <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">
        chartered
       </a>
       vessels.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTAL_6-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-OTAL-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Since 1984, the national catch has averaged about 200,000 to 300,000 metric tonnes per annum. Ghana's tuna catch has maintained a stable level of 30,000 tonnes per annum since 1981.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTAL_6-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-OTAL-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Canoe Basin caters for the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artisanal" title="Artisanal">
        artisanal
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fishermen" title="Fishermen">
        fishermen
       </a>
       . The basin is normally occupied by about 400
       <a href="/wiki/Canoe" title="Canoe">
        canoes
       </a>
       . These are mainly two types: wooden vessels, locally called "Legelege", and metallic vessels. The wooden canoes have lengths over all (
       <a href="/wiki/Length_overall" title="Length overall">
        LOA
       </a>
       ) ranging between 30–70 m, mainly owned and operated by
       <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples" title="Indigenous peoples">
        indigenous
       </a>
       Ghanaians.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GPHA_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-GPHA-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Their operations peak during the months of June–September. Main catches during this period are
       <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">
        herrings
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">
        salmon
       </a>
       . Artisanal canoe fishing is responsible for about 70% of the catch.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GPHA_9-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-GPHA-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Education">
        Education
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       SOS Hermann Gmeiner International College (SOS-HGIC), a private mixed
       <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">
        boarding school
       </a>
       catering to the 10th to 13th grades, is located in Tema. It uses the
       <a href="/wiki/International_General_Certificate_of_Secondary_Education" title="International General Certificate of Secondary Education">
        International General Certificate of Secondary Education
       </a>
       (IGCSE) for the 10th and 11th grades and the
       <a href="/wiki/International_Baccalaureate" title="International Baccalaureate">
        International Baccalaureate
       </a>
       (IB) for the 12th and 13th grades. The school was headed by Margaret Nkrumah for over 15 years, and is now headed by Mr Israel Titi Ofei and Nii Amaa Akita. Tema also has an
       <a href="/wiki/International_school" title="International school">
        international school
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tema_International_School" title="Tema International School">
        Tema International School
       </a>
       (TIS), which is second to HGIC, and a senior high school, Tema Secondary School (TSS or Temasco), which was built in 22 September 1961. Tema has a number of public Secondary schools such as Chemu Senior High School in Community 4, Tema Methodist Day School, Mahean Senior High School, Our Lady of Mercy Senior High School andTema Technical Institute. Private Preparatory Schools like Creator Schools, St Paul Methodist Primary and JHS, Marbs Preparatory School, Datus School Complex, Deks Educational Institute, Angel School Complex, Naylor SDA School, Tema Christian Centre, Tema Parents Association, First Baptist School, Tema Regular Baptist School, Queen Esther School, Dorsons School, Adwen Memorial, Creator Montessori, New Covenant School, St Alban's School, Lorenz Wolf School, Bexhill School Complex, Life International School, Mazon Grace Academy, Santabarbera School, First Star Academy, Pentecost School, Star School Complex, Tema Ridge, St John Bosco School and Rosharon School. Public primary and JHSs exist in Tema. Twedaase Primary School, Star School, Aggrey Road School, Republic Road School, Padmore School, Mante Din Drive School among others.
      </p>
