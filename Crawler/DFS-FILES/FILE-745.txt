      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Preposition_and_postposition" title="Preposition and postposition">
        prepositions
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       —like prepositions in other languages—are a set of
       <a href="/wiki/Function_word" title="Function word">
        connecting words
       </a>
       (such as
       <i>
        con
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        de
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        para
       </i>
       ) that serve to indicate a relationship between a
       <a href="/wiki/Content_word" title="Content word">
        content word
       </a>
       (noun, verb, or adjective) and a following noun phrase (or noun, or pronoun), called the object of the preposition. The relationship is typically spatial or temporal, but prepositions express other relationships as well. As implied by the name, Spanish "pre-positions" (like those of English) are positioned
       <i>
        before
       </i>
       their objects. Spanish does not place these function words
       <i>
        after
       </i>
       their objects; the language does not use
       <a href="/wiki/Preposition_and_postposition" title="Preposition and postposition">
        postpositions
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Spanish prepositions can be classified as either "simple", consisting of a single word, or "compound", consisting of two or three words. The prepositions of Spanish form a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parts_of_speech#Open_and_closed_classes" title="Parts of speech">
        closed class
       </a>
       , meaning that they constitute a limited set to which new items are rarely added. Many Spanish school pupils memorize the following list:
       <i>
        a,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ante#Spanish" title="wikt:ante">
         ante
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/bajo#Spanish" title="wikt:bajo">
         bajo
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cabe#Spanish" title="wikt:cabe">
         cabe
        </a>
        , con,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/contra#Spanish" title="wikt:contra">
         contra
        </a>
        , de,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/desde#Spanish" title="wikt:desde">
         desde
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/durante#Spanish" title="wikt:durante">
         durante
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/en#Spanish" title="wikt:en">
         en
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/entre#Spanish" title="wikt:entre">
         entre
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hacia#Spanish" title="wikt:hacia">
         hacia
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hasta#Spanish" title="wikt:hasta">
         hasta
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mediante#Spanish" title="wikt:mediante">
         mediante
        </a>
        , para, por, según, sin,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/so#Spanish" title="wikt:so">
         so
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/sobre#Spanish" title="wikt:sobre">
         sobre
        </a>
        , and
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tras#Spanish" title="wikt:tras">
         tras
        </a>
        .
       </i>
       This list includes two
       <a href="/wiki/Archaism" title="Archaism">
        archaic
       </a>
       prepositions —
       <i>
        so
       </i>
       (“under”) and
       <i>
        cabe
       </i>
       (“beside”) — and it excludes
       <i>
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/v%C3%ADa#Spanish" title="wikt:vía">
         vía
        </a>
       </i>
       (“by way of, via”) and
       <i>
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/pro#Spanish" title="wikt:pro">
         pro
        </a>
       </i>
       (“in favor of”), two
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latinisms
       </a>
       recently adopted into the language.
      </p>
      <p>
       Some common Spanish prepositions, simple and compound, are listed below, with their meanings.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Some_frequent_simple_prepositions_in_Spanish">
        Some frequent simple prepositions in Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Some frequent simple prepositions in Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="a">
        <i>
         a
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: a">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <table class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" role="presentation" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">
       <tr>
        <td class="mbox-image">
         <img alt="" class="noviewer" data-file-height="391" data-file-width="391" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/40px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/60px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/80px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png 2x" width="40"/>
        </td>
        <td class="mbox-text plainlist">
         Look up
         <i>
          <b>
           <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/a#Spanish" title="wiktionary:a">
            a
           </a>
          </b>
         </i>
         in
         <a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">
          Wiktionary
         </a>
         , the free dictionary.
        </td>
       </tr>
      </table>
      <p>
       <i>
        A
       </i>
       is most often translated as "to" or "at"; its main uses are the following:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        It indicates movement to a destination:
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           Viajaron
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           Madrid.
          </i>
          = "They traveled to Madrid."
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           Llegaron
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           Madrid.
          </i>
          = "They arrived in Madrid."
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        It indicates a stationary point on a quantitative scale, as in telling time (but usually not a spatial location, which is normally expressed by
        <i>
         en
        </i>
        ):
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           Llegaron
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           las dos.
          </i>
          = "They arrived at two o'clock."
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           Se venden
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           dos dólares la libra.
          </i>
          = "They are sold at two dollars a pound."
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        It introduces indirect objects that Latin would have marked with the
        <a href="/wiki/Dative_case" title="Dative case">
         dative case
        </a>
        :
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           Le envié la carta
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           Ana.
          </i>
          = "I sent Ana the letter", "I sent the letter
          <b>
           to
          </b>
          Ana."
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           ¿Les regalaste el coche
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           tus padres?
          </i>
          = "Did you give your parents the car as a gift?", "Did you give the car
          <b>
           to
          </b>
          your parents (as a gift)?"
         </li>
         <li>
          Note that the indirect object pronoun forms
          <i>
           le
          </i>
          and
          <i>
           les
          </i>
          appear, even when the indirect object is given in full; see
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/Spanish_pronouns" title="Spanish pronouns">
            Spanish pronouns
           </a>
          </i>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        It introduces
        <a href="/wiki/Infinitive" title="Infinitive">
         infinitives
        </a>
        after several common verbs, including
        <i>
         ir
        </i>
        ("to be going to [do something]", a
        <a href="/wiki/Periphrasis" title="Periphrasis">
         periphrastic
        </a>
        future),
        <i>
         volver
        </i>
        ("to [do something] again"),
        <i>
         comenzar
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         empezar
        </i>
        (both "to begin"),
        <i>
         ayudar
        </i>
        ("to help"),
        <i>
         aprender
        </i>
        ("to learn"), and
        <i>
         enseñar
        </i>
        ("to teach"):
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           Voy
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           enfadarme.
          </i>
          = "I am going
          <b>
           to
          </b>
          become angry."
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           Aprende no sólo
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           hablar sino también
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           escribir el castellano.
          </i>
          = "Do not just learn
          <b>
           to
          </b>
          speak Spanish, but also
          <b>
           to
          </b>
          write it", "Learn not just
          <b>
           to
          </b>
          speak, but also
          <b>
           to
          </b>
          write Spanish."
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        It introduces a direct object referring either to a person or a
        <a href="/wiki/Anthropomorphism" title="Anthropomorphism">
         personalised
        </a>
        thing (pet, organization, vehicle):
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           Veo
           <b>
            a
           </b>
           María.
          </i>
          = "I see María."
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           Te quieren ver
           <b>
            a
           </b>
          </i>
          ti
          <i>
           .
          </i>
          = "They want to see
          <i>
           you
          </i>
          ."
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        The preposition
        <i>
         <b>
          a
         </b>
        </i>
        is a component of many
        <a href="#Compound_prepositions">
         compound prepositions
        </a>
        , detailed in Section 2.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <b>
        Prepositional contraction:
       </b>
       <i>
        al
       </i>
       (“to the”, “to”) is the contraction formed with
       <i>
        a
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        el
       </i>
       (“the”), the masculine definite article, yet the contraction is waived when the article is part of a proper noun:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Voy
         <b>
          al
         </b>
         país de mis sueños
        </i>
        = "I am going to the country of my dreams."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Lo voy a mandar
         <b>
          a
         </b>
        </i>
        <b>
         El
        </b>
        País = "I am going to send it to [the newspaper]
        <i>
         El País
        </i>
        .”
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="con">
        <i>
         con
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: con">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <table class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" role="presentation" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">
       <tr>
        <td class="mbox-image">
         <img alt="" class="noviewer" data-file-height="391" data-file-width="391" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/40px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/60px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/80px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png 2x" width="40"/>
        </td>
        <td class="mbox-text plainlist">
         Look up
         <i>
          <b>
           <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/con#Spanish" title="wiktionary:con">
            con
           </a>
          </b>
         </i>
         in
         <a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">
          Wiktionary
         </a>
         , the free dictionary.
        </td>
       </tr>
      </table>
      <p>
       <i>
        Con
       </i>
       is usually translated by English "with", both in the sense of accompaniment (
       <i>
        con mi hermano
       </i>
       , "with my brother") and in the
       <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_case" title="Instrumental case">
        instrumental
       </a>
       sense (
       <i>
        con un martillo
       </i>
       , "with a hammer"). Unlike other prepositions,
       <i>
        con
       </i>
       combines with the prepositional pronouns
       <i>
        mí
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        ti
       </i>
       , and
       <i>
        sí
       </i>
       in the forms
       <i>
        conmigo
       </i>
       (“with me”),
       <i>
        contigo
       </i>
       (“with you”), and
       <i>
        consigo
       </i>
       (“with her-, himself”). These forms are derived historically from forms with the Latin preposition
       <i>
        cum
       </i>
       postposed to its object:
       <i>
        mēcum
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        tēcum
       </i>
       , etc. In an
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
        Ibero-Romance
       </a>
       ancestor of Spanish, before the time of written records, an etymologically redundant
       <i>
        con
       </i>
       was prefixed to these forms. Compare the concept of
       <a href="/wiki/Inflected_preposition" title="Inflected preposition">
        inflected preposition
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Ven conmigo y con él ahora
        </i>
        = "Come with me and him now."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Iré a la fiesta contigo
        </i>
        = "I will go to the party with you."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Es raro llevar un billete de 200€ consigo
        </i>
        = "It is unusual to carry a €200 note on oneself."
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="de">
        <i>
         de
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: de">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <table class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" role="presentation" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">
       <tr>
        <td class="mbox-image">
         <img alt="" class="noviewer" data-file-height="391" data-file-width="391" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/40px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/60px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg/80px-Wiktionary-logo-en-v2.svg.png 2x" width="40"/>
        </td>
        <td class="mbox-text plainlist">
         Look up
         <i>
          <b>
           <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/de#Spanish" title="wiktionary:de">
            de
           </a>
          </b>
         </i>
         in
         <a href="/wiki/Wiktionary" title="Wiktionary">
          Wiktionary
         </a>
         , the free dictionary.
        </td>
       </tr>
      </table>
      <p>
       <i>
        De
       </i>
       is the most frequent preposition in Spanish, and in fact it vies with
       <i>
        que
       </i>
       to be the most frequent
       <i>
        word
       </i>
       in the language. It is most often translated in English by "of" or "from", but also denotes several other relationships as well.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Es la más famosa de todas
        </i>
        = "She is the most famous [one] of all [of them]."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Soy de Madrid
        </i>
        = "I am from Madrid."
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The English possessive with apostrophe-
       <i>
        s
       </i>
       is translated by a construction with
       <i>
        de
       </i>
       :
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         La hermana de David
        </i>
        = "David's sister."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Ese libro es del profesor
        </i>
        = "That book is the teacher's."
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <b>
        Prepositional contraction:
       </b>
       When
       <i>
        de
       </i>
       is followed by the masculine singular
       <a href="/wiki/Article_(grammar)" title="Article (grammar)">
        definite article
       </a>
       <i>
        el
       </i>
       (“the”), together they form the contraction
       <i>
        del
       </i>
       (“of the”). However,
       <i>
        de
       </i>
       does not contract with the
       <a href="/wiki/Homophone" title="Homophone">
        homophonous
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Pronoun" title="Pronoun">
        personal pronoun
       </a>
       <i>
        él
       </i>
       ("him"), nor, in writing, with a proper noun; thus:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Soy pariente
         <b>
          del
         </b>
         alcalde
         <b>
          de
         </b>
         <a href="/wiki/El_Escorial" title="El Escorial">
          El Escorial
         </a>
        </i>
        , "I am a relative of the mayor of El Escorial."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Soy pariente
         <b>
          de él
         </b>
         , "I am a relative of his."
        </i>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       <b>
        Typography:
       </b>
       the uppercase form
       <small>
        DE
       </small>
       was configured as the
       <a href="/wiki/Scribal_abbreviation" title="Scribal abbreviation">
        siglum
       </a>
       <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/D_with_stroke_(disambiguation)" title="D with stroke (disambiguation)">
        Đ
       </a>
       — a
       <a href="/wiki/Typographic_ligature" title="Typographic ligature">
        typographic ligature
       </a>
       adopted as a concise written and printed word-character, that originated as a
       <a href="/wiki/Lapidary" title="Lapidary">
        lapidary
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Scribal_abbreviation" title="Scribal abbreviation">
        scribal abbreviation
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The preposition
       <i>
        de
       </i>
       is part of many compound prepositions, such as
       <i>
        dentro de
       </i>
       (“within”, “inside of”) and
       <i>
        en contra de
       </i>
       (“against . . .”); see Section 2, below, for fuller description.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="por_and_para">
        <i>
         por
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         para
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: por and para">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Both
       <i>
        por
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        para
       </i>
       are frequently translated into English as "for", and thus they pose a challenge for English-speaking learners of Spanish. In the broadest terms,
       <i>
        por
       </i>
       denotes cause or stimulus (with a retrospective focus), while
       <i>
        para
       </i>
       denotes destination or purpose (with a prospective focus). The following are common uses of these prepositions:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         por
        </i>
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        "somewhere in", a diffuse location in space or time:
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Lo perdí por ahí.
          </i>
          = "I lost it thereabout, somewhere around there."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "in exchange for, in place of"
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Cambié mis euros por dólares.
          </i>
          = "I exchanged my euros into dollars."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "per" (day, hour, mile, etc.)
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Pagan un euro por hora.
          </i>
          = "They pay one euro per hour."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "by means of", "by way of"
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Es más rápido por la autopista.
          </i>
          = "It is faster by the motorway."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "because of" (compare
        <i>
         porque
        </i>
        , "because")
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Me multaron por exceso de velocidad.
          </i>
          = "They fined me for speeding."
         </dd>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Mi jefe está enfermo y por eso tengo que trabajar
          </i>
          = "My boss is sick, and therefore I have to work."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "for the sake of", "for the benefit of"
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Todo lo que hago, lo hago por ti.
          </i>
          = "Everything I do, I do [it] for you."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "in favor of"
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Yo voto por el partido de derecha.
          </i>
          = "I vote for the right-wing party."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "by" (the agent of a passive construction)
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           La nueva ley fue mal redactada por el partido gobernante
          </i>
          = "The new law was badly written by the governing party."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "for" (a period of time; often replaced by
        <i>
         durante
        </i>
        )
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Vivieron en Nueva York por tres meses.
          </i>
          = "They lived in New York for three months."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <i>
         para
        </i>
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <ul>
       <li>
        "intended for" (a purpose or recipient); "so that" (with a clause of purpose)
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           dinero para pagar el café
          </i>
          = "money to pay for coffee"
         </dd>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Estas flores son para ti.
          </i>
          = "These flowers are for you."
         </dd>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Lo lavé para que lo guardaras.
          </i>
          = "I washed it so you could keep it."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "toward" (a destination; informal, replaces
        <i>
         a
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         hacia
        </i>
        )
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Voy para el sur.
          </i>
          = "I am going [to the] south."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "by" (a certain time)
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Para esta época del año siempre llueve.
          </i>
          = "By this time of the year, it always rains."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "in order to"
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Fuimos a la tienda para comprar tortillas.
          </i>
          = "We went to the store to buy tortillas."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "for, considering that..." (to express a comparison)
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Para una persona tan joven, se queja demasiado.
          </i>
          = "For such a young person, he complains too much."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
       <li>
        "about to" (in the expression
        <i>
         estar para
        </i>
        , "to be about to [do something]")
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Yo estaba para salir, cuando sonó el teléfono
          </i>
          = "I was about to leave, when the telephone rang."
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       In fast spoken language, the preposition
       <i>
        para
       </i>
       often is
       <a href="/wiki/Clipping_(phonetics)" title="Clipping (phonetics)">
        clipped
       </a>
       to
       <i>
        pa
       </i>
       , as in the
       <a href="/wiki/Colloquialism" title="Colloquialism">
        colloquial
       </a>
       <i>
        Amos p'alante.
       </i>
       (“Let’s go forward.”)—compare the
       <a href="/wiki/Standard_language" title="Standard language">
        standard
       </a>
       <i>
        Vamos para adelante.
       </i>
       (“Let us go forward.")
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="seg.C3.BAn">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="según">
        <i>
         según
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: según">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <i>
        Según
       </i>
       translates as "according to". With some uses of
       <i>
        según
       </i>
       , part or all of the object of the preposition is omitted and merely implied. Often the missing words can be taken as
       <i>
        lo que
       </i>
       ("what"):
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Según dice, es un buen libro
        </i>
        . "According to what he says (according to him), it is a good book."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Según convenga
        </i>
        . "As may be required."
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Popular speech uses it alone, as an equivalent of "It depends."
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Q:
        <i>
         ¿Te gusta el cine francés?
        </i>
        ("Do you like French cinema?")
       </li>
      </ul>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        A:
        <i>
         Según.
        </i>
        ("It depends.")
       </dd>
      </dl>
      <p>
       Regional colloquial usage of the preposition
       <i>
        según
       </i>
       , with
       <i>
        que
       </i>
       , expresses
       <a href="/wiki/Evidentiality" title="Evidentiality">
        evidential mood
       </a>
       , indicating hearsay or non-commitment ("supposedly", "it is said").
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Según que tiene SIDA.
        </i>
        "They say that he has AIDS."
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="sin">
        <i>
         sin
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: sin">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <i>
        Sin
       </i>
       translates as "without":
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Un té sin leche, por favor
        </i>
        = "A tea without milk, please."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Se metió en la cama sin despertarla
        </i>
        = "He got in bed without waking her."
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       When the object of the preposition
       <i>
        sin
       </i>
       is a clause introduced by
       <i>
        que
       </i>
       (alternatively interpreted as a compound conjunction,
       <i>
        sin que
       </i>
       ), verb in the clause must be in the
       <a href="/wiki/Subjunctive_mood" title="Subjunctive mood">
        subjunctive mood
       </a>
       :
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Se metió en la cama sin que se despertara
        </i>
        = "He got in bed without her waking up."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         No se puede poner a esos niños en la misma habitación sin que se peleen.
        </i>
        = "You cannot put those children in the same room without their fighting."
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Los ladrones entraron sin que los notase nadie
        </i>
        = "The thieves entered without anyone noticing them."
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Compound_prepositions">
        Compound prepositions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Compound prepositions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Some compound prepositions duplicate the meaning of a simple preposition, but often with a more formal tone or with greater specificity. For example,
       <i>
        de acuerdo con
       </i>
       ("in accordance with") is equivalent to
       <i>
        según
       </i>
       ("according to").
       <i>
        En dirección a
       </i>
       ("in the direction of") is more ponderous than
       <i>
        hacia
       </i>
       ("toward"). The English counterpart of Spanish
       <i>
        en
       </i>
       may be either "on" or "in", while
       <i>
        dentro de
       </i>
       specifies "within". "Because of" is only one of several possible meanings of
       <i>
        por
       </i>
       , but
       <i>
        por causa de
       </i>
       conveys that meaning exclusively. In some cases the compound preposition denotes a literal spatial relationship, while the corresponding simple preposition expresses a figurative version of that relationship: thus,
       <i>
        <b>
         debajo de
        </b>
        una mesa
       </i>
       ("under a table") vs.
       <i>
        <b>
         bajo
        </b>
        un régimen
       </i>
       ("under a regime"), or
       <i>
        <b>
         delante de
        </b>
        un edificio
       </i>
       ("in front of a building") vs.
       <i>
        <b>
         ante
        </b>
        un tribunal
       </i>
       ("before a court of law").
      </p>
      <p>
       The list of compound prepositions is much longer than that of the simple ones, and only some representative examples are listed here.
      </p>
      <p>
       Spanish compound prepositions can be composed of:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        a preposition + noun + preposition:
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           por causa de
          </i>
          /
          <i>
           a causa de
          </i>
          /
          <i>
           en razón de
          </i>
          = "because of"
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           sin perjuicio de
          </i>
          = "notwithstanding", "without prejudice to"
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           con respecto a
          </i>
          = "with respect to", "regarding"
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           a favor de
          </i>
          = "in favour of"
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           en contra de
          </i>
          = "against . . ." (e.g.
          <i>
           en mi contra
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           en tu contra
          </i>
          ,
          <i>
           en su contra
          </i>
          , etc.)
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           en lugar de
          </i>
          /
          <i>
           en vez de
          </i>
          = "instead of", "in
          <i>
           lieu
          </i>
          of"
         </li>
         <li>
          etc.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        or of an adverb + preposition:
        <ul>
         <li>
          <i>
           después de
          </i>
          = "after"
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           debajo de
          </i>
          = "beneath", “underneath”
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           antes de
          </i>
          = "before" (i.e. “prior to”)
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           junto a
          </i>
          = "beside", "alongside"
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           delante de
          </i>
          = "in front of" and “[positioned] before”
         </li>
         <li>
          etc.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Other Spanish compound prepositions include the following:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         tras de
        </i>
        = "behind"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         a fuerza de
        </i>
        = "by dint of"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         junto a
        </i>
        = "next to"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         encima de
        </i>
        = "above"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         detrás de
        </i>
        = "behind"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         en medio de
        </i>
        = "in the middle of"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         en pos de
        </i>
        = "in pursuit of"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         con rumbo a
        </i>
        = "headed for"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         con destino a
        </i>
        = "on the way to"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         a través de
        </i>
        = "across"
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Serial_prepositions">
        Serial prepositions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Serial prepositions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In certain cases, Spanish prepositions can be used serially, that is, two—or occasionally even three—in succession, as in the following examples:
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="a_por">
        <i>
         a por
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: a por">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In Spain the sequence
       <i>
        a por
       </i>
       , used mainly with verbs of movement, such as
       <i>
        ir
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        salir
       </i>
       , can be used to mean "in search of", or "to go fetch [something]". Many speakers consider it to be incorrect and prefer to replace it with
       <i>
        por
       </i>
       alone, but according to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Real Academia Española">
        Real Academia Española
       </a>
       , there is no
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_prescription" title="Linguistic prescription">
        normative
       </a>
       reason to condemn the use of
       <i>
        a por
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In some contexts,
       <i>
        a por
       </i>
       expresses a clearer meaning than
       <i>
        por
       </i>
       :
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Subí por la escalera
        </i>
        = "I went up the stairs" or "I went up the ladder"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Subí a por la escalera
        </i>
        = "I went up for the ladder"
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="para_con">
        <i>
         para con
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: para con">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       This compound means "toward" in the context of an attitude or demeanor toward someone or something:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Es muy generoso para con los necesitados
        </i>
        , He is very generous toward/with the needy".
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         No tengo pruritos para con ellos
        </i>
        , "I have nothing against them".
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Other possible serial combinations of prepositions include the following:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         por en medio de
        </i>
        = "through the midst of"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         por delante de
        </i>
        = "somewhere in front of"
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Translating_English_postpositions_into_Spanish">
        Translating English postpositions into Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_prepositions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Translating English postpositions into Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">
        English language
       </a>
       features three types of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postpositions" title="Postpositions">
        adpositions
       </a>
       — prepositions (preceding), postpositions (following), and circumpositions (enclosing) — allowing constructions such as “
       <u>
        in
       </u>
       the box”, “
       <u>
        on
       </u>
       the airplane”, and “
       <u>
        out of
       </u>
       Africa”, as in Spanish. But the postposition “three years
       <u>
        ago
       </u>
       ” is as impossible in Spanish usage as: “ago three years” is in English. Thus Spanish prepositions function exclusively as such; these examples express equivalent concepts using other mechanisms:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         <u>
          Hace
         </u>
         tres años
        </i>
        = “three years
        <u>
         ago
        </u>
        ” (“It makes three years.”)
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         <u>
          Dentro de
         </u>
         tres años
        </i>
        = “three years
        <u>
         hence
        </u>
        ” (“Within [a period] of three years.”)
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         <u>
          A
         </u>
         tres kilómetros
        </i>
        = “three kilometres
        <u>
         away
        </u>
        ” (“To/Some three kilometres [from here].”)
       </li>
      </ul>
