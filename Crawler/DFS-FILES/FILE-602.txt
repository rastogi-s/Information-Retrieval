      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Sahrawi Republic
       </b>
       , officially the
       <b>
        Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        SADR
       </b>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         República Árabe Saharaui Democrática
        </span>
       </i>
       ;
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
        Arabic
       </a>
       :
       <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        الجمهورية العربية الصحراوية الديمقراطية
       </span>
       ‎
       <i>
        al-Jumhūrīyah al-‘Arabīyah aṣ-Ṣaḥrāwīyah ad-Dīmuqrāṭīyah
       </i>
       ), is a
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_states_with_limited_recognition" title="List of states with limited recognition">
        partially recognized state
       </a>
       that controls a thin strip of area in the
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">
        Western Sahara
       </a>
       region and claims sovereignty over the entire territory of Western Sahara, a former
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spanish
       </a>
       colony and later province. SADR was proclaimed by the
       <a href="/wiki/Polisario_Front" title="Polisario Front">
        Polisario Front
       </a>
       on February 27, 1976, in
       <a href="/wiki/Bir_Lehlou" title="Bir Lehlou">
        Bir Lehlou
       </a>
       , Western Sahara, a former socialist liberation force which has since reformed its ideological and political views.
      </p>
      <p>
       The SADR government controls about 20–25% of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Territory_(country_subdivision)" title="Territory (country subdivision)">
        territory
       </a>
       it claims.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It calls the territories under its control the Liberated Territories or the
       <a href="/wiki/Free_Zone_(region)" title="Free Zone (region)">
        Free Zone
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">
        Morocco
       </a>
       controls and administers the rest of the disputed territory and calls these lands its
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Provinces" title="Southern Provinces">
        Southern Provinces
       </a>
       . The SADR government considers the Moroccan-held territory to be occupied territory, while Morocco considers the much smaller SADR-held territory to be a
       <a href="/wiki/Buffer_zone" title="Buffer zone">
        buffer zone
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The claimed
       <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">
        capital
       </a>
       of the SADR is
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El-Aai%C3%BAn" title="El-Aaiún">
        El-Aaiún
       </a>
       , while the
       <a href="/wiki/Temporary_capital" title="Temporary capital">
        temporary capital
       </a>
       has been moved from Bir Lehlou to
       <a href="/wiki/Tifariti" title="Tifariti">
        Tifariti
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Sahrawi Republic maintains diplomatic relations with 40 UN states, and is a full member of the
       <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">
        African Union
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/History_of_Western_Sahara" title="History of Western Sahara">
        History of Western Sahara
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Following the Spanish evacuation of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Sahara" title="Spanish Sahara">
        Spanish Sahara
       </a>
       , Spain, Morocco, and
       <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">
        Mauritania
       </a>
       signed the
       <a href="/wiki/Madrid_Accords" title="Madrid Accords">
        Madrid Accords
       </a>
       on November 14, 1975, leading to both Morocco and Mauritania moving in to annex the territory of Western Sahara. On 26 February 1976, Spain informed the
       <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">
        United Nations
       </a>
       that as of that date it had terminated its presence in Western Sahara and relinquished its responsibilities, leaving no Administering Power.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Neither Morocco nor Mauritania gained international recognition, and war ensued with the independence-seeking
       <a href="/wiki/Polisario_Front" title="Polisario Front">
        Polisario Front
       </a>
       . The United Nations considers the Polisario Front to be the legitimate representative of the
       <a href="/wiki/Sahrawi_people" title="Sahrawi people">
        Sahrawi people
       </a>
       , and maintains that the people of Western Sahara have a right to "
       <a href="/wiki/Self-determination" title="Self-determination">
        self-determination
       </a>
       and independence."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The creation of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic was proclaimed on February 27, 1976, as the Polisario declared the need for a new entity to fill what they considered a political void left by the departing Spanish colonisers. While the claimed capital is El-Aaiún, in Moroccan-controlled territory, the proclamation was made in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government-in-exile" title="Government-in-exile">
        government-in-exile
       </a>
       's provisional capital,
       <a href="/wiki/Bir_Lehlou" title="Bir Lehlou">
        Bir Lehlou
       </a>
       , which remained in Polisario-held territory under the 1991
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cease-fire" title="Cease-fire">
        cease-fire
       </a>
       (see
       <a href="/wiki/Settlement_Plan" title="Settlement Plan">
        Settlement Plan
       </a>
       ). On February 27, 2008, the provisional capital was formally moved to
       <a href="/wiki/Tifariti" title="Tifariti">
        Tifariti
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Day-to-day business, however, is conducted in the
       <a href="/wiki/Tindouf_Province" title="Tindouf Province">
        Tindouf
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Refugee_camp" title="Refugee camp">
        refugee camps
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">
        Algeria
       </a>
       , which house most of the Sahrawi exile community.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Constitution">
        Constitution
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Constitution">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Constitution of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">
        Constitution of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       A new 1999 Constitution of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic took a form similar to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentarism" title="Parliamentarism">
        parliamentary
       </a>
       constitutions of many European states, but with some paragraphs suspended until the achievement of "full independence". Among key points, the
       <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">
        head of state
       </a>
       is constitutionally the Secretary General of the Polisario Front during what is referred to as the "pre-independence phase," with provision in the constitution that on independence, Polisario is supposed to be dismantled or separated completely from the government structure. Provisions are detailed for a transitory phase beginning with independence, in which the present SADR is supposed to act as Western Sahara's government, ending with a constitutional reform and eventual establishment of a state along the lines specified in the constitution.
      </p>
      <p>
       The broad guidelines laid down for an eventual Western Saharan state in the constitution include eventual multi-party democracy with a
       <a href="/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">
        market economy
       </a>
       . The constitution also defines Sahrawis as a Muslim, African and Arab people.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Constitution also declares a commitment to the principles of
       <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">
        human rights
       </a>
       and to the concept of a
       <a href="/wiki/Arab_Maghreb_Union" title="Arab Maghreb Union">
        Greater Maghreb
       </a>
       , as a regional variant of
       <a href="/wiki/Pan-Arabism" title="Pan-Arabism">
        Pan-Arabism
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <p>
       Since August 1982, the highest office of the republic is the
       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="President of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">
        President of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic
       </a>
       , a post held by the secretary-general of the Polisario, presently
       <a href="/wiki/Brahim_Ghali" title="Brahim Ghali">
        Brahim Ghali
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zunes_2010_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Zunes_2010-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       who appoints the
       <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Prime Minister of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">
        Prime Minister
       </a>
       , presently
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdelkader_Taleb_Oumar" title="Abdelkader Taleb Oumar">
        Abdelkader Taleb Oumar
       </a>
       . The SADR's government structure consists of a Council of Ministers (a cabinet led by the Prime Minister), a judicial branch (with judges appointed by the President) and the parliamentary
       <a href="/wiki/Sahrawi_National_Council" title="Sahrawi National Council">
        Sahrawi National Council
       </a>
       (SNC, present
       <a href="/wiki/Speaker_(politics)" title="Speaker (politics)">
        speaker
       </a>
       is
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kathri_Aduh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kathri Aduh (page does not exist)">
        Kathri Aduh
       </a>
       ). Since its inception in 1976, the various
       <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Constitution of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">
        constitutional revisions
       </a>
       have transformed the republic from an ad hoc managerial structure into something approaching an actual governing apparatus. From the late 1980s the parliament began to take steps to institute a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_of_powers" title="Division of powers">
        division of powers
       </a>
       and disentangle the republic's structures from those of the Polisario party, although without clear effect to date.
      </p>
      <p>
       Its various ministries are responsible for a variety of services and functions. The
       <a href="/wiki/Judiciary" title="Judiciary">
        judiciary
       </a>
       , complete with trial courts,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Appeals_court" title="Appeals court">
        appeals courts
       </a>
       and a
       <a href="/wiki/Supreme_court" title="Supreme court">
        supreme court
       </a>
       , operates in the same areas. As a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government-in-exile" title="Government-in-exile">
        government-in-exile
       </a>
       , many branches of government do not fully function, and has affected the constitutional roles of the institutions. Institutions parallel to government structures also have arisen within the Polisario Front, which is fused with the SADR's governing apparatus, and with operational competences overlapping between these party and governmental institutions and offices. A 2012 report mentioned the existence of the Sahrawi Bar Association.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2016, the bar association (going by the name Union of Sahrawi Lawyers) issued a report calling for the implementation of political and civil rights.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Unfortunately, there is no clear indication as to certain demographic groups, such as women, have fared in the legal field.
      </p>
      <p>
       The SNC is weak in its legislative role, having been instituted as a mainly consultative and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consensus" title="Consensus">
        consensus
       </a>
       -building institution, but it has strengthened its theoretical legislative and controlling powers during later constitutional revisions. Among other things, it has added a ban on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_penalty" title="Death penalty">
        death penalty
       </a>
       to the constitution, and brought down the government in 1999 through a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vote_of_no-confidence" title="Vote of no-confidence">
        vote of no-confidence
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Area_of_authority">
        Area of authority
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Area of authority">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The SADR acted as a government administration in the Sahrawi
       <a href="/wiki/Refugee_camp" title="Refugee camp">
        refugee camps
       </a>
       located in the
       <a href="/wiki/Tindouf_Province" title="Tindouf Province">
        Tindouf Province
       </a>
       of western Algeria. It is headquartered in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Refugee_camps_in_Tindouf_Province,_Algeria" title="Refugee camps in Tindouf Province, Algeria">
        Camp Rabouni
       </a>
       , south of
       <a href="/wiki/Tindouf" title="Tindouf">
        Tindouf
       </a>
       , although some official events have taken place in
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Western_Sahara" title="List of cities in Western Sahara">
        towns
       </a>
       in the Free Zone, including the provisional capitals, first
       <a href="/wiki/Bir_Lehlou" title="Bir Lehlou">
        Bir Lehlou
       </a>
       until 2008, then Tifariti. The government of the SADR claims sovereignty over all of the Western Sahara territory, but has control only within the Free Zone. Several
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_aid" title="Foreign aid">
        foreign aid
       </a>
       agencies, including the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN_High_Commission_for_Refugees" title="UN High Commission for Refugees">
        UN High Commission for Refugees
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nongovernmental_organization" title="Nongovernmental organization">
        nongovernmental organizations
       </a>
       are continually active in the camps.
      </p>
      <h2>

       <span class="mw-editsection">

      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">

       <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_the_Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Foreign relations of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">
        Foreign relations of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       As of 2017, the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic has been recognized by 85 states. Of these, 39 have since "frozen" or "withdrawn" recognition for a number of reasons. A total of 40 UN states maintain diplomatic relations with the SADR, while a further 7 also recognise the state.
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_of_the_Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="List of diplomatic missions of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">
        Sahrawi embassies exist in 18 states
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Although it is not recognised by the United Nations, the SADR has held full membership of the
       <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">
        African Union
       </a>
       (AU, formerly the
       <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_African_Unity" title="Organisation of African Unity">
        Organisation of African Unity
       </a>
       , OAU) since 1982. Morocco withdrew from the OAU in protest during 1984 and was the only African United Nations member not a member of the AU since
       <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
        South Africa
       </a>
       's admittance in 1994 until it was readmitted on 30 January 2017.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldbulletin_15-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-worldbulletin-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The SADR participates as guest on meetings of the
       <a href="/wiki/16th_Summit_of_the_Non-Aligned_Movement" title="16th Summit of the Non-Aligned Movement">
        Non-Aligned Movement
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       or the
       <a href="/wiki/New_Asian%E2%80%93African_Strategic_Partnership" title="New Asian–African Strategic Partnership">

       </a>

       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NAASP_18-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-NAASP-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Asian-African_Strategic_Partnership_19-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Asian-African_Strategic_Partnership-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       over Moroccan objections to SADR participation.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Asian-African_Strategic_Partnership_2_20-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Asian-African_Strategic_Partnership_2-20">

       <a href="/wiki/James_Baker" title="James Baker">
        James Baker
       </a>
       , former UN Secretary-General
       <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">
        Kofi Annan
       </a>
       's personal envoy to Western Sahara, the SADR would have been replaced with a five-year transitional
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Sahara_Authority" title="Western Sahara Authority">
        Western Sahara Authority
       </a>
       (WSA), a non-sovereign
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_region" title="Autonomous region">
        autonomous region
       </a>
       supervised by Morocco, to be followed by a referendum on independence. It was endorsed by the UN in 2003. However, as Morocco has declined to participate, the plan appears dead.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In April 2007, the government of Morocco suggested that a self-governing entity, through the
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Advisory_Council_for_Saharan_Affairs" title="Royal Advisory Council for Saharan Affairs">
        Royal Advisory Council for Saharan Affairs
       </a>
       (CORCAS), should govern the territory with some degree of autonomy for Western Sahara. The project was presented to the United Nations Security Council in mid-April 2007. A stalemate over the Moroccan proposal led the UN in an April 2007 "Report of the UN Secretary-General" to ask the parties to enter into direct and unconditional negotiations to reach a mutually accepted political solution.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN_28-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-UN-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
