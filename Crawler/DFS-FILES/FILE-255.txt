      <p>
       The
       <b>
        60th parallel north
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">
        circle of latitude
       </a>
       that is 60
       <a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">
        degrees
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/True_north" title="True north">
        north
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equator
       </a>
       . It crosses
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
        Asia
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Although it lies approximately twice as far away from the Equator as from the
       <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">
        North Pole
       </a>
       , the 60th parallel is half as long as the Equator line. This is where the Earth bulges halfway as much as on the Equator.
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude, the
       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">
        Sun
       </a>
       is visible for 18 hours, 52 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/June_solstice" title="June solstice">
        June solstice
       </a>
       and 5 hours, 52 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/December_solstice" title="December solstice">
        December solstice
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The maximum
       <a href="/wiki/Horizontal_coordinate_system" title="Horizontal coordinate system">
        altitude
       </a>
       of the Sun is 53.44° on 21 June and 6.56° on 21 December.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:60th_parallel_Canada.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="650" data-file-width="600" height="238" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/60th_parallel_Canada.svg/220px-60th_parallel_Canada.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/60th_parallel_Canada.svg/330px-60th_parallel_Canada.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/60th_parallel_Canada.svg/440px-60th_parallel_Canada.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:60th_parallel_Canada.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The 60th parallel north in Canada, marking the southern borders of
         <a href="/wiki/Yukon" title="Yukon">
          Yukon
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Territories" title="Northwest Territories">
          Northwest Territories
         </a>
         , and the
         <a href="/wiki/Nunavut" title="Nunavut">
          Nunavut
         </a>
         mainland.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       , the 60th parallel constitutes the mainland boundary between the
       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Canada" title="Northern Canada">
        northern territories
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Yukon" title="Yukon">
        Yukon
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Territories" title="Northwest Territories">
        Northwest Territories
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Nunavut" title="Nunavut">
        Nunavut
       </a>
       to the north, and the
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Canada" title="Western Canada">
        western
       </a>
       provinces of
       <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">
        British Columbia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">
        Alberta
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">
        Saskatchewan
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">
        Manitoba
       </a>
       to the south.
      </p>
      <p>
       Accordingly, "north of 60" is an expression often used for the territories, although parts of Nunavut (the islands in
       <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Bay" title="Hudson Bay">
        Hudson Bay
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/James_Bay" title="James Bay">
        James Bay
       </a>
       ) are located south of the 60th parallel, and parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">
        Quebec
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">
        Newfoundland and Labrador
       </a>
       are located north, to the east of Hudson Bay. A 1990s TV show on
       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Canadian Broadcasting Corporation">
        CBC
       </a>
       about life in the
       <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Territories" title="Northwest Territories">
        Northwest Territories
       </a>
       was called
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/North_of_60" title="North of 60">
         North of 60
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Canada's only
       <a href="/wiki/Four_corners_(Canada)" title="Four corners (Canada)">
        four corners
       </a>
       are located at the intersection of the 60th parallel and the
       <a href="/wiki/102nd_meridian_west" title="102nd meridian west">
        102nd meridian west
       </a>
       , between the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, Saskatchewan, and Manitoba. However, this is not a true
       <a href="/wiki/Quadripoint" title="Quadripoint">
        quadripoint
       </a>
       as the measurement of the Saskatchewan/Manitoba border
       <a href="/wiki/Dominion_Land_Survey#Meridians_and_baselines" title="Dominion Land Survey">
        in the 1880s
       </a>
       placed it approximately 400 metres (440 yd) west of the 102nd meridian, which defines part of the Northwest Territories/Nunavut border.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Greenland">
        Greenland
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=60th_parallel_north&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Greenland">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Between 1776 and 1950, the 60th parallel formed the southern limit of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Greenland_Trade_Department" title="Royal Greenland Trade Department">
        Royal Greenland Trade Department
       </a>
       's exclusive monopoly on trade near the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denmark-Norway" title="Denmark-Norway">
        Dano-Norwegian
       </a>
       and later
       <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">
        Danish
       </a>
       colonies of
       <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">
        Greenland
       </a>
       (1776–1782) and
       <a href="/wiki/South_Greenland" title="South Greenland">
        South Greenland
       </a>
       (1782–1950).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ole!_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ole!-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
