      <p>
       The
       <b>
        meridian 75° west of
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Meridian" title="Prime Meridian">
         Greenwich
        </a>
       </b>
       is a line of
       <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">
        longitude
       </a>
       that extends from the
       <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">
        North Pole
       </a>
       across the
       <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">
        Arctic Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">
        Caribbean Sea
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">
        South America
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">
        Southern Ocean
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">
        Antarctica
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">
        South Pole
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The mean solar time of this meridian is the base for the
       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">
        Eastern Time Zone
       </a>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC-5" title="UTC-5">
        UTC-5
       </a>
       during standard time).
      </p>
      <p>
       Stations belonging to the US
       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">
        National Weather Service
       </a>
       begin submitting weather reports when the mean solar time of this meridian is 8:00 am. Report collection ends 30–40 minutes later and the data is used to create the day's weather forecast.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moore1910p217_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Moore1910p217-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The 75th meridian west forms a
       <a href="/wiki/Great_circle" title="Great circle">
        great circle
       </a>
       with the
       <a href="/wiki/105th_meridian_east" title="105th meridian east">
        105th meridian east
       </a>
       .
      </p>
