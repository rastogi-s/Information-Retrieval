      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Cervantes Institute
       </b>
       is a worldwide
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" title="Non-profit organization">
        non-profit organization
       </a>
       created by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_government" title="Spanish government">
        Spanish government
       </a>
       in 1991.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is named after
       <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_Cervantes" title="Miguel de Cervantes">
        Miguel de Cervantes
       </a>
       (1547–1616), the author of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Don_Quixote" title="Don Quixote">
         Don Quixote
        </a>
       </i>
       and perhaps the most important figure in the history of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_literature" title="Spanish literature">
        Spanish literature
       </a>
       . The Cervantes Institute, a government agency, is the largest organization in the world responsible for promoting the study and the teaching of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_culture" title="Spanish culture">
        culture
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This organization has branched out in over 20 different countries with 54 centres devoted to the Spanish and Hispanic American culture and Spanish Language.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ciweb_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ciweb-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Article 3 of Law 7/1991, created by the Instituto Cervantes on March 21, explains that the ultimate goals of the Institute are to promote the education, the study and the use of Spanish universally as a second language, to support the methods and activities that would help the process of Spanish language education, and to contribute to the advancement of the Spanish and Hispanic American cultures throughout non-Spanish-speaking countries.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_programme">
        Academic programme
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Instituto_Cervantes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academic programme">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       General language courses are offered at four different levels, elementary, intermediate, advanced and high advanced.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ciweb_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-ciweb-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        Improving specific language skills, such as reading, speaking, and writing
       </li>
       <li>
        Teaching Spanish for specific purposes, such as business, medical, legal, or translation
       </li>
       <li>
        Learning about the literature and cultures of the Spanish-speaking countries
       </li>
       <li>
        Preparation for Diplomas of Spanish as Foreign Language
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       The Cervantes Institute offers comprehensive Spanish language classes to
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        Develop the student's ability to use the language in a variety of contexts, paying special attention to the differences of Spanish accents.
       </li>
       <li>
        To provide different resources, including Spanish on-line, to improve the teaching.
       </li>
       <li>
        To allow the students to use any of the facilities of the Cervantes Institute around the world.
       </li>
       <li>
        To issue Spanish language diplomas and certificates
       </li>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Virtual_presence">
        Virtual presence
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Instituto_Cervantes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Virtual presence">
         edit

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Centro Virtual Cervantes is an online service that was created in December 1997 by the Instituto Cervantes of Spain to contribute to the diffusion of the Spanish language and Hispanic cultures. It became one of the most important reference sites devoted to Spanish language and culture, having reached an average of over 100,000 visitors a month, including both professionals and the general public.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Pais_Edición_Impresa_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-El_Pais_Edición_Impresa-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It offers resources and services to teachers, students, translators, journalists and other professionals involved with the Spanish language, and as well as to Hispanists throughout the world, who study Hispanic cultures, and to any person who is interested in the language and Hispanic cultures.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ciweb_2-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-ciweb-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It also includes announcements of cultural events (Actos Culturales), four discussion forums, each of which focuses on a specific topic, and the language classroom, Aula de Lengua, designed for both teachers and learners of Spanish.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Pais_Edición_Impresa_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-El_Pais_Edición_Impresa-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Instituto Cervantes also has a virtual presence in the 3D virtual world
       <a href="/wiki/Second_Life" title="Second Life">
        Second Life
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>

        </i>
        ]
       </sup>

      <h2>
       <span id="Centro_de_Formaci.C3.B3n_de_Profesores">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Centro_de_Formación_de_Profesores">
        Centro de Formación de Profesores
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Instituto_Cervantes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Centro de Formación de Profesores">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       On 13 December 2004, the Cervantes Institute formed its
       <i>
        Centro de Formación de Profesores
       </i>
       in its headquarters, located in
       <a href="/wiki/Alcal%C3%A1_de_Henares" title="Alcalá de Henares">
        Alcalá de Henares
       </a>
       , where Cervantes was born. This institution was founded to encourage the teaching of Spanish as a foreign language and to provide useful resources to its members. Throughout the year, it organises the specific activities and programmes for the centre, with several different goals, which include teaching, providing the resources to the teachers to use in their education field, and developing specialised knowledge of the Spanish language for educational purposes. The Centre supports the future teachers of Spanish through its 'Master of Education in Spanish as a Foreign Language', in collaboration with the International University Menéndez Pelayo. For teachers already involved in teaching, the Centre provides advanced activities that produce a deeper knowledge, focusing on research or discussion in the classroom, and improving the students’ skills in Spanish language for them to become responsive in any communicational situations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ciweb_2-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-ciweb-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Free_translator_service">
        Free translator service
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Instituto_Cervantes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Free translator service">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Automatic Translator Service of the Cervantes Institute, "Portal de las Tecnologías Lingüisticas en España", was launched by the Cervantes Institute on 10 January 2005.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ciweb_2-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-ciweb-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica.com_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-britannica.com-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is a free service of machine translation sponsored by Telefónica. Since its launch, demand has steadily increased monthly to 55%,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica.com_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-britannica.com-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and it has performed more than 582,000 free translations.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica.com_7-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-britannica.com-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Cervantes Institute's purpose in creating this service was to facilitate the access of Spanish to those
       <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">
        information technology
       </a>
       contents developed in other languages, and so people who speak other languages can have access to contents that are only available in Spanish.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica.com_7-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-britannica.com-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This service permits translation of texts and text files, as well as web pages, from Spanish to Catalan, French, Galician, English or Portuguese and vice versa.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ciweb_2-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-ciweb-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The pairs of language percentages used are as follows: English–Spanish, 23.47% of the total; Spanish–Catalan, 21.12%; Spanish–English, 13.64%; Portuguese–Spanish, 12.63%; Spanish–Portuguese, 10.39%; Catalan–Spanish, 6.48%; Spanish–French, 6.24%, and French–Spanish, 6.03%.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britannica.com_7-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-britannica.com-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       The Instituto Cervantes operates
       <b>
        Centros Cervantes
       </b>
       (full-fledged centres), and
       <b>
        Aulas Cervantes
       </b>
       (smaller "lecture halls"), as well as a network of associated institutions. These are
       <b>
        Centros Acreditados
       </b>
       (within Spain) and
       <b>
        Centros Asociados
       </b>
       (outside Spain). Its locations can be found:
      </p>
