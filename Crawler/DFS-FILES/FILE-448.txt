      <p>
       <b>
        Spanish adjectives
       </b>
       are similar to those in most other
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indo-European_language" title="Indo-European language">
        Indo-European languages
       </a>
       . They are generally
       <a href="/wiki/Postpositive_adjective" title="Postpositive adjective">
        postpositive
       </a>
       , and they agree in both gender and number with the noun they modify.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Inflection_and_usage">
        Inflection and usage
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_adjectives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Inflection and usage">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Spanish adjectives can be broadly divided into two groups: those whose lemma (the base form, the form found in dictionaries) ends in
       <i>
        -o
       </i>
       , and those whose lemma does not. The former generally inflect for both gender and number; the latter generally inflect just for number.
       <i>
        Frío
       </i>
       ("cold"), for example, inflects for both gender and number. When it is used with a masculine singular noun, the masculine singular form
       <i>
        frío
       </i>
       (the lemma) is used. When it is used with a feminine singular noun, it becomes
       <i>
        fría
       </i>
       ;
       <i>
        -a
       </i>
       is generally the feminine singular ending for adjectives that inflect for gender. When it is used with a masculine plural noun, it becomes
       <i>
        fríos
       </i>
       , and when it is used with a feminine plural noun, it becomes
       <i>
        frías
       </i>
       ;
       <i>
        -s
       </i>
       is the plural marker for both the masculine and feminine with adjectives that inflect for gender. Thus:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         frío
        </i>
        ("cold") →
        <i>
         frío, fría, fríos, frías
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         pequeño
        </i>
        ("small") →
        <i>
         pequeño, pequeña, pequeños, pequeñas
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         rojo
        </i>
        ("red") →
        <i>
         rojo, roja, rojos, rojas
        </i>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Adjectives whose lemma does not end in
       <i>
        -o
       </i>
       , however, inflect differently. These adjectives almost always inflect only for number.
       <i>
        -s
       </i>
       is once again the plural marker, and if the lemma ends in a consonant, the adjective takes
       <i>
        -es
       </i>
       in the plural. Thus:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         caliente
        </i>
        ("hot") →
        <i>
         caliente, caliente, calientes, calientes
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         formal
        </i>
        ("formal") →
        <i>
         formal, formal, formales, formales
        </i>
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         verde
        </i>
        ("green") →
        <i>
         verde, verde, verdes, verdes
        </i>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       This division into two groups is a generalisation, however. There are many examples, such as the adjective
       <i>
        español
       </i>
       itself, of adjectives whose lemmas do not end in
       <i>
        -o
       </i>
       but nevertheless take
       <i>
        -a
       </i>
       in the feminine singular as well as
       <i>
        -as
       </i>
       in the feminine plural and thus have four forms: in the case of
       <i>
        español
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        español, española, españoles, españolas
       </i>
       . There are also adjectives that do not inflect at all (generally words borrowed from other languages, such as the French
       <i>
        beige
       </i>
       (also Hispanicised to
       <i>
        beis
       </i>
       )).
      </p>
      <p>
       Spanish adjectives are very similar to nouns and are often interchangeable with them. Bare adjectives can be used with articles and thus function as nouns where English would require nominalisation using the pronoun
       <i>
        one(s)
       </i>
       . For example:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         El rojo va aquí/acá, ¿no?
        </i>
        = "The red one goes here, doesn't it?"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Tenemos que tirar las estropeadas
        </i>
        = "We have to throw away the broken ones"
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Masculine singular adjectives can also be used with the neuter article
       <i>
        lo
       </i>
       to signify "the [adjective] thing, the [adjective] part". Thus:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         lo extraño
        </i>
        = "the strange thing, the strange part"
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         lo inusual
        </i>
        = "the unusual thing, the unusual part"
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The only inflectionally irregular adjectives in Spanish are those that have irregular
       <a href="/wiki/Comparative" title="Comparative">
        comparative
       </a>
       forms, and only
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_adjectives#Comparative_and_superlative_constructions" title="Spanish adjectives">
        four
       </a>
       do.
      </p>
      <p>
       Spanish adjectives are generally
       <a href="/wiki/Postpositive_adjective" title="Postpositive adjective">
        postpositive
       </a>
       , that is, they come after the noun they modify. Thus
       <i>
        el libro
        <b>
         largo
        </b>
       </i>
       ("the long book"),
       <i>
        la casa
        <b>
         grande
        </b>
       </i>
       ("the big house"),
       <i>
        los hombres
        <b>
         altos
        </b>
       </i>
       ("the tall men"), etc. There are, however, a small number of adjectives, including all
       <a href="/wiki/Ordinal_number_(linguistics)" title="Ordinal number (linguistics)">
        ordinal numerals
       </a>
       as well as words such as
       <i>
        otro
       </i>
       ("other") and
       <i>
        todo
       </i>
       ("all"), that must be placed before the noun they modify. There are also a
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_adjectives#Adjectives_that_change_meaning" title="Spanish adjectives">
        small number
       </a>
       that can be placed both before and after the noun and that change meaning according to that positioning, and some adjectives, especially those that form something of a fixed phrase with the noun (e.g.
       <i>
        oscura noche
       </i>
       ("dark night"),
       <i>
        alta montaña
       </i>
       ("high mountain")), can be placed before or after the noun with little change in meaning.
      </p>

       <i>
        algún fresco pan
       </i>
       ("some fresh bread"),
       <i>
        el primer gran árbol
       </i>
       ("the first big tree"),
       <i>
        ningún otro hombre
       </i>
       ("no other man"), etc. In the case of
       <i>
        grande
       </i>
       , which is the only apocopic adjective with regular comparative and superlative forms (
       <i>
        más grande
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        el más grande
       </i>
       , respectively), the comparative and superlative apocopate in the same manner as the positive:
       <i>
        la más gran casa
       </i>
       but
       <i>
        la casa más grande
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        el más gran coche de los dos
       </i>
       but
       <i>
        el coche más grande de los dos
       </i>
       , etc. If a
       <a href="/wiki/Conjunction_(grammar)" title="Conjunction (grammar)">
        conjunction
       </a>
       intervenes between the adjective and the noun, however, apocopic forms are not used:
       <i>
        esta grande y bella casa
       </i>
       ("this big and beautiful house"),
       <i>
        el primero o segundo día
       </i>
       ("the first or second day"), etc.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Comparatives_and_superlatives">
        Comparatives and superlatives
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_adjectives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Comparatives and superlatives">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Comparatives are normally expressed with the adverbs
       <i>
        más
       </i>
       ("more") and
       <i>
        menos
       </i>
       ("less") followed by the adjective; the object of comparison is introduced with the particle
       <i>
        que
       </i>
       ("than"). For example,
       <i>
        X es más grande que Y
       </i>
       ("X is bigger/greater than Y"). Superlatives (in the cross-linguistic, semantic sense) are also expressed with the adverbs
       <i>
        más
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        menos
       </i>
       , but this time with a definite article preceding the noun:
       <i>
        la persona más interesante
       </i>
       ("the most interesting person"); the object of comparison is introduced with the preposition
       <i>
        de
       </i>
       ("of"). The adjectives
       <i>
        bueno
       </i>
       ("good"),
       <i>
        malo
       </i>
       ("bad"),
       <i>
        joven
       </i>
       ("young"), and
       <i>
        viejo
       </i>
       ("old") have irregular comparative forms:
       <i>
        mejor
       </i>
       ("better"),
       <i>
        peor
       </i>
       ("worse"),
       <i>
        menor
       </i>
       ("younger"), and
       <i>
        mayor
       </i>
       ("older"), respectively.
       <i>
        Mejor
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        peor
       </i>
       are placed before the nouns they modify:
       <i>
        la mejor cosa
       </i>
       , ("the best thing"),
       <i>
        el peor libro
       </i>
       ("the worst book"), etc.
      </p>
      <p>
       Because the definite article is, along with
       <i>
        más
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        menos
       </i>
       , the superlative marker, the comparative is grammatically indistinguishable from the superlative when used with it; an additional qualifier phrase such as
       <i>
        de los dos
       </i>
       ("of the two") must therefore be used to indicate that the adjective is the comparative and not the superlative.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_superlative">
        The superlative
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_adjectives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The superlative">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Instead of putting
       <i>
        muy
       </i>
       , "very" before an adjective, one can use a special form called the superlative to intensify an idea. This consists of the suffix
       <i>
        -ísimo
       </i>

       <i>

       .
      </p>
      <p>
       As in English and other languages influenced by it, a

        teenspeak
       </a>
       superlative can be formed by the prefix
       <i>
        super-
       </i>
       , or sometimes
       <i>
        hiper-
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        ultra-
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        re-
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        requete-
       </i>
       . They can also be written as adverbs separate from the word.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         Superlargo
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         súper largo
        </i>
        = "super-long", "way long"
       </li>
      </ul>
