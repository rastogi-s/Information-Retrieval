      <p>
       <b>
        Castile
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
            k
           </span>
           <span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">
            æ
           </span>
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'s' in 'sigh'">
            s
           </span>
           <span title="'t' in 'tie'">
            t
           </span>
           <span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">
            iː
           </span>
           <span title="'l' in 'lie'">
            l
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Castilla
        </span>
       </i>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">
         [kasˈtiʎa]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is a vaguely defined historical region of
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       . There are different conceptions and definitions of Castile, and since it lacks modern day official recognition, it has no clearly defined borders. Historically, the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">
        Kingdom of Castile
       </a>
       occupied the area. After the kingdom merged with its neighbours to become the
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">
        Crown of Castile
       </a>
       and later the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Spain" title="Kingdom of Spain">
        Kingdom of Spain
       </a>
       , when it united with the
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">
        Crown of Aragon
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Navarre" title="Kingdom of Navarre">
        Kingdom of Navarre
       </a>
       , the definition of what constituted
       <i>
        Castile
       </i>
       gradually began to change. Its historical capital was
       <a href="/wiki/Burgos" title="Burgos">
        Burgos
       </a>
       . In modern Spain, it is usually considered to comprise the majority of the autonomous communities of
       <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">
        Castile and León
       </a>
       (south and central), and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castile%E2%80%93La_Mancha" title="Castile–La Mancha">
        Castile–La Mancha
       </a>
       (everything except the south portion) as well as entire
       <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Madrid" title="Community of Madrid">
        Community of Madrid
       </a>
       as its heart.
       <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">
        Cantabria
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" title="La Rioja (Spain)">
        La Rioja
       </a>
       in the north of Spain are sometimes also included in the definition, but this is controversial for historical reasons and the strong sense of unique cultural identity of those regions.
      </p>
      <p>
       Castile was traditionally divided between
       <a href="/wiki/Old_Castile" title="Old Castile">
        Old Castile
       </a>
       , which from 1833 is made up of
       <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">
        Cantabria
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" title="La Rioja (Spain)">
        La Rioja
       </a>
       , and the eastern half of
       <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">
        Castile and León
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/New_Castile_(Spain)" title="New Castile (Spain)">
        New Castile
       </a>
       , which was
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castile%E2%80%93La_Mancha" title="Castile–La Mancha">
        Castile–La Mancha
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Madrid" title="Community of Madrid">
        Community of Madrid
       </a>
       . Modern Spanish monarchs are numbered according to the system of Castile.
      </p>
      <p>
       Castile's name is thought to derive from Spanish for "land of castles" (Spanish word for
       <i>
        castle
       </i>
       is
       <i>
        castillo
       </i>
       ) in reference to the castles built in the area to consolidate the Christian
       <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">
        Reconquest
       </a>
       from the oncoming
       <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">
        Moors
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Castile_(historical_region)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Historically, the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">
        Castilian Kingdom
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castilian_people" title="Castilian people">
        people
       </a>
       were considered to be the main architects of the Spanish State by a process of expansion to the South against the Muslims and of marriages, wars, assimilation, and annexation of their smaller Eastern and Western neighbours. From the advent of the Bourbon Monarchy following the
       <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">
        War of the Spanish Succession
       </a>
       until the arrival of parliamentary democracy in 1977, the Castilian language was the only one with official status in the Spanish state.
      </p>
      <p>
       Originally an eastern county of the kingdom of
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of León">
        León
       </a>
       , in the 11th century Castile became an independent realm with its capital at
       <a href="/wiki/Burgos" title="Burgos">
        Burgos
       </a>
       . The County of Castile, which originally included most of
       <a href="/wiki/Burgos" title="Burgos">
        Burgos
       </a>
       and parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Biscay" title="Biscay">
        Vizcaya
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lava" title="Álava">
        Álava
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">
        Cantabria
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" title="La Rioja (Spain)">
        La Rioja
       </a>
       .,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-martinez_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-martinez-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       became the leading force in the northern Christian states' 800-year
       <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">
        Reconquista
       </a>
       ("reconquest") of central and southern Spain from the
       <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">
        Muslim rulers
       </a>
       who had
       <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_Hispania" title="Umayyad conquest of Hispania">
        dominated most of the peninsula
       </a>
       since the early 8th century.
      </p>
      <p>
       The capture of
       <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Spain" title="Toledo, Spain">
        Toledo
       </a>
       in 1085 added
       <a href="/wiki/New_Castile_(Spain)" title="New Castile (Spain)">
        New Castile
       </a>
       to the crown's territories, and the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Las_Navas_de_Tolosa" title="Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa">
        battle of Las Navas de Tolosa
       </a>
       (1212) heralded the Muslim loss of most of southern Spain. León was finally reunited with Castile in 1230, and the following decades saw the capture of
       <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba,_Spain" title="Córdoba, Spain">
        Córdoba
       </a>
       (1236),
       <a href="/wiki/Murcia" title="Murcia">
        Murcia
       </a>
       (1243) and
       <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">
        Seville
       </a>
       (1248). By the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Alca%C3%A7ovas" title="Treaty of Alcaçovas">
        Treaty of Alcaçovas
       </a>
       with Portugal on March 6, 1460, the ownership of the
       <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">
        Canary Islands
       </a>
       was transferred to Castile.
      </p>
      <p>
       The dynastic union of Castile and
       <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">
        Aragon
       </a>
       in 1469, when
       <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">
        Ferdinand II of Aragon
       </a>
       wed
       <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">
        Isabella I of Castile
       </a>
       , would eventually lead to the formal creation of Spain as a single entity in 1516 when their grandson
       <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">
        Charles V
       </a>
       assumed both thrones. See
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Spanish_monarchs" title="List of Spanish monarchs">
        List of Spanish monarchs
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kings_of_Spain_family_tree" title="Kings of Spain family tree">
        Kings of Spain family tree
       </a>
       . The Muslim Kingdom of Granada (roughly encompassing the modern day provinces of Granada, Malaga and Almeria) was conquered in 1492, formally passing to the Crown of Castile in that year.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
        Geography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Castile_(historical_region)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       At the present time there are two nominally Castilian
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities" title="Autonomous communities">
        autonomous communities
       </a>
       in Spain, as they hold the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toponym" title="Toponym">
        toponym
       </a>
       in their own official name:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castile_and_Leon" title="Castile and Leon">
        Castile and Leon
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castile-La_Mancha" title="Castile-La Mancha">
        Castile-La Mancha
       </a>
       . A third, the
       <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Madrid" title="Community of Madrid">
        Community of Madrid
       </a>
       is considered also Castilian,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
          <span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (July 2017)">
           according to whom?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       by its geographic enclosure and, above all, by the statements of its Statute of Autonomy, since its autonomic process originated by national interest and not by popular disaffection with Castile.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Synthesis_of_published_material" title="Wikipedia:No original research">
          <span title="The material near this tag may be based upon an improper synthesis of sources. (July 2017)">
           improper synthesis?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Other territories in the former
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">
        Crown of Castile
       </a>
       are left out for different reasons.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (July 2017)">
           which?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In fact, the territory of the Castilian Crown actually comprised all other autonomous communities within Spain with the exception of
       <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">
        Aragon
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">
        Balearic Islands
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencia_(autonomous_community)" title="Valencia (autonomous community)">
        Valencia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">
        Catalonia
       </a>
       , which all belonged to the late Crown of Aragon, and
       <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">
        Navarre
       </a>
       , heir of the older Kingdom of the same name. Castile was divided
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">
          <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (July 2017)">
           when?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       between Old Castile in the north, which was called so because it was where the Kingdom of Castile was formed, and New Castile, which was called the Kingdom of Toledo in the Middle Ages. The Leonese region, while being in the Crown of Castile from 1230 was considered a region in its own right
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2017)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       from medieval times on par with the two Castiles and appeared on maps alongside Old Castile until the two joined into one region, Castilla-y-Leon in the 1980s. However, in 1833, Spain was organised into administrative
       <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Spain" title="Provinces of Spain">
        provinces
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Two non administrative nominally
       <i>
        Castilian
       </i>
       regions existed from 1833 to 1982:
       <a href="/wiki/Old_Castile" title="Old Castile">
        Old Castile
       </a>
       , including
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_Santander" title="Province of Santander">
        Santander
       </a>
       (autonomous community of Cantabria since 1981),
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Burgos" title="Province of Burgos">
        Burgos
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logro%C3%B1o_(province)" title="Logroño (province)">
        Logroño
       </a>
       (autonomous community of La Rioja since 1982),
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Palencia" title="Province of Palencia">
        Palencia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Valladolid" title="Province of Valladolid">
        Valladolid
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Soria" title="Province of Soria">
        Soria
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Segovia" title="Province of Segovia">
        Segovia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_%C3%81vila" title="Province of Ávila">
        Ávila
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/New_Castile_(Spain)" title="New Castile (Spain)">
        New Castile
       </a>
       consisting of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_Madrid" title="Province of Madrid">
        Madrid
       </a>
       (autonomous community of Madrid since 1983),
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Guadalajara" title="Province of Guadalajara">
        Guadalajara
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_Cuenca_(Spain)" title="Province of Cuenca (Spain)">
        Cuenca
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Toledo" title="Province of Toledo">
        Toledo
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Ciudad_Real" title="Province of Ciudad Real">
        Ciudad Real
       </a>
       .
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Language">
        Language
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">

       <a href="/wiki/Names_given_to_the_Spanish_language" title="Names given to the Spanish language">
        Names given to the Spanish language
       </a>
       .
      </p>

         </a>
         . He was the first king who initiated the use of the Castilian language extensively, instead of Latin
         <br/>
         (Latin was
         <i>
          de jure
         </i>
         around Iberian peninsula in that time).

      <p>
       Castilians are defined
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
          <span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (July 2017)">
           according to whom?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       as a community with a shared culture and history.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words">
          <span title="Who says this? (July 2017)">
           weasel words
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Their origin is, as well as most of other parts of Spain, a heterogeneous mixture of peoples who settled the area over millennia.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words">
          <span title="Who says this? (July 2017)">
           weasel words
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Thus, it is not correct to establish a strict common origin, but a common
       <a href="/wiki/Cultural_identity" title="Cultural identity">
        cultural identity
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2017)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Over time, most Castilians
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (July 2017)">
           who?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       have mixed with other Spaniards due to their past political dominance, and present-day cultural dominance. Castilians and their cultural influence spread throughout the entire plateau of central Spain during the
       <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">
        Reconquista
       </a>
       , carried out principally by the Kingdom of Toledo which was renamed
       <i>
        New Castile
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Castilian
       <i>
        ethnicity
       </i>
       is the product of the conquest,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       by a small kingdom in northern Spain, of vast tracts of sparsely populated lands (the central
       <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Spain#The_Inner_Plateau_and_associated_mountains" title="Geography of Spain">
        "mesetas"
       </a>
       ). These lands were populated, during the reconquest, by peoples from all over the peninsula, even from southern Spain (see exile of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mozarab" title="Mozarab">
        Mozarabs
       </a>
       from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Andalus" title="Al Andalus">
        Al Andalus
       </a>
       and even the dispersal of
       <a href="/wiki/Morisco" title="Morisco">
        Moriscos
       </a>
       from Granada in the 16th century).
      </p>
      <p>
       The Castilian language, now usually known as "Spanish", gradually became the main language of Spain; it is still frequently called "Castilian" in Spain. At present, Castilians are known as the inhabitants of those regions of Spain where there is no regional identity which conflicts with that of Castilians. The Castilian territories roughly coincide with the plateaus of north-central Spain, historically sparsely populated harsh highlands.
      </p>
      <p>
       Castilian identity is ambiguous: since
       <a href="/wiki/Castilian_nationalism" title="Castilian nationalism">
        Castilian nationalism
       </a>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute">
          <span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (July 2017)">
           dubious
          </span>
         </a>
         <span class="metadata">
          –
          <a href="/wiki/Talk:Castile_(historical_region)#Dubious" title="Talk:Castile (historical region)">
           discuss
          </a>
         </span>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       was the first to have been suppressed by the Spanish Crown during the revolt and war of the
       <a href="/wiki/Revolt_of_the_Comuneros" title="Revolt of the Comuneros">
        Castilian War of the Communities
       </a>
       against the Spanish Monarchy, between 1520 and 1521, a strong sense of identity cannot be found in Castilians and there are differences about what can be considered Castile.
      </p>
      <p>
       Castilian identity is thus excluded
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="Please clarify the following statement or statements with a good explanation from a reliable source. (July 2017)">
           how?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       from the historical nationalities of Spain such as
       <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">
        Catalonia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">
        Galicia
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(historical_territory)" title="Basque Country (historical territory)">
        Basque Country
       </a>
       , but also other autonomous communities of Spain which due to historical reasons have their own strong identities, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">
        Andalusia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">
        Asturias
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">
        Aragon
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">
        Balearic Islands
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">
        Canary Islands
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">
        Valencian Community
       </a>
       . Both Andalusia and the Canary Islands experienced an early Castilian colonisation and subsequent immigration, but the survival and absorption of by the large local populations allowed for characteristics and separate identities particular to those regions to survive.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Certain regions are not considered Castilian, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">
        Valencia
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">
        Balearic Islands
       </a>
       because historically they belong to the
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">
        Crown of Aragon
       </a>
       and because their local languages are
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       dialects.
       <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">
        Galicians
       </a>
       are not considered to be Castilian, although the former
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Galicia" title="Kingdom of Galicia">
        Kingdom of Galicia
       </a>
       was annexed by the
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">
        Crown of Castile
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Their language,
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
        Galician
       </a>
       , is akin to
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       , and they possess a local culture and identity.
       <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">
        Navarre
       </a>
       , a former kingdom, has a culture that is mainly Basque in the north and closer to that of Old Castile in the south, especially in
       <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" title="La Rioja (Spain)">
        La Rioja
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">
        Aragon
       </a>
       , in the south.
       <a href="/wiki/Extremadura" title="Extremadura">
        Extremadura
       </a>
       , a region in western Spain, is usually not considered to be Castilian; it has a strong link to neighbouring Andalusia.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Murcia" title="Murcia">
        Murcia
       </a>
       has always been a region of its own.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2017)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Region of León">
        Leonese region
       </a>
       was a separate region before democracy in the 1970s, and had its own language,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonese_language" title="Leonese language">
        Leonese
       </a>
       , which is moribund. However,
       <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">
        Castile and León
       </a>
       (Spanish:
       <i>
        Castilla y León
       </i>
       ) had the Spanish conjunction
       <i>
        y
       </i>
       ('and') inserted as the León region is not considered to be in Castile (the north half) but joint with it in this autonomous community.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Leonese regionalists want Castile and León to be dissolved and become an autonomous community in its own right as the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonese_Country" title="Leonese Country">
        Leonese Country
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">
        Cantabria
       </a>
       was previously considered as part of Old Castile, but they have their own identity and had achieved independence from Castile in the 1970s and are now a comunidad autonoma in their own right.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_Madrid" title="Province of Madrid">
        Madrid
       </a>
       had been in New Castile until the 1970s and has traditionally been considered Castilian. However, its status as the capital of Spain, meant that it gained its own
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_community_of_Spain" title="Autonomous community of Spain">
        autonomous community
       </a>
       . Also, its people are not all Castilian as there have been immigration from other regions in Spain and indeed the world.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In terms of cultural identity, there is a divide and controversy.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Older Madrid people as well as Castilians nationalist in the region consider their city to be in Castile (cultural identity).
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Younger Madrid people, look at the modern reality of the administration, and do not consider it to be in Castile, as there is no reference to it in the name or the symbols of the autonomous community.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       However, the folk culture in the Madrid region is Castilian, even though the city itself has its own folklore, the chotis.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       As for
       <a href="/wiki/La_Mancha" title="La Mancha">
        La Mancha
       </a>
       , they are still in Castile
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2017)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       as their autonomous community is called
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castile%E2%80%93La_Mancha" title="Castile–La Mancha">
        Castile–La Mancha
       </a>
       . However, their cultural identity focuses on the La Mancha rather than on the Castile.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       They have their own identity even though they are recognised as fully Castilian,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       for example, they had no language of their own, unlike, León.
      </p>
