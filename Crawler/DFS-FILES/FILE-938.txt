      <p>
       <b>
        Indian French
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">
         français indien
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       spoken by
       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
        Indians
       </a>
       in the former colonies of
       <a href="/wiki/Puducherry" title="Puducherry">
        Puducherry
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Chandannagar" title="Chandannagar">
        Chandannagar
       </a>
       . In this dialect, there is a considerable influence from
       <a href="/wiki/Dravidian_languages" title="Dravidian languages">
        Dravidian languages
       </a>
       like
       <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">
        Tamil
       </a>
       (Pondicherry Tamil dialect),
       <a href="/wiki/Telugu_language" title="Telugu language">
        Telugu
       </a>
       (Yanam Telugu dialect) and
       <a href="/wiki/Malayalam" title="Malayalam">
        Malayalam
       </a>
       (Mahe Malayalam dialect).
      </p>
      <p>
       There are several varieties of Indian French, corresponding to the former French colonies, as follows:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Tamil people (nearly 60,000 in France, 10,000 people in Puducherry)
       </li>
       <li>
        Telugu people (nearly 10,000 in France, 60 people in Yanam)
       </li>
       <li>
        Malayali people (4000-5000 in France)
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
