   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Bolivian Spanish
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

     <a href="#mw-head">
      navigation
     </a>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The Spanish (or Castilian) language is spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">
        Bolivia
       </a>
       by most of the population, either as a mother tongue or as a second language. Within the Spanish of Bolivia there are different regional varieties. In the border areas, Bolivia shares dialectal features with the neighboring countries.
      </p>
      <p>
       Throughout Bolivia the preservation of phonemic contrast between
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʝ/
       </span>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Lateral_consonant" title="Lateral consonant">
        lateral
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʎ/
       </span>
       (i.e. the absence of
       <a href="/wiki/Ye%C3%ADsmo" title="Yeísmo">
        yeísmo
       </a>
       ) is the norm.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_1994:188_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski_1994:188-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Debuccalization" title="Debuccalization">
        Aspiration
       </a>
       of syllable-final
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       is frequent in the lowlands, while in the highlands the sibilant
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       tends to be preserved, realized either as a
       <a href="/wiki/Laminal_consonant" title="Laminal consonant">
        laminal
       </a>
       or, frequently, an
       <a href="/wiki/Apical_consonant" title="Apical consonant">
        apical
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [s]
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_1994:188_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski_1994:188-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canfield_1981:29_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canfield_1981:29-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In highland dialects, the
       <a href="/wiki/Trill_consonant" title="Trill consonant">
        "trill"
       </a>
       phoneme (orthographic &lt;rr&gt; or word-initial &lt;r&gt;) is often
       <a href="/wiki/Assibilation" title="Assibilation">
        assibilated
       </a>
       , realized as a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voiced_apicoalveolar_fricative" title="Voiced apicoalveolar fricative">
        voiced apicoalveolar fricative
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canfield_1981:29_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Canfield_1981:29-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In highland Bolivian Spanish there is "intense reduction" of unstressed vowels in contact with
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       , often resulting in syllables with
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       as their nucleus, e.g.
       <i>
        pues
       </i>
       ("well,...") pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ps]
       </span>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_1994:188_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski_1994:188-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Andean_Spanish">
        Andean Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivian_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Andean Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Andean_Spanish" title="Andean Spanish">
        Andean Spanish
       </a>
      </div>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Camba_Spanish">
        Camba Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivian_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Camba Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       This variety of Spanish is spoken on the Chaco-Beni plain and in the Santa Cruz valleys, a region that includes the departments of
       <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_Department_(Bolivia)" title="Santa Cruz Department (Bolivia)">
        Santa Cruz
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Beni" title="Department of Beni">
        Beni
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Pando" title="Department of Pando">
        Pando
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Spanish is spoken by almost the entire population of these regions, and—like Spanish throughout the Americas—has its basis in
       <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_Spanish" title="Andalusian Spanish">
        Andalusian Spanish
       </a>
       , but with influences of native languages such as
       <a href="/wiki/Chiquitano" title="Chiquitano">
        Chiquitano
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Chan%C3%A9_language" title="Chané language">
        Chané
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Guarani_language" title="Guarani language">
        Guarani
       </a>
       , as well as Old World languages including
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
        Arabic
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       And although it is fairly uniform across regions and social classes, there are subtle geographical differences.
      </p>
      <p>
       This dialect is characterized by the
       <a href="/wiki/Debuccalization" title="Debuccalization">
        debuccalization
       </a>
       ("aspiration") of final
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       . For example, the word
       <i>
        pues
       </i>
       is pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [pwe]
       </span>
       . For the second-person-singular pronoun and verb forms, the use of "
       <a href="/wiki/Voseo" title="Voseo">
        voseo
       </a>
       " is dominant. The use of
       <a href="/wiki/Diminutive" title="Diminutive">
        diminutive
       </a>
       <i>
        ‑ingo
       </i>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Augmentative" title="Augmentative">
        augmentative
       </a>
       <i>
        ‑ango
       </i>
       is unique to this dialect. For example:
       <i>
        chiquitingo
       </i>
       ("very small") and
       <i>
        grandango
       </i>
       ("very large").
      </p>
      <p>
       <br/>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Chapaco_Spanish">
        Chapaco Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivian_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chapaco Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       This dialect is spoken mainly in the valleys and the
       <a href="/wiki/Gran_Chaco" title="Gran Chaco">
        Gran Chaco
       </a>
       of the department of
       <a href="/wiki/Tarija_Department" title="Tarija Department">
        Tarija
       </a>
       , but also in the region of
       <a href="/wiki/Villa_Abecia" title="Villa Abecia">
        Villa Abecia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Camargo,_Chuquisaca" title="Camargo, Chuquisaca">
        Camargo
       </a>
       (in the department of
       <a href="/wiki/Chuquisaca_Department" title="Chuquisaca Department">
        Chuquisaca
       </a>
       ), in the province of
       <a href="/wiki/Sud_Chichas_Province" title="Sud Chichas Province">
        Sud Chichas
       </a>
       (capital
       <a href="/wiki/Tupiza" title="Tupiza">
        Tupiza
       </a>
       ), and in the Chaco regions of Chuquisaca and Santa Cruz. The second-person-singular
       <a href="/wiki/Voseo" title="Voseo">
        voseo
       </a>
       is in full use in
       <a href="/wiki/Tupiza" title="Tupiza">
        Tupiza
       </a>
       , in the west of Tarija, and in the rest of the aforementioned areas. The Chapaco accent has an intonation similar to that of Jujuy, Salta, and Tucumán in Argentina, as the territory where it was spoken before was the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_Tarija" title="Province of Tarija">
        Río de la Plata Province of Tarija
       </a>
       . It has similar intonation throughout the Bolivian Chaco, Tupiza (Sud Chichas) and the Chuquisaca valleys of Camargo, Villa Abecia, Azurduy, Alcalá, etc.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Valluno_Spanish">
        Valluno Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivian_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Valluno Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       This variety is spoken in the departments of
       <a href="/wiki/Cochabamba" title="Cochabamba">
        Cochabamba
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Chuquisaca_Department" title="Chuquisaca Department">
        Chuquisaca
       </a>
       . It is somewhat similar to
       <a href="/wiki/Andean_Spanish" title="Andean Spanish">
        Andean Spanish
       </a>
       but differs in intonation and the use of idiomatic expressions, due to the mixture of Spanish and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quechua_Boliviano" title="Quechua Boliviano">
        Quechua
       </a>
       spoken in the valleys of Bolivia.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vallegrandino_Spanish">
        Vallegrandino Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivian_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Vallegrandino Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       See
       <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Espa%C3%B1ol_vallegrandino" title="es:Español vallegrandino">
        Español vallegrandino
       </a>
       (in Spanish).
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Note">
        Note
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolivian_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Note">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Because many institutions and companies use "tú" and the "tuteante" verb forms for the familiar second-person singular, it is common to encounter the erroneous statement that "tuteo" rather than "voseo" is the usual form in the speech of Bolivia.
      </p>
