      <p>
       <b>
        Northern Catalan
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="ca" xml:lang="ca">
         català septentrional
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <small>
        pronounced
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Catalan" title="Help:IPA/Catalan">
         [kətəˈla səptəntɾiuˈnal]
        </a>
       </span>
       , also known as
       <i>
        rossellonès
       </i>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       mostly spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Catalonia" title="Northern Catalonia">
        Northern Catalonia
       </a>
       , but also extending in the northeast part of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Catalonia" title="Southern Catalonia">
        Southern Catalonia
       </a>
       in a transition zone with
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Catalan" title="Central Catalan">
        Central Catalan
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       As this is an Eastern Catalan dialect, unstressed
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /e/
       </span>
       are realized as schwa
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ə]
       </span>
       , and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [u]
       </span>
       substitutes unstressed
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o/
       </span>
       . It is also the Catalan dialect with the fewest stressed vowels, only five:
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /i e a o u/
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       There are some instances of historic stressed
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o/
       </span>
       that has changed to
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /u/
       </span>
       (e.g.,
       <i>
        canigó
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        canigú
       </i>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       As in
       <a href="/wiki/Balearic_dialect" title="Balearic dialect">
        Balearic dialects
       </a>
       , final
       <i>
        a'
       </i>
       s are not pronounced in words ending with
       <i>
        ia
       </i>
       and containing the stress before the penultimate
       <a href="/wiki/Syllable" title="Syllable">
        syllable
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Some subdialects keep the singular masculine definite article
       <i>
        lo
       </i>
       , as in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North-Western_Catalan" title="North-Western Catalan">
        North-Western Catalan
       </a>
       and many varieties of
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Northern Catalan has a large body of words imported from
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       and Occitan. It also features some grammatical forms and structures typical of Occitan, such as the use of a lone post-verbal
       <i>
        pas
       </i>
       rather than a lone pre-verbal
       <i>
        no
       </i>
       to express basic negation (e.g., Northern Catalan
       <i>
        canti pas
       </i>
       vs.
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Catalan" title="Central Catalan">
        Central Catalan
       </a>
       <i>
        no canto
       </i>
       , 'I don't sing' or 'I'm not singing');
       <i>
        pas
       </i>
       is also used in some other Catalan dialects for emphasis, but always with
       <i>
        no
       </i>
       before the verb (e.g., Central Catalan
       <i>
        no canto pas
       </i>
       , 'I do
       <b>
        not
       </b>
       sing' or 'I am
       <b>
        not
       </b>
       singing').
      </p>
