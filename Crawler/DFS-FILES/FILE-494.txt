      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Yeísmo
        </b>
       </i>
       (
       <small>
        Spanish pronunciation:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">
         [ɟʝeˈizmo]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is a distinctive feature of many
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_dialects_and_varieties" title="Spanish dialects and varieties">
        dialects
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       , which consists of the loss of the traditional
       <a href="/wiki/Palatal_lateral_approximant" title="Palatal lateral approximant">
        palatal lateral approximant
       </a>
       phoneme
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʎ/
       </span>
       (written ⟨ll⟩) and its
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phonemic_differentiation" title="Phonemic differentiation">
        merger
       </a>
       into the phoneme
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʝ/
       </span>
       (written ⟨y⟩), usually realized as a palatal approximant or affricate. It is an example of
       <a href="/wiki/Delateralization" title="Delateralization">
        delateralization
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In other words, ⟨ll⟩ and ⟨y⟩ represent the same sound
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʝ/
       </span>
       . The term
       <i>
        yeísmo
       </i>
       comes from the Spanish name of the letter ⟨y⟩ (
       <i>
        ye
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ). Now, over 90% of Spanish dialects exhibit this phonemic merger.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEColoma2011103_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEColoma2011103-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Similar mergers exist in other languages, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">
        Hungarian
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">
        Basque
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
        Galician
       </a>
       , with different social considerations.
      </p>
      <p>
       Occasionally, the term
       <i>
        lleísmo
       </i>
       (
       <small>
        pronounced
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">
         [ʎeˈizmo]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) has been used to refer to the maintenance of the phonemic distinction between
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʝ/
       </span>
       (spelled "y") and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʎ/
       </span>
       (spelled "ll").
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEÁlvarez_Menendez2005104_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEÁlvarez_Menendez2005104-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchweglerKempffAmeal-Guerra2009399_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchweglerKempffAmeal-Guerra2009399-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETravis200976_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETravis200976-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Pronunciation">
        Pronunciation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ye%C3%ADsmo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pronunciation">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ye%C3%ADsmo_idioma_espa%C3%B1ol.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="599" data-file-width="452" height="464" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Ye%C3%ADsmo_idioma_espa%C3%B1ol.png/350px-Ye%C3%ADsmo_idioma_espa%C3%B1ol.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c0/Ye%C3%ADsmo_idioma_espa%C3%B1ol.png 1.5x" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ye%C3%ADsmo_idioma_espa%C3%B1ol.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Regions with the merger (
         <i>
          yeísmo
         </i>
         ) in dark blue, and regions with distinction in pink.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Most dialects that merge the two sounds represented by ⟨ll⟩ and ⟨y⟩ realize the remaining sound as a
       <a href="/wiki/Voiced_palatal_fricative" title="Voiced palatal fricative">
        voiced palatal fricative
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ʝ]
       </span>
       , which is similar to the ⟨y⟩ in English
       <i>
        your
       </i>
       , but it sometimes sounds like ⟨j⟩ in English
       <i>
        jar
       </i>
       , especially after
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /n/
       </span>
       or
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /l/
       </span>
       or at the beginning of a word. For example,
       <i>
        relleno
       </i>
       is pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [reˈʝeno]
       </span>
       and
       <i>
        conllevar
       </i>
       is pronounced
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [koɲɟ͡ʝeˈβaɾ]
       </span>
       or
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [koɲdʒeˈβaɾ]
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span id="Zhe.C3.ADsmo">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Zheísmo">
        <i>
         Zheísmo
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ye%C3%ADsmo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Zheísmo">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Rioplatense_Spanish" title="Rioplatense Spanish">
        Rioplatense Spanish
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In most of
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
        Uruguay
       </a>
       , the merged sound is pronounced as a
       <a href="/wiki/Sibilant" title="Sibilant">
        sibilant
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [
        <a href="/wiki/Voiced_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiced postalveolar fricative">
         ʒ
        </a>
        ]
       </span>
       ;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMartínez-CeldránFernández-PlanasCarrera-Sabaté2003258_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMartínez-CeldránFernández-PlanasCarrera-Sabaté2003258-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       this is referred to as
       <i>
        zheísmo
       </i>
       . In
       <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">
        Buenos Aires
       </a>
       , the sound
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [
        <a href="/wiki/Voiced_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiced postalveolar fricative">
         ʒ
        </a>
        ]
       </span>
       has recently been devoiced to
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [
        <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiceless postalveolar fricative">
         ʃ
        </a>
        ]
       </span>
       (
       <i>
        sheísmo
       </i>
       ) among younger speakers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELipski1994170_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELipski1994170-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Note that the same shift from
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [
        <a href="/wiki/Palatal_lateral_approximant" title="Palatal lateral approximant">
         ʎ
        </a>
        ]
       </span>
       to
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [
        <a href="/wiki/Voiced_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiced postalveolar fricative">
         ʒ
        </a>
        ]
       </span>
       to
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [
        <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiceless postalveolar fricative">
         ʃ
        </a>
        ]
       </span>
       (to modern
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [
        <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_velar_fricative" title="Voiceless velar fricative">
         x
        </a>
        ]
       </span>
       ) historically occurred in the development of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Spanish" title="Old Spanish">
        Old Spanish
       </a>
       ; this accounts for such pairings as Spanish
       <i>
        mujer
       </i>
       vs Portuguese
       <i>
        mulher
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        ojo
       </i>
       vs
       <i>
        olho
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        hija
       </i>
       vs
       <i>
        filha
       </i>
       and so on.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="Extent_of_ye.C3.ADsmo_and_lle.C3.ADsmo">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Extent_of_yeísmo_and_lleísmo">
        Extent of
        <i>
         yeísmo
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         lleísmo
        </i>
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ye%C3%ADsmo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Extent of yeísmo and lleísmo">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Yeísmo
       </i>
       has always been common in much of
       <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">
        Latin America
       </a>
       , mainly in lowlands, and in large areas in
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The distinction between
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʝ/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʎ/
       </span>
       remains in the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">
        Ecuadoran
       </a>
       highlands, Andean
       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
        Peru
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">
        Paraguay
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">
        Bolivia
       </a>
       , and the northeastern portions of
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       that border with Paraguay.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEColoma201195_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEColoma201195-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The distinction is more common in areas with a common bilingualism with indigenous languages, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Aymara_language" title="Aymara language">
        Aymara
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quechua_language" title="Quechua language">
        Quechua
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Guarani_language" title="Guarani language">
        Guaraní
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In Spain, most of the northern half of the country and several areas in the south used to retain the distinction, but
       <i>
        yeísmo
       </i>
       has spread throughout the country, and the distinction is now lost in most of Spain, particularly outside areas with linguistic contact with
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">
        Basque
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEColoma2011110–111_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEColoma2011110–111-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Minimal_pairs">
        Minimal pairs
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ye%C3%ADsmo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Minimal pairs">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        Yeísmo
       </i>
       produces
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homophones" title="Homophones">
        homophony
       </a>
       in a number of cases. For example, the following word pairs sound the same to speakers of dialects with
       <i>
        yeísmo
       </i>
       , but they would be
       <a href="/wiki/Minimal_pair" title="Minimal pair">
        minimal pairs
       </a>
       in regions with
       <i>
        lleísmo
       </i>
       :
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         haya
        </i>
        ("beech tree" / "that there be") ~
        <i>
         halla
        </i>
        ("s/he finds")
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         cayó
        </i>
        ("s/he fell") ~
        <i>
         calló
        </i>
        ("s/he became silent")
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         hoya
        </i>
        ("pit, hole") ~
        <i>
         olla
        </i>
        ("pot")
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         baya
        </i>
        ("berry") /
        <i>
         vaya
        </i>
        ("that he go") ~
        <i>
         valla
        </i>
        ("fence")
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The relatively low frequency of both
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʝ/
       </span>
       and
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ʎ/
       </span>
       makes confusion unlikely. However, orthographic mistakes are common (for example, writing
       <i>
        llendo
       </i>
       instead of
       <i>
        yendo
       </i>
       ). A similar effect took place in the local name of the island of
       <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">
        Majorca
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Mallorca
       </i>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        continental Catalan
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Hypercorrection" title="Hypercorrection">
        hypercorrection
       </a>
       of the earlier
       <i>
        Maiorca
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_languages">
        Other languages
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ye%C3%ADsmo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other languages">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Similar phenomena occurred in other languages.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
         French
        </a>
        ,
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ʎ/
        </span>
        turned into
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /j/
        </span>
        , but the spelling
        <i>
         ll
        </i>
        was preserved, hence
        <i>
         briller
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">
          [bʁije]
         </a>
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">
         Hungarian
        </a>
        ,
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ʎ/
        </span>
        turned into
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /j/
        </span>
        , but the spelling
        <i>
         ly
        </i>
        was preserved, hence
        <i>
         lyuk
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Hungarian" title="Help:IPA/Hungarian">
          [juk]
         </a>
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">
         Swedish
        </a>
        ,
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /lj/
        </span>
        turned into
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /j/
        </span>
        , but the spelling
        <i>
         lj
        </i>
        was preserved, hence
        <i>
         ljus
        </i>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Swedish" title="Help:IPA/Swedish">
          [jʉːs]
         </a>
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        In
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
         Portuguese
        </a>
        , yeísmo is observed mainly among speakers of the
        <a href="/wiki/Caipira_dialect" title="Caipira dialect">
         Caipira dialect
        </a>
        (Brazil), who pronounce both
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ʎ/
        </span>
        (spelled ⟨lh⟩) and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /j/
        </span>
        (spelled ⟨i⟩) as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [j]
        </span>
        . Hence for those speakers
        <i>
         teia
        </i>
        "web" and
        <i>
         telha
        </i>
        "roof tile" are both pronounced as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">
          [teja]
         </a>
        </span>
        , while in other dialects the later is
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">
          [teʎa]
         </a>
        </span>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
