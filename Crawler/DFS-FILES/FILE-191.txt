      <p>
       The
       <b>
        meridian 15° east of
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Meridian" title="Prime Meridian">
         Greenwich
        </a>
       </b>
       is a line of
       <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">
        longitude
       </a>
       that extends from the
       <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">
        North Pole
       </a>
       across the
       <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">
        Arctic Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">
        Southern Ocean
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">
        Antarctica
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">
        South Pole
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <p>
       The 15th meridian east forms a
       <a href="/wiki/Great_circle" title="Great circle">
        great circle
       </a>
       with the
       <a href="/wiki/165th_meridian_west" title="165th meridian west">
        165th meridian west
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The meridian is the central axis of
       <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Time" title="Central European Time">
        Central European Time
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
