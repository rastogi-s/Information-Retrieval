      <p>
       <b>
        Andean Spanish
       </b>
       is a dialect of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       spoken in the central
       <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">
        Andes
       </a>
       , from western
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
        Venezuela
       </a>
       , southern
       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">
        Colombia
       </a>
       , with influence as far south as northern
       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
        Chile
       </a>
       and Northwestern
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       , passing through
       <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">
        Ecuador
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
        Peru
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">
        Bolivia
       </a>
       . It is influenced principally by
       <a href="/wiki/Castilian_Spanish" title="Castilian Spanish">
        Castilian
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Canarian_Spanish" title="Canarian Spanish">
        Canarian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_Spanish" title="Andalusian Spanish">
        Andalusian Spanish
       </a>
       , which is favoured in the cities, but in rural areas and some cities, there is influence of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quechua_languages" title="Quechua languages">
        Quechua
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Aymara_language" title="Aymara language">
        Aymara
       </a>
       , and other
       <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">
        indigenous languages
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">
        Phonology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Andean_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Phonology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        In Andean Spanish, the
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /s/
        </span>
        is never aspirated in the final position and so is pronounced
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [s]
        </span>
        , not
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [h]
        </span>
        , but it is sometimes pronounced apical, rather than laminal,
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lip320_1-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-Lip320-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
        a trait characteristic of Northern Spain. The latter sound is transitional between
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_alveolar_fricative#Voiceless_alveolar_sibilant" title="Voiceless alveolar fricative">
          s
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiceless postalveolar fricative">
          ʃ
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        , unique in the Americas, and it is associated with a large number of northern Spanish settlers in Andean region.
       </li>
       <li>
        As in every other American dialects, Andean Spanish has
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seseo" title="Seseo">
          seseo
         </a>
        </i>
        (traditional
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /θ/
        </span>
        merges with
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /s/
        </span>
        ). Thus,
        <i>
         casa
        </i>
        ("house") and
        <i>
         caza
        </i>
        ("hunt") are homophones. However, in
        <a href="/wiki/Cusco_Region" title="Cusco Region">
         Cusco Region
        </a>
        , many speakers realize
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /s/
        </span>
        as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [θ]
        </span>
        in some words, particularly in
        <i>
         once, doce, trece
        </i>
        .
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lip320_1-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-Lip320-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
        <i>
         Seseo
        </i>
        is common to all of America, the Canary Islands, and several areas in southern Spain.
       </li>
       <li>
        Especially in the Ecuadorian variant, coda
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /s/
        </span>
        is often voiced to
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Voiced_alveolar_fricative#Voiced_alveolar_sibilant" title="Voiced alveolar fricative">
          z
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        before a voiced consonant (including sonorants) or a before vowel. In the Peruvian variant, it is palatalized before
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /i/
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        In Bolivia, Ecuador, and southern Peru,
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /
         <a href="/wiki/Palatal_lateral_approximant" title="Palatal lateral approximant">
          ʎ
         </a>
         /
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /
         <a href="/wiki/Voiced_palatal_fricative#Palatal" title="Voiced palatal fricative">
          ʝ
         </a>
         /
        </span>
        do not merge (lack of
        <a href="/wiki/Ye%C3%ADsmo" title="Yeísmo">
         yeísmo
        </a>
        ).
       </li>
       <li>
        Often the vowels
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /e/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /i/
        </span>
        or
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /o/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /u/
        </span>
        are merged because of the influence of the trivocal system of Quechua and Aymara.
       </li>
       <li>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /r/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɾ/
        </span>
        are assibilated to
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Raised_alveolar_trill" title="Raised alveolar trill">
          r̝
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voiced_alveolar_tapped_fricative" title="Voiced alveolar tapped fricative">
          ɾ̞
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        , respectively.
       </li>
       <li>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /x/
        </span>
        is velar
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_velar_fricative" title="Voiceless velar fricative">
          x
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        rather than glottal
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_glottal_fricative" title="Voiceless glottal fricative">
          h
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /f/
        </span>
        is realised as bilabial
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Voiceless_bilabial_fricative" title="Voiceless bilabial fricative">
          ɸ
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        , the same one that adds an epenthetic
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /w/
        </span>
        is often confused with
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /x/
        </span>
        .
        <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
         [
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
           <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2015)">
            clarification needed
           </span>
          </a>
         </i>
         ]
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        Emphasis is given to the consonants but the vowels are weakened, especially for unstressed syllables (like in
        <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
         Mexico
        </a>
        , but not as marked).
       </li>
       <li>
        Stress is or tends to be penultimate.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_on_nearby_areas">
        Influence on nearby areas
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Andean_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Influence on nearby areas">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In northwest Argentina and northern Chile today, it is possible to say that there is a certain fusion in the dialects of both countries, but the local dialects are more dominant.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Andean dialect can be heard in the northeast, with respect to the pronunciation and lexicon. The
       <a href="/wiki/Rioplatense_Spanish" title="Rioplatense Spanish">
        Rioplatense dialect
       </a>
       provides some of the pronunciation, a variety of modes, and the Argentine dialect.
      </p>
      <p>
       Rioplatense replaces the Andean use of "tú" as the second person singular familiar pronoun with "vos". It is very similar in Chile, but "tú" and "vos" are there both used as the singular familiar second-person pronoun. Also, there is influence of
       <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Spanish" title="Chilean Spanish">
        Chilean Spanish
       </a>
       and some Andean Spanish.
      </p>
