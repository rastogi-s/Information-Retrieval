      <p>
       The
       <b>
        meridian 100° west of
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Meridian" title="Prime Meridian">
         Greenwich
        </a>
       </b>
       is a line of
       <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">
        longitude
       </a>
       that extends from the
       <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">
        North Pole
       </a>
       across the
       <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">
        Arctic Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">
        Southern Ocean
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">
        Antarctica
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">
        South Pole
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The 100th meridian west forms a
       <a href="/wiki/Great_circle" title="Great circle">
        great circle
       </a>
       with the
       <a href="/wiki/80th_meridian_east" title="80th meridian east">
        80th meridian east
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">
        United States
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=100th_meridian_west&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United States">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:100th_meridian_US.svg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="593" data-file-width="959" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/100th_meridian_US.svg/220px-100th_meridian_US.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/100th_meridian_US.svg/330px-100th_meridian_US.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/100th_meridian_US.svg/440px-100th_meridian_US.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:100th_meridian_US.svg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Part of the border between
         <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
          Texas
         </a>
         and
         <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">
          Oklahoma
         </a>
         is defined by the 100th meridian west.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        United States
       </a>
       the meridian 100° west of Greenwich forms the eastern border of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texas_panhandle" title="Texas panhandle">
        Texas panhandle
       </a>
       with
       <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">
        Oklahoma
       </a>
       (which traces its origin to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adams-On%C3%ADs_Treaty" title="Adams-Onís Treaty">
        Adams-Onís Treaty
       </a>
       in 1819 which settled the border between
       <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">
        New Spain
       </a>
       and the United States between the
       <a href="/wiki/Red_River_of_the_South" title="Red River of the South">
        Red River
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_River" title="Arkansas River">
        Arkansas River
       </a>
       ).
       <a href="/wiki/Dodge_City,_Kansas" title="Dodge City, Kansas">
        Dodge City, Kansas
       </a>
       lies exactly at the intersection of the Arkansas River and the 100th meridian.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the central
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">
        Great Plains
       </a>
       , the meridian roughly marks the western boundary of the normal reach of moist air from the
       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">
        Gulf of Mexico
       </a>
       , and the approximate boundary (although some areas do push the boundary slightly farther east) between the
       <a href="/wiki/Semi-arid_climate" title="Semi-arid climate">
        semi-arid climate
       </a>
       to the west and the
       <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">
        humid continental
       </a>
       (north of about
       <a href="/wiki/37th_parallel_north" title="37th parallel north">
        37°N
       </a>
       ) and
       <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">
        humid subtropical
       </a>
       (south of about
       <a href="/wiki/37th_parallel_north" title="37th parallel north">
        37°N
       </a>
       ) climates to the east. The type of
       <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">
        agriculture
       </a>
       west of the meridian typically relies heavily on
       <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">
        irrigation
       </a>
       . Historically the meridian has often been taken as a rough boundary between the eastern and western United States. White settlement, spreading westward after the
       <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
        American Civil War
       </a>
       , settled the area around this meridian during the 1870s.
      </p>
      <p>
       A sign across
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Highway_30" title="U.S. Highway 30">
        U.S. Highway 30
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Cozad,_Nebraska" title="Cozad, Nebraska">
        Cozad, Nebraska
       </a>
       , marks the place where the 100th meridian intersects with the routes of the
       <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Trail" title="Oregon Trail">
        Oregon Trail
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Pony_Express" title="Pony Express">
        Pony Express
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_transcontinental_railroad_(North_America)" title="First transcontinental railroad (North America)">
        transcontinental railroad
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Highway" title="Lincoln Highway">
        Lincoln Highway
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">
        In popular culture
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=100th_meridian_west&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Wallace_Stegner" title="Wallace Stegner">
        Wallace Stegner
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        Beyond the Hundredth Meridian
       </i>
       (1954), is a biography of
       <a href="/wiki/John_Wesley_Powell" title="John Wesley Powell">
        John Wesley Powell
       </a>
       , an explorer of the American West.
      </p>
      <p>
       The song "
       <a href="/wiki/At_the_Hundredth_Meridian" title="At the Hundredth Meridian">
        At the Hundredth Meridian
       </a>
       " by
       <a href="/wiki/The_Tragically_Hip" title="The Tragically Hip">
        The Tragically Hip
       </a>
       is about the 100th meridian west, specifically in Canada, and how it has traditionally been considered "where the great plains begin."
      </p>
