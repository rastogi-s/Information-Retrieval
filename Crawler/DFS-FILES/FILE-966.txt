      <p>
       <b>
        Western Lombard
       </b>
       is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romance_language" title="Romance language">
        Romance language
       </a>
       spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       , in the
       <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">
        Lombard
       </a>
       provinces
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Milan" title="Province of Milan">
        of Milan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Monza_and_Brianza" title="Province of Monza and Brianza">
        Monza
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Varese" title="Province of Varese">
        Varese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Como" title="Province of Como">
        Como
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Lecco" title="Province of Lecco">
        Lecco
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Sondrio" title="Province of Sondrio">
        Sondrio
       </a>
       , a small part
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Cremona" title="Province of Cremona">
        of Cremona
       </a>
       (except
       <a href="/wiki/Crema,_Lombardy" title="Crema, Lombardy">
        Crema
       </a>
       and its neighbours),
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Lodi" title="Province of Lodi">
        Lodi
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Pavia" title="Province of Pavia">
        Pavia
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">
        Piedmont
       </a>
       provinces
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Novara" title="Province of Novara">
        of Novara
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Verbano-Cusio-Ossola" title="Province of Verbano-Cusio-Ossola">
        Verbano-Cusio-Ossola
       </a>
       , the eastern part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Alessandria" title="Province of Alessandria">
        Province of Alessandria
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Tortona" title="Tortona">
        Tortona
       </a>
       ), a small part
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Vercelli" title="Province of Vercelli">
        of Vercelli
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Valsesia" title="Valsesia">
        Valsesia
       </a>
       ), and
       <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
        Switzerland
       </a>
       (the Canton of
       <a href="/wiki/Ticino" title="Ticino">
        Ticino
       </a>
       and part of the Canton of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graub%C3%BCnden" title="Graubünden">
        Graubünden
       </a>
       ). After the name of the region involved, land of the former
       <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Milan" title="Duchy of Milan">
        Duchy of Milan
       </a>
       , this language is often referred to as
       <b>
        Insubric
       </b>
       (see
       <a href="/wiki/Insubria" title="Insubria">
        Insubria
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Insubres" title="Insubres">
        Insubres
       </a>
       ) or
       <b>

       , or, after Clemente Merlo,
       <i>
        <b>
         Cisabduano
        </b>
       </i>
       (literally "of this side of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adda_River" title="Adda River">
        Adda River
       </a>
       ").
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>

          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">

          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Western_Lombard_and_Italian">
        Western Lombard and Italian
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Western Lombard and Italian">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In Italian-speaking contexts, Western Lombard is often incorrectly called a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Western Lombard and Standard Italian are very different.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ethnologue_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ethnologue-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Some speakers of Lombard varieties may have difficulty understanding each other and require a standard to communicate, but all Western Lombard varieties are mutually intelligible.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ethnologue_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ethnologue-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Western Lombard is relatively homogeneous (much more so than
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Lombard_language" title="Eastern Lombard language">
        Eastern Lombard language
       </a>
       ), though it does present a number of variations,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       mainly in relation to the
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel" title="Vowel">
        vowels
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /o/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ɔ/
       </span>
       and the development of
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /ts/
       </span>
       into
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /s/
       </span>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       At the present time, Western Lombard has no official status in
       <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">
        Lombardy
       </a>
       or anywhere else. The only
       <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">
        official language
       </a>
       in Lombardy is
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar">
        Grammar
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Grammar">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The general lines of diachronics of Western Lombard plural declension are drawn here, with reference to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milanese" title="Milanese">
        Milanese
       </a>
       orthography:
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Feminine">
        Feminine
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Feminine">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The bulk of feminine words ends with the inflection
       <i>
        -a
       </i>
       ; the feminine plural is non-inflected (
       <i>
        la legora
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        i legor
       </i>
       ;
       <i>
        la cadrega
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        i cadregh
       </i>
       ). The final vowel finds
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (May 2014)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       its original length (in non-final syllable you can't hear the difference)
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (June 2009)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       that's often long when followed by a voiced consonant, short when followed by a voiceless consonant. When the stem ends with a particular group of consonants there can be the addition of a final
       <i>
        -i
       </i>
       or of a schwa between consonants (for example: in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milanese" title="Milanese">
        Milanese
       </a>
       sing.
       <i>
        scendra
       </i>
       , plur.
       <i>
        scendr
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        scender
       </i>
       ). For adjectives, the plural form and masculine form are often the same.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Masculine">
        Masculine
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Masculine">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The bulk of masculine nouns end without inflections and plural masculine is always non-inflected (
       <i>
        el tramvaj
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        i tramvaj
       </i>
       ;
       <i>
        el lett
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        i lett
       </i>
       ). When the word stem terminates with a particular group of consonants there can be, in both singular and plural forms, the addition of a schwa between consonants. When the addition of schwa appears unnatural, a final
       <i>
        -o
       </i>
       (pron. /u/) is added to singular nouns,
       <i>
        -i
       </i>
       for plurals.
      </p>
      <p>
       The masculine words ending in
       <i>
        -in
       </i>
       , or, less commonly, in
       <i>
        -ett
       </i>
       , have plural
       <i>
        itt
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        fiolin
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        fiolitt
       </i>
       ). The masculine words ending in
       <i>
        -ll
       </i>
       have plura
       <i>
        -j
       </i>
       derived from dropping of
       <i>
        -ll-
       </i>
       and the addition of
       <i>
        -i
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        el sidell
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        i sidej
       </i>
       ;
       <i>
        el porscell
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        i porscej
       </i>
       ;
       <i>
        el cavall
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        i cavaj
       </i>
       ). The same occurs in the determinate article: singular
       <i>
        ell
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        el
       </i>
       , plural
       <i>
        elli
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        ej
       </i>
       &gt;
       <i>
        i
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Masculine words ending in
       <i>
        -a
       </i>
       are unvarying. These are proper names, words from ancient Greek or idiomatic words such as
       <i>
        pirla
       </i>
       , a derogatory term for a person.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Varieties">
        Varieties
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Varieties">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Western Lombard can be divided into four main varieties, referred by many Italian linguists
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
          <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2009)">
           who?
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       as
       <i>
        lombardo alpino
       </i>
       (spoken in the provinces of Sondrio and of Verbania,
       <a href="/wiki/Sopraceneri" title="Sopraceneri">
        Sopraceneri
       </a>
       of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canton_Ticino" title="Canton Ticino">
        Canton Ticino
       </a>
       and Grigioni in Switzerland),
       <i>
        lombardo-prealpino occidentale
       </i>
       (spoken in the provinces of Como, Varese and Lecco, Lugano and its neighbors in Canton Ticino),
       <i>
        basso-lombardo occidentale
       </i>
       (Pavia and Lodi), and
       <i>
        macromilanese
       </i>
       (provinces of Milan, Monza, Novara and Valsesia of Vercelli). The boundaries are obviously schematic, since the political division in provinces and municipalities are usually independent from languages spoken.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">
        Writing
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Lombard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Writing">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The most important orthography in Western Lombard literature is the
       <a href="/wiki/Classical_Milanese_orthography" title="Classical Milanese orthography">
        Classical Milanese orthography
       </a>
       . It was used by
       <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Porta" title="Carlo Porta">
        Carlo Porta
       </a>
       (1775–1821) and
       <a href="/wiki/Delio_Tessa" title="Delio Tessa">
        Delio Tessa
       </a>
       (1886–1939). It was perfected by the Circolo Filologico di Milano.
       <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Insubric_orthographies" title="Alternative Insubric orthographies">
        Other orthographies
       </a>
       are the Ticinese, the Comasca, the Bosina, the Nuaresat, and the Lecchese.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

       </a>

       </a>
       .
