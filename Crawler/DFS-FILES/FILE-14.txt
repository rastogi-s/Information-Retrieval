       <b>
        Argentina Brunetti
       </b>
       (August 31, 1907 – December 20, 2005)

       </a>
       stage and film actress and writer.

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
        Biography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentina_Brunetti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Brunetti was born
       <b>
        Argentina Ferrau
       </b>
       in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires,_Argentina" title="Buenos Aires, Argentina">
        Buenos Aires, Argentina
       </a>
       to Italian parents; her mother was the Sicilian actress
       <a href="/wiki/Mimi_Aguglia" title="Mimi Aguglia">
        Mimi Aguglia
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       She began her show-business career at the age of three with a walk-on role in the opera
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cavalleria_Rusticana" title="Cavalleria Rusticana">
         Cavalleria Rusticana
        </a>
       </i>
       and followed in the footsteps of her mother, performing supporting roles on stage throughout Europe and South America.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-i_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-i-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1937, she was placed under contract to
       <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">
        MGM
       </a>
       and began dubbing the voices of
       <a href="/wiki/Jeanette_MacDonald" title="Jeanette MacDonald">
        Jeanette MacDonald
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Norma_Shearer" title="Norma Shearer">
        Norma Shearer
       </a>
       into Italian. She became a narrator for the Voice of America, interviewing American movie stars for broadcast in Italy. At the same time, she made her movie debut in the classic
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Wonderful_Life" title="It's a Wonderful Life">
         It's a Wonderful Life
        </a>
       </i>
       (1946) as Mrs. Maria Martini.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ia_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ia-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Brunetti wrote and performed in daily radio shows; she became a member of the
       <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Foreign_Press_Association" title="Hollywood Foreign Press Association">
        Hollywood Foreign Press Association
       </a>
       , writing numerous articles on Hollywood personalities; she authored books, wrote music, and appeared in nearly 60 television programmes and almost 70 films.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ia_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ia-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       She hosted a weekly weblog on the Internet called
       <i>
        Argentina Brunetti's Hollywood Stories
       </i>
       , which her son plans to continue running, and wrote a
       <a href="/wiki/Biographical_novel" title="Biographical novel">
        biographical novel
       </a>
       called
       <i>
        In Sicilian Company
       </i>
       . She continued to act into her nineties, most notably as a relative from the Old World who visits and stays with the (wrong) Barone family in a 1999 episode of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Everybody_Loves_Raymond" title="Everybody Loves Raymond">
         Everybody Loves Raymond
        </a>
       </i>
       ; her last role was in 2002.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">
        Marriage
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentina_Brunetti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriage">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       She wed Miro Brunetti, a foreign correspondent in Hollywood. The two helped to co-found the Hollywood Foreign Press Association. The couple had one son, Mario. Miro Brunetti died in 1966. Argentina Brunetti never remarried.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="Last_years.2Fdeath">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Last_years/death">
        Last years/death
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentina_Brunetti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Last years/death">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       She moved to Rome in 2004 to be with her family and died there from natural causes on December 20, 2005, aged 98.
      </p>
