      <p>
       <b>
        Chilote
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       spoken on the southern Chilean islands of
       <a href="/wiki/Chilo%C3%A9_Archipelago" title="Chiloé Archipelago">
        Chiloé Archipelago
       </a>
       (Spanish:
       <i>
        Archipiélago de Chiloé
       </i>
       or simply,
       <i>
        Chiloé
       </i>
       ). It has distinct differences from standard
       <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Spanish" title="Chilean Spanish">
        Chilean Spanish
       </a>
       in accent, pronunciation, grammar and vocabulary, especially by influences from local dialect of
       <a href="/wiki/Mapuche_language" title="Mapuche language">
        Mapuche language
       </a>
       (called
       <i>
        huilliche
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        veliche
       </i>
       ) and some
       <a href="/wiki/Conservative_(language)" title="Conservative (language)">
        conservative traits
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       After the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Curalaba" title="Battle of Curalaba">
        battle of Curalaba
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Destruction_of_the_Seven_Cities" title="Destruction of the Seven Cities">
        Destruction of the Seven Cities
       </a>
       Chiloé was further isolated from the rest of
       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
        Chile
       </a>
       and developed a culture with little influences from Spain or mainland Chile. During the 17th and 18th century most of the archipelagoes population was
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bilingual" title="Bilingual">
        bilingual
       </a>
       and according to
       <a href="/wiki/John_Byron" title="John Byron">
        John Byron
       </a>
       many Spaniards preferred to use the Indian language by considering it more beautiful.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Around the same time, governor
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Narciso_de_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Narciso de Santa María (page does not exist)">
        Narciso de Santa María
       </a>
       complained that Spanish settlers in the islands could not speak Spanish properly but did well on veliche and that this second language was more used.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
