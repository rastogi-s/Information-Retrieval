      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Association of Academies of the Spanish Language
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         Asociación de Academias de la Lengua Española
        </span>
       </i>
       , ASALE) is the entity which regulates the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       . It was created in
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
        Mexico
       </a>
       in 1951 and represents the union of all the separate academies in the Spanish-speaking world. The association publishes reference works on the Spanish language and commemorative editions of Hispanic literature, among other publications.

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_of_Academies_of_the_Spanish_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Through the initiative of then-president of Mexico
       <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Alem%C3%A1n_Vald%C3%A9s" title="Miguel Alemán Valdés">
        Miguel Alemán Valdés
       </a>
       , the first congress of academies convened with the purpose of maintaining the integrity of and fostering the further growth of Spanish.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The meeting was held from April 23 to May 6, 1951 and resulted in the creation of the association
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and its permanent commission
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       . The
       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Spanish_Academy" title="Royal Spanish Academy">
        Royal Spanish Academy
       </a>
       (Spanish:
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         Real Academia Española
        </span>
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         RAE
        </span>
       </i>
       ) was not present at the initial meeting but participated in the permanent commission.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Ever since the second congress convened in 1956, the
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         RAE
        </span>
       </i>
       has been a regular participant.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In 2000 the association organized the School of Hispanic Lexicography and the Carolina Foundation to promote Spanish lexicography
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       , and together with the
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         RAE
        </span>
       </i>
       , the association earned the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Asturias_Awards" title="Prince of Asturias Awards">
        Prince of Asturias Award
       </a>
       for peace.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       An academy for
       <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
        Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       was created in 2013 and joined the association in 2016.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Works">
        Works
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_of_Academies_of_the_Spanish_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Works">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The collaboration between
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         RAE
        </span>
       </i>
       and the other academies was expressed in the coauthorship, since the 22nd edition published in 2001, of the Dictionary of the Spanish Language (Spanish:
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         Diccionario de la Lengua Española
        </span>
       </i>
       ), and the 1999 edition of the Orthography (Spanish:
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         Ortografía
        </span>
       </i>
       ) was considered a true pan-Hispanic work. Joint projects include the editing of the Grammar (Spanish:
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         <i>
          Gramática
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       ) and the compilation of the Dictionary of Americanisms (Spanish:
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         <i>
          Diccionario de americanismos
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       ).
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">
        Organization
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_of_Academies_of_the_Spanish_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Organization">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The association convenes every four years, led by a permanent commission composed of a president (
       <a href="/wiki/Dar%C3%ADo_Villanueva" title="Darío Villanueva">
        Darío Villanueva
       </a>
       as of October 2017), secretary general (
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Francisco_Javier_P%C3%A9rez&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Francisco Javier Pérez (page does not exist)">
        Francisco Javier Pérez
       </a>
       as of October 2017), treasurer, and at least two board members drawn from the associated academies, whose nomination rotate annually.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       During the Third Congress of Academies, held in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1,_Colombia" title="Bogotá, Colombia">
        Bogotá, Colombia
       </a>
       , in 1960, an agreement was reached whereby the governments of countries with a member in the association would be obliged to provide financial support to their respective academies and the greater association.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Belize" title="Languages of Belize">

       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Gibraltar" title="Languages of Gibraltar">

       </a>
       , or
       <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Andorra" title="Languages of Andorra">
        Andorra
       </a>
       to have their own academies, despite each having a majority Spanish-speaking population either as a first or second language. There is also a substantial Spanish population in
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">
        Western Sahara
