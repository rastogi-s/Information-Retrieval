      <p>
       <b>
        West Iberian
       </b>
       is a branch of the
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       that includes
       <a href="/wiki/Castilian_languages" title="Castilian languages">
        Castilian
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Judaeo-Spanish" title="Judaeo-Spanish">
        Judaeo-Spanish/Ladino
       </a>
       ),
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astur-Leonese" title="Astur-Leonese">
        Astur-Leonese
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">
        Asturian
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Extremaduran_language" title="Extremaduran language">
        Extremaduran
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonese_language" title="Leonese language">
        Leonese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mirandese_language" title="Mirandese language">
        Mirandese
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Cantabrian_dialect" title="Cantabrian dialect">
        Cantabrian
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unesco_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-unesco-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       ), and the modern descendants of
       <a href="/wiki/Galician-Portuguese" title="Galician-Portuguese">
        Galician-Portuguese
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
        Galician
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Fala_language" title="Fala language">
        Fala language
       </a>
       ). According to
       <a href="/wiki/Historical_linguistics" title="Historical linguistics">
        historical linguistic
       </a>
       analysis, these languages are significantly closer to each other in historical terms than to any other living language in the peninsula—including
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       , the other major Romance language of the
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">
        Iberian Peninsula
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Until a few centuries ago, they formed a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect_continuum" title="Dialect continuum">
        dialect continuum
       </a>
       covering the western, central and southern parts of the Iberian Peninsula—excepting the
       <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">
        Basque
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan-speaking
       </a>
       territories. This is still the situation in a few regions, particularly in the northern part of the peninsula, but due to the differing sociopolitical histories of these languages (independence of
       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
        Portugal
       </a>
       since the early 12th century, unification of
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       in the late 15th century under the
       <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">
        Catholic Monarchs
       </a>
       , who privileged Castilian over the other Iberian languages), Spanish and Portuguese have tended to overtake and to a large extent absorb their sister languages while they kept diverging from each other.
      </p>
      <p>
       There is controversy over whether the members of the modern Galician-Portuguese and Astur-Leonese subgroups are
       <a href="/wiki/Language" title="Language">
        languages
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialects
       </a>
       . A common, though disputed, classification is to state that Portuguese and Galician are separate languages, as are Asturian, Leonese, and Mirandese. Cantabrian and Extremaduran are considered codialects of the Leonese language for UNESCO, whereas the latter is a Castilian dialect in the ISO codes.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Papiamento" title="Papiamento">
        Papiamento
       </a>
       is a West Iberian
       <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">
        creole language
       </a>
       spoken in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_West_Indies" title="Dutch West Indies">
        Dutch West Indies
       </a>
       and believed to
       <a href="/wiki/Stratum_(linguistics)" title="Stratum (linguistics)">
        be derived
       </a>
       from Portuguese,
       <a href="/wiki/Judaeo-Portuguese" title="Judaeo-Portuguese">
        Judaeo-Portuguese
       </a>
       , Spanish and
       <a href="/wiki/Judaeo-Spanish" title="Judaeo-Spanish">
        Judaeo-Spanish
       </a>
       .
      </p>
