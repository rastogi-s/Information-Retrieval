    45th parallel south
   </h1>

          45th parallel south
         </b>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        45th parallel south
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">
        circle of latitude
       </a>
       that is 45
       <a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">
        °
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/South" title="South">
        south
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth's
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equator
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:45th-south.JPG">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/45th-south.JPG/250px-45th-south.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/45th-south.JPG/375px-45th-south.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/45th-south.JPG/500px-45th-south.JPG 2x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:45th-south.JPG" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Highway sign marking the 45th parallel in New Zealand.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       It is the line that marks the theoretical halfway point between the
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equator
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">
        South Pole
       </a>
       . The true halfway point is 16.2 km (10.1 mi) south of this parallel because
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       is not a perfect
       <a href="/wiki/Sphere" title="Sphere">
        sphere
       </a>
       , but
       <a href="/wiki/Spheroid" title="Spheroid">
        bulges
       </a>
       at the equator and is
       <a href="/wiki/Flattening" title="Flattening">
        flattened
       </a>
       at the poles.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Unlike its
       <a href="/wiki/45th_parallel_north" title="45th parallel north">
        northern counterpart
       </a>
       , almost all (97%) of it passes through open ocean. It crosses the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_Ocean" title="South Atlantic Ocean">
        South Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">
        Indian Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">
        Australasia
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">
        New Zealand
       </a>
       and just south of
       <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">
        Tasmania
       </a>
       ), the
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">
        Southern Ocean
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Patagonia" title="Patagonia">
        Patagonia
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       At this latitude,
       <a href="/wiki/Daytime" title="Daytime">
        daytime
       </a>
       lasts for 15 hours, 37 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/December_solstice" title="December solstice">
        December solstice
       </a>
       and 8 hours, 46 minutes during the
       <a href="/wiki/June_solstice" title="June solstice">
        June solstice
       </a>
       .
      </p>
