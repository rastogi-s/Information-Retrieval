      <p>
       <b>
        Amazonic Spanish
       </b>
       (
       <i>
        español amazónico
       </i>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        Loreto-Ucayali Spanish
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        Jungle Spanish
       </b>
       (
       <i>
        español de la selva
       </i>
       ), is a variety of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       spoken in the Amazon, especially in the Peruvian Amazon provinces of
       <a href="/wiki/Loreto_Region" title="Loreto Region">
        Loreto
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ucayali_Region" title="Ucayali Region">
        Ucayali
       </a>
       . Amazonic Spanish is also spoken in areas of Brazil adjoining Loreto and Ucayali and in the
       <a href="/wiki/Amazonas_Department" title="Amazonas Department">
        Amazonas Department
       </a>
       of Colombia.

        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ethnologue.com_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ethnologue.com-4">
         [4]
        </a>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Distinctive_features">
        Distinctive features
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Amazonic_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distinctive features">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Morphosyntax">
        Morphosyntax
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Amazonic_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Morphosyntax">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       One of the distinguishing features of Amazonic Spanish is the method of constructing the possessive form: speakers say "de la
       <i>
        X
       </i>
       su
       <i>
        Y
       </i>
       " (of the
       <i>
        X
       </i>
       its
       <i>
        Y
       </i>
       ), instead of standard Spanish "la
       <i>
        Y
       </i>
       de
       <i>
        X
       </i>
       " (the
       <i>
        Y
       </i>
       of
       <i>
        X
       </i>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spanish-in-the-world.net_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-spanish-in-the-world.net-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Another distinctive grammatical feature is the use of possessive forms in place of certain genitive forms; compare standard Spanish "Le preguntó a la yaminahua
       <i>
        delante de mí
       </i>
       " (He asked the Yaminahua woman
       <i>
        in front of me
       </i>
       ) with the Loreto-Ucayali "Le preguntó a la yaminahua
       <i>
        en mi delante
       </i>
       " (He asked the Yaminahua woman
       <i>
        in my front
       </i>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Personal names are prefixed with a definite article (
       <i>
        el
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        la
       </i>
       , depending on the gender).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spanish-in-the-world.net_5-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-spanish-in-the-world.net-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">
        Phonology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Amazonic_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Phonology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /x/
       </span>
       and especially the sequence
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /xw/
       </span>
       are frequently realized as
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [f]
       </span>
       (as in Juana
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        [ˈfana]
       </span>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spanish-in-the-world.net_5-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-spanish-in-the-world.net-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Amazonic Spanish also incorporates words and expressions borrowed from local indigenous languages.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Status">
        Status
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Amazonic_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Status">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Amazonic Spanish is sometimes classified as a separate language from standard Spanish, as for example by the
       <a href="/wiki/Ethnologue" title="Ethnologue">
        Ethnologue
       </a>
       ; Amazonic Spanish even has its own ISO 639-3 code: spq.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ethnologue.com_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ethnologue.com-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, Ethnologue's reasons for doing this are poorly documented.
      </p>
