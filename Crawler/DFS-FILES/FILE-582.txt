      <p>
       <b>
        Equatoguinean Spanish
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Español ecuatoguineano
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is the variety of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
        Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       . This is the only Spanish variety that holds national official status in
       <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">
        Sub-Saharan Africa
       </a>
       . It is regulated by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Equatoguinean_Academy_of_the_Spanish_Language" title="Equatoguinean Academy of the Spanish Language">
        Equatoguinean Academy of the Spanish Language
       </a>
       and is spoken by about 90% of the population, estimated at 1,170,308 for the year 2010
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       (though population figures for this country are highly dubious), all of them second-language speakers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Equatoguinean_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Guinea" title="Spanish Guinea">
        Spanish Guinea
       </a>
       (along with the islands of
       <a href="/wiki/Bioko" title="Bioko">
        Bioko
       </a>
       , formerly Fernando Pó) became a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_colonial_empire" title="Spanish colonial empire">
        Spanish colony
       </a>
       after being obtained from Portugal in exchange for American territories in 1778 under the
       <a href="/wiki/First_Treaty_of_San_Ildefonso" title="First Treaty of San Ildefonso">
        First Treaty of San Ildefonso
       </a>
       . Full colonization of the continental interior was not established until the end of the 19th century. The present nation of Equatorial Guinea became independent on October 12, 1968.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote,_1969.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="298" data-file-width="467" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote%2C_1969.jpg/220px-Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote%2C_1969.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote%2C_1969.jpg/330px-Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote%2C_1969.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote%2C_1969.jpg/440px-Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote%2C_1969.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Old_Equatorial_Guinean_1000_pesetas_banknote,_1969.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         1000 Equatoguinean pesetas banknote from 1969
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       While the country has maintained its indigenous linguistic diversity, Spanish is the national and official language. Spanish is spoken by about 90% of the population in Bioko and coastal
       <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Muni" title="Río Muni">
        Río Muni
       </a>
       and between 60% to 70% in the interior of Río Muni.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-psu_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-psu-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Features">
        Features
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Equatoguinean_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Equatoguinean Spanish is more like
       <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Spanish" title="Peninsular Spanish">
        Peninsular Spanish
       </a>
       than
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_Americas" title="Spanish language in the Americas">
        American Spanish
       </a>
       dialects. Here are some features of Equatoguinean Spanish:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Both syllable- and word-final /s/ are strongly pronounced.
       </li>
       <li>
        /r/ and /rr/ are merged.
       </li>
       <li>
        Articles are omitted.
       </li>
       <li>
        The pronoun
        <i>
         usted
        </i>
        can be used with the
        <i>
         tú
        </i>
        verbal conjugation.
       </li>
       <li>
        There is no distinction between indicative and subjunctive moods.
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         Vosotros
        </i>
        is used interchangeably with
        <i>
         ustedes
        </i>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        The preposition
        <i>
         en
        </i>
        replaces
        <i>
         a
        </i>
        to mark a destination:
        <i>
         voy
        </i>
        <i>
         <b>
          en
         </b>
        </i>
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Bata,_Equatorial_Guinea" title="Bata, Equatorial Guinea">
          Bata
         </a>
        </i>
        instead of
        <i>
         voy
        </i>
        <i>
         <b>
          a
         </b>
        </i>
        <i>
         Bata
        </i>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Comparison_to_Caribbean_Spanish">
        Comparison to Caribbean Spanish
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Equatoguinean_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Comparison to Caribbean Spanish">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       According to John Lipski, a comparison between the Spanish spoken in Equatorial Guinea and
       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Spanish" title="Caribbean Spanish">
        Caribbean Spanish
       </a>
       does not hint at an influence of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_languages" title="African languages">
        African languages
       </a>
       in Caribbean Spanish, despite some earlier theories. Both varieties of Spanish are very different. The main influence on the Spanish spoken in Equatorial Guinea seems to be the varieties spoken by native Spanish colonizers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski1_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski1-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In a different paper, however, Lipski admits that the
       <a href="/wiki/Phonotactics" title="Phonotactics">
        phonotactics
       </a>
       of African languages might have reinforced, in Caribbean Spanish, the consonant reduction that was already taking place in Spanish from Southern Spain.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski2_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski2-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
