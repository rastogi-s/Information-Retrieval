        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ECEF.svg">
         <img alt="Earth Centered, Earth Fixed coordinates" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="787" data-file-width="975" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/ECEF.svg/220px-ECEF.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/ECEF.svg/330px-ECEF.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/ECEF.svg/440px-ECEF.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>

        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">

         </div>
         Earth Centered, Earth Fixed coordinates in relation to latitude and longitude.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <b>
        ECEF
       </b>
       ("
       <b>
        earth-centered, earth-fixed
       </b>
       "), also known as
       <b>
        ECR
       </b>
       ("
       <b>
        earth-centered rotational
       </b>
       "), is a
       <a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">
        geographic coordinate system
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cartesian_coordinate" title="Cartesian coordinate">
        Cartesian coordinate
       </a>
       system, and is sometimes known as a "conventional terrestrial" system.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It represents positions (in meters) as X, Y, and Z coordinates. The point (0,0,0) is defined as the center of mass of the earth,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       hence the name "earth-centered." Its axes are aligned with the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=International_reference_pole&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="International reference pole (page does not exist)">
        international reference pole
       </a>
       (IRP) and
       <a href="/wiki/IERS_Reference_Meridian" title="IERS Reference Meridian">
        international reference meridian
       </a>
       (IRM) that are fixed with respect to the surface of the earth,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceFrames_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ReferenceFrames-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       hence the description "earth-fixed." This term can cause confusion since the earth does not rotate about the z-axis (unlike an inertial system such as
       <a href="/wiki/Earth-centered_inertial" title="Earth-centered inertial">
        ECI
       </a>
       ), and is therefore alternatively called ECR.
      </p>
      <p>
       The z-axis extends through
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_North" title="True North">
        True north
       </a>
       , which does not coincide with the instantaneous earth rotational axis.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceFrames_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ReferenceFrames-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The slight "wobbling" of the rotational axis is known as
       <a href="/wiki/Polar_motion" title="Polar motion">
        polar motion
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The x-axis intersects the sphere of the earth at 0°
       <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">
        latitude
       </a>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_equator" title="The equator">
        the equator
       </a>
       ) and 0°
       <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">
        longitude
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Prime_meridian" title="Prime meridian">
        prime meridian in Greenwich
       </a>
       ). This means that ECEF rotates with the earth, and therefore coordinates of a point fixed on the surface of the earth do not change. Conversion from a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WGS84" title="WGS84">
        WGS84
       </a>
       datum to ECEF can be used as an intermediate step in converting velocities to the
       <a href="/wiki/North_east_down" title="North east down">
        north east down
       </a>
       coordinate system.
      </p>
