      <p>
       <b>
        Donato Francisco Ndongo-Bidyogo Makina
       </b>
       (born December 12, 1950 in Niefang,
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Guinea" title="Spanish Guinea">
        Spanish Guinea
       </a>
       ) is an
       <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
        Equatorial Guinean
       </a>
       -
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spanish
       </a>
       writer/journalist and part of a movement of young Afro-descended authors who have attributed their African experience and traditions to
       <a href="/wiki/Hispanic" title="Hispanic">
        Hispanic
       </a>
       culture.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Writings">
        Writings
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Donato_Ndongo-Bidyogo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writings">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Some of his best-known books include
       <i>
        Historia y tragedia de Guinea Ecuatorial
       </i>
       (1977) and
       <i>
        El Comercio Español con Africa
       </i>
       (1980), in addition to numerous novels, historical, cultural and political articles written in Spain and for the foreign press. In 1984 he coordinated an anthology of Equatorial Guinean literature and was a finalist for the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A9samo_Prize&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sésamo Prize (page does not exist)">
        Sésamo Prize
       </a>
       for his work entitled "Las Tinieblas de tu Memoria Negra".
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_activities">
        Professional activities
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Donato_Ndongo-Bidyogo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional activities">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Subsequently, he has served as the adjunct director for "Nuestra Señora de África de Madrid" (Our Lady of Africa in Madrid) and the "Centro Cultural Hispano-Guineano de Malabo" (The Center for Hispanic-Guinean Culture in
       <a href="/wiki/Malabo" title="Malabo">
        Malabo
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       Recently he has been a visiting professor at several United States universities.
      </p>
