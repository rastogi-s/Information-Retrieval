      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Organization of Ibero-American States
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         Organização dos Estados Ibero-americanos
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Organización de Estados Iberoamericanos
        </span>
       </i>
       , usually abbreviated
       <b>
        OEI
       </b>
       ), formally the
       <b>
        Organization of Ibero-American States for Education, Science and Culture
       </b>
       , is an
       <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">
        international organization
       </a>
       whose members are the
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       - and
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       -speaking nations of the
       <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">
        Americas
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
        Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       . Its purpose is to promote intergovernmental cooperation among its member nations which will aid the planning and development of regional projects in education, science, technology and the arts. OEI members include
       <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">
        Andorra
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">
        Bolivia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">
        Colombia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">
        Costa Rica
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">
        Cuba
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
        Chile
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">
        Dominican Republic
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">
        Ecuador
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">
        El Salvador
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">
        Guatemala
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
        Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">
        Honduras
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
        Mexico
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">
        Nicaragua
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">
        Panama
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">
        Paraguay
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">
        Peru
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
        Portugal
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
        Uruguay
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
        Venezuela
       </a>
       . Its General Secretariat is located in
       <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">
        Madrid
       </a>
       , with regional offices in Argentina, Brazil, Colombia, El Salvador, Spain, Mexico and Peru and field offices in Chile, Honduras, Nicaragua and Paraguay. Financing of OEI operations and programs is derived from obligatory quotas and voluntary contributions from the governments of member nations and private cultural and educational institutions and foundations. The honorary president of the organization is King
       <a href="/wiki/Felipe_VI_of_Spain" title="Felipe VI of Spain">
        Felipe VI of Spain
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_Ibero-American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">

        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>

      <p>
       The OEI was founded in 1949 as an international agency, the Office of Latin American Education, as a result of the first Latin American Education Congress in Madrid. In 1954 the second congress was held in
       <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">
        Quito
       </a>
       , where it was decided that the OEI would become an intergovernmental organization of sovereign states. On March 15, 1957, at the third Education Congress in
       <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">
        Santo Domingo
       </a>
       , the first statutes of the OEI became effective until 1985.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1979, the fourth congress met in Madrid; in 1983 the fifth congress took place in
       <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">
        Lima
       </a>
       . In May, 1985 an extraordinary meeting of the congress was held in
       <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">
        Bogotá
       </a>
       , where it was decided to change the name of the OEI to its present name (keeping its acronyms and extending its objectives); the Latin American Education Congress became the General Assembly. At the December 1985 directive council meeting in Panama, its members agreed to the present statutes of the OEI, replacing the 1957 text and approving the organization's regulations. Since the 1991
       <a href="/wiki/Ibero-American_Summit" title="Ibero-American Summit">
        Ibero-American Summit
       </a>
       in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guadalajara,_Jalisco" title="Guadalajara, Jalisco">
        Guadalajara
       </a>
       , the OEI has promoted conferences of ministers of education and the execution of the educative, scientific and cultural programs delegated to it.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">
        <span id="Aims_and_objectives">
        </span>
        Objectives
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_Ibero-American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Objectives">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        To contribute to the fortification of knowledge, mutual understanding, integration, solidarity and peace among the Ibero-American peoples, and to spread education, science, technology and culture
       </li>
       <li>
        To encourage the development of education and culture for peace by preparing people to preserve freedom, solidarity and human rights, supporting change which make these possible
       </li>
       <li>
        To collaborate in the interchange of economic, political and cultural influences between Europe and Ibero-America
       </li>
       <li>
        To collaborate with its members ensure that their education systems are compassionate, democratic (socially fair) and productive, preparing for working life
       </li>
       <li>
        To collaborate in the diffusion of a common culture, respecting the uniqueness of its member states, encouraging global advances in science and technology while retaining cultural identity
       </li>
       <li>
        To facilitate relationships among science, technology and society in the Ibero-American countries and citizen understanding of their effects
       </li>
       <li>
        To promote the equitable distribution of cultural, technological and scientific concepts
       </li>
       <li>
        To promote lateral cooperation among member states and between the states and other regional institutions
       </li>
       <li>
        To contribute to the spread of the Spanish and Portuguese languages, encouraging bilingual education while preserving the multicultural identity of the Ibero-American communities with a multilingual culture
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">
        Structure
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_Ibero-American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Structure">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The OEI consists of three bodies: the General Assembly, the Directive Council and the General Secretariat.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="General_Assembly">
        General Assembly
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_Ibero-American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: General Assembly">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The General Assembly, the supreme authority of the organization, is composed of representatives (or delegations) from member States. It establishes OEI policies, evaluates and approves the organization's plan of activities, program and global budget, fixes annual dues and chooses the secretary-general.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Directive_Council">
        Directive Council
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_Ibero-American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Directive Council">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Directive Council is delegated by the General Assembly to control the OEI's government and administration. Made up of the member states' ministers of education (or their representatives), it is governed by the education minister of the country in which the next General Assembly meeting will be held. Its primary function is to analyze and approve the report of activities, programs, the biennial budget and the OEI's financial statement.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="General_Secretariat">
        General Secretariat
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_Ibero-American_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: General Secretariat">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The General Secretariat is delegated by the General Assembly as executive director of the OEI, representing it to governments, international organizations and other institutions. It directs, administers and executes the organization's programs and projects. The General Secretariat has a flexible internal structure, adaptable according to policies, strategies and the plan of activities.
      </p>
