      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Spanish East Indies
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Indias orientales españolas
       </i>
       ;
       <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">
        Filipino
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Silangang Indiyas ng Espanya
       </i>
       ) were the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">
        Spanish territories
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">
        Asia-Pacific
       </a>
       from 1565 until 1899. They comprised the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Islands" title="Mariana Islands">
        Mariana Islands
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Islands" title="Caroline Islands">
        Caroline Islands
       </a>
       (also known as the New Philippine Islands), and briefly also included
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Formosa" title="Spanish Formosa">
        Spanish Formosa
       </a>
       and parts of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spice_Islands" title="Spice Islands">
        Spice Islands
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Cebu_City" title="Cebu City">
        Cebu
       </a>
       was the first seat of government, later transferred to
       <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">
        Manila
       </a>
       . From 1565 to 1821 these territories, together with the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_West_Indies" title="Spanish West Indies">
        Spanish West Indies
       </a>
       , were administered through the
       <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">
        Viceroyalty of New Spain
       </a>
       based in
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">
        Mexico City
       </a>
       . Its administration was then directly made from Spain after the successful independence movements in Mexico.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Spain" title="Monarchy of Spain">
        King of Spain
       </a>
       traditionally styled himself "King of the East and West Indies" (
       <i>
        <span lang="es">
         Rey de las Indias orientales y occidentales
        </span>
       </i>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [a]
        </a>
       </sup>
       After Mexican independence, they were ruled directly from
       <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">
        Madrid
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Administrative affairs of the Spanish East Indies were handled by the
       <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_the_Philippines" title="Captaincy General of the Philippines">
        Captaincy General of the Philippines
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Real_Audiencia_of_Manila" title="Real Audiencia of Manila">
        Real Audiencia of Manila
       </a>
       . As a result of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">
        Spanish–American War
       </a>
       in 1898, most of the islands were occupied by the United States while about 6,000 of the remaining smaller islands were sold to Germany in the
       <a href="/wiki/German%E2%80%93Spanish_Treaty_(1899)" title="German–Spanish Treaty (1899)">
        German–Spanish Treaty
       </a>
       of 1899. The few remaining islands were ceded to the United States when the
       <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Washington_(1900)" title="Treaty of Washington (1900)">
        Treaty of Washington
       </a>
       was ratified in 1901.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main articles:
       <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Philippines_(1521%E2%80%931898)" title="History of the Philippines (1521–1898)">
        History of the Philippines (1521–1898)
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_settlement_in_the_Philippines" title="Spanish settlement in the Philippines">
        Spanish settlement in the Philippines
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/History_of_Guam" title="History of Guam">
        History of Guam
       </a>
      </div>
      <h3>
       <span id="Exploration_and_Settlement_.281521.E2.80.931643.29">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Exploration_and_Settlement_(1521–1643)">
        Exploration and Settlement (1521–1643)
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Exploration and Settlement (1521–1643)">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Spanish contact began on 6 March 1521, when a Spanish expedition led by
       <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">
        Ferdinand Magellan
       </a>
       reached the Mariana Islands. He named Guam and the other islands "
       <i>
        Islas de los Ladrones
       </i>
       " (Islands of the Thieves) because the natives came aboard his galleon (the
       <i>
        Trinidad
       </i>
       ) and pilfered many of its supplies. The expedition later continued its journey west and reached the island of
       <a href="/wiki/Homonhon" title="Homonhon">
        Homonhon
       </a>
       in the eastern Philippines on 16 March, with only 150 crewmen. There they were able to communicate with the local peoples because the Malayan interpreter,
       <a href="/wiki/Enrique_of_Malacca" title="Enrique of Malacca">
        Enrique of Malacca
       </a>
       , could understand their language. The expedition took them further into the archipelago to the
       <a href="/wiki/Visayas" title="Visayas">
        Visayan
       </a>
       island of
       <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">
        Cebu
       </a>
       , where Magellan's chaplain, Pedro Valderrama, baptised the local monarch
       <a href="/wiki/Rajah_Humabon" title="Rajah Humabon">
        Rajah Humabon
       </a>
       , his chief consort, and his subjects.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Itinerario_legazpi.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="716" data-file-width="328" height="437" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Itinerario_legazpi.jpg/200px-Itinerario_legazpi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Itinerario_legazpi.jpg/300px-Itinerario_legazpi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/85/Itinerario_legazpi.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Itinerario_legazpi.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Routes of early Spanish expeditions in the Philippines.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Seeking to develop trade between the
       <a href="/wiki/East_Indies" title="East Indies">
        East Indies
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">
        Americas
       </a>
       across the Pacific Ocean,
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Mendoza" title="Antonio de Mendoza">
        Antonio de Mendoza
       </a>
       encouraged the exploration of these Asian territories and commissioned the expedition of
       <a href="/wiki/Ruy_L%C3%B3pez_de_Villalobos" title="Ruy López de Villalobos">
        Ruy López de Villalobos
       </a>
       to the Philippines in 1542–1543.
       <a href="/wiki/Miguel_L%C3%B3pez_de_Legazpi" title="Miguel López de Legazpi">
        Miguel López de Legazpi
       </a>
       set out from Mexico, and established the first Spanish settlement in the Philippines in 1565, which became the town of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Miguel,_Cebu" title="San Miguel, Cebu">
        San Miguel
       </a>
       in present-day Cebu. That same year, another member of the expedition,
       <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_de_Urdaneta" title="Andrés de Urdaneta">
        Andrés de Urdaneta
       </a>
       discovered a maritime route from the Philippines to Mexico, across the Pacific, leading to the important transpacific trade link of the
       <a href="/wiki/Manila_galleon" title="Manila galleon">
        Manila-Acapulco Galleons
       </a>
       . In 1571, Manila was captured from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Brunei" title="Sultanate of Brunei">
        Sultanate of Brunei
       </a>
       and then Manila was made the seat of the Spanish
       <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_the_Philippines" title="Captaincy General of the Philippines">
        Captaincy General of the Philippines
       </a>
       . These and other Asian territories claimed by the Spanish crown were to be governed from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Spain" title="Viceroyalty of New Spain">
        Viceroyalty of New Spain
       </a>
       in Mexico City.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Manila-Acapulco galleons shipped products gathered from both Asia-Pacific and the Americas, such as silk, spices, silver, gold and other Asian-Pacific islander products to
       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">
        Mexico
       </a>
       . Products brought from Asia-Pacific were sent to
       <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">
        Veracruz
       </a>
       and shipped to Spain and, via trading, to the rest of Europe, while Spanish-Mexican colonists brought with them Hispanic and indigenous Mexican customs, religion, languages, foods, and cultural traditions to the Philippines, Guam, and the Mariana Islands.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1606, the Spaniards established some form of trade links with the
       <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">
        Maluku Islands
       </a>
       and remained until 1663. Contacts with
       <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">
        Japan
       </a>
       were also established and
       <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Vizca%C3%ADno" title="Sebastián Vizcaíno">
        Sebastián Vizcaíno
       </a>
       was sent as ambassador in 1611, until Japan closed its trading post in 1630. On the north eastern coastal region of Taiwan, the Spaniards built
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Santo_Domingo" title="Fort Santo Domingo">
        Fort Santo Domingo
       </a>
       near
       <a href="/wiki/Keelung" title="Keelung">
        Keelung
       </a>
       in 1626 and a mission in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tamsui" title="Tamsui">
        Tamsui
       </a>
       in 1628, which they occupied until 1642. Several Pacific islands were visited by Spanish ships in the 16th century, including
       <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">
        New Guinea
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Y%C3%B1igo_Ortiz_de_Retez" title="Yñigo Ortiz de Retez">
        Yñigo Ortiz de Retez
       </a>
       in 1545), the
       <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">
        Solomon Islands
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Sarmiento_de_Gamboa" title="Pedro Sarmiento de Gamboa">
        Pedro Sarmiento de Gamboa
       </a>
       in 1568), and the
       <a href="/wiki/Marquesas_Islands" title="Marquesas Islands">
        Marquesas Islands
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_de_Menda%C3%B1a_de_Neira" title="Álvaro de Mendaña de Neira">
        Álvaro de Mendaña de Neira
       </a>
       in 1595), but they made no effort to trade with or colonize them.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1668,
       <a href="/wiki/Beatification" title="Beatification">
        Blessed
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Diego_Luis_de_San_Vitores" title="Diego Luis de San Vitores">
        Diego Luis de San Vitores
       </a>
       established the first mission on Guam, where he and Saint
       <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Calungsod" title="Pedro Calungsod">
        Pedro Calungsod
       </a>
       were later martyred.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1762 British troops briefly
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Manila_(1762)" title="Battle of Manila (1762)">
        captured the city of Manila
       </a>
       during the
       <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">
        Seven Years' War
       </a>
       . However, they were unable to establish control over areas outside Manila—through the efforts of the Spanish lieutenant governor,
       <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_de_Anda_y_Salazar" title="Simón de Anda y Salazar">
        Simón de Anda y Salazar
       </a>
       , the remainder of the islands remained loyal to Spain. The British promised support for an uprising led by
       <a href="/wiki/Diego_Silang" title="Diego Silang">
        Diego Silang
       </a>
       and his wife
       <a href="/wiki/Gabriela_Silang" title="Gabriela Silang">
        Gabriela
       </a>
       but the British force never materialized. Under the peace settlement Manila was exchanged, along with British-occupied
       <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">
        Havana
       </a>
       , for
       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">
        Florida
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Menorca" title="Menorca">
        Menorca
       </a>
       . It was handed back to Spanish authorities in April 1764.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Colonial_government">
        Colonial government
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Colonial government">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_New_Spain" title="Viceroyalty of New Spain">
        Viceroyalty of New Spain
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_the_Philippines" title="Captaincy General of the Philippines">
        Captaincy General of the Philippines
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Philippines" title="Governor-General of the Philippines">
        Governor-General of the Philippines
       </a>
      </div>
      <div class="center">
       <div class="thumb tnone">
        <div class="thumbinner" style="width:527px;">
         <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:EscoltaManila1899.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="696" data-file-width="1374" height="266" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/EscoltaManila1899.jpg/525px-EscoltaManila1899.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/EscoltaManila1899.jpg/788px-EscoltaManila1899.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/EscoltaManila1899.jpg/1050px-EscoltaManila1899.jpg 2x" width="525"/>
         </a>
         <div class="thumbcaption">
          <div class="magnify">
           <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:EscoltaManila1899.jpg" title="Enlarge">
           </a>
          </div>
          <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">
           Manila
          </a>
          , capital of the Spanish East Indies, 1899.
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Seven Years' War prompted
       <a href="/wiki/Charles_III_of_Spain" title="Charles III of Spain">
        Charles III
       </a>
       to initiate
       <a href="/wiki/Bourbon_Reforms" title="Bourbon Reforms">
        extensive governmental reforms
       </a>
       throughout the overseas possessions. An
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Intendant#Spanish_monarchy" title="Intendant">
         intendencia
        </a>
       </i>
       was established in Manila in 1784 to handle the government finances and to promote the economy. (The plan to introduce more intendencias throughout the Philippines did not materialize.) In a similar vein, to promote innovation and education among the residents of the islands,
       <span id="basco">
       </span>
       Governor-General
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Basco_y_Vargas" title="José Basco y Vargas">
        José Basco y Vargas
       </a>
       established the
       <a href="/wiki/Sociedad_Econ%C3%B3mica_de_los_Amigos_del_Pa%C3%ADs" title="Sociedad Económica de los Amigos del País">
        Economic Society of the Friends of the Country
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       For over 256 years, the Spanish East Indies were governed by a governor-captain general, and an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audiencia_Real" title="Audiencia Real">
        audiencia
       </a>
       . All economic matters of the Philippines were managed by the Viceroyalty of New Spain, located in Mexico. Because the eastward route was more widely used for military purposes, in addition to commerce that included the
       <a href="/wiki/Manila_galleon" title="Manila galleon">
        Manila-Acapulco galleon
       </a>
       trade, most government correspondence went through Mexico, rather than directly to Spain (with the exception of a short period at the end of the 18th century).
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1821 the New Spanish Viceroyalty collapsed following the
       <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">
        Mexican War of Independence
       </a>
       , which resulted in the
       <a href="/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire" title="First Mexican Empire">
        First Mexican Empire
       </a>
       . All control of the Spanish East Indies government was then transferred to Madrid, until the United States annexed most Spanish territories in the Asia-Pacific region after the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">
        Spanish–American War
       </a>
       of 1898.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Audiencia_and_Captaincy_General">
        The Audiencia and Captaincy General
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Audiencia and Captaincy General">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main articles:
       <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_the_Philippines" title="Captaincy General of the Philippines">
        Captaincy General of the Philippines
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Audience_of_Manila" title="Royal Audience of Manila">
        Royal Audience of Manila
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In 1574 the
       <a href="/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_the_Philippines" title="Captaincy General of the Philippines">
        Captaincy General of the Philippines
       </a>
       was created as a dependency of the Viceroyalty of New Spain. The
       <a href="/wiki/Real_Audiencia_of_Manila" title="Real Audiencia of Manila">
        Real Audiencia of Manila
       </a>
       was created on 5 May 1583 and established in 1584 as the highest tribunal of the Spanish Empire in the East Indies, that had the Governor-General of the Philippines as its
       <i>
        ex officio
       </i>
       highest judge. Both institutions were created by Royal Decree from
       <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">
        King Felipe II
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Law XI (Audiencia y Chancillería Real de Manila en las Filipinas) of Title XV (De las Audiencias y Chancillerias Reales de las Indias) of Book II of the
       <i>
        Recopilación de
        <a href="/wiki/Laws_of_the_Indies" title="Laws of the Indies">
         Leyes de los Reynos de las Indias
        </a>
       </i>
       of 1680—which compiles the original decree and the one of 25 May 1596—describes the limits and functions of the Audiencia and its President.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leyesii_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-leyesii-6">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        "In the city of Manila on the Island of Luzon, Head of the Philippines, shall reside another Royal Audiencia and Chancellery of ours, with a president, who shall be governor and captain general; four judges of
        <a href="/wiki/Private_law" title="Private law">
         civil cases
        </a>
        [
        <i>
         oidores
        </i>
        ], who will also be judges of
        <a href="/wiki/Criminal_law" title="Criminal law">
         criminal cases
        </a>
        [
        <i>
         alcaldes del crimen
        </i>
        ]; a crown attorney [
        <i>
         fiscal
        </i>
        ]; a bailiff [
        <i>
         alguacil mayor
        </i>
        ]; a lieutenant of the Gran Chancellor; and the other necessary ministers and officials; and which shall have for district said Island of Luzon, and the rest of the Philippines, the
        <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">
         Archipelago of China
        </a>
        , and its
        <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
         Mainland
        </a>
        , discovered and to be discovered. And we order that the governor and captain general of said Islands and Provinces, and president of their Royal Audiencia, have exclusively the superior government of the entire district of said Audiencia in war and peace, and shall make provisions and favors in our Royal Name, which in conformity to the laws of this Compilation and the rest of the
        <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">
         Kingdoms of Castile
        </a>
        and the instructions and powers that We shall grant, he should and can do; and in gubernatorial matters and cases that shall arise, that are of importance, said president-governor should consult on them with the judges of said Audiencia, so that they give their consultive opinions, and having heard them, he should provide the most convenient to the service of God and ours and the peace and tranquility of said Province and Republic".
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Territories">
        Territories
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Territories">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spanish_Provinces_in_the_Pacific.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="250" data-file-width="411" height="213" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Spanish_Provinces_in_the_Pacific.png/350px-Spanish_Provinces_in_the_Pacific.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d5/Spanish_Provinces_in_the_Pacific.png 1.5x" width="350"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Spanish_Provinces_in_the_Pacific.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Spanish East Indies in the 19th century
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Spanish East Indies came to be defined as:
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Las Islas Filipinas (today the Republic of the Philippines): Manila, Luzon, the Visayas, Palawan, Balambangan Island, Northern Mindanao, Zamboanga, Basilan, Jolo, Palmas Islands, Spratly Islands; including isolated outposts in
        <a href="/wiki/Keelung" title="Keelung">
         Keelung
        </a>
        , Taiwan, and in the islands of Gilolo,
        <a href="/wiki/Ternate" title="Ternate">
         Ternate
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="/wiki/Tidore" title="Tidore">
         Tidore
        </a>
        in the Maluku Islands and
        <a href="/wiki/Manado" title="Manado">
         Manado
        </a>
        in Northern part of Sulawesi (formerly Celebes).
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Islands" title="Caroline Islands">
         Islas Carolinas
        </a>
        (the
        <a href="/wiki/Federated_States_of_Micronesia" title="Federated States of Micronesia">
         Federated States of Micronesia
        </a>
        )
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Mariana_Islands" title="Mariana Islands">
         Islas Marianas
        </a>
        (
        <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">
         Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands
        </a>
        and the United States
        <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">
         Territory of Guam
        </a>
        )
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Palau" title="Palau">
         Islas Palau
        </a>
        (Republic of Palau)
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The Spanish used several names that are not currently used. Gran Moluca (Great Molluccas) for the island of Mindanao and Nueva Castilla (New Castile) for
       <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">
        Luzon
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Because Spanish interest in the region was primarily focused on its use as a base for trade with East Asia, direct Spanish control over the area expanded slowly. The
       <a href="/wiki/Batanes" title="Batanes">
        Batanes
       </a>
       Islands were conquered in the 18th century by
       <a href="#basco">
        José Basco
       </a>
       . The highlands of
       <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">
        Luzon
       </a>
       remained outside Spanish control until the early 19th century, and the southernmost tip of
       <a href="/wiki/Palawan" title="Palawan">
        Palawan
       </a>
       , not until the late 1890s. The rest of
       <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">
        Mindanao
       </a>
       (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caesarea_Karoli" title="Caesarea Karoli">
        Caesarea Karoli
       </a>
       )—aside from outposts in Northern Mindanao,
       <a href="/wiki/Zamboanga_City" title="Zamboanga City">
        Zamboanga
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cotabato_City" title="Cotabato City">
        Cotabato
       </a>
       , and the islands of
       <a href="/wiki/Basilan" title="Basilan">
        Basilan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jolo" title="Jolo">
        Jolo
       </a>
       , the rest was nominally under Spanish control, recognizing Spanish rule, but left to administer their own affairs, as in the cases of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sulu_Sultanate" title="Sulu Sultanate">
        Sulu
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Maguindanao" title="Sultanate of Maguindanao">
        Maguindanao
       </a>
       sultanates, as well as a number of other
       <a href="/wiki/Lumad" title="Lumad">
        Lumad
       </a>
       tribes not affiliated with either. Similarly, Palau and the vast majority of the Caroline Islands were not governed by Spanish missions until the early 19th century.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_Influence_in_the_former_Spanish_East_Indies">
        Cultural Influence in the former Spanish East Indies
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cultural Influence in the former Spanish East Indies">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hispanic">
        Hispanic
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hispanic">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_influence_on_Filipino_culture" title="Hispanic influence on Filipino culture">
        Hispanic influence on Filipino culture
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Spain's influence on its former colonies in Asia-Pacific is significant to this day. The majority of the people of the Philippines, Guam and the Mariana Islands belong to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">
        Roman Catholic faith
       </a>
       which was introduced by Spanish missionaries in the 16th and 17th centuries. A large part of the population in these countries use
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alphabetical_Catalog_of_Surnames" title="Alphabetical Catalog of Surnames">
        Spanish names
       </a>
       and surnames. Also, because of the introduction of new tools, products, crops and technology by Spaniards and Mexicans in the three centuries of colonial rule, many Spanish loanwords entered the native languages of these countries. Art forms such as music, architecture and fashion also have much Spanish influence. The national cuisines of these countries also have Mexican and Spanish elements. In terms of ethnicity, the inhabitants of these territories (1/3rd of
       <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">
        Luzon
       </a>
       island's population and port-cities and military outposts, mainly
       <a href="/wiki/Iloilo_City" title="Iloilo City">
        Iloilo
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cebu_City" title="Cebu City">
        Cebu
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Legazpi,_Albay" title="Legazpi, Albay">
        Legaspi
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Vigan" title="Vigan">
        Vigan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Zamboanga_City" title="Zamboanga City">
        Zamboanga
       </a>
       etc.) are descendants of Latin American and Spanish settlers. These descendants of mixed heritage are known as
       <a href="/wiki/Filipino_mestizo" title="Filipino mestizo">
        mestizos
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Filipino">
        Filipino
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_East_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Filipino">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       A sizeable proportion of the current population of the Northern Marianas Islands (45–55%) and Guam (30–45%), as well as that of Palau (15–25%) is of Filipino descent. Some of the local peoples in the previously stated territories also use Filipino names and surnames (one example is the surname Pangelinan, which comes from the Filipino surname Pangilinan). The current
       <a href="/wiki/Chamorro_people" title="Chamorro people">
        Chamorro
       </a>
       population is believed to be partly of Filipino descent, both because of the historic links between Guam and the Philippines during Spanish rule, and currently through different waves of migration.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
