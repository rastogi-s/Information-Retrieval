      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Argentina
       </b>
       is an Italian river in the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Imperia" title="Province of Imperia">
        province of Imperia
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
        Geography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Argentina_(river)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Taggia011.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1952" data-file-width="2592" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Taggia011.jpg/220px-Taggia011.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Taggia011.jpg/330px-Taggia011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Taggia011.jpg/440px-Taggia011.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Taggia011.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Motorway bridge on the Argentina
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Its source is near the French border. The river flows past
       <a href="/wiki/Triora" title="Triora">
        Triora
       </a>
       and flows south before emptying into the
       <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_Sea" title="Ligurian Sea">
        Ligurian Sea
       </a>
       , near
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arma_di_Taggia" title="Arma di Taggia">
        Arma di Taggia
       </a>
       .
      </p>
