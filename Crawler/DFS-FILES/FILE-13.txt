      <p>
       <b>
        Brigasc
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligurian_language_(Romance)" title="Ligurian language (Romance)">
        Ligurian language
       </a>
       . It is spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Area_of_use">
        Area of use
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Brigasc_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Area of use">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Brigasc dialect is spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/La_Brigue" title="La Brigue">
        La Brigue
       </a>
       (France) and
       <a href="/wiki/Briga_Alta" title="Briga Alta">
        Briga Alta
       </a>
       (Italy) and some villages of the communes of
       <a href="/wiki/Ormea" title="Ormea">
        Ormea
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Triora" title="Triora">
        Triora
       </a>
       . It is very close to
       <a href="/wiki/Royasc" title="Royasc">
        Royasc
       </a>
       dialect.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Brigasc_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       During the
       <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">
        Renaissance
       </a>
       the
       <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_(Romance_language)" title="Ligurian (Romance language)">
        Ligurian language
       </a>
       was spoken in all the territories of the
       <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">
        Republic of Genoa
       </a>
       : in the western area of this republic one of its groups (spoken mainly in the area between the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Principality_of_Monaco" title="Principality of Monaco">
        Principality of Monaco
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Sanremo" title="Sanremo">
        Sanremo
       </a>
       ) was called
       <i>
        Intemelio
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The language spoken in the mountains around
       <a href="/wiki/La_Brigue" title="La Brigue">
        Briga
       </a>
       was called
       <i>
        Brigasc
       </i>
       and received some influence from the Occitan language.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
