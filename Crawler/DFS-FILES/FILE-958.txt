      <p>
       <b>
        Savoyard
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Franco-Proven%C3%A7al_language" title="Franco-Provençal language">
        Franco-Provençal language
       </a>
       . It is spoken in some territories of the historical
       <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Savoy" title="Duchy of Savoy">
        Duchy of Savoy
       </a>
       , nowadays a geographic area spanning
       <a href="/wiki/Savoie" title="Savoie">
        Savoie
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Haute-Savoie" title="Haute-Savoie">
        Haute-Savoie
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Geneva" title="Canton of Geneva">
        Canton of Geneva
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
        Switzerland
       </a>
       . The varieties are commonly known as
       <i>
        patois
       </i>
       . It has around 35,000 speakers today.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Some_words">
        Some words
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Savoyard_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Some words">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Several subdialects of Savoyard exist that exhibit unique features in terms of phonetics and vocabulary. This includes many words that have to do with the weather:
       <i>
        bacan
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        temps mauvais
       </i>
       );
       <i>
        coussie
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        tempête
       </i>
       );
       <i>
        royé
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        averse
       </i>
       );
       <i>
        ni[v]ole
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        nuage
       </i>
       ); ...and, the environment:
       <i>
        clapia, perrier
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        éboulis
       </i>
       );
       <i>
        égra
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        sorte d'escalier de pierre
       </i>
       );
       <i>
        balme
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        grotte
       </i>
       );
       <i>
        tova
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        tourbière
       </i>
       ); and
       <i>
        lanche
       </i>
       (French:
       <i>
        champ en pente
       </i>
       ).
      </p>
