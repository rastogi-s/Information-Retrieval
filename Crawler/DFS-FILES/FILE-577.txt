      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Nashville metropolitan area
       </b>
       is centered on
       <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">
        Nashville, Tennessee
       </a>
       , in the United States. It includes 14
       <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">
        counties
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_Tennessee" title="Middle Tennessee">
        Middle Tennessee
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Management_and_Budget" title="Office of Management and Budget">
        Office of Management and Budget
       </a>
       defines the metro area as the
       <b>
        Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin, TN, Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </b>
       , a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Metropolitan Statistical Area">
        Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </a>
       (MSA) designated for statistical use by the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">
        United States Census Bureau
       </a>
       and other agencies. The area is the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Table_of_United_States_Metropolitan_Statistical_Areas" title="Table of United States Metropolitan Statistical Areas">
        36th largest MSA
       </a>
       in the United States
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and is the largest
       <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">
        metropolitan area
       </a>
       in the state of
       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">
        Tennessee
       </a>
       . The metropolitan statistical area was first designated in 1950 and initially included only
       <a href="/wiki/Davidson_County,_Tennessee" title="Davidson County, Tennessee">
        Davidson County
       </a>
       . As surrounding counties increased in population and population densities and in the number of the number of their residents employed in Davidson County, the OMB added new counties to the MSA. Today, the metro area includes Davidson and 13 other counties.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Combined_Statistical_Area">
        Combined Statistical Area
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nashville_metropolitan_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Combined Statistical Area">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro, TN,
       <a href="/wiki/Combined_statistical_area" title="Combined statistical area">
        Combined Statistical Area
       </a>
       (CSA) is the result of the addition of the Micropolitan Statistical Areas of
       <a href="/wiki/Shelbyville,_Tennessee" title="Shelbyville, Tennessee">
        Shelbyville
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Bedford_County,_Tennessee" title="Bedford County, Tennessee">
        Bedford County
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Lawrenceburg,_Tennessee" title="Lawrenceburg, Tennessee">
        Lawrenceburg
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_County,_Tennessee" title="Lawrence County, Tennessee">
        Lawrence County
       </a>
       ) and
       <a href="/wiki/Lewisburg,_Tennessee" title="Lewisburg, Tennessee">
        Lewisburg
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Marshall_County,_Tennessee" title="Marshall County, Tennessee">
        Marshall County
       </a>
       ) to the Nashville-Davidson–Murfreesboro–Franklin, TN, Metropolitan Statistical Area. The estimated population of the CSA as of July 1, 2016, was 1,987,778.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census1_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-census1-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
