      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Tampa Bay Area
       </b>
       is a major populated area surrounding
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay" title="Tampa Bay">
        Tampa Bay
       </a>
       on the west coast of
       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">
        Florida
       </a>
       in the United States.

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Definitions">
        Definitions
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definitions">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The exact boundaries of the metro area can differ in different contexts.
       <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_County,_Florida" title="Hillsborough County, Florida">
        Hillsborough County
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinellas_County" title="Pinellas County">
        Pinellas County
       </a>
       (including the cities of
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">
        Tampa
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">
        St. Petersburg
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Clearwater,_Florida" title="Clearwater, Florida">
        Clearwater
       </a>
       , and several smaller communities) make up the most limited definition. The
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">
        United States Census Bureau
       </a>
       defines the
       <b>
        Tampa–St. Petersburg–Clearwater Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_statistical_area" title="Metropolitan statistical area">
        MSA
       </a>
       ) as including Hillsborough and Pinellas Counties along with
       <a href="/wiki/Hernando_County,_Florida" title="Hernando County, Florida">
        Hernando
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Pasco_County,_Florida" title="Pasco County, Florida">
        Pasco
       </a>
       Counties to the north.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSAdef_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-MSAdef-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Other definitions are
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        the four counties in the MSA plus
        <a href="/wiki/Citrus_County,_Florida" title="Citrus County, Florida">
         Citrus
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Manatee_County,_Florida" title="Manatee County, Florida">
         Manatee
        </a>
        Counties, used by the Tampa Bay Regional Planning Council
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-4">
          [4]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        the four counties in the MSA plus Citrus, Manatee and
        <a href="/wiki/Sarasota_County,_Florida" title="Sarasota County, Florida">
         Sarasota
        </a>
        Counties, used by the
        <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Area_Regional_Transportation_Authority" title="Tampa Bay Area Regional Transportation Authority">
         Tampa Bay Area Regional Transportation Authority
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        the four counties in the MSA plus Citrus, Manatee, Sarasota and
        <a href="/wiki/Polk_County,_Florida" title="Polk County, Florida">
         Polk
        </a>
        Counties, used by the Tampa Bay Partnership
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
         <a href="#cite_note-5">
          [5]
         </a>
        </sup>
        and the
        <a href="/wiki/Media_in_the_Tampa_Bay_Area" title="Media in the Tampa Bay Area">
         Tampa Bay media market
        </a>
        .
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nielsen_6-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-nielsen-6">
          [6]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span id="Tampa.E2.80.93St._Petersburg.E2.80.93Clearwater_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tampa–St._Petersburg–Clearwater_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area">
        Tampa–St. Petersburg–Clearwater Metropolitan Statistical Area
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tampa–St. Petersburg–Clearwater Metropolitan Statistical Area">

         ]
        </span>

        </a>
       </sup>

        <a href="#cite_note-2015TBMSA2010-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>

      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Migration">
        Migration
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Migration">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       From 2000 to 2004, total net
       <a href="/wiki/Human_migration" title="Human migration">
        migration
       </a>
       for the Tampa Bay region was 262,961 or an average of 65,740 per year. During this time Tampa Bay accounted for nearly 20% of Florida's total net migration. The annual migration totals grew steadily since 2000 until 200 people a day moved to Tampa Bay in 2004. Two Tampa Bay region counties are among the top counties in the country for net in-migration.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pasco_County" title="Pasco County">
        Pasco County
       </a>
       ranks 8th in the nation for net migration and
       <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_County,_Florida" title="Hillsborough County, Florida">
        Hillsborough County
       </a>
       ranks 13th out of more than 3,000 counties.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">
        Climate
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Climate">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2196" data-file-width="2196" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg/200px-TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg/300px-TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg/400px-TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TampaBay_ETM_2000nov3.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Tampa Bay Area from space
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Climate_of_the_Tampa_Bay_Area" title="Climate of the Tampa Bay Area">
        Climate of the Tampa Bay Area
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Tampa Bay area has a
       <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">
        humid subtropical climate
       </a>
       (Koppen
       <i>
        Cfa
       </i>
       ), with hot, humid summers, with daily
       <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">
        thunderstorms
       </a>
       , drier, predominantly sunny winters, and warm-to-hot springs with a pronounced dry season maximum. On average, two days experience frost per year in the cooler parts of the Tampa Bay area, less than annually in the coastal parts. However, hard freezes (low temperatures below 28 °F/−2 °C) are very rare, occurring only a few times in the last 75 years. The
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Agriculture" title="United States Department of Agriculture">
        United States Department of Agriculture
       </a>
       designates the area as being in
       <a href="/wiki/Hardiness_zone#North_American_hardiness_zones" title="Hardiness zone">
        hardiness zones 9b and 10a
       </a>
       (the latter being western and coastal, due significantly to maritime influences of the Gulf of Mexico and the
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay" title="Tampa Bay">
        400-square-mile Tampa Bay
       </a>
       ). Plant climate-indicator species, such as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coconut_palm" title="Coconut palm">
        coconut palms
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_palm" title="Royal palm">
        royal palms
       </a>
       , as well as other elements of Florida's native tropical flora, reach their northern limits of reliable culture and native range in the area.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Highs usually range between 65 and 95 °F (18 and 35 °C) year-round. Tampa's official high has never reached 100 °F (38 °C)—the all-time record high temperature is 99 °F (37 °C). St. Petersburg's all-time record high is exactly 100 °F (38 °C).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <div class="center">
       <div class="thumb tnone">
        <div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;">
         <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:St_Petersburg_City_Hall.png">
          <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="640" height="267" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/St_Petersburg_City_Hall.png/400px-St_Petersburg_City_Hall.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/St_Petersburg_City_Hall.png/600px-St_Petersburg_City_Hall.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fa/St_Petersburg_City_Hall.png 2x" width="400"/>
         </a>
         <div class="thumbcaption">
          <div class="magnify">
           <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:St_Petersburg_City_Hall.png" title="Enlarge">
           </a>
          </div>
          Royal Palms in front of St. Petersburg's city hall
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Pinellas_County,_Florida" title="Pinellas County, Florida">
        Pinellas County
       </a>
       lies on a
       <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">
        peninsula
       </a>
       between Tampa Bay and the
       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">
        Gulf of Mexico
       </a>
       , and much of the city of Tampa lies on a smaller peninsula jutting out into Tampa Bay. This proximity to large bodies of water both moderates local temperatures and introduces large amounts of
       <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">
        humidity
       </a>
       into the atmosphere. In general, the communities farthest from the coast have more extreme temperature differences, both during a single day and throughout the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seasons" title="Seasons">
        seasons
       </a>
       of the year.

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Health_care">
        Health care
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Health care">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>

      <p>
       With more than 50 hospitals, dozens of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clinics" title="Clinics">
        clinics
       </a>
       and ambulatory care centers, the Tampa Bay has an abundance of top-rated health care facilities for children and adults. The region also has a wealth of well-trained medical professionals—nearly 53,000 nurses and more than 9,200 physicians (including physician assistants)—provide care to Tampa Bay residents and visitors every year.
      </p>
      <p>
       Tampa Bay ranks in the top 20 nationwide for medical device manufacturing clusters.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The industry employs more than 10,000 people with an average wage in excess of $49,000 and produces over $2 billion worth of goods and services for an economic impact of more than 51,000 jobs and $5 billion. Tampa Bay's history of manufacturing for the defense industry has created a workforce skilled in high-precision fabrication of electronic parts and assemblies and experience in dealing with government relations, easing the transition to the highly regulated medical manufacturing industry.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Information_technology">
        Information technology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>

         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Tampa Bay serves as the gateway to the Florida High Tech Corridor which spans 23 counties. Created as a partnership between the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Florida" title="University of South Florida">
        University of South Florida
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Florida" title="University of Central Florida">
        University of Central Florida
       </a>
       and now including the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">
        University of Florida
       </a>
       , the Florida High Tech Corridor promotes the growth of the high-tech industry across
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Florida" title="Central Florida">
        Central Florida
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Higher_education_and_research">
        Higher education and research
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Higher education and research">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_research" title="Academic research">
        Academic research
       </a>
       is a key component of high-tech growth and a powerful economic engine. The presence of cutting-edge research in the region is vital to technology transfer, which enables innovative ideas discovered in academia to achieve
       <a href="/wiki/Commercialization" title="Commercialization">
        commercialization
       </a>
       in the marketplace. Tampa Bay has several
       <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">
        powerhouse
       </a>
       research centers that are engaged in both pure scientific research and aggressively pursuing technology transfer to enrich people's lives.
      </p>
      <p>
       Researchers at the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Florida" title="University of South Florida">
        University of South Florida
       </a>
       's Nanomaterials and Nanomanufacturing Research Center (NNRC), H. Lee Moffitt Cancer Center and the Center for Ocean Technogy at USF's College of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_Science" title="Marine Science">
        Marine Science
       </a>
       are researching how to use nanotechnology for a myriad of targeted uses including drug delivery, mechanized microsurgery, customized laser
       <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">
        microchips
       </a>
       , ways to turn sunlight into
       <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">
        electricity
       </a>
       , purifying water, storing
       <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">
        hydrogen
       </a>
       in small nanotubes, designing and developing marine sensors using microelectromechanical systems (
       <a href="/wiki/Microelectromechanical_systems" title="Microelectromechanical systems">
        MEMS
       </a>
       ) and curing
       <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">
        cancer
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tampa" title="University of Tampa">
        University of Tampa
       </a>
       is located in Downtown Tampa, Florida on the
       <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_River_(Florida)" title="Hillsborough River (Florida)">
        Hillsborough River
       </a>
       and is a historic university linked back to
       <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt" title="Theodore Roosevelt">
        Teddy Roosevelt.
       </a>
      </p>

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <p>

        financial crisis of 2007–2010

        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Changes in
       <a href="/wiki/House_price_index" title="House price index">
        house prices
       </a>
       for the area are publicly tracked on a regular basis using the
       <a href="/wiki/Case%E2%80%93Shiller_index" title="Case–Shiller index">

       </a>
       ; the statistic is published by
       <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">
        Standard &amp; Poor's
       </a>
       and is also a component of S&amp;P's 20-city
       <a href="/wiki/Composite_(finance)" title="Composite (finance)">
        composite index
       </a>
       of the value of the U.S. residential real estate market.
      </p>

       <a href="/wiki/Air_Force_Reserve_Command" title="Air Force Reserve Command">

       </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KC-135_Stratotanker" title="KC-135 Stratotanker">

       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Central_Command" title="United States Central Command">

       </a>
       (USSOCOM), which has responsibility for all
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_operations_forces" title="Special operations forces">
        special operations forces
       </a>
       in the U.S. Armed Forces.

        CGAS Clearwater
       </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg%E2%80%93Clearwater_International_Airport" title="St. Petersburg–Clearwater International Airport">

       </a>
       . It is the largest air station in the
       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">

       </a>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-27J" title="C-27J">

       </a>
       aircraft beginning in 2017. Coast Guard Station St. Petersburg is located on the site of the former Coast Guard Air Station St. Petersburg at
       <a href="/wiki/Albert_Whitted_Airport" title="Albert Whitted Airport">

       in Tampa contain historic architecture. Fresh seafood and locally grown produce are available in many restaurants and in weekly
       <a href="/wiki/Farmers%27_market" title="Farmers' market">
        farmers' markets
       </a>
       in multiple urban centers in the area.
       <a href="/wiki/Yuengling" title="Yuengling">
        Yuengling
       </a>
       , the largest American-owned brewer, operates a brewery in Tampa, as does the highly regarded craft brewer Cigar City Brewing.
      </p>
      <p>
       Arts and culture make a big impact in Tampa Bay. In a single year, the economic impact of the cultural institutions in the Tampa Bay area was $521.3 million, according to a recent
       <a href="/wiki/PricewaterhouseCoopers" title="PricewaterhouseCoopers">
        PricewaterhouseCoopers
       </a>
       study. In 2004 5.6 million people attended plays, musical performances, museum exhibits, and other cultural institutions in Tampa Bay, supporting 7,800 jobs.
      </p>

      <p>
       The Tampa Bay area is highly noted for its beaches, with the warm, blue gulf waters and nearly 70 miles of barriers islands from North Pinellas south to
       <a href="/wiki/Venice,_Florida" title="Venice, Florida">
        Venice
       </a>
       , attracting tourists from all over the world. Three of the beaches in this area,
       <a href="/wiki/Fort_De_Soto_Park" title="Fort De Soto Park">
        Fort De Soto's
       </a>
       North Beach (2005),
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caladesi_Island" title="Caladesi Island">
        Caladesi Island
       </a>
       (2008), and Sarasota's
       <a href="/wiki/Siesta_Key,_Florida" title="Siesta Key, Florida">
        Siesta Key
       </a>
       (2011) have been named by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Beach" title="Dr. Beach">
        Dr. Beach
       </a>
       as America's Top Beach.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The 15th
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IIFA_Awards" title="IIFA Awards">
        IIFA Awards
       </a>
       would be held at Tampa Bay Area in April 2014.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Sports attractions, in addition to the teams listed below, include many professional quality golf courses, tennis courts, and pools. Ybor and the Channel District in Tampa, downtown St. Petersburg, and the beaches all along the coast all attract a vibrant nightlife.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_public_parks_and_recreation_areas">
        Notable public parks and recreation areas
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Notable public parks and recreation areas">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Tampa Bay area is home to an extensive system of state, county, and city parks.
       <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_River_State_Park" title="Hillsborough River State Park">
        Hillsborough River State Park
       </a>
       in Thonotosassa is one of Florida's original eight state parks and
       <a href="/wiki/Honeymoon_Island_State_Park" title="Honeymoon Island State Park">
        Honeymoon Island State Park
       </a>
       , near Dunedin, is Florida's most visited state park. Pinellas County is home to the
       <a href="/wiki/Pinellas_Trail" title="Pinellas Trail">
        Fred Marquis Pinellas Trail
       </a>
       , a 37-mile running and cycling trail over a former railroad bed connecting
       <a href="/wiki/Tarpon_Springs,_Florida" title="Tarpon Springs, Florida">
        Tarpon Springs
       </a>
       to St. Petersburg.
       <a href="/wiki/Skyway_Fishing_Pier_State_Park" title="Skyway Fishing Pier State Park">
        Skyway Fishing Pier State Park
       </a>
       , the remnants of the approaches to the original
       <a href="/wiki/Sunshine_Skyway_Bridge" title="Sunshine Skyway Bridge">
        Sunshine Skyway Bridge
       </a>
       forms the world's largest fishing pier in Pinellas and Manatee counties. The shallow waters and many
       <a href="/wiki/Mangrove" title="Mangrove">
        mangrove
       </a>
       islands of the bay and gulf make the area popular with
       <a href="/wiki/Kayak" title="Kayak">
        kayakers
       </a>
       . The gulf is also home to a large number of
       <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">
        natural
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Artificial_reef" title="Artificial reef">
        artificial
       </a>
       coral reefs that are popular for fishing and
       <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">
        scuba diving
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <p>
       The Tampa Bay Area is home to three major professional sports teams — the Buccaneers (NFL), Rays (MLB), and Lightning (NHL).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
        <a href="#cite_note-31">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Tampa Bay area also hosts a number of minor-league and college teams. Regardless of the specific city where they play their games, all of the major pro teams use "Tampa Bay" in their name to signify that they represent the entire area.

      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">
        Major League Baseball
       </a>
       teams have come to the Tampa Bay area for
       <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">
        spring training
       </a>
       since the Chicago Cubs played in Tampa 1913 and the St. Louis Browns played in St. Petersburg in 1914.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
        <a href="#cite_note-32">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grapefruit_League" title="Grapefruit League">
        Grapefruit League
       </a>
       games are still a favorite pastime for both residents and tourists alike every March. The following six Major League Baseball teams play spring training games in the Tampa Bay area:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">
         New York Yankees
        </a>
        play at
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steinbrenner_Field" title="Steinbrenner Field">
         Steinbrenner Field
        </a>
        in Tampa
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">
         Philadelphia Phillies
        </a>
        play at
        <a href="/wiki/Spectrum_Field" title="Spectrum Field">
         Spectrum Field
        </a>
        in Clearwater
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">
         Toronto Blue Jays
        </a>
        play at
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Auto_Exchange_Stadium" title="Florida Auto Exchange Stadium">
         Florida Auto Exchange Stadium
        </a>
        in Dunedin
       </li>
      </ul>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">
        Transportation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      <p>
       Transportation in the Tampa Bay Area is heavily affected by its position around
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay" title="Tampa Bay">
        Tampa Bay
       </a>
       . For more about marine transportation in the area, including the many bridges over Tampa Bay, see

      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Air">
        Air
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Air">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_airports_in_the_Tampa_Bay_area" title="List of airports in the Tampa Bay area">
        List of airports in the Tampa Bay area
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa_International_Airport" title="Tampa International Airport">
        Tampa International Airport
       </a>
       is the largest airport in the region with 21 carriers and more than 17 million passengers served last year. In addition to the recent opening of a new
       <a href="/wiki/Airport_terminal" title="Airport terminal">
        terminal
       </a>
       , improvements are being planned to handle 25 million passengers by 2020.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg%E2%80%93Clearwater_International_Airport" title="St. Petersburg–Clearwater International Airport">
        St. Petersburg–Clearwater International Airport
       </a>
       provides access to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commercial_airliner" title="Commercial airliner">
        commercial airliners
       </a>
       , and smaller charter craft. The airport is currently planning an expansion which will include new terminal facilities and runway extension. Dotting the landscape throughout the area, are many general aviation airports for the aircraft enthusiast and smaller corporate jets.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">
        Rail
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tampa_Bay_Area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Rail">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">
        Amtrak
       </a>
       provides passenger rail service from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Station_(Tampa,_Florida)" title="Union Station (Tampa, Florida)">
        Union Station
       </a>
       in Tampa.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CSX" title="CSX">
        CSX
       </a>
       provides freight rail service for the entire Tampa Bay region. The railroad serves every major population and industrial center east of the
       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">
        Mississippi
       </a>
       including
       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">
        Ontario
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">
        Montreal
       </a>
       . CSX also connects with more than 166 bulk
       <a href="/wiki/Intermodal_freight_transport" title="Intermodal freight transport">
        intermodal
       </a>
       distribution terminals and rail-to-truck bulk
       <a href="/wiki/Transloading" title="Transloading">
        transloading
       </a>
       facilities throughout its service area, linking it to all of North America.
      </p>

      <p>
       Bus service is provided in Hillsborough County by
       <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Area_Regional_Transit" title="Hillsborough Area Regional Transit">
        Hillsborough Area Regional Transit
       </a>
       (HART), in Pinellas County by
       <a href="/wiki/Pinellas_Suncoast_Transit_Authority" title="Pinellas Suncoast Transit Authority">
        Pinellas Suncoast Transit Authority
       </a>
       (PSTA), in Pasco County by
       <a href="/wiki/Pasco_County_Public_Transportation" title="Pasco County Public Transportation">
        Pasco County Public Transportation
       </a>
       and in Hernando County by
       <a href="/wiki/THE_Bus_(Hernando_County,_Florida)" title="THE Bus (Hernando County, Florida)">
        THE Bus
       </a>
       . HART and PSTA provide express services between Tampa and Pinellas County, and PSTA provides connections to Pasco County.
      </p>
      <p>
       HART also operates the
       <a href="/wiki/TECO_Line_Streetcar_System" title="TECO Line Streetcar System">
        TECO streetcar
       </a>
       between downtown Tampa and
       <a href="/wiki/Ybor_City" title="Ybor City">
        Ybor City
       </a>
       . In 2013 HART also began operating a
       <a href="/wiki/Bus_rapid_transit" title="Bus rapid transit">
        Bus rapid transit
       </a>
       system called
       <a href="/wiki/MetroRapid" title="MetroRapid">
        MetroRapid
       </a>
       that runs from downtown Tampa to the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Florida" title="University of South Florida">
        University of South Florida
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       On July 1, 2007, an
       <a href="/wiki/Intermodal_passenger_transport" title="Intermodal passenger transport">
        intermodal transportation authority
       </a>
       was created to serve the seven county Tampa Bay area. The
       <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Area_Regional_Transportation_Authority" title="Tampa Bay Area Regional Transportation Authority">
        Tampa Bay Area Regional Transportation Authority
       </a>
       (TBARTA) was formed to develop bus,
       <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">
        rapid transit
       </a>
       , and other transportation options for the region.
      </p>

      <p>
       Hillsborough County is also served by other roadways such as the
       <a href="/wiki/Lee_Roy_Selmon_Expressway" title="Lee Roy Selmon Expressway">
        Lee Roy Selmon Expressway
       </a>
       (SR 618) which commutes workers from Brandon into downtown Tampa and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veterans_Expressway" title="Veterans Expressway">
        Veterans Expressway
       </a>
       /
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suncoast_Parkway" title="Suncoast Parkway">
        Suncoast Parkway
       </a>
       (Toll 589) which serves traffic from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citrus_County" title="Citrus County">
        Citrus
       </a>
       /Hernando County border southward into Tampa.
      </p>
      <p>
       In Pinellas County,
       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_19_in_Florida" title="U.S. Route 19 in Florida">
        U.S. 19
       </a>
       is the main north–south route through the county, and is being upgraded to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freeway" title="Freeway">
        freeway
       </a>
       standards complete with
       <a href="/wiki/Frontage_road" title="Frontage road">
        frontage roads
       </a>
       to ease congestion through the north part of the county. Also, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayside_Bridge" title="Bayside Bridge">
        Bayside Bridge
       </a>
       allows traffic to go from Clearwater into St. Petersburg without having to use U.S. 19.
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Campbell_Causeway" title="Courtney Campbell Causeway">
        Courtney Campbell Causeway
       </a>
       (SR 60) is one of the three roads that connect Pinellas County to Hillsborough County across the bay. The other two are the
       <a href="/wiki/Howard_Frankland_Bridge" title="Howard Frankland Bridge">
        Howard Frankland Bridge
       </a>
       (I-275) and
       <a href="/wiki/Gandy_Bridge" title="Gandy Bridge">
        Gandy Bridge
       </a>
       (U.S. 92). The
       <a href="/wiki/Sunshine_Skyway_Bridge" title="Sunshine Skyway Bridge">
        Sunshine Skyway Bridge
       </a>
       is part of I-275 and connects
       <a href="/wiki/Bradenton,_Florida" title="Bradenton, Florida">
        Bradenton
       </a>
       and other
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manatee_County" title="Manatee County">
        Manatee County
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarasota_County" title="Sarasota County">
        Sarasota County
       </a>
       commuters into Pinellas County.
      </p>
