      <p>
       <b>
        Mentonasc
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        Mentonasco
       </b>
       in Italian,
       <b>
        Mentonnais
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        Mentonasque
       </b>
       in French) is a Romance dialect historically spoken in and around
       <a href="/wiki/Menton" title="Menton">
        Menton
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       . It is classified as a dialect of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occitan" title="Occitan">
        Occitan
       </a>
       and a sub-dialect of
       <a href="/wiki/Vivaro-Alpine_dialect" title="Vivaro-Alpine dialect">
        Vivaro-Alpine
       </a>
       , with some strong influence from the neighbouring
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intemelio" title="Intemelio">
        Intemelian
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligurian_language_(Romance)" title="Ligurian language (Romance)">
        Ligurian
       </a>
       dialect spoken from
       <a href="/wiki/Ventimiglia" title="Ventimiglia">
        Ventimiglia
       </a>
       to
       <a href="/wiki/Sanremo" title="Sanremo">
        San Remo
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">
        Classification
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mentonasc_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Mentonasc is considered to be a transitional language; it is an intermediate language between
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_language_(ancient)" title="Ligurian language (ancient)">
        Ligurian
       </a>
       , which is why the classification of Mentonasc is often debated. However, it is traditionally assigned to the
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       language.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Mentonasc dialect bears strong similarities with the common alpine dialects, such as,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Royasque&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Royasque (page does not exist)">
        Royasque
       </a>
       or
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pignasque.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pignasque. (page does not exist)">
        Pignasque.
       </a>
       It differs quite significantly especially in the ear from Ligurian coastal dialects
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Italian_languages" title="Northern Italian languages">
        (Northern Italian)
       </a>
       , like those of
       <a href="/wiki/Ventimiglia" title="Ventimiglia">
        Ventimiglia
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Intemelio_dialect" title="Intemelio dialect">
        Intemelio dialect
       </a>
       ) or
       <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">
        Monaco
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mentonasc_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       When the area of Menton was part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">
        Republic of Genoa
       </a>
       and later of the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">
        Kingdom of Sardinia
       </a>
       , Mentonasc was used in all of the coastal area between Monaco and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ventimiglia,_Italy" title="Ventimiglia, Italy">
        Ventimiglia
       </a>
       , and in the hinterland.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Frontiere_Monaco.gif">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Frontiere_Monaco.gif/250px-Frontiere_Monaco.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Frontiere_Monaco.gif/375px-Frontiere_Monaco.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Frontiere_Monaco.gif/500px-Frontiere_Monaco.gif 2x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Frontiere_Monaco.gif" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Map of the territory of the "Free cities of Menton &amp; Roquebrune in 1848
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
          <a href="#cite_note-2">
           [2]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the 19th century Mentonasc was used in the territories of the
       <i>
        Free Cities of Menton &amp; Roquebrune
       </i>
       , an independent statelet created in connection with the Italian
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Risorgimento" title="Risorgimento">
        Risorgimento
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       When
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       annexed most of the County of Nice in 1860, Mentonasc began its decline, substituted by the
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French language
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_distribution">
        Geographic distribution
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mentonasc_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geographic distribution">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Mentonasc dialect is currently spoken by about 10% of the population in
       <a href="/wiki/Menton" title="Menton">
        Menton
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Roquebrune-Cap-Martin" title="Roquebrune-Cap-Martin">
        Roquebrune
       </a>
       , and the surrounding villages (
       <a href="/wiki/Castellar,_Alpes-Maritimes" title="Castellar, Alpes-Maritimes">
        Castellar
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Castillon,_Alpes-Maritimes" title="Castillon, Alpes-Maritimes">
        Castillon
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Gorbio" title="Gorbio">
        Gorbio
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Sainte-Agn%C3%A8s,_Alpes-Maritimes" title="Sainte-Agnès, Alpes-Maritimes">
        Sainte-Agnès
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Moulinet,_Alpes-Maritimes" title="Moulinet, Alpes-Maritimes">
        Moulinet
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Sospel" title="Sospel">
        Sospel
       </a>
       ). Now the language is being taught within the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_educational_system" title="French educational system">
        French educational system
       </a>
       , as a variety of
       <a href="/wiki/Ni%C3%A7ard_dialect" title="Niçard dialect">
        Niçard
       </a>
       (i.e.
       <a href="/wiki/Proven%C3%A7al_dialect" title="Provençal dialect">
        Provençal
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       ), so this may change.
      </p>

       <span class="mw-headline" id="Official_status">

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">
        Literature
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mentonasc_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Literature">
         edit

         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are some texts and songs that have been published recently in
       <a href="/wiki/Menton" title="Menton">
        Menton
       </a>
       (most from the twentieth century.)
      </p>
      <p>
       Among the various publications:
       <i>
        A Lambrusca de Paigran
       </i>
       (la Vigne vierge de Grand-père) by Jean-Louis Caserio, illustrations by M. and F. Guglielmelli, SAHM, Menton, 1987.
       <i>
        Brandi Mentounasc, Livret de Poésies Bilingue
       </i>
       by Jean Ansaldi, 2010.
       <i>
        Ou Mentounasc per ou Bachelerà, le Mentonasque au Baccalauréat,
       </i>
       by JL Caserio, 5th edition, 2008., etc.
      </p>
