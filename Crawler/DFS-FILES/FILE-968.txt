      <p>
       <b>
        Emilian-Romagnol
       </b>
       (
       <i>
        emiliân-rumagnōl
       </i>
       or
       <i>
        langua emiglièna-rumagnôla
       </i>
       ), also known as
       <b>
        Emiliano-Romagnolo
       </b>
       , is a
       <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Italic_languages" title="Gallo-Italic languages">
        Gallo-Italic language
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="488" data-file-width="800" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg/220px-Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg/330px-Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg/440px-Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Emiliano-Romagnolo_area.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Map
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Its two dialects are
       <a href="/wiki/Emilian_dialect" title="Emilian dialect">
        Emilian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Romagnol_dialect" title="Romagnol dialect">
        Romagnol
       </a>
       , which are spoken in the Northern Italian region of
       <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">
        Emilia-Romagna
       </a>
       , parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">
        Lombardy
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Umbria" title="Umbria">
        Umbria
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">
        Marche
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Liguria" title="Liguria">
        Liguria
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">
        Piedmont
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">
        Tuscany
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">
        San Marino
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
