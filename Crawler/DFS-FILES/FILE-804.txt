    List of English–Spanish interlingual homographs
   </h1>

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       This is a list of
       <a href="/wiki/Word" title="Word">
        words
       </a>
       that occur in both the
       <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">
        English language
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       , but which have different meanings and/or
       <a href="/wiki/Pronunciation" title="Pronunciation">
        pronunciations
       </a>
       in each language. Such words are called
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Interlingual_homograph" title="Interlingual homograph">
         interlingual homographs
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kecskes_Albertazzi_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Kecskes_Albertazzi-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Homograph" title="Homograph">
         Homographs
        </a>
       </i>
       are two or more words that have the same written form.
      </p>
      <p>
       This list includes only homographs that are written precisely the same in English and Spanish: They have the same
       <a href="/wiki/Spelling" title="Spelling">
        spelling
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">
        hyphenation
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Capitalization" title="Capitalization">
        capitalization
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Word_divider" title="Word divider">
        word dividers
       </a>
       , etc. It excludes
       <a href="/wiki/Proper_noun" title="Proper noun">
        proper nouns
       </a>
       and words that have different
       <a href="/wiki/Diacritic" title="Diacritic">
        diacritics
       </a>
       (e.g.,
       <i>
        invasion
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        invasión
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        pâté
       </i>
       /
       <i>
        paté
       </i>
       ).
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships_between_words">
        Relationships between words
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_English%E2%80%93Spanish_interlingual_homographs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Relationships between words">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="694" data-file-width="944" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png/200px-Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png/300px-Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png/400px-Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Homograph_homophone_venn_diagram.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         How words in one or more languages can differ in pronunciation, spelling, and meaning
         <i>
          (click to enlarge)
         </i>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The words below are categorised based on their relationship:
       <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">
        cognates
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/False_friend" title="False friend">
        false friends
       </a>
       , and modern
       <a href="/wiki/Loanword" title="Loanword">
        loanwords
       </a>
       .
       <i>
        Cognates
       </i>
       are words that have a common
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Etymological" title="Etymological">
        etymological
       </a>
       origin.
       <i>
        False friends
       </i>
       are words in two languages that look or sound similar, but differ significantly in meaning.
       <i>
        Loanwords
       </i>
       are words that are adopted from one language into another.
      </p>
      <p>
       Many of the words in the list are
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latin
       </a>
       cognates. Because Spanish is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romance_language" title="Romance language">
        Romance language
       </a>
       (which means it evolved from Latin), many of its words are either inherited from Latin or derive from Latin words. Although English is a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Germanic_language" title="Germanic language">
        Germanic language
       </a>
       , it, too, incorporates thousands of Latinate words that are related to words in Spanish.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Yet even with so many Latin cognates, only a small minority are written precisely the same in both languages.
      </p>
      <p>
       Even though the words in this list are written the same in both languages, none of them are pronounced the same—not even the word
       <i>
        no
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cognates">
        Cognates
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_English%E2%80%93Spanish_interlingual_homographs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cognates">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The cognates in the table below share meanings in English and Spanish, but have different pronunciation.
      </p>
      <p>
       Some words entered
       <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">
        Middle English
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Early_Modern_Spanish" title="Early Modern Spanish">
        Early Modern Spanish
       </a>
       indirectly and at different times. For example, a Latinate word might enter English by way of
       <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">
        Old French
       </a>
       , but enter Spanish directly from Latin. Such differences can introduce changes in spelling and meaning.
      </p>
      <p>
       Although most of the cognates have at least one meaning shared by English and Spanish, they can have other meanings that are not shared. A word might also be used in different contexts in each language.
      </p>

       </span>

       The table below lists English-to-Spanish and Spanish-to-English loanwards, as well as loanwards from other modern languages that share the same orthography in both English and Spanish.        and

        w
