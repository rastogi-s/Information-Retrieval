      <p>
       <b>
        Western Romance languages
       </b>
       are one of the two subdivisions of a proposed subdivision of the
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       based on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Spezia%E2%80%93Rimini_line" title="La Spezia–Rimini line">
        La Spezia–Rimini line
       </a>
       . They include the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance" title="Gallo-Romance">
        Gallo-Romance
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
        Iberian-Romance
       </a>
       branches as well as northern Italian. The subdivision is based solely on the use of the "s" for pluralization and the weakening of some consonants, but that makes the categorization highly problematic because there is much higher lexical similarity between all dialects of Italian and French than between French and Spanish. There is also much higher morphological similarity between Spanish and Italian dialects than between Italian and French.
      </p>
      <p>
       Based on
       <a href="/wiki/Mutual_intelligibility" title="Mutual intelligibility">
        mutual intelligibility
       </a>
       , Dalby counts a dozen languages:
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asturian-Leonese_language" title="Asturian-Leonese language">
        Asturian-Leonese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">
        Aragonese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Gascon_language" title="Gascon language">
        Gascon
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Proven%C3%A7al_dialect" title="Provençal dialect">
        Provençal
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Langues_d%27o%C3%AFl" title="Langues d'oïl">
        Gallo-Wallon
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Franco-Proven%C3%A7al_language" title="Franco-Provençal language">
        Franco-Provençal
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Romansh_language" title="Romansh language">
        Romansh
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Ladin_language" title="Ladin language">
        Ladin
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalby_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dalby-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This classification criterion is however problematic, due to the much higher levels of mutual intelligibility between Italic and Iberian languages than between either of these with Gallo-Romance languages.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ciobanu_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ciobanu-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Some classifications include
       <a href="/wiki/Italo-Dalmatian_languages" title="Italo-Dalmatian languages">
        Italo-Dalmatian
       </a>
       ; the resulting clade is generally called
       <a href="/wiki/Italo-Western_languages" title="Italo-Western languages">
        Italo-Western Romance
       </a>
       . Other classifications place Italo-Dalmatian with
       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Romance_languages" title="Eastern Romance languages">
        Eastern Romance
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian
       </a>
       does not fit into either Western or Eastern Romance, and may have split off before either.
      </p>
      <p>
       Today the four most-widely spoken standardized Western Romance languages are
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       (c. 410 million native),
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       (c. 220 million native, another 45 million or so second-language speakers, mainly in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_in_Africa" title="Portuguese in Africa">
        Lusophone Africa
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       (c. 75 million native speakers, another 70 million or so second-language speakers, mostly in Francophone Africa), and
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       (c. 7.2 million native). Many of these languages have large numbers of non-native speakers; this is especially the case for French, in widespread use throughout
       <a href="/wiki/African_French" title="African French">
        West Africa
       </a>
       as a
       <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">
        lingua franca
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gallo-Romance">
        Gallo-Romance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Romance_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gallo-Romance">
         edit

        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance_languages" title="Gallo-Romance languages">
        Gallo-Romance languages
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Gallo-Romance includes:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glottolog_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-glottolog-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Langues_d%27o%C3%AFl" title="Langues d'oïl">
         Langues d'oïl
        </a>
        , or Oïl languages. These include
        <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
         Standard French
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Picard_language" title="Picard language">
         Picard
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Walloon_language" title="Walloon language">
         Walloon
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Lorrain_language" title="Lorrain language">
         Lorrain
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Norman_language" title="Norman language">
         Norman
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Franco-Proven%C3%A7al_language" title="Franco-Provençal language">
         Arpitan
        </a>
        language, also known as Franco-Provençal. It shares features of both
        <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
         French
        </a>
        and the
        <a href="/wiki/Proven%C3%A7al_dialect" title="Provençal dialect">
         Provençal dialect of Occitan
        </a>
        . Sometimes included in the Oïl languages.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Gallo-Romance can include:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Occitano-Romance_languages" title="Occitano-Romance languages">
         Occitano-Romance languages
        </a>
        of
        <a href="/wiki/Southern_France" title="Southern France">
         Southern France
        </a>
        and
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iberia" title="Iberia">
         East Iberia
        </a>
        , includes
        <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
         Occitan
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
         Catalan
        </a>
        . Occitano-Romance languages can be classified as
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance" title="Gallo-Romance">
         Gallo-Romance
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
         Iberian-Romance
        </a>
        , or as an independent branch of the Western Romance languages.
        <ul>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
           Occitan language
          </a>
          , or langue d'oc, has dialects such as
          <a href="/wiki/Proven%C3%A7al_dialect" title="Provençal dialect">
           Provençal dialect
          </a>
          , and
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gascon_dialect" title="Gascon dialect">
           Gascon-Aranese dialect
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
           Catalan language
          </a>
          has standard forms of Catalan and
          <a href="/wiki/Valencian" title="Valencian">
           Valencian
          </a>
          . Can be classified as
          <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
           East Iberian
          </a>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Rhaeto-Romance_languages" title="Rhaeto-Romance languages">
         Rhaeto-Romance languages
        </a>
        . They include
        <a href="/wiki/Romansh_language" title="Romansh language">
         Romansh
        </a>
        of
        <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
         Switzerland
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Ladin_language" title="Ladin language">
         Ladin
        </a>
        of the Dolomites area,
        <a href="/wiki/Friulian_language" title="Friulian language">
         Friulian
        </a>
        of Friuli. Rhaeto-Romance languages can be classified as
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance" title="Gallo-Romance">
         Gallo-Romance
        </a>
        , or as an independent branch of the Western Romance languages.
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Italic_languages" title="Gallo-Italic languages">
         Gallo-Italic languages
        </a>
        . They include
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piedmontese" title="Piedmontese">
         Piedmontese
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligurian_language_(Romance)" title="Ligurian language (Romance)">
         Ligurian
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Lombard_language" title="Lombard language">
         Lombard
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emiliano-Romagnolo_language" title="Emiliano-Romagnolo language">
         Emiliano-Romagnolo
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Italic_of_Sicily" title="Gallo-Italic of Sicily">
         Gallo-Italic of Sicily
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Italic_of_Basilicata" title="Gallo-Italic of Basilicata">
         Gallo-Italic of Basilicata
        </a>
        . Gallo-Italic languages can be classified as
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance" title="Gallo-Romance">
         Gallo-Romance
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The Oïl languages, Arpitan and Rhaeto-Romance languages are sometimes called Gallo-Rhaetian, but it is difficult to exclude from this group Gallo-Italic, which according to several linguists forms a particular unity with Rhaeto-Romance.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Iberian-Romance">
        Iberian-Romance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Romance_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Iberian-Romance">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
        Iberian Romance languages
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Iberian Romance languages of the
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">
        Iberian Peninsula
       </a>
       include:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glottolog_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-glottolog-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/West_Iberian_languages" title="West Iberian languages">
         West Iberian languages
        </a>
        :
        <ul>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="/wiki/Castilian_languages" title="Castilian languages">
           Castilian languages
          </a>
          : includes
          <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
           Spanish
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Judaeo-Spanish" title="Judaeo-Spanish">
           Judaeo-Spanish
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="/wiki/Galician-Portuguese" title="Galician-Portuguese">
           Galician-Portuguese languages
          </a>
          : includes
          <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
           Portuguese
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
           Galician
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Fala_language" title="Fala language">
           Fala
          </a>
          and
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Riverense_Portu%C3%B1ol_language" title="Riverense Portuñol language">
           Uruguayan Portuñol
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          The
          <a href="/wiki/Astur-Leonese_languages" title="Astur-Leonese languages">
           Astur-Leonese languages
          </a>
          : they are, from east to west,
          <a href="/wiki/Cantabrian_dialect" title="Cantabrian dialect">
           Cantabrian
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">
           central-eastern Asturian
          </a>
          and
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonese_language" title="Leonese language">
           Leonese proper
          </a>
          . Going from north to south, they are
          <a href="/wiki/Leonese_dialect" title="Leonese dialect">
           Leonese proper
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Mirandese_language" title="Mirandese language">
           Mirandese
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Extremaduran_language" title="Extremaduran language">
           Extremaduran
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Barranquenho" title="Barranquenho">
           Barranquenho
          </a>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyrenean%E2%80%93Mozarabic" title="Pyrenean–Mozarabic">
         Pyrenean–Mozarabic
        </a>
        languages: includes
        <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">
         Aragonese
        </a>
        , and the extinct
        <a href="/wiki/Mozarabic_language" title="Mozarabic language">
         Mozarabic
        </a>
        . Can be classified as
        <a href="/wiki/West_Iberian_languages" title="West Iberian languages">
         West Iberian
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
         East Iberian language
        </a>
        , or
        <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
         Catalan language
        </a>
        : usually classified as part of
        <a href="/wiki/Occitano-Romance_languages" title="Occitano-Romance languages">
         Occitano-Romance
        </a>
        , see
        <a href="/wiki/Western_Romance_languages#Gallo-Romance" title="Western Romance languages">
         Gallo-Romance
        </a>
        above.
       </li>
      </ul>
