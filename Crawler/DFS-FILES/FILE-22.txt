      <p>
       The
       <b>
        name of Argentina
       </b>
       (a
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Adjective" title="Adjective">
        adjective
       </a>
       meaning "
       <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">
        silvery
       </a>
       "), traditionally called
       <b>
        the Argentine
       </b>
       in English,
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Disputed statement">
          <span title="This claim has reliable sources with contradicting facts (July 2015)">
           disputed
          </span>
         </a>
         <span class="metadata">
          –
          <a href="/wiki/Talk:Name_of_Argentina#Argentine" title="Talk:Name of Argentina">
           discuss
          </a>
         </span>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">
          <span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2015)">
           not in citation given
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       is ultimately derived from the
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latin
       </a>
       <i>
        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/argentum" title="wikt:argentum">
         argentum
        </a>
       </i>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">
          <span title='""argentum"'>
           not in citation given
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       "
       <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">
        silver
       </a>
       " and the
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_gender" title="Grammatical gender">
        feminine
       </a>
       of the adjectival suffix
       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-inus#Latin" title="wikt:-inus">
        -īnus
       </a>
       , the Latin "argentum" has its origin from the ancient Greek-Hellenic word "argyro(s)", άργυρο(ς) meaning
       <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">
        silver
       </a>
       . The first use of the name "Argentina" can be traced back to the first voyages made by the
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spanish
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Conquistador" title="Conquistador">
        conquistadors
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Río de la Plata">
        Río de la Plata
       </a>
       (meaning "River of Silver"), in the first years of the 16th century.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Colonization">
        Colonization
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Name_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Colonization">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Aleixo_Garcia" title="Aleixo Garcia">
        Aleixo Garcia
       </a>
       , one of the survivors of the shipwrecked expedition mounted by
       <a href="/wiki/Juan_D%C3%ADaz_de_Sol%C3%ADs" title="Juan Díaz de Solís">
        Juan Díaz de Solís
       </a>
       in 1516, heard notices about a powerful White King in a country very rich in silver, at the mountains called "
       <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_la_Plata" title="Sierra de la Plata">
        Sierra de la Plata
       </a>
       ". Garcia then organized an expedition and reached
       <a href="/wiki/Potos%C3%AD" title="Potosí">
        Potosí
       </a>
       's area, gaining several silver objects and gifts. He was killed by the
       <a href="/wiki/Payagu%C3%A1" title="Payaguá">
        Payaguás
       </a>
       , returning to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(island)" title="Santa Catarina (island)">
        Santa Catarina
       </a>
       (Brazil), but the
       <a href="/wiki/Guaran%C3%AD_people" title="Guaraní people">
        Guaraní
       </a>
       people who were part of the expedition took the silver objects back and spread the Sierra de Plata legend, and explained that it was possible to reach that fabulous land through the wide river to the south.
      </p>
      <p>
       While the exact origin of the name "
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata" title="Rio de la Plata">
        Rio de la Plata
       </a>
       " is unknown,
       <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Cabot_(explorer)" title="Sebastian Cabot (explorer)">
        Sebastian Cabot
       </a>
       's exploration between 1526 and 1529 is widely credited to have inspired such name due to his obtaining and collecting a variety of silver objects from the Guaraní tribes along the Paraguay river.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The river received other names, such as "Mar Dulce", "Río de Solís", "Río de Santa María", and "Río Jordán", but "Río de La Plata" was the one that prevailed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ING_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-ING-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Portuguese cartographer
       <a href="/wiki/Lopo_Homem" title="Lopo Homem">
        Lopo Homem
       </a>
       made reference to the place as "Terra Argentea" in a 1554 map.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ING_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ING-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The first mention of the name "Argentina" was in
       <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_del_Barco_Centenera" title="Martín del Barco Centenera">
        Martín del Barco Centenera's
       </a>
       poem
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/La_Argentina_(poem)" title="La Argentina (poem)">
         Argentina y conquista del Río de la Plata, con otros acaecimientos de los reinos del Perú, Tucumán y estado del Brasil
        </a>
       </i>
       , published in Spain in 1602.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ING_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-ING-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ten years later, in 1612,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ruy_D%C3%ADaz_de_Guzm%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ruy Díaz de Guzmán (page does not exist)">
        Ruy Díaz de Guzmán
       </a>
       published the book
       <i>
        Historia del descubrimiento, población, y conquista del Río de la Plata
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        History of the discovery, population, and conquest of the Río de la Plata
       </i>
       ), naming the territory discovered by Solís "Tierra Argentina" ("Land of Silver", "Silvery Land").
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1776 the Virreinato del Río de la Plata (
       <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty_of_the_R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="Viceroyalty of the Río de la Plata">
        Viceroyalty of the Río de la Plata
       </a>
       ) was created, named after the river; it included present-day
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">
        Bolivia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">
        Paraguay
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
        Uruguay
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Independence_of_Argentina">
        Independence of Argentina
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Name_of_Argentina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Independence of Argentina">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Rise_of_the_Argentine_Republic" title="Rise of the Argentine Republic">
        Rise of the Argentine Republic
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Spanish viceroy was ousted during the
       <a href="/wiki/May_Revolution" title="May Revolution">
        May Revolution
       </a>
       , starting the
       <a href="/wiki/Argentine_War_of_Independence" title="Argentine War of Independence">
        Argentine War of Independence
       </a>
       . The new government removed the "
       <i>
        Viceroyalty
       </i>
       " word from the name, renaming the territory the "Provincias Unidas del Río de la Plata" (
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_the_R%C3%ADo_de_la_Plata" title="United Provinces of the Río de la Plata">
        United Provinces of the Río de la Plata
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ING_4-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-ING-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This denomination was ratified years later by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assembly_of_Year_XIII" title="Assembly of Year XIII">
        Assembly of Year XIII
       </a>
       . The
       <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Tucum%C3%A1n" title="Congress of Tucumán">
        Congress of Tucumán
       </a>
       , seeking a higher
       <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_integration" title="Latin American integration">
        Latin American integration
       </a>
       , used instead the name "Provincias Unidas de Sud América" (United Provinces of South America).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ING_4-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-ING-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The name "Argentina" was mainly used among the
       <a href="/wiki/High_society_(social_class)" title="High society (social class)">
        high society
       </a>
       , and in limited cases. The use became popular with the sanction of the second
       <a href="/wiki/Argentine_National_Anthem" title="Argentine National Anthem">
        Argentine National Anthem
       </a>
       , written by
       <a href="/wiki/Vicente_L%C3%B3pez_y_Planes" title="Vicente López y Planes">
        Vicente López y Planes
       </a>
       . However, it was not widely used because it was not associated to the whole territory, but just to the Buenos Aires province.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ING_4-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-ING-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Constitution_of_1826" title="Argentine Constitution of 1826">
        Constitution of 1826
       </a>
       was sanctioned as the
       <i>
        Constitución de la República Argentina
       </i>
       . The other provinces rejected its high centralism, and the president
       <a href="/wiki/Bernardino_Rivadavia" title="Bernardino Rivadavia">
        Bernardino Rivadavia
       </a>
       was deposed shortly after.
      </p>
      <p>
       During the second government of
       <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_de_Rosas" title="Juan Manuel de Rosas">
        Juan Manuel de Rosas
       </a>
       ,
       <b>
        Confederación Argentina
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Confederation" title="Argentine Confederation">
        Argentine Confederation
       </a>
       ) (founded in lily) was the main name used for the young country, but others were also used, including
       <i>
        República de la Confederación Argentina
       </i>
       (Republic of the Argentine Confederation) and
       <i>
        Federación Argentina
       </i>
       (Argentine Federation).
       <a href="/wiki/Justo_Jos%C3%A9_de_Urquiza" title="Justo José de Urquiza">
        Justo José de Urquiza
       </a>
       deposed Rosas in the
       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Caseros" title="Battle of Caseros">
        battle of Caseros
       </a>
       and called for a Constituent Assembly that would write the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Argentina_of_1853" title="Constitution of Argentina of 1853">
        Constitution of Argentina of 1853
       </a>
       . Buenos Aires did not accept it, and seceded from the Confederation as the
       <a href="/wiki/State_of_Buenos_Aires" title="State of Buenos Aires">
        State of Buenos Aires
       </a>
       . For a decade, Buenos Aires and the Confederation existed as distinct administrative divisions. Buenos Aires rejoined the Confederation in 1860, after an amendment to the 1853 Constitution. The name was changed to
       <b>
        Nación Argentina
       </b>
       , though including a paragraph with the historical names as "equivalent and valid" denominations. Then on October 8, 1860, President
       <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Derqui" title="Santiago Derqui">
        Santiago Derqui
       </a>
       decreed the official name to be
       <b>
        República Argentina
       </b>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In common speech, the country is referred to as "la Argentina" (the Argentine) in Spanish, bypassing the noun in any of the above expressions ("the Argentine [land]", "the Argentine [Republic]", etc.).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ING_4-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-ING-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
