      <p>
       <b>
        Antonio de Nebrija
       </b>
       (1441 – 5 July 1522), also known as
       <b>
        Antonio de Lebrija
       </b>
       ,
       <b>
        Elio Antonio de Lebrija
       </b>
       ,
       <b>
        Antonius Nebrissensis
       </b>
       , and
       <b>
        Antonio of Lebrixa
       </b>
       , was a
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spanish
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">
        Renaissance
       </a>
       scholar. He was a teacher, historian, poet and astronomer, best known for writing a
       <a href="/wiki/Grammar" title="Grammar">
        grammar
       </a>
       of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castilian_language" title="Castilian language">
        Castilian language
       </a>
       . His grammar is often credited as the first published grammar of a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romance_language" title="Romance language">
        Romance language
       </a>
       and set the standard for subsequent modern Spanish language grammars.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
        Name
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_de_Nebrija&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       De Nebrija's given name was
       <b>
        Antonio Martínez de Cala
       </b>
       . In typical Renaissance
       <a href="/wiki/Humanism" title="Humanism">
        humanist
       </a>
       fashion, he
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latinisation_(literature)" title="Latinisation (literature)">
        latinized
       </a>
       his name as
       <b>
        Aelius Antonius Nebrissensis
       </b>
       by taking
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Aelia_(gens)" title="Aelia (gens)">
         Aelius
        </a>
       </i>
       from the Roman inscriptions of his native Lebrija, the Roman
       <i>
        Nebrissa Veneria
       </i>
       . He also signed his books as
       <i>
        Antonius Lebrixen
       </i>
       (1481) and
       <i>
        Antonius Nebrissen
       </i>
       (1501).
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
        Biography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_de_Nebrija&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="806" data-file-width="544" height="296" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg/200px-Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg/300px-Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg/400px-Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg 2x" width="200"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Juan_de_Z%C3%BA%C3%B1iga_dibujo_con_orla.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Antonio de Nebrija teaching
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:167px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="862" data-file-width="498" height="286" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg/165px-Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg/248px-Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg/330px-Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg 2x" width="165"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gram%C3%A1tica_castellana.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Prologue of his
         <i>
          Gramática de la lengua castellana
         </i>
         (1492)
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       He was born in the former Nebrissa Veneria, known today as
       <a href="/wiki/Lebrija" title="Lebrija">
        Lebrija
       </a>
       , in the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Seville" title="Province of Seville">
        Province of Seville
       </a>
       , almost bordering the
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_C%C3%A1diz" title="Province of Cádiz">
        Province of Cádiz
       </a>
       . His father was Antonio de Cala e Hinojosa and his mother was Catalina de Xarava y Ojo, or Catalina de Jaraba y Ojo. Her father was of
       <a href="/wiki/Marrano" title="Marrano">
        marrano
       </a>
       origin and his mother was
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">
        Jewish
       </a>
       converted to Catholicism. He was the second of five children.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Urbano_p80_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Urbano_p80-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       After studying at
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salamanca" title="University of Salamanca">
        Salamanca
       </a>
       , Nebrija resided for ten years in
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       , having received a scholarship from the diocese of Córdoba to study theology at the
       <a href="/wiki/Collegio_di_Spagna" title="Collegio di Spagna">
        Colegio de San Clemente
       </a>
       in Bologna. He completed his education at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bologna_University" title="Bologna University">
        Bologna University
       </a>
       . On his return to Spain (1473), he devoted himself to the advancement of classical learning among his fellow countrymen. After obtaining the professorship of poetry and grammar at Salamanca, he was transferred to the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alcal%C3%A1" title="University of Alcalá">
        University of Alcalá de Henares
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Nebrija wrote about a variety of subjects including cosmography, botany and theology. In 1481 he published a grammar entitled,
       <i>
        Introductiones latinae
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Nebrija's services to the cause of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classical_literature" title="Classical literature">
        classical literature
       </a>
       in Spain have been compared with those rendered by
       <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Valla" title="Lorenzo Valla">
        Valla
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Erasmus" title="Erasmus">
        Erasmus
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scidaeus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scidaeus (page does not exist)">
        Scidaeus
       </a>
       to Italy, the
       <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">
        Netherlands
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       , respectively.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 1492, he published the first grammar of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish language
       </a>
       . He dedicated this book to
       <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">
        Isabella I of Castile
       </a>
       , the catholic queen. When the book was presented to her, she asked: “Why would I want a work like this? I already know the language.” He answered:
      </p>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>
        “Majesty, the language is the instrument of the empire.” Moreover, he said of his intentions: “Presently, they [the people] waste their leisure on novels and fancy stories full of lies. I have decided, therefore, that my most urgent task is to transform Castilian speech into an artifact so that whatever henceforth shall be written in this language may be of one standard tenor, one coinage that can outlast the times. Greek and Latin have been governed by art, and thus have kept their uniformity throughout the ages. Unless the like of this be done for our language, in vain Your Majesty's chroniclers ... shall praise your deeds. Your labor will not last more than a few years, and we shall continue to feed on Castilian translations of foreign tales about our own kings. Either your feats will fade with the language or they will roam among aliens abroad, homeless, without a dwelling in which they can settle... Your Majesty, I want to lay the foundations for the dwelling in which your fame can settle... Now, Your Majesty, let me come to the last advantage that you shall gain from my grammar. For the purpose, recall the time when I presented you with a draft of this book earlier this year in Salamanca. At this time, you asked me what end such a grammar could possibly serve. Upon this, the Bishop of Avila interrupted to answer in my stead. What he said was this: "Soon Your Majesty will have placed her yoke upon many barbarians who speak outlandish tongues. By this, your victory, these people shall stand in a new need; the need for the laws the victor owes to the vanquished, and the need for the language we shall bring with us. My grammar shall serve to impart to them the Castilian tongue, as we have used grammar to teach Latin to our young.”
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       Nebrija produced a large number of works on a variety of subjects, including a Latin–Spanish
       <a href="/wiki/Dictionary" title="Dictionary">
        dictionary
       </a>
       , and commentaries on
       <a href="/wiki/Coelius_Sedulius" title="Coelius Sedulius">
        Sedulius
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Persius" title="Persius">
        Persius
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He was also in charge of correcting the Latin text of the
       <a href="/wiki/Complutensian_Polyglot_Bible" title="Complutensian Polyglot Bible">
        Complutensian Polyglot Bible
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       He died in Alcalá de Henares. His possible grandson
       <a href="/wiki/Antonio_de_Lebrija_(conquistador)" title="Antonio de Lebrija (conquistador)">
        Antonio de Lebrija
       </a>
       was
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_conquistadors_in_Colombia" title="List of conquistadors in Colombia">
        conquistador in Colombia
       </a>
       and treasurer of the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Muisca" title="Spanish conquest of the Muisca">
        Spanish conquest of the Muisca
       </a>
       expedition.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carnero_p69_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Carnero_p69-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Grammatical_ideas">
        Grammatical ideas
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_de_Nebrija&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Grammatical ideas">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:167px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Antonio_de_Nebrija_(A._Nogu%C3%A9s)_Madrid_01.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2587" data-file-width="1101" height="388" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Antonio_de_Nebrija_%28A._Nogu%C3%A9s%29_Madrid_01.jpg/165px-Antonio_de_Nebrija_%28A._Nogu%C3%A9s%29_Madrid_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Antonio_de_Nebrija_%28A._Nogu%C3%A9s%29_Madrid_01.jpg/248px-Antonio_de_Nebrija_%28A._Nogu%C3%A9s%29_Madrid_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Antonio_de_Nebrija_%28A._Nogu%C3%A9s%29_Madrid_01.jpg/330px-Antonio_de_Nebrija_%28A._Nogu%C3%A9s%29_Madrid_01.jpg 2x" width="165"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Antonio_de_Nebrija_(A._Nogu%C3%A9s)_Madrid_01.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Statue outside the
         <a href="/wiki/Biblioteca_Nacional_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Biblioteca Nacional de España">
          Biblioteca Nacional de España
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">
          Madrid
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       De Nebrija's work on grammar was mainly based on the classical Latin authors
       <a href="/wiki/Priscian" title="Priscian">
        Priscian
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Diomedes_Grammaticus" title="Diomedes Grammaticus">
        Diomedes Grammaticus
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Aelius_Donatus" title="Aelius Donatus">
        Aelius Donatus
       </a>
       but he also introduced new concepts to the field. He considered grammar to be the highest science. He subdivided it into
       <a href="/wiki/Orthography" title="Orthography">
        Orthography
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Prose" title="Prose">
        Prose
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">
        Etymology
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Syntax" title="Syntax">
        Syntax
       </a>
       . He was the first author to make claims of
       <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">
        intellectual property
       </a>
       .
      </p>
