      <p>
       <b>
        Leonese
       </b>
       is a set of vernacular Romance dialects spoken in the northern and western portions of the
       <a href="/wiki/Historical_region" title="Historical region">
        historical region
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Le%C3%B3n_(historical_region)" title="León (historical region)">
        León
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       (the modern provinces of
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Province of León">
        León
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Zamora" title="Province of Zamora">
        Zamora
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salamanca" title="Province of Salamanca">
        Salamanca
       </a>
       ) and a few adjoining areas in
       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
        Portugal
       </a>
       . In this narrow sense Leonese is distinct from the dialects grouped under
       <a href="/wiki/Asturian_language" title="Asturian language">
        Asturian
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krüger,_Fritz_2006_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Krüger,_Fritz_2006-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       although there is no clear linguistic division. The current number of Leonese speakers is estimated at 20,000 to 50,000.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XAGR_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-XAGR-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSP_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-RSP-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The westernmost fringes of the provinces of León and Zamora are in the territory of the
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
        Galician language
       </a>
       , although there is
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect_continuum" title="Dialect continuum">
        dialectal continuity
       </a>
       between the linguistic areas.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Leonese and Asturian dialects have long been recognized as a single language, currently known as
       <a href="/wiki/Astur-Leonese_languages" title="Astur-Leonese languages">
        Astur-Leonese
       </a>
       or Asturian-Leonese and formerly known as Leonese. For most of the 20th century, linguists (including
       <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Men%C3%A9ndez_Pidal" title="Ramón Menéndez Pidal">
        Ramón Menéndez Pidal
       </a>
       in his landmark 1906 study of the language)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGarcía_Gil200910_11-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGarcía_Gil200910-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       discussed a Leonese language (or historical dialect) descending from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">
        Latin
       </a>
       and encompassing two groups: the Asturian dialects on one hand, and dialects spoken in the provinces of León and Zamora in Spain and a related dialect in
       <a href="/wiki/Tr%C3%A1s-os-Montes_e_Alto_Douro_Province" title="Trás-os-Montes e Alto Douro Province">
        Trás-os-Montes
       </a>
       , Portugal, on the other hand.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krüger,_Fritz_2006_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Krüger,_Fritz_2006-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Unlike Asturian, the Leonese dialects of Spain are not officially promoted or regulated.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
        Name
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Menéndez Pidal used "Leonese" for the entire linguistic area, including Asturias. This designation has been replaced by Ibero-Romance scholars with "Asturian-Leonese", but "Leonese" is still often used to denote Asturian-Leonese by non-speakers of Asturian or Mirandese.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Linguistic_description">
        Linguistic description
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Linguistic description">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology_and_writing">
        <span id="Phonology">
        </span>
        <span id="Writing">
        </span>
        <span id="Sample_texts">
        </span>
        <span id="Historical_changes">
        </span>
        Phonology and writing
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Phonology and writing">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In Leonese, any of five
       <a href="/wiki/Vowel" title="Vowel">
        vowel
       </a>
       phonemes,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a, e, i, o, u/
       </span>
       , may occur in stressed position and the two
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archiphoneme" title="Archiphoneme">
        archiphonemes
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /I/
       </span>
       ,
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /U/
       </span>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Phoneme" title="Phoneme">
        phoneme
       </a>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        /a/
       </span>
       may occur in unstressed position.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar">
        Grammar
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Grammar">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Leonese has two
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_gender" title="Grammatical gender">
        genders
       </a>
       (masculine and feminine) and two
       <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_number" title="Grammatical number">
        numbers
       </a>
       (singular and
       <a href="/wiki/Plural" title="Plural">
        plural
       </a>
       ). The main masculine noun and adjective endings are
       <i>
        -u
       </i>
       for singular and
       <i>
        -os
       </i>
       for plural. Typical feminine endings are
       <i>
        -a
       </i>
       for singular and
       <i>
        -as
       </i>
       for plural. Masculine and feminine nouns ending in
       <i>
        -e
       </i>
       in the singular take
       <i>
        -es
       </i>
       for the plural.
      </p>

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        <span id="History_of_the_language">
        </span>
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">

       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Conventus_Asturum.jpg">
         <img alt="Historical map of northern Spain" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="686" data-file-width="920" height="186" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Conventus_Asturum.jpg/250px-Conventus_Asturum.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Conventus_Asturum.jpg/375px-Conventus_Asturum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Conventus_Asturum.jpg/500px-Conventus_Asturum.jpg 2x" width="250"/>

      <p>
       The native languages of Leon, Zamora, Asturias, and the Terra de Miranda in Portugal are the result of the evolution of Latin introduced by
       <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">
        Roman
       </a>
       conquerors in the region. Their colonization and organization led to the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Conventus_Astururum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Conventus Astururum (page does not exist)">
        Conventus Astururum
       </a>
       , with its capital at
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asturica_Augusta" title="Asturica Augusta">
        Asturica Augusta
       </a>
       (present-day
       <a href="/wiki/Astorga,_Spain" title="Astorga, Spain">
        Astorga, Spain
       </a>
       , the centre of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latinization_(historical)" title="Latinization (historical)">
        Romanization
       </a>
       for the indigenous tribes).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The region remained unified until the seventh-century Islamic invasion. Around the 11th century, it began to be defined as Leonese territory roughly corresponding to the southern
       <i>
        conventus
       </i>
       . In medieval León, the Romance
       <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">
        Galician
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astur-Leonese" title="Astur-Leonese">
        Asturian-Leonese
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Castilian
       </a>
       languages evolved and spread south.
      </p>
      <p>
       The first known text in Asturian-Leonese is the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Nodicia_de_kesos" title="Nodicia de kesos">
         Nodizia de Kesos
        </a>
       </i>
       , written between 974 and 980 AD, an inventory of cheeses owned by a monastery written in the margin of the reverse of a document written in Latin.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       During the 12th and 13th centuries, Leonese reached its territorial zenith as the administrative language of the Kingdom of León, a literary language (
       <i>
        Poema de Elena y María
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        El Libro de Alexandre
       </i>
       ),
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       in the Leonese court, judiciary (with the translation of the
       <i>
        Liber Iudicum o Liber Iudiciorum Visigoth
       </i>
       into Leonese), administration and organization.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       After the 1230 union of Leon and Castile, Leonese had greater written and institutional use, although at the end of the 13th century Castilian began to replace it as a written language.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Leonese became an oral, rural language with little literary development.
      </p>
      <p>
       At the beginning of the 20th century, it survived in northern and mid-western Leon and western Zamora. Its scientific study and a nascent cultural movement began in the province of Leon in 1906. During the 1950s and 60s, the number of Leonese speakers and the area in which it was spoken decreased.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Use_and_distribution">
        <span id="Geographical_distribution">
        </span>
        <span id="Use_and_status">

       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">

      <p>
       Although the Astur-Leonese linguistic domain covers most of the principality of Asturias, the north and west of the province of Leon, the northeast of Zamora, both provinces in Castile and León, and the region of Miranda do Douro in the east of the Portuguese district of
       <a href="/wiki/Bragan%C3%A7a_District" title="Bragança District">
        Bragança
       </a>
       , this article focuses on the autonomous community of Castile and León. Julio Borrego Nieto, in
       <i>
        Manual de dialectología española. El español de España
       </i>
       (1996), wrote that the area in where Leonese is best preserved, defined as "area 1", consists of the regions of
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Babia,_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Babia, Spain (page does not exist)">
        Babia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Laciana" title="Laciana">
        Laciana
       </a>
       , part of Los
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Arg%C3%BCellos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Argüellos (page does not exist)">
        Argüellos
       </a>
       , eastern
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bierzo" title="Bierzo">
        Bierzo
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Cabrera_(Le%C3%B3n)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Cabrera (León) (page does not exist)">
        La Cabrera
       </a>
       ; in Zamora, non-Galician
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senabria" title="Senabria">
        Sanabria
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Borrego Nieto describes another geographical circle, which he calls "area 2", where Leonese is fading: " ... It is extended to the regions between the interior area and the Ribera del
       <a href="/wiki/%C3%93rbigo" title="Órbigo">
        Órbigo
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Maragater%C3%ADa" title="Maragatería">
        Maragatería
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/La_Cepeda" title="La Cepeda">
        Cepeda
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oma%C3%B1a&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Omaña (page does not exist)">
        Omaña
       </a>
       ... ). In Zamora, the region of
       <a href="/wiki/La_Carballeda" title="La Carballeda">
        La Carballeda
       </a>
       – with the subregion
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Requejada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Requejada (page does not exist)">
        La Requejada
       </a>
       - and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aliste_(shire)" title="Aliste (shire)">
        Aliste
       </a>
       , with at least a part of its adjacent lands (
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alba_region&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alba region (page does not exist)">
        Alba
       </a>
       <span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">
        (
        <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concejo_de_Alba" title="es:Concejo de Alba">
         es
        </a>
        )
       </span>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A1bara" title="Tábara">
        Tábara
       </a>
       ). This area is characterized by a blur and progressive disappearance, greater as we move to the East, of the features still clearly seen in the previous area. The gradual and negative character of this characteristic explains how vague the limits are".
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#What_information_to_include" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">
          <span title="A more detailed citation is required. (January 2017)">
           full citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>

      <p>
       Two sociolinguistic studies, in northern Leon and the entire province (
       <i>
        Estudiu sociollingüísticu de Lleón
       </i>
       . Uviéu, ALLA, 2006, and
       <i>
        II Estudiu sociollingüísticu de Lleón
       </i>
       . Uviéu, ALLA, 2008) analysed the prevalence of Leonese and the linguistic attitudes of its speakers. According to the latter, maintenance of the language is the primary wish but opinions differ about how to do so. Almost 37 percent think that the language should be kept for nonofficial uses, and about 30 percent believe it should be on a par with Spanish. Twenty-two percent favour its disappearance. Nearly the population supports granting official status to Leonese by amending the Statute of Autonomy. About 70 percent favour linguistic coordination between León and Asturias, with 20 percent opposing. Leonese in education is favoured by more than 63 percent of the population, and opposed by about 34 percent. Institutional promotion of the dialect, especially by town councils, was favoured by more than 83 percent of respondents.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition">
        <span id="Political_recognition">
        </span>
        Recognition
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Recognition">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       The Statute of Autonomy of Castile and León, amended 30 November 2007, addresses the status of Spanish, Leonese, and Galician. According to Section 5.2, "Leonese will be specifically protected by the institutions for its particular value within the linguistic patrimony of the Community. Its protection, usage and promotion will be regulated".
      </p>
      <p>
       On 24 February 2010, a parliamentary group from the
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party" title="Spanish Socialist Workers' Party">
        Spanish Socialist Workers' Party
       </a>
       presented a proposition to the courts of Castile and León to recognize the value of Leonese and implement a plan to protect and promote it. Although the proposition was approved unanimously by the plenary session of the parliament of Castile and León on 26 May, the government's position has not changed.
      </p>

      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Standardization">
        Standardization
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Standardization">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       The Autonomous Community of Castile and León lacks a government agency to promote minority languages and a nongovernmental agency in an advisory capacity in matters pertaining to minority languages. The Academy of the Asturian Language has sponsored linguistic and sociolinguistic research, which encompasses the non-Asturian dialects of Asturian-Leonese. Two congresses about Leonese have been held, at which the following measures were proposed to move towards language standardization:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Based on articles 5.2 and 5.3 of the Statute of Autonomy, raise the legal status of Leonese to equal that of Galician.
       </li>
       <li>
        Create an autonomous administrative organ under the Departament of Culture and Tourism responsible for protecting and promoting Leonese and Galician.
       </li>
       <li>
        Introduce Leonese into adult and childhood education.
       </li>
       <li>
        Recover native toponymy with bilingual signage.
       </li>
       <li>
        Support cultural and literary Leonese and its publications and collaborate with associations which base their work on the recovery of Leonese, encourage Leonese in social media, and promote literary contests in the dialect.
       </li>
       <li>
        Promote study of Leonese through the universities and centres of study and investigation such as the Institute of Studies in Zamora, the Cultural Institute in Leon, the Institute of Studies of El Bierzo and the Marcelo Macías Institute of Studies in Astorga.
       </li>
       <li>
        Coordinate and cooperate with linguistic institutions, study centres, and administrations in the rest of the Asturialeonese linguistic area.
       </li>
       <li>
        Require local governments to assume responsibility for the recovery of Leonese.
       </li>
      </ul>

        </a>
       </i>
       courses, following an agreement between the Leonese provincial government and the organization. The Leonese Language Teachers and Monitors Association (
       <a href="/wiki/Asociaci%C3%B3n_de_Profesores_y_Monitores_de_Llingua_Ll%C3%AFonesa" title="Asociación de Profesores y Monitores de Llingua Llïonesa">
