      <p>
       <b>
        Algherese
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Standard Catalan
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        Alguerès
       </i>
       ,
       <small>
        pronounced
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Catalan" title="Help:IPA/Catalan">
         [əlɣəˈɾɛs]
        </a>
       </span>
       ; Algherese:
       <i>
        Alguerés
       </i>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Catalan" title="Help:IPA/Catalan">
         [alɣaˈɾes]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is the variant of the
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan language
       </a>
       spoken in the city of
       <a href="/wiki/Alghero" title="Alghero">
        Alghero
       </a>
       (
       <i>
        L'Alguer
       </i>
       in Catalan), in the northwest of
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">
        Sardinia
       </a>
       , Italy. Catalan-speaking colonists repopulated the town and expelled the native population in 1372, after several revolts.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Catalan was replaced as the official language by
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       , then by
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       in the mid 18th century, but its use remained widespread until at least the 1970s.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Today it has semi-official status alongside Italian.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       According to recent linguistic research, 22.4% of the population in Alghero speak Algherese natively and above 90% have some knowledge of this Catalan dialect. The majority of the native speakers are elderly.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Based on additional linguistic studies, there are approximately 20,000 to 30,000 native speakers of the language worldwide. In communities where Algherese is spoken,
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Logudorese_dialect" title="Logudorese dialect">
        Logudorese
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian
       </a>
       are often used as well.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Algherese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Algherese is a regional dialect spoken by anywhere from 20,000 to 30,000 individuals, most of which reside in the town of
       <a href="/wiki/Alghero" title="Alghero">
        Alghero
       </a>
       , located in the northwest of
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">
        Sardinia
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The language, though secular, is initially derived from, and thus considered a variant of, the
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan language
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The origins of the language can be traced back to 1372, when Catalan invaders repopulated the city of
       <a href="/wiki/Alghero" title="Alghero">
        Alghero
       </a>
       after exiling the indigenous populations in
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">
        Sardinia
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The language has roots in several
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       , including

       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Italo-Western_languages" title="Italo-Western languages">

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_distribution">
        Geographic distribution
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Algherese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geographic distribution">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the northwest region of
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">
        Sardinia
       </a>
       , it is estimated that
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       is now the first language of close to 60% of individuals, Algherese approximately 22%.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The use of the dialect in schools and media, to name a few, is sparse. Prior to 1997, teaching of the dialect in school was rare. However, in an attempt to reverse the trend, the Regional Council of Sardinia has officially recognized "Algherese Catalan" as a separate language in order to promote its use and circulation.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As of a 2008 study, Algherese is used by approximately 14% of the population for daily interactions.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-:1-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The dialect is mostly a local language, often used to supplement
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian
       </a>
       in small circles.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Official_status">
        Official status
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Algherese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Official status">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the northwest region of
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">
        Sardinia
       </a>
       , where this language is spoken,
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       is the primary language. Algherese Catalan acts as a secondary language or dialect in the overall region.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">
        Phonology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Algherese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Phonology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Like in other languages of Sardinia
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɛ/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /e/
        </span>
        as well as
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɔ/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /o/
        </span>
        may merge into
        <a href="/wiki/Mid_vowel" title="Mid vowel">
         mid vowels
        </a>
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Mid_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Mid front unrounded vowel">
          e̞
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Mid back rounded vowel">
          o̞
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        , respectively.
       </li>
       <li>
        Coalescing of unstressed vowels
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /a/
        </span>
        ,
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɛ/
        </span>
        and
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /e/
        </span>
        to
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Open_central_unrounded_vowel" title="Open central unrounded vowel">
          a
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        (unlike the rest of
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Catalan" title="Eastern Catalan">
         Eastern Catalan
        </a>
        , which uses
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Mid_central_vowel#Mid_central_unrounded_vowel" title="Mid central vowel">
          ə
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        ).
       </li>
       <li>
        Algherese preserves
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /v/
        </span>
        as a distinct phoneme from
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /b/
        </span>
        , like
        <a href="/wiki/Balearic_dialect" title="Balearic dialect">
         Balearic
        </a>
        and most of
        <a href="/wiki/Valencian" title="Valencian">
         Valencian
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Mutation of intervocalic
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /d/
        </span>
        or
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /l/
        </span>
        to
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alveolar_trill" title="Alveolar trill">
          r
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        : 'Barceloneta' (little Barcelona): Eastern Standard
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [bərsəɫuˈnɛtə]
        </span>
        , Algherese
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [balsaruˈne̞ta]
        </span>
        ; and
        <i>
         vila
        </i>
        ('town') and
        <i>
         vida
        </i>
        ('life') are homophones in Algherese
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈvira]
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Mutation of syllable final
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /r/
        </span>
        to lateral
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alveolar_lateral_approximant" title="Alveolar lateral approximant">
          l
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        , and the possible resulting group
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /r/
        </span>
        +
        <a href="/wiki/Consonant" title="Consonant">
         consonant
        </a>
        is further simplified to
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alveolar_lateral_approximant" title="Alveolar lateral approximant">
          l
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        :
        <i>
         forn
        </i>
        ('furnace, oven'): Standard
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈfo̞rn]
        </span>
        , Algherese
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈfo̞l]
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Depalatalization of syllable final sonorants: lateral
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ʎ/
        </span>
        to
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alveolar_lateral_approximant" title="Alveolar lateral approximant">
          l
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        , nasal
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /ɲ/
        </span>
        to
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [
         <a href="/wiki/Dental,_alveolar_and_postalveolar_nasals#Alveolar" title="Dental, alveolar and postalveolar nasals">
          n
         </a>
         ]
        </span>
        ; e.g.
        <i>
         any
        </i>
        ('year'): Standard
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈaɲ]
        </span>
        , Algherese
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈan]
        </span>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Unlike most Catalan dialects,
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         /l/
        </span>
        is never
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dark_l" title="Dark l">
         velarized
        </a>
        in Algherese:
        <i>
         sol
        </i>
        ('sun'): Standard
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈsɔɫ]
        </span>
        , Algherese
        <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
         [ˈso̞l]
        </span>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">
        Morphology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Algherese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Morphology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The simple past is replaced by the present perfect (present of
        <i>
         haver
        </i>
        "to have" + past participle), possibly by Italian influence.
       </li>
       <li>
        The imperfect past preserves etymological -v- in all conjugations: 1st -
        <i>
         ava
        </i>
        , 2nd -
        <i>
         iva
        </i>
        , 3rd -
        <i>
         iva
        </i>
        (unlike modern Eastern and Western Standard Catalan, which use 1st -
        <i>
         ava
        </i>
        , 2nd -
        <i>
         ia
        </i>
        , 3rd -
        <i>
         ia
        </i>
        , a feature shared with the
        <a href="/wiki/Ribagor%C3%A7an_dialect" title="Ribagorçan dialect">
         Ribagorçan dialect
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        Large-scale lexical borrowing and
        <a href="/wiki/Calque" title="Calque">
         calques
        </a>
        from
        <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
         Sardinian
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
         Spanish
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
         Italian
        </a>
        : nearly half of the vocabulary is not from Catalan.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
         <a href="#cite_note-11">
          [11]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
