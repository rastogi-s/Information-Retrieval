      <p>
       <b>
        Gallurese
       </b>
       (
       <i>
        gadduresu
       </i>
       ) is an
       <a href="/wiki/Italo-Dalmatian_languages" title="Italo-Dalmatian languages">
        Italo-Dalmatian
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lect" title="Lect">
        lect
       </a>
       spoken in the region of
       <a href="/wiki/Gallura" title="Gallura">
        Gallura
       </a>
       , in the northeastern part of
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">
        Sardinia
       </a>
       . It is often considered a
       <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">
        dialect
       </a>
       of southern
       <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
        Corsican
       </a>
       , or even a transitional language between
       <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
        Corsican
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Gallurese's morphology and vocabulary are close to those of
       <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
        Corsican
       </a>
       , in particular with the southern dialects of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sartene" title="Sartene">
        Sartene
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Porto-Vecchio" title="Porto-Vecchio">
        Porto-Vecchio
       </a>
       , whereas its phonology and syntax are similar to those of Sardinian.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Palau_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Palau-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       One third of Gallurese vocabulary is also influenced by
       <a href="/wiki/Logudorese_dialect" title="Logudorese dialect">
        Logudorese
       </a>
       Sardinian,
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Palau_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Palau-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Sassarese_language" title="Sassarese language">
        Sassarese language
       </a>
       , spoken in the area of
       <a href="/wiki/Sassari" title="Sassari">
        Sassari
       </a>
       , shares similar transitional traits between
       <a href="/wiki/Tuscan_dialect" title="Tuscan dialect">
        Tuscan
       </a>
       , Corsican and Sardinian.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Typical_constitutional_elements_of_Gallurese">
        Typical constitutional elements of Gallurese
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gallurese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Typical constitutional elements of Gallurese">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dialetti_corsi.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1324" data-file-width="1200" height="243" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Dialetti_corsi.png/220px-Dialetti_corsi.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Dialetti_corsi.png/330px-Dialetti_corsi.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Dialetti_corsi.png/440px-Dialetti_corsi.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dialetti_corsi.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Corsican dialects, including Gallurese.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <ul>
       <li>
        the plural form of nouns in
        <i>
         -i
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         ghjanni
        </i>
        or
        <i>
         polti
        </i>
        'doors') like in Corsican and Italian, and not in
        <i>
         -s
        </i>
        like in Sardinian (
        <i>
         jannas
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         portas
        </i>
        ), French, Spanish, Catalan, etc.
       </li>
       <li>
        Latin 'll' has become
        <i>
         -dd-
        </i>
        (like
        <i>
         casteddu, coraddu
        </i>
        'castle', 'coral'), the same as in Sardinian, southern Corsican and Sicilian (but
        <i>
         castellu, corallu
        </i>
        in northern Corsican);
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         -r-
        </i>
        modified to
        <i>
         -l-
        </i>
        (
        <i>
         poltu
        </i>
        'port', while
        <i>
         portu
        </i>
        in Corsican and Sardinian);
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         -chj-
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         -ghj-
        </i>
        sounds (
        <i>
         ghjesgia
        </i>
        'church',
        <i>
         occhji
        </i>
        'eyes'), like in Corsican, while Sardinian is
        <i>
         cresia, ogros
        </i>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        articles
        <i>
         lu
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         la
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         li
        </i>
        , like in ancient Corsican dialects (
        <i>
         u
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         a
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         i
        </i>
        in modern Corsican,
        <i>
         su
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         sa
        </i>
        ,
        <i>
         sos

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Relation_to_Corsican">
        Relation to Corsican
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gallurese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Relation to Corsican">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Gallurese is classified by some linguists as a dialect of Corsican,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and by others as a dialect of Sardinian.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In any case, a great deal of similarity exists between
       <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
        Southern Corsican dialects
       </a>
       and Gallurese, while there is relatively more distance from the neighbouring
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sardo_logudorese" title="Sardo logudorese">
        Sardinian varieties
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinia#Government_and_politics" title="Sardinia">
        Regional Government of Sardinia
       </a>
       has recognized Gallurese, along with
       <a href="/wiki/Sassarese_language" title="Sassarese language">
        Sassarese
       </a>
       as separate languages, distinct from
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gallurese_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The most ancient literary sources in Gallurese date back to the early 17th century, mainly as poetry and religious odes. Some late Middle Age fragments suggest that the formation of the language could be dated to the early 15th century. The origin and the development of Gallurese are debated.
       <a href="/wiki/Max_Leopold_Wagner" title="Max Leopold Wagner">
        Max Leopold Wagner
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Le_Lannou" title="Maurice Le Lannou">
        Maurice Le Lannou
       </a>
       argued that successive migration waves from Southern
       <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">
        Corsica
       </a>
       , promoted under the
       <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">
        Aragonese
       </a>
       rule to repopulate an area devastated by famine and pandemics, were crucial in the formation of a slightly hybridized version of Corsican.
      </p>
