   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Para-Romani
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Para-Romani
       </b>
       are non-Romani languages with considerable
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adstratum#Adstratum" title="Adstratum">
        admixture
       </a>
       from Romani (as with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yiddish_language" title="Yiddish language">
        Yiddish
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ladino_language" title="Ladino language">
        Ladino
       </a>
       among
       <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">
        Jews
       </a>
       ) spoken by
       <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">
        Romani
       </a>
       communities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Matras_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Matras-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Some Para-Romani languages have no structural features of Romani at all, taking only the vocabulary from Romani
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       . The technical term in
       <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">
        linguistics
       </a>
       for such a language is a
       <a href="/wiki/Mixed_language" title="Mixed language">
        mixed language
       </a>
       . Reflecting the linguistic makeup of Europe, most are based on
       <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_languages" title="Indo-European languages">
        Indo-European
       </a>
       languages, except for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laiuse_Romani" title="Laiuse Romani">
        Laiuse Romani
       </a>
       , which is based on
       <a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">
        Estonian
       </a>
       , and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erromintxela" title="Erromintxela">
        Erromintxela
       </a>
       , which is based on
       <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">
        Basque
       </a>
       .
      </p>
