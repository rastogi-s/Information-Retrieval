    Academia Ecuatoguineana de la Lengua Española
   </h1>

     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Equatoguinean Academy of the Spanish Language
       </b>
       (
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
         Spanish
        </a>
        :
       </i>
       <b>
        Academia Ecuatoguineana de la Lengua Española
       </b>
       ) is an association of academics and experts on the use of the Spanish language in
       <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
        Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       , a republic in
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Africa" title="Central Africa">
        Central Africa
       </a>
       in which Spanish is the national official language.
       <a href="/wiki/Equatoguinean_Spanish" title="Equatoguinean Spanish">
        Equatoguinean Spanish
       </a>
       is the particular variety of Spanish that is spoken in the country. Since 19 March 2016, it has been a member of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_of_Spanish_Language_Academies" title="Association of Spanish Language Academies">
        Association of Spanish Language Academies
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aegle-asale_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-aegle-asale-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Context">
        Context
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Academia_Ecuatoguineana_de_la_Lengua_Espa%C3%B1ola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Context">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Spanish was the administrative language of the territory of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Guinea" title="Spanish Guinea">
        Spanish Guinea
       </a>
       before its independence from Spain as the Republic of Equatorial Guinea in 1968. The
       <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Equatorial_Guinea" title="Constitution of Equatorial Guinea">
        Constitution of Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       codifies the status of Spanish as a national official language.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Since 2009, Equatorial Guinea has participated in the
       <a href="/wiki/Ibero-American_Summit" title="Ibero-American Summit">
        Ibero-American Summit
       </a>
       as an "associate member" in light of its cultural and linguistic connection with the nations of
       <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_America" title="Hispanic America">
        Hispanic America
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The country is also a member of the
       <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_Ibero-American_States" title="Organization of Ibero-American States">
        Organization of Ibero-American States
       </a>
       since 1979.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Academia_Ecuatoguineana_de_la_Lengua_Espa%C3%B1ola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In 2007, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Academia_Espa%C3%B1ola" title="Real Academia Española">
        Real Academia Española
       </a>
       invited two academics from the
       <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Equatorial_Guinea" title="National University of Equatorial Guinea">
        National University of Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       to its international summit with the intent that they serve as representatives of their country to the RAE.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In July 2009, the RAE named a further five Equatoguineans as "Academic Correspondents", including artist
       <a href="/wiki/Leandro_Mbomio_Nsue" title="Leandro Mbomio Nsue">
        Leandro Mbomio Nsue
       </a>
       , with a stated goal of creating an Equatoguinean Spanish academy in the future.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Subsequently, the institution was officially established in October 2013 by Presidential Decree number 163/2013.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It was also announced that the official
       <a href="/wiki/Diccionario_de_la_lengua_espa%C3%B1ola" title="Diccionario de la lengua española">
        RAE dictionary of Spanish
       </a>
       would, for the first time, include selected words of Equatoguinean origin.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       At the international summit of the Association of Spanish Language Academies in Puerto Rico in 2015, the Equatoguinean Academy of the Spanish Language made a formal request to join the association.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aegle-asale_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-aegle-asale-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pogge_13-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-pogge-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The admission was finalized in 2016.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-:0-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
