      <p>
       The
       <b>
        45×90 points
       </b>
       are the four points on earth which are halfway between the
       <a href="/wiki/Geographical_pole" title="Geographical pole">
        geographical poles
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        equator
       </a>
       , the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Meridian" title="Prime Meridian">
        Prime Meridian
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/180th_meridian" title="180th meridian">
        180th meridian
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span id="45.C2.B0N.2C_90.C2.B0W">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="45°N,_90°W">
        45°N, 90°W
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=45%C3%9790_points&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 45°N, 90°W">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The best-known and most frequently visited such point is
       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">
        <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=45%C3%9790_points&amp;params=45_0_0_N_90_0_0_W_">
         <span class="geo-default">
          <span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           <span class="latitude">
            45°0′0″N
           </span>
           <span class="longitude">
            90°0′0″W
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="geo-multi-punct">
          ﻿ / ﻿
         </span>
         <span class="geo-nondefault">
          <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           45.00000°N 90.00000°W
          </span>
          <span style="display:none">
           ﻿ /
           <span class="geo">
            45.00000; -90.00000
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       which is located on land at 410 m (1,345 ft) above sea level in the town of
       <a href="/wiki/Rietbrock,_Wisconsin" title="Rietbrock, Wisconsin">
        Rietbrock, Wisconsin
       </a>
       near the unincorporated community of
       <a href="/wiki/Poniatowski,_Wisconsin" title="Poniatowski, Wisconsin">
        Poniatowski
       </a>
       . An incorrect geographic marker was placed there by the
       <a href="/wiki/Marathon_County,_Wisconsin" title="Marathon County, Wisconsin">
        Marathon County
       </a>
       Park Commission, but in 2017, it was removed and relocated to the actual location
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       . It re-opened September 12, 2017
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The point has become something of a pop culture phenomenon thanks to Gesicki's Tavern in the tiny cluster of establishments in Poniatowski.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gesicki_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-gesicki-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They once sold 45×90
       <a href="/wiki/T-shirt" title="T-shirt">
        T-shirts
       </a>
       and registered visitors as members of the "45x90 Club".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gesicki_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-gesicki-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Since 2006, the Wausau/Central Wisconsin Convention &amp; Visitors Bureau has been the holder of the official "45x90 Club" registration book. The book is on loan from the Gesicki family.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Upon becoming a member of the "45x90 Club", the Wausau/Central Wisconsin Convention &amp; Visitors Bureau will give new members a commemorative coin.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="45.C2.B0N.2C_90.C2.B0E">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="45°N,_90°E">
        45°N, 90°E
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=45%C3%9790_points&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 45°N, 90°E">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The only other 45×90 point located on land is
       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">
        <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=45%C3%9790_points&amp;params=45_0_0_N_90_0_0_E_">
         <span class="geo-default">
          <span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           <span class="latitude">
            45°0′0″N
           </span>
           <span class="longitude">
            90°0′0″E
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="geo-multi-punct">
          ﻿ / ﻿
         </span>
         <span class="geo-nondefault">
          <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           45.00000°N 90.00000°E
          </span>
          <span style="display:none">
           ﻿ /
           <span class="geo">
            45.00000; 90.00000
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       which has a height of 1,009 m (3,311 ft) above sea level and is located in a desolate region of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xinjiang_Uyghur_Autonomous_Region" title="Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region">
        Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
        China
       </a>
       near the
       <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">
        Mongolian
       </a>
       border, some 240 km (150 mi) northeast of
       <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Cr%C3%BCmqi" title="Ürümqi">
        Ürümqi
       </a>
       . Administratively, it is on the border of
       <a href="/wiki/Qitai_County" title="Qitai County">
        Qitai
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Qinggil_County" title="Qinggil County">
        Qinggil
       </a>
       Counties. Greg Michaels, an American, and Ru Rong Zhao, a taxi driver from the closest town of
       <a href="/wiki/Qitai_County" title="Qitai County">
        Qitai
       </a>
       , itself 110 km to the south-southwest, visited this point on April 13, 2004 and documented the visit on the
       <a href="/wiki/Degree_Confluence_Project" title="Degree Confluence Project">
        Degree Confluence Project
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Their visit shows that there is no monument or any physical sign that recognition has been given to this location.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="45.C2.B0S.2C_90.C2.B0E">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="45°S,_90°E">
        45°S, 90°E
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=45%C3%9790_points&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 45°S, 90°E">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MH370_SIO_search.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="5720" data-file-width="7461" height="307" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/MH370_SIO_search.png/400px-MH370_SIO_search.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/MH370_SIO_search.png/600px-MH370_SIO_search.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/MH370_SIO_search.png/800px-MH370_SIO_search.png 2x" width="400"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MH370_SIO_search.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Possible debris from
         <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines_Flight_370" title="Malaysia Airlines Flight 370">
          Malaysia Airlines Flight 370
         </a>
         (colored spots in lower left) were spotted by satellite near 45°S, 90°E.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Located in the southern
       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">
        Indian Ocean
       </a>
       ,
       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">
        <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=45%C3%9790_points&amp;params=45_0_0_S_90_0_0_E_">
         <span class="geo-default">
          <span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           <span class="latitude">
            45°0′0″S
           </span>
           <span class="longitude">
            90°0′0″E
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="geo-multi-punct">
          ﻿ / ﻿
         </span>
         <span class="geo-nondefault">
          <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           45.00000°S 90.00000°E
          </span>
          <span style="display:none">
           ﻿ /
           <span class="geo">
            -45.00000; 90.00000
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       has an ocean floor depth of 3,197 m (10,489 ft) below sea level and is 1,244 km (773 mi) southeast of the nearest uninhabited island of
       <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_Saint-Paul" title="Île Saint-Paul">
        Île Saint-Paul
       </a>
       , 1,480 km (920 mi) northeast of
       <a href="/wiki/Elephant_Spit" title="Elephant Spit">
        Elephant Spit
       </a>
       on
       <a href="/wiki/Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands" title="Heard Island and McDonald Islands">
        Heard Island
       </a>
       , 1,569 km (975 mi) east north east of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_aux_Fran%C3%A7ais" title="Port aux Français">
        Port aux Français
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Kerguelen_Islands" title="Kerguelen Islands">
        Kerguelen Islands
       </a>
       , 2,425 km (1,507 mi) north of
       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">
        Antarctica
       </a>
       , 2,448 km (1,521 mi) southwest of the closest inhabited land of
       <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Western_Australia" title="Augusta, Western Australia">
        Augusta, Western Australia
       </a>
       , 4,080 km (2,535 mi) southeast of
       <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">
        Réunion
       </a>
       Island, and 5,550 km (3,450 mi) southeast of
       <a href="/wiki/Benguerra_Island" title="Benguerra Island">
        Benguerra Island
       </a>
       , Mozambique, and 5,800 km (3,600 mi) southeast of
       <a href="/wiki/Mossel_Bay" title="Mossel Bay">
        Mossel Bay
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
        South Africa
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Antipodes" title="Antipodes">
        antipode
       </a>
       of the southern Indian Ocean location is the (45°N,90°W) location in Wisconsin.
      </p>
      <p>
       In March 2014, the location in the southern Indian Ocean was the subject of an aerial search for the missing
       <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines_Flight_370" title="Malaysia Airlines Flight 370">
        Malaysia Airlines Flight 370
       </a>
       , after potential debris were spotted by satellite centered on the 45°S, 90°E location.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span id="45.C2.B0S.2C_90.C2.B0W">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="45°S,_90°W">
        45°S, 90°W
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=45%C3%9790_points&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 45°S, 90°W">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Located in the southern
       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">
        Pacific Ocean
       </a>
       , 1,297 km (806 mi) west south west of
       <a href="/wiki/Guaitecas" title="Guaitecas">
        Guaitecas
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
        Chile
       </a>
       , and 3,070 km (1,910 mi) north of Antarctica,
       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">
        <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=45%C3%9790_points&amp;params=45_0_0_S_90_0_0_W_">
         <span class="geo-default">
          <span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           <span class="latitude">
            45°0′0″S
           </span>
           <span class="longitude">
            90°0′0″W
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="geo-multi-punct">
          ﻿ / ﻿
         </span>
         <span class="geo-nondefault">
          <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">
           45.00000°S 90.00000°W
          </span>
          <span style="display:none">
           ﻿ /
           <span class="geo">
            -45.00000; -90.00000
           </span>
          </span>
         </span>
        </a>
       </span>
       has an ocean floor depth of 4,180 m (13,730 ft) below sea level.
      </p>
      <p>
       The antipode of the southern Pacific Ocean location is the (45°N,90°E) location in China.
      </p>
