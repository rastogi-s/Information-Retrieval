      <p>
       <b>
        Panhispanism
       </b>
       is a political trend aimed to achieve social, economic, and political cooperation (at the extreme, unification) of the
       <a href="/wiki/Hispanidad" title="Hispanidad">
        Spanish-speaking countries
       </a>
       , principally those of
       <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_America" title="Hispanic America">
        Hispanic America
       </a>
       , due to the distance between
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">
        Western Sahara
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">
        Equatorial Guinea
       </a>
       . It focuses principally on the former
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">
        Spanish Empire
       </a>
       's territories in
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">
        Central
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">
        South America
       </a>
       . It has been present consistently in literature, revolutionary movements, and political institutions. The term may be also used to talk specifically about projects of Hispanic American unity held by
       <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Simón Bolívar">
        Simón Bolívar
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_San_Mart%C3%ADn" title="José de San Martín">
        José de San Martín
       </a>
       .
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Panhispanism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">
        Spanish colonization of America
       </a>
       began in 1492 and ultimately was part of a larger historical process of world
       <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">
        colonialism
       </a>
       , through which various
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        European
       </a>
       powers incorporated a considerable amount of territory and peoples in the Americas, Asia, and Africa between the 15th and 20th centuries. Hispanic America became the main part of the vast
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">
        Spanish Empire
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Due to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I" title="Napoleon I">
        Napoleon
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">
        takeover of Spain
       </a>
       in 1808 and the consequent chaos initiated the dismemberment of the Spanish Empire as American territories began their
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_American_wars_of_independence" title="Spanish American wars of independence">
        struggle for emancipation
       </a>
       , by 1830 the only remaining Spanish American colonies were
       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">
        Cuba
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">
        Puerto Rico
       </a>
       (and in the Pacific Ocean
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">
        Guam
       </a>
       ) until the 1898
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">
        Spanish–American War
       </a>
       .
      </p>
