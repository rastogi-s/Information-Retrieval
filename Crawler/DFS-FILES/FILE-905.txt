      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Occitano-Romance
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        Gallo-Narbonnese
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="ca" xml:lang="ca">
         <b>
          llengües occitanoromàniques
         </b>
        </span>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="oc" xml:lang="oc">
         <b>
          lengas occitanoromanicas
         </b>
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a branch of the
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance language group
       </a>
       that encompasses the
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan language
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan language
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Aragonese_language" title="Aragonese language">
        Aragonese language
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Extent">
        Extent
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Occitano-Romance_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Extent">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The group covers the languages of the southern part of
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Occitania" title="Occitania">
        Occitania
       </a>
       including
       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Catalonia" title="Northern Catalonia">
        Northern Catalonia
       </a>
       ), and, in Spain,
       <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">
        Catalonia
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencia_(autonomous_community)" title="Valencia (autonomous community)">
        Valencian Community
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">
        Balearic Islands
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/La_Franja" title="La Franja">
        La Franja
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Carche" title="Carche">
        Carche
       </a>
       ), together with
       <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">
        Andorra
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">
        Monaco
       </a>
       , parts of
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_Valleys" title="Occitan Valleys">
        Occitan Valleys
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Alghero" title="Alghero">
        Alghero
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Guardia_Piemontese" title="Guardia Piemontese">
        Guardia Piemontese
       </a>
       ), and historically in the
       <a href="/wiki/County_of_Tripoli" title="County of Tripoli">
        County of Tripoli
       </a>
       and the possessions of the
       <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">
        Crown of Aragon
       </a>
       . The existence of this group of languages is discussed on both linguistic and political bases.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">
        Classification
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Occitano-Romance_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Classification">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       According to some linguists both
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       should be considered
       <a href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance_languages" title="Gallo-Romance languages">
        Gallo-Romance languages
       </a>
       . Other linguists concur as regarding Occitan but consider Catalan to be part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
        Ibero-Romance languages
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The issue at debate is as political as it is linguistic, because the division into
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Romance" title="Gallo-Romance">
        Gallo-Romance
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ibero-Romance_languages" title="Ibero-Romance languages">
        Ibero-Romance languages
       </a>
       stems from the current nation-states of
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       , and thus is based more on territorial criteria than historic and linguistic criteria. One of the main proponents of the unity of the languages of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iberian_peninsula" title="Iberian peninsula">
        Iberian peninsula
       </a>
       was Spanish philologist
       <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Men%C3%A9ndez_Pidal" title="Ramón Menéndez Pidal">
        Ramón Menéndez Pidal
       </a>
       , while for a long time others such as Swiss linguist
       <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Meyer-L%C3%BCbke" title="Wilhelm Meyer-Lübke">
        Wilhelm Meyer-Lübke
       </a>
       (
       <i>
        Das Katalanische
       </i>
       , Heidelberg, 1925) have supported the kinship of
       <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
        Occitan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       From the 8th century up until the 13th, there was no clear linguistic distinction between Occitania and Catalonia. For instance, the
       <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">
        Provençal
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Troubadour" title="Troubadour">
        troubadour
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Albertet_de_Sestaro" title="Albertet de Sestaro">
        Albertet de Sestaró
       </a>
       , says: "Monks, tell me which according to your knowledge are better: the French or the Catalans? and here I shall put Gascony, Provence, Limousin, Auvergne and Viennois while there shall be the land of the two kings."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">
        Marseille
       </a>
       , a typical Provençal song is called 'Catalan song'. (M. Milà i Fontanals,
       <i>
        De los Trobadores en España
       </i>
       , p. 487)
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Internal_variation">
        Internal variation
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [

      </h2>

      <p>
       Even though most linguists do separate Catalan and Occitan, both languages have been treated as one in studies by Occitan linguists attempting to classify the dialects of Occitan in supradialectal groups. This is the case of
       <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Bec" title="Pierre Bec">
        Pierre Bec
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and, more recently, of
       <a href="/wiki/Domergue_Sumien" title="Domergue Sumien">
        Domergue Sumien
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Both join together in an Aquitano-Pyrenean or Pre-Iberian group including Catalan, Gascon and a part of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Languedocien" title="Languedocien">
        Languedocian
       </a>
       , leaving the rest of Occitan in one (Sumien: Arverno-Mediterranean) or two groups (Bec: Arverno-Mediterranean, Central Occitan).
      </p>
