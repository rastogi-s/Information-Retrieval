      <p>
       <b>
        Azuero Peninsula
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       :
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         Península de Azuero
        </span>
       </i>
       ) is a large peninsula in southern
       <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">
        Panama
       </a>
       . It is surrounded by the Pacific Ocean in the south; the Pacific and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_of_Montijo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gulf of Montijo (page does not exist)">
        Gulf of Montijo
       </a>
       to the west, and by the
       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Panama" title="Gulf of Panama">
        Gulf of Panama
       </a>
       in the east. The peninsula is effectively divided into two regions; the Western Azuero and the Eastern Azuero, as no serviceable roads join the two peninsula regions past the
       <a href="/wiki/Pan-American_Highway" title="Pan-American Highway">
        Pan-American Highway
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Eastern Azuero Peninsula is known for baseball and is also a center of activity during the annual '
       <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">
        carnaval
       </a>
       ' (
       <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">
        carnival
       </a>
       ), with Las Tablas being the hub.
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedas%C3%AD_township,_Los_Santos" title="Pedasí township, Los Santos">
        Pedasi
       </a>
       is a small fishing town with sport fishing.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Western Azuero Peninsula is known for its cattle ranching, farming, fishing, sunsets and beaches.
      </p>
      <p>
       Tourism has begun to increase in the area both for the aforementioned sport fishing, surfing and for the local charm of cities like
       <a href="/wiki/Chitr%C3%A9" title="Chitré">
        Chitré
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Las_Tablas,_Los_Santos" title="Las Tablas, Los Santos">
        Las Tablas
       </a>
       and Pedasi. Due to a rise in tourism, real estate development has begun. The area enjoys some of the best weather in Panama being in a region known as the "
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Arco_Seco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Arco Seco (page does not exist)">
        Arco Seco
       </a>
       " (dry arc).
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">
        Geography
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Azuero_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Azuero is divided into three provinces,
       <a href="/wiki/Herrera_Province" title="Herrera Province">
        Herrera
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Los_Santos_Province" title="Los Santos Province">
        Los Santos
       </a>
       (which are entirely on the peninsula), and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veraguas" title="Veraguas">
        Veraguas
       </a>
       which extends into it on the west side of the peninsula. Most of the people live on the east coastal region. The southern tip is sparsely populated, and western area (the Veraguas area) is just now opening up to development. The largest towns are
       <a href="/wiki/Chitr%C3%A9" title="Chitré">
        Chitré
       </a>
       , the capital of Herrera, and
       <a href="/wiki/Las_Tablas,_Los_Santos" title="Las Tablas, Los Santos">
        Las Tablas
       </a>
       . The main road to the eastern Azuero peninsula connects to the
       <a href="/wiki/Pan-American_Highway" title="Pan-American Highway">
        Pan-American Highway
       </a>
       in
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Divisa,_Panama&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Divisa, Panama (page does not exist)">
        Divisa
       </a>
       and runs south along the eastern side of the peninsula. The road to the western Azuero connects to the Pan-American just east of Santiago.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Punta_Mariato" title="Punta Mariato">
        Punta Mariato
       </a>
       on the western tip of the peninsula is the southernmost point on the mainland of Central America.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Azuero_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Azuero region was one of the first parts of Panama to be settled more than 10,000 years ago, and the area in the north of the peninsula was cultivated thousands of years before the arrival of the Spaniards.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">
        Environment
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Azuero_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Environment">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Its long history of cultivation and grazing has had a negative impact on the environment, resulting in what is sometimes called the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sarigua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sarigua (page does not exist)">
        Sarigua
       </a>
       desert in the northeastern area of the peninsula, even though it is not technically a desert. Along the coast there are areas of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mangroves" title="Mangroves">
        mangroves
       </a>
       and just inland is a dry coastal forest called
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Albina_(vegetation)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Albina (vegetation) (page does not exist)">
        Albina
       </a>
       . The Azuero is one of the most heavily deforested parts of Panamá, although
       <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_land_management" title="Sustainable land management">
        sustainable land management
       </a>
       systems such as SilvoPastoral grazing systems are emerging.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the extreme south,
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_Hoya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cerro Hoya (page does not exist)">
        Cerro Hoya
       </a>
       is a national park and home of most of the remaining jungle habitat in the Azuero region. Isla Canas, a coastal island connected via a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandbar" title="Sandbar">
        sandbar
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">
        peninsula
       </a>
       , is used by a large number of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_turtles" title="Sea turtles">
        sea turtles
       </a>
       to lay their eggs each year.
      </p>
