      <p>
       <b>
        Caló
       </b>
       (
       <small>
        Spanish:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">
         [kaˈlo]
        </a>
       </span>
       ;
       <small>
        Catalan:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Catalan" title="Help:IPA/Catalan">
         [kəˈɫo]
        </a>
       </span>
       ;
       <small>
        Galician:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Galician" title="Help:IPA/Galician">
         [kaˈlɔ]
        </a>
       </span>
       ;
       <small>
        Portuguese:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">
         [kɐˈlɔ]
        </a>
       </span>
       ) is a language spoken by the
       <a href="/wiki/Romani_people_in_Spain" title="Romani people in Spain">
        Spanish
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Romani_people_in_Portugal" title="Romani people in Portugal">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       Romani. It is a
       <a href="/wiki/Mixed_language" title="Mixed language">
        mixed language
       </a>
       (referred to as a
       <a href="/wiki/Para-Romani" title="Para-Romani">
        Para-Romani
       </a>
       language in Romani linguistics) based on
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Romance_languages" title="Iberian Romance languages">
        Romance grammar
       </a>
       , with an
       <a href="/wiki/Stratum_(linguistics)#Adstratum" title="Stratum (linguistics)">
        adstratum
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Romani_language" title="Romani language">
        Romani
       </a>
       lexical items
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       through
       <a href="/wiki/Language_shift" title="Language shift">
        language shift
       </a>
       by the Romani community. It is often used as an
       <a href="/wiki/Argot" title="Argot">
        argot
       </a>
       , a secret language for discreet communication amongst Iberian Romani. Catalan, Galician, Portuguese, and Spanish
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          caló
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       are closely related varieties that share a common root.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adiego_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Adiego-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Spanish caló, or Spanish Romani, was originally known as
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <i>
          zincaló
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       . Portuguese
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         <i>
          caló
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       , or Portuguese Romani, also goes by the term
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         <i>
          lusitano-romani
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       ; it used to go by
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         <i>
          calão
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       , but this word acquired the general sense of jargon or slang, often in a negative conotation (cf.
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         <i>
          baixo calão
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       , 'obscene language', lit. low-level
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         <i>
          calão
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       ).
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">
        Etymology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cal%C3%B3_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          Calé
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       is the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endonym" title="Endonym">
        endonym
       </a>
       of the Romani people in Iberia, and
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          caló
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       means "the language spoken by the
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          calé
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       . However, the
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          calé
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       are commonly known in Portuguese- and Spanish-speaking countries by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exonym" title="Exonym">
        exonyms
       </a>
       <i>
        <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
         <i>
          ciganos
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <i>
          gitanos
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          caló
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       and other varieties of
       <a href="/wiki/Romani_language" title="Romani language">
        Romani
       </a>
       ,
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          kalo
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       means "black or "absorbing all light",
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       hence closely resembling words for "black" and/or "dark" in
       <a href="/wiki/Indo-Aryan_languages" title="Indo-Aryan languages">
        Indo-Aryan
       </a>
       languages (e.g.
       <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">
        Sanskrit
       </a>
       काल
       <i>
        kāla
       </i>
       "black", "of a dark colour"). Hence
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>
          caló
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <span lang="rmq" xml:lang="rmq">
         <i>

       <a href="/wiki/Domba" title="Domba">

        <a href="#cite_note-7">

       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>

       <i>

       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">

          caló
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>
       or Spanish Romani:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Spanish caló (
        <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
         Spanish
        </a>
        :
        <i>
         <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
          caló español
         </span>
        </i>
        )
       </li>
       <li>
        Catalan caló (
        <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
         Catalan
        </a>
        :
        <i>
         <span lang="ca" xml:lang="ca">
          caló català
         </span>
        </i>
        )
       </li>
       <li>
        Occitan caló (
        <a href="/wiki/Occitan_language" title="Occitan language">
         Occitan
        </a>
        :
        <i>
         <span lang="oc" xml:lang="oc">
          caló occitan
         </span>
        </i>
        )
       </li>
       <li>
        Portuguese caló (
        <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">

        </a>

         <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt">
          caló português
         </span>
        </i>

       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <i>
          iberorromaní
         </i>
        </span>
       </i>

       <i>
        <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">
         <i>

        </span>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Language_maintenance">
        Language maintenance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cal%C3%B3_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Language maintenance">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There is a growing awareness and appreciation for Caló: "...until the recent work by Luisa Rojo, in the
       <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_University_of_Madrid" title="Autonomous University of Madrid">
        Autonomous University of Madrid
       </a>
       , not even the linguistics community recognized the significance and problems of Caló and its world."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Its world includes songs, poetry and flamenco.
      </p>
      <p>
       As Iberian Romani proper is extinct and as Caló is endangered, some people are trying to
       <a href="/wiki/Language_revitalization" title="Language revitalization">
        revitalise
       </a>
       the language. The Spanish politician
       <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Dios_Ram%C3%ADrez_Heredia" title="Juan de Dios Ramírez Heredia">
        Juan de Dios Ramírez Heredia
       </a>
       promotes
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roman%C3%B2-Kal%C3%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Romanò-Kalò (page does not exist)">
        Romanò-Kalò
       </a>
       , a variant of
       <a href="/wiki/Romani_language_standardization" title="Romani language standardization">
        International Romani
       </a>
       , enriched by Caló words.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CursoRamírez_18-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-CursoRamírez-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       His goal is to reunify the Caló and Romani roots.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Literature">
        Literature
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cal%C3%B3_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Literature">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In 1838, the first edition of
       <i>
        Embéo E Majaró Lucas
       </i>
       translated by
       <a href="/wiki/George_Borrow" title="George Borrow">
        George Borrow
       </a>
       was published and began to be distributed in
       <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">
        Madrid
       </a>
       . This was Borrow's translation of the
       <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_Luke" title="Gospel of Luke">
        Gospel of Luke
       </a>
       into Caló.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A revision of this was printed in 1872.
      </p>
