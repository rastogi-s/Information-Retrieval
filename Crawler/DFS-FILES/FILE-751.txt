      <p>
       <b>
        Portuñol
       </b>
       (Spanish spelling) or
       <b>
        Portunhol
       </b>
       (Portuguese spelling) (
       <span class="unicode haudio">
        <span class="fn">
         <span style="white-space:nowrap">
          <a href="/wiki/File:Br-Portunol.ogg" title="About this sound">
           <img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/>
          </a>
         </span>
         <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/8/8c/Br-Portunol.ogg" title="Br-Portunol.ogg">
          pronunciation
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       ) is the name often given to any unsystematic mixture of
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       with
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garcia_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Garcia-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The word is a
       <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">
        portmanteau
       </a>
       of the words
       <b>
        Portugués/Português
       </b>
       ("Portuguese") and
       <b>
        Español/Espanhol
       </b>
       ("Spanish").
      </p>
      <p>
       Portuñol, or Portunhol, is a pidgin, or simplified mixture of the two languages, that allows speakers of either Spanish or Portuguese who are not proficient in the other language to communicate with one another.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcos_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Marcos-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       When speakers of one of the languages attempt to speak the other language, there is often interference from the native language, which causes the phenomenon of
       <a href="/wiki/Code-switching" title="Code-switching">
        code-switching
       </a>
       to occur.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is possible to conduct a moderately fluent conversation in this way because Portuguese and Spanish are closely related
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       . They have almost identical syntactic structures, as well as overlapping lexicons due to cognates, which means that a single macro-grammar is produced when the two mix.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The phrase
       <i>
        en el hueco de la noite longa e langue
       </i>
       illustrates a code-switch between the Spanish article
       <i>
        la
       </i>
       and the Portuguese noun
       <i>
        noite
       </i>
       . This example reveals the grammatical possibilities of code-switching between the two languages.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">
        Origins
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portu%C3%B1ol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tmulti tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:204px;max-width:204px">
        <div class="tsingle" style="margin:1px;width:202px;max-width:202px">
         <div class="thumbimage">
          <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iberian_Peninsula_location_map.svg">
           <img alt="" data-file-height="1016" data-file-width="1184" height="172" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Iberian_Peninsula_location_map.svg/200px-Iberian_Peninsula_location_map.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Iberian_Peninsula_location_map.svg/300px-Iberian_Peninsula_location_map.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Iberian_Peninsula_location_map.svg/400px-Iberian_Peninsula_location_map.svg.png 2x" width="200"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="thumbcaption" style="clear:left">
          Map showing the border that separates Portugal (left) from Spain (right) in the Iberian Peninsula, a region where Portuñol is generally spoken.
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Language_contact" title="Language contact">
        Language contact
       </a>
       between Spanish and Portuguese is the result of sustained contact between the two languages in border communities and multilingual trade environments.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_3-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Such regions include the border regions between
       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">
        Portugal
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">
        Iberian Peninsula
       </a>
       , as well as the ones between
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       , whose official language is Portuguese,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ethnologue_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ethnologue-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and most of its neighboring countries, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
        Uruguay
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">
        Paraguay
       </a>
       , whose official languages are Spanish.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ethnologue_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Ethnologue-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Because
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       is a spontaneous register resulting from the occasional mixing of Spanish and Portuguese, it is highly diverse;
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garcia_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Garcia-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       there is no one dialect or standard of
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       . There does, however, tend to be a stronger presence of Spanish in
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcos_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Marcos-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Contemporary">
        Contemporary
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portu%C3%B1ol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Contemporary">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In recent years,
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       has begun to appear in realms other than everyday speech. It has become a literary medium, especially in
       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
        Uruguay
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       . María Jesus Fernández García describes it as a linguistic recreation of the actual language. However, she goes on to say that the literature only occasionally provides a true representation of
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       , and that authors often choose to select only some of the features that are characteristic of
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garcia_1-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Garcia-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       One important literary work written in
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       is
       <i>
        Mar paraguayo
       </i>
       by Brazilian author Wilson Bueno. The following passage shows the mixing of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       in his novel.
      </p>
      <p>
       The appearance of
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       has prompted two opposing opinions or attitudes towards its existence. On the one hand, it is viewed as the product of laziness among speakers unwilling to learn a different language. On the other hand, it is seen as the logical product of globalization.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipski_3-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-Lipski-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As far as the future of
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       is concerned, according to
       <a href="/wiki/Francisco_A._Marcos-Mar%C3%ADn" title="Francisco A. Marcos-Marín">
        Francisco A. Marcos-Marín
       </a>
       , it is too difficult to evaluate possible repercussions that
       <i>
        Portuñol
       </i>
       could have on future linguistic maps because it is not easy to separate linguistic tendencies that are merely in style and those that are permanent.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcos_2-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Marcos-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sample_text">
        Sample text
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Portu%C3%B1ol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sample text">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <table>
       <tr>
        <th>
         Portuguese
        </th>
        <th>
         Portuñol
        </th>
        <th>
         Spanish
        </th>
       </tr>
       <tr>
        <td>
         Hoje me vejo diante de seu olhar de morto, este homem que me faz dançar castanholas na cama, que me faz sofrer, que me faz, que me construiu de dor e sangue, o sangue que verteu minha vida amarga. Desde seus ombros, meu destino igual àquele feito de um punhal na chave direita do coração.
         <sup>
          1
         </sup>
         Agora neste momento, eu não sei o que falar com sua cara dura, vermelhos/roxos
         <sup>
          2
         </sup>
         os olhos soterrados, estes que eram meus olhos.
        </td>
        <td>
         <i>
          “Hoy me vejo adelante de su olhar de muerto, este hombre que me hace dançar castanholas en la cama, que me hace sofrir, que me hace, que me há construído de dolor y sangre, la sangre que vertiô mi vida amarga. Desde sus ombros, mi destino igual quel hecho de uno punhal en la clave derecha del corazón.
          <sup>
           1
          </sup>
          Ahora en neste momento, yo no sê que hablar com su cara dura, rojos
          <sup>
           2
          </sup>
          los olhos soterrados, estos que eram mis ojos.”
         </i>
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
          <a href="#cite_note-5">
           [5]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </td>
        <td>
         Hoy me veo delante de su mirada de muerto, este hombre que me hace bailar castañuelas en la cama, que me hace sufrir, que me hace, que me ha construido de dolor y sangre, la sangre que vertió mi vida amarga. Desde sus hombros, mi destino igual aquél hecho de un puñal en la clave derecha del corazón.
         <sup>
          1
         </sup>
         Ahora en este momento, yo no sé qué hablar con su cara dura, rojos
         <sup>
          2
         </sup>
         los ojos soterrados, estos que eran mis ojos.
        </td>
       </tr>
       <tr>
        <td colspan="3" style="border-top:solid 1px;">
         <b>
          Notes:
         </b>
         <dl>
          <dd>
           <sup>
            1
           </sup>
           <i>
            [uno punhal] en la clave derecha del corazón
           </i>
           ([a dagger] in the right key of the heart) does not make much sense in either language; there is, however, the expression "
           <i>
            clavar el puñal"/"cravar o punhal
           </i>
           " (to stab with a dagger), which would give: "
           <i>
            un puñal clavado en la derecha del corazón"/"um punhal cravado à direita do coração
           </i>
           " (a dagger stabbed to the right side of the heart) or "
           <i>
            un puñal clavado derecho
           </i>
           (or
           <i>
            directo
           </i>
           )
           <i>
            en el corazón"/"um punhal cravado direito no coração
           </i>
           " (a dagger stabbed right into the heart)
          </dd>
          <dd>
           <sup>
            2
           </sup>
           <i>
            rojo/roxo
           </i>
           is a
           <a href="/wiki/False_friend" title="False friend">
            false friend
           </a>
           , meaning red in Spanish and purple in Portuguese. Because of that, it is difficult to determine which color the author had in mind.
          </dd>
         </dl>
        </td>
