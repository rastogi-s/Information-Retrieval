      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Tropic of Capricorn
       </b>
       (or the
       <b>
        Southern Tropic
       </b>
       ) is the
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">
        circle of latitude
       </a>
       that contains the
       <a href="/wiki/Subsolar_point" title="Subsolar point">
        subsolar point
       </a>
       on the December (or southern)
       <a href="/wiki/Solstice" title="Solstice">
        solstice
       </a>
       . It is thus the southernmost latitude where the Sun can be directly overhead. Its northern equivalent is the
       <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">
        Tropic of Cancer
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The Tropic of Capricorn is one of the five major circles of latitude that mark
       <a href="/wiki/Map" title="Map">
        maps
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">
        Earth
       </a>
       . As of 1 February 2018, its latitude is 23°26′13.0″ (or 23.43694°)
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       south of the
       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">
        Equator
       </a>
       , but it is very gradually moving northward, currently at the rate of 0.47
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arcseconds" title="Arcseconds">
        arcseconds
       </a>
       , or 15 metres, per year.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_environment">
        Geography and environment
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropic_of_Capricorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and environment">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Tropic of Capricorn is the dividing line between the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate">
        Southern Temperate Zone
       </a>
       to the south and the tropics to the north. The
       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">
        Northern Hemisphere
       </a>
       equivalent of the Tropic of Capricorn is the
       <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">
        Tropic of Cancer
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The position of the Tropic of Capricorn is not fixed, but rather it varies in a complex manner over time; see under
       <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude#Movement_of_the_Tropical_and_Polar_circles" title="Circle of latitude">
        circles of latitude
       </a>
       for information.
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
        Australia
       </a>
       , areas on the Tropic have some of the most variable
       <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">
        rainfall
       </a>
       in the world
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and thus even the wetter areas cannot be generally
       <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">
        farmed
       </a>
       , since irrigation sources invariably dry up in
       <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">
        drought
       </a>
       years. In
       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">
        southern Africa
       </a>
       , where rainfall is more reliable, farming is possible, though yields are low even with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fertiliser" title="Fertiliser">
        fertilisers
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">
        South America
       </a>
       , whilst in the continental
       <a href="/wiki/Craton" title="Craton">
        cratons
       </a>
       soils are almost as old as in Australia and Southern Africa, the presence of the geologically young and evolving
       <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">
        Andes
       </a>
       means that this region is on the western side of the subtropical
       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">
        anticyclones
       </a>
       and thus receives warm and humid air from the
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">
        Atlantic Ocean
       </a>
       . As a result, areas in
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       adjacent to the Tropic are extremely important agricultural regions, producing large quantities of crops such as
       <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">
        sugarcane
       </a>
       , and the natural
       <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">
        rainforest
       </a>
       vegetation has been almost entirely cleared, except for a few remaining patches of
       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Forest" title="Atlantic Forest">
        Atlantic Forest
       </a>
       . Further south in
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       , the temperate grasslands of the
       <a href="/wiki/Pampas" title="Pampas">
        Pampas
       </a>
       region is one of the most productive agricultural regions in the world, producing of
       <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">
        wheat
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soybeans" title="Soybeans">
        soybeans
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">
        maize
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Beef" title="Beef">
        beef
       </a>
       , making the country one of the largest worldwide agricultural exporters, similar to the role played by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prairies" title="Prairies">
        Prairies
       </a>
       region in
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       West of the Andes, however, the
       <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">
        Humboldt Current
       </a>
       makes conditions extremely arid, creating the
       <a href="/wiki/Atacama_Desert" title="Atacama Desert">
        Atacama Desert
       </a>
       , one of the driest in the world, so that no
       <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">
        glaciers
       </a>
       exist between
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volc%C3%A1n_Sajama" title="Volcán Sajama">
        Volcán Sajama
       </a>
       at 18˚30'S and
       <a href="/wiki/Nevado_Tres_Cruces" title="Nevado Tres Cruces">
        Cerro Tres Cruces
       </a>
       at
       <a href="/wiki/27th_parallel_south" title="27th parallel south">
        27˚S
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">
        Vegetation
       </a>
       here is almost non-existent, though on the eastern slopes of the Andes rainfall is adequate for rainfed agriculture.
      </p>
      <p>
       Further south in the region of
       <a href="/wiki/Patagonia" title="Patagonia">
        Patagonia
       </a>
       , shared between
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">
        Chile
       </a>
       , climate becomes colder, with heavy snowfall in the winter months and throughout the year. On the Western portion of Patagonia, climate is more humid, and dominated by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fjords" title="Fjords">
        fjords
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coniferous_forests" title="Coniferous forests">
        coniferous forests
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lakes" title="Lakes">
        lakes
       </a>
       owing to the presence of thawing currents from the permafrost in the peaks of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andes_mountains" title="Andes mountains">
        Andes mountains
       </a>
       which divide the region. This makes the region a popular destination for
       <a href="/wiki/Ski" title="Ski">
        ski
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_sports" title="Winter sports">
        winter sports
       </a>
       , particularly on the Argentine city of
       <a href="/wiki/Bariloche" title="Bariloche">
        Bariloche
       </a>
       and surrounding area. On the Eastern portion of Patagonia climate is generally cold and dry
       <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">
        steppe
       </a>
       , with high mesas and strong wind conditions.
      </p>
      <p>
       The southern tip of the island of
       <a href="/wiki/Tierra_del_Fuego" title="Tierra del Fuego">
        Tierra del Fuego
       </a>
       and other islands of the South Atlantic have
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sub-Antarctic" title="Sub-Antarctic">
        Sub-Antarctic
       </a>
       climate conditions.
      </p>

        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropic_of_Capricorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: List of countries entirely south of the Tropic of Capricorn">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       As the major portion of earth's land is located in the
       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">
        Northern Hemisphere
       </a>
       there are only four countries entirely south of the Tropic of Capricorn (there are 74 countries entirely north of the
       <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">
        Tropic of Cancer
       </a>
       ):
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">
         Lesotho
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">
         New Zealand
        </a>
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
         <a href="#cite_note-4">
          [4]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">
         Swaziland
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">
         Uruguay
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
        Name
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropic_of_Capricorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Name">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Tropic of Capricorn is so named because, when it was named about 2,000 years ago, the sun was also in the direction of the
       <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">
        constellation
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Capricornus" title="Capricornus">
        Capricornus
       </a>
       (
       <i>
        capricorn
       </i>
       is
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latin
       </a>
       for
       <i>
        goat horn
       </i>
       ) at the
       <a href="/wiki/December_solstice" title="December solstice">
        December solstice
       </a>
       . In modern times the sun appears in the constellation
       <a href="/wiki/Sagittarius_(constellation)" title="Sagittarius (constellation)">
        Sagittarius
       </a>
       during this time. The change is due to
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precession_of_the_equinoxes" title="Precession of the equinoxes">
        precession of the equinoxes
       </a>
       . The word "tropic" itself comes from the Greek
       <i>
        trope (τροπή)
       </i>
       , meaning
       <i>
        turn, change in direction or circumstances
       </i>
       , referring to the fact that the sun appears to "turn back" at the solstices.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Length">
        Length
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropic_of_Capricorn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Length">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Length of the Tropic on 11 June 2015, at 23°26′14″S is
       <b>
        36,788 kilometres (22,859 mi)
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
