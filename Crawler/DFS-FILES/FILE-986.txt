      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Italo-Dalmatian languages
       </b>
       , or
       <b>
        Central Romance languages
       </b>
       , are a group of
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">
        Corsica
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       ) and formerly in
       <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">
        Dalmatia
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">
        Croatia
       </a>
       ).
      </p>
      <p>
       Italo-Dalmatian can be split into:
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glottolog2.3_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Glottolog2.3-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Italo-Romance, which includes most central and southern Italian languages.
       </li>
       <li>
        Dalmatian Romance, which includes
        <a href="/wiki/Dalmatian_language" title="Dalmatian language">
         Dalmatian
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Istriot_language" title="Istriot language">
         Istriot
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The generally accepted four branches of the
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       are
       <a href="/wiki/Western_Romance_languages" title="Western Romance languages">
        Western Romance
       </a>
       , Italo-Dalmatian,
       <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
        Sardinian
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Romance_languages" title="Eastern Romance languages">
        Eastern Romance
       </a>
       . But there are other ways that the languages of Italo-Dalmatian can be classified in these branches:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Italo-Dalmatian is sometimes included in
        <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Romance_languages" title="Eastern Romance languages">
         Eastern Romance
        </a>
        (which includes
        <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">
         Romanian
        </a>
        ), leading to: Western, Sardinian, and Eastern branches.
       </li>
       <li>
        Italo-Dalmatian is sometimes included in
        <a href="/wiki/Western_Romance_languages" title="Western Romance languages">
         Western Romance
        </a>
        (which includes the Gallic and Iberian languages) as
        <a href="/wiki/Italo-Western_languages" title="Italo-Western languages">
         Italo-Western
        </a>
        , leading to: Italo-Western, Sardinian, and Eastern branches.
       </li>
       <li>
        Italo-Romance is sometimes included in Italo-Western, with Dalmatian Romance included in Eastern Romance, leading to: Italo-Western, Sardinian, and Eastern branches.
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
         Corsican
        </a>
        (from Italo-Dalmatian) and
        <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">
         Sardinian
        </a>
        are sometimes included together as
        <a href="/wiki/Southern_Romance_languages" title="Southern Romance languages">
         Southern Romance
        </a>
        , or Island Romance, leading to: Western, Italo-Dalmatian, Southern, and Eastern branches.
       </li>
      </ul>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">
        Languages
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Italo-Dalmatian_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Languages">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Based on
       <a href="/wiki/Mutual_intelligibility" title="Mutual intelligibility">
        mutual intelligibility
       </a>
       , Dalby lists four languages:
       <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
        Corsican
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuscan_language" title="Tuscan language">
        Tuscan
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napolitan_language" title="Napolitan language">
        Napolitan
       </a>
       –
       <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_language" title="Sicilian language">
        Sicilian
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Dalmatian_language" title="Dalmatian language">
        Dalmatian
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dalby_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dalby-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Dalmatian_Romance">
        Dalmatian Romance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Italo-Dalmatian_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Dalmatian Romance">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Dalmatian_language" title="Dalmatian language">
         Dalmatian language
        </a>
        was spoken in the
        <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">
         Dalmatia
        </a>
        region of
        <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">
         Croatia
        </a>
        . It became extinct in the 19th century.
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Istriot_language" title="Istriot language">
         Istriot
        </a>
        , spoken in the southwestern part of
        <a href="/wiki/Istria" title="Istria">
         Istrian peninsula
        </a>
        in
        <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">
         Croatia
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tuscan">
        Tuscan
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Italo-Dalmatian_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tuscan">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuscan_language" title="Tuscan language">
         Tuscan
        </a>
        -
        <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
         Corsican
        </a>
        : group of dialects spoken in the Italian region of
        <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">
         Tuscany
        </a>
        , and the French island of
        <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">
         Corsica
        </a>
        .
        <ul>
         <li>
          Northern Tuscan dialects:
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a href="/wiki/Florentine_dialect" title="Florentine dialect">
             Florentine
            </a>
            is spoken in the city of
            <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">
             Florence
            </a>
            , and was the basis for
            <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
             Standard Italian
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            Other dialects: Pistoiese; Pesciatino or Valdinievolese; Lucchese; Versiliese; Viareggino; Pisano-Livornese.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </li>
         <li>
          Southern Tuscan dialects:
          <ul>
           <li>
            Dialects of Aretino-Chianaiolo, Senese, Grossetano.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Corsican_language" title="Corsican language">
           Corsican
          </a>
          , spoken on
          <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">
           Corsica
          </a>
          , is thought to be descended mainly from Tuscan.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harris_4-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-Harris-4">
            [4]
           </a>
          </sup>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallurese" title="Gallurese">
             Gallurese
            </a>
            and
            <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassarese" title="Sassarese">
             Sassarese
            </a>
            , spoken on the northern tip of Sardinia, can be considered as dialects of Corsican.
           </li>
          </ul>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>

       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
         Italian
        </a>
        is an official language in
        <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
         Italy
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
         Switzerland
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">
         San Marino
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">
         Vatican City
        </a>
        and western
        <a href="/wiki/Istria" title="Istria">
         Istria
        </a>
        (in
        <a href="/wiki/Slovene_Istria" title="Slovene Istria">
         Slovenia
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Istria_County" title="Istria County">
         Croatia
        </a>
        ). It used to have official status in
        <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">
         Albania
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">
         Malta
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">
         Monaco
        </a>
        , where it is still widely spoken, as well as in former
        <a href="/wiki/Italian_East_Africa" title="Italian East Africa">
         Italian East Africa
        </a>
        and
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_North_Africa" title="Italian North Africa">
         Italian North Africa
        </a>
        regions where it plays a significant role in various sectors. Italian is also spoken by large
        <a href="/wiki/Italian_diaspora" title="Italian diaspora">
         expatriate communities
        </a>
        in
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Americas" title="The Americas">
         the Americas
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
         Australia
        </a>
        . The
        <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
         Italian language
        </a>
        was initially and primarily based on
        <a href="/wiki/Florentine_dialect" title="Florentine dialect">
         Florentine
        </a>
        : it has been then deeply influenced by almost all
        <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Italy" title="Languages of Italy">
         regional languages of Italy
        </a>
        while its
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Received_pronunciation" title="Received pronunciation">
         received pronunciation
        </a>
        (known as
        <i>
         Pronuncia Fiorentina Emendata
        </i>
        , Amended Florentine Pronunciation) is based on the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accent_(dialect)" title="Accent (dialect)">
         accent
        </a>
        of
        <a href="/wiki/Romanesco_dialect" title="Romanesco dialect">
         Romanesco
        </a>
        (
        <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">
         Roman dialect
        </a>
        ); these are the reasons why
        <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
         Italian
        </a>
        differs significantly from
        <a href="/wiki/Tuscan_dialect" title="Tuscan dialect">
         Tuscan
        </a>
        and its
        <a href="/wiki/Florentine_dialect" title="Florentine dialect">
         Florentine variety
        </a>
        .
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
         <a href="#cite_note-5">
          [5]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Central-Southern_Italian">
        Central-Southern Italian
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Italo-Dalmatian_languages&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Central-Southern Italian">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Central_Italian" title="Central Italian">
         Central Italian
        </a>
        , or Latin-Umbrian-Marchegian dialects: mainly spoken in the regions of:
        <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">
         Lazio
        </a>
        (which includes
        <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">
         Rome
        </a>
        );
        <a href="/wiki/Umbria" title="Umbria">
         Umbria
        </a>
        ; central
        <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">
         Marche
        </a>
        ; a small part of
        <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">
         Abruzzo
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">
         Tuscany
        </a>
        .
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Romanesco_dialect" title="Romanesco dialect">
           Romanesco
          </a>
          , spoken in
          <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">
           Rome
          </a>
          and Lazio.
         </li>
         <li>
          Castelli Romani: spoken in the
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castelli_Romani" title="Castelli Romani">
           Castelli Romani
          </a>
          , in the Provence of Rome, Lazio.
         </li>
         <li>
          Tuscia or Viterbo: spoken in the
          <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Viterbo" title="Province of Viterbo">
           Province of Viterbo
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">
           Lazio
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          Ciociaro: spoken in
          <a href="/wiki/Ciociaria" title="Ciociaria">
           Ciociaria
          </a>
          , in the
          <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Frosinone" title="Province of Frosinone">
           Province of Frosinone
          </a>
          , Lazio.
         </li>
         <li>
          Umbrian: spoken in
          <a href="/wiki/Umbria" title="Umbria">
           Umbria
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          Central
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marchigiano" title="Marchigiano">
           Marchigiano
          </a>
          : spoken in central
          <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">
           Marche
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Sabino_dialect" title="Sabino dialect">
           Sabino
          </a>
          : spoken in the city of
          <a href="/wiki/L%27Aquila" title="L'Aquila">
           L'Aquila
          </a>
          (
          <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">
           Abruzzo
          </a>
          ) and the
          <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Rieti" title="Province of Rieti">
           Province of Rieti
          </a>
          (Lazio).
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Neapolitan_language" title="Neapolitan language">
         Neapolitan language
        </a>
        , or known in the "southern dialect group" is spoken in: southern
        <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">
         Marche
        </a>
        ; southern
        <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">
         Lazio
        </a>
        ;
        <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">
         Abruzzo
        </a>
        ;
        <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">
         Molise
        </a>
        ;
        <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">
         Campania
        </a>
        (including
        <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">
         Naples
        </a>
        );
        <a href="/wiki/Basilicata" title="Basilicata">
         Basilicata
        </a>
        ; and the north of both
        <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">
         Apulia
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Calabria" title="Calabria">
         Calabria
        </a>
        .
        <ul>
         <li>
          Campano dialects of
          <a href="/wiki/Neapolitan_language" title="Neapolitan language">
           Neapolitan
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irpino" title="Irpino">
           Irpino
          </a>
          , Southern Laziale: spoken in
          <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">
           Naples
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">
           Campania
          </a>
          ; and southern
          <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">
           Lazio
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Neapolitan_language" title="Neapolitan language">
           Abruzzese
          </a>
          -Southern Marchigiano: spoken in the
          <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">
           Abruzzo
          </a>
          region and southern
          <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">
           Marche
          </a>
          region.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Molisan" title="Molisan">
           Molisan
          </a>
          : spoken in the
          <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">
           Molise
          </a>
          region.
         </li>
         <li>
          Basilicatine (Lucanian): spoken in the region of
          <a href="/wiki/Basilicata" title="Basilicata">
           Basilicata
          </a>
          , also known as Lucania.
         </li>
         <li>
          Pugliese, or Apulian: spoken in the northern region of
          <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">
           Apulia
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Calabria#Northern_Calabrian_.28Cosentino.29" title="Languages of Calabria">
           Cosentino
          </a>
          , also known as or Northern Calabrian:
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calabrian_6-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-calabrian-6">
            [6]
           </a>
          </sup>
          spoken in the
          <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Cosenza" title="Province of Cosenza">
           Province of Cosenza
          </a>
          , in northern
          <a href="/wiki/Calabria" title="Calabria">
           Calabria
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lu_indialett_di_lu_Ua%C5%A1t" title="Lu indialett di lu Uašt">
           Lu indialett di lu Uašt
          </a>
          Spoken in the town of Vasto.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_language" title="Sicilian language">
         Sicilian language
        </a>
        , or known in Italian linguistics as the "extreme southern dialect group", is spoken on the island of
        <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">
         Sicily
        </a>
        ; and in the south of both
        <a href="/wiki/Calabria" title="Calabria">
         Calabria
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">
         Apulia
        </a>
        ; and some of
        <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">
         Campania
        </a>
        .
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_language" title="Sicilian language">
           Sicilian
          </a>
          , spoken on the island of
          <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">
           Sicily
          </a>
          : Western Sicilian; Central Metafonetica; Southeast Metafonetica; Ennese; Eastern Nonmetafonetica; Messinese.
         </li>
         <li>
          Sicilian dialects on other islands: Isole Eolie, on the Aeolian Islands; Pantesco, on the island of Pantelleria.
         </li>
         <li>
          <i>
           <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Calabria#Central-Southern_Calabrian" title="Languages of Calabria">
            Calabro
           </a>
          </i>
          ,
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calabrian_6-1">
           <a href="#cite_note-calabrian-6">
            [6]
           </a>
          </sup>
          or Central-Southern Calabrian:
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calabrian_6-2">
           <a href="#cite_note-calabrian-6">
            [6]
           </a>
          </sup>
          dialects are spoken in the central and southern areas of the region of
          <a href="/wiki/Calabria" title="Calabria">
           Calabria
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Salentino_dialect" title="Salentino dialect">
           Salentino
          </a>
          , spoken in the
          <a href="/wiki/Salento" title="Salento">
           Salento
          </a>
          region of southern
          <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">
           Apulia
          </a>
          .
         </li>
         <li>
          Southern Cilentan: spoken in
          <a href="/wiki/Roccagloriosa" title="Roccagloriosa">
           Roccagloriosa
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Rofrano" title="Rofrano">
           Rofrano
          </a>
          in southern tip of
          <a href="/wiki/Cilento" title="Cilento">
           Cilento
          </a>
          , which is southern
          <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salerno" title="Province of Salerno">
           Province of Salerno
          </a>
          , in the Campania region.
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Cilentan_dialect" title="Cilentan dialect">
         Cilentan
        </a>
        : spoken in Cilento, influenced by both Neapolitan language and Sicilian language.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       In addition, some
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallo-Italic" title="Gallo-Italic">
        Gallo-Italic
       </a>
       languages are spoken in Central-Southern Italy.
      </p>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judeo-Italian_language" title="Judeo-Italian language">
        Judeo-Italian languages
       </a>
       are varieties of Italian used by Jewish communities, between the 10th and the 20th centuries, in
       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
        Italy
       </a>

       <a href="/wiki/Corfu" title="Corfu">
        Corfu
       </a>
       and

        Zante
       </a>
       .
