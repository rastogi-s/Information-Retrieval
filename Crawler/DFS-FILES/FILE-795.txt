      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Latin Union
       </b>
       is a defunct
       <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">
        international organization
       </a>
       of nations that used
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance languages
       </a>
       , with the aim of protecting, projecting, and promoting the common cultural heritage of
       <a href="/wiki/Latin_peoples" title="Latin peoples">
        Latin peoples
       </a>
       and unifying identities of the
       <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
        Latin
       </a>
       , and Latin-influenced, world. It was created in 1954 in
       <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">
        Madrid
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">
        Spain
       </a>
       , and existed as a functional institution from 1983 to 2012. Its membership rose from 12 to 36 states, including countries in
       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">
        North America
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">
        South America
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
        Europe
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
        Africa
       </a>
       , and the
       <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific" title="Asia-Pacific">
        Asia-Pacific
       </a>
       region.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The official names of the Latin Union were:
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="ca">
          Unió Llatina
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       ,
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="fr">
          Union Latine
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       ,
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="it">
          Unione Latina
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       ,
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="pt">
          União Latina
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       ,
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="ro">
          Uniunea Latină
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">
        Romanian
       </a>
       , and
       <b>
        <i>
         <span lang="es">
          Unión Latina
         </span>
        </i>
       </b>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Due to financial difficulties, the Latin Union announced on 26 January 2012 the suspension of its activities, the dissolution of its Secretariat General (effective July 31, 2012) and termination of employment for all the organization's personnel.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">
        Membership
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Membership">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_Latin_Union" title="Member states of the Latin Union">
        Member states of the Latin Union
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       According to the Latin Union's website, membership was open to any nation that meets the following criteria:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">
         Linguistic
        </a>
        criteria :
        <ul>
         <li>
          Official language derived from Latin
         </li>
         <li>
          Latin-derived language used in
          <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">
           education
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          Latin-derived language commonly used in the
          <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">
           mass media
          </a>
          or in daily life
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        Linguistic/cultural criteria :
        <ul>
         <li>
          Existence of significant
          <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">
           literature
          </a>
          in a Latin-derived language
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">
           Press
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Publishing" title="Publishing">
           publication
          </a>
          in Latin-derived language
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
           Television
          </a>
          with a strong proportion of the programming in a Latin-derived language
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">
           Radio
          </a>
          widely broadcast in a Latin-derived language
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        Cultural criteria :
        <ul>
         <li>
          Direct or indirect inheritance of the legacy of
          <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">
           Ancient Rome
          </a>
          , to which the state remains faithful and which it perpetuates mainly through the education of
          <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
           Latin
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">
           Cultural
          </a>
          education of Latin-derived foreign languages
         </li>
         <li>
          Exchange programmes with other Latin countries
         </li>
         <li>
          Societal organization, particularly in the
          <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">
           legal
          </a>
          plane, based on respect for fundamental
          <a href="/wiki/Liberty" title="Liberty">
           liberties
          </a>
          , the general principles of
          <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">
           human rights
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">
           democracy
          </a>
          , tolerance, and freedom of
          <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">
           religion
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Official_languages">
        Official languages
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Official languages">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The official languages of the Latin Union were
       <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">
        Catalan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
        Italian
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">
        Portuguese
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">
        Romanian
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       . French, Italian, Portuguese, and Spanish are used as working languages. All the texts of general diffusion were translated into these four languages, with some also going into Romanian and Catalan.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">
        Organization
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Organization">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The Union was composed of three main bodies, namely, the Congress, the Executive Council, and the General Secretariat.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Congress">
        Congress
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Congress">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Congress, which consisted of the representatives of all the Member States, met in ordinary assembly every two years. Its main functions were
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        to adopt the budget,
       </li>
       <li>
        to define the general direction of the Union,
       </li>
       <li>
        to receive the new Member States formally, and
       </li>
       <li>
        to elect and appoint different Member States to be the Presidents, Vice-Presidents, and members of the sub-organisations of the Union.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       A President and two Vice-Presidents were also elected by the Congress.
       <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Serebrian" title="Oleg Serebrian">
        Oleg Serebrian
       </a>
       from the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Moldova" title="Republic of Moldova">
        Republic of Moldova
       </a>
       was the last President.
      </p>
      <p>
       There were also two auxiliary bodies of the Congress, namely, the Commission of Adhesions and the Commission of Candidacies.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The Commission of Adhesions was composed of 10 Member States and was responsible for promoting the adherence of all the Member States of the Union.
       </li>
       <li>
        The Commission of Candidacies was composed of 9 Member States and was responsible for examining the validity of the candidacies, taking account of the geographical and linguistic-cultural division.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Council">
        Executive Council
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Executive Council">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Executive Council was the executive branch of the Union. It consisted of 12 Member States, which were elected by the Congress every four years, and led by a President and two Vice-Presidents, which were also elected by the Congress.
      </p>
      <p>
       There were also two auxiliary commissions sub-ordinated to the Executive Council:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The Commission of Finance and Programmes
       </li>
       <li>
        The Commission of Statues
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="General_Secretariat">
        General Secretariat
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: General Secretariat">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The Latin Union was directed by a Secretary-General appointed every four years by the Congress. The Secretary was in charge of the execution of the programmes and implemented the decisions made by the Congress and the Executive Council in the matter of budget and general direction.
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jose_Luis_Dicenta_Ballester&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jose Luis Dicenta Ballester (page does not exist)">
        Jose Luis Dicenta Ballester
       </a>
       was at one time Secretary-General of the Union.
      </p>
      <p>
       Subordinated to the Secretary-General, there were 4 directors:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Director of Administration and Finance
       </li>
       <li>
        Director of Culture and Communication
       </li>
       <li>
        Director of Promotion and Education of the Languages
       </li>
       <li>
        Director of Terminology and Industry of the Language
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Finance">
        Finance
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Finance">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       The finance of the Union was mainly supported by the obligatory contributions from the Member States. For some activities, the Union may have collaborated with other public or private institutions.
      </p>
