       <b>
        Haketia
       </b>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">
        Hebrew
       </a>
       :
       <span class="script-hebrew" dir="rtl" style="font-size: 115%; font-family: 'Ezra SIL', 'Ezra SIL SR', 'Keter Aram Tsova', 'Taamey Ashkenaz', 'Taamey David CLM', 'Taamey Frank CLM', 'Frenk Ruehl CLM', 'Keter YG', 'Shofar', 'David CLM', 'Hadasim CLM', 'Simple CLM', 'Nachlieli', 'SBL BibLit', 'SBL Hebrew', Cardo, Alef, 'Noto Serif Hebrew', 'Noto Sans Hebrew', 'David Libre', David, 'Times New Roman', Gisha, Arial, FreeSerif, FreeSans;">
        חקיטייה
       </span>
       ‬,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
        Arabic
       </a>
       :
       <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        حاكيتيا
       </span>
       ‎) (also written as
       <b>
        Hakitia
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        Haquitía
       </b>
       ) is an
       <a href="/wiki/Endangered_language" title="Endangered language">
        endangered
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Jewish_languages" title="Jewish languages">
        Jewish
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">
        Romance language
       </a>
       also known as
       <i>
        Djudeo Spañol
       </i>
       ,
       <i>
        Ladino Occidental
       </i>
       , or Western
       <a href="/wiki/Judaeo-Spanish" title="Judaeo-Spanish">
        Judaeo-Spanish
       </a>
       . It was historically spoken by the
       <a href="/wiki/North_African_Sephardim" title="North African Sephardim">
        North African Sephardim
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">
        Moroccan
       </a>
       cities of
       <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A9touan" title="Tétouan">
        Tétouan

       <a href="/wiki/Tangier" title="Tangier">
        Tangier
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Asilah" title="Asilah">
        Asilah
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Larache" title="Larache">
        Larache
       </a>
       and the Spanish towns of
       <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">
        Ceuta
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Melilla" title="Melilla">
        Melilla
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Tetuani_Ladino" title="Tetuani Ladino">
        Tetuani Ladino
       </a>
       was also spoken in
       <a href="/wiki/Oran" title="Oran">
        Oran
       </a>
       , Algeria.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
        Description
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Haketia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Delacroix_letter.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="618" data-file-width="712" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Delacroix_letter.png/260px-Delacroix_letter.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Delacroix_letter.png/390px-Delacroix_letter.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Delacroix_letter.png/520px-Delacroix_letter.png 2x" width="260"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Delacroix_letter.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         An original letter in Haketia from Tangier, written in 1832.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The well-known form of Judaeo-Spanish spoken by Jews living in the Balkans, Greece, Turkey and Jerusalem is "Ladino Oriental" (eastern Ladino). Haketia may be described by contrast as "Ladino Occidental". The language is a variety of
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       that borrows heavily from
       <a href="/wiki/Judeo-Moroccan_Arabic" title="Judeo-Moroccan Arabic">
        Judeo-Moroccan Arabic
       </a>
       . It evidently also contains a number of words of Hebrew origin and was originally written using
       <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_alphabet" title="Hebrew alphabet">
        Hebrew letters
       </a>
       . There is some cultural resemblance between the two Judaeo-Spanish dialect communities, including a rich shared stock of
       <i>
        Romanzas
       </i>
       (ballads) from medieval Spain, though both words and music often differ in detail (as indeed they do between one Oriental-Sephardic community and another).
      </p>
      <p>
       The name "Haketia" derived from the Arabic
       <i>
        ḥaká
       </i>
       حكى, "tell",
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and is therefore pronounced with
       <small>
        IPA:
       </small>
       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">
        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">
         [ḥ]
        </a>
       </span>
       , reflecting the Arabic
       <i>
        ḥāʾ
       </i>
       <span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        ح
       </span>
       . In some places it is written "Jaquetía" with the same pronunciation.
      </p>
      <p>
       Haketia is considered to have influenced
       <a href="/wiki/Llanito" title="Llanito">
        Llanito
       </a>
       , the
       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Vernacular" title="wikt:Vernacular">
        vernacular
       </a>
       spoken in the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_overseas_territory" title="British overseas territory">
        British overseas territory
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">
        Gibraltar
       </a>
       due to migration of
       <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Gibraltar" title="History of the Jews in Gibraltar">
        Moroccan Jews
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_use">
        Modern use
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Haketia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern use">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Haketia, unlike other varieties of Judaeo-Spanish, did not develop a literary tradition and so the language remained as a colloquial form of communication and was not used as a vehicle for formal education since in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Morocco" title="Spanish Morocco">
        Spanish Morocco
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
        Spanish
       </a>
       was used, along with
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       , at the
       <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Isra%C3%A9lite_Universelle" title="Alliance Israélite Universelle">
        Alliance Israélite Universelle
       </a>
       schools. The influence of the Spanish and French conquests and the large number of Jews from northern Morocco who emigrated to
       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">
        Venezuela
       </a>
       , Spain and later
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">
        Argentina
       </a>
       , the language was levelled with modern Spanish, which has contributed greatly to its extinction. Still, there has been a slow renaissance of the language, helped by musicians such as
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Doris_Benmaman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Doris Benmaman (page does not exist)">
        Doris Benmaman
       </a>
       <span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">
        (
        <a class="extiw" href="https://lad.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doris_Benmaman" title="lad:Doris Benmaman">
         lad
        </a>
        )
       </span>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Mor_Karbasi" title="Mor Karbasi">
        Mor Karbasi
       </a>
       and
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kol_Oud_Tof_Trio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kol Oud Tof Trio (page does not exist)">
        Kol Oud Tof Trio
       </a>
       <span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">
        (
        <a class="extiw" href="https://lad.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kol_Oud_Tof_Trio" title="lad:Kol Oud Tof Trio">
         lad
        </a>
        )
       </span>
       , among others.
